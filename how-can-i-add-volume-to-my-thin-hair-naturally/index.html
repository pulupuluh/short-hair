How Can I Add Volume To My Thin Hair Naturally

Written by Frank May 20, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have thin hair, adding volume to it can be a real challenge. You want to avoid using products that weigh your hair down, but at the same time, you need something that will give it lift and body. In this post, we’ll show you how to add volume to thin hair naturally. Here’s what we’ll cover:

Description

Thin hair can be a real challenge to style. It’s flat, limp, and lacks body. But don’t worry, there are ways to add volume to your hair without using harsh chemicals or expensive hair products. In this blog post, we’ll show you how to add volume to your thin hair naturally. We’ll give you step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice to help you achieve the voluminous hair you’ve always wanted.

How to Add Volume to Thin Hair Naturally

There are many ways to add volume to your hair naturally, but here are a few of the most effective methods:

Cut Your Hair

The first step to adding volume to your hair is to get a good haircut. A layered cut can help to create the illusion of thicker hair. Ask your hairstylist to add layers to your hair, starting at the crown and working down to the ends.

Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner can help to add body to your hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, and collagen, which can help to strengthen your hair and make it look thicker.

Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow drying your hair upside down can help to add volume to the roots. Start by flipping your hair over and blow dry your hair from the roots to the ends. Use a round brush to lift your hair at the roots and create volume.

Use Velcro Rollers

Velcro rollers can help to add volume to your hair. Start by blow drying your hair until it’s about 80% dry. Then, section your hair and roll each section onto a velcro roller. Leave the rollers in for 10-15 minutes, then remove them and style your hair as desired.

Avoid Heavy Hair Products

Avoid using heavy hair products that weigh your hair down, such as pomades and waxes. Instead, use lightweight products like mousse or sea salt spray, which can help to add texture and volume to your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to adding volume to your thin hair:

  1. Get a layered haircut to create the illusion of thicker hair.
  2. Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  3. Blow dry your hair upside down, using a round brush to lift your hair at the roots.
  4. Use velcro rollers to add volume to your hair.
  5. Avoid using heavy hair products that weigh your hair down.

Tips for Adding Volume to Thin Hair

Here are a few tips to help you add volume to your thin hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it look flat.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as this can help to prevent breakage and damage.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools like straighteners and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it look flat.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, which can help to strengthen your hair and make it look thicker.

FAQ

Q: Can I add volume to my hair without using heat?

A: Yes, you can use velcro rollers to add volume to your hair without using heat. Simply roll your hair onto the rollers and leave them in for 10-15 minutes.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It’s best to wash your hair every 2-3 days, as this can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent it from looking flat.

Hair Care for Thin Hair

To keep your thin hair looking healthy and voluminous, it’s important to take good care of it. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools like straighteners and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it look flat.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, which can help to strengthen your hair and make it look thicker.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back tightly, as this can cause breakage and damage.

By following these tips and using natural methods to add volume to your hair, you can achieve the voluminous, healthy hair you’ve always wanted.
