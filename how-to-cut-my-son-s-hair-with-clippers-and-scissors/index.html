How To Cut My Son s Hair With Clippers And Scissors

Written by Eveline Jun 26, 2023 · 6 min read

Cutting your son's hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have never done it before. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can give your son a great haircut in the comfort of your own home. In this article, we will go over how to cut my son's hair with clippers and scissors.

Table of Contents

  • Tools Needed
  • How To Cut My Son's Hair with Clippers and Scissors
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Son's Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After the Haircut

Tools Needed

Before we get started, you will need to gather the necessary tools. Here is what you will need:

  • Clippers with various guards
  • Scissors
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle of water
  • A towel or cape to catch hair
  • A mirror
  • Patience and a steady hand

How To Cut My Son's Hair with Clippers and Scissors

Using clippers and scissors to cut your son's hair is a great way to achieve a professional-looking haircut. The clippers will help you achieve an even cut on the sides and back, while scissors will help you achieve the desired length and style on the top. Here are the steps to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Decide on the desired haircut style and length.
  2. Wash your son's hair and dry it thoroughly.
  3. Use clippers with a guard to trim the sides and back. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one until you achieve the desired length. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly.
  4. Use scissors to cut the hair on the top of your son's head. Start with longer length and work your way down. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly.
  5. Blend the sides and back with the top of the head using scissors to create a smooth transition.
  6. Trim the hair around the ears and the neckline with clippers or scissors, depending on your preference.
  7. Check for any uneven spots and make necessary adjustments.
  8. Clean up any loose hair and style the hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Your Son's Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve a great haircut:

  • Start with longer guards and work your way down to a shorter one until you achieve the desired length.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly.
  • Take your time and be patient.
  • Check the hair in different lighting to ensure evenness.
  • Keep the clippers and scissors clean and oiled for optimal performance.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your son's hair:

Q: How often should I cut my son's hair?

A: It depends on the desired length and style. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is a good time frame for a trim.

Q: How do I know which guard to use?

A: It depends on the desired length. Start with a longer guard and work your way down until you achieve the desired length.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: Don't panic! Mistakes can be fixed by blending the hair or adjusting the length. Take your time and make necessary adjustments.

Hair Care After the Haircut

After the haircut, it is important to take care of your son's hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash the hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep the hair soft and smooth.
  • Apply a styling product, if desired, to achieve the desired look.
  • Trim the hair regularly to maintain the desired length and style.

By following these steps and tips, you can give your son a great haircut with clippers and scissors. Remember to be patient and take your time, and don't be afraid to make adjustments as needed. With practice, you'll become a pro at cutting your son's hair!
