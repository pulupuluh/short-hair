Am I Losing My Hair Because Of Covid A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia May 20, 2023 · 7 min read

2023 has been a challenging year for all of us, as we continue to deal with the aftermath of the Covid-19 pandemic. While the virus has wreaked havoc on our physical and mental health, it has also had unexpected effects on our hair. Many people have noticed a sudden increase in hair loss, which has led to concerns about whether Covid-19 is the culprit. In this article, we will explore the relationship between Covid-19 and hair loss, as well as provide tips on how to care for your hair during these uncertain times.

Does Covid-19 Cause Hair Loss?

The short answer is yes, Covid-19 can cause hair loss. However, it's important to note that not everyone who contracts Covid-19 will experience hair loss. Hair loss is a common side effect of any illness that causes a high fever or severe stress on the body, which is why it's not surprising that some Covid-19 patients have reported hair loss.

How Does Covid-19 Cause Hair Loss?

When your body is under stress, it enters a state of shock that can cause hair follicles to enter the resting phase of the hair cycle. This means that the hair stops growing and eventually falls out. Covid-19 causes a lot of stress on the body, which can trigger this reaction in some people. Additionally, Covid-19 can cause inflammation throughout the body, which can damage hair follicles and lead to hair loss.

How Can I Prevent Hair Loss During Covid-19?

While there's no guaranteed way to prevent hair loss during Covid-19, there are a few things you can do to minimize your risk:

1. Practice Good Hair Care

Make sure you're taking good care of your hair by washing it regularly, using a gentle shampoo, and avoiding harsh chemicals or heat styling tools.

2. Get Enough Nutrients

Eating a healthy diet can help support hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough protein, iron, and vitamins like B12 and D.

3. Manage Your Stress

Stress is a major contributor to hair loss, so finding ways to manage your stress levels is key. This could include exercise, meditation, or talking to a therapist.

4. Talk to Your Doctor

If you're experiencing hair loss or other symptoms after contracting Covid-19, talk to your doctor. They may be able to provide additional advice or recommend treatments to help support hair growth.

FAQ

Can wearing a mask cause hair loss?

There is no evidence to suggest that wearing a mask can cause hair loss. However, if you're wearing a tight-fitting mask for extended periods of time, it could potentially cause friction on the hairline or behind the ears, which could lead to breakage or hair loss in those areas.

Will my hair grow back after Covid-19?

In most cases, hair loss caused by Covid-19 is temporary and your hair will eventually grow back. However, it can take several months for your hair to return to its normal growth cycle, so be patient.

Tips for Hair Care During Covid-19

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid hairstyles that put too much tension on the hair, such as tight braids, ponytails, or buns. Instead, opt for looser styles that allow your hair to breathe.

2. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help reduce friction on your hair, which can prevent breakage and hair loss.

3. Avoid Overwashing

While it's important to keep your hair clean, overwashing can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or as needed.

4. Try a Hair Growth Supplement

There are many hair growth supplements on the market that claim to support healthy hair growth. Talk to your doctor before starting any new supplement regimen.

Conclusion

While Covid-19 can cause hair loss, it's important to remember that this is a temporary side effect for most people. By taking good care of your hair and managing your stress levels, you can minimize your risk of hair loss and support healthy hair growth. If you're concerned about hair loss or other symptoms, don't hesitate to talk to your doctor for advice.
