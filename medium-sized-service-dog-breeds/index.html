Medium Sized Service Dog Breeds A Guide

Written by Petter Mar 06, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're looking for a service dog that's not too big and not too small, a medium sized breed might be the perfect fit. In this guide, we'll introduce you to some of the most popular medium sized service dog breeds, as well as provide tips and advice for caring for them.

What is a medium sized dog?

Medium sized dogs typically weigh between 30 and 60 pounds and stand between 18 and 24 inches tall. They are larger than small dogs, but not as large as many of the giant breeds.

How to choose a medium sized service dog breed

When choosing a service dog breed, it's important to consider your individual needs and lifestyle. Some breeds may be better suited for certain tasks or living situations than others.

For example, if you need a dog to assist with mobility, a breed with a sturdy build, such as a Labrador Retriever or Golden Retriever, may be a good choice. If you live in an apartment or don't have a lot of space, a breed that doesn't require a lot of exercise, such as a Poodle or Cavalier King Charles Spaniel, may be a better fit.

Popular medium sized service dog breeds

1. Labrador Retriever

The Labrador Retriever is one of the most popular service dog breeds, thanks to its intelligence, loyalty, and easy-going nature. Labs are often used as guide dogs for the blind, as well as for other tasks such as mobility assistance and search and rescue.

2. Golden Retriever

Golden Retrievers are another popular choice for service dog work, particularly as guide dogs and therapy dogs. They are friendly, patient, and eager to please, making them ideal for working with people.

3. Standard Poodle

Poodles are known for their intelligence and hypoallergenic coats, making them a great choice for people with allergies. They are often used as assistance dogs for people with disabilities, as well as for search and rescue work.

4. Boxer

Boxers are a high-energy breed that can make great service dogs for people who need help with mobility or balance. They are also known for their loyalty and affectionate nature.

5. Border Collie

Border Collies are highly intelligent and trainable, making them great candidates for service dog work. They are often used as hearing dogs for the deaf, as well as for other tasks such as search and rescue.

Tips for caring for your medium sized service dog

Regardless of which breed you choose, it's important to provide your service dog with plenty of love, attention, and proper care. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Feed your dog a balanced and nutritious diet
  • Provide plenty of exercise and mental stimulation
  • Keep up with regular vet visits and vaccinations
  • Train your dog using positive reinforcement techniques
  • Invest in a good quality harness or vest for your dog to wear while working

FAQ: Medium Sized Service Dog Breeds

Q: Can any medium sized breed be trained as a service dog?

A: While any breed has the potential to be trained as a service dog, certain breeds may be better suited for certain tasks or living situations than others. It's important to do your research and choose a breed that fits your individual needs.

Q: How long does it take to train a service dog?

A: The length of time it takes to train a service dog can vary depending on the individual dog and the tasks it's being trained for. It can take anywhere from several months to a few years to fully train a service dog.

Q: Can I train my own service dog?

A: Yes, it's possible to train your own service dog, but it can be a challenging and time-consuming process. It's important to have the right temperament and mindset, as well as the necessary training skills and resources.

Hair care for medium sized service dog breeds

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your medium sized service dog healthy and comfortable. Depending on the breed, your dog may require regular grooming, including brushing, bathing, and trimming. It's important to use high-quality grooming tools and products, and to establish a regular grooming routine to keep your dog's coat looking and feeling its best.
