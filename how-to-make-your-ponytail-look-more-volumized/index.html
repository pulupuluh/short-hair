How To Make Your Ponytail Look More Volumized

Written by Robby Mar 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have thin or fine hair, creating a voluminous ponytail can be a challenge. However, with a few tricks and techniques, you can get the full, bouncy ponytail you've always wanted. In this article, we'll go over how to make your ponytail look more volumized, including step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Make Your Ponytail Look More Volumized
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for a Voluminous Ponytail

  • Hair Care for a Volumized Ponytail

How to Make Your Ponytail Look More Volumized

There are a few ways to add volume to your ponytail, including:

  • Teasing your hair
  • Using hair extensions
  • Adding texture
  • Creating height at the crown of your head

By combining these techniques, you can achieve a full, voluminous ponytail that will stay in place all day.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here's how to make your ponytail look more volumized:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with volumizing products. Blow-dry your hair, using a round brush to create volume at the roots. If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, use a diffuser to enhance your natural texture.


Step 2: Tease Your Hair

Using a fine-tooth comb, tease the crown of your head by backcombing small sections of hair. This will create volume and height at the top of your head.


Step 3: Add Hair Extensions

If you want even more volume, clip-in hair extensions can help. Choose extensions that match your hair color and texture, and clip them in at the crown of your head. Make sure to blend the extensions with your natural hair.


Step 4: Create Your Ponytail

Gather your hair into a high ponytail, using a hair tie to secure it in place. If you want a more voluminous ponytail, pull out a few strands of hair at the front to frame your face.


Step 5: Add Texture

To create texture and volume, use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo on your roots and ends. This will give your hair a matte finish and help it hold its shape.


Step 6: Finish with Hairspray

Finally, spritz your ponytail with a light-hold hairspray to keep it in place all day. Use your fingers to gently tease the ponytail for even more volume.


Tips for a Voluminous Ponytail

Here are a few additional tips for creating a full, voluminous ponytail:

  • Use a hair tie that won't slip, such as a spiral hair tie or scrunchie.
  • Wrap a small section of hair around the hair tie to conceal it.
  • Use bobby pins to secure any loose strands around your face.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about creating a voluminous ponytail:

Q: Will teasing my hair damage it?

A: Teasing your hair can cause damage, but as long as you use a fine-tooth comb and don't tease too aggressively, it should be fine. Just be sure to use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy.


Q: Can I create a voluminous ponytail with short hair?

A: Yes, you can still create a voluminous ponytail with short hair. Use a hair tie to gather your hair at the crown of your head, and tease it for extra volume.


Q: How can I make my ponytail look more polished?

A: To make your ponytail look more polished, use a smoothing serum on your ends to tame any flyaways. You can also wrap a small section of hair around the hair tie and secure it with a bobby pin for a more polished look.


Hair Care for a Volumized Ponytail

To keep your hair healthy and voluminous, use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat on your hair. Use a heat protectant spray before blow-drying or using hot tools, and try to air-dry your hair whenever possible.


By following these tips and techniques, you can create a full, voluminous ponytail that will make you feel confident and stylish.
