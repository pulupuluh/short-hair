Hair Styles For Curly Hair Over 50 Tips How To And Faqs

As we age, our hair tends to lose its elasticity and moisture, making it more susceptible to breakage and frizz. For women over 50 with curly hair, finding the right hairstyle can be challenging. In this post, we'll discuss some tips, how-to, and frequently asked questions about hair styles for curly hair over 50.

Hair Care for Curly Hair Over 50

Before we dive into hairstyles, let's talk about hair care for curly hair over 50. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can dry out your curls.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.

Hairstyles for Curly Hair Over 50

Now, let's talk about some hairstyles that are flattering for women over 50 with curly hair:

Short Curly Bob

A short curly bob is a chic and easy-to-maintain hairstyle that's perfect for women over 50. This hairstyle works well with hair that's shoulder-length or shorter. To style, apply a curl-defining cream to damp hair and scrunch your curls as you blow dry.

Long Curly Layers

If you have long curly hair, consider adding layers to give your hair more shape and movement. To style, apply a leave-in conditioner to damp hair and scrunch your curls as you air dry. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your curls.

Curly Pixie Cut

A curly pixie cut is a bold and flattering hairstyle for women over 50. This hairstyle works well with hair that's on the shorter side. To style, apply a curl-enhancing gel to damp hair and use your fingers to shape your curls as you air dry.

How to Style Curly Hair Over 50

Styling curly hair over 50 can be challenging, but with the right products and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls. Here's how:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a curl-enhancing product, such as a cream or gel, to your hair.
  3. Scrunch your hair to enhance your curls.
  4. Let your hair air dry, or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low setting.
  5. If your hair is prone to frizz, apply an anti-frizz serum to your hair after it's dry.

Tips for Styling Curly Hair Over 50

Here are some additional tips for styling curly hair over 50:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid tangles and breakage.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as these can dry out your hair.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your curls healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQs on Curly Hair Over 50

Q: Can I dye my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can dye your curly hair. However, it's important to use a dye that's specifically formulated for curly hair, as curly hair tends to be more fragile and prone to breakage than straight hair.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It's best to wash your curly hair every 2-3 days, as washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils and cause frizz.

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but it's important to use a heat protectant spray and a low heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.

Q: How can I prevent my curls from becoming frizzy?

A: To prevent frizz, avoid using products that contain alcohol, sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase, and apply an anti-frizz serum to your hair after it's dry.


In conclusion, there are plenty of beautiful and flattering hairstyles for women over 50 with curly hair. By following the tips and techniques outlined in this post, you can keep your curls healthy, shiny, and frizz-free. Remember to embrace your natural curls and experiment with different styles to find the one that suits you best.
