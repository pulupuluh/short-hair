How To Thin Short Hair With Thinning Scissors

Written by Eveline Jun 23, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Thinning out short hair can be tricky, especially if you have thick or coarse hair. Thankfully, there is a tool that can make thinning your hair much easier: thinning scissors. Thinning scissors are a must-have for anyone looking to achieve a more balanced, textured hairstyle. In this article, we will show you how to thin short hair with thinning scissors and provide some helpful tips for achieving the perfect look.

Description

Thinning scissors are specialized scissors that have one serrated blade and one straight blade. The serrated blade has teeth that are spaced apart, allowing the scissors to remove hair without creating blunt lines or choppy cuts. Thinning scissors are perfect for thinning out short hair because they allow you to remove bulk while maintaining a natural look.

How to Thin Short Hair with Thinning Scissors

Before you start thinning your hair, make sure it is completely dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut and can cause uneven results. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thin short hair with thinning scissors:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using hair clips. This will help you work on one section at a time and prevent you from accidentally cutting too much hair.

Step 2: Start at the Bottom

Start at the bottom of each section and work your way up. Hold the thinning scissors at a 45-degree angle and cut into your hair, removing small sections of hair at a time. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

After each section, comb your hair to see how much you have thinned. If you need to thin more, repeat the previous step until you achieve your desired look.

Step 4: Blend Your Hair

Blend your hair by using regular scissors to cut any remaining blunt lines. This will help create a more natural look and prevent any choppy cuts.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect look when thinning your short hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. You can always thin more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Be patient. Thinning hair can be time-consuming, but taking your time will help you achieve a more balanced look.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Don't thin too close to your scalp. You want to maintain some volume and texture in your hair.

FAQ

Can I thin my hair with regular scissors?

You can thin your hair with regular scissors, but thinning scissors are designed to create a more natural look. Regular scissors can create blunt lines and choppy cuts, which can be difficult to blend.

How often should I thin my short hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, you should thin your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain a balanced look.

Hair Care

Thinning your hair can cause some damage, so it's important to take care of your hair afterwards. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often. Heat can cause damage and breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools.

Thinning short hair with thinning scissors is a great way to achieve a balanced, textured look. By following these tips and using the right tools, you can achieve the perfect hairstyle for your hair type and personal style.
