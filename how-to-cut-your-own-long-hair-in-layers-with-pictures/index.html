How To Cut Your Own Long Hair In Layers With Pictures

Written by Bardi Aug 10, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're tired of your long, flat hair and want to add some volume and dimension, you might be considering layers. But, going to a salon can be expensive, and sometimes you just don't have the time. That's why we've put together a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own long hair in layers with pictures, so you can achieve the look you want from the comfort of your own home.

Table of Contents

  • How To Cut Your Own Long Hair in Layers
  • Step-by-step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

How To Cut Your Own Long Hair in Layers

Before you start, there are a few things you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips
  • Large mirror

It's also a good idea to have a reference photo of the layered haircut you want to achieve.

Step-by-step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own long hair in layers:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. Comb it out to remove any tangles.
  2. Section off the top layer of your hair with a clip, leaving the bottom layer loose.
  3. Take a section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your middle and index fingers, with your fingers parallel to the floor.
  4. Slide your fingers down to the length you want to cut. Be sure to leave a little extra length, as hair will spring up slightly after being cut.
  5. Using your scissors, cut straight across the section of hair. Make sure to keep your fingers straight and parallel to the floor, so you get a straight cut.
  6. Release the next section of hair, and repeat the process. Make sure to match the length of the previous section of hair.
  7. Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, unclip the top layer and repeat the process.
  8. When you're finished cutting all your hair, use your scissors to lightly trim any uneven ends or jagged pieces.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when cutting your own hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair, and cut more as needed. It's easier to cut more than to fix a mistake.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so don't be afraid to take a break and come back to it later.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair is longer and heavier, and will look shorter when it dries.
  • Remember that hair will spring up slightly after being cut. Leave a little extra length to account for this.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair in layers:

Is it easy to cut your own hair in layers?

It can be challenging to cut your own hair, but with practice and patience, it is possible.

How do I know how much hair to cut?

Start with a small amount of hair, and cut more as needed. It's easier to cut more than to fix a mistake.

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Cutting curly hair can be more challenging, as it's harder to see the individual strands. You may want to consider getting help from a friend or stylist.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as blow dryers and straightening irons, too often. These can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and soft.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking great after cutting it in layers.

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve a layered haircut at home. Follow our step-by-step guide, and don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or stylist if you need it. With practice and patience, you can achieve the look you want and save money on salon appointments.
