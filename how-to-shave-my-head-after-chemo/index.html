How To Shave My Head After Chemo A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter May 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have recently undergone chemotherapy treatment, you may be experiencing hair loss. This can be a difficult time, but shaving your head can be empowering and help you feel in control of your appearance. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of shaving your head after chemo, including tips and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Shave My Head After Chemo
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Shaving Your Head
  • FAQs

How to Shave My Head After Chemo

Before you begin, gather the necessary supplies:

  • Electric clippers or a razor
  • Shaving cream or gel
  • Warm water
  • A towel

Once you have your supplies, find a comfortable and well-lit area to begin. It can be helpful to have a friend or family member assist you.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to shave your head after chemo:

  1. Trim your hair with electric clippers or scissors to make the process easier.
  2. Wet your head with warm water and apply shaving cream or gel.
  3. Begin shaving in the direction of hair growth, using a razor or clippers with a guard.
  4. Rinse your head with warm water and check for any missed spots.
  5. Apply a moisturizer or aftershave to soothe your skin.

Remember to take your time and be gentle, as your scalp may be sensitive after chemo.

Tips for Shaving Your Head

Here are some tips to make the process of shaving your head after chemo easier:

  • Use a moisturizing shaving cream or gel to prevent irritation.
  • Shave in the direction of hair growth to avoid ingrown hairs.
  • If using a razor, change the blade frequently to prevent nicks and cuts.
  • Consider wearing a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and cold weather.

FAQs

Is it necessary to shave my head after chemo?

No, it is not necessary to shave your head after chemo. Some people choose to do so to take control of their appearance and embrace a new look.

Will my hair grow back after chemo?

Yes, hair typically grows back within a few months after chemotherapy treatment ends. However, the texture and color of your hair may be different than before.

Can I dye my hair after chemo?

It is best to wait until your hair has fully grown back before dyeing it, as chemotherapy can make your scalp sensitive and more susceptible to damage.

Hair Care After Shaving

After shaving your head, it is important to take care of your scalp and hair:

  • Moisturize your scalp with a gentle lotion or oil.
  • Protect your scalp from the sun with a hat or scarf.
  • Be gentle when washing your hair, using a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as your hair may be more fragile.

Remember that hair loss can be a difficult experience, but it is important to take care of yourself and your mental health during this time. Reach out to friends, family, or a support group for help and support.
