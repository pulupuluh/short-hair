How To Cut Short Shaggy Layers Yourself

Written by Petter Aug 14, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon every few weeks to get a trim? Do you want to save some money and learn how to cut your own hair? If you have short hair and want to add some texture and volume, then cutting shaggy layers is the way to go. In this article, we will show you how to cut short shaggy layers yourself.

Description

Short shaggy layers are all about creating texture and movement in your hair. They are perfect for those who want a low-maintenance hairstyle that still looks trendy and stylish. These layers can be cut using a razor or scissors, and they work best on hair that is already short or medium-length.

How to Cut Short Shaggy Layers Yourself

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of scissors, a razor, a comb, and hair clips. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp while cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts. Create a top section, two side sections, and a back section. Use hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Cut the Top Section

Begin by cutting the top section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up. Use your scissors to cut into the hair at an angle, creating a choppy, shaggy effect. Continue cutting small sections until you have completed the entire top section.

Step 4: Cut the Side Sections

Next, move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair from the front of your head and hold it at a 45-degree angle. Use your scissors to cut into the hair, creating a shaggy effect. Continue cutting small sections until you have completed both side sections.

Step 5: Cut the Back Section

Finally, move on to the back section. Take small sections of hair and cut into them using your razor or scissors. Be sure to create a choppy, shaggy effect throughout the back section.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind while cutting your short shaggy layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use a razor to create a more textured look.
  • Keep your hair damp throughout the cutting process to make it easier to cut.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment and try different techniques to achieve the look you want.

FAQ

How often should I cut my short shaggy layers?

Short shaggy layers can be cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and texture.

Can I cut my short shaggy layers dry?

It's best to cut your short shaggy layers when your hair is damp. This will make it easier to cut and will help you achieve a more even look.

Hair Care

After cutting your short shaggy layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair from drying out. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to add moisture and shine. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

Conclusion

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. Follow these steps to cut your own short shaggy layers and enjoy a trendy, low-maintenance hairstyle. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
