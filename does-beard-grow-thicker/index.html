Does Beard Grow Thicker Everything You Need To Know

Written by Oliver Jul 16, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're one of those men who have always wanted to grow a thick beard, you've probably asked yourself this question many times - does beard grow thicker? The truth is, there are many factors that influence beard growth, including genetics, age, and lifestyle. In this article, we will explore these factors and give you some tips on how to promote a thicker beard.

Table of Contents

  • Genetics and Beard Growth
  • Age and Beard Growth
  • Lifestyle and Beard Growth
  • How to Promote Thicker Beard Growth
  • Tips for Growing a Thicker Beard

Genetics and Beard Growth

One of the most important factors that influence beard growth is genetics. Some men are naturally blessed with thick, full beards, while others struggle to grow even a small amount of facial hair. This is because the amount and thickness of facial hair that a man can grow is largely determined by his genes.

There is no way to change your genetics, so if you come from a family of men with patchy beards, you may have a harder time growing a thick beard. However, this doesn't mean that you can't grow a beard at all. With the right care and attention, you can still achieve a full, healthy beard.

Age and Beard Growth

Another factor that affects beard growth is age. Young men in their late teens and early twenties tend to have less facial hair than older men. However, as they get older, their beard growth tends to increase. This is because as men age, their testosterone levels increase, which can lead to increased facial hair growth.

So, if you're a young man struggling to grow a beard, don't worry. As you get older, your beard will likely become thicker and fuller.

Lifestyle and Beard Growth

Your lifestyle can also have an impact on your beard growth. If you're not getting enough sleep, not eating a healthy diet, or experiencing high levels of stress, this can affect your body's ability to grow hair. In order to promote beard growth, it's important to take care of yourself both physically and mentally.

Additionally, smoking and excessive drinking can also have a negative effect on your beard growth. These habits can decrease blood flow to your hair follicles, which can lead to slower hair growth.

How to Promote Thicker Beard Growth

If you're looking to promote thicker beard growth, there are a few things you can do. First, make sure you're getting enough sleep and eating a healthy diet. This will help promote overall hair growth.

Additionally, consider using a beard oil or balm. These products can help moisturize your skin and hair, which can promote healthy hair growth. You should also consider using a beard brush or comb to help distribute the oil evenly throughout your beard.

Tips for Growing a Thicker Beard

If you're serious about growing a thicker beard, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Be patient - it can take several weeks or even months for your beard to grow in fully.
  • Avoid shaving or trimming your beard too frequently.
  • Don't wash your beard too often - this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Consider taking a biotin supplement, which can help promote hair growth.

FAQs About Beard Growth

Q: Can I make my beard grow faster?

A: Unfortunately, there is no way to make your beard grow faster. However, by following the tips we've outlined in this article, you can promote healthy hair growth and make your beard appear fuller and thicker.

Q: Will shaving my beard make it grow back thicker?

A: No, shaving your beard will not make it grow back thicker. This is a common myth. However, shaving can help promote healthy hair growth by removing dead skin cells and allowing new hair to grow.

Q: Should I use a beard trimmer to shape my beard?

A: Yes, a beard trimmer can be a great tool for shaping your beard. However, be careful not to trim it too short, as this can make it appear thinner.

Proper Hair Care for a Thicker Beard

Finally, it's important to take proper care of your beard if you want it to grow thicker and fuller. This means washing it regularly with a gentle shampoo, conditioning it, and using a beard oil or balm to keep it moisturized. You should also avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your beard, as these can damage your hair and make it appear thinner.

By following these tips and taking good care of your beard, you can promote healthy hair growth and achieve the thick, full beard you've always wanted.
