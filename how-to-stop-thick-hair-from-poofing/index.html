How To Stop Thick Hair From Poofing Tips And Tricks

Written by William Apr 24, 2023 · 8 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it may look full and voluminous, it can also be prone to poofing and becoming unmanageable. If you struggle with poofy hair, don't worry – there are plenty of ways to tame it. In this blog post, we'll go over some tips and tricks for how to stop thick hair from poofing.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Thick Hair from Poofing
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Managing Thick Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

How to Stop Thick Hair from Poofing

There are a few different ways to stop thick hair from poofing. Some involve changing your hair care routine, while others involve styling techniques. Here are some methods to try:

1. Use a smoothing shampoo and conditioner

One of the easiest ways to prevent poofing is to use a smoothing shampoo and conditioner. These products are designed to hydrate and tame frizz, which can help keep your hair looking smooth and sleek. Look for products that contain ingredients like argan oil, keratin, or coconut oil.

2. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can be damaging to your hair, and it can also make it more prone to poofing. Try to avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons as much as possible. If you do need to use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb

When you're combing out your hair, use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. This will help prevent breakage and can also help prevent poofing. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up, being gentle and avoiding tugging or pulling.

4. Apply a hair serum

A hair serum can help tame frizz and add shine to your hair. Apply a small amount to your hair after washing and conditioning, focusing on the ends. Be careful not to use too much, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for how to stop thick hair from poofing:

  1. Wash your hair with a smoothing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  3. Apply a hair serum to your hair.
  4. Blow dry your hair using a round brush.
  5. Avoid heat styling as much as possible.
  6. If you need to use hot tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.
  7. Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day.
  8. If your hair starts to poof, apply a small amount of hair serum to the affected areas.
  9. Consider getting a haircut that is better suited to your hair type.
  10. Visit a stylist who specializes in thick hair for more personalized advice.

Tips for Managing Thick Hair

In addition to the tips above, here are some other ways to manage thick hair:

  • Avoid using too many styling products, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Consider getting a keratin treatment to help smooth and tame your hair.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for your hair type.
  • Use a microfiber towel to dry your hair, as this is gentler than a regular towel.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about how to stop thick hair from poofing:

What causes thick hair to poof?

Thick hair can poof for a number of reasons, including humidity, heat styling, and using the wrong hair care products.

Can thinning hair reduce poofing?

Thinning your hair can help reduce poofing, as it can make your hair lighter and easier to manage. However, this isn't a solution for everyone and should only be done under the guidance of a professional stylist.

What type of haircut is best for thick hair?

A layered haircut is often best for thick hair, as it can help reduce bulk and make your hair easier to manage.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

Finally, here are some general hair care tips for thick hair:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove buildup from styling products.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can dry it out.
  • Use a detangling spray or leave-in conditioner to make combing easier.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you should be able to keep your thick hair from poofing and looking its best. Remember to be gentle with your hair and avoid over-styling, and you should be able to achieve the smooth, sleek look you're after.
