How Does Depression Cause Hair Loss

Written by Christine Feb 18, 2023 · 8 min read

Depression is a serious mental health condition that affects millions of people worldwide. It's a complex disorder that can cause a range of physical symptoms, including hair loss. In this article, we'll explore how depression can cause hair loss, and what you can do to prevent and treat it.

Table of Contents

  • What is depression?
  • How does depression cause hair loss?
  • How to prevent hair loss caused by depression?
  • Step by step guide to treating hair loss caused by depression
  • Tips for managing depression-related hair loss
  • FAQs
  • Hair care tips for people with depression-related hair loss

What is depression?

Depression is a mental health disorder that affects the way a person thinks, feels, and behaves. It's characterized by feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and a loss of interest in activities that the person once enjoyed. Depression can also cause physical symptoms, such as fatigue, insomnia, and changes in appetite.

How does depression cause hair loss?

Depression can cause hair loss in several ways:

  • Stress: Depression is a stressful condition, and stress is a known cause of hair loss. When you're under stress, your body releases a hormone called cortisol, which can lead to hair loss over time.
  • Lack of nutrition: Depression can cause a lack of appetite, which can lead to a lack of proper nutrition. When your body doesn't get enough nutrients, it can't produce healthy hair.
  • Changes in hormone levels: Depression can cause changes in hormone levels in the body, which can affect hair growth. For example, depression can cause an increase in the production of androgens, which can lead to hair loss.

How to prevent hair loss caused by depression?

The best way to prevent hair loss caused by depression is to manage your depression. This can involve a range of strategies, including:

  • Talking to a therapist: Therapy can help you learn coping skills and manage your depression.
  • Taking medication: Antidepressant medication can help manage symptoms of depression.
  • Exercising: Exercise can help reduce stress and improve mood.
  • Eating a healthy diet: Eating a diet rich in nutrients can help support healthy hair growth.

Step by step guide to treating hair loss caused by depression

If you're already experiencing hair loss due to depression, there are several steps you can take to treat it:

  1. Start by managing your depression. This may involve talking to a therapist or taking medication.
  2. Make sure you're eating a healthy diet that's rich in nutrients.
  3. Consider taking a supplement that's designed to support healthy hair growth.
  4. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's designed to promote healthy hair growth.
  5. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage hair and lead to breakage.
  6. Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, as they can pull on hair and lead to breakage.

Tips for managing depression-related hair loss

If you're experiencing hair loss due to depression, here are some tips to help you manage it:

  • Be patient: It can take time for hair to grow back, so be patient and don't get discouraged.
  • Focus on self-care: Taking care of yourself can help improve your mood and reduce stress.
  • Consider a new hairstyle: You may want to consider a new hairstyle that works with your hair loss.
  • Wear a hat: Wearing a hat can help protect your scalp from the sun and cover up hair loss.

FAQs

Q: Can hair loss caused by depression be permanent?

A: In most cases, hair loss caused by depression is temporary and will grow back once the depression is managed.

Q: Can medication for depression cause hair loss?

A: Some antidepressant medications can cause hair loss as a side effect. Talk to your doctor if you're experiencing hair loss while taking medication for depression.

Hair care tips for people with depression-related hair loss

If you're experiencing hair loss due to depression, it's important to take extra care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner: Look for products that are designed to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid harsh chemical treatments: Chemical treatments, such as coloring and perming, can damage hair and lead to breakage.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, can pull on hair and lead to breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun: Wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF to protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

Depression can be a difficult condition to manage, but with the right strategies, it's possible to prevent and treat hair loss caused by depression. If you're experiencing hair loss, talk to your doctor or a dermatologist to determine the underlying cause and develop a treatment plan.
