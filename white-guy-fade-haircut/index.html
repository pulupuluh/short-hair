White Guy Fade Haircut A Guide For 2023

Written by Oliver May 01, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're a white guy looking for a stylish and trendy haircut, you can't go wrong with the fade. The fade is a popular haircut that involves gradually tapering the hair from the top of the head down to the sides and back. It's a versatile haircut that can be customized to suit your preferences, whether you want a high, mid, or low fade. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to get a white guy fade haircut, along with tips and FAQs to help you maintain your new look.

How to Get a White Guy Fade Haircut

If you're ready to get a white guy fade haircut, here's what you need to do:

Step 1: Choose the Type of Fade You Want

Before you head to the barber, you should decide what type of fade you want. There are three main types of fades:

  • High fade: The hair is faded high up on the head, just above the temples.
  • Mid fade: The hair is faded halfway up the head, around the ear level.
  • Low fade: The hair is faded low down on the head, just above the neckline.

Think about what will suit your face shape and personal style, and discuss your options with your barber.

Step 2: Choose the Length of Your Hair

The length of your hair will depend on the type of fade you want. For a high fade, you'll need to keep the hair on top of your head longer, while for a low fade, you'll need to keep it shorter. Your barber can help you choose the right length for your hair.

Step 3: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before your haircut, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier for your barber to work with.

Step 4: Cut the Hair on Top of Your Head

Your barber will start by cutting the hair on top of your head to the desired length. They may use scissors or clippers, depending on your hair type and the look you're going for.

Step 5: Start Fading the Sides and Back

Your barber will then start fading the hair on the sides and back of your head, using clippers and different guard sizes to create a gradual taper. They may also use scissors to blend the hair and create a seamless look.

Step 6: Clean up the Edges

Finally, your barber will clean up the edges around your hairline and neckline, using clippers or a razor to create a sharp, polished finish.

Tips for Maintaining Your White Guy Fade Haircut

Once you've got your white guy fade haircut, you'll want to keep it looking sharp. Here are some tips to help you maintain your new look:

  • Visit the barber regularly to keep your fade looking fresh.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and clean.
  • Style your hair with a matte pomade or wax for a natural, textured look.
  • Use a comb or brush to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

FAQs About White Guy Fade Haircut

Q: How long does a white guy fade haircut last?

A: A white guy fade haircut can last anywhere from 2-4 weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade grows out.

Q: Can I get a white guy fade haircut if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can still get a white guy fade haircut if you have curly hair. Your barber will need to use different techniques to create a seamless fade, but it's definitely possible!

Q: How do I choose the right barber for my white guy fade haircut?

A: Look for a barber who has experience with fades and has a good reputation. Ask for recommendations from friends or check online reviews to find a barber in your area.

Hair Care for White Guy Fade Haircut

Proper hair care is important to keep your white guy fade haircut looking sharp. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and strong:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a good quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.

With these tips and tricks, you can rock a stylish and trendy white guy fade haircut in 2023 and beyond. Whether you prefer a high, mid, or low fade, this versatile haircut is sure to turn heads and make a statement.
