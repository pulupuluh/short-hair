How To Get Rid Of Seborrheic Dermatitis On The Scalp Naturally

Written by Daniel Jul 31, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you are suffering from seborrheic dermatitis, you are not alone. This common skin condition affects millions of people worldwide. It is characterized by red, itchy, and scaly patches on the scalp, face, ears, and chest. While there are several treatment options available, many people prefer natural remedies. In this article, we will discuss how to get rid of seborrheic dermatitis on the scalp naturally.

  • How to Get Rid of Seborrheic Dermatitis on the Scalp Naturally

Description

Seborrheic dermatitis is a common skin condition that affects the scalp, face, ears, and chest. It is caused by an overgrowth of yeast on the skin. This overgrowth can be triggered by several factors, including stress, hormonal changes, and certain medications. Seborrheic dermatitis can be uncomfortable and embarrassing, but it is not contagious.

How to Get Rid of Seborrheic Dermatitis on the Scalp Naturally

There are several natural remedies that can help relieve the symptoms of seborrheic dermatitis on the scalp. These include:

1. Apple Cider Vinegar

Apple cider vinegar has antifungal and antibacterial properties that can help kill the yeast responsible for seborrheic dermatitis. To use, mix equal parts apple cider vinegar and water and apply to the scalp with a cotton ball. Leave on for 15-20 minutes, then rinse with cool water. Repeat twice a week for best results.

2. Tea Tree Oil

Tea tree oil has antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce the symptoms of seborrheic dermatitis. To use, mix a few drops of tea tree oil with a carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil, and apply to the scalp. Leave on for 30 minutes, then rinse with cool water. Repeat twice a week for best results.

3. Aloe Vera

Aloe vera has anti-inflammatory properties that can help soothe the scalp and reduce itching. To use, apply aloe vera gel directly to the scalp and leave on for 30 minutes. Rinse with cool water and repeat twice a week for best results.

4. Coconut Oil

Coconut oil has antifungal and anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce the symptoms of seborrheic dermatitis. To use, warm up the coconut oil and apply it to the scalp. Leave on for 30 minutes, then rinse with cool water. Repeat twice a week for best results.

5. Baking Soda

Baking soda has antifungal properties that can help kill the yeast responsible for seborrheic dermatitis. To use, mix baking soda with water to form a paste and apply to the scalp. Leave on for 10-15 minutes, then rinse with cool water. Repeat once a week for best results.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide on how to use natural remedies to get rid of seborrheic dermatitis on the scalp:

  1. Choose a natural remedy, such as apple cider vinegar, tea tree oil, aloe vera, coconut oil, or baking soda.
  2. Prepare the remedy as directed.
  3. Apply the remedy to the scalp, focusing on the affected areas.
  4. Leave on for the recommended time, then rinse with cool water.
  5. Repeat the treatment twice a week for best results.

Tips to Prevent Seborrheic Dermatitis on the Scalp

While seborrheic dermatitis cannot be completely prevented, there are several things you can do to reduce your risk of developing it:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Avoid using harsh hair products, such as hairspray and gel.
  • Avoid wearing tight-fitting hats and clothing.
  • Avoid stress as much as possible.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet.

FAQs

1. Is seborrheic dermatitis contagious?

No, seborrheic dermatitis is not contagious.

2. Can seborrheic dermatitis be cured?

While seborrheic dermatitis cannot be cured, it can be managed with treatment.

3. How long does it take to see results with natural remedies?

Results may vary, but most people see an improvement in their symptoms within a few weeks of starting treatment.

Hair Care Tips for Seborrheic Dermatitis

In addition to using natural remedies, there are several things you can do to care for your hair when you have seborrheic dermatitis:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Avoid using hot water on your scalp.
  • Avoid using harsh hair products, such as hairspray and gel.
  • Avoid scratching your scalp.
  • Wear a hat or scarf in cold weather to protect your scalp.

In conclusion, seborrheic dermatitis can be uncomfortable and embarrassing, but there are several natural remedies that can help relieve the symptoms. By following the tips and guidelines outlined in this article, you can get rid of seborrheic dermatitis on the scalp naturally and prevent it from recurring in the future.
