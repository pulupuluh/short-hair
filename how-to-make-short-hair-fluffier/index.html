How To Make Short Hair Fluffier Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Apr 27, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Short hair is often associated with sleek and smooth styles, but it's also possible to achieve a fluffier, more voluminous look. Whether you have naturally thin hair or just want to add some extra oomph to your short cut, there are several techniques you can try. In this article, we'll cover how to make short hair fluffier, step-by-step. Here's what we'll cover:

Table of Contents

  • Hair Care for Fluffy Short Hair

How to Make Short Hair Fluffier

Before we dive into the specifics, it's worth noting that there's no one-size-fits-all approach to making short hair fluffier. Your hair type, texture, and length will all play a role in determining which techniques work best for you. That said, here are some general tips:

  • Use the right products: Look for volumizing shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that are specifically designed for fine or thin hair.
  • Blow dry upside down: Flipping your head over and blow drying your hair upside down can help add volume at the roots.
  • Tease your hair: Teasing, also known as backcombing, involves gently combing your hair backwards towards the scalp. This creates a temporary bump of volume that can be smoothed over with the top layer of hair.
  • Use hot tools: Curling irons, straighteners, and hot rollers can all be used to add volume and texture to short hair.
  • Experiment with parting: Changing the way you part your hair can make a big difference in how it looks. Try a deep side part or a zig-zag part for more volume.

Now that you have an idea of some general techniques, let's get into the step-by-step process.

Step-by-Step Guide to Fluffy Short Hair

Here's a basic process you can follow to make your short hair fluffier:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots.
  3. Flip your head over and blow dry your hair upside down until it's about 80% dry.
  4. Flip your head back over and use a round brush to finish drying your hair. Use the brush to lift your hair at the roots and create volume.
  5. Once your hair is completely dry, use a curling iron or straightener to add texture and volume. Take small sections of hair and curl or straighten them in different directions to create a tousled, fluffy look.
  6. If desired, tease the hair at the crown of your head to add even more volume.
  7. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your style in place.

Of course, you can modify this process based on your hair type and the specific look you're going for. The key is to experiment and find what works best for you.

Tips and Tricks for Fluffy Short Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the fluffiest, most voluminous short hair possible:

  • Don't be afraid to use a lot of product. Volumizing products, in particular, can be applied liberally without weighing down your hair.
  • Use your fingers to tousle your hair and create more texture.
  • Try a dry shampoo to add volume and texture on days when you don't wash your hair.
  • Consider getting layers or a shag cut to add more volume and movement to your hair.

FAQ: Common Questions About Fluffy Short Hair

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference, but most people with short hair can get away with washing every 2-3 days. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can make it harder to achieve a fluffy, voluminous look.

What if my hair is naturally curly or wavy?

You can still achieve a fluffy look with curly or wavy hair! Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your natural texture, and use a curling iron or straightener to add more definition where needed.

Can I still wear my hair in a sleek, smooth style?

Yes! The great thing about short hair is that it's versatile. You can easily switch between a sleek, smooth look and a fluffy, voluminous look depending on your mood and the occasion.

Hair Care for Fluffy Short Hair

To keep your short hair looking its best, here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't weigh down your hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair. If you do use hot tools, use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

With these tips and tricks, you should be able to achieve a fluffy, voluminous look with your short hair. Don't be afraid to experiment and have fun with your style!
