How Do I Know What Texture My Natural Hair Is

Written by Luffy Aug 02, 2023 · 6 min read

When it comes to taking care of your natural hair, one of the most important things you need to know is your hair texture. Understanding your hair texture can help you determine what products to use, how to style your hair, and what kind of treatments your hair needs. In this article, we’ll take you through some simple steps to help you determine what texture your natural hair is.

Table of Content

  • Step 1: Look at your hair
  • Step 2: Feel your hair
  • Step 3: Check your hair’s elasticity
  • Step 4: Consider your hair’s porosity
  • Tips for Taking Care of Your Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Different Textures

Step 1: Look at your hair

The first thing you need to do is look at your hair. Take a close look at your hair and see if it is straight, wavy, curly, or coily. This will give you a good idea of what texture your hair is.

Step 2: Feel your hair

Next, you need to feel your hair. Is it soft and silky, or is it rough and dry? Does it feel thin or thick? These are all things you need to consider when determining your hair texture.

Step 3: Check your hair’s elasticity

Elasticity refers to how well your hair can stretch and return to its original shape. To test your hair’s elasticity, take a strand of hair and gently pull it. If your hair stretches and returns to its natural shape without breaking, then it has good elasticity.

Step 4: Consider your hair’s porosity

Porosity refers to how well your hair absorbs and retains moisture. To determine your hair’s porosity, take a strand of hair and place it in a bowl of water. If your hair sinks to the bottom, it has high porosity. If it floats on top, it has low porosity.

Tips for Taking Care of Your Hair

Now that you know your hair texture, it’s important to take care of your hair properly. Here are some tips for taking care of your hair:

  • Use products that are specifically designed for your hair texture
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Deep condition your hair regularly
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can my hair texture change over time?

A: Yes, your hair texture can change over time due to hormonal changes, aging, and other factors.

Q: Is it important to know my hair texture?

A: Yes, knowing your hair texture can help you take care of your hair properly and achieve the best results.

Hair Care for Different Textures

Here are some general hair care tips for different hair textures:

  • Straight hair: Use a light shampoo and conditioner, and avoid heavy products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Wavy hair: Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and scrunch your hair with a towel or diffuser to enhance your natural waves.
  • Curly hair: Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls hydrated and defined.
  • Coily hair: Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and apply a hair oil or butter to seal in moisture and prevent breakage.

By following these tips and understanding your hair texture, you can keep your natural hair healthy, strong, and beautiful!
