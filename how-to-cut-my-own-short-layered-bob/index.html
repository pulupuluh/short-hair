How To Cut My Own Short Layered Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Mar 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle and want to take matters into your own hands, then a short layered bob is a great option for you. Not only is it stylish and easy to maintain, but it can also add volume and texture to your hair. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own short layered bob.

Description

A short layered bob is a hairstyle that is cut shorter in the back and longer in the front. It's a versatile haircut that can be styled in various ways and is perfect for those who want a low-maintenance yet fashionable look.

How to Cut my Own Short Layered Bob: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather your tools

Before you start, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Wash and dry your hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before you start cutting. This will help you to see your natural hair texture and make it easier to cut.

Step 3: Section off your hair

Using your comb, section off your hair into four parts: the top, left, right, and back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 4: Start cutting the back

Start with the back section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut it to the desired length. Follow this process until you've cut all the hair in the back section.

Step 5: Move on to the sides

Next, move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut it at an angle, starting from the back and moving towards the front. Follow this process until you've cut all the hair in the side sections.

Step 6: Cut the top section

Now it's time to cut the top section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut it to the desired length. Follow this process until you've cut all the hair in the top section.

Step 7: Blend the layers

Once you've cut all the sections, it's time to blend the layers. Take a comb and run it through your hair to blend the layers together. This will create a seamless and natural look.

Step 8: Trim any uneven ends

After blending the layers, check your hair for any uneven ends. Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven ends.

Step 9: Style your hair

Now that you've cut your hair, it's time to style it. You can add volume and texture to your hair by using a curling iron, straightening iron, or hair products.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much off at once
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Check your hair for any uneven ends and trim them with scissors
  • Don't cut too much off at once, as it can be difficult to fix

FAQ

Can I cut my own short layered bob?

Yes, you can cut your own short layered bob. However, it's important to have the right tools and follow the steps carefully.

How often should I cut my short layered bob?

It's recommended to cut your short layered bob every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and length.

What hair type is best for a short layered bob?

A short layered bob works well for all hair types, including straight, wavy, curly, and thick hair.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to ensure it stays healthy and shiny. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Trim your hair regularly to maintain its shape and length, and use hair masks and oils to keep it moisturized.
