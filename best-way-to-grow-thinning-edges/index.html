The Best Way To Grow Thinning Edges A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Jun 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you struggling with thinning edges? You are not alone. Thinning edges is a common problem among women of all ages and races. Thinning edges can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, stress, hormonal changes, and over-styling. But don't worry, with the right hair care routine and some patience, you can grow your edges back. In this article, we will cover the best way to grow thinning edges, step by step. Let's get started!

Description

Thinning edges are the result of hair loss around the hairline. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including tight hairstyles, chemical treatments, and hormonal changes. Thinning edges can be a frustrating and embarrassing problem, but there are ways to grow them back.

How to Grow Thinning Edges

Growing thinning edges is a process that requires patience and consistency. You need to implement a hair care routine that promotes healthy hair growth and protects your edges from further damage. Here are the steps you need to follow:

Step 1: Identify the Cause of Your Thinning Edges

The first step in growing thinning edges is to identify the cause of the problem. If your thinning edges are caused by tight hairstyles, you need to avoid them. If your thinning edges are caused by chemical treatments, you need to stop using them. If your thinning edges are caused by hormonal changes, you need to talk to your doctor to find a solution that works for you.

Step 2: Use Gentle Products

When you have thinning edges, you need to be gentle with your hair. Use gentle shampoos and conditioners that are free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals. Avoid using heat styling tools, and if you must use them, use a heat protectant spray. Be gentle when detangling your hair, and use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush.

Step 3: Moisturize Your Hair

Moisture is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure you are moisturizing your hair regularly. Use a leave-in conditioner or a hair oil to keep your hair moisturized. You can also use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to give your hair an extra boost of moisture.

Step 4: Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help stimulate hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp in circular motions. This will increase blood flow to your scalp, which will promote hair growth. You can do this for a few minutes every day.

Step 5: Protect Your Edges

Protecting your edges is essential when you have thinning edges. Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your edges, such as braids and weaves. Use a silk or satin scarf or bonnet when you sleep to protect your hair from friction. You can also use edge control products to smooth down your edges and protect them from further damage.

Tips for Growing Thinning Edges

Here are some additional tips for growing thinning edges:

  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Be patient. It takes time to grow hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I use hair growth products to grow my thinning edges?

A: Yes, there are many hair growth products on the market that can help stimulate hair growth. Look for products that contain natural ingredients like biotin, castor oil, and peppermint oil.

Q: How long does it take to grow thinning edges?

A: It can take several months to a year to grow thinning edges, depending on the severity of the problem. Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.

Q: Can stress cause thinning edges?

A: Yes, stress can cause thinning edges. When you are stressed, your body produces cortisol, which can affect hair growth.

Hair Care for Healthy Edges

Healthy hair starts with a healthy scalp. Take care of your scalp and edges by following a hair care routine that promotes healthy hair growth. Be gentle with your hair, avoid harsh chemicals, and protect your edges from further damage. With patience and consistency, you can grow your thinning edges back.
