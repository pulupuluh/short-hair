How To Know What You Will Look Like With A Fringe

Written by Bardi Jun 25, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you thinking about getting a fringe but not sure if it will suit you? It's a common dilemma. A fringe can be a great way to change up your hairstyle without a drastic cut, but it's important to make sure it will flatter your face shape and features.

Description

A fringe, also known as bangs, is a hair style where a section of hair is cut to fall over the forehead. Fringes can be cut in different lengths and styles, from blunt to side-swept, and can be a great way to frame the face and highlight features.

How To

There are a few ways to get an idea of how you will look with a fringe before committing to the cut.

1. Use Virtual Try-On Tools

Many hair salons and websites offer virtual try-on tools where you can upload a photo of yourself and "try on" different hairstyles and fringes. This can give you a good idea of how you will look with a fringe and help you decide on the style and length that suits you best.

2. Try A Clip-In Fringe

If you're not quite ready to commit to a fringe cut, you can try a clip-in fringe extension. This is a temporary solution that allows you to experiment with different styles and lengths without the commitment of a cut. Clip-in fringes can be found at hair supply stores or online.

3. Look For Inspiration

Take a look at celebrities or people with similar face shapes and features to you who have a fringe. This can give you a good idea of how a fringe can enhance your features and flatter your face shape.

Step By Step

If you've decided to take the plunge and get a fringe cut, here are some steps to follow:

1. Choose The Right Style

Before getting your fringe cut, decide on the style and length that suits you best. Consider your face shape, hair texture and lifestyle when choosing a fringe style.

2. Consult With Your Hair Stylist

Consult with your hair stylist about the best fringe style for you. They can help you decide on the length and shape of your fringe based on your face shape and hair type.

3. Prepare For The Cut

Wash your hair and dry it as you normally would before your fringe cut. This will give your stylist a better idea of how your hair falls naturally.

4. Cut The Fringe

Your stylist will section off the hair for your fringe and cut it to the desired length and shape. They may use scissors or a razor to create a soft or blunt edge depending on the style you've chosen.

5. Style The Fringe

Once your fringe is cut, your stylist will style it to your liking. They may blow-dry it straight or add some texture with a curling iron or flat iron.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting a fringe:

1. Consider Your Face Shape

Choose a fringe style that flatters your face shape. For example, if you have a round face, a side-swept fringe can help elongate your face.

2. Maintain Your Fringe

A fringe requires regular maintenance to keep it looking its best. Trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it at the right length and shape.

3. Experiment With Styling

Try different styling techniques to switch up the look of your fringe. You can sweep it to the side, pin it back, or add some texture with a styling product.

FAQ

1. Will A Fringe Suit Me?

A fringe can suit anyone, but it's important to choose a style that flatters your face shape and features. Consult with your hair stylist to determine the best fringe style for you.

2. Can I Wear A Fringe With Curly Hair?

Absolutely! A fringe can look great with curly hair. Just make sure to choose a style that works with your natural texture.

3. Can I Cut My Own Fringe?

We recommend getting your fringe cut by a professional hair stylist. A DIY fringe cut can be risky and lead to uneven or unflattering results.

Hair Care

Keep your fringe looking its best by following these hair care tips:

1. Shampoo And Condition Regularly

Keep your hair clean and healthy by shampooing and conditioning regularly. Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and a hydrating conditioner to keep your hair soft and nourished.

2. Protect Your Hair From Heat

Avoid using too much heat on your hair to prevent damage. Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools, and try air-drying your hair whenever possible.

3. Use A Styling Product

Use a styling product like a texturizing spray or a smoothing serum to keep your fringe in place and add some volume or texture.
