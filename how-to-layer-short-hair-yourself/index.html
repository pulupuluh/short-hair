How To Layer Short Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 24, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of your short hair looking flat and lifeless? Adding layers can give it the volume and texture it needs. But going to the salon can be expensive and time-consuming. Why not learn how to layer short hair yourself? With a few tools and some patience, you can give yourself a fresh new look without leaving your home.

How to:

1. Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties

2. Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see where the layers should go. Use a shampoo and conditioner that works for your hair type, and towel dry your hair gently.

3. Divide Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use clips or hair ties to keep the sections separated.

4. Cut the First Layer

Start with the bottom layer. Take a small section of hair from the back and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors vertically and make small snips at an angle, working your way up the section. This will create a subtle, tapered effect.

5. Blend the Layers

Once you have cut the first layer, release the next section of hair and use it as a guide to cut the next layer. This will help you blend the layers seamlessly. Repeat this process for the other sections of hair.

6. Check Your Work

Once you have finished cutting, check your work in a mirror. Make any necessary adjustments, but be careful not to overdo it. You can always go back and cut more later, but you can't undo a bad haircut.

7. Style Your Hair

Now that you have layered your hair, it's time to style it. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you. You can try blow drying your hair with a round brush to add volume, or use a curling iron to create loose waves.

Tips:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up. It's easier to add more layers than to take them away.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can stretch and bounce back, making it difficult to achieve the desired length.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing through the process can result in a sloppy haircut.

FAQ:

Can I layer my hair if it's curly?

Absolutely! Layering can add definition and shape to curly hair. Just make sure to cut each section when it's dry, as curly hair can shrink up to 50% when it dries.

Can I layer my hair if it's thin?

Yes, layering can actually make thin hair look fuller and thicker. Just be careful not to overdo it, as too many layers can make thin hair look stringy.

Hair Care:

Now that you have layered your hair, it's important to take care of it. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. You can also try using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Learning how to layer short hair yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these simple steps and tips, you can give yourself a fresh new look without breaking the bank. So grab your scissors and get ready to transform your hair!
