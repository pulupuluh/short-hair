What To Do With Straight Asian Hair Tips How To s And More

Written by Daniel Jun 28, 2023 · 7 min read

Having straight Asian hair can be both a blessing and a curse. On the one hand, it’s low-maintenance and easy to style. On the other hand, it can be difficult to add volume and texture to it. If you’re wondering what to do with your straight Asian hair, you’ve come to the right place. In this article, we’ll share some tips, how-to’s, and other helpful information to help you make the most of your locks.

Table of Contents

  • Hair Care
  • How-To
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Hair Care

Before we get into specific styles and techniques, it’s important to talk about hair care. Taking care of your hair is the foundation for any good hairstyle. Here are some tips for keeping your straight Asian hair healthy:

- Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that’s formulated for your hair type. Look for products that are free of sulfates, parabens, and other harsh chemicals.

- Don’t wash your hair every day. Washing too often can strip your scalp of natural oils and leave your hair dry and brittle. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

- Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle your hair when it’s wet. Wet hair is more fragile than dry hair, so be gentle.

- Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to the ends of your hair to keep them moisturized.

- Limit heat styling. Heat can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. If you do use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature on the lower side.

How-To

Now that you know how to take care of your hair, let’s talk about some specific styles and techniques. Here are a few ideas:

Sleek and Straight

If you’re looking for a classic, polished look, you can’t go wrong with sleek and straight hair. To achieve this look, start by blow-drying your hair with a round brush to smooth out any kinks or bumps. Then, use a flat iron to straighten your hair in small sections. Finish with a shine spray or serum to add some extra gloss.

Low Ponytail

A low ponytail is a simple yet chic style that’s perfect for any occasion. To create this look, start by smoothing your hair with a brush or comb. Then, gather your hair at the nape of your neck and secure it with a hair tie. Use a small section of hair to wrap around the hair tie and secure it with a bobby pin. For extra polish, apply a bit of hairspray to tame any flyaways.

Beachy Waves

If you’re craving some texture and volume, beachy waves are a great option. To create this look, start by applying a texturizing spray to your hair. Then, use a curling iron to create loose waves in random sections of your hair. Finish with a bit of hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips for styling straight Asian hair:

- Experiment with different hair accessories, such as headbands, clips, and barrettes. These can add some extra interest to your hairstyle.

- Try different parts. A simple change in your part can completely transform your look.

- If you’re looking for extra volume, try backcombing your hair at the crown. Just be sure not to overdo it, as this can cause damage to your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about styling straight Asian hair:

Q: How can I add volume to my straight Asian hair?

A: Try blow-drying your hair upside down or using a volumizing mousse or spray.

Q: What’s the best way to style my hair for a special occasion?

A: It depends on the occasion, but a low bun or half-up half-down style are both elegant options.

Q: How can I prevent my hair from getting oily?

A: Try using a dry shampoo in between washes and avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day.

With these tips, how-to’s, and other helpful information, you should have plenty of ideas for what to do with your straight Asian hair. Remember to take good care of your hair and have fun experimenting with different styles!
