What Do Barbers Hate A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 03, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Barbers are the unsung heroes of the hair industry, keeping people looking sharp and fresh day in and day out. However, there are certain things that barbers just can't stand. In this article, we're going to explore the top things that barbers hate, so you can avoid them next time you're in the chair.

Table of Contents

  1. Not showing up on time
  2. Being too picky
  3. Not being honest about your hair
  4. Bringing in a photo of a celebrity
  5. Not washing your hair
  6. Using your phone during the appointment
  7. Moving your head too much
  8. Not tipping your barber
  9. Talking too much
  10. Being disrespectful

Not showing up on time

One of the biggest things that barbers hate is when clients don't show up on time. Barbers have schedules to keep, and if you're late, it can throw off their whole day. Make sure you're on time for your appointment, or at least give your barber a heads up if you're running behind.

Being too picky

While it's important to communicate what you want with your barber, being too picky can be a huge annoyance. Trust your barber's expertise and let them do their job. If you're not happy with the result, politely communicate your concerns and work with your barber to find a solution.

Not being honest about your hair

Barbers need to know the truth about your hair in order to give you the best cut possible. If you have any specific concerns or issues, like a sensitive scalp or thinning hair, make sure you're honest with your barber. They're there to help you, so don't be shy.

Bringing in a photo of a celebrity

While it's okay to show your barber a photo of a style you like, bringing in a photo of a celebrity and expecting to look exactly like them is a big no-no. Everyone's hair is different, and what looks good on one person may not work for you. Trust your barber to create a look that's tailored specifically to you.

Not washing your hair

Barbers hate it when clients come in with dirty hair. Not only is it unhygienic, but it can also make it harder for your barber to work with your hair. Make sure you wash your hair before your appointment to make the process smoother for both you and your barber.

Using your phone during the appointment

Using your phone during your appointment is not only rude, but it can also be dangerous. Moving your head around while looking at your phone can make it difficult for your barber to do their job properly. Plus, it's always nice to disconnect from technology for a little while and enjoy the experience.

Moving your head too much

It's important to stay still during your appointment so your barber can create a clean, precise cut. Moving your head around can make it difficult for your barber to work with your hair and can result in a less-than-perfect cut. If you need to shift your position, communicate with your barber first.

Not tipping your barber

Tipping your barber is a way to show appreciation for their hard work and dedication. Not tipping your barber can be seen as disrespectful and can make it less likely for them to go above and beyond for you in the future. A good rule of thumb is to tip 20% of the cost of your haircut.

Talking too much

While it's nice to have a conversation with your barber, talking too much can be distracting and make it difficult for your barber to concentrate on your hair. Try to keep the conversation light and easy, and don't be afraid to let your barber know if you need some quiet time to relax.

Being disrespectful

Barbers are professionals who deserve respect just like any other service provider. Being disrespectful, rude, or demanding can make the experience unpleasant for both you and your barber. Treat your barber with kindness and courtesy, and you'll likely receive the same treatment in return.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you know what barbers hate, here are some tips to help you take care of your hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid over-styling your hair with heat tools, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Invest in a good quality brush or comb to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about what barbers hate:

What should I do if I'm running late for my appointment?

Try to give your barber as much notice as possible that you'll be late. If it's only a few minutes, they may be able to still fit you in. If you're going to be significantly late, it's best to reschedule your appointment.

How can I communicate what I want without being too picky?

Be clear about what you're looking for, but also trust your barber's expertise. If you're not sure what will work for you, ask for their advice.

Is it okay to bring in a photo of a style I like?

Yes, it's okay to bring in a photo of a style you like, but don't expect to look exactly like the person in the photo. Your barber will work with you to create a look that's tailored to your hair and face shape.

How much should I tip my barber?

A good rule of thumb is to tip 20% of the cost of your haircut, but you can adjust this based on the quality of service you received.

What should I do if I'm not happy with my haircut?

Communicate your concerns to your barber in a polite and respectful manner. They'll likely be happy to work with you to find a solution.

Now that you know what barbers hate, you can make your next trip to the barber a pleasant and enjoyable experience for both you and your barber. Remember to communicate clearly, be respectful, and take good care of your hair.
