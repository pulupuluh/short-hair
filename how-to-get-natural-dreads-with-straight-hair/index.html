How To Get Natural Dreads With Straight Hair

Written by William Jun 14, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your straight hair and want to switch to natural dreadlocks? You're not alone! Many people with straight hair dream of having beautiful and natural-looking dreadlocks. However, the process of getting natural dreads with straight hair can be challenging, and it requires patience and dedication. In this article, we'll guide you through the steps on how to get natural dreads with straight hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Natural Dreads with Straight Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Getting Natural Dreads with Straight Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Natural Dreads with Straight Hair

How to Get Natural Dreads with Straight Hair

The first step in getting natural dreads with straight hair is to stop using any hair products that contain chemicals. This includes shampoos, conditioners, and styling products. Chemicals in hair products can make your hair slippery and difficult to lock.

You'll also need to stop brushing or combing your hair. This may be difficult for people with straight hair, as their hair tends to be more manageable and tangle-free. However, it's essential to let your hair tangle naturally to form dreadlocks.

Next, you'll need to wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo. This will help remove any build-up from previous hair products and prepare your hair for the locking process.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Let Your Hair Grow

The first step in getting natural dreads with straight hair is to let your hair grow. Your hair needs to be at least 6 inches long to begin the locking process. It's also essential to keep your hair healthy, so make sure you're eating a balanced diet and taking care of your hair with natural oils and vitamins.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Once your hair has grown to the desired length, divide it into sections. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreadlocks you want. Use hair bands or rubber bands to secure each section of hair.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Now, it's time to start the locking process. Take a section of hair and hold it at the root. Use a comb to backcomb the hair towards your scalp. Repeat this process until the hair begins to matte and tangle. Continue this process with each section of hair until all sections are backcombed.

Step 4: Palm Roll Your Hair

After backcombing your hair, it's time to palm roll each section. Roll each section of hair between your palms in a circular motion. This will help smooth out any bumps and encourage the hair to lock together.

Step 5: Wait

Once your hair is backcombed and palm-rolled, it's time to wait. Dreads take time to form, and it can take up to several months for them to mature fully. During this time, avoid using any hair products and continue to palm roll your hair regularly.

Tips for Getting Natural Dreads with Straight Hair

Getting natural dreads with straight hair can be challenging, but these tips can help:

  • Be patient: Dreads take time to form, so be prepared to wait several months before your hair locks together.
  • Don't use any hair products: Chemicals in hair products can make your hair slippery and difficult to lock.
  • Palm roll your hair regularly: This helps smooth out any bumps and encourage the hair to lock together.
  • Wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo: This will help remove any build-up from previous hair products and prepare your hair for the locking process.

FAQs

Can you get natural dreads with straight hair?

Yes, you can get natural dreads with straight hair, but it requires patience and dedication. You'll need to stop using any hair products that contain chemicals and let your hair tangle naturally to form dreadlocks.

How long does it take to get natural dreads with straight hair?

It can take several months for natural dreadlocks to form with straight hair. However, the time it takes will depend on the thickness and length of your hair and how often you palm roll your hair.

Hair Care for Natural Dreads with Straight Hair

Once your natural dreads have formed, it's essential to take care of them to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo to keep your scalp clean.
  • Use natural oils to moisturize your hair and scalp.
  • Avoid using any hair products that contain chemicals.
  • Palm roll your hair regularly to keep your dreads looking neat and tidy.

Getting natural dreads with straight hair can be a challenging process, but with patience and dedication, you can achieve the look you've always wanted. Follow these steps and tips, and you'll be on your way to having beautiful and natural-looking dreadlocks.
