How To Cut A Bob Haircut For Curly Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Mar 19, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have curly hair and want to try out a bob haircut, you might be wondering how to get the perfect cut. Cutting curly hair can be tricky, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a beautiful and stylish bob haircut. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps of cutting a bob haircut for curly hair.

Description

A bob haircut is a timeless and versatile hairstyle that can be customized to suit any face shape, hair texture, and personal style. A bob haircut for curly hair is a great option for those who want to show off their natural curls while keeping their hair manageable and easy to style. This haircut can be customized to suit different lengths, from chin-length bobs to shoulder-length bobs.

How to Cut a Bob Haircut for Curly Hair

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure that it is clean, dry, and detangled. Comb through your curls with a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to remove any knots and tangles. You can also apply a leave-in conditioner or a curl-enhancing product to help define your curls and make them more manageable.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start by sectioning off the top layer of your hair and securing it with a clip. Then, divide the rest of your hair into sections, depending on how thick your hair is and how many layers you want to create. The more sections you create, the more layers your haircut will have.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair to your desired length, keeping in mind that curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries. If you want a shorter bob, cut your hair a bit longer than your desired length. If you want a longer bob, cut your hair a bit shorter than your desired length.

Work your way around your head, cutting each section of hair to the same length. Make sure that you are using a sharp pair of scissors and cutting at a slight angle, which will help create a natural-looking bob.

Step 4: Create Layers

If you want to add layers to your bob haircut, take small sections of hair and cut them at different lengths. Start by cutting the top layer of your hair, then work your way down to the bottom layer, cutting each section at a slightly different length. This will help create a layered and textured look.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

Once you have finished cutting your hair, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls. This will help define your curls and give your haircut a more polished look. You can also use a curl-enhancing product to help define your curls and add shine.

Tips for Cutting a Bob Haircut for Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a bob haircut for curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and jagged cuts
  • Cut your hair when it is dry, as curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries
  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more hair if you want a shorter bob
  • Consult a professional hairstylist if you are unsure about cutting your hair yourself

FAQ

Can I cut my curly hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair yourself, but it is important to use the right technique and tools. Make sure that you are using sharp scissors and cutting your hair when it is dry to avoid any surprises when your curls shrink up.

How often should I cut my curly bob?

The frequency of your haircuts will depend on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain it. As a general rule, you should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy, shiny, and defined. Here are some tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls without causing frizz
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or a curl-enhancing product to help define your curls and reduce frizz

With these tips and techniques, you can cut a beautiful and stylish bob haircut for your curly hair. Remember to be patient, take your time, and consult a professional hairstylist if you are unsure about cutting your hair yourself. With the right care and maintenance, your curly bob will look gorgeous and healthy.
