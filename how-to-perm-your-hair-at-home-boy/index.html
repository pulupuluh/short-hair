How To Perm Your Hair At Home Boy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Feb 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're a boy and looking to add some curls or waves to your hair, perming is a great option. Perming your hair at home may seem intimidating, but with the right tools and steps, you can achieve the desired results. In this article, we will guide you on how to perm your hair at home boy in relaxed English language. We'll cover everything from how to prepare your hair and scalp, the perm process, and hair care tips to maintain your new look.

How to Prepare Your Hair and Scalp

Before perming your hair, it's important to prepare your hair and scalp. Here are some things to keep in mind:

1. Shampoo Your Hair

Shampoo your hair a day before perming. This will help remove any dirt, oil or product buildup on your hair and scalp. Do not use a conditioner as it can interfere with the perm process.

2. Protect Your Skin

Apply petroleum jelly or a barrier cream to your ears, neck, and hairline to protect your skin from the perm solution.

The Perm Process

Now that your hair and scalp are ready, it's time to start the perming process. Here's how to perm your hair at home boy:

1. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make the perming process easier and ensure that all parts of your hair are covered.

2. Apply the Perm Solution

Put on gloves and mix the perm solution according to the instructions on the kit. Apply the perm solution to each section of your hair, starting from the roots and working your way down to the ends. Make sure to saturate each section evenly.

3. Cover with Plastic Wrap

Cover your hair with plastic wrap to keep the perm solution in place and prevent it from dripping onto your skin or clothes. Leave the plastic wrap on for the recommended time on the kit instructions.

4. Rinse Your Hair

After the recommended time has passed, remove the plastic wrap and rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water. Do not shampoo your hair at this point.

5. Apply Neutralizer

Apply the neutralizer provided in the kit to your hair. This will help lock in the curls and set them. Leave the neutralizer on for the recommended time on the kit instructions.

6. Rinse and Dry Your Hair

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water and gently blot it dry with a towel. Do not rub your hair vigorously as this can damage the curls. Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair gently.

Tips for Maintaining Your New Look

Now that you have permed your hair, here are some tips to maintain your new look:

1. Avoid Washing Your Hair for 48 Hours

Wait at least 48 hours after perming your hair before washing it. This will give your curls time to set and last longer.

2. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner specially formulated for permed hair. This will help keep your curls soft and hydrated.

3. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons or curling irons as they can damage your curls. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

4. Get Regular Trims

Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. This will also help maintain the shape of your curls.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Can I perm my hair if it's already colored or highlighted?

Yes, you can perm colored or highlighted hair, but it's best to wait at least two weeks after coloring or highlighting before perming.

2. How often can I perm my hair?

You should wait at least six to eight weeks before perming your hair again to prevent damage and breakage.

3. Can I perm my hair if it's already damaged or chemically treated?

It's best to avoid perming your hair if it's already damaged or chemically treated as it can cause further damage. Consult with a professional stylist before perming.

4. Can I perm my hair if I have dandruff or a sensitive scalp?

It's best to avoid perming your hair if you have dandruff or a sensitive scalp. Consult with a professional stylist before perming.

Hair Care is Key

Perming your hair at home boy can be a fun and exciting way to change up your hairstyle. However, it's important to take proper care of your hair to maintain the health and vibrancy of your curls. Follow the tips and steps outlined in this article to achieve the best results.
