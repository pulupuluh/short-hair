How To Get Messy Looking Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Feb 21, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of your hair looking too perfect? Do you want to achieve that effortlessly chic messy hair look? Look no further! In this article, we’ll show you how to get messy looking hair in a few easy steps. So, let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Start with the Right Haircut
  • Step 2: Use the Right Products
  • Step 3: Embrace the Messiness
  • Tips for Maintaining Messy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Messy Hair

Step 1: Start with the Right Haircut

The first step to achieving messy hair is to start with the right haircut. A layered cut or a shaggy cut with choppy ends will help create texture and movement in your hair. Talk to your hairstylist about the type of messy look you want to achieve, and they can suggest the right cut for you.

Related Keywords:

messy hair, haircut, layered cut, shaggy cut, choppy ends, texture, movement, hairstylist

Step 2: Use the Right Products

Using the right products is key to achieving messy hair. Start with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add texture and body to your hair. Then, apply a texturizing spray or a sea salt spray to create that messy, beachy look. You can also use a dry shampoo to add more texture and grip to your hair.

Related Keywords:

products, volumizing shampoo, conditioner, texture, body, texturizing spray, sea salt spray, beachy look, dry shampoo, grip

Step 3: Embrace the Messiness

The key to messy hair is to embrace the messiness. Don’t try to make your hair look too perfect or polished. Instead, use your fingers to tousle your hair and create that messy, undone look. You can also try scrunching your hair with your hands or using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural waves.

Related Keywords:

messiness, perfect, polished, fingers, tousle, undone look, scrunching, diffuser attachment, natural waves

Tips for Maintaining Messy Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining your messy hair:

  • Don’t wash your hair too often. Messy hair looks better when it’s a little dirty.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Keep some hair ties and bobby pins on hand for when you want to pull your hair back into a messy bun or ponytail.

Related Keywords:

maintaining, messy hair, wash, dirty, wide-tooth comb, detangle, brush, product, greasy, hair ties, bobby pins, messy bun, ponytail

FAQs

1. Can I achieve messy hair with short hair?

Yes, you can achieve messy hair with short hair. Talk to your hairstylist about the best cut for your hair length and texture.

2. Can I use a curling iron to create messy waves?

Yes, you can use a curling iron to create messy waves. Just be sure to use a larger barrel size and don’t curl your hair too perfectly.

3. Can I achieve messy hair without using any products?

It’s possible to achieve messy hair without using any products, but using a texturizing spray or sea salt spray will help enhance the messy look.

Related Keywords:

FAQs, short hair, hairstylist, cut, texture, curling iron, waves, barrel size, products, texturizing spray, sea salt spray

Hair Care for Messy Hair

To keep your messy hair looking healthy and vibrant, it’s important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage and dry out your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a hair protectant spray.

Related Keywords:

hair care, messy hair, healthy, vibrant, sulfate-free shampoo, conditioner, natural oils, deep condition, hydrated, nourished, heat, damage, dry out, sun, chlorine, hat, hair protectant spray

There you have it! Follow these simple steps, tips, and hair care advice to achieve that perfect messy hair look. Remember to embrace the messiness and have fun with it!
