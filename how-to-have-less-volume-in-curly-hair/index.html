How To Have Less Volume In Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it provides plenty of texture and movement, it can also be prone to frizz and excessive volume. If you're looking for ways to tame your curly locks and achieve a more streamlined look, read on for our top tips and tricks.

How to Have Less Volume in Curly Hair

The key to achieving less volume in curly hair is to focus on reducing bulk and controlling frizz. Here are some simple steps you can take to achieve a more streamlined look:

1. Cut Your Hair

If you have particularly thick or unruly hair, consider getting a haircut. Regular trims can help to remove excess bulk and create a more manageable shape. Opt for a layered cut, which can add movement and definition to your curls without adding too much volume.

2. Use the Right Products

Choosing the right hair products is crucial for achieving less volume in curly hair. Look for lightweight, non-greasy formulas that won't weigh your curls down. Avoid heavy styling creams or gels, which can make your hair look oily and clumpy.

3. Blow Dry with a Diffuser

When blow-drying your hair, use a diffuser attachment to help reduce frizz and create a more defined curl pattern. Use a low heat setting and aim the dryer nozzle downwards to avoid lifting the hair at the roots.

4. Style with Care

When styling your hair, use a light touch and avoid pulling or tugging on your curls. Use your fingers to gently scrunch and shape your hair, then let it air dry or use a diffuser attachment to dry it more quickly.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to achieving less volume in curly hair:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or ingredients that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a lightweight leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends and mid-lengths. This will help to moisturize your curls and reduce frizz.

Step 3: Use a Curl Cream or Gel

Apply a small amount of curl cream or gel to your hair, using your fingers to distribute it evenly. This will help to define your curls and reduce frizz.

Step 4: Blow Dry with a Diffuser

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends, using a low heat setting and aiming the nozzle downwards.

Step 5: Style as Desired

Once your hair is dry, style it as desired. Use your fingers to gently scrunch and shape your curls, then let them air dry or use a diffuser attachment to dry them more quickly.

Tips for Maintaining Less Volume

To maintain less volume in your curly hair, follow these tips:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair
  • Avoid brushing your hair, which can cause frizz and breakage
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce frizz
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, which can cause frizz and disrupt your curl pattern
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners, which can damage your hair and cause more volume

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with curly hair prefer to wash their hair every day, while others wash it once or twice a week. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Q: Can I use hairspray to tame my curly hair?

A: While hairspray can be helpful for holding your style in place, it can also make your hair look stiff and crunchy. Instead, try using a lightweight hair oil or serum to add shine and control frizz.

Q: How can I reduce frizz in my curly hair?

A: To reduce frizz, try using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, using a leave-in conditioner, and avoiding hot tools like straighteners. You can also try using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer and sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase.
