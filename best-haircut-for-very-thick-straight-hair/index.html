Types of Haircuts for Very Thick Straight Hair

When it comes to haircuts for very thick straight hair, there are a few different options to consider:

  • Layered cuts: Layering can help thin out very thick hair and add movement and texture.
  • Blunt cuts: A blunt cut can work well for thick, straight hair that's on the shorter side.
  • Bobs: Bobs can be a great choice for thick, straight hair, especially if you opt for a layered or angled bob.
  • Long haircuts: If you have very thick hair, you may be able to pull off a longer style. Just be sure to add some layers or texturizing to prevent it from looking too heavy.

How to Choose the Best Haircut for Your Face Shape

When choosing a haircut, it's important to consider your face shape. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Oval: Lucky you! Oval faces can pull off just about any haircut.
  • Square: Soften your features with a layered, shoulder-length cut or a side-swept bang.
  • Round: Go for a longer cut with layers near the face to create angles.
  • Heart-shaped: Try a chin-length bob or a layered cut that hits at the collarbone.

10 Best Haircuts for Very Thick Straight Hair

Here are some of our favorite haircuts for very thick straight hair:

The Long Layered Cut

This classic cut is perfect for very thick hair. It adds movement and texture without sacrificing length. Plus, it's versatile enough to be worn straight or curled.

The Blunt Bob

A blunt bob can work well for thick, straight hair that's on the shorter side. It's a chic, modern look that's easy to style.

The Pixie Cut

If you're feeling daring, a pixie cut can be a great choice for thick, straight hair. Just be sure to add some texture or layers to prevent it from looking too heavy.

The Layered Lob

A long bob with layers is a great option for thick, straight hair. It's sophisticated and on-trend, and the layers add movement and dimension.

The Shag

A shag haircut is perfect for very thick hair. It's a bit edgy and rock-and-roll, and the layers add texture and movement.

The Angled Bob

An angled bob is a great way to add interest to thick, straight hair. The longer pieces in front draw attention to the face, and the layers add texture and movement.

The Side-Swept Bang

A side-swept bang can work well for thick, straight hair. It softens the face and adds interest without requiring a major cut.

The Layered Pixie

If you're not ready to go full-on pixie, try a layered version. It's a bit softer and more feminine, and the layers add texture and movement.

The Textured Lob

A lob with lots of texture is a great choice for very thick hair. It's stylish and modern, and the layers add movement and dimension.

The Curtain Bang

Curtain bangs are having a moment right now, and they can work well for thick, straight hair. They're versatile and easy to style, and they add interest without requiring a major cut.

Tips for Styling and Maintaining Very Thick Straight Hair

Here are some tips and tricks for keeping your very thick straight hair looking great:

  • Invest in a good haircut: A great haircut can make all the difference when it comes to styling very thick hair. Look for a stylist who has experience working with thick hair.
  • Use the right products: Choose products that are designed for thick hair, like a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Use a lightweight styling product to add texture and hold.
  • Avoid over-washing: Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and damage. Aim to wash every 2-3 days, or as needed.
  • Protect your hair: Heat styling can damage very thick hair, so be sure to use a heat protectant before blow-drying or using a curling iron. Try to air-dry your hair as much as possible.

FAQ: Everything You Need to Know About Very Thick Straight Hair

Q: Can I get a short haircut with very thick straight hair?

A: Absolutely! Just be sure to work with a stylist who has experience working with thick hair. A good stylist can create a customized cut that flatters your face shape and works with your hair's texture.

Q: How can I prevent my very thick straight hair from looking too heavy?

A: Layers and texture are your friends! Work with your stylist to create a cut that adds movement and dimension to your hair. You can also try adding some waves or curls with a curling iron or hot rollers.

Q: What's the best way to style very thick straight hair?

A: This depends on your personal preference and the cut you choose. Some people prefer to wear their hair straight and sleek, while others like to add some texture and volume. Experiment with different styling products and techniques to find what works best for you.

Hair Care for Very Thick Straight Hair

Here are some tips for keeping your very thick straight hair healthy and looking great:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb: A wide-toothed comb is gentler on very thick hair than a brush, which can cause breakage.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Tight ponytails, braids, and buns can put stress on very thick hair and cause breakage.
  • Deep condition regularly: Very thick hair can be prone to dryness and damage, so be sure to use a deep conditioner once a week to keep it healthy and hydrated.
  • Trim regularly: Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and fresh.

With the right cut and care, very thick straight hair can be a beautiful and versatile asset. Experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you, and don't be afraid to work with a stylist to create a customized look that flatters your face shape and suits your lifestyle. With a little TLC, your thick straight locks will be the envy of everyone around you!
