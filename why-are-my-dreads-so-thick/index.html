Why Are My Dreads So Thick Understanding The Causes And Solutions

Written by Luffy Apr 17, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are a symbol of cultural identity, spiritual beliefs, and personal expression. However, some people may find that their dreads are thicker than they expected or desired. This can be due to a variety of reasons, including genetics, hair care practices, and environmental factors. In this article, we will explore the causes of thick dreads and provide some tips on how to care for them.

Description: What are Thick Dreads?

Thick dreads are locks that have a larger diameter than average. They can occur naturally due to genetic factors or as a result of hair care practices. Thick dreads can be challenging to manage and maintain, as they require more product and time to dry. They can also be heavy and uncomfortable, especially for those who have a sensitive scalp.

How to: Achieving Thick Dreads

Some people may intentionally choose to have thick dreads, as they prefer the look and feel of larger locks. Achieving thick dreads can be done by using specific techniques during the locking process, such as backcombing or twisting. However, it is essential to note that thick dreads can put more strain on the scalp and hair follicles, which can lead to hair loss or damage over time.

Step by Step: How to Care for Thick Dreads

If you have naturally thick dreads or have chosen to make them thicker, it is crucial to take care of them properly. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash Your Dreads Regularly

Washing your dreads regularly can help remove any buildup of dirt, oils, and product residue. Use a gentle shampoo that is specifically designed for dreadlocks, and avoid using any products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates.

Step 2: Dry Your Dreads Properly

Drying your dreads thoroughly after washing is crucial to prevent mold or mildew from forming. Use a clean towel to squeeze out any excess water, and then use a hairdryer on a low setting to dry each dread individually.

Step 3: Maintain Your Scalp Health

Keeping your scalp healthy is essential for promoting healthy hair growth and preventing scalp issues such as dandruff or itchiness. Use a scalp oil or moisturizer to keep your scalp hydrated, and avoid scratching or picking at your scalp.

Tips: How to Manage Thick Dreads

Here are some additional tips on how to manage thick dreads:

Tip 1: Use a Lightweight Product

Using a lightweight product, such as a spray or serum, can help keep your dreads moisturized without weighing them down. Avoid using heavy oils or waxes, as they can make your dreads greasy and difficult to manage.

Tip 2: Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help prevent friction and tangling, which can lead to breakage or damage. It can also help keep your dreads in place and prevent them from unraveling.

Tip 3: Be Gentle When Styling

Avoid pulling or twisting your dreads too tightly when styling, as this can cause tension and breakage. Use a soft-bristled brush or comb to gently detangle your dreads, and avoid using any heat styling tools.

FAQ: Common Questions About Thick Dreads

Q: Can I Thin Out My Thick Dreads?

A: Yes, you can thin out your thick dreads by using a pair of scissors or clippers. However, it is essential to do this carefully to avoid cutting off too much hair or causing any damage.

Q: Will My Dreads get Thinner Over Time?

A: It is possible for dreads to get thinner over time, especially if you use products or techniques that promote shrinkage. However, this can also depend on your hair type and how well you care for your dreads.

Q: How Often Should I Retwist My Dreads?

A: This can vary depending on your hair type and how fast your hair grows. Generally, it is recommended to retwist your dreads every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and prevent them from unraveling.

Hair Care: Why Proper Care is Important for Thick Dreads

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining the health and appearance of your thick dreads. Neglecting your hair can lead to issues such as dryness, breakage, and hair loss. By following the steps and tips outlined in this article, you can keep your thick dreads looking and feeling great.

Conclusion: Embracing Your Thick Dreads

Thick dreads can be challenging to manage, but they can also be a beautiful and unique expression of your personal style. Whether you have naturally thick dreads or have chosen to make them thicker, it is essential to take care of them properly to maintain their health and appearance. By following the tips and steps outlined in this article, you can embrace your thick dreads and enjoy all the benefits that come with this iconic hairstyle.
