How To Layer Bangs And Face Frame A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Layering your bangs and face frame can add depth, texture, and movement to your hair. It's a great way to update your look without sacrificing length. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of layering your bangs and face frame, along with some tips and tricks to ensure a perfect result.

Table of Contents

  • How to Layer Bangs and Face Frame
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Layer Bangs and Face Frame

Layering your bangs and face frame is a simple process that can be done at home with a few basic tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties

Before you begin, make sure your hair is clean and dry. It's also a good idea to have a photo of the style you want to achieve for reference.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Separate your bangs from the rest of your hair and clip them out of the way. Then, divide your hair into two sections - one on either side of your head. Clip the sections up to keep them out of the way.

Step 2: Determine Your Length

Decide on the length you want your bangs and face frame to be. Use your comb to comb your hair straight down, and then hold the hair you want to cut between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut straight across. Remember to start with a little length and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.

Step 3: Create Layers

Once you've determined the length of your bangs and face frame, it's time to create layers. Take a small section of hair from one side of your face and hold it between your fingers. Cut into the section at a slight angle to create a soft, layered effect. Repeat on the other side of your face.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've created the layers on either side of your face, it's time to blend them. Take a small section of hair from one side and pull it over to the other side. Cut into the section at the same angle as before, to create a seamless blend between the two sections. Repeat on the other side of your face.

Step 5: Add Finishing Touches

Once you've blended the layers, it's time to add some finishing touches. Use your scissors to soften any harsh lines or edges, and to add texture and movement to your hair. You can also use your comb to tease your hair, or to create a side-swept effect.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take your time and be patient. Layering your hair can be a time-consuming process, but the results are worth it.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're not confident in your ability to layer your hair, ask a friend or a professional stylist for assistance.

FAQs

How often should I layer my bangs and face frame?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how quickly you want to update your look. Generally, it's recommended to layer your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

Can I layer my hair if it's curly?

Absolutely! Layering can add depth and texture to curly hair. Just be sure to cut your hair when it's dry, to ensure that you're cutting the curls at the right length.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've layered your bangs and face frame, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair, or use a heat protectant spray if you must.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your layers.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Layering your bangs and face frame is a great way to update your look and add some movement and texture to your hair. With a little patience and practice, you can achieve a salon-worthy style from the comfort of your own home.
