Can Prp Stop Hair Fall Everything You Need To Know

Written by Frank Aug 28, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you experiencing hair fall? You're not alone. Hair fall is a common problem that affects people of all ages and genders. Fortunately, there are various treatments available to address this issue, including PRP or Platelet Rich Plasma therapy. In this article, we'll discuss if PRP can stop hair fall and give you all the information you need to know about this treatment.

What is PRP?

PRP or Platelet Rich Plasma is a treatment that uses your blood's plasma to stimulate hair growth. It involves drawing your blood and spinning it in a centrifuge to separate the plasma from the rest of the blood components. The plasma is then injected into the scalp to promote hair growth.

How Does PRP Work?

PRP works by activating and stimulating hair follicles. The plasma contains growth factors that promote cell growth and regeneration. When injected into the scalp, PRP increases blood flow to the hair follicles, providing more oxygen and nutrients to the hair roots. This, in turn, strengthens the hair and promotes growth.

Can PRP Stop Hair Fall?

PRP can help reduce hair fall and promote hair growth. The growth factors in the plasma can help revive dormant hair follicles and stimulate new hair growth. However, PRP is not a magical cure that can stop hair fall overnight. It takes time and several sessions to see visible results. PRP is best used as a preventive measure to stop hair fall before it becomes severe.

How Many Sessions of PRP are Required?

The number of PRP sessions required depends on the severity of hair fall and the individual's response to the treatment. Typically, four to six sessions are required, spaced four to six weeks apart. Maintenance sessions are required every six to twelve months to maintain the results.

Is PRP Safe?

PRP is a safe treatment with minimal risks. Since the plasma is obtained from your blood, there is no risk of an allergic reaction or disease transmission. However, as with any medical procedure, there is a risk of infection, bruising, and swelling. It is essential to choose a qualified and experienced practitioner to perform the treatment.

What are the Benefits of PRP?

There are several benefits of PRP for hair fall, including:

  • Non-surgical solution
  • Minimal downtime
  • Safe and natural treatment
  • Improved hair density and thickness
  • Reduced hair fall

How to Prepare for PRP Treatment?

Before the PRP treatment, you must avoid blood-thinning medications, including aspirin, for at least one week. You must also avoid alcohol and smoking for a few days before the treatment. On the day of the treatment, make sure to wash your hair thoroughly and avoid using any hair products.

What to Expect During the PRP Treatment?

The PRP treatment takes about an hour to complete. First, your blood is drawn, and the plasma is separated from the rest of the blood components. The scalp is then cleaned and numbed with a local anesthetic. The plasma is injected into the scalp using a syringe and a microneedle. The treatment is generally painless, but you may feel some discomfort or mild pain.

How to Take Care of Your Hair After PRP Treatment?

After the PRP treatment, you must avoid washing your hair for at least 24 hours. You must also avoid swimming or sweating excessively for a few days. It is essential to follow your doctor's instructions and use any prescribed hair care products.

Conclusion

PRP can help reduce hair fall and promote hair growth. However, it is not a magical cure that can stop hair fall overnight. It takes time and several sessions to see visible results. PRP is best used as a preventive measure to stop hair fall before it becomes severe. If you're considering PRP treatment, make sure to choose a qualified and experienced practitioner to perform the treatment.
