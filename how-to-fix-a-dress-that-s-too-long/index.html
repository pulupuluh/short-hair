How To Fix A Dress That s Too Long Tutorial For 2023

Written by Eveline Apr 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Welcome to our tutorial on how to fix a dress that's too long! We understand how frustrating it can be to find the perfect dress, only to realize that it's too long for your liking. But don't worry, with a few simple steps, you can easily hem your dress to your desired length. In this blog post, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to fix a dress that's too long, along with some tips and frequently asked questions. Let's get started!

How to Fix a Dress That's Too Long

If you're wondering how to shorten a dress that's too long, the good news is that it's a simple process that you can do at home. Here are the steps that you need to follow:

Step 1: Measure the Length

The first step is to measure the length of the dress. Put on the dress and stand in front of a mirror. Decide how much shorter you want the dress to be and measure the length from the bottom of the dress to where you want it to end. Mark the spot with a pin or chalk.

Step 2: Prepare the Dress

Take off the dress and turn it inside out. Use a seam ripper to take out the stitches at the hemline. Make sure that you remove all the stitches so that the fabric is loose and easy to work with.

Step 3: Cut the Fabric

Using sharp scissors, cut the fabric along the marked line. Make sure that you cut straight and even. If the dress is made of a lightweight fabric, consider using pinking shears to prevent fraying.

Step 4: Hem the Dress

Now it's time to hem the dress. Fold the fabric up to the desired length and pin it in place. Use a sewing machine or needle and thread to sew a straight stitch all the way around the hemline. Make sure that the stitches are small and even.

Step 5: Finish the Hem

Once you have finished sewing, turn the dress right side out and try it on. If you're happy with the length, trim any excess fabric and use a serger or zigzag stitch to finish the raw edges of the fabric.

Tips for Hemming a Dress

Here are some tips to keep in mind when hemming a dress:

  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • Use sharp scissors and a good quality sewing machine or needle and thread.
  • If you're hemming a dress made of a lightweight fabric, consider using a stabilizer to prevent puckering.
  • Practice on a scrap piece of fabric before you start hemming your dress.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I hem my dress without a sewing machine?

A: Yes, you can hem your dress by hand using a needle and thread. It may take longer, but it's a good option if you don't have a sewing machine.

Q: How much should I shorten my dress?

A: It's up to you! Decide how short you want your dress to be and measure accordingly. Keep in mind that it's always better to hem a dress a little longer than too short.

Q: Can I use fabric glue to hem my dress?

A: While fabric glue can be used to hem a dress, it's not the most durable option. We recommend using a sewing machine or needle and thread for a more secure hem.

Hair Care Tip

When hemming a dress, it's important to keep your hair out of the way. Tie your hair back or wear a headband to prevent it from getting caught in the sewing machine or needle and thread.

Conclusion

Now that you know how to fix a dress that's too long, you can easily hem your dress to your desired length. Remember to measure carefully, cut straight, and sew evenly. With a little patience and practice, you'll have a perfectly hemmed dress that you'll be proud to wear. Happy sewing!
