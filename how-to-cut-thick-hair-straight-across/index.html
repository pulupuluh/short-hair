How To Cut Thick Hair Straight Across A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 19, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have thick hair, you know how challenging it can be to cut it straight across. But with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-quality cut at home. In this article, we’ll show you how to cut thick hair straight across, so you can achieve a sleek, polished look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Thick Hair Straight Across
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Thick Hair
  • FAQs

Description

Cutting thick hair straight across can be a daunting task, especially if you’re not a professional hairdresser. However, with the right technique, you can achieve a sleek, polished look that rivals any salon cut.

In this article, we’ll walk you through the steps to cut thick hair straight across, including the tools you’ll need, the best techniques to use, and tips for achieving a flawless finish. Whether you’re looking to save money on salon visits or simply want to try your hand at cutting your own hair, this guide has everything you need to know.

How to Cut Thick Hair Straight Across

The key to cutting thick hair straight across is to use sharp, high-quality scissors and to work in small sections. It’s also important to make sure your hair is clean and dry before you begin.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut thick hair straight across:

  1. Start by parting your hair down the middle and combing it straight down on both sides.
  2. Take a small section of hair from one side and comb it straight down. Use your fingers to hold the hair at the desired length, then snip off the excess with sharp scissors. Repeat on the other side.
  3. Work your way up the head, taking small sections of hair and cutting them to the desired length. Use the previously cut sections as a guide to ensure your cuts are even.
  4. When you reach the top of the head, comb the hair straight back and cut any remaining sections to the desired length.
  5. Use a mirror to check the back of your head and make any necessary adjustments.
  6. Once you’re happy with the length, use thinning shears to soften the ends and create a more natural look. This will also help to remove any bulk and make your hair easier to style.
  7. Finish by trimming any stray hairs and styling your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Thick Hair

Cutting thick hair can be challenging, but these tips will help you achieve a salon-quality cut:

  • Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you begin cutting. Wet hair can be difficult to cut evenly, and it can also shrink as it dries, resulting in a shorter cut than intended.
  • Invest in high-quality scissors. Dull or low-quality scissors can damage your hair and make it more difficult to achieve a clean, even cut.
  • Work in small sections, especially if you have very thick hair. This will help you achieve even, precise cuts and prevent any unevenness or mistakes.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you go. This will help you spot any unevenness or areas that need more attention.

FAQs

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting thick hair:

Can I cut my thick hair straight across myself?

Yes, with the right tools and techniques, you can cut your own thick hair straight across. However, if you’re not confident in your skills, it’s always best to seek professional help.

What type of scissors should I use to cut thick hair?

It’s important to use sharp, high-quality scissors when cutting thick hair. Look for scissors with a serrated edge or teeth, as these can help grip the hair and prevent it from slipping as you cut.

How often should I cut my thick hair?

How often you should cut your thick hair depends on your individual hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it’s best to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its health and prevent split ends.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

Thick hair requires special care to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips to help you care for your thick locks:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and prevent dryness.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too frequently, as these can damage your hair and make it more difficult to style.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Apply a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair and keep it looking healthy.

By following these tips and using the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a flawless, salon-quality cut for your thick hair. With a little practice and patience, you’ll be able to master the art of cutting your own hair and enjoy a polished, professional look without ever leaving your home.
