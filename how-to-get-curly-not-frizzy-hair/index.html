Introduction

Do you want to achieve those beautiful, bouncy curls without the frizz? We know it can be a struggle, especially when you have naturally straight or wavy hair. But fear not, we've got you covered with some tips and tricks on how to get curly not frizzy hair. Whether you're using heat tools, styling products or natural methods, we'll guide you through it step by step.

How To Get Curly Not Frizzy Hair

The key to getting curly not frizzy hair is to use the right products and techniques. Here are some methods you can try:

1. Heat Styling

If you don't have naturally curly hair, you can use heat tools to achieve those curls. Start by blow drying your hair until it's about 80% dry. Then, use a curling iron or wand to curl small sections of your hair. Make sure to use a heat protectant spray before styling to prevent damage.

2. Styling Products

If you have naturally curly hair, you can enhance your curls with styling products. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair such as curl defining creams, gels, and mousses. Apply these products to damp hair and scrunch your hair upwards to encourage the curls.

3. Natural Methods

If you want to avoid heat styling and chemicals, you can try these natural methods:

  • Braid your hair before going to bed and unravel it in the morning for natural waves.
  • Twist small sections of your hair and leave them overnight for tighter curls.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls without disrupting them.

Step By Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide on how to get curly not frizzy hair:

1. Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to prevent frizz. Rinse your hair with cool water to seal the cuticle and prevent frizz.

2. Apply Products

If you're using styling products, apply them to damp hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

3. Dry Your Hair

If you're heat styling, blow dry your hair until it's about 80% dry. Then, use a curling iron or wand to curl small sections of your hair. If you're air drying, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer or let your hair air dry naturally.

4. Finish With Hairspray

Once you've achieved your desired curls, finish with a light hold hairspray to keep them in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you get curly not frizzy hair:

  • Use a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel to prevent frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry as it can disrupt your curls and cause frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends which can cause frizz.

FAQs

1. How often should I wash my curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and texture. If you have dry or coarse hair, you can wash it once or twice a week. If you have fine or oily hair, you may need to wash it more frequently.

2. How can I prevent frizz in humid weather?

Humidity can cause your hair to frizz. To prevent this, apply a leave-in conditioner or anti-frizz serum to your hair before going outside. You can also tie your hair up in a bun or ponytail to keep it under control.

3. Can I use a straightener to curl my hair?

Yes, you can use a straightener to curl your hair. Simply twist small sections of your hair around the straightener and pull downwards.

Hair Care Routine

Here's a basic hair care routine for curly hair:

1. Wash Your Hair

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to prevent frizz. Rinse your hair with cool water to seal the cuticle and prevent frizz.

2. Apply Products

Apply a leave-in conditioner, curl defining cream or serum to damp hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

3. Dry Your Hair

If you're heat styling, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer or let your hair air dry naturally. If you're air drying, avoid touching your hair until it's completely dry to prevent frizz.

4. Style Your Hair

Use a curling iron or wand to enhance your curls or leave them natural. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep them in place.

With these tips, tricks and hair care routine, you'll be able to get curly not frizzy hair in no time. Remember to be patient and experiment with different methods to find what works best for you.
