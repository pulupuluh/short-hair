Which Small Dogs Are Easy To Train A Guide For New Dog Owners In 2023

Written by William Jul 19, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're a new dog owner, you may be wondering which small dogs are easy to train. After all, training a dog can be challenging, especially if you're not sure where to start. In this guide, we'll take a look at some of the easiest small dog breeds to train, as well as tips for successful training.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Train Small Dogs
  3. Step by Step Guide to Training Small Dogs
  4. Tips for Successful Training
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care for Small Dogs

Description

When it comes to training small dogs, some breeds are easier to train than others. Generally speaking, small dogs are known for being stubborn and difficult to train. However, there are exceptions to this rule. Some small dog breeds are highly intelligent and eager to please, which makes them much easier to train.

When choosing a small dog breed, it's important to consider your lifestyle and what you're looking for in a pet. Some small dogs require a lot of exercise and attention, while others are content to lounge around the house all day. By choosing a breed that fits your lifestyle, you'll be more likely to have a successful training experience.

How to Train Small Dogs

Training a small dog requires patience, consistency, and positive reinforcement. Here are some tips for successful training:

  • Start training early: The earlier you start training your dog, the easier it will be to establish good habits.
  • Be consistent: Use the same commands and rewards every time you train your dog.
  • Use positive reinforcement: Reward your dog with treats, praise, and attention when they do something right.
  • Keep training sessions short: Small dogs have short attention spans, so keep training sessions to 10-15 minutes at a time.
  • Be patient: Training a dog takes time, so don't get frustrated if your dog doesn't learn quickly.

Step by Step Guide to Training Small Dogs

Here is a step-by-step guide to training your small dog:

  1. Teach basic commands: Start by teaching your dog basic commands like "sit," "stay," and "come."
  2. Train on a leash: Once your dog knows basic commands, start training them on a leash. This will help you control your dog and prevent them from running away.
  3. Train in a distraction-free environment: When first starting out, train your dog in a quiet, distraction-free environment. This will help them focus on the training.
  4. Gradually increase distractions: Once your dog is comfortable training in a quiet environment, gradually increase the distractions. This will help them learn to focus even when there are distractions around.
  5. Practice regularly: Consistency is key when it comes to training. Practice with your dog every day to establish good habits.

Tips for Successful Training

Here are some additional tips for successful training:

  • Be patient and consistent: Training a dog takes time and consistency. Don't get frustrated if your dog doesn't learn quickly.
  • Use positive reinforcement: Reward your dog with treats, praise, and attention when they do something right.
  • Make training fun: Training should be a fun, positive experience for both you and your dog.
  • Take breaks: If your dog is getting frustrated or tired, take a break and come back to training later.
  • Work on one command at a time: Don't overwhelm your dog by trying to teach them too many commands at once.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about training small dogs:

What are the easiest small dog breeds to train?

Some of the easiest small dog breeds to train include:

  • Poodle
  • Papillon
  • Shetland Sheepdog
  • Cavalier King Charles Spaniel
  • Border Terrier

How long does it take to train a small dog?

Training a small dog can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months, depending on the dog's temperament and the owner's consistency with training.

What if my small dog is stubborn and doesn't want to train?

If your dog is stubborn, it's important to be patient and consistent with training. You may also want to consider working with a professional dog trainer.

Hair Care for Small Dogs

While training is important for small dogs, it's also important to take care of their hair. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Brush your dog's hair regularly to prevent tangles and matting.
  • Bathe your dog as needed, but be careful not to over-bathe, as this can dry out their skin.
  • Trim your dog's nails regularly to prevent them from getting too long.
  • Take your dog to the groomer for regular haircuts.

Conclusion

Training a small dog can be challenging, but with patience, consistency, and positive reinforcement, it's possible to establish good habits. By choosing a breed that fits your lifestyle and following the tips in this guide, you'll be on your way to a successful training experience.
