How To Cut Bangs On Thin Curly Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Christine May 11, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Cutting bangs on thin curly hair can be a daunting task, but with the right approach and technique, it can be done with ease. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to cut bangs on thin curly hair, and provide you with some tips and tricks to ensure that you get the best results possible.

Description

Bangs are a popular hairstyle that can give you a fresh and trendy look. However, cutting bangs on thin curly hair can be challenging because curly hair tends to shrink up when cut, making it difficult to get the length and shape you desire. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut bangs on thin curly hair in a way that works with your hair's natural texture and thickness.

How to

Before you start cutting your bangs, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a sharp pair of haircutting scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. Here are the steps to follow to cut your bangs on thin curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

The first step is to wash and condition your hair as you normally would. This will help to remove any buildup and make your hair easier to work with.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Once your hair is clean and dry, use your comb to section off the hair that you want to cut into bangs. You can do this by creating a triangle shape that starts at the crown of your head and extends to your hairline.

Step 3: Cut Your Bangs

To cut your bangs, start by holding the hair between your index and middle fingers and cutting straight across with your scissors. It's important to cut slowly and carefully, making small cuts at a time, so that you don't cut too much hair at once.

Step 4: Check the Length

Once you've cut your bangs, check the length to make sure that they're even. If they're not, you can make small adjustments by cutting a little bit more off until you achieve the desired length.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

After you've cut your bangs, you can style them in a way that suits your face shape and personal style. You can blow dry them straight, use a curling iron to add some curls, or let them air dry for a natural, tousled look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when cutting bangs on thin curly hair:

  • Cut your bangs when your hair is dry, as this will give you a better idea of how they will look once styled.
  • Use a spray bottle filled with water to dampen your hair as you cut, as this will help to prevent frizz and make your hair easier to work with.
  • Cut your bangs slightly longer than you think you want them, as curly hair has a tendency to shrink up when cut.
  • Use point cutting to create texture and movement in your bangs, rather than cutting straight across.
  • Consider getting your bangs cut by a professional stylist if you're not confident in your ability to do it yourself.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting bangs on thin curly hair:

Can you cut bangs on thin curly hair?

Yes, you can cut bangs on thin curly hair, but it's important to follow the right technique to avoid cutting too much hair and creating an uneven or choppy look.

How do you cut bangs on thin curly hair without making them too short?

To avoid cutting your bangs too short, start by cutting a little bit at a time and checking the length as you go. It's also a good idea to cut your bangs slightly longer than you want them to be, as curly hair has a tendency to shrink up when cut.

How often do you need to trim your bangs on thin curly hair?

How often you need to trim your bangs will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to lose their shape. As a general rule, you should plan to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and styled.

Hair Care

To keep your thin curly hair looking its best, it's important to follow a regular hair care routine that includes washing, conditioning, and styling with the right products. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your hair to help prevent frizz and add moisture.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair without causing frizz or damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can dry out your hair and cause damage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful and stylish set of bangs on your thin curly hair. Whether you're going for a bold and edgy look or a soft and romantic style, cutting bangs on thin curly hair can be a great way to switch up your look and keep your hair looking fresh and healthy.
