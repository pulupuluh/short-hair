How To Say Low Fade Haircut In Spanish A Relaxed English Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 09, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you planning to get a low fade haircut in a Spanish-speaking country but don't know how to explain it? Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this guide, we will teach you how to say "low fade haircut" in Spanish and provide you with some helpful tips and hair care advice.

How to Say Low Fade Haircut in Spanish

The Spanish translation of "low fade haircut" is "corte degradado bajo." This phrase is pronounced as "CORE-tay day-grah-GAH-doh BAH-hoh."

It's important to note that haircuts are often referred to differently in different Spanish-speaking countries. For example, in Mexico, a low fade might be called a "degradado" or a "desvanecido," while in Spain, it might be referred to as a "degradado bajo." If you're unsure about the terminology in the country you're in, don't be afraid to ask your hairstylist or a local for clarification.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Low Fade Haircut in Spanish

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting a low fade haircut in Spanish:

  1. Find a reputable hairstylist. Look for reviews online or ask for recommendations from locals.
  2. Book an appointment. You can either call or visit the salon in person to make an appointment.
  3. Explain the type of haircut you want. Use the phrase "corte degradado bajo" to describe the low fade haircut.
  4. Communicate your desired length. You can say "Quiero que sea corto" for short hair or "Quiero que sea largo" for long hair.
  5. Discuss the fade. You can say "Quiero un degradado bajo" for a low fade or "Quiero un degradado alto" for a high fade.
  6. Choose your preferred hairstyle. You can show a picture or describe the style you want.
  7. Sit back and relax. Let the hairstylist work their magic!
  8. Pay for your haircut. Don't forget to tip your hairstylist if you're happy with the result.

Tips for Getting a Low Fade Haircut in Spanish

Here are some tips to help you get the best low fade haircut possible:

  • Bring a picture of the hairstyle you want. This will help the hairstylist understand your preferences better.
  • Communicate clearly. Don't be afraid to ask questions or repeat yourself if necessary.
  • Be patient. Getting a good haircut takes time and effort, so don't rush the hairstylist.
  • Take care of your hair. Use a good shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

FAQ About Low Fade Haircuts in Spanish

What is a low fade haircut?

A low fade haircut is a style where the hair gradually tapers from longer on top to shorter on the sides and back.

How long does a low fade haircut last?

A low fade haircut typically lasts around two to four weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows.

How much does a low fade haircut cost?

The cost of a low fade haircut varies depending on the salon and the country you're in. In general, it can range from $10 to $50.

Hair Care Tips for Low Fade Haircuts

Here are some hair care tips to keep your low fade haircut looking fresh:

  • Wash your hair regularly with shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a comb or brush to style your hair daily.
  • Apply hair products, such as pomade or gel, to keep your hair in place.
  • Visit a hairstylist regularly for touch-ups and trims.

Now that you know how to say "low fade haircut" in Spanish and how to get one, you're ready to rock this stylish look on your next trip to a Spanish-speaking country. Follow our tips and hair care advice to keep your low fade looking sharp and stylish!
