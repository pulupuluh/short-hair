How To Curl Hair With Straightener Step By Step

Written by Oliver Jul 02, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of using a curling iron to curl your hair? Why not try curling your hair with a straightener? It may seem daunting, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve gorgeous curls. In this article, we will guide you through the steps on how to curl hair with straightener step by step.

Table of Contents

  • Materials Needed
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Curling Your Hair
  • Cooling Your Curls
  • Finishing Your Look
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

Materials Needed

Before we start, let's make sure we have the right tools.

  • Straightener
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Hairspray
  • Sectioning clips
  • Comb or brush

Prepping Your Hair

Before using any heat styling tool, it's important to protect your hair from damage. Apply a heat protectant spray evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends. Comb or brush your hair to remove any tangles.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair will make it easier to curl and ensure that you don't miss any strands. Start by separating your hair into two sections, top and bottom. Secure the top section with a sectioning clip, and divide the bottom section into two or three smaller sections.

Curling Your Hair

Now, it's time to start curling your hair. Take a small section of hair, about 1-2 inches wide, and clamp your straightener at the root. Twist your straightener away from your face, and slowly glide it down the length of your hair. The slower you go, the tighter the curl will be. Repeat this technique throughout your hair, alternating the direction of the twist for a more natural look.

Cooling Your Curls

Once you've curled all of your hair, it's important to let your curls cool down before touching them. This will help set the curls and prevent them from falling out. You can use sectioning clips to hold your curls in place while they cool down.

Finishing Your Look

After your curls have cooled down, remove the sectioning clips and gently run your fingers through your hair to break up the curls. Finish your look with a light mist of hairspray to hold the curls in place.

Tips and Tricks

  • For tighter curls, use a smaller section of hair and glide the straightener slowly down the length of your hair.
  • For looser curls, use a larger section of hair and glide the straightener quickly down the length of your hair.
  • Alternate the direction of the twist for a more natural and voluminous look.
  • Don't touch your curls until they're completely cool to prevent them from falling out.

FAQs

  • Is it safe to curl your hair with a straightener? Yes, as long as you use a heat protectant spray and don't apply too much heat to your hair.
  • Can I curl my hair with a wet straightener? No, it's important to only use a straightener on dry hair.
  • How long will my curls last? It depends on your hair type and the products you use, but typically, your curls should last all day.

Hair Care

To keep your curls looking their best, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy. Avoid using heat styling tools every day, and always use a heat protectant spray when you do. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Congratulations, you now know how to curl hair with straightener step by step. With a little practice, you'll be able to achieve beautiful curls that will last all day. Try experimenting with different techniques and products to find the perfect look for you.
