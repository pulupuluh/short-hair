Does Combing Hair Make It Less Frizzy

Written by Oliver Jul 10, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Frizzy hair can be a real pain to deal with, and many people wonder if combing their hair can help to reduce frizz. In this article, we'll explore whether or not combing your hair can help to make it less frizzy, along with some tips for managing frizz-prone hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Frizz is caused by the hair cuticle becoming rough and raised, which allows moisture to enter the hair shaft and create a frizzy look. Combing your hair can help to smooth the cuticle and distribute natural oils from your scalp throughout your hair, which can help to reduce frizz. However, it's important to use the right type of comb and technique to avoid damaging your hair and causing even more frizz.

How to

If you want to try combing your hair to reduce frizz, follow these tips:

Choose the Right Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair when it's wet, and a boar bristle brush to smooth your hair when it's dry. Avoid using plastic combs or brushes with metal bristles, as these can cause static and damage your hair.

Start at the Tips

When brushing or combing your hair, start at the tips and work your way up to the roots. This will help to avoid pulling on tangles and causing breakage.

Don't Over-Brush

Over-brushing can actually cause more frizz, so be gentle and don't brush your hair more than necessary.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to combing your hair to reduce frizz:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can lead to frizz.

Step 2: Towel-Dry Your Hair

Gently towel-dry your hair by blotting it with a microfiber towel. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel, as this can cause frizz.

Step 3: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your hair to help smooth the cuticle and prevent frizz.

Step 4: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair, starting at the tips and working your way up to the roots.

Step 5: Finish with a Boar Bristle Brush

Finish by brushing your hair with a boar bristle brush to smooth the cuticle and distribute natural oils throughout your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for managing frizz-prone hair:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can dry out your hair and cause frizz.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can transfer oils and dirt from your hands to your hair, which can cause frizz.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and cause frizz.
  • Consider getting regular haircuts to help prevent split ends, which can contribute to frizz.

FAQ

Q: Can combing your hair too much cause frizz?

A: Yes, over-brushing your hair can cause more frizz. Be gentle and don't brush your hair more than necessary.

Q: Can using a plastic comb cause frizz?

A: Yes, plastic combs can cause static and damage your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb made from a material like wood or bamboo instead.

Hair Care

Managing frizz-prone hair can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to keep your hair smooth and shiny. Remember to choose the right type of comb, start at the tips, and be gentle when brushing or combing your hair. And don't forget to avoid heat styling tools and products that contain alcohol. With a little bit of effort, you can enjoy frizz-free hair every day!
