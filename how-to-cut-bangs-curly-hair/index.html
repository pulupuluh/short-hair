How To Cut Bangs For Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Mar 14, 2023 · 9 min read

Curly hair can be a challenge, especially when it comes to cutting bangs. But with the right technique and a little bit of patience, you can achieve the perfect look. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut bangs for curly hair in a relaxed English language. We’ll cover everything from how to section your hair to tips for styling your new bangs.

  • How to Cut Bangs for Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Bangs for Curly Hair

Cutting bangs for curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. Curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you need to account for that when cutting your bangs. Here are the basic steps to follow:

Step 1: Section your hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Use a comb to create a part down the middle of your head, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, use clips to secure the hair on either side of your head.

Step 2: Decide on the length

Decide on the length of your bangs. Keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you may want to cut them longer than you think you need.

Step 3: Cut the hair

Start cutting your hair from the middle of your forehead towards the outer edges. Use scissors to cut your hair in a straight line. Make sure to cut the hair in small sections, so you can control the length and avoid cutting too much at once.

Step 4: Check the length

Check the length of your bangs. Remember that curly hair will shrink when it dries, so you may need to cut a little bit more off.

Step 5: Style your bangs

Style your bangs by using a diffuser to dry them. Use a curling iron or a flat iron to smooth out any frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you’re still not sure how to cut bangs for curly hair, follow this step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Prep your hair

Wash your hair and let it dry naturally. Don’t use any styling products yet.

Step 2: Section your hair

Use a comb to create a part down the middle of your head, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Use clips to secure the hair on either side of your head.

Step 3: Decide on the length

Step 5: Check the length

Step 6: Style your bangs

Style your bangs by using a diffuser to dry them. Use a curling iron or a flat iron to smooth out any frizz.

Tips for Cutting Bangs for Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting bangs for curly hair:

  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet. Curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you need to account for that when cutting your bangs.
  • Use a diffuser when drying your hair. This will help to enhance your curls and prevent frizz.
  • Start with small snips. You can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s cut.
  • Don’t cut your bangs too short. Curly hair tends to shrink, so your bangs may end up shorter than you intended.

FAQs:

Q: Can I cut my own bangs?

A: Yes, you can cut your own bangs. Just make sure to follow the steps above and use caution when cutting your hair.

Q: How often should I cut my bangs?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs become unruly. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a little extra TLC to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry. This can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it’s wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often. This can cause damage to your hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut bangs for curly hair. Remember to take your time and use caution when cutting your hair. And always consult with a professional if you’re unsure about anything.
