How To Find Your Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Mar 23, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Are you looking for a new haircut, but don't know where to start? Finding the right haircut can be a daunting task, but with the right approach, you can find a style that suits you perfectly. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of finding your haircut, from determining your face shape to choosing the right stylist.

Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding your haircut is to determine your face shape. Your face shape will determine which haircuts will look best on you. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back. Look at the shape of your face and compare it to the six basic face shapes.

Oval Face Shape

If your face is longer than it is wide, and your forehead is slightly wider than your chin, you have an oval face shape. Most haircuts will look great on an oval face shape, so you have a lot of options.

Round Face Shape

If your face is as wide as it is long, and your cheeks are full, you have a round face shape. Look for haircuts that add height to the top of your head and length to your face.

Square Face Shape

If your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are all about the same width, you have a square face shape. Look for haircuts that soften your angular features.

Heart Face Shape

If your forehead is wider than your cheeks and your chin is pointed, you have a heart face shape. Look for haircuts that add volume to the bottom of your head.

Diamond Face Shape

If your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, and your forehead and jawline are narrower, you have a diamond face shape. Look for haircuts that create balance between your forehead and jawline.

Oblong Face Shape

If your face is longer than it is wide, and your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are all about the same width, you have an oblong face shape. Look for haircuts that add width to your face.

Consider Your Hair Type

Once you've determined your face shape, you need to consider your hair type. Your hair type will determine which haircuts will look best on you and which ones will be easy to maintain. There are four basic hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and kinky.

Straight Hair

Straight hair is easy to manage and can be styled in a variety of ways. Look for haircuts that add volume and texture to your hair.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair is versatile and can be styled in a variety of ways. Look for haircuts that enhance your natural waves.

Curly Hair

Curly hair can be difficult to manage, but with the right haircut, it can be stunning. Look for haircuts that are layered and add volume to your hair.

Kinky Hair

Kinky hair is unique and requires a specific haircut to look its best. Look for haircuts that are layered and add volume to your hair.

Research Haircuts

Now that you know your face shape and hair type, it's time to research haircuts. Look for haircuts that flatter your face shape and enhance your hair type. You can use social media, magazines, and hairstyle websites for inspiration. Save pictures of haircuts that you like and bring them to your stylist.

Find the Right Stylist

Finding the right stylist is crucial to finding your perfect haircut. Look for a stylist who specializes in your hair type and has experience with your face shape. Ask for referrals from friends and family, or read reviews online. When you find a stylist, schedule a consultation to discuss your hair goals.

Try On Haircuts

Before committing to a haircut, try it on virtually or in person. Many hairstyle websites and apps allow you to upload a photo of yourself and try on different haircuts. You can also ask your stylist to create a mockup of the haircut before cutting your hair. This will give you an idea of how the haircut will look on you.

Ask for Advice

If you're unsure about a haircut, ask your stylist for advice. They can recommend haircuts that will flatter your face shape and enhance your hair type. They can also recommend styling products and techniques that will help you maintain your haircut.

Maintain Your Haircut

Once you've found your perfect haircut, it's important to maintain it. Follow your stylist's advice on styling products and techniques. Schedule regular haircuts to keep your hair healthy and stylish.

FAQ

How often should I get a haircut?

It depends on your hair type and style. Generally, you should get a haircut every 4-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and maintained.

What should I do if I don't like my new haircut?

Don't panic. Talk to your stylist and explain what you don't like about the haircut. They can make adjustments to fix the problem.

How can I make my haircut last longer?

Use styling products and techniques recommended by your stylist to maintain your haircut. Schedule regular haircuts to keep your hair healthy and styled.

Hair Care Tips

In addition to finding the right haircut, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's right for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair every day.
  • Use a heat protectant before using hot tools.
  • Avoid brushing wet hair, which can cause breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy.

By following these tips and finding your perfect haircut, you can have healthy, stylish hair that makes you feel confident and beautiful.
