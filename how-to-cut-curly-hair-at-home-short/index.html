How To Cut Curly Hair At Home Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 25, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how difficult it can be to find a stylist who knows how to cut it properly. And if you're looking to go shorter, the stakes are even higher. But fear not, because with the right tools, some patience, and a little bit of know-how, you can cut your own curly hair at home and achieve a chic, flattering look. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting curly hair at home short, including what tools you'll need, how to prepare your hair, and step-by-step instructions for cutting.

What You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to make sure you have the right tools on hand. Here's what you'll need:

  • A sharp pair of scissors
  • A comb
  • Hair clips
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A mirror

How to Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to prepare your hair properly. Here's what you'll need to do:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would, then let it air dry until it's about 80% dry.
  2. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  3. Part your hair where you normally would, and clip back any sections that you don't want to cut.
  4. Spray your hair with water to dampen it slightly.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that your hair is prepped and ready to go, it's time to start cutting! Follow these step-by-step instructions:

Step 1: Start with the Sides

Take a small section of hair from the front of one side of your head. Hold it between your index and middle fingers, and slide your fingers down to the length you want to cut. Use your scissors to cut straight across. Repeat on the other side.

Step 2: Move to the Back

Take a section of hair from the back of your head, and hold it between your fingers as you did with the sides. Cut straight across. Continue taking small sections and cutting until you've cut all the way across the back of your head.

Step 3: Blend the Top

Take a small section of hair from the top of your head, and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut at an angle, so that the hair is shorter in the back and longer in the front. Continue taking small sections and cutting at an angle until you've blended the top with the sides and back.

Step 4: Check Your Work

Take a look in the mirror to make sure everything is even and blended. If you notice any areas that need a little more trimming, go ahead and make those adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair at Home

Cutting curly hair can be tricky, but there are a few tips and tricks that can make the process easier:

  • Don't cut your hair when it's completely dry, as it will spring up and look shorter than you intended.
  • Cut your hair in small sections, and take your time. It's better to go slowly and get it right than to rush and end up with a choppy, uneven cut.
  • Use the point-cutting technique to add texture and remove bulk. This involves cutting into the ends of the hair at an angle, rather than straight across.
  • If you're not comfortable cutting your hair all at once, try cutting it in stages over a few days or weeks.

FAQ

Still have questions about cutting curly hair at home? Here are some frequently asked questions:

Is it really possible to cut curly hair at home?

Yes, it is! With the right tools and a little bit of practice, anyone can cut their own curly hair at home.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it starts to look unruly. As a general rule, most people with curly hair should get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Can I cut my curly hair while it's wet?

Yes, you can, but keep in mind that your hair will look shorter once it dries. If you're going for a shorter cut, it's better to cut your hair when it's dry or slightly damp.

Hair Care

Cutting your curly hair at home is just one aspect of hair care. To keep your curls looking their best, make sure you're using the right products and techniques. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to damp hair to help define your curls and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • When drying your hair, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry. Avoid rubbing your hair with a regular towel, as this can cause frizz.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to cut your curly hair at home short and keep it looking fabulous!
