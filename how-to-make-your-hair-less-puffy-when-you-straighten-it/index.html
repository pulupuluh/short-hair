How To Make Your Hair Less Puffy When You Straighten It

Written by Daniel Feb 20, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of spending hours straightening your hair only to have it end up looking puffy and frizzy? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with this issue, but the good news is that there are ways to combat it. In this article, we'll share some tips for how to make your hair less puffy when you straighten it, so you can achieve a sleek, smooth look without all the frustration.

How to

The first step to achieving less puffy hair is to start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is wet or damp when you straighten it, it's more likely to end up looking frizzy. So, make sure you blow dry your hair thoroughly before using a straightener.

Next, apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair. This will help protect your hair from damage caused by the heat of the straightener, and it will also help to smooth your hair and reduce puffiness. Be sure to apply the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends where damage is most likely to occur.

When you're ready to start straightening, section your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work through your entire head of hair, and it will also help to ensure that each section gets evenly straightened.

When you're straightening each section, be sure to use a high-quality straightener with adjustable heat settings. Lower heat settings can actually make your hair more prone to puffiness and frizz, so it's important to use a higher heat setting to get the best results.

As you straighten each section, be sure to pull the straightener all the way through your hair, rather than stopping partway through. This will help to ensure that your hair is evenly straightened, which will reduce puffiness and frizz.

Finally, once you've straightened your entire head of hair, use a light hair oil or serum to smooth down any flyaways and add shine. This will help to give your hair a polished, finished look.

Step by Step

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Before you start straightening your hair, it's important to make sure it's completely dry. Wet or damp hair is more prone to puffiness and frizz, so be sure to blow dry your hair thoroughly before moving on to the next step.

Step 2: Apply a heat protectant

Once your hair is dry, apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair. This will help to protect your hair from damage caused by the heat of the straightener, and it will also help to smooth your hair and reduce puffiness.

Step 3: Section your hair

Use clips or hair ties to section your hair into small sections. This will make it easier to work through your entire head of hair, and it will also help to ensure that each section gets evenly straightened.

Step 4: Use a high-quality straightener

Use a high-quality straightener with adjustable heat settings. Lower heat settings can actually make your hair more prone to puffiness and frizz, so it's important to use a higher heat setting to get the best results.

Step 5: Straighten each section

Straighten each section of hair, pulling the straightener all the way through your hair. Be sure to straighten each section evenly, which will reduce puffiness and frizz.

Step 6: Finish with a hair oil or serum

Once you've straightened your entire head of hair, use a light hair oil or serum to smooth down any flyaways and add shine. This will help to give your hair a polished, finished look.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve less puffy hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before straightening it.
  • Don't straighten your hair too often, as this can lead to damage and breakage.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

FAQ

How often should I straighten my hair?

You should avoid straightening your hair too often, as this can lead to damage and breakage. Aim to straighten your hair no more than once a week.

What should I do if my hair still looks puffy after straightening it?

If your hair still looks puffy after straightening it, try using a light hair oil or serum to smooth down any flyaways. You can also try using a flat iron with smaller plates, which will help to give you a more precise straightening result.

Can I straighten my hair without using a heat protectant?

It's not recommended to straighten your hair without using a heat protectant, as this can lead to damage and breakage. Heat protectants help to protect your hair from the heat of the straightener, which is essential for achieving a sleek, smooth look.

Hair Care

When it comes to keeping your hair healthy and looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. This means using high-quality products, avoiding excessive heat styling, and eating a healthy, balanced diet. By taking care of your hair from the inside out, you can achieve a beautiful, healthy-looking mane that you'll love to show off.
