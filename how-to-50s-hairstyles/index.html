How To 50S Hairstyles A Step By Step Guide

If you're obsessed with vintage fashion, then you must have heard about the iconic 50s hairstyles. These hairstyles are still popular today and can be seen on many celebrities and fashion icons. The 50s era was all about big curls, pin-up styles, and glamorous looks. In this article, we'll show you how to recreate these stunning hairstyles in a modern way.

What are 50s hairstyles?

The 50s hairstyles are characterized by voluminous curls, sleek updos, and pin-up styles. Women used to spend hours in the salon to get their hair perfectly styled. The most popular hairstyles of the 50s include the bouffant, beehive, and the victory roll. These hairstyles were inspired by Hollywood stars like Marilyn Monroe, Elizabeth Taylor, and Audrey Hepburn.

How to achieve the perfect 50s hairstyle?

To achieve the perfect 50s hairstyle, you need to have the right tools and products. You'll need a curling iron, hot rollers, hairspray, and bobby pins. You'll also need to have patience and practice. It takes time to perfect these hairstyles, but with a little practice, you'll be able to create the perfect 50s hairstyle.

Step-by-Step Guide to 50s Hairstyles

1. Bouffant
The bouffant is a classic 50s hairstyle that involves teasing the hair to create volume. Here's how to achieve the perfect bouffant:
  1. Start by sectioning off the top part of your hair and clipping it away.
  2. Tease the crown of your hair to create volume.
  3. Smooth the teased hair with a brush and secure it with bobby pins.
  4. Release the top section of your hair and brush it back.
  5. Tease the crown of the top section to create volume.
  6. Smooth the teased hair with a brush and secure it with bobby pins.
  7. Spray the hair with hairspray to hold the style in place.
2. Beehive
The beehive is a high-volume hairstyle that was popular in the 50s. Here's how to create the perfect beehive:
  1. Start by sectioning off the top part of your hair and clipping it away.
  2. Tease the crown of your hair to create volume.
  3. Smooth the teased hair with a brush and secure it with bobby pins.
  4. Release the top section of your hair and backcomb it to create volume.
  5. Smooth the backcombed hair with a brush and secure it with bobby pins.
  6. Twist the sides of your hair and secure them with bobby pins.
  7. Spray the hair with hairspray to hold the style in place.
3. Victory Roll
The victory roll is a classic 50s hairstyle that involves rolling the hair to create a vintage look. Here's how to create the perfect victory roll:
  1. Part your hair on one side and section off a piece of hair at the front.
  2. Roll the section of hair away from your face and secure it with bobby pins.
  3. Repeat on the other side.
  4. Tease the back of your hair to create volume.
  5. Smooth the teased hair with a brush and secure it with bobby pins.
  6. Spray the hair with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Maintaining 50s Hairstyles

To maintain your 50s hairstyle, you need to use the right products and tools. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking perfect:
  • Use a volumizing mousse or spray to add volume to your hair.
  • Invest in a high-quality hairspray to hold your style in place.
  • Use hot rollers or a curling iron to create curls and waves.
  • Use bobby pins and hair elastics to secure your hair in place.
  • Don't wash your hair too often as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.

FAQs About 50s Hairstyles

1. Can I create 50s hairstyles on short hair?
Yes, you can create 50s hairstyles on short hair. You'll need to use hot rollers or a curling iron to create curls and waves. You can also use bobby pins and hair elastics to secure your hair in place. 2. How do I choose the right 50s hairstyle for my face shape?
The right 50s hairstyle for your face shape depends on your features. If you have a round face, you can try a bouffant or beehive. If you have a long face, you can try a victory roll or a sleek updo.

Hair Care for 50s Hairstyles

To keep your hair healthy and strong, you need to take care of it. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:
  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often as it can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes vitamins and minerals for healthy hair.
In conclusion, 50s hairstyles are still popular today and can be worn in a modern way. With the right tools and products, you can easily recreate these vintage looks. Whether you choose a bouffant, beehive, or victory roll, these hairstyles are sure to turn heads. Don't forget to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong.
