How To Cut A Temp Fade On Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of spending money and time at the barbershop? Do you want to try cutting your own hair at home? A temp fade haircut is a stylish and popular choice for men, and it can be done at home with the right tools and techniques. In this article, we will guide you through the process of how to cut a temp fade on yourself.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut a Temp Fade on Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting a Temp Fade

How to Cut a Temp Fade on Yourself

A temp fade, also known as a temple fade or Brooklyn fade, is a short haircut where the hair gradually fades from short to shorter as it approaches the temples. It is a popular and versatile hairstyle that suits almost any face shape and hair type.

To cut a temp fade on yourself, you will need:

  • Clippers with guards of different lengths
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • Hair scissors
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut a temp fade on yourself:

  1. Start with clean and dry hair. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut, and it may not show the true length.
  2. Decide on the length you want for the top of your hair. You can use clippers with guards or scissors to cut the top of your hair to your desired length.
  3. Use a comb to separate the longer hair on top from the shorter hair on the sides and back. The line where the hair changes length is called the part. The part can be straight or curved, depending on your preference.
  4. Choose the guard size you want to use for the sides and back of your hair. A smaller guard will create a shorter fade, while a larger guard will create a longer fade. Start with a larger guard and work your way down if you want a shorter fade.
  5. Start at the bottom of your hairline and work your way up. Use the clippers to cut the hair in an upward motion, stopping at the part line. Repeat on the other side.
  6. Change to a smaller guard size and repeat the process, starting below the first cut line and blending into the previous cut. Be sure to use a smooth and steady motion.
  7. Continue changing to smaller guard sizes and blending in the previous cut until you reach the desired fade length. Be patient and take your time.
  8. Use the scissors to trim any longer hairs on top of your head or around the temples. Be careful not to cut too much or too unevenly.
  9. Check your work in the mirror from different angles. Make any necessary touch-ups or adjustments.
  10. Clean up any hair clippings and wash your hair to remove any loose hair.

Tips for Cutting a Temp Fade

Here are some tips to help you achieve a great temp fade haircut:

  • Start with a larger guard size and work your way down gradually. It is easier to cut more hair than to grow it back.
  • Use a steady and consistent motion when cutting with the clippers. Avoid jerky or uneven movements.
  • Check your work often in the mirror from different angles to ensure an even and smooth fade.
  • Be patient and take your time. A good temp fade takes practice and attention to detail.
  • If you are unsure or uncomfortable with cutting your own hair, consider watching online tutorials or asking a friend for help.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting a temp fade:

Q: How long does a temp fade last?

A: A temp fade typically lasts 2-3 weeks before it needs to be touched up. This can vary depending on your hair type and growth rate.

Q: Can a temp fade work for any hair type?

A: Yes, a temp fade can work for almost any hair type, including straight, curly, and wavy hair. It is important to choose the right guard sizes and techniques for your specific hair type.

Q: Do I need to use clippers or can I use scissors?

A: Clippers are typically used for a temp fade, as they provide a smoother and more even cut. However, scissors can be used for touch-ups or to create a more textured look.

Hair Care After Cutting a Temp Fade

After cutting a temp fade, it is important to take care of your hair to maintain the style and promote healthy growth. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat or styling products, as they can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Trim any split ends or damaged hair regularly to prevent breakage and maintain the style.
  • Consider using a hair oil or serum to add shine and moisture to your hair.
  • Visit a professional barber every few weeks for touch-ups and maintenance.

Cutting a temp fade on yourself can be a rewarding and cost-effective way to maintain a stylish and trendy haircut. With the right tools, techniques, and patience, you can achieve a great temp fade haircut at home. Remember to take care of your hair after cutting it to maintain the style and promote healthy growth.
