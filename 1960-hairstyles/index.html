1960 Hairstyles A Guide To Achieving The Classic Look

Written by Robby Mar 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you a fan of the vintage look? Do you love the style of the 1960s? If so, then you might want to consider trying out some 1960 hairstyles. This era was known for its iconic hairdos, and they are still popular today. In this article, we will give you a step-by-step guide on how to achieve some of the most popular 1960 hairstyles. We will also provide you with tips and tricks to make sure that your hair looks just as fabulous as it did in the 60s.

Description: What are 1960 Hairstyles?

1960 hairstyles were the epitome of glamour and style. Women in this era wore their hair in a variety of ways, from short and sleek to long and voluminous. Some of the most popular 1960 hairstyles include the beehive, the bouffant, and the flip. These hairdos were achieved using a variety of techniques, such as teasing, backcombing, and setting with rollers.

How to Achieve 1960 Hairstyles

Now that you know what 1960 hairstyles are, it's time to learn how to achieve them. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to do some of the most popular 1960 hairstyles:

The Beehive

The beehive is one of the most iconic 1960 hairstyles. To achieve this look, follow these steps:

  1. Start by sectioning off the top of your hair, leaving the bottom section down.
  2. Tease the top section of your hair using a fine-tooth comb. The more you tease, the higher the beehive will be.
  3. Gather the teased hair into a ponytail and secure it with an elastic band.
  4. Twist the ponytail into a bun and secure it with bobby pins.
  5. Smooth down any flyaways with hairspray.

The Bouffant

The bouffant is another popular 1960 hairstyle. Here's how to achieve it:

  1. Start by sectioning off the top of your hair, leaving the bottom section down.
  2. Tease the top section of your hair using a fine-tooth comb.
  3. Smooth down the top layer of your hair using a brush.
  4. Gather the teased hair into a ponytail and secure it with an elastic band.
  5. Roll the ponytail under and pin it in place.
  6. Smooth down any flyaways with hairspray.

The Flip

The flip is a classic 1960 hairstyle that is still popular today. Here's how to achieve it:

  1. Start by blow-drying your hair, making sure to flip the ends outwards.
  2. Once your hair is dry, use a curling iron to curl the ends of your hair upwards.
  3. Brush through your curls to create a softer look.
  4. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Achieving 1960 Hairstyles

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect 1960 hairstyle:

  • Invest in some good quality styling tools, such as a curling iron, hairspray, and bobby pins.
  • Use a fine-tooth comb for teasing your hair, as this will create the most volume.
  • Don't be afraid to use a lot of hairspray to hold your style in place.
  • Practice makes perfect â don't give up if you don't get it right the first time.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

What kind of hair works best for 1960 hairstyles?

1960 hairstyles work best on medium to long hair. If you have short hair, you can still achieve some of these styles with the help of extensions or wigs.

How long does it take to achieve a 1960 hairstyle?

The amount of time it takes to achieve a 1960 hairstyle depends on the style you choose and how skilled you are with styling your hair. It can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour or more.

Can 1960 hairstyles be worn for everyday occasions?

While some 1960 hairstyles are more suitable for special occasions, such as the beehive, others, such as the flip, can be worn for everyday occasions. It really depends on the style you choose and how you want to wear it.

Hair Care Tips for 1960 Hairstyles

When you're wearing a 1960 hairstyle, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it. Use heat protectant products before styling with heat tools.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove any buildup from styling products.
  • Avoid using too much hairspray, as this can dry out your hair and make it look dull.

Now that you know how to achieve 1960 hairstyles, it's time to start experimenting with different styles. Whether you want to go for a classic beehive or a modern take on the flip, there's a 1960 hairstyle for everyone. Just remember to take care of your hair and have fun with your new look!
