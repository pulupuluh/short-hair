How To Ask For A Haircut At A Salon A Relaxed English Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you nervous about asking for a haircut at a salon because you don't know the right words to use? Don't worry, it's a common feeling! In this guide, we'll provide you with all the information you need to confidently ask for a haircut at a salon. From the proper terminology to the best ways to communicate your desired style, this guide has got you covered!

Table of Contents:

  • Terminology
  • Communication
  • Pictures
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Terminology

Before you go to the salon, it's important to know some basic hair terminology. This will help you communicate your desired style to your stylist. Here are some terms you should know:

  • Layers: When hair is cut at different lengths to create volume and movement.
  • Bangs: Fringe of hair that falls over the forehead.
  • Taper: Gradual change in length from the top of the head to the nape of the neck.
  • Bob: Short haircut that is typically straight and cut at the jawline.
  • Pixie: Very short haircut that is typically styled to be messy and textured.

Communication

When you arrive at the salon, the receptionist will ask you what service you'd like. Simply say, "I'd like a haircut, please." Your stylist will then ask you a few questions to get a better understanding of what you're looking for. Here are some things they may ask:

  • What length do you want? Be specific; say things like "I want it to hit just below my shoulders" or "I want it to be chin-length."
  • How much do you want to take off? If you're not sure, your stylist can make a recommendation based on your hair type and desired style.
  • Do you want layers? If you're not sure, your stylist can explain the pros and cons of layers and help you decide.
  • Do you want bangs? If so, be specific about the length and style you want.

Pictures

If you're having trouble communicating your desired style, consider bringing pictures with you to the salon. This will give your stylist a visual reference and help ensure you're both on the same page. You can find hairstyle inspiration on Pinterest, Instagram, and even Google Images.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you ask for a haircut at a salon:

  • Be honest about your hair history. Let your stylist know if you've recently dyed, permed, or chemically treated your hair.
  • Be open to suggestions. Your stylist is a professional and may have ideas for a style that would look great on you.
  • Don't be afraid to speak up if you're not happy with the cut. Your stylist wants you to be happy with the results, so let them know if something isn't working for you.

FAQ

How much should I tip my stylist?

A 15-20% tip is standard for haircuts.

How often should I get a haircut?

It depends on your hair type and desired style. As a general rule, most people get a haircut every 6-8 weeks.

What should I do if I'm not happy with the cut?

Speak up! Your stylist wants you to be happy with the results, so they'll likely be willing to make adjustments.

Hair Care

Once you've gotten your new haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to protect your hair color and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut's shape and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to ask for a haircut at a salon, you can confidently book your next appointment and get the style you've been dreaming of! Remember to communicate clearly with your stylist, be open to suggestions, and take good care of your hair after your haircut. Happy styling!
