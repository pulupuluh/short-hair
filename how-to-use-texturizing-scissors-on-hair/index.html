How To Use Texturizing Scissors On Hair A Step By Step Guide

Texturizing scissors are a great tool to use when you want to add texture and volume to your hair. They are especially useful for those with thick or heavy hair that tends to lay flat. However, using texturizing scissors can be intimidating, especially if you've never used them before. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to use texturizing scissors on hair.

How to Use Texturizing Scissors on Hair

Texturizing scissors are designed to cut hair in a way that removes some of the bulk, while still maintaining length. They are typically used on the ends of the hair to create layers, thin out thick hair, and add texture and volume.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to use texturizing scissors on hair:

  1. Start with clean and dry hair. It's best to use texturizing scissors on hair that has not been washed for at least 24 hours, as clean hair can be more difficult to cut.
  2. Section the hair. Divide your hair into sections using hair clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any areas.
  3. Start with the bottom layer. Begin with the bottom layer of your hair and work your way up. Use the texturizing scissors to cut small sections of hair at a time, holding the scissors at a slight angle.
  4. Cut at an angle. Hold the texturizing scissors at a 45-degree angle and cut into the hair, rather than straight across. This will create a more natural-looking texture.
  5. Work your way up. Continue cutting small sections of hair with the texturizing scissors, working your way up to the top layer of your hair.
  6. Blend the layers. Once you've finished cutting, use a comb to blend the layers together. This will create a more natural-looking texture and ensure that there are no visible lines or choppy sections.

Tips for Using Texturizing Scissors

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your texturizing scissors:

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's better to cut too little than too much.
  • Use the right scissors. Make sure you are using texturizing scissors, as regular scissors can create blunt lines and uneven cuts.
  • Practice on a mannequin head or a willing friend before attempting to use the scissors on your own hair.
  • Go slow. Cutting too much hair at once can result in choppy or uneven layers.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about using texturizing scissors on hair:

Can I use texturizing scissors on curly hair?
Yes, texturizing scissors can be used on curly hair to add texture and reduce bulk. However, it's important to be careful not to cut too much, as curly hair tends to shrink when cut. How often should I use texturizing scissors on my hair?
It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to use texturizing scissors every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair. Can texturizing scissors be used on short hair?
Yes, texturizing scissors can be used on short hair to add texture and volume.

Hair Care After Using Texturizing Scissors

After using texturizing scissors on your hair, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and texture. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, for at least 24 hours after using texturizing scissors.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or hair dyes for at least a week after using texturizing scissors.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the texture and shape created by the texturizing scissors.

By following these tips and using texturizing scissors correctly, you can achieve a natural-looking texture and volume in your hair.
