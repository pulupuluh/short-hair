How To Sleep With Tape Hair Extensions A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Jul 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're one of the millions of people who've discovered the wonders of tape hair extensions, you're probably wondering how to sleep with them without damaging your hair or extensions. The good news is that sleeping with tape hair extensions is relatively easy and straightforward, as long as you follow a few simple steps. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about sleeping with tape hair extensions, including how to prepare your hair, how to protect your extensions, and what to do in the morning.

How to Prepare Your Hair

Before you hit the hay, it's essential to prepare your hair for sleeping with tape hair extensions. Here are a few steps you should follow:

Step 1: Brush Your Hair

Before bed, make sure to brush your hair thoroughly to remove any tangles or knots. This step will prevent matting and tangling while you sleep.

Step 2: Braid Your Hair

Next, create a loose braid that will keep your hair in place while you sleep. A braid will prevent your hair from tangling and matting overnight.

Step 3: Secure Your Extensions

Use a silk scrunchie or hair tie to secure your extensions to your head. Make sure to place the scrunchie or hair tie as close to your scalp as possible to prevent the extensions from slipping or pulling.

How to Protect Your Extensions

In addition to preparing your hair, you should take steps to protect your tape hair extensions while you sleep. Here are a few tips:

Tip 1: Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Silk pillowcases are gentle on hair and skin, reducing the risk of hair breakage and tangling. Sleeping on a silk pillowcase will help protect your tape hair extensions and keep them looking healthy and shiny.

Tip 2: Use a Silk Scarf

If you're worried about your extensions slipping or shifting while you sleep, consider wrapping a silk scarf around your head. This step will help keep your extensions in place and prevent them from rubbing against your pillow.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat

It's best to avoid using heat styling tools on your extensions before bed. Heat can damage the adhesive on your tape hair extensions, causing them to slip or fall out.

What to Do in the Morning

When you wake up in the morning, remove the silk scrunchie or hair tie and gently brush out your hair. Avoid pulling or tugging on your extensions. If you notice any slippage or damage, contact your stylist immediately.

Tips for Maintaining Your Tape Hair Extensions

To keep your tape hair extensions looking their best, follow these tips:

Tip 1: Brush Your Hair Daily

Use a soft-bristled brush to gently brush your hair and extensions each day. This step will prevent tangling and matting and keep your extensions looking healthy and shiny.

Tip 2: Avoid Chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals like bleach or hair dyes on your extensions. These products can damage the adhesive and cause your extensions to fall out.

Tip 3: Schedule Regular Maintenance

It's essential to schedule regular maintenance appointments with your stylist to ensure that your extensions are properly maintained and adjusted as needed.

FAQ

Can I Sleep with Wet Hair Extensions?

No, it's best to avoid sleeping with wet hair extensions. Wet extensions are more prone to tangling and matting, which can cause damage to both your hair and extensions.

How Long Do Tape Hair Extensions Last?

Tape hair extensions typically last between 6-12 weeks, depending on how well they're maintained and how quickly your hair grows.

Can I Use Hair Products on My Tape Hair Extensions?

Yes, you can use hair products on your tape hair extensions, but it's essential to avoid using products that contain alcohol or sulfates. These ingredients can damage the adhesive on your extensions and cause them to fall out.

In Conclusion

Sleeping with tape hair extensions doesn't have to be a hassle. By following a few simple steps and taking proper care of your extensions, you can wake up with beautiful, healthy-looking hair every day. Remember to prepare your hair before bed, protect your extensions while you sleep, and follow proper maintenance tips to keep your extensions looking their best.
