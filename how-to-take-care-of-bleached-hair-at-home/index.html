How To Take Care Of Bleached Hair At Home

Bleaching your hair can be a fun and exciting way to switch up your look, but it can also be damaging to your hair. With the right care and maintenance, you can keep your bleached hair healthy and beautiful.

How to Bleach Your Hair at Home

If you are looking to bleach your hair at home, it is important to follow the instructions carefully to avoid damaging your hair. Here are some steps to follow:

  1. Choose a good quality bleach kit. Look for a kit that includes a developer and bleach powder.
  2. Wash your hair and dry it completely before starting the bleaching process.
  3. Put on gloves and mix the bleach powder and developer according to the instructions on the kit.
  4. Apply the bleach mixture to your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  5. Leave the bleach on your hair for the recommended amount of time, usually around 30 minutes.
  6. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water and apply a toner if necessary.

Remember to always do a patch test before bleaching your hair and to follow the instructions carefully to avoid damaging your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Caring for Bleached Hair

Caring for bleached hair is essential to keep it healthy and prevent damage. Here is a step-by-step guide to caring for bleached hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  2. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  3. Avoid using heat styling tools such as hair dryers, straighteners, and curling irons as much as possible.
  4. If you do use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to avoid further damage.
  5. Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet as this can cause breakage.
  6. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  7. Avoid tying your hair up too tightly as this can cause breakage.
  8. Avoid exposing your hair to the sun for prolonged periods of time as this can cause further damage.
  9. Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends and promote healthy growth.
  10. Consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish and strengthen your hair.

Tips for Caring for Bleached Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for bleached hair:

  • Consider using a purple shampoo to keep your hair from turning brassy.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol as this can dry out your hair even more.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Consider using a hair oil to keep your hair shiny and healthy.

Frequently Asked Questions about Bleached Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about bleached hair:

How long does bleached hair last?

Bleached hair can last for several months depending on how well you take care of it. It is important to follow a good hair care routine to keep your bleached hair healthy and prevent damage.

Can you dye bleached hair?

Yes, you can dye bleached hair. However, it is important to wait at least two weeks after bleaching your hair to dye it to avoid further damage.

Can you swim with bleached hair?

Yes, you can swim with bleached hair. However, it is important to wear a swimming cap to protect your hair from chlorine and other chemicals in the water.

Hair Care Products for Bleached Hair

Here are some hair care products that are specifically designed for bleached hair:

  • Sulfate-free shampoo
  • Deep conditioning treatment
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Purple shampoo
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Hair oil

Remember to always read the labels and choose products that are specifically designed for bleached hair to avoid further damage.

That concludes our blog post about how to take care of bleached hair at home. We hope you found this article helpful and informative. Remember to always follow a good hair care routine to keep your bleached hair healthy and beautiful.
