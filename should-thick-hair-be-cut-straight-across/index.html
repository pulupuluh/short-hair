Should Thick Hair Be Cut Straight Across

Description

Thick hair is a blessing and a curse. It's luscious and full, but it can also be difficult to manage. One of the most important decisions you need to make as someone with thick hair is how to cut it. So, the question is – should thick hair be cut straight across?

The answer is yes and no. It depends on your hair type, face shape, and personal preference. Cutting thick hair straight across can be a great option for some people, while others may prefer a different style. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about cutting thick hair straight across, including how to do it, step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

How to Cut Thick Hair Straight Across

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you have thick hair. However, with the right tools and a little bit of practice, you can cut your hair straight across at home. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties
  • Clip

Step by Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to cut your thick hair straight across:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Divide your hair into two equal sections and tie each section with a hair tie.
  3. Comb each section of hair straight down and place the hair tie at the desired length.
  4. Use the clip to secure the top section of hair out of the way.
  5. Take the bottom section of hair and comb it straight down again.
  6. Using the scissors, cut across the hair at the desired length.
  7. Repeat on the top section of hair.
  8. Check for any unevenness and make any necessary adjustments.

Remember to cut your hair slowly and carefully. It's better to cut less than to cut too much at once. You can always go back and cut more if needed.

Tips for Cutting Thick Hair

Cutting thick hair can be tricky, but with these tips, you'll be able to achieve a straight across cut:

  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. Sharp scissors will make the cutting process easier and give you a cleaner cut.
  • Take your time. Cutting your hair in a rush can result in unevenness.
  • Section your hair properly. Dividing your hair into sections will help you achieve a straight across cut.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to cut less and go back for more than to cut too much and have to wait for your hair to grow back.
  • Ask for help. If you're not comfortable cutting your hair yourself, ask a friend or family member to help you.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting thick hair straight across:

Is cutting thick hair straight across bad?

No, cutting thick hair straight across is not bad. It's a great option for many people, as long as it's done properly.

Will cutting my thick hair straight across make it look thinner?

No, cutting your thick hair straight across will not make it look thinner. It may make your hair appear more voluminous, depending on the length of the cut.

What face shapes look good with a straight across haircut?

A straight across haircut can look good on a variety of face shapes, including oval, square, and heart-shaped faces.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

After cutting your thick hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy, thick hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, and use a heat protectant spray if you do use them.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

By following these hair care tips, you'll be able to keep your thick hair looking its best.

In conclusion, cutting thick hair straight across can be a great option for many people. Whether you decide to do it yourself or go to a professional, make sure to take your time and follow the proper steps. With the right tools and knowledge, you can achieve a straight across cut that looks great and is easy to maintain.
