How To Start Off Being A Barber A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 30, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you interested in pursuing a career in barbering? This guide will walk you through the steps of how to start off being a barber, from education and training to setting up your own business.

Table of Contents

  • Education and Training
  • Licensing and Certification
  • Apprenticeship
  • Equipment and Supplies

Education and Training

The first step to becoming a barber is to obtain a high school diploma or equivalent. After that, you can enroll in a barbering program at a vocational school, community college, or specialized barbering school. These programs typically last 9-12 months and cover topics such as hair cutting, styling, coloring, and chemical treatments.

While in school, it's important to practice your skills as much as possible and seek out opportunities for hands-on experience. You can also start building your network of industry contacts by attending trade shows and events.

Licensing and Certification

After completing your education and training, you'll need to obtain a barber's license in the state where you plan to work. Licensing requirements vary by state, but typically involve passing a written and practical exam.

In addition to a barber's license, you may also want to consider obtaining certification in a particular area of barbering, such as shaving or men's grooming. This can help you stand out in a competitive job market and attract more clients.

Apprenticeship

Another option for gaining hands-on experience in the barbering industry is to complete an apprenticeship program. This involves working under the supervision of a licensed barber and learning on the job. Apprenticeships typically last 1-2 years and may be required in some states.

Equipment and Supplies

As a barber, you'll need to invest in high-quality equipment and supplies in order to provide top-notch service to your clients. This includes items such as clippers, shears, combs, brushes, and styling products. You'll also need to maintain a clean and organized workspace, so be sure to stock up on cleaning supplies as well.

Building Your Business

Once you've obtained your license and equipment, it's time to start building your business. This may involve renting a booth at a barber shop or salon, or opening your own shop. Be sure to research your local market and competition to determine the best location and pricing strategy.

You'll also need to develop a business plan that includes your goals, target market, marketing strategy, and financial projections. This will help guide your decisions and ensure that you stay on track.

Marketing Your Services

In order to attract new clients and grow your business, you'll need to develop a strong marketing strategy. This may include creating a website, social media presence, and advertising in local publications. You can also offer promotions or discounts to first-time clients to encourage them to try your services.

Pricing Your Services

Setting the right prices for your services is crucial to the success of your business. You'll need to consider factors such as your location, competition, and level of experience when determining your prices. Be sure to also factor in the cost of your equipment, supplies, and overhead expenses.

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to become a licensed barber?

A: The length of time it takes to become a licensed barber varies by state, but typically ranges from 9-12 months for a barbering program plus additional time for licensing and certification exams.

Q: How much can I expect to make as a barber?

A: The average salary for a barber in the United States is around $30,000 per year, but this can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and clientele.

Hair Care Tips for Barbers

As a barber, it's important to stay up-to-date on the latest hair care techniques and trends. Attend continuing education classes and workshops to keep your skills sharp and learn new styles. You should also stay informed about the latest products and tools on the market.

Additionally, be sure to educate your clients on proper hair care techniques and recommend products that will help them maintain healthy hair. This will not only help them look and feel their best, but also build trust and loyalty with your clients.

By following these steps and staying committed to your craft, you can build a successful career as a barber. Good luck on your journey!
