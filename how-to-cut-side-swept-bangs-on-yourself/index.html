How To Cut Side Swept Bangs On Yourself

Written by Aprilia Jun 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you looking for a new hairstyle that will give you a fresh look without having to chop off all your hair? Side swept bangs are a great option that can add a touch of elegance and style to your hair. The best part is that you can even cut them yourself at home! In this article, we will guide you on how to cut side swept bangs on yourself, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Side Swept Bangs on Yourself
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Side Swept Bangs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Side Swept Bangs

How to Cut Side Swept Bangs on Yourself

The first thing you need to do is to gather all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. Once you have all these tools, you can follow the steps below:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. This will help you get a more accurate cut.

Step 2: Part Your Hair

Use the comb to create a side parting in your hair. Decide which side you want your bangs to fall on and part your hair accordingly.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Clip the rest of your hair away from your face, leaving only the section that will become your bangs. This will help you to focus on the bangs and avoid cutting the rest of your hair by mistake.

Step 4: Determine the Length of Your Bangs

Decide how long you want your bangs to be. It's important to note that when you cut your hair wet, it will appear longer than when it dries. So, if you want shorter bangs, cut them a little longer than you want.

Step 5: Cut Your Bangs

Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut the ends using the scissors. Start from the middle of your forehead and work your way down. This will create a gradual length that will fall perfectly on your face.

Step 6: Check the Length

Once you are done cutting, check the length of your bangs. If they are too long, you can always trim them a little more.

Step 7: Style Your Bangs

Use a hairdryer and a round brush to style your bangs into the desired shape.

Tips for Cutting Side Swept Bangs

Here are some tips that will help you to achieve the perfect side swept bangs:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry for a more accurate cut.
  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if necessary.
  • Always cut less than you think you need to. You can always trim more later.
  • Don't cut straight across. Instead, cut at an angle to create a more natural look.

FAQs

1. How do I know if side swept bangs will suit me?

Side swept bangs can suit most face shapes, but it's best to consult a hairstylist before you make the cut. They will be able to recommend the best length and style for your face shape.

2. Can I cut my side swept bangs with regular scissors?

No, it's best to use sharp hairdressing scissors for a clean cut. Using regular scissors can result in split ends and an uneven cut.

3. How often do I need to trim my side swept bangs?

You should trim your side swept bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape.

Hair Care After Cutting Side Swept Bangs

After cutting your side swept bangs, you may notice that they need more care and attention than the rest of your hair. Here are some tips to keep them looking great:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when styling your hair with heat tools.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can make them look greasy.
  • Visit a hairstylist regularly to maintain the shape and length of your bangs.

Cutting your own side swept bangs may seem daunting, but with the right tools and technique, it's easy to achieve a salon-worthy look. Follow the steps above, and you'll have beautiful, face-framing bangs in no time!
