How To Do An Undercut Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Mar 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to try a new hairstyle, the undercut fade could be the perfect choice for you. This style has been popular for a few years now and shows no sign of slowing down. The undercut fade is a combination of two haircuts: the undercut and the fade. The undercut is characterized by short hair on the sides and back of the head, while the hair on top is left longer. The fade is a gradual tapering of the hair from short to long. In this article, we'll show you how to do an undercut fade in a relaxed English language, step-by-step.

Description

The undercut fade is a stylish and trendy haircut that is perfect for men who want a low-maintenance but fashionable look. This hairstyle is versatile and can be worn in many different ways, depending on your preferences. The undercut fade is characterized by short hair on the sides and back of the head that gradually fades into longer hair on top. This haircut can be customized to suit your face shape, hair type, and personal style.

How to

Step 1: Choose the Right Clippers

The first step in doing an undercut fade is to choose the right clippers. You'll need a clipper with a range of guard sizes to achieve the fade. Ideally, you should have clippers that come with at least three guards: a #1, a #2, and a #3. If you want a shorter fade, you can use a #0 guard instead of a #1.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to wash and dry it. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that your hair is clean and healthy. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to wash your hair, and then towel dry it or blow dry it on a low heat setting.

Step 3: Cut the Sides and Back

Start by cutting the sides and back of your hair with the shortest guard. This will create the undercut. Use the clippers to trim the hair on the sides and back of your head, moving from the bottom up. Don't worry too much about making it perfect at this stage, as you'll be blending it later.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Next, blend the hair from the undercut into the longer hair on top. Change to a longer guard and use the clippers to gradually blend the hair between the two lengths. Move the clippers up and down, blending the hair as you go. Be careful not to take off too much hair, as you can always go back and trim more later.

Step 5: Define the Hairline

Use the clippers to define the hairline around the ears and the nape of the neck. Use a shorter guard to create a clean line around the edges of the undercut fade.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's time to style it. You can use a hair product, such as a pomade or wax, to create the look you want. Use your fingers to style your hair into place, and use a comb or brush to smooth it out.

Tips

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down if you want a shorter fade.
  • Be patient and take your time when cutting your hair. It's better to go slowly and get it right than to rush and make a mistake.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you if you're not confident in cutting your own hair.
  • Experiment with different hair products to find the one that works best for you.

FAQ

Q: How often should I get an undercut fade?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade starts to look messy. Generally, you should get an undercut fade every 2-4 weeks to keep it looking fresh and clean.

Q: Can I do an undercut fade on curly hair?

A: Yes, you can do an undercut fade on curly hair. However, it may be more difficult to blend the hair, so it's important to go slowly and use the right clippers and guards.

Q: What face shape is best for an undercut fade?

A: An undercut fade is a versatile haircut that can suit many different face shapes. However, it tends to look best on men with oval, square, or diamond-shaped faces.

Hair Care

After you've done an undercut fade, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and stylish. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to wash your hair, and avoid using too much heat when styling it. You can also use a hair serum or oil to keep your hair moisturized and shiny. Finally, visit your barber regularly to get your hair trimmed and maintain the look of your undercut fade.
