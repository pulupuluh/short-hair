How To Grow Thick Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 10, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Curly hair can be a challenge to manage, but it's also a beautiful and unique feature that many people envy. If you're looking to grow thick, healthy curls, you're in the right place. In this guide, we'll cover everything from how to promote hair growth to styling tips for curly hair.

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Growing Thick Curly Hair

How to Grow Thick Curly Hair

There are a few things you can do to encourage hair growth and promote thick, healthy curls:

  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of protein and vitamins.
  • Minimize heat styling and chemical treatments that can damage hair.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to stimulate blood flow and promote growth.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back too tightly, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're looking for a more detailed plan for growing thick curly hair, follow these steps:

  1. Start by assessing the health of your hair. If you have damage or split ends, trim them off before you begin.
  2. Invest in a high-quality shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair. Look for products that are sulfate-free and contain moisturizing ingredients like shea butter or coconut oil.
  3. Massage your scalp regularly to stimulate blood flow and promote growth. Use your fingertips to gently rub your scalp in circular motions for a few minutes each day.
  4. Avoid heat styling and chemical treatments as much as possible. If you must use heat, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first.
  5. Consider taking a biotin supplement to promote hair growth. Biotin is a B vitamin that is essential for healthy hair, skin, and nails.
  6. Avoid pulling your hair back too tightly, as this can cause breakage and damage. If you need to pull your hair back, use a soft scrunchie or hair tie instead of a tight elastic band.
  7. Protect your hair while you sleep by using a silk or satin pillowcase. These materials are gentler on hair than cotton and can help prevent breakage and frizz.
  8. Experiment with different styling techniques to find what works best for your hair. Some people find that diffusing their curls with a hair dryer works well, while others prefer to air dry.
  9. Consider getting regular deep conditioning treatments to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. You can either do this at home with a deep conditioning mask or visit a salon for a professional treatment.
  10. Be patient! Growing thick curly hair takes time and dedication, but with the right care and attention, you'll see results.

Tips for Growing Thick Curly Hair

Here are a few additional tips to help you grow thick, healthy curls:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to moisturize your hair and prevent frizz.
  • Try not to wash your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils. Aim for 2-3 times per week instead.
  • Consider getting a professional haircut that is designed for curly hair. A good stylist can help you find a cut that flatters your face shape and enhances your natural curls.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I still straighten my hair if I'm trying to grow thick curly hair?

Yes, you can still straighten your hair if you want to, but try to minimize heat styling as much as possible. If you must use heat, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first and avoid using the highest heat setting.

What are some good hairstyles for curly hair?

There are many great hairstyles for curly hair, including buns, braids, and half-up styles. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for your hair type and face shape.

How often should I trim my hair?

It's generally recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

Here are a few additional tips for caring for thick curly hair:

  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can dry it out and cause frizz. Use lukewarm water instead.
  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel. These materials are gentler on hair and can help prevent breakage and frizz.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and cause damage.
  • Consider using a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove product buildup and keep your hair healthy and clean.

With these tips and strategies, you'll be on your way to growing thick, healthy curls in no time. Remember to be patient and consistent, and you'll see results before you know it!
