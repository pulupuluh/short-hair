Starting Locs With Natural Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 23, 2023 · 8 min read

Starting locs with natural hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're new to the process. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this guide, we'll take you through everything you need to know about starting locs with natural hair, including how to do it, the steps involved, and tips for maintaining your locs. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Start Locs with Natural Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Starting Locs with Natural Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Locs

Description

Locs, also known as dreadlocks, are a popular and versatile hairstyle that can be worn by anyone, regardless of hair texture. For those with natural hair, starting locs involves allowing your hair to naturally coil and lock together, creating individual strands that grow together over time. This process requires patience, time, and dedication, but the end result is a unique and beautiful hairstyle that can last for years.

How to Start Locs with Natural Hair

There are several methods for starting locs with natural hair, including the twist and rip method, the comb coil method, and the freeform method. Each method has its own benefits and drawbacks, so it's important to do your research and choose the method that works best for you. Some people prefer to start their locs on their own, while others choose to visit a professional stylist for assistance.

Twist and Rip Method

The twist and rip method involves separating your hair into small sections, twisting each section tightly, and then ripping the twist apart to create two separate strands. This method creates well-defined, uniform locs that can be easily maintained.

Comb Coil Method

The comb coil method involves using a small comb to twist your hair into individual coils. This method creates smaller, more defined locs that can be easily styled and maintained.

Freeform Method

The freeform method involves allowing your hair to naturally coil and lock on its own, without any manipulation or styling. This method creates a more organic, natural look, but can be more difficult to maintain and style.

Step-by-Step Guide

Regardless of which method you choose, starting locs with natural hair requires patience and dedication. Here is a general step-by-step guide to starting locs:

  1. Wash and condition your hair using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Allow your hair to air dry or gently towel dry.
  3. Separate your hair into small sections.
  4. Twist, coil, or allow your hair to naturally coil and lock.
  5. Avoid using hair products for the first few weeks to allow your locs to form naturally.
  6. Maintain your locs by washing and conditioning them regularly, and retwisting or recoiling them as needed.

Tips for Starting Locs with Natural Hair

Starting locs with natural hair can be a long process, but there are several things you can do to make the process easier and more enjoyable:

  • Be patient – the process of starting locs can take several months or even years.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain heavy oils or waxes, as these can build up in your locs and cause them to become heavy and difficult to maintain.
  • Protect your locs at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.
  • Be gentle when washing and styling your locs to avoid causing breakage or damage.
  • Consider visiting a professional stylist for assistance with starting your locs.

FAQs

How long does it take to start locs with natural hair?

The process of starting locs with natural hair can take several months or even years, depending on your hair texture and the method you choose.

Can I start locs on my own?

Yes, it is possible to start locs on your own, but it can be helpful to seek advice from a professional stylist, especially if you're new to the process.

Do I need to cut my hair to start locs?

No, you do not need to cut your hair to start locs. You can start locs with any length of hair.

Hair Care for Locs

Once you've started locs with natural hair, it's important to take good care of them to keep them healthy and looking their best. Here are some tips for maintaining your locs:

  • Wash your locs regularly using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or waxes that can build up in your locs and cause them to become heavy and difficult to maintain.
  • Retwist or recoil your locs as needed to maintain their shape and prevent them from unraveling.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can cause damage to your locs.
  • Protect your locs from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or scarf.

Starting locs with natural hair can be a rewarding and beautiful process. By following these tips and taking good care of your locs, you can enjoy this unique and versatile hairstyle for years to come.
