Do Layers Make Your Hair Thicker Or Thinner

Written by William Apr 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you are looking for a way to change up your hairstyle, you may be considering getting layers. But, before you take the plunge, you may be wondering whether layers make your hair thicker or thinner. The answer is that it depends on a few factors, including the length of your hair, the type of layers you get, and how much hair you have. In this article, we will explore the different ways that layers can affect the thickness of your hair, and provide some tips on how to care for your hair after getting layers.

How Do Layers Affect Hair Thickness?

The way that layers affect hair thickness depends largely on the type of layers you get. Generally speaking, layers that are shorter and closer to the top of your head can make your hair appear thicker, while longer layers can make your hair appear thinner. This is because shorter layers add volume and movement to your hair, while longer layers can weigh your hair down and make it look flat.

Types of Layers

There are several different types of layers that you can get, each of which can affect the thickness of your hair in different ways. Here are some of the most common types of layers:

  • Long Layers: Long layers start at the mid-length of your hair and continue down to the ends. These layers can make your hair look thinner, but can also add movement and texture.
  • Short Layers: Short layers start at the crown of your head and continue down to the ends. These layers can make your hair appear thicker, but can also make it look choppy.
  • Graduated Layers: Graduated layers are shorter at the back of your head and longer at the front. These layers can add volume and movement to your hair, but can also make it look thinner.
  • Choppy Layers: Choppy layers are uneven and can be cut at different angles. These layers can add texture and movement to your hair, but can also make it look thinner.

How to Care for Your Hair After Getting Layers

If you decide to get layers, it is important to know how to care for your hair in order to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use the right shampoo: Use a shampoo that is appropriate for your hair type and texture. If you have fine hair, look for a volumizing shampoo that will help add body.
  • Avoid using too much product: Using too much product can weigh your hair down and make it look flat. Use a small amount of styling product, and apply it evenly throughout your hair.
  • Get regular trims: Getting regular trims will help keep your layers looking fresh and prevent split ends.
  • Use heat protectant: Before using heat styling tools, be sure to apply a heat protectant to your hair to prevent damage.
  • Avoid over-washing: Over-washing your hair can strip it of its natural oils and make it look dull. Try to wash your hair every other day, or use a dry shampoo in between washes.

FAQ

Do layers make your hair look thinner?

Long layers can make your hair look thinner, while shorter layers can make it appear thicker.

What type of layers should I get?

The type of layers you should get depends on your hair type, texture, and the look you are going for. Talk to your stylist to determine which type of layers will work best for you.

How often should I get my hair trimmed?

You should get your hair trimmed every 6-8 weeks to keep your layers looking fresh and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Overall, layers can be a great way to change up your hairstyle and add volume and movement to your hair. By choosing the right type of layers and taking care of your hair, you can achieve a look that is both stylish and healthy.
