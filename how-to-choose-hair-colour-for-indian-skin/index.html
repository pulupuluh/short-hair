How To Choose Hair Colour For Indian Skin

Written by Robby May 19, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Choosing the right hair colour can be tricky, especially if you have Indian skin. With so many options available, it's important to find a shade that complements your skin tone and enhances your natural beauty. In this article, we'll explore how to choose hair colour for Indian skin, step by step. We'll also provide tips, FAQ, and hair care advice to help you maintain your new look.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone
  • Step 2: Consider Your Natural Hair Colour
  • Step 3: Choose a Shade That Complements Your Skin Tone
  • Tips for Choosing Hair Colour for Indian Skin
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your New Look

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in choosing the right hair colour for Indian skin is to determine your skin tone. Indian skin tones can range from light to dark, and each tone has its own unique characteristics. One way to determine your skin tone is to look at the veins on your wrist. If they appear blue or purple, you likely have cool-toned skin. If they appear green, you likely have warm-toned skin. If you can't tell, you likely have neutral-toned skin.

Undertones

Once you've determined your skin tone, it's important to consider your undertones. Undertones are the subtle hues that lie beneath the surface of your skin. Cool-toned skin has pink or blue undertones, warm-toned skin has yellow or golden undertones, and neutral-toned skin has a mix of both.

Step 2: Consider Your Natural Hair Colour

The next step is to consider your natural hair colour. This will help you determine how drastic of a change you want to make. If you have dark hair, you may want to opt for a darker shade or add subtle highlights. If you have lighter hair, you may be able to go for a more dramatic change.

Step 3: Choose a Shade That Complements Your Skin Tone

Now that you've determined your skin tone and considered your natural hair colour, it's time to choose a shade that complements your skin tone. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Cool-toned skin: Shades of brown, burgundy, and dark red are flattering on cool-toned skin. Avoid shades that are too warm or orange.
  • Warm-toned skin: Shades of copper, honey, and golden blonde are flattering on warm-toned skin. Avoid shades that are too cool or ashy.
  • Neutral-toned skin: Neutral-toned skin can pull off a wide range of colours, but it's best to avoid extremes. Stick to shades that are neither too warm nor too cool.

Tips for Choosing Hair Colour for Indian Skin

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing hair colour for Indian skin:

  • Consider your eye colour. Your eye colour can also play a role in which hair colour will look best on you.
  • Consult with a professional. If you're not sure which shade to choose, it's always a good idea to consult with a professional stylist.
  • Test the colour. Before committing to a full head of colour, do a patch test to make sure you're not allergic and to see how the colour will look on your hair.
  • Gradually lighten or darken. If you're not ready to make a drastic change, consider gradually lightening or darkening your hair over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about choosing hair colour for Indian skin:

What hair colour suits Indian skin?

Shades of brown, burgundy, and dark red are generally flattering on cool-toned Indian skin, while shades of copper, honey, and golden blonde are flattering on warm-toned Indian skin. Neutral-toned Indian skin can pull off a wide range of colours.

Can Indian skin tone pull off blonde hair?

Yes, Indian skin tone can pull off blonde hair, but it's important to choose the right shade. Warm-toned Indian skin looks best with golden blonde shades, while cool-toned Indian skin looks best with ashy or platinum blonde shades.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your New Look

Once you've chosen your new hair colour, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the colour and keep it looking healthy. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when heat styling.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

Choosing the right hair colour for Indian skin can be a fun and exciting process. By following these tips and guidelines, you can find a shade that enhances your natural beauty and complements your skin tone. Don't be afraid to experiment and have fun with your new look!
