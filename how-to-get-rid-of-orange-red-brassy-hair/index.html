How To Get Rid Of Orange Red Brassy Hair

Written by Aprilia Apr 25, 2023 · 6 min read

Table of Contents:

  2. How to Get Rid of Orange Red Brassy Hair
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care

Description

Orange red brassy hair can be a nightmare for anyone who has ever dyed their hair. It can happen to anyone and can be caused by a variety of reasons such as using the wrong hair dye, over-processing the hair, or even exposure to chlorine in the swimming pool. Whatever the reason, it is important to know how to get rid of orange red brassy hair to restore your hair’s natural color and shine.

How to Get Rid of Orange Red Brassy Hair

There are several ways to get rid of orange red brassy hair. The most common method is to use a toner. A toner is a type of hair dye that is used to neutralize any unwanted colors in the hair. This can be done at home or at a salon. Another option is to use a purple or blue shampoo that is designed to neutralize brassy tones in the hair. This can be used in conjunction with a toner for best results.

Step by Step

Follow these steps to get rid of orange red brassy hair:

  1. Choose a toner or purple/blue shampoo that is appropriate for your hair type and color.
  2. Apply the toner or shampoo to your hair, following the instructions on the packaging.
  3. Leave the toner or shampoo on for the recommended amount of time.
  4. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  5. Condition your hair to restore moisture and shine.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when trying to get rid of orange red brassy hair:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to help preserve your hair color.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner before swimming.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as this can damage your hair and make it more prone to brassiness.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting rid of orange red brassy hair:

What causes orange red brassy hair?

Orange red brassy hair can be caused by a variety of factors such as using the wrong hair dye, over-processing the hair, or exposure to chlorine in the swimming pool.

Can toner damage my hair?

While toner is generally safe to use, it can be drying to the hair if left on for too long. Be sure to follow the instructions on the packaging and rinse your hair thoroughly after use.

How often should I use purple or blue shampoo?

You can use purple or blue shampoo as often as you like, but it is recommended to use it once or twice a week to maintain your hair color.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to keeping your hair healthy and free from brassiness. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type and color.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as this can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and health.

With these tips and tricks, you can get rid of orange red brassy hair and keep your locks looking healthy and shiny. Remember to take care of your hair and treat it with kindness to keep it looking its best.
