How To Fix Balding Lace Frontal Tips Steps And Hair Care

Written by Bardi Apr 16, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a wig enthusiast, then you know that a lace frontal is a fantastic hairpiece that can transform your look instantly. However, with constant wear and tear, your lace frontal may start to bald. Balding lace frontals can be a frustrating experience, but there's no need to throw it away. In this guide, we'll show you how to fix balding lace frontal in a few easy steps.

Description

A lace frontal is a hairpiece that covers the front hairline of your wig. It's designed to give you a natural-looking hairline and allows you to wear your wig in a variety of styles. However, with constant wear and tear, your lace frontal may start to bald. This happens when the hair strands on the lace start to fall out, creating bald spots. If you're struggling with a balding lace frontal, don't worry. There's a way to fix it without having to buy a new one.

How to

Step 1: Identify the bald spots

The first step in fixing a balding lace frontal is to identify the bald spots. Look closely at the lace to see where the hair strands have fallen out. Once you've identified the bald spots, you can move on to the next step.

Step 2: Apply a Knot Sealer

The next step is to apply a knot sealer to the bald spots. A knot sealer is a liquid solution that seals the knots on the lace, preventing the hair strands from falling out. To apply the knot sealer, use a small brush or Q-tip and dab it onto the bald spots. Make sure that you apply enough knot sealer to cover the entire bald spot. Allow the knot sealer to dry completely before moving on to the next step.

Step 3: Fill in the Bald Spots

Once the knot sealer has dried, it's time to fill in the bald spots. You can use either hair fibers or liquid foundation to fill in the bald spots. If you choose to use hair fibers, sprinkle them onto the bald spots and gently pat them down with your fingers. If you choose to use liquid foundation, apply a small amount onto the bald spots and blend it in with a makeup sponge.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once you've filled in the bald spots, it's time to style your hair. You can wear your wig in a variety of styles, including a sleek ponytail, a messy bun, or loose waves. Make sure that you use heat protectant spray before using any hot tools on your wig.

Tips

  • Use a knot sealer that matches the color of your lace
  • Don't apply too much knot sealer as it can make the lace stiff
  • Use a toothbrush to brush out any excess hair fibers
  • Avoid using too much heat on your wig as it can damage the lace

FAQ

Can I fix a balding lace frontal myself?

Yes, you can fix a balding lace frontal yourself. All you need is a knot sealer, hair fibers or liquid foundation, and some patience.

How long does a fixed lace frontal last?

A fixed lace frontal can last up to a few months, depending on how well you take care of it. Make sure that you avoid using too much heat on your wig and use a knot sealer to prevent further balding.

Hair Care

To keep your lace frontal looking its best, make sure that you take care of it properly. Use a wig stand to store your wig when you're not wearing it, and avoid using too much heat on it. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your wig, and wash it regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.

Now that you know how to fix balding lace frontal, you can keep your wig looking its best for longer. With a little bit of patience and some hair care, you can extend the life of your lace frontal and enjoy beautiful, natural-looking hair every day.
