How To Know If Hairstyle Will Suit Me Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Feb 22, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you planning to change your hairstyle but unsure of what will suit you? It's a common dilemma that many people face. A new hairstyle can be a great way to refresh your look and boost your confidence. But, choosing the right one can be tricky. In this guide, we'll show you how to know if a hairstyle will suit you.

Table of Contents

  • Know Your Face Shape
  • Consider Your Hair Texture
  • Assess Your Lifestyle
  • Know Your Hair Type
  • Experiment with Different Styles
  • Take Care of Your Hair
  • FAQs

Know Your Face Shape

The first step in determining if a hairstyle will suit you is to know your face shape. There are several face shapes, including oval, round, square, heart, and diamond. Each shape has hairstyles that flatter it best. A good hairstylist can help you identify your face shape and suggest hairstyles that will suit you.

For example, if you have an oval face, you can pull off most hairstyles, including short, long, curly, or straight. If you have a round face, you want to avoid hairstyles that add width to your face, such as blunt bangs or chin-length bobs. Instead, opt for hairstyles that add height and length, such as long layers or side-swept bangs.

Consider Your Hair Texture

Another factor to consider when choosing a new hairstyle is your hair texture. Do you have curly, wavy, or straight hair? Is it thick or thin? Different hairstyles work best with different hair textures. For example, if you have curly hair, a short bob may not work for you because it requires more maintenance than longer styles. Alternatively, if you have thin hair, you may want to avoid very long hairstyles as they can make your hair appear flat and lifeless.

Assess Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle should also influence your choice of hairstyle. Are you a busy mom who doesn't have much time to style her hair every day? Or, do you work in a corporate environment where a polished look is a must? Your lifestyle will determine how much time and effort you can dedicate to your hair. Choose a hairstyle that fits your lifestyle and is easy to maintain.

Know Your Hair Type

Understanding your hair type can also help you choose a hairstyle that will suit you. Do you have fine, medium, or thick hair? Is it straight, wavy, or curly? Knowing your hair type can help you choose a hairstyle that works with your natural hair texture. For example, if you have thick, curly hair, a pixie cut may not work for you because it requires a lot of styling to maintain its shape.

Experiment with Different Styles

The best way to know if a hairstyle will suit you is to try it out. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and lengths. You may be surprised at what looks great on you. Consider getting a wig or trying out virtual hairstyling apps to see what different styles would look like on you.

Take Care of Your Hair

Lastly, taking care of your hair is essential to ensure that any hairstyle you choose looks great. Healthy hair is easier to style and looks better than damaged or unhealthy hair. Invest in high-quality hair products and visit your hairstylist regularly to keep your hair looking its best.

FAQs

1. Can I pull off a short hairstyle if I have a round face?

Yes, you can. However, you should avoid blunt bangs and chin-length bobs that add width to your face. Opt for styles that add height and length, such as long layers or side-swept bangs.

2. Is it okay to try out different hair colors?

Yes, it is. Experimenting with hair colors can be fun and a great way to refresh your look. However, it's essential to choose a color that complements your skin tone and eye color.

3. How often should I get my hair trimmed?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every six to eight weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

4. Can I change my hairstyle if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can. However, you should choose a style that works with your natural curls and texture. Avoid styles that require a lot of heat styling or maintenance as they can damage your hair.

5. Should I consider my age when choosing a hairstyle?

Age should not be a limiting factor when choosing a hairstyle. However, it's essential to choose a style that complements your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle.

In conclusion, choosing a new hairstyle can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. By considering your face shape, hair texture, lifestyle, hair type, and experimenting with different styles, you can find a hairstyle that suits you. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
