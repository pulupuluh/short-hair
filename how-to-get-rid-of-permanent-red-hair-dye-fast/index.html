How To Get Rid Of Permanent Red Hair Dye Fast

Written by Christine Jul 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you've ever tried to dye your hair red, you know that it can be a tricky color to maintain. And if you've decided that it's time to switch back to your natural hair color or try something new, you might be wondering how to get rid of permanent red hair dye fast. Fortunately, there are several methods you can try at home that can help you get rid of that stubborn red dye quickly and safely.

Table of Contents

  • Method 1: Using a Color Remover
  • Method 2: Using a Clarifying Shampoo
  • Method 3: Using Baking Soda and Anti-Dandruff Shampoo
  • Tips for Removing Red Hair Dye
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Removing Red Hair Dye

Method 1: Using a Color Remover

One of the most effective ways to get rid of permanent red hair dye is to use a color remover. These products work by breaking down the dye molecules in your hair and allowing them to be washed out. You can find color removers at most drugstores or beauty supply stores.

To use a color remover, follow these steps:

  1. Apply the color remover to dry hair, following the instructions on the package.
  2. Cover your hair with a shower cap or plastic wrap and let the color remover sit for the recommended amount of time.
  3. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  4. Shampoo your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any remaining dye.

Keep in mind that color removers can be drying to your hair, so it's important to follow up with a deep conditioning treatment.

Method 2: Using a Clarifying Shampoo

If you don't want to use a color remover, you can try using a clarifying shampoo to fade the red dye. Clarifying shampoos are designed to remove buildup from your hair, including hair dye. Look for a clarifying shampoo that contains sulfates, which can help remove the dye.

To use a clarifying shampoo, follow these steps:

  1. Wet your hair with warm water.
  2. Apply the clarifying shampoo to your hair and lather well.
  3. Let the shampoo sit on your hair for a few minutes to allow it to penetrate.
  4. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  5. Repeat the process as needed until the red dye has faded to your desired level.

Clarifying shampoos can be drying, so be sure to follow up with a deep conditioning treatment.

Method 3: Using Baking Soda and Anti-Dandruff Shampoo

You can also try using a mixture of baking soda and anti-dandruff shampoo to fade the red dye. Baking soda is a natural exfoliant that can help remove the dye from your hair.

To use this method, follow these steps:

  1. Mix equal parts baking soda and anti-dandruff shampoo in a bowl.
  2. Apply the mixture to your hair and massage it in well.
  3. Cover your hair with a shower cap or plastic wrap and let it sit for 30 minutes.
  4. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  5. Follow up with a deep conditioning treatment.

Keep in mind that this method can be drying to your hair, so it's important to use a deep conditioning treatment afterward.

Tips for Removing Red Hair Dye

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when trying to get rid of permanent red hair dye:

  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can open up the hair cuticle and make the dye more difficult to remove.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb when detangling your hair to avoid pulling out any strands.
  • Be patient. Removing red hair dye can take several treatments, so don't expect to see results overnight.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about removing red hair dye:

Can I bleach my hair to remove red dye?

Bleaching your hair can be a harsh process that can cause damage and breakage, especially if you've already dyed your hair. It's best to avoid bleaching your hair if possible and try other methods first.

Will removing red hair dye damage my hair?

While some methods can be drying to your hair, they shouldn't cause permanent damage. Be sure to follow up with a deep conditioning treatment after each treatment to keep your hair healthy.

How long does it take to remove red hair dye?

The time it takes to remove red hair dye can vary depending on the method you use and how long you've had the dye in your hair. It can take several treatments to completely remove the dye, so be patient.

Hair Care After Removing Red Hair Dye

After you've successfully removed the red dye from your hair, it's important to take steps to keep your hair healthy and prevent further damage. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or relaxers.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair healthy and vibrant after removing red hair dye.
