What Does The Bible Say About Hair Growth

Written by Daniel Mar 02, 2023 · 8 min read

Have you ever wondered what the Bible says about hair growth? As it turns out, there are several passages in the Bible that mention hair and its growth. In this article, we'll take a look at what the Bible has to say about hair and how it can help us understand the importance of taking care of our hair.

Table of Contents

  • Hair Growth in the Bible
  • Hair Care Tips from the Bible
  • FAQs

Hair Growth in the Bible

One of the most well-known passages about hair in the Bible is found in the book of Luke:

"And even the very hairs of your head are all numbered. So don't be afraid; you are worth more than many sparrows." - Luke 12:7 (NIV)

This verse reminds us that God cares about the smallest details of our lives, including the number of hairs on our head. It also serves as a reminder that our worth is not determined by our appearance, but rather by our value as children of God.

Another passage about hair growth can be found in the book of Numbers:

"All the days of his vow of separation, no razor shall touch his head. Until the time is completed for which he separates himself to the Lord, he shall be holy. He shall let the locks of hair of his head grow long." - Numbers 6:5 (ESV)

This passage is referring to the Nazirite vow, which was a voluntary vow of separation in which a person would dedicate themselves to God for a period of time. During this time, they were not allowed to cut their hair or consume any alcohol or grape products. The growing of their hair was a symbol of their dedication to God and their desire to be set apart.

Hair Care Tips from the Bible

While the Bible doesn't give us specific instructions on how to care for our hair, there are several principles that we can apply to our hair care routine:

1. Take care of your body

The Bible tells us that our bodies are temples of the Holy Spirit, and we are called to take care of them:

"Do you not know that your bodies are temples of the Holy Spirit, who is in you, whom you have received from God? You are not your own; you were bought at a price. Therefore honor God with your bodies." - 1 Corinthians 6:19-20 (NIV)

This means that we should strive to eat a healthy diet, exercise regularly, and get enough rest. When we take care of our bodies, our hair will also benefit.

2. Avoid vanity

The Bible warns us against being vain and placing too much importance on our outward appearance:

"Charm is deceptive, and beauty is fleeting; but a woman who fears the Lord is to be praised." - Proverbs 31:30 (NIV)

While it's important to take care of our appearance, we should not place too much importance on it. Our true beauty comes from our character and our relationship with God.

3. Practice humility

The Bible also teaches us the importance of humility:

"Do nothing out of selfish ambition or vain conceit. Rather, in humility value others above yourselves." - Philippians 2:3 (NIV)

When we practice humility, we are not focused on our own appearance or accomplishments, but rather on serving others and living a life that honors God.

FAQs

What does the Bible say about cutting hair?

While the Bible does not forbid cutting one's hair, there are several passages that mention not cutting one's hair as a symbol of dedication to God. These include the Nazirite vow in Numbers 6 and the story of Samson in Judges 13-16.

Does the Bible say anything about hair loss?

The Bible does not specifically mention hair loss, but it does remind us that God cares about the smallest details of our lives, including the number of hairs on our head. It also teaches us to trust in God in all circumstances, even when we are facing challenges and difficulties.

Is it a sin to dye your hair?

The Bible does not specifically mention dying one's hair, so it is up to each individual to decide whether or not it is something that is consistent with their values and beliefs.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Bible has several passages about hair and its growth, reminding us of the importance of taking care of our bodies and valuing our true beauty that comes from our character and relationship with God. While the Bible does not give us specific instructions on hair care, we can apply its principles to our hair care routine and live a life that honors God.
