Is Hair Loss Associated With Hypothyroidism

Written by Daniel Apr 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Hypothyroidism is a condition where the thyroid gland does not produce enough thyroid hormones. This can lead to a variety of symptoms including weight gain, fatigue, depression, and hair loss. In this blog post, we will explore the relationship between hypothyroidism and hair loss and provide tips on how to care for your hair if you have this condition.

What Causes Hair Loss in Hypothyroidism?

Hair loss in hypothyroidism is caused by several factors. One of the most important factors is the lack of thyroid hormones. These hormones are essential for the growth and maintenance of hair follicles. When there is a deficiency of these hormones, the hair follicles become weak and brittle, leading to hair loss.

Another factor that contributes to hair loss in hypothyroidism is the autoimmune disease. In some cases, hypothyroidism is caused by an autoimmune disease called Hashimoto's thyroiditis. This disease causes the immune system to attack the thyroid gland, which can also damage hair follicles.

How to Diagnose Hypothyroidism-Related Hair Loss?

If you are experiencing hair loss and suspect that it may be related to hypothyroidism, it is important to see a healthcare provider. Your healthcare provider will perform a physical examination and may order blood tests to check your thyroid hormone levels. In some cases, they may also perform a scalp biopsy to rule out other causes of hair loss.

How to Treat Hair Loss in Hypothyroidism?

The most effective way to treat hair loss in hypothyroidism is to treat the underlying condition. This involves taking thyroid hormone replacement therapy. This therapy helps to replace the missing thyroid hormones and can help to restore hair growth. In addition to thyroid hormone replacement therapy, there are other treatments that can help to promote hair growth, including:

1. Minoxidil:

Minoxidil is a medication that is used to treat hair loss. It works by increasing blood flow to the hair follicles, which can help to promote hair growth. Minoxidil is available over the counter and can be used topically on the scalp.

2. Hair Transplant:

Hair transplant is a surgical procedure that involves transplanting hair follicles from one area of the scalp to another. This procedure can be effective in restoring hair growth in areas where hair loss has occurred.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair with Hypothyroidism

If you have hypothyroidism, it is important to take steps to care for your hair to prevent further damage and promote hair growth. Here are some tips:

1. Use Gentle Shampoo:

Use a gentle shampoo that is free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals that can damage hair follicles.

2. Avoid Heat Styling:

Avoid using heat styling tools such as blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons, as these can damage hair follicles.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet:

Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth, such as iron, biotin, and vitamin D.

4. Manage Stress:

Stress can worsen hypothyroidism and contribute to hair loss. Practice stress management techniques such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises.

FAQs

Q: Can hypothyroidism cause permanent hair loss?

A: Hair loss in hypothyroidism is usually temporary and can be treated with thyroid hormone replacement therapy. However, in some cases, permanent hair loss may occur.

Q: Can hypothyroidism cause hair to turn gray?

A: Hypothyroidism is not known to cause hair to turn gray. However, some people with hypothyroidism may experience premature graying of the hair.

Q: Can hypothyroidism cause hair to become brittle?

A: Yes, hypothyroidism can cause hair to become brittle and prone to breakage.

Conclusion

Hypothyroidism can lead to hair loss due to a lack of thyroid hormones and autoimmune disease. The best way to treat hair loss in hypothyroidism is to treat the underlying condition with thyroid hormone replacement therapy. In addition, there are other treatments and tips that can help to promote hair growth and prevent further damage to hair follicles.
