How To Cut Curly Hair Boy At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Mar 15, 2023 · 7 min read

Cutting curly hair can be a daunting task, especially if you’re a parent with no prior experience. But don’t worry, with a little patience and practice, you can give your curly-haired boy a great haircut right at home. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps of how to cut curly hair boy at home, along with some tips and hair care advice to ensure a successful outcome.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Curly Hair Boy at Home
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care for Curly Hair Boy

How to Cut Curly Hair Boy at Home

Before you start cutting your boy’s curly hair, make sure you have the right tools. You’ll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. It’s also a good idea to have a mirror and some clips to section off the hair.

When it comes to cutting curly hair, it’s important to keep in mind that the curls will bounce up once they’re dry. So, it’s always better to cut less than you think you need to. You can always go back and trim more if necessary.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut curly hair boy at home:

Step 1: Wash and Dry

Start by washing your boy’s hair and let it dry naturally. Don’t use a blow dryer as it can cause frizz and damage the hair.

Step 2: Comb and Section

Once the hair is dry, comb it through to remove any tangles. Then, use clips to section off the hair into manageable sections.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Take one section of hair at a time and dampen it with the spray bottle. Comb it through again to make sure it’s smooth. Then, starting at the ends, trim off any split ends or damaged hair. Work your way up the hair, cutting small sections at a time. Remember to cut less than you think you need to.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Once you’ve cut all the sections, blend the hair together by combing it through again. Check to make sure there aren’t any uneven spots or long strands.

Step 5: Check the Length

Check the length of the hair to make sure it’s the desired length. If it’s too long, you can go back and trim some more. If it’s too short, you’ll have to wait for it to grow out.

Step 6: Clean Up

Once you’re done cutting, clean up any stray hairs and put away your tools.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some tips to make cutting curly hair boy at home easier:

  • Work in small sections to ensure even cutting
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends
  • Cut less than you think you need to
  • Don’t use a blow dryer to dry the hair
  • Check the length as you go

FAQ

How often should I cut my boy’s curly hair?

It depends on how fast his hair grows and how short you want it. It’s best to trim curly hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and manageable.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

If you accidentally cut off too much, don’t panic. Wait for the hair to grow out and try again. You can also take your boy to a professional stylist to fix any mistakes.

Can I use regular scissors to cut curly hair?

No, it’s best to use sharp, professional scissors to avoid damaging the hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair Boy

Caring for curly hair is important to keep it healthy and manageable. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair
  • Avoid brushing the hair when it’s dry
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair when it’s wet
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep the curls hydrated
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as they can damage the hair

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you can confidently cut your curly-haired boy’s hair at home. Remember to be patient and take your time, and the end result will be a great haircut that your boy will love.
