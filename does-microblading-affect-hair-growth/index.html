Does Microblading Affect Hair Growth The Truth Revealed

Written by Christine Jul 08, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you planning to get your eyebrows microbladed but worried about its effects on your hair growth? If you're someone who is obsessed with thick, luscious eyebrows, you've probably heard of microblading. It's a semi-permanent tattooing technique that involves creating fine hair-like strokes on the eyebrow area to give the appearance of fuller and more defined eyebrows. But the question remains, does microblading affect hair growth? In this article, we'll reveal the truth and everything you need to know about microblading and hair growth.

How Does Microblading Work?

Before we dive into the effects of microblading on hair growth, let's first understand how this technique works. Microblading involves using a small handheld tool with tiny needles to create small cuts on the surface of your skin. The cuts are made in the shape of hair-like strokes, and a pigment is deposited into the skin to create the illusion of fuller eyebrows. The results of microblading can last anywhere from one to three years.

Does Microblading Affect Hair Growth?

The short answer is no, microblading does not affect hair growth. Microblading is a superficial procedure that does not penetrate deep enough into the skin to affect the hair follicles. The hair follicles are located much deeper in the skin, and the microblading pigment is only deposited in the uppermost layers of the skin. Therefore, microblading does not have any impact on hair growth or hair loss.

How to Take Care of Your Microbladed Eyebrows?

While microblading does not affect hair growth, it's essential to take care of your microbladed eyebrows to ensure their longevity. Here are some tips to help you take care of your newly microbladed eyebrows:

1. Avoid Getting Them Wet

Avoid getting your eyebrows wet for at least a week after the procedure. Water can cause the pigment to fade and affect the healing process.

2. Avoid Direct Sunlight

Avoid exposing your eyebrows to direct sunlight for at least two weeks after the procedure. Sunlight can fade the pigment and cause the area to become irritated.

3. Avoid Makeup and Skincare Products

Avoid using makeup and skincare products on your eyebrows for at least a week after the procedure. These products can cause irritation and affect the healing process.

4. Apply Ointment

Apply the ointment provided by your microblading technician to your eyebrows for the first few days after the procedure. The ointment will help soothe the area and promote healing.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is Microblading Safe?

Yes, microblading is safe when performed by a licensed and trained technician. It's essential to choose a reputable technician to ensure the procedure is performed correctly and safely.

2. Does Microblading Hurt?

Mild discomfort is normal during the procedure. However, your technician will apply a numbing cream to help minimize any pain or discomfort.

3. How Long Does Microblading Take?

The procedure can take anywhere from one to three hours, depending on the extent of the work required.

Final Thoughts

Now that you know that microblading does not affect hair growth, you can confidently go ahead and get your eyebrows microbladed without any worries. Remember to take proper care of your microbladed eyebrows to ensure their longevity and keep them looking fabulous. With the right technician and aftercare, you can achieve the eyebrows of your dreams with microblading.
