How To Make A Nigerian Boy Hair Curly A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Mar 24, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're a Nigerian boy who wants to add a little bounce to your hair, you're in luck! There are several ways to make your hair curly, and in this post, we'll show you how. Whether you're looking for a temporary or permanent solution, we've got you covered. So, let's get started!

Description

Curls are a great way to add volume and texture to your hair, and they look great on everyone, no matter their age or gender. However, if you have naturally straight hair, it can be challenging to get your hair to hold a curl. That's where our guide comes in. We'll show you how to make a Nigerian boy hair curly using various methods, so you can find the one that works best for you.

How To

Method 1: Twist-Out

One of the easiest ways to make your hair curly is by doing a twist-out. Here's how:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a styling product, like a curl defining cream, to your hair.
  3. Divide your hair into small sections.
  4. Twist each section of hair tightly, starting from the root and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Allow your hair to air dry or use a hair dryer on a low heat setting.
  6. Once your hair is dry, unravel the twists, and voila! You have beautiful, bouncy curls.

Method 2: Perm

If you're looking for a more permanent solution, you can get a perm. Here's how:

  1. Choose the type of perm you want. There are several different types, including spiral, body, and digital.
  2. Visit a salon that specializes in perms.
  3. Prepare your hair by washing it thoroughly and avoiding any styling products or oils.
  4. Apply the perm solution to your hair, according to the instructions provided by your stylist.
  5. Allow the solution to set for the recommended amount of time.
  6. Rinse your hair thoroughly and apply a neutralizer to set the curls.
  7. Style your hair as desired.

Step-by-Step

Method 1: Twist-Out

  1. Start with clean, damp hair. This will help your twists hold better.
  2. Apply a styling product, like a curl defining cream, to your hair. This will help define your curls and hold them in place.
  3. Divide your hair into small sections. The smaller the sections, the tighter the curls will be.
  4. Twist each section of hair tightly, starting from the root and working your way down to the ends. Use your fingers to twist the hair, or use a comb to help you create a tighter twist.
  5. Allow your hair to air dry or use a hair dryer on a low heat setting. If you're in a rush, you can use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to speed up the drying process.
  6. Once your hair is dry, unravel the twists. Be gentle when unraveling the twists to avoid frizzing your hair.
  7. Style your hair as desired. You can use a hair pick to fluff out your curls and add volume.

Method 2: Perm

  1. Choose the type of perm you want. Spiral perms create tight, corkscrew curls, while body perms create looser waves. Digital perms use heat to create curls.
  2. Visit a salon that specializes in perms. Perming your hair at home can be dangerous and can result in damage to your hair.
  3. Prepare your hair by washing it thoroughly and avoiding any styling products or oils. Your hair should be completely dry before you begin perming.
  4. Apply the perm solution to your hair, according to the instructions provided by your stylist. Your stylist will section your hair and apply the solution to each section.
  5. Allow the solution to set for the recommended amount of time. This can take up to an hour, depending on the type of perm you're getting.
  6. Rinse your hair thoroughly and apply a neutralizer to set the curls. The neutralizer will stop the perming process and help set your curls.
  7. Style your hair as desired. You can use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to speed up the drying process.

Tips

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair before styling.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using hot styling tools.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends and keep it healthy.

FAQ

Can I make my hair curly without using heat or chemicals?

Yes! You can use methods like twist-outs, braids, and bantu knots to create curls without using heat or chemicals.

How long will my perm last?

Your perm will last for several months, depending on how fast your hair grows. However, the curls will start to loosen over time, so you may need to get a touch-up to maintain the look.

Is it safe to perm my hair?

Perming your hair can be safe if done correctly. However, if done incorrectly or too often, it can damage your hair and cause breakage.

Hair Care

Whether you choose to use heat, chemicals, or natural methods to make your hair curly, it's essential to take care of your hair to keep it healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Get regular trims to remove split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

By following these tips and using our methods to make your hair curly, you can achieve the look you want without damaging your hair.
