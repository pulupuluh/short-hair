How To Straighten Naturally Curly Hair Without Damaging It

Written by Robby Apr 10, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that straightening it can be a challenge. Not only do you have to deal with the frizz and flyaways that come with curly hair, but you also have to worry about damaging your hair in the process. Fortunately, there are ways to straighten your hair without causing damage. Here's how to straighten naturally curly hair without damaging it.

Description

Straightening naturally curly hair can be a real challenge. It is a delicate process that requires careful attention to detail to avoid damaging the hair. In this article, we will give you step-by-step instructions on how to straighten naturally curly hair without causing any damage.

How To

The first step to straightening naturally curly hair is to wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo. This will help to prevent any damage to your hair while you are straightening it. After you have washed your hair, apply a heat protectant to your hair to protect it from the heat of the straightener.

Next, blow dry your hair using a round brush. This will help to smooth out your hair and make it easier to straighten. Once your hair is dry, section it off and clip it up.

Starting with the bottom section, take a small section of hair and run the straightener through it. Be sure to use a low heat setting and go slowly to avoid damaging your hair. Repeat this process with each section of hair until your entire head is straightened.

Step by Step

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant to your hair to protect it from the heat of the straightener.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush to smooth out your hair and make it easier to straighten.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Section your hair off and clip it up.

Step 5: Straighten Your Hair

Starting with the bottom section, take a small section of hair and run the straightener through it. Repeat this process with each section of hair until your entire head is straightened.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you straighten your hair without damaging it:

  • Use a low heat setting on your straightener
  • Go slowly to avoid damaging your hair
  • Use a heat protectant to protect your hair from the heat
  • Blow dry your hair using a round brush to smooth it out

FAQ

Q: Can I straighten my hair without damaging it?

A: Yes, you can straighten your hair without damaging it. Just be sure to use a low heat setting, go slowly, and use a heat protectant.

Q: Should I use a sulfate-free shampoo?

A: Yes, using a sulfate-free shampoo can help to prevent damage to your hair while you are straightening it.

Q: How often can I straighten my hair?

A: It is best to limit how often you straighten your hair to avoid damaging it. Try to only straighten your hair once a week or less.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and prevent damage, be sure to take care of your hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo, avoid using heat tools too often, and use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can straighten your naturally curly hair without causing any damage.
