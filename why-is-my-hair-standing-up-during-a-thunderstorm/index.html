Why Is My Hair Standing Up During A Thunderstorm

Written by Robby May 18, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Have you ever experienced standing outside during a thunderstorm and feeling your hair stand up? You might have wondered what is happening to your hair, and if it is something to be worried about. In this article, we will explain why your hair stands up during a thunderstorm and provide tips on how to stay safe.

Description

During a thunderstorm, you may feel a tingling sensation on your skin or see your hair standing up. This happens because of the buildup of static electricity in the air. Thunderstorms are often accompanied by lightning strikes, which create an electrical field around them. This electrical field can cause electrical charges to accumulate on the ground and on objects on the ground, including your hair.

How to

If you are outside during a thunderstorm and feel your hair standing up, take immediate action to protect yourself. The buildup of static electricity in the air can create a dangerous situation if not handled correctly. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Get indoors

Find a safe place to shelter immediately. Stay away from trees, metal objects, and water. If you are outside and cannot find shelter, try to find a low-lying area and crouch down with your feet together. Avoid lying down on the ground.

Step 2: Wait it out

Wait until the thunderstorm passes before going back outside. Lightning can strike up to 10 miles away from the storm, so wait at least 30 minutes after the last thunderclap before venturing outside.

Tips

Here are some tips to stay safe during a thunderstorm:

  • Avoid using electronic devices and water during a thunderstorm
  • Stay away from windows and doors
  • Do not use a landline phone during a thunderstorm
  • Stay away from tall objects, such as trees and poles
  • Avoid swimming and boating during a thunderstorm

FAQ

What causes my hair to stand up during a thunderstorm?

The buildup of static electricity in the air during a thunderstorm can cause your hair to stand up. This happens because of the electrical charges that accumulate on your hair and other objects on the ground.

Is it dangerous if my hair stands up during a thunderstorm?

If your hair stands up during a thunderstorm, it is a sign that there is a buildup of static electricity in the air. This can create a dangerous situation if not handled correctly. Take immediate action to protect yourself by finding shelter and avoiding tall objects.

Hair Care

If you are concerned about the effects of static electricity on your hair, there are several steps you can take to prevent it. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using plastic combs and brushes, as they can create static electricity
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help prevent static buildup
  • Avoid over-drying your hair with a blow dryer
  • Wear natural fibers, such as cotton, to help reduce static electricity

Now that you know why your hair stands up during a thunderstorm, you can take steps to stay safe and protect yourself from the effects of static electricity. Remember to always take thunderstorms seriously and stay informed about weather conditions in your area.
