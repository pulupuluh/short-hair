How To Cut Curly Hair To Frame Your Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 31, 2023 Â· 8 min read
If you have curly hair, you know that it can be both a blessing and a curse. While it's beautiful and unique, it can also be difficult to style and manage. One of the best ways to make your curly hair look its best is to cut it in a way that frames your face. In this post, we'll show you how to cut curly hair to frame your face in a way that flatters your features and enhances your natural curls.

Description

Curly hair is often unpredictable, so it can be challenging to know how to cut it to achieve a certain look. However, if you want to frame your face with your curly locks, there are a few key things to keep in mind. First, you'll need to consider your face shape and the type of curls you have. From there, you can choose a cut that will flatter your features and enhance your natural texture.

How to Cut Curly Hair to Frame Your Face

Cutting curly hair can be intimidating, but with the right technique, it's easy to do at home. Here's our step-by-step guide to cutting curly hair to frame your face:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see your natural curl pattern and ensure that you're cutting evenly.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, use clips to section your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. This will help you to focus on one area at a time and ensure that you're cutting evenly.

Step 3: Cut the Front Sections

Start with the front sections, as these will frame your face. Take a small section of hair and twist it gently. Use hair cutting shears to trim the ends, being careful not to cut too much off. Repeat this process on the other side, making sure that both sides are even.

Step 4: Cut the Back Sections

After you've finished the front sections, move on to the back. Take a small section of hair and twist it gently. Use hair cutting shears to trim the ends, being careful not to cut too much off. Repeat this process on the other side, making sure that both sides are even.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've finished cutting all four sections, it's time to blend the layers. Take a small section of hair from the top and bottom layers and twist them together. Use hair cutting shears to trim any uneven ends. Repeat this process all over your head, blending the layers together.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Cutting curly hair can be tricky, so here are a few tips to keep in mind: - Use hair cutting shears, not regular scissors. This will ensure a clean cut and prevent split ends. - Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to take small sections and trim a little at a time. - Cut your hair when it's dry, not wet. This will help you to see your natural curl pattern and ensure that you're cutting evenly. - Consider going to a professional. If you're not confident in your cutting skills, it's always better to go to a stylist who specializes in curly hair.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair? A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, you should aim to cut your hair every 6-12 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain your style. Q: Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet? A: While it's possible to cut curly hair when it's wet, it's not recommended. Wet hair can be deceptive, and you may end up cutting more than you intended.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

In addition to cutting your hair to frame your face, it's essential to take good care of your curly locks. Here are a few tips: - Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and frizz. - Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and keep them hydrated. - Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls. - Sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent friction and frizz.

Conclusion

Cutting curly hair can be intimidating, but with the right technique, it's easy to do at home. By following our step-by-step guide, you can cut your hair to frame your face and enhance your natural curls. Remember to use the right tools, cut slowly and carefully, and consider going to a professional if you're not confident in your skills. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at cutting curly hair in no time!
