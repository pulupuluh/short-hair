How To Wear Clip In Extensions With Thin Hair

Written by Daniel Mar 15, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of your thin hair and want to add volume and length? Clip in extensions can be a great solution for thin hair, but wearing them can be tricky if you don't know how to do it properly. In this article, we will guide you on how to wear clip in extensions with thin hair and provide you with some tips and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Wear Clip in Extensions with Thin Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Wearing Clip in Extensions with Thin Hair

  5. Hair Care Tips for Clip in Extensions

How to Wear Clip in Extensions with Thin Hair

Clip in extensions are a great way to add volume and length to your hair, especially if you have thin hair. Here's how to wear clip in extensions with thin hair:

Choose the Right Extensions

The first step is to choose the right extensions. Make sure you choose clip in extensions that are made of high-quality human hair, which will blend in seamlessly with your natural hair. Synthetic extensions may look fake and can damage your hair.

Also, choose extensions that match your hair color and texture. You can always dye or style the extensions to match your hair, but it's easier if you choose extensions that are similar to your natural hair.

Section Your Hair

Before you start clipping in the extensions, section your hair. Use a rat-tail comb to create a horizontal parting at the back of your head, just above your ears. Clip the top section of your hair away using hair clips.

Clip in the Extensions

Start by clipping in the extensions at the bottom of your hairline. Take one weft of hair and clip it in just below the horizontal parting you created. Clip the weft in securely, making sure it's tight against your scalp.

Continue to clip in the wefts, working your way up to the top of your head. Use smaller wefts at the sides of your head and larger wefts at the back.

Blend the Extensions

Once you've clipped in all the extensions, blend them with your natural hair. Use a brush or comb to gently blend the extensions with your hair. You can also use a curling iron or flat iron to style your hair, making sure to blend the extensions with your natural hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to wear clip in extensions with thin hair:

  1. Choose the right extensions
  2. Section your hair
  3. Clip in the extensions
  4. Blend the extensions with your hair

Tips for Wearing Clip in Extensions with Thin Hair

Here are some tips for wearing clip in extensions with thin hair:

  • Choose extensions that are made of high-quality human hair
  • Choose extensions that match your hair color and texture
  • Use smaller wefts at the sides of your head and larger wefts at the back
  • Clip the wefts in securely, making sure they're tight against your scalp
  • Blend the extensions with your natural hair using a brush or comb
  • Use a curling iron or flat iron to style your hair and blend the extensions with your natural hair

FAQs

Can I wear clip in extensions with thin hair?

Yes, you can wear clip in extensions with thin hair. Just make sure you choose high-quality human hair extensions and clip them in securely.

How many wefts should I use?

The number of wefts you use depends on the look you want to achieve. For a natural look, start with two to three wefts and add more if needed.

Can clip in extensions damage my thin hair?

Clip in extensions are safe to use and won't damage your thin hair if you clip them in securely and follow proper hair care practices.

Hair Care Tips for Clip in Extensions

Here are some hair care tips for clip in extensions:

  • Brush your extensions gently to avoid tangling or damaging them
  • Wash your extensions with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools on your extensions too often
  • Store your extensions properly when not in use

By following these tips and techniques, you can wear clip in extensions with thin hair and achieve a fuller, more voluminous look. Just remember to choose high-quality extensions, clip them in securely, and blend them with your natural hair for a seamless look.
