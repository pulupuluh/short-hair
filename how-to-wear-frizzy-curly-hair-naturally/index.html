How To Wear Frizzy Curly Hair Naturally

Written by Oliver Mar 10, 2023 · 9 min read

Frizzy curly hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right techniques and products, you can embrace your natural curls and wear them with confidence. In this guide, we'll show you how to wear frizzy curly hair naturally and offer some tips for keeping your curls healthy and defined.

Description

Frizzy curly hair is characterized by curls that are undefined, dry, and prone to frizz. This type of hair can be difficult to style, and many people resort to using heat tools or chemical treatments to tame their curls. However, these methods can damage your hair and lead to further frizz and dryness.

How to Wear Frizzy Curly Hair Naturally

Wearing your frizzy curly hair naturally involves embracing your curls and working with them instead of against them. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Start with clean, conditioned hair.

Before styling your hair, make sure it's clean and conditioned. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner to nourish your curls and prevent dryness. Rinse your hair with cool water to help seal in moisture and prevent frizz.

Step 2: Apply a leave-in conditioner.

A leave-in conditioner can help hydrate your curls and protect them from frizz. Apply a small amount to your hair, focusing on the ends and the areas that tend to be the driest.

Step 3: Use a curl-enhancing product.

A curl-enhancing product, such as a curl cream or a gel, can help define your curls and prevent frizz. Apply it to your hair, scrunching it from the ends up to the roots, and let it air dry.

Step 4: Avoid touching your hair while it dries.

Touching your hair while it's drying can disrupt your curls and lead to frizz. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls without disturbing them.

Step 5: Scrunch out the crunch.

If you used a curl-enhancing gel, your hair may feel crunchy once it's dry. Scrunch your hair with your hands to break up the gel and loosen your curls, revealing soft, defined curls.

Tips for Wearing Frizzy Curly Hair Naturally

Here are some additional tips for wearing your frizzy curly hair naturally:

Tip 1: Don't brush or comb your curls.

Brushing or combing your curls can cause frizz and disrupt your curl pattern. Instead, use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair while it's wet.

Tip 2: Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase.

A cotton pillowcase can absorb moisture from your hair and cause frizz. Sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase can help your curls retain moisture and prevent frizz.

Tip 3: Avoid heat styling.

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and lead to further frizz and dryness. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and a low heat setting.

Tip 4: Deep condition your hair regularly.

Deep conditioning treatments can help restore moisture to your curls and prevent damage. Use a deep conditioner once a week or as needed to keep your curls healthy and hydrated.

FAQ

Q: Can I wear my frizzy curly hair naturally if it's short?

A: Absolutely! Short curly hair can be just as beautiful and natural as long curly hair. Follow the same steps for styling and use products that are appropriate for your hair length and texture.

Q: How can I prevent frizz on humid days?

A: Humidity can cause frizz in curly hair, but there are a few things you can do to prevent it. Use a lightweight curl-enhancing product, such as a mousse or a serum, and avoid touching your hair as much as possible. You can also wear your hair in a protective style, such as a bun or a braid, to keep it from being exposed to humidity.

Hair Care for Frizzy Curly Hair

Proper hair care is essential for managing frizzy curly hair. Here are some tips for keeping your curls healthy and defined:

Tip 1: Use a sulfate-free shampoo.

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, leading to dryness and frizz. Use a sulfate-free shampoo that is gentle on your curls.

Tip 2: Deep condition regularly.

Deep conditioning treatments can help restore moisture to your curls and prevent damage. Use a deep conditioner once a week or as needed to keep your curls healthy and hydrated.

Tip 3: Avoid harsh chemicals.

Chemical treatments, such as relaxers and perms, can damage your hair and lead to further frizz and dryness. Avoid using these treatments and opt for natural styling methods instead.

Tip 4: Protect your curls at night.

Wear a satin or silk bonnet or scarf to protect your curls while you sleep. This will help prevent frizz and keep your curls looking defined and healthy.

Tip 5: Trim your hair regularly.

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your curls looking their best. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks, or as needed.

By following these tips and techniques, you can wear your frizzy curly hair naturally and embrace your beautiful curls. Remember to be patient and experiment with different products and styling methods to find what works best for your hair.
