Best Product To Slick Back Black Women s Hair Top Picks And Tips

Written by Robby Jun 17, 2023 Â· 8 min read

When it comes to styling black women's hair, there are numerous options available. However, slicking back hair is one of the most popular looks among black women. This hairstyle gives a sleek and polished look that is perfect for any occasion. But what is the best product to slick back black women's hair? In this article, we will discuss the top picks and tips for achieving the perfect slicked-back look.

Table of Contents

  • Top Picks
  • How to Slick Back Black Women's Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Slicking Back Black Women's Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Slicked-Back Hair

Top Picks

Choosing the right product to slick back black women's hair can be a daunting task, especially with so many options available. Here are some top picks:

Gel

Gel is a popular choice for slicking back black women's hair. It provides a strong hold and a shiny finish. However, using too much gel can make hair look greasy and weigh it down.

Pomade

Pomade is a great option for those who want a more natural look. It provides a medium hold and a matte finish. Pomades are also easy to wash out and do not leave any residue.

Hair Wax

Hair wax is perfect for those who want a flexible hold. It provides a natural finish and is great for creating different styles. However, it can be difficult to wash out and can leave residue if not applied properly.

How to Slick Back Black Women's Hair

Slicking back black women's hair is a simple process that involves a few basic steps. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before slicking it back. This will help the product to hold better and prevent any buildup.

Step 2: Choose your product

Choose the product that best suits your hair type and the look you want to achieve. Gel, pomade, and hair wax are all great options.

Step 3: Apply the product

Take a small amount of product and rub it between your palms. Apply it evenly to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way to the tips. Make sure to distribute the product evenly and avoid using too much.

Step 4: Comb your hair back

Using a wide-tooth comb, comb your hair back in the direction you want it to go. Make sure to smooth out any bumps or flyaways.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to achieve the perfect slicked-back look:

Step 1: Prepare your hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly. If your hair is long, consider tying it back into a low ponytail to make it easier to slick back.

Step 2: Choose your product

Choose the product that best suits your hair type and the look you want to achieve.

Step 3: Apply the product

Take a small amount of product and rub it between your palms. Apply it evenly to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way to the tips. Make sure to distribute the product evenly and avoid using too much.

Step 4: Comb your hair back

Using a wide-tooth comb, comb your hair back in the direction you want it to go. Make sure to smooth out any bumps or flyaways.

Step 5: Secure your hair

Use bobby pins or hair clips to secure any loose strands. You can also use a hair tie to create a low ponytail.

Tips for Slicking Back Black Women's Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect slicked-back look:

  • Start with a small amount of product and gradually add more if needed.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Apply the product evenly to avoid any clumps or buildup.
  • Use a hair tie to create a low ponytail if your hair is long.
  • Secure any loose strands with bobby pins or hair clips.

FAQs

Q: Can I slick back my hair if it's not straight?

A: Yes, you can slick back curly, wavy, or coily hair. However, you may need to use a stronger hold product and more bobby pins or hair clips to keep your hair in place.

Q: How often should I wash my hair if I slick it back?

A: It's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent any buildup from the product.

Hair Care for Slicked-Back Hair

Slicking back your hair can put stress on your hairline and cause breakage if not done properly. Here are some tips to care for your hair:

  • Avoid slicking back your hair every day to give your hairline a break.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Avoid using too much product to prevent buildup and weigh down your hair.
  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent any buildup from the product.

In conclusion, the best product to slick back black women's hair depends on your hair type and the look you want to achieve. Gel, pomade, and hair wax are all great options. Follow the steps and tips above to achieve the perfect slicked-back look while also taking care of your hair.
