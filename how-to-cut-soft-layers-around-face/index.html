How To Cut Soft Layers Around Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank May 03, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're looking for a way to add some volume and movement to your hair, cutting soft layers around your face is a great option. This technique can work for any hair length and texture, and can help frame your face in a flattering way. In this article, we'll go over how to cut soft layers around your face in a relaxed, easy-to-understand way.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Soft Layers Around Face
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Soft Layers
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Soft Layers

How to Cut Soft Layers Around Face

Before we get into the specifics of how to cut soft layers around your face, it's important to understand what this technique involves. Essentially, you'll be cutting your hair at an angle to create layers that frame your face. The layers will be longer in the back and gradually get shorter towards the front, creating a soft, face-framing effect.

Here are the basic steps for cutting soft layers around your face:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is curly or wavy, straighten it before you begin cutting.
  2. Part your hair down the middle.
  3. Gather a section of hair at the crown of your head and comb it straight up. This will be your guide for the length of your layers.
  4. Using your guide section, cut a straight line across the back of your head. This will be the longest layer.
  5. Section off the hair on either side of your head, leaving the back layer loose.
  6. Take a small section of hair from the left side of your head and comb it straight out from your head. Hold the section between your index and middle fingers, with your fingers pointing towards the floor.
  7. Cut the hair at an angle, using your guide section as a reference. The hair should be slightly shorter in the front than in the back.
  8. Continue cutting small sections of hair on the left side of your head, working your way towards your face.
  9. Repeat steps 6-8 on the right side of your head.
  10. Check for any uneven sections and trim as needed.

Tips for Cutting Soft Layers

While cutting soft layers around your face can be a simple process, there are a few tips to keep in mind to ensure the best results:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Work in small sections to ensure even cutting.
  • Take your time and double-check your work to avoid any mistakes.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, consider getting it done by a professional.

FAQs

Here are some common questions people have about cutting soft layers around their face:

Can I cut soft layers in curly hair?

Yes, you can cut soft layers in curly hair. Make sure to straighten your hair before cutting to ensure even layers.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

If you make a mistake while cutting, don't panic. You can always trim the uneven section or go to a professional to fix it.

How often do I need to cut my soft layers?

The frequency of cutting your soft layers will depend on your hair growth and personal preference. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and volume of your layers.

Hair Care for Soft Layers

Caring for your hair after cutting soft layers is important to maintain the shape and health of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape of your layers.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve soft, face-framing layers that add volume and movement to your hair.
