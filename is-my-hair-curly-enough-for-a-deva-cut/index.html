Is My Hair Curly Enough For A Deva Cut

Written by Eveline Apr 08, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you considering getting a Deva cut but wondering if your hair is curly enough? This is a common concern among people with naturally curly hair. In this post, we will discuss whether or not your hair is curly enough for a Deva cut, how to prepare for the cut, and how to maintain your curls after the cut.

Description

A Deva cut is a specialized haircut for people with curly hair. It is designed to enhance the natural curl pattern of your hair, creating a more defined and voluminous look. Deva cuts are performed on dry hair, which allows the stylist to see the natural curl pattern and customize the cut to your unique hair texture.

How to

If you're considering getting a Deva cut, the first step is to find a stylist who specializes in cutting curly hair. Look for a stylist who is certified in the DevaCurl method, which is a specialized technique for cutting and styling curly hair.

Before your appointment, it's important to prepare your hair. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and allow it to air dry or diffuse it with a hair dryer. Avoid brushing or combing your hair before the cut, as this can disrupt the natural curl pattern.

During the cut, the stylist will carefully analyze your hair texture and curl pattern, and customize the cut to enhance your natural curls. They may use a combination of scissors and a razor to create layers and shape your curls.

Step by Step

1. Find a stylist who specializes in cutting curly hair.

2. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.

3. Allow your hair to air dry or diffuse it with a hair dryer.

4. Avoid brushing or combing your hair before the cut.

5. During the cut, the stylist will analyze your hair texture and curl pattern.

6. The stylist will customize the cut to enhance your natural curls.

7. They may use a combination of scissors and a razor to create layers and shape your curls.

Tips

1. Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as this can damage your curls.

2. Use a microfiber towel or t-shirt to dry your hair, as regular towels can cause frizz.

3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl-enhancing product to your hair before styling.

4. Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz.

5. Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can disrupt the curl pattern.

FAQ

Q: Can I get a Deva cut if my hair is only slightly curly?

A: Yes, you can still get a Deva cut if your hair is only slightly curly. The stylist will customize the cut to enhance your natural curl pattern.

Q: How often should I get a Deva cut?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain your curls. Generally, it's recommended to get a Deva cut every 3-4 months.

Hair Care

After your Deva cut, it's important to maintain your curls with proper hair care. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and apply a leave-in conditioner or curl-enhancing product to your hair before styling. Avoid using heat styling tools, as this can damage your curls. Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz, and avoid touching your hair too much.

In conclusion, if you have naturally curly hair, a Deva cut can enhance the natural curl pattern of your hair and create a more defined and voluminous look. With proper preparation, finding a certified stylist, and maintaining your curls with proper hair care, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls.
