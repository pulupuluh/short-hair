What To Do If Your Perm Isn t Curly Enough Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Jun 16, 2023 · 7 min read

Getting a perm is a great way to add curls and bounce to your hair, but what happens when your perm doesn't turn out as curly as you had hoped? Don't worry – it's not the end of the world. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you achieve the curly hair you desire.

Table of Contents

  • How to Fix a Perm That's Not Curly Enough
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Fixing Your Perm
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Curly Perm
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Permed Hair

How to Fix a Perm That's Not Curly Enough

There are a few different things you can do if your perm isn't curly enough:

  • Wait a few days: Sometimes, it takes a few days for the curls to fully form. Give it some time before you panic.
  • Get a touch-up: If it's been a few days and your curls still haven't formed, you can go back to the salon and ask for a touch-up. This will help to re-activate the perming solution.
  • Use a curling iron: If you don't want to go back to the salon, you can use a curling iron to add more curls to your hair. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.
  • Try a different perm solution: If you're not happy with the results of your perm, you can try a different perm solution next time.

Step-by-Step Guide to Fixing Your Perm

If you decide to try fixing your perm at home, here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup.
  2. Towel dry your hair and apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle and protect your hair.
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.
  4. Apply a curl-enhancing product to your hair, such as mousse or curl cream.
  5. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair. This will help to enhance your curls.
  6. If your curls still aren't curly enough, use a curling iron to add more curls to your hair. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.
  7. Finish with a hairspray to hold your curls in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your Curly Perm

Once you've achieved the curly hair of your dreams, you'll want to make sure you maintain it properly:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause frizz.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about permed hair:

How long does a perm last?

A perm typically lasts around 6 months, but this can vary depending on your hair type and how well you maintain your curls.

Can I color my permed hair?

Yes, you can color your permed hair, but it's best to wait at least a week after getting a perm before coloring your hair.

Can I straighten my permed hair?

Yes, you can straighten your permed hair, but it's important to use a heat protectant spray and to not straighten your hair too often, as this can cause damage.

Hair Care for Permed Hair

Permed hair requires special care to keep it healthy and curly:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve the curly hair of your dreams and maintain it properly. Remember to be patient and give your curls time to form, and if all else fails, don't be afraid to go back to the salon for a touch-up.
