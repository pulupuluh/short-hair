How To Make A Man Bun With Really Short Hair

Are you tired of having the same old hairstyle and want to try something new? A man bun might be what you're looking for! However, if you have really short hair, you might think that a man bun is out of reach. Fear not, because in this article, we'll show you how to make a man bun with really short hair.

How to Make a Man Bun with Really Short Hair

Making a man bun with really short hair can be a bit tricky, but it's definitely possible. Here's what you need to do:

1. Start with Clean Hair

Before attempting to make a man bun, make sure your hair is clean and free from any product buildup. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it healthy and easy to manage.

2. Use a Hair Tie

Grab a hair tie and tie your hair into a small ponytail at the back of your head. Make sure the hair tie is tight enough to hold your hair in place, but not too tight that it causes discomfort.

3. Twist and Tuck

Twist the ponytail and tuck it under the hair tie, creating a small bun. Use bobby pins to secure the bun in place if necessary.

4. Finish with Hairspray

Apply a light mist of hairspray to keep your man bun in place throughout the day. Avoid using too much hairspray as it can make your hair look greasy or stiff.

Step by Step Guide

If you're still having trouble making a man bun with really short hair, here's a step by step guide to help you out:

1. Comb Your Hair

Comb your hair backwards to create a neat and tidy look. Use a comb with wider teeth to avoid tugging or pulling on your hair.

2. Tie Your Hair into a Ponytail

Use a hair tie to tie your hair into a small ponytail at the back of your head. Make sure the hair tie is tight enough to hold your hair in place, but not too tight that it causes discomfort.

3. Twist the Ponytail

Twist the ponytail to create a small bun. Make sure the bun is positioned at the center of your head, not too high or too low.

4. Tuck the Ends

Tuck the ends of the hair under the hair tie to create a more polished look. Use bobby pins to secure the bun in place if necessary.

5. Finish with Hairspray

Apply a light mist of hairspray to keep your man bun in place throughout the day. Avoid using too much hairspray as it can make your hair look greasy or stiff.

Tips for Making a Man Bun with Really Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you make a man bun with really short hair:

  • Use a hair tie that matches your hair color to create a more natural look.
  • Avoid using too much product as it can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Experiment with different styles and accessories, such as headbands or scarves, to add some variety to your look.
  • Be patient and practice, as it may take a few tries to perfect your man bun.

FAQs

Can I make a man bun with really short hair?

Yes, it's possible to make a man bun with really short hair. Follow our step by step guide and use the right products and accessories to help you achieve the look.

Do I need to use hairspray to keep my man bun in place?

While hairspray can help keep your man bun in place, it's not necessary. You can use bobby pins or other hair accessories to secure your bun without using hairspray.

Can I wear a man bun to formal events?

Yes, you can wear a man bun to formal events as long as it's neat and tidy. Make sure to style your hair appropriately and choose the right accessories to complement your outfit.

Hair Care Tips for Man Buns

To keep your man bun looking healthy and stylish, here are some hair care tips you should follow:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers or straighteners, as it can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain a healthy look.
  • Use hair products, such as oils or serums, to help keep your hair soft and shiny.

Now that you know how to make a man bun with really short hair, it's time to give it a try! Remember to be patient and practice, and soon you'll be rocking a stylish and trendy man bun.
