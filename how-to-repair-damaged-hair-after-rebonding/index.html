How To Repair Damaged Hair After Rebonding

Written by Oliver Jul 11, 2023 · 8 min read

If you’ve recently undergone a hair rebonding treatment, you may be dealing with the aftermath of damaged hair. Rebonding can leave your locks dry, brittle, and prone to breakage, but don’t worry – there are steps you can take to repair and restore your hair’s health. In this article, we’ll walk you through how to repair damaged hair after rebonding, step by step.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Repair Damaged Hair After Rebonding
  2. Step by Step
  3. Tips
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care

How to Repair Damaged Hair After Rebonding

First things first – it’s important to understand why rebonding can cause damage to your hair. The process of rebonding involves using chemicals to break down the natural bonds in your hair, then restructuring them to create straighter locks. This can leave your hair dehydrated, weak, and damaged.

The good news is that repairing damaged hair is possible with the right care and treatment. Here are some steps you can take:

1. Trim Your Hair

If the damage to your hair is severe, you may need to consider cutting off the affected strands. This will help to prevent further damage and split ends, and also help your hair to grow back healthier and stronger.

2. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein is essential for repairing damaged hair, as it helps to rebuild the hair’s structure and strengthen it from within. Look for a protein treatment that contains keratin or amino acids, and use it once a week to help repair your hair.

3. Moisturize Your Hair

Dry hair is prone to breakage, so it’s important to keep your hair moisturized after a rebonding treatment. Use a moisturizing conditioner and hair mask regularly to help hydrate your hair and prevent further damage.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons can further damage already weakened hair. Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, and if you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

5. Be Gentle with Your Hair

When your hair is damaged, it’s more fragile and prone to breakage. Be gentle with your hair when brushing or combing it, and avoid tight hairstyles that can put stress on your hair.

Step by Step

Here’s a step-by-step guide to repairing damaged hair after rebonding:

Step 1: Trim Your Hair

If your hair is severely damaged, consider getting a trim to remove the affected strands.

Step 2: Use a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair once a week to help repair and strengthen your hair.

Step 3: Moisturize Your Hair

Use a moisturizing conditioner and hair mask regularly to help hydrate your hair and prevent further damage.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, and if you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Step 5: Be Gentle with Your Hair

Be gentle with your hair when brushing or combing it, and avoid tight hairstyles that can put stress on your hair.

Tips

  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, as it’s gentler on your locks.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and further damage to your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help protect your hair from damage.

FAQ

Can damaged hair be repaired?

Yes, damaged hair can be repaired with the right care and treatment.

How long does it take to repair damaged hair?

The length of time it takes to repair damaged hair depends on the extent of the damage. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months to see improvement.

What causes hair damage?

Hair damage can be caused by a variety of factors, including heat styling, chemical treatments, and environmental factors like sun exposure and pollution.

Hair Care

After a rebonding treatment, it’s important to take good care of your hair to prevent further damage and promote healing. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy, beautiful hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that’s formulated for your hair type.
  • Limit your use of heat styling tools, and always use a heat protectant spray.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet that’s rich in vitamins and minerals to help nourish your hair from within.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can repair and restore your locks to their former health and beauty after a rebonding treatment.
