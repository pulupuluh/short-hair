What Does Braided Hair Mean In The Bible

Written by Oliver Jul 28, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Have you ever wondered what does braided hair mean in the bible? Braided hair is mentioned several times in the bible, and it has both positive and negative connotations. In this blog post, we will explore the meaning of braided hair in the bible and its significance.

What Does Braided Hair Mean in the Bible?

Braided hair is mentioned in several passages in the bible, including 1 Timothy 2:9, 1 Peter 3:3, and Isaiah 3:24. In these passages, braided hair is associated with vanity, pride, and immorality. The women who braided their hair were seen as overly concerned with their appearance and lacking in spiritual depth.

On the other hand, there are also positive references to braided hair in the bible. In Song of Solomon 7:5, the bride describes herself as having hair that is "wavy and black as a raven" and is adorned with "braids of gold." Here, braided hair is seen as a symbol of beauty and adornment.

How to Braid Hair?

If you're interested in braiding your hair, there are several different types of braids to choose from. Here are some step-by-step instructions for braiding hair:

1. French Braid

To create a French braid, start by dividing your hair into three sections at the top of your head. Cross the right section over the middle section, then cross the left section over the middle section. Continue braiding in this way, adding more hair to each section as you go, until you reach the nape of your neck. Finish the braid with a regular three-strand braid and secure with an elastic band.

2. Dutch Braid

A Dutch braid is similar to a French braid, but instead of crossing the sections over the middle, you cross them under. Start by dividing your hair into three sections at the top of your head. Cross the right section under the middle section, then cross the left section under the middle section. Continue braiding in this way, adding more hair to each section as you go, until you reach the nape of your neck. Finish the braid with a regular three-strand braid and secure with an elastic band.

Tips for Braiding Hair

Here are some tips for braiding hair:

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Use a detangling brush or comb to remove any knots or tangles
  • Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to smooth the hair and reduce frizz
  • Use small, even sections of hair for a neater braid
  • Secure the end of the braid with an elastic band or hair tie to prevent it from unraveling

FAQs

1. Is braided hair a sin?

No, braided hair is not a sin in and of itself. However, the bible does caution against being overly concerned with outward appearance and neglecting one's spiritual life.

2. Why is braided hair associated with vanity?

Braided hair was often seen as a sign of wealth and status in ancient times. Women who were able to afford elaborate braids and adornments were often seen as vain and overly concerned with their appearance.

Hair Care for Braided Hair

If you choose to wear braids, it's important to take proper care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips for caring for braided hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent buildup and keep your scalp healthy
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your braids, as this can cause damage to your hair and scalp
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized
  • Cover your head with a satin or silk scarf at night to prevent frizz and breakage

In conclusion, braided hair has both positive and negative connotations in the bible. While it can be seen as a symbol of vanity and pride, it can also be a beautiful adornment. If you choose to wear braids, be sure to take proper care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage.
