How To Sleep With Curly Hair Wet Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Jun 24, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that it can be a struggle to keep it looking its best. One of the biggest challenges is figuring out how to sleep with curly hair that's wet. Sleeping on wet hair can cause frizz and damage, which can be especially frustrating if you've just spent time styling your hair. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for how to sleep with curly hair wet, so you can wake up with beautiful, defined curls.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Sleep with Curly Hair Wet
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Sleeping with Wet Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

How to Sleep with Curly Hair Wet

Before we get started, it's important to note that sleeping with wet hair can cause damage and breakage, especially if you toss and turn in your sleep. However, we understand that sometimes it's necessary to sleep with wet hair, so here are some tips to minimize damage:

1. Use a Microfiber Towel

Before you go to bed, gently squeeze excess water from your hair using a microfiber towel. Microfiber towels are gentler on hair than regular towels, and they absorb water more efficiently, so your hair will dry faster.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair before you go to bed. This will help to keep your hair moisturized and prevent friction and breakage while you sleep.

3. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to reduce friction and prevent breakage while you sleep. Silk is also gentler on hair than cotton, which can cause frizz and tangles.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for how to sleep with curly hair wet:

1. Squeeze Excess Water from Your Hair

Use a microfiber towel to gently squeeze excess water from your hair. Don't rub your hair, as this can cause breakage and frizz.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly through your hair.

3. Plop Your Hair

Plopping is a technique where you use a t-shirt or microfiber towel to wrap your hair and encourage curl formation. To plop your hair, place a t-shirt or microfiber towel on a flat surface, flip your hair over onto the towel, and wrap the ends of the towel around your head. Secure the towel in place using a hair tie or clip.

4. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleep on a silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent breakage. If you don't have a silk pillowcase, you can wrap your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet to protect it while you sleep.

Tips for Sleeping with Wet Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you sleep with wet curly hair:

1. Don't Sleep with Your Hair Loose

Sleeping with your hair loose can cause tangles and frizz. To keep your curls defined, either plop your hair or braid it before you go to bed.

2. Use a Diffuser Attachment

If you need to dry your hair before you go to bed, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair gently without causing frizz.

3. Avoid Hair Ties with Metal

Hair ties with metal can cause breakage and damage, so use hair ties without metal when you sleep.

FAQs

Q: Is it bad to sleep with wet curly hair?

A: Sleeping with wet curly hair can cause damage and breakage, especially if you toss and turn in your sleep. However, if you need to sleep with wet hair, there are ways to minimize damage.

Q: How do I prevent frizz when sleeping with wet curly hair?

A: To prevent frizz, use a microfiber towel to gently squeeze excess water from your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner, and sleep on a silk pillowcase.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for curly hair:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Curly hair is often dry and prone to damage, so use a gentle shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Condition Your Hair Regularly

Conditioner is essential for keeping curly hair moisturized and healthy. Use a deep conditioner once a week to give your hair an extra boost of moisture.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause damage and breakage, so avoid using hot tools on your hair. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can sleep with wet curly hair without causing damage or frizz. Remember to be gentle with your hair, and always use products and tools that are designed for curly hair.
