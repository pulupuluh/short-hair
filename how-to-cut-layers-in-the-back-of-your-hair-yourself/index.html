How To Cut Layers In The Back Of Your Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Feb 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to add some dimension and movement to your hair, layers are a great way to do it. But what about cutting the layers in the back of your hair yourself? It can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process step by step so you can achieve the look you want without having to leave your house.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You'll Need
  2. Preparing Your Hair
  3. Sectioning Your Hair
  4. Cutting Your Layers
  5. Blending Your Layers
  6. Finishing Touches
  7. Tips and Tricks
  8. FAQs
  9. Hair Care After Cutting Layers
  10. Conclusion

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the right tools on hand:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips to section your hair
  • Mirror (preferably a handheld and a larger one to see the back of your head)

Preparing Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. Comb through any tangles and make sure your hair is smooth and even.

It's important to keep in mind that cutting wet hair can lead to uneven results, so avoid cutting your hair when it's wet. If you need to, you can lightly mist your hair with water to make it easier to work with.

Sectioning Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections. You can do this by parting your hair down the middle and then creating a horizontal part from ear to ear. Clip the top section out of the way.

Take a small section of hair at the back of your head and comb it straight down. This will be your guide for cutting the rest of your hair.

Cutting Your Layers

Hold your scissors vertically and make small, upward snips into the hair. Start at the bottom of the section and work your way up, using the hair you've already cut as a guide for the next section.

Remember to take your time and cut small sections at a time. You can always go back and cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.

Blending Your Layers

Once you've cut your layers, it's time to blend them. Take a comb and run it through your hair, gently pulling the layers down. This will help to blend them together and create a more natural look.

Finishing Touches

After you've blended your layers, it's time to make any final adjustments. Check your hair in the mirror from different angles to make sure everything is even and looks the way you want it to.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with small snips and work your way up if you want to take off more.
  • Take breaks if you need to â cutting your own hair can be tiring!
  • Use a handheld mirror to help you see the back of your head.

FAQs

Can I cut layers in my curly hair?

Yes, you can! Just make sure to cut your hair while it's dry and use a technique called "twist cutting" to create layers that work with your curls.

What if I mess up?

Don't worry â hair grows back! If you're not happy with the results, you can always go to a professional to fix it.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After you've cut your layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep your layers looking fresh.

Conclusion

Cutting layers in the back of your hair yourself can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. Remember to take your time, start with small snips, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. With a little practice, you'll be able to achieve the look you want without having to leave your house.
