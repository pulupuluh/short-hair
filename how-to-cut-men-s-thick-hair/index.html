How To Cut Men s Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy May 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thick hair can be a challenge to cut, especially if you're not experienced with cutting men's hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut for your client. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut men's thick hair, along with some tips and FAQs.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Men's Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Men's Thick Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Men's Thick Hair

How to Cut Men's Thick Hair

The first step in cutting men's thick hair is to make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a good pair of scissors, clippers, and a comb. You may also want to have a spray bottle of water handy to wet the hair as needed.

Next, you'll need to decide on the length and style of the haircut. It's important to listen to your client's preferences and make sure you understand what they want. Once you have a clear idea of the desired style, you can begin cutting.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Before you begin cutting, make sure the hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut and style, so it's best to start with dry hair. Use a towel or blow dryer to dry the hair if needed.

Step 2: Comb the Hair and Section It Off

Use a comb to section off the hair into manageable sections. Start at the back of the head and work your way forward, combing the hair as you go. This will help you see where you need to cut and ensure that you don't miss any sections.

Step 3: Start Cutting with Scissors

Begin cutting the hair with scissors, starting at the back and working your way forward. Use the comb to hold the hair in place and cut small sections at a time. Be sure to cut at an angle to create layers and texture.

Step 4: Use Clippers for the Sides and Back

Once you've cut the top and front of the hair with scissors, use clippers to trim the sides and back. Use a guard to ensure that you don't cut the hair too short, and work in small sections to ensure an even cut.

Step 5: Blend the Hairline

Use the clippers to blend the hairline on the sides and back. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to create a natural-looking fade.

Step 6: Finish with Scissors

Once you've blended the hairline, use scissors to finish the haircut. Trim any stray hairs and make sure the hair is even on all sides.

Tips for Cutting Men's Thick Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting men's thick hair:

  • Use sharp scissors and clippers to ensure a clean cut.
  • Work in small sections to ensure even cutting.
  • Use a comb to hold the hair in place while cutting.
  • Take breaks to rest your hands and avoid making mistakes.
  • Listen to your client's preferences and adjust the cut as needed.

FAQs

Q: How often should men with thick hair get a haircut?

A: It depends on the desired length and style of the haircut, but most men with thick hair should get a trim every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style of the cut.

Q: How can I prevent frizz when cutting men's thick hair?

A: Use a spray bottle of water to keep the hair damp while cutting, and avoid using too much product. You can also use a smoothing serum or oil to tame frizz after the cut.

Hair Care for Men's Thick Hair

To keep men's thick hair looking its best, it's important to use the right products and techniques. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often, as they can damage the hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep the hair soft and shiny.
  • Trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can give your clients a great haircut that will keep them coming back for more. Remember to listen to their preferences, use the right tools, and take your time to ensure a clean and even cut.
