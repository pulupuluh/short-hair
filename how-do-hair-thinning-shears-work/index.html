How Do Hair Thinning Shears Work A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Apr 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning shears are a must-have tool for any hairdresser or stylist. They are used to create texture and remove bulk from thick hair, making it easier to manage and style. If you're wondering how do hair thinning shears work, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll explain everything you need to know about thinning shears and how to use them.

What Are Hair Thinning Shears?

Hair thinning shears, also known as texturizing shears, are scissors with teeth-like blades that are used to remove bulk from the hair. They are designed to cut only some of the hair strands, leaving others intact, which creates texture and reduces weight. Thinning shears are commonly used on thick, coarse hair, but can also be used on fine hair to add volume and texture.

How Do Hair Thinning Shears Work?

Thinning shears work by cutting some of the hair strands while leaving others uncut. The teeth on the blade of the shear are designed to remove some of the hair with each snip, creating a layered effect. The more teeth the shear has, the less hair it will remove with each snip. This allows the stylist to control the amount of hair that is removed and the level of texture that is created.

Step by Step Guide: How to Use Hair Thinning Shears

Now that you know what thinning shears are and how they work, let's take a look at how to use them. Follow these steps for best results:

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair that you want to thin out. Use clips to hold the rest of the hair out of the way.

Step 2: Hold the Thinning Shears Correctly

Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle to the hair, with the teeth facing up. This will ensure that the blade cuts only some of the hair strands and leaves the rest intact.

Step 3: Snip the Hair

Take a small section of hair between the thinning shears and snip the hair once or twice, depending on how much hair you want to remove. Move the shears down the section of hair and repeat the process until you have thinned out the desired amount of hair.

Step 4: Repeat on Other Sections

Repeat the process on other sections of hair that you want to thin out, making sure to blend the sections that you have already cut with the uncut sections for a seamless look.

Tips for Using Hair Thinning Shears

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using hair thinning shears:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and thin it out gradually. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's gone.
  • Be careful not to thin out too much hair, or the hair may look stringy and uneven.
  • Use thinning shears sparingly on curly hair, as they can cause the hair to frizz.
  • Always clean and oil your thinning shears after use to keep them in good condition.

FAQ About Hair Thinning Shears

Q: Can hair thinning shears be used on all hair types?

A: Thinning shears are best suited for thick, coarse hair, but can also be used on fine hair to add volume and texture.

Q: Can thinning shears damage hair?

A: Thinning shears can damage hair if used incorrectly or if too much hair is removed. It's important to use them sparingly and gradually thin out the hair.

Q: How often should thinning shears be sharpened?

A: Thinning shears should be sharpened every six months to a year, depending on how often they are used.

Hair Care Tips for Thinning Hair

If you have thinning hair, there are several hair care tips that can help you maintain healthy hair:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals and heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is designed for thinning hair.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to improve circulation and promote hair growth.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles that pull on the hair, such as braids and ponytails.

By following these tips and using hair thinning shears correctly, you can achieve healthy, manageable hair that looks and feels great.
