How To Get Back Of Hair Straight Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Mar 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're struggling with getting the back of your hair straight, you're not alone. Many people find it challenging to straighten the hair at the back of their heads. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve sleek, straight hair all over your head. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to get the back of hair straight.

  1. How to Get Back of Hair Straight
  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Get Back of Hair Straight

Before we dive into the steps, it's essential to understand that straightening the back of your hair requires patience and practice. Here are some ways to get started:

1. Use a Flat Iron

A flat iron is a popular tool for straightening hair. It's easy to use and can achieve a straight, sleek look. Start by sectioning your hair and working on one section at a time. Clamp the flat iron at the roots and slowly glide it towards the ends, following the natural curve of your head. Repeat until all sections are straightened.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow-drying your hair can also help straighten it. Use a round brush to pull the hair straight as you blow-dry. Start at the roots and work your way to the ends, making sure to dry each section thoroughly.

3. Use a Straightening Brush

A straightening brush is a hybrid of a brush and a flat iron. It's an excellent tool for straightening the back of your hair as it's easy to use and can cover a large area quickly. Start by sectioning your hair and working on one section at a time. Glide the brush from the roots to the ends, following the natural curve of your head. Repeat until all sections are straightened.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to straighten the back of your hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a straightening shampoo and conditioner to prep it for styling.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent heat damage.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable sections using hair clips or elastic bands.

Step 4: Start Straightening

Start straightening the back of your hair using a flat iron, straightening brush, or blow dryer. Work on one section at a time, starting from the roots and gliding towards the ends.

Step 5: Repeat

Repeat the process until all sections are straightened.

Tips for Getting Straight Hair

Here are some tips for getting straight hair:

1. Use the Right Tools

Invest in a good quality flat iron, straightening brush, or blow dryer to achieve the best results.

2. Use Heat Protectant

Always use a heat protectant spray to prevent heat damage to your hair.

3. Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable sections to make the straightening process more manageable.

4. Use the Right Technique

Use the right technique when straightening your hair, whether it's using a flat iron, straightening brush, or blow dryer.

FAQs

Q: Can I straighten my hair every day?

A: It's not recommended to straighten your hair every day as it can cause heat damage and breakage. Try to limit straightening to a few times a week.

Q: How long will straight hair last?

A: Straight hair can last for a few days, depending on your hair type and the weather conditions.

Q: Can I straighten my hair if it's wet?

A: It's not recommended to straighten your hair when it's wet as it can cause damage. Always make sure your hair is dry before straightening.

Hair Care for Straight Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your straight hair:

1. Use a Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent your hair from drying out and becoming damaged.

2. Condition Regularly

Condition your hair regularly to keep it soft and shiny.

3. Avoid Over-Washing

Avoid over-washing your hair as it can strip the natural oils from your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle.

4. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve straight hair all over your head, including the back. Remember to be patient and practice, and you'll soon be a pro at straightening the back of your hair.
