How To Do Dreads With Natural Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 28, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you are looking for a unique and stylish hairstyle, then dreadlocks may be the perfect choice for you. Dreads are a popular hairstyle that is easy to maintain and can last for years. In this step-by-step guide, we will show you how to do dreads with natural hair.

Description

Dreadlocks, also known as dreads, are a hairstyle that involves twisting and matting hair to form a rope-like strand. This hairstyle originated from African culture and has become popular all over the world. Dreads can be done with both natural and relaxed hair. In this guide, we will focus on how to do dreads with natural hair.

How to Do Dreads with Natural Hair

Before you start, make sure your hair is clean and free of any products. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections using a rat-tail comb. The sections should be about one inch wide. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 2: Apply Product

Apply a locking gel or wax to each section of hair. This will help the hair to lock and form into dreads.

Step 3: Twist the Hair

Take one section of hair and twist it tightly in one direction. Use your fingers to roll the hair into a rope-like strand. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 4: Palm Roll

After twisting each section of hair, use your palms to roll the hair in the opposite direction. This will help to tighten the dreads and make them more compact.

Step 5: Maintain Your Dreads

To maintain your dreads, you should wash them regularly with a residue-free shampoo. You can also use a dreadlock conditioner to keep them moisturized. Avoid using any products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out the hair and cause damage.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when doing dreads with natural hair:

  • Use a locking gel or wax that is specifically designed for dreads.
  • Make sure your hair is completely dry before starting the process.
  • Be patient, as the process can take several hours depending on the length and thickness of your hair.
  • Avoid using any heat styling tools, as this can damage the hair and cause breakage.
  • Cover your hair with a scarf or cap at night to prevent frizz and maintain the shape of your dreads.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about doing dreads with natural hair:

Can I do dreads with short hair?

Yes, you can do dreads with short hair. However, the process may take longer and the dreads may be thinner than with longer hair.

Can I wash my hair with dreads?

Yes, you should wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo. This will help to keep your dreads clean and healthy.

How often should I retwist my dreads?

You should retwist your dreads every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and prevent frizz.

Hair Care

To keep your dreads healthy, you should follow a regular hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Use a dreadlock conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using any products that contain alcohol or harsh chemicals.
  • Cover your hair with a scarf or cap at night to prevent frizz.
  • Trim any loose or frayed ends to prevent breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve beautiful and healthy dreads with natural hair. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products.
