How To Properly Layer Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 25, 2023 · 9 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it’s beautiful and unique, it can also be challenging to manage, especially when it comes to layering. Layering is essential for adding depth and dimension to your curls while reducing volume and frizz. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps of properly layering curly hair, including tips and techniques for achieving the best results.

Table of Contents

  • How to Layer Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Layering Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Layer Curly Hair

The key to layering curly hair is to work with the natural texture of your curls. Curly hair tends to have a lot of volume, which can make it difficult to create a layered look without creating a frizzy mess. To achieve the best results, follow these steps:

Step 1: Consult with a Professional Stylist

Before attempting to layer your curly hair on your own, it’s best to consult with a professional stylist who has experience working with curly hair. They’ll be able to assess your hair type and texture and recommend the best layering technique for your needs. In addition, they’ll be able to offer tips and tricks for maintaining your curly hair at home.

Step 2: Use the Right Tools

When layering curly hair, it’s important to use the right tools to avoid damaging your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently. Avoid using a brush, as it can break your curls and cause frizz. You may also want to invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors, as regular scissors can create uneven ends.

Step 3: Start with Dry, Styled Hair

When layering curly hair, it’s best to start with dry, styled hair. This will allow you to see the natural shape of your curls and prevent over-layering. If your hair is wet, it will be more difficult to see the natural shape of your curls, and you may end up cutting too much.

Step 4: Cut from the Bottom Up

When layering curly hair, it’s best to cut from the bottom up. This will help you achieve a more natural look and prevent the top layer from looking too heavy. Start by dividing your hair into sections and cutting the bottom layer first. Use your fingers or a comb to hold each section in place while you cut.

Step 5: Work with Your Natural Curl Pattern

When layering curly hair, it’s important to work with your natural curl pattern. This means not cutting your hair too short or too straight. Instead, follow the natural shape of your curls and cut at an angle to create a more natural look.

Step 6: Finish with a Trim

Once you’ve finished layering your curly hair, finish with a trim to ensure that the ends are even. This will help prevent split ends and breakage and keep your curls looking healthy and bouncy. Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs and create a more polished look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to layering curly hair:

  1. Consult with a professional stylist
  2. Use the right tools
  3. Start with dry, styled hair
  4. Cut from the bottom up
  5. Work with your natural curl pattern
  6. Finish with a trim

Tips for Layering Curly Hair

Here are some tips for achieving the best results when layering curly hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and cut gradually to avoid over-layering.
  • Use a mirror to help you see the back of your head and make sure that your layers are even.
  • Don’t cut your hair too short, as this can cause your curls to lose their shape.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can cause damage to curly hair.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to help prevent frizz.

FAQs

How often should I layer my curly hair?

The frequency of layering your curly hair depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much volume you want to maintain. Generally, it’s recommended to layer curly hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain a healthy shape and prevent split ends.

Can I layer my curly hair at home?

While it’s possible to layer your curly hair at home, it’s best to consult with a professional stylist before attempting it on your own. This will ensure that you’re using the right technique and tools and prevent any damage to your hair.

What are some common mistakes to avoid when layering curly hair?

Some common mistakes to avoid when layering curly hair include cutting too much at once, cutting your hair too short or too straight, and using a brush instead of a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

To keep your curly hair healthy and looking its best, it’s important to follow a regular hair care routine. This includes:

  • Washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoiding heat styling tools
  • Using a diffuser when blow-drying your hair
  • Avoiding harsh chemicals and dyes
  • Trimming your hair regularly to prevent split ends

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, healthy, and well-layered curly hair that will turn heads wherever you go.
