How To Choose The Glasses With Face Shape

Choosing the right glasses can be a daunting task, especially if you don't know where to start. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll guide you through the process of finding the perfect glasses for your face shape. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Before we dive into the details, let's first understand why it's essential to choose glasses according to your face shape. Wearing glasses that don't complement your face shape can make your overall look unbalanced and unflattering. On the other hand, choosing the right glasses can enhance your features and make you look more stylish.

How to

The first step in choosing the right glasses is to determine your face shape. There are six common face shapes: round, oval, square, heart, diamond, and triangle. You can determine your face shape by looking at yourself in the mirror and tracing the outline of your face with a lipstick or eyeliner.

Once you know your face shape, you can start looking for glasses that complement it. Here's a quick guide:

Round Face

If you have a round face, opt for glasses that have angular or geometric frames. Rectangular or square frames can help balance out the roundness of your face. Avoid round or oval frames as they can make your face look even rounder.

Oval Face

Oval faces are versatile and can pull off almost any frame shape. Look for glasses that are as wide as the widest part of your face and avoid frames that are too large or too small.

Square Face

If you have a square face, choose glasses that have soft curves and rounded edges. Oval or round frames can help soften the angles of your face. Avoid square or rectangular frames that can make your face look boxy.

Heart Face

Heart-shaped faces have a wider forehead and a narrower chin. Look for glasses that have wider bottom halves and narrower top halves. Cat-eye or butterfly frames can complement your face shape. Avoid glasses that are too heavy on the top.

Diamond Face

Diamond faces have high cheekbones and a narrow forehead and chin. Look for glasses that have oval or round frames to soften your face's angles. Avoid glasses that have sharp angles or heavy detailing on the brow line.

Triangle Face

Triangle faces have a wider jawline and a narrower forehead. Look for glasses that have wider top halves and narrower bottom halves. Cat-eye or semi-rimless frames can complement your face shape. Avoid glasses that are too heavy on the bottom.

Step by Step

Now that you know which frames to choose according to your face shape let's walk through the process step by step:

  1. Determine your face shape by tracing it in the mirror.
  2. Research which frame shapes complement your face shape.
  3. Try on different frames to see which ones look best on you.
  4. Make sure the frames fit your face correctly and don't slide down your nose or pinch your temples.
  5. Consider the color and material of the frames. Choose a color that complements your skin tone and hair color.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing glasses:

  • Consider your lifestyle when choosing glasses. If you're active, choose frames that are more durable and lightweight.
  • Think about the occasion when choosing glasses. If you're wearing glasses to a formal event, choose frames that are more elegant and sophisticated.
  • If you're unsure about which frames to choose, ask for a second opinion from a friend or family member.

FAQ

What if I have a combination of face shapes?

If you have a combination of face shapes, choose glasses that complement the dominant features of your face. For example, if you have a heart-shaped face with a prominent jawline, choose glasses that complement your jawline instead of your forehead.

Can I wear glasses if I have short hair?

Absolutely! Glasses can complement any hairstyle. If you have short hair, consider choosing glasses that have thicker or bolder frames to make a statement.

Do I have to follow the rules?

No, you don't have to follow the rules. Ultimately, you should choose glasses that make you feel confident and comfortable. If you want to wear round frames with a round face, go for it!

Hair Care

Wearing glasses can sometimes cause your hair to get tangled or flattened. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking great:

  • Use a volumizing spray or mousse to add volume to your hair.
  • Consider getting a haircut that complements your glasses.
  • Avoid wearing your glasses on the top of your head as it can create kinks in your hair.
  • Use a hair tie or clip to keep your hair away from your face.

Choosing the right glasses doesn't have to be complicated. By following these simple steps, you can find the perfect frames that complement your face shape and enhance your features. Happy shopping!
