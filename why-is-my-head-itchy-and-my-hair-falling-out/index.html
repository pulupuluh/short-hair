Why Is My Head Itchy And My Hair Falling Out

Written by Petter Apr 19, 2023 · 9 min read

If you're experiencing an itchy scalp and hair loss, you're not alone. This is a common problem that affects many people, both men and women. There are several reasons why your head may be itchy and your hair may be falling out, and in this article, we'll explore some of the most common causes and what you can do about them.

Table of Contents:

  • Dry Scalp
  • Dandruff
  • Psoriasis
  • Eczema
  • Hair Products
  • Diet
  • Stress
  • Hormones
  • Medications
  • Medical Conditions

Dry Scalp

If your scalp is dry, it can become itchy and flaky. This can lead to hair loss as well, as the dryness can cause the hair to become brittle and break off. Dry scalp can be caused by a variety of factors, including harsh shampoos, cold weather, and a lack of moisture. To treat dry scalp, try using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot water when washing your hair. You may also want to try a scalp treatment or oil to help moisturize the scalp.

Dandruff

Dandruff is a common condition that causes flaky skin on the scalp. It can be itchy and uncomfortable, and can also lead to hair loss if left untreated. Dandruff may be caused by a variety of factors, including a fungal infection, stress, or hormonal changes. To treat dandruff, try using an anti-dandruff shampoo and conditioner. You may also want to try a scalp treatment or oil to help soothe the scalp and reduce itching.

Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a chronic skin condition that can affect the scalp. It causes red, scaly patches on the skin, and can be itchy and uncomfortable. Psoriasis can also lead to hair loss if left untreated. To treat psoriasis, you may need to use a medicated shampoo or topical medication prescribed by your doctor. You may also want to try a scalp treatment or oil to help soothe the scalp and reduce itching.

Eczema

Eczema is a skin condition that can affect the scalp, causing redness, itching, and flaking. It can also lead to hair loss if left untreated. Eczema may be caused by a variety of factors, including stress, allergies, and irritants. To treat eczema, you may need to use a medicated shampoo or topical medication prescribed by your doctor. You may also want to try a scalp treatment or oil to help soothe the scalp and reduce itching.

Hair Products

If you're using hair products that contain harsh chemicals or ingredients that your scalp is sensitive to, it can lead to itching and hair loss. To prevent this, try using natural or organic hair products, and avoid products that contain sulfates or alcohol. You may also want to try a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup from your hair and scalp.

Diet

Your diet can also play a role in the health of your scalp and hair. If you're not getting enough nutrients, it can lead to dryness, itching, and hair loss. Make sure to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, and protein. You may also want to take a multivitamin or supplement to ensure you're getting all the nutrients you need.

Stress

Stress can also have an impact on the health of your scalp and hair. When you're stressed, your body produces hormones that can cause inflammation and other issues. This can lead to itching, dryness, and hair loss. To manage stress, try practicing relaxation techniques like yoga or meditation, and make sure to get enough sleep each night.

Hormones

Hormonal changes can also affect the health of your scalp and hair. This is especially true for women, who may experience hair loss or thinning during menopause or pregnancy. To manage hormonal changes, you may want to talk to your doctor about hormone replacement therapy or other treatments.

Medications

Some medications can also cause itching and hair loss as a side effect. If you're experiencing these symptoms after starting a new medication, talk to your doctor about the possibility of switching to a different medication or adjusting your dosage.

Medical Conditions

Finally, there are several medical conditions that can cause itching and hair loss. These include autoimmune disorders, thyroid problems, and infections. If you're experiencing these symptoms and are unsure of the cause, it's important to talk to your doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Tips for Healthy Hair and Scalp:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair.
  • Use a conditioner to help moisturize your hair and scalp.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain harsh chemicals or irritants.
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, and protein.
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques and getting enough sleep.

Frequently Asked Questions:

  • Can stress cause hair loss?
  • Yes, stress can cause hair loss by disrupting the normal growth cycle of hair.

  • What are some natural remedies for itchy scalp?
  • Some natural remedies for itchy scalp include tea tree oil, coconut oil, and aloe vera.

  • How can I prevent hair loss?
  • You can prevent hair loss by taking good care of your hair and scalp, and by managing any underlying medical conditions.

Hair Care for Healthier Hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to avoid pulling on your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails that can pull on your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun with a hat or scarf.
