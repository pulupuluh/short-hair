How To Give Yourself A Good Fade Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

A good fade can make all the difference in your haircut. Whether you're looking for a classic or modern style, a fade is always a great choice. But what if you can't make it to the barbershop? With the right tools and a little patience, you can give yourself a great fade at home. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a good fade.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Blending Your Hair
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for a Perfect Fade
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Your Fade

Tools You'll Need

Before you start, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need:

  • Clippers with adjustable guards
  • Mirror
  • Comb
  • Haircutting scissors
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Haircutting spray bottle (optional)

Once you have everything you need, you're ready to start.

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel drying it. Comb your hair in the direction you want it to lay. If you have longer hair, you may want to use a haircutting spray bottle to dampen your hair. This will make it easier to cut.

Cutting Your Hair

Begin cutting your hair with the longest guard on your clippers. Start at the bottom of your head and work your way up, using the comb to lift your hair as you go. Use a scooping motion to ensure that the hair is cut evenly.

As you move up, switch to a shorter guard and repeat the process. Remember to blend the hair as you go, so there are no harsh lines.

Once you've cut the back and sides, move on to the top of your head. Use the scissors to cut the top to your desired length.

Blending Your Hair

Now it's time to blend your hair. Use the clippers without a guard to create a bald fade around the ears and the back of your head. Use the comb to hold your hair out of the way as you work. Blend the hair so that there are no harsh lines.

Next, use the clippers with a guard to blend the longer hair with the shorter hair. Use a scooping motion to blend the hair evenly. Be sure to blend the hair at the back of your head as well.

Finishing Touches

Once you've blended your hair, it's time to finish up. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs and to define your neckline. Use the clippers to clean up any stray hairs around your ears and on your forehead.

Tips for a Perfect Fade

  • Take your time. A good fade takes patience.
  • Use a mirror to see the back of your head.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way shorter.
  • Blend the hair as you go to avoid harsh lines.
  • Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs.

FAQs

How long does it take to give yourself a good fade?

A good fade can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour, depending on your experience and the length of your hair.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always start over or go to a professional barber to fix it.

Do I need to use a haircutting spray bottle?

No, you don't need to use a spray bottle, but it can make cutting your hair easier.

Hair Care After Your Fade

Once you've given yourself a good fade, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a good shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. You may also want to use a styling product to keep your hair looking great.

With these tips and a little practice, you can give yourself a great fade at home. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and blend the hair as you go. Happy cutting!
