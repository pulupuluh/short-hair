How To Part And Cut Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Apr 29, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle but don't want to make a drastic change, cutting bangs is a great option. Bangs can frame your face and add a fun, flirty touch to your hair. However, if you've never cut your own bangs before, it can be a daunting task. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to part and cut bangs like a pro.

Table of Contents

  • How to Part Your Bangs
  • How to Cut Your Bangs
  • Tips for Cutting Your Bangs
  • FAQs About Cutting Bangs
  • Hair Care Tips for Bangs

How to Part Your Bangs

The first step in cutting bangs is to decide where you want your part to be. This will determine how your bangs will fall and how they will frame your face. Here's how to part your bangs:

  1. Start with dry, clean hair. Wet hair can be more difficult to part and can lead to uneven bangs.
  2. Using a comb, create a straight part in the middle of your hairline. This will create even bangs on both sides.
  3. If you want side-swept bangs, create a diagonal part on the side of your head where you want your bangs to fall. This will create a more natural, relaxed look.
  4. Once you've created your part, clip the rest of your hair back so it's out of the way.

How to Cut Your Bangs

Now that you've created your part, it's time to start cutting. Here's how to cut your bangs:

  1. Start with small snips. It's better to cut off too little than too much.
  2. Hold your scissors vertically and start cutting your bangs in a straight line. Be sure to cut in small sections and check your progress frequently.
  3. If you want side-swept bangs, angle your scissors diagonally and cut in a downward motion. This will create a softer, more natural look.
  4. Once you've cut your bangs to the desired length, use your scissors to add texture and movement. This will prevent your bangs from looking too blunt.

Tips for Cutting Your Bangs

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your own bangs:

  • Start with dry, clean hair. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut and can lead to uneven bangs.
  • Use sharp, high-quality scissors. Dull scissors can lead to split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Take your time. Cutting bangs can be a delicate process, so be patient and go slow.
  • Don't cut your bangs too short. It's better to start with longer bangs and work your way shorter.
  • When in doubt, seek professional help. If you're unsure about cutting your own bangs, it's always better to go to a salon and have a professional do it for you.

FAQs About Cutting Bangs

Can I cut my bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still cut your bangs if you have curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so you may want to cut your bangs a little longer than you normally would.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It's recommended to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and healthy.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my bangs?

If you make a mistake while cutting your bangs, don't panic. Try to even out the mistake by cutting the other side to match. If all else fails, you can always pin your bangs back until they grow out.

Hair Care Tips for Bangs

Once you've cut your bangs, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some hair care tips for bangs:

  • Wash your bangs every 2-3 days to keep them clean and prevent oil build-up.
  • Blow dry your bangs with a round brush to add volume and bounce.
  • Use a small amount of styling product, such as hair spray or pomade, to keep your bangs in place.
  • Avoid touching your bangs throughout the day, as this can lead to oil build-up and breakouts on your forehead.
  • Consider investing in a dry shampoo to extend the life of your bangs between washes.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to part and cut your own bangs like a pro. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to seek professional help if needed. Happy cutting!
