How To Cut Short Hair With Thinning Shears A Step By Step Guide

Written by William May 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a way to add texture and volume to your short hair, using thinning shears is a great option. Thinning shears are a type of scissors designed to remove bulk from hair without drastically altering the length. In this article, we'll show you how to cut short hair with thinning shears in a relaxed and easy-to-understand manner.

Description

First, it's important to understand what thinning shears are and how they work. Thinning shears have teeth on one or both blades, which allows them to cut only some of the hair strands, leaving others intact. This creates a more natural, textured look that's perfect for short hair. Thinning shears can also be used to blend layers and remove weight from thick hair, making them a versatile tool for hairstyling.

How to

Before you start cutting your hair with thinning shears, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of thinning shears, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair, making sure it's completely clean and free of tangles. If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, it's best to let it air-dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to avoid frizz.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section off your hair into manageable sections. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Take a section of hair and comb it straight. This will ensure that you're cutting the hair evenly and not cutting any strands too short.

Step 4: Cut with Thinning Shears

Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle and cut the hair in a zig-zag motion, taking care not to cut too much at once. It's best to start with a small amount of hair and gradually thin it out as needed.

Step 5: Repeat

Continue cutting each section of hair with the thinning shears, working your way up to the top layer. Remember to comb the hair straight before cutting and take your time to ensure a natural, textured look.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting short hair with thinning shears:

  • Use sharp, high-quality thinning shears to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time to avoid over-thinning.
  • Always comb your hair straight before cutting to ensure an even cut.
  • Work in a well-lit area with a large mirror to see what you're doing.
  • If you're unsure how much to thin out your hair, start with a small amount and gradually cut more if needed.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short hair with thinning shears:

Can I use thinning shears on any length of hair?

Thinning shears are best suited for short to medium-length hair. If you have long hair, it's best to use regular scissors or consult a hairstylist.

How often should I use thinning shears on my hair?

It's best to use thinning shears sparingly, as over-thinning can cause damage and breakage. Aim to use them once every few months or as needed.

Can I use thinning shears to create layers in my hair?

Yes, thinning shears can be used to blend layers and remove weight from thick hair. However, it's best to consult a hairstylist if you're unsure how to achieve the desired look.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair with thinning shears, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and texture. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. You can also use hair serums and oils to add shine and smoothness to your hair.

Cutting short hair with thinning shears is a great way to add texture and volume to your look. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a natural, textured look that's perfect for any occasion.
