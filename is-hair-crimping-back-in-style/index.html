Description

Are you wondering if hair crimping is back in style? The answer is yes! Hair crimping is making a comeback in 2023, and it's an easy way to add volume and texture to your hair. Crimped hair is a look that was popular in the 80s and 90s, and now it's back with a modern twist. You can create beachy waves, add texture to braids, or simply crimp your hair for a fun and unique look. In this article, we'll go over how to crimp your hair, step-by-step, and provide tips for achieving the perfect crimped hairstyle.

How to Crimp Your Hair

What You'll Need

  • Crimping iron
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Hairspray
  • Sectioning clips

Steps

Step 1: Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before using the crimping iron.

Step 2: Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the heat of the crimping iron.

Step 3: Divide your hair into small sections using sectioning clips. The smaller the section, the more defined the crimp will be.

Step 4: Take one section of hair and clamp the crimping iron at the root. Hold for a few seconds and then release.

Step 5: Move the crimping iron down the section of hair, making sure to crimp all the way to the ends.

Step 6: Repeat steps 4 and 5 on all sections of hair until you have crimped your entire head.

Step 7: Finish with a light hairspray to hold the crimp in place.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to help you achieve the perfect crimped hairstyle:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is naturally curly or wavy, use a straightener to straighten it out before crimping.

Step 2: Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the crimping iron.

Step 3: Divide your hair into small sections using sectioning clips. Start at the bottom and work your way up.

Step 4: Take one section of hair and clamp the crimping iron at the root. Hold for 5-10 seconds and then release.

Step 5: Move the crimping iron down the section of hair, making sure to crimp all the way to the ends. Repeat this process on all sections of hair.

Step 6: Once you've crimped all of your hair, gently brush through it to soften the crimp and create a more natural look.

Step 7: Finish with a light hairspray to hold the crimp in place.

Tips for Hair Crimping

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect crimped hairstyle:

  • Use a crimping iron with ceramic plates for even heat distribution.
  • Start with small sections of hair for a more defined crimp.
  • Apply a heat protectant spray before using the crimping iron to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Brush through your hair after crimping to create a more natural look.
  • Use a light hairspray to hold the crimp in place.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is hair crimping damaging to your hair?

Any heat styling can be damaging to your hair if you don't use the right precautions. To prevent damage, use a heat protectant spray before crimping your hair, and don't crimp your hair too often.

What hair types are best for crimping?

Crimping works best on straight or slightly wavy hair. If your hair is naturally curly, you'll need to straighten it before crimping for the best results.

Can I crimp my hair every day?

No, it's best to limit heat styling to once or twice a week to prevent damage to your hair.

Hair Care for Crimped Hair

Here are some tips for caring for your crimped hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip the natural oils from your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray every time you use a crimping iron or other hot tool on your hair.

So, is hair crimping back in style? Absolutely! With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a fun and unique crimped hairstyle that will turn heads. Just remember to use a heat protectant spray, start with small sections of hair, and brush through your hair after crimping to create a more natural look. Follow these tips, and you'll be rocking a crimped hairstyle like a pro in no time!
