How To Cut Someone s Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Mar 10, 2023 · 8 min read
How to Cut Someone's Hair at Home: A Step-by-Step Guide | Hair Care Tips

Are you tired of spending a fortune on haircuts? Or maybe you can't make it to the salon due to the pandemic? Whatever the reason may be, cutting someone's hair at home can be a daunting task. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can give your loved ones a salon-quality haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the step-by-step process of how to cut someone's hair at home, along with some tips and tricks to make the experience as smooth as possible.

How to Cut Someone's Hair at Home

Cutting someone's hair at home may seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a breeze. Here's what you'll need:

Tools:

  • Scissors or clippers
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Cape or towel

Techniques:

  • Sectioning
  • Point cutting
  • Graduation
  • Blending

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your tools and techniques sorted, here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut someone's hair at home:

Step 1: Prep the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair thoroughly. Comb through any tangles and knots, and section the hair as needed using sectioning clips or hair ties. Drape a cape or towel over the person's shoulders to protect their clothing from hair clippings.

Step 2: Determine the Length

Ask the person what length they'd like their hair to be, and use your scissors or clippers to trim the hair to the desired length. Use a comb to lift the hair and cut evenly across the section. Start with a longer length and gradually work your way shorter to avoid taking off too much at once.

Step 3: Blend the Hair

Use your comb and scissors to blend the hair in the back and sides with the top section. Hold the hair between your fingers and point cut to create a seamless blend. Make sure to check your work from all angles and adjust as needed.

Step 4: Finish the Cut

Once you've blended the hair, use your scissors or clippers to clean up any stray hairs and create a finished look. Use your comb to check for any uneven spots and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Hair at Home

Cutting someone's hair at home can be a bit nerve-wracking, but with these tips, you'll be a pro in no time:

  • Invest in quality tools that will last you a long time.
  • Start with a longer length and gradually work your way shorter to avoid taking off too much at once.
  • Use a spray bottle with water to keep the hair damp and easier to cut.
  • Take your time and work in small sections.
  • Check your work from all angles to ensure a seamless blend.

FAQs

1. Can I cut someone's hair at home if I have no experience?

While it's always recommended to go to a professional hairstylist, cutting someone's hair at home is doable with the right tools and techniques. Just make sure to start with a longer length and take your time working in small sections.

2. How do I know what length to cut?

Ask the person what length they'd like their hair to be, and use your scissors or clippers to trim the hair to the desired length. Make sure to check your work as you go to ensure you're achieving the desired length.

3. What's the difference between point cutting and blunt cutting?

Blunt cutting involves cutting straight across the hair, while point cutting involves cutting into the hair at an angle to create a softer, more textured look.

Hair Care After Cutting

Once you've finished cutting someone's hair at home, make sure to follow up with some hair care tips to keep their locks looking their best:

  • Wash the hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools on the hair for a few days after the cut.
  • Trim the hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the desired length.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep the hair healthy and hydrated.

Now that you know how to cut someone's hair at home, you can save money and time by doing it yourself. Just remember to take your time, work in small sections, and follow up with some hair care tips to keep those locks looking their best.
