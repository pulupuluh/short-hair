How To Comb Curly Frizzy Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby May 30, 2023 · 8 min read

Curly hair is beautiful, but it can also be a challenge to style and maintain. One of the biggest struggles for those with curly hair is dealing with frizz. Fortunately, there are some simple steps you can take to keep your curls looking smooth and defined. In this article, we’ll explore how to comb curly frizzy hair in relaxed English and offer some tips and tricks for keeping your hair looking its best.

Table of Contents

  • How to Comb Curly Frizzy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Combating Frizz
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Comb Curly Frizzy Hair

Combing curly frizzy hair can be a bit tricky, as you don’t want to disrupt the natural curl pattern or create more frizz. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Use the Right Tools

Start by using a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush specifically designed for curly hair. Avoid using a regular brush, as this can cause more frizz and breakage.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Before combing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle and moisturize your curls. This will make it easier to comb through your hair without causing more frizz.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections to make it easier to comb through. Use clips or hair ties to keep each section in place.

Step 4: Start Combing

Starting at the bottom of each section, gently comb through your hair using the wide-tooth comb or detangling brush. Work your way up, being careful not to tug or pull at your hair.

Step 5: Finish with a Styling Product

Once you’ve combed through your hair, apply a styling product to help define your curls and combat frizz. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair and encourage your natural curl pattern.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a more detailed step-by-step guide for combing curly frizzy hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends and any particularly dry or frizzy areas.
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to gently comb through your hair, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Be sure to detangle each section before moving on to the next.
  4. Once you’ve combed through your hair, apply a curl-defining or anti-frizz styling product. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair and encourage your natural curl pattern.
  5. Allow your hair to air-dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting.
  6. Once your hair is dry, use your fingers to gently fluff your curls and create volume.

Tips for Combating Frizz

In addition to combing your hair properly, there are some other things you can do to combat frizz:

  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons, as these can dry out your hair and create more frizz.
  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel, which can create friction and cause frizz.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can disrupt the curl pattern and create more frizz.
  • Invest in a satin or silk pillowcase to help prevent frizz while you sleep.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about combing curly frizzy hair:

How often should I comb my curly hair?

It’s best to comb your curly hair when it’s wet and has a leave-in conditioner applied. This will help prevent breakage and keep your curls defined. You can also finger comb your hair when it’s dry to avoid disrupting the curl pattern.

What’s the best way to detangle curly hair?

Start by using a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush specifically designed for curly hair. Apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle and moisturize your curls. Divide your hair into sections and work your way up from the bottom of each section, being careful not to tug or pull at your hair.

What’s the best product for defining curls?

There are a variety of products on the market designed to define curls and combat frizz. Look for a curl-defining cream or gel that works well with your hair type and texture. Experiment with different products to find the one that works best for you.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

In addition to combing your hair properly and using the right products, there are some other things you can do to care for your curly hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause dryness and frizz.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause more frizz.
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair looking healthy and to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your curly frizzy hair looking smooth, defined, and beautiful.
