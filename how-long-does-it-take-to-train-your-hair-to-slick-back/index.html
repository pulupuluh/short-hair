How Long Does It Take To Train Your Hair To Slick Back

Written by Bardi Aug 14, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're someone who loves the classic, slicked-back hairstyle but has never tried it before, you may be wondering how long it takes to train your hair to slick back. This style is popular among men and women alike and has been around for decades. It is a timeless look that exudes sophistication and elegance. In this article, we'll discuss how to achieve this look, how long it takes to train your hair to slick back, and tips for maintaining it.

Description

The slicked-back hairstyle is achieved by using a styling product, such as gel or pomade, to slick the hair back from the hairline. This style requires some patience and persistence to achieve the desired look, especially if you have naturally curly or wavy hair. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months to train your hair to slick back.

How to Achieve the Slicked-Back Look

If you're ready to try the slicked-back hairstyle, there are a few steps you can take to achieve it:

Step 1: Choose the Right Product

The key to achieving the slicked-back look is using the right product. You'll want to choose a product that provides a strong hold and a shiny finish, such as pomade or gel. You can experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your hair type and texture.

Step 2: Start with Damp Hair

Before you apply the product, make sure your hair is damp. This will make it easier to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair. You can either towel-dry your hair or use a spray bottle to dampen it.

Step 3: Apply the Product

Apply the product to your hair, starting at the front and working your way back. Use your fingers or a comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair. Make sure to apply enough product to achieve the desired hold and shine.

Step 4: Comb Your Hair Back

Using a fine-tooth comb, comb your hair back from the hairline. Make sure to comb your hair in the direction you want it to stay. Continue combing your hair back until you achieve the desired look.

Tips for Maintaining the Slicked-Back Look

Once you've achieved the slicked-back look, you'll want to maintain it to keep it looking fresh and polished. Here are a few tips:

Tip #1: Touch Up Throughout the Day

Depending on your hair type and the product you use, your hair may start to lose its hold throughout the day. To keep your hair looking slicked back, keep a small amount of product with you and touch up as needed.

Tip #2: Don't Overdo It

While it's important to use enough product to achieve the desired hold and shine, it's also important not to overdo it. Using too much product can make your hair look greasy and weighed down.

Tip #3: Wash Your Hair Regularly

To avoid buildup of product in your hair, make sure to wash your hair regularly. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup and keep your hair looking healthy and clean.

FAQ

How long does it take to train your hair to slick back?

It can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months to train your hair to slick back, depending on your hair type and texture. The key is to be patient and persistent.

What product should I use to achieve the slicked-back look?

You'll want to choose a product that provides a strong hold and a shiny finish, such as pomade or gel. You can experiment with different products to find the one that works best for your hair type and texture.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

Tip #1: Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that is free of harsh chemicals and sulfates. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent damage.

Tip #2: Condition Regularly

Condition your hair regularly to keep it hydrated and nourished. This will help prevent breakage and keep your hair looking shiny and healthy.

Tip #3: Protect Your Hair from Heat

Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools, such as a flat iron or curling iron. This will help prevent damage and keep your hair looking healthy.

In conclusion, the slicked-back hairstyle is a classic look that exudes sophistication and elegance. While it can take some time to train your hair to slick back, with patience and persistence, you can achieve the desired look. Remember to choose the right product, start with damp hair, comb your hair back, and maintain the look with regular touch-ups and hair care.
