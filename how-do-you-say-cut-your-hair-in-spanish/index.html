How Do You Say Cut Your Hair In Spanish

Written by Daniel Jun 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you planning to visit a Spanish-speaking country and want to get a haircut? Or maybe you're just curious about how to say "cut your hair" in Spanish? Whatever the reason, we've got you covered!

How to Say "Cut Your Hair" in Spanish

The phrase "cut your hair" in Spanish is "cortarse el pelo". It's pronounced as "kor-tar-se el pe-lo".

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you pronounce "cortarse el pelo" correctly:

  1. Start with the first syllable "kor".
  2. Move on to the second syllable "tar".
  3. Next, say "se" which is pronounced as "say".
  4. Finally, say "el pe-lo" which means "the hair" in Spanish.

When you put it all together, it should sound like "kor-tar-se el pe-lo".

Tips for Pronouncing "Cortarse el pelo" Correctly

Here are some tips to keep in mind while pronouncing "cortarse el pelo" in Spanish:

  • Make sure to stress the second syllable "tar".
  • Pronounce the "r" sound with a slight roll of the tongue.
  • Practice saying the phrase slowly and gradually increase your speed.

FAQs

1. How do you say "haircut" in Spanish?

The word for "haircut" in Spanish is "corte de pelo". It's pronounced as "kor-te de pe-lo".

2. Can I use "cortar" instead of "cortarse"?

Yes, you can use "cortar" instead of "cortarse" to mean "cut" in Spanish. However, "cortarse" is the reflexive form which means "to cut one's own hair".

3. Are there any other phrases I need to know while getting a haircut in a Spanish-speaking country?

Yes, here are some other phrases you might find helpful:

  • "Quiero un corte de pelo." - I want a haircut.
  • "Por favor, corta solo las puntas." - Please just cut the ends.
  • "Déjalo un poco más largo." - Leave it a little longer.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you know how to say "cut your hair" in Spanish, here are some tips to take care of your hair:

  • Get a haircut regularly to keep your hair healthy.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals for healthy hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and pollution.

Now you're ready to get a haircut in a Spanish-speaking country! Remember to practice pronouncing "cortarse el pelo" correctly and use the additional phrases we provided to communicate effectively with your hairstylist.
