How To Describe A Haircut To Your Barber In Relaxed English Language

Written by Frank Aug 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Getting a haircut can be daunting, especially if you're not sure how to describe the style you want. However, with the right words and phrases, you can easily communicate your desired haircut to your barber. In this article, we'll show you how to describe a haircut to your barber in relaxed English language.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Describe a Haircut to Your Barber
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Describing Your Haircut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Description

Before you head to the barbershop, it's important to have an idea of the haircut you want. This can be a specific style or a general idea of the length and shape you're looking for. The clearer you are about what you want, the easier it will be for your barber to deliver the desired haircut. You should also consider your hair type and face shape when choosing a haircut.

How to Describe a Haircut to Your Barber

When describing your desired haircut to your barber, it's important to use clear and concise language. Here are some phrases and terms you can use:

  • "I want a trim" - if you just want a little off the length
  • "I want a buzz cut" - if you want a very short haircut
  • "I want a fade" - if you want the hair to gradually get shorter towards the neck
  • "I want a taper" - if you want the hair to be longer on top and gradually shorter towards the neck
  • "I want a layered cut" - if you want the hair to be cut at different lengths for texture and movement
  • "I want a side part" - if you want your hair parted to one side
  • "I want a crew cut" - if you want a short, classic men's style

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you describe your desired haircut to your barber:

  1. Research different hairstyles and choose the one you want.
  2. Consider your hair type and face shape to ensure the style suits you.
  3. Use clear and concise language to describe the style to your barber.
  4. Show pictures or bring in a reference if possible.
  5. Be open to your barber's suggestions and advice.

Tips for Describing Your Haircut

Here are some additional tips to help you describe your desired haircut to your barber:

  • Be specific about the length you want. Use a number or reference point (e.g. "a little off the top" or "just above the ears").
  • Consider the texture of your hair. Use phrases like "thick" or "fine" to help your barber understand your hair type.
  • Be clear about any specific details you want (e.g. a side part, a hard part, or a fringe).
  • Don't be afraid to ask questions or for clarification if you're not sure about a term or phrase.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about describing a haircut to your barber:

What if I don't know what haircut I want?

If you're not sure what haircut you want, it's okay to ask your barber for suggestions. They can recommend styles based on your hair type and face shape.

What if I don't know the terminology?

If you're not familiar with haircut terminology, you can describe the style in your own words or show a picture as reference.

What if I'm not happy with the haircut?

If you're not happy with the haircut, communicate your concerns to your barber. They may be able to make adjustments or suggest a different style.

Hair Care Tips

After getting your haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your haircut.

By using clear and concise language, considering your hair type and face shape, and being open to your barber's suggestions, you can easily describe your desired haircut to your barber. With the right communication, you can leave the barbershop feeling confident and happy with your new haircut.
