What Happens If I Stop Microblading My Eyebrows

Written by Eveline Jul 28, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you've ever had your eyebrows microbladed, you know how much it can transform your face. Microblading is a semi-permanent tattoo technique that fills in sparse eyebrows or creates a new shape. But what happens if you decide to stop microblading your eyebrows? In this article, we'll explore the effects of stopping microblading and what you can do to maintain your brows.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To Stop Microblading Your Eyebrows
  • Step By Step Guide
  • Tips To Maintain Your Brows
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Description

Microblading typically lasts between one to three years, depending on your skin type and how well you take care of your eyebrows. Over time, the pigment will fade, and you may notice that your brows start to look less defined. If you decide to stop microblading and let your eyebrows go back to their natural state, they will eventually return to their original shape and thickness.

However, the process of growing out your brows can take time, and during that period, your eyebrows may look uneven or patchy. This can be frustrating, especially if you're used to having perfectly shaped brows. But don't worry; there are ways to make the transition easier.

How To Stop Microblading Your Eyebrows

The first step in stopping microblading is to let your current microblading fade naturally. Avoid any touch-ups or adding more pigment, as this will only prolong the process. Once the microblading has faded, you can start to grow out your brows. This may take several months, so be patient.

In the meantime, you can use makeup to fill in any sparse areas and create a more natural-looking shape. Use an eyebrow pencil or powder that matches your natural hair color and lightly fill in any areas that need it. Avoid using too much product, as this can look unnatural and draw attention to the fact that you're growing out your brows.

Step By Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to stop microblading your eyebrows:

  1. Let your current microblading fade naturally.
  2. Avoid any touch-ups or adding more pigment.
  3. Start growing out your brows.
  4. Use makeup to fill in any sparse areas and create a more natural-looking shape.
  5. Be patient and wait for your eyebrows to reach their natural state.

Tips To Maintain Your Brows

Here are some tips to help maintain your brows as you grow them out:

  • Avoid over-plucking or waxing your brows.
  • Use a growth serum to help promote hair growth.
  • Trim any stray hairs to keep your brows looking neat.
  • Visit a professional eyebrow stylist for shaping and maintenance.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about stopping microblading:

How long does it take for microblading to fade?

Microblading typically lasts between one to three years, depending on your skin type and how well you take care of your eyebrows. Over time, the pigment will fade, and you may notice that your brows start to look less defined.

Will my eyebrows return to their natural state?

Yes, if you stop microblading, your eyebrows will eventually return to their natural state. However, this process may take several months, and during that period, your eyebrows may look uneven or patchy.

Can I speed up the process of growing out my brows?

You can use a growth serum to help promote hair growth and avoid over-plucking or waxing your brows. However, there's no quick fix for growing out your brows, so be patient and let nature take its course.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, it's important to take care of your hair as you grow out your brows. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind.

In conclusion, stopping microblading is a personal choice, and there's no right or wrong answer. If you decide to stop, be patient and let your eyebrows grow out naturally. Use makeup to fill in any sparse areas and visit a professional eyebrow stylist for shaping and maintenance. And don't forget to take care of your hair and overall health as you go through the process.
