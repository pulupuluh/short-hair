Hairstyles For Very Thin Hair Over 60

Written by Oliver Jun 01, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As we age, our hair naturally becomes thinner and more delicate. For those over 60, this can be an especially big concern. However, there are still plenty of hairstyles that can work well with very thin hair. Here are some options to consider:

Short Pixie Cut

A short pixie cut can be a great option for those with very thin hair. This style can help to give the illusion of thicker hair, as the short layers can add volume and texture. This style is also very low-maintenance, making it a great option for those who don't want to spend a lot of time styling their hair.

Bob with Bangs

A bob with bangs can be another great option for those with thin hair. The bangs can help to add volume to the front of the hair, while the bob can add some texture and movement. This style is also very versatile, as it can be worn straight or with some natural waves.

Layered Bob

A layered bob can also be a great option for those with very thin hair. The layers can help to add volume and movement, while still keeping the hair relatively short and easy to manage. This style can be worn straight or with some natural waves.

Messy Bun

For those who prefer to wear their hair up, a messy bun can be a great option. This style can be achieved by pulling the hair back into a loose bun at the nape of the neck, and then pulling out some pieces to create a messy, textured look. This style is great for those with very thin hair, as it can help to create the illusion of more volume.

How to Style Your Hair for Thin Hair Over 60

When styling your hair for thin hair over 60, it's important to keep a few things in mind. First, it's a good idea to avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and make your hair even thinner. Instead, try to air dry your hair as much as possible, and use a heat protectant spray when you do use heat tools.

Another important tip is to use the right products for your hair type. Look for products that are specifically designed for thin hair, as these will be lighter and less likely to weigh your hair down. You can also try using volumizing sprays or mousses to add some extra texture and volume to your hair.

Step by Step Guide for Thin Hair Over 60

If you're looking to create a specific hairstyle for thin hair over 60, here's a step by step guide to help you out:

Step 1: Wash and condition your hair with products designed for thin hair.


Step 2: Apply a volumizing spray or mousse to your hair, focusing on the roots.


Step 3: Blow dry your hair, using a round brush to add volume and texture.


Step 4: Use a curling iron or straightener to create the desired style.


Step 5: Finish with a light-hold hairspray to keep the style in place.


Tips for Thin Hair Over 60

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when styling thin hair over 60:

  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as these can weigh your hair down and make it look even thinner.
  • Try to avoid harsh chemicals and treatments, as these can be damaging to thin hair.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for your hair type and face shape.

FAQs

Q: Can I still dye my hair if it's very thin?

A: Yes, but it's important to be gentle with your hair and use products that are specifically designed for thin hair. You can also try using temporary hair color products or highlights to add some color without causing too much damage.

Q: What kind of haircut should I get for thin hair over 60?

A: Shorter hairstyles tend to work best for thin hair over 60, as they can help to create the illusion of more volume. Pixie cuts, bobs, and layered cuts can all be great options.

Hair Care for Thin Hair Over 60

When it comes to caring for thin hair over 60, it's important to be gentle and avoid harsh chemicals and treatments. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for thin hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and make your hair even thinner.
  • Try to avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, as this can cause breakage and thinning.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of protein and vitamins to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and experimenting with different hairstyles, you can find a look that works well for your thin hair over 60. Remember to be gentle with your hair, and to use products that are designed to add volume and texture without causing damage.
