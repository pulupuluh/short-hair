Why Is My Cat Losing Hair Above His Eyes

Written by Daniel Apr 05, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’ve noticed your cat losing hair above their eyes, it can be a cause for concern. The hair loss could be due to a number of reasons, ranging from simple allergies to more serious health issues. In this article, we’ll explore the possible causes of hair loss above your cat's eyes and what you can do to help.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Check for Hair Loss
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Treating Hair Loss
  • Tips to Prevent Hair Loss
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Your Cat

Description

Hair loss above your cat's eyes can be caused by a variety of factors. The most common causes of hair loss in this area include allergies, skin infections, and stress. Cats with allergies may develop redness and itching around their eyes, leading to hair loss. Skin infections like ringworm can cause hair loss and scabs around the eyes. Stress can also cause hair loss in cats, and this may be more common in cats that are kept indoors or have a history of anxiety.

How to Check for Hair Loss

To check for hair loss around your cat's eyes, you can gently comb through their fur with a fine-toothed comb. Look for any patches of missing hair, redness, or scabbing around the eyes. If you notice any of these symptoms, it's important to take your cat to the vet as soon as possible.

Step 1: Make an Appointment with Your Vet

If you suspect that your cat may be experiencing hair loss above their eyes, the first step is to make an appointment with your vet. Your vet will be able to examine your cat and determine the underlying cause of the hair loss.

Step 2: Rule Out Allergies

If your cat is experiencing hair loss due to allergies, the vet may recommend an elimination diet or allergy testing to determine the allergen causing the reaction. In some cases, antihistamines or corticosteroids may be prescribed to help reduce the symptoms.

Step 3: Treat Skin Infections

If your cat has a skin infection like ringworm, the vet may prescribe topical or oral medications to treat the infection. It's important to follow your vet's instructions and complete the full course of treatment to ensure that the infection is fully cleared up.

Step 4: Reduce Stress

If stress is the underlying cause of your cat's hair loss, there are several things you can do to help. Providing your cat with plenty of toys and scratching posts can help reduce stress and anxiety. You may also want to consider using pheromone sprays or diffusers to help calm your cat.

Tips to Prevent Hair Loss

To prevent hair loss in your cat, it's important to keep them healthy and stress-free. Regular grooming can help keep their fur healthy and prevent hair loss due to matting or tangles. Providing your cat with a healthy diet and plenty of exercise can also help reduce stress and keep them in good health.

FAQs

Q: Is hair loss above the eyes in cats common?

A: Hair loss above the eyes is not uncommon in cats, and there are many possible causes of this condition.

Q: Can I treat my cat's hair loss at home?

A: It's important to take your cat to the vet if you suspect they are experiencing hair loss. Your vet will be able to determine the underlying cause of the condition and recommend appropriate treatment.

Hair Care for Your Cat

Regular grooming is an important part of maintaining your cat's fur and preventing hair loss. Brushing your cat's fur regularly can help remove tangles and mats and distribute natural oils throughout their fur. You may also want to consider using a cat-specific shampoo to keep their fur clean and healthy.

If you suspect your cat is experiencing hair loss above their eyes, it's important to take them to the vet as soon as possible. With proper diagnosis and treatment, your cat can make a full recovery and enjoy a healthy, happy life.
