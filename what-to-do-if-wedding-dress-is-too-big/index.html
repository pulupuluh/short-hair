What To Do If Wedding Dress Is Too Big

Written by Frank May 18, 2023 · 6 min read

When it comes to wedding dress fittings, it's not uncommon for alterations to be needed. In some cases, you may find that your dress is too big, which can be a cause for concern. But don't panic, there are a few things you can do to ensure your dress fits perfectly on your big day. Read on for our tips on what to do if your wedding dress is too big.

Description

If your wedding dress is too big, it can be a stressful experience. But don't worry, it's a common issue and there are solutions that can help. Whether you have bought a dress that is too big or have lost weight since your final fitting, there are steps you can take to ensure that your dress fits perfectly on your wedding day.

How to

If you find that your wedding dress is too big, it's important to take action as soon as possible. Here's what you can do:

Step 1: Speak to your seamstress

If you have already been fitted for your dress, speak to your seamstress about your concerns. They will be able to assess the dress and suggest alterations that can be made to ensure it fits perfectly.

Step 2: Consider a corset or bustier

If your dress is too big around the bust area, consider wearing a corset or bustier. This will help to give you the support you need and ensure your dress fits snugly.

Step 3: Add padding

If your dress is too big around the hips or bottom, consider adding padding. This can help to fill out the dress and give you a more shapely silhouette.

Step 4: Use a belt or sash

If your dress is too big around the waist, use a belt or sash to cinch it in. This will help to create a more defined waistline and ensure your dress fits perfectly.

Step 5: Consider reordering

If your wedding is still a few months away and you are concerned that your dress will not fit properly, consider reordering a smaller size. This may incur additional costs, but it will ensure that you have a dress that fits perfectly on your big day.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to ensure your dress fits perfectly:

  • Make sure you wear the right underwear during your fitting
  • Don't lose too much weight before your wedding day
  • Try to maintain a consistent weight in the months leading up to your wedding
  • Be honest with your seamstress about any concerns you have

FAQ

What if my dress is too small?

If your dress is too small, speak to your seamstress about the possibility of letting it out. If this is not possible, consider ordering a larger size.

How much does it cost to alter a wedding dress?

The cost of altering a wedding dress will depend on the extent of the alterations needed. Speak to your seamstress for a quote.

When should I have my final fitting?

Your final fitting should ideally take place around 2-3 weeks before your wedding day.

Hair Care

On your wedding day, you want your hair to look its best. Here are a few tips to ensure your hair is in great condition:

  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair in the months leading up to your wedding
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid using too much product on your hair on your wedding day to prevent it from becoming greasy
  • Consider using a hair mask the night before your wedding to add extra shine and moisture
