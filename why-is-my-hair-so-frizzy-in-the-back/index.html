Why Is My Hair So Frizzy In The Back

Written by Petter Jul 11, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizzy hair in the back? You're not alone. Many people struggle with frizz, especially in the back of their hair. But why does it happen? And more importantly, how can you fix it?

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Fix Frizzy Hair in the Back
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Frizz
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Routine to Prevent Frizz

Description

Frizzy hair in the back can be caused by a number of factors. One of the most common reasons is due to the friction that occurs between your hair and clothing. This is especially true if you wear tight or rough clothing, such as a backpack or a wool sweater.

Another reason for frizzy hair in the back is due to the lack of moisture. If your hair is dry, it can become brittle and prone to breakage, which can lead to frizz. Additionally, if you use too many hair products, especially those that contain alcohol, they can strip your hair of its natural oils, leading to dryness and frizz.

How to Fix Frizzy Hair in the Back

If you're tired of dealing with frizzy hair in the back, don't worry. There are several ways to fix it. Here are a few tips to get you started:

1. Use a Hair Serum

A hair serum can be a game-changer when it comes to controlling frizz. Look for one that contains ingredients like argan oil or coconut oil, which can help moisturize your hair and prevent frizz.

2. Invest in a Silk Pillowcase

If you're waking up with frizzy hair in the back, it could be due to your pillowcase. A cotton pillowcase can create friction, which can lead to frizz. Invest in a silk pillowcase instead, which can help keep your hair smooth and frizz-free.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

If you're using heat styling tools like a flat iron or curling wand, it could be contributing to your frizz problem. Heat can dry out your hair, leading to frizz. Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray before styling.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're not sure where to start when it comes to fixing frizzy hair in the back, here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help add moisture to your hair and prevent dryness, which can lead to frizz.

Step 2: Apply a Hair Serum

Next, apply a hair serum to your hair, focusing on the back. Use a dime-sized amount and work it through your hair, making sure to evenly distribute it.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a diffuser attachment. This will help evenly distribute the heat and prevent frizz. Use a low heat setting and hold the dryer at least six inches away from your hair.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

If you need to style your hair, use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools. Use a low heat setting and avoid using them on the highest setting, which can lead to dryness and frizz.

Tips to Prevent Frizz

Preventing frizz in the back of your hair starts with good hair care habits. Here are a few tips to help you prevent frizz:

1. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and frizz. Try to wash your hair every two to three days instead.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb can help prevent tangles and breakage, which can lead to frizz. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

3. Don't Rub Your Hair with a Towel

When you get out of the shower, don't rub your hair with a towel. This can create friction, which can lead to frizz. Instead, gently blot your hair with a towel or use a microfiber towel to absorb excess water.

FAQs

Q: Can Frizzy Hair Be Fixed?

A: Yes, frizzy hair can be fixed. It often requires a combination of good hair care habits and the right hair products.

Q: Should I Brush My Hair When It's Wet?

A: No, you should avoid brushing your hair when it's wet. Wet hair is more prone to breakage, and brushing it can lead to frizz and damage.

Q: Can a Haircut Help Reduce Frizz?

A: Yes, getting regular haircuts can help reduce frizz. Trimming your hair can help get rid of split ends, which can lead to frizz.

Hair Care Routine to Prevent Frizz

If you're looking for a hair care routine to prevent frizz, here are a few tips:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Look for a shampoo and conditioner that are designed to moisturize your hair. This will help prevent dryness, which can lead to frizz.

2. Apply a Hair Mask Once a Week

Using a hair mask once a week can help add moisture to your hair and prevent frizz. Look for one that contains ingredients like argan oil or coconut oil.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help add moisture to your hair and prevent frizz. Apply it to damp hair, focusing on the ends.

Dealing with frizzy hair in the back can be frustrating, but it's not impossible to fix. By following these tips and developing a good hair care routine, you can prevent frizz and keep your hair looking smooth and healthy.
