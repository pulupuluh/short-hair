Can Pcos Cause Hair Thinning

Written by Petter Sep 05, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're a woman who is experiencing hair thinning or hair loss, it's possible that you may be suffering from PCOS, or polycystic ovary syndrome. This condition affects millions of women worldwide and can cause a range of symptoms, including acne, weight gain, and irregular periods. In this article, we'll explore the link between PCOS and hair thinning, and what you can do to manage this condition.

What is PCOS?

PCOS is a hormonal disorder that affects women of reproductive age. It's caused by an imbalance of hormones in the body, and can lead to a range of symptoms, including:

  • Irregular periods
  • Acne
  • Weight gain
  • Hair thinning or hair loss
  • Excessive hair growth in unusual places

How Does PCOS Cause Hair Thinning?

PCOS can cause hair thinning or hair loss due to the hormonal imbalances that occur in the body. Women with PCOS have higher levels of androgens, which are male hormones that are also present in women. These androgens can cause the hair follicles to shrink, leading to hair thinning or hair loss.

In addition to androgens, women with PCOS may also have higher levels of insulin in their bodies. This can lead to a condition called insulin resistance, which can cause inflammation in the body. Inflammation can also contribute to hair thinning or hair loss.

How to Manage Hair Thinning Caused by PCOS

If you're experiencing hair thinning or hair loss due to PCOS, there are several things you can do to manage this condition:

  1. Consult with your doctor: Your doctor can help you manage your symptoms and recommend treatments that may help prevent hair thinning or hair loss.
  2. Eat a healthy diet: Eating a balanced diet that is low in processed foods and high in fruits and vegetables can help improve your overall health and reduce inflammation in the body.
  3. Exercise regularly: Regular exercise can help improve insulin resistance and reduce inflammation in the body.
  4. Take supplements: Certain supplements, such as biotin and iron, may help improve hair health and reduce hair thinning or hair loss.
  5. Use hair care products: Using gentle hair care products that are free from harsh chemicals can help prevent further damage to your hair.

FAQs

Can PCOS be cured?

PCOS cannot be cured, but it can be managed through lifestyle changes and medications.

Is hair thinning permanent?

Hair thinning can be permanent if the hair follicles have been permanently damaged. However, in many cases, hair thinning can be reversed with proper treatment.

Can stress cause hair thinning?

Yes, stress can cause hair thinning or hair loss. Stress can disrupt the hair growth cycle and lead to hair loss.

Hair Care Tips for Women with PCOS

If you have PCOS and are experiencing hair thinning or hair loss, there are several things you can do to care for your hair:

  • Use gentle hair care products that are free from harsh chemicals.
  • Avoid heat styling tools.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on the hair.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair.
  • Avoid wearing hats or headbands that can trap sweat and bacteria on the scalp.

By following these tips and working with your doctor to manage your PCOS symptoms, you can help prevent hair thinning or hair loss and maintain healthy hair.
