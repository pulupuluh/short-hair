How To Cut My Own Hair Short Style A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to shake up your look and try a short haircut, but don't want to spend a fortune at the salon, cutting your own hair is a great option. With the right tools and some patience, you can achieve a stylish and modern short haircut in the comfort of your own home. Follow this step-by-step guide to learn how to cut your own hair short style.

How to:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting your own hair is to gather all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Choose Your Short Style

Before you start cutting, it's important to decide on the short style you want. Look for inspiration online or in magazines and choose a style that will suit your face shape and hair type.

Step 3: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash and dry your hair before starting to cut. This will help you see the natural texture and shape of your hair, making it easier to cut evenly.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Using hair clips, section your hair into four parts: top, bottom, left, and right. This will help you work on one section at a time and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Starting with the back section, use the comb to lift a small section of hair and cut it straight across. Work your way up to the crown of your head, then move to the sides and front. Remember to cut small sections at a time and check your progress frequently.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

If your short style requires layers, use the scissors to blend the layers together. Hold the hair between your fingers and snip into the ends to create a natural-looking blend.

Step 7: Trim the Bangs

If your short style includes bangs, use the scissors to trim them to the desired length. Start with longer bangs and work your way up to shorter ones, checking your progress frequently.

Step 8: Check for Evenness

When you're finished cutting, use the comb to check for evenness. Make small adjustments as needed, but be careful not to overdo it.

Step 9: Style Your New Short Haircut

Once you're happy with the cut, style your hair using your preferred products and techniques. Enjoy your new short haircut!

Tips:

  • Start with a longer length and make small cuts to avoid cutting too much off at once.
  • Use the scissors to cut vertically into the hair for a textured look.
  • Invest in quality hair cutting scissors for the best results.
  • Take breaks and step away from the mirror to avoid becoming too critical of your progress.

FAQ:

Can I cut my own hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if you have curly hair. It's important to keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so cut less than you think you need to.

Do I need to wet my hair before cutting it?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and look longer than it actually is. However, if you prefer to cut your hair when it's wet, make sure to cut less than you think you need to account for the shrinkage when it dries.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

If you make a mistake while cutting, don't panic. Wait until you've finished the rest of your hair, then assess the situation. You may be able to make small adjustments to fix the mistake, or you may need to seek professional help.

Hair Care:

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and style. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner that's suited for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat when styling. Regular trims every 6-8 weeks will also help keep your short haircut looking fresh.
