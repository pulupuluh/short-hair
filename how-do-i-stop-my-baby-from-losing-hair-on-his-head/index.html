How To Stop Your Baby From Losing Hair On His Head

Written by Daniel Sep 07, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As a parent, you may be worried when you notice your baby's hair falling out. Although it's normal for babies to lose some hair in their first few months, excessive hair loss can be a cause for concern. In this article, we will explore the reasons why babies lose hair and provide you with tips on how to stop your baby from losing hair on his head.

Table of Contents

  1. Reasons why babies lose hair
  2. How to stop your baby from losing hair
  3. Step-by-step guide to caring for your baby's hair
  4. Tips for preventing hair loss in babies
  5. FAQs about baby hair loss
  6. Hair care products for babies

Reasons why babies lose hair

It's normal for babies to lose some hair in their first few months of life. This is because the hormone levels in their bodies are adjusting to life outside the womb. However, if your baby is losing an excessive amount of hair, there could be several reasons why:

  • Friction: Babies who spend a lot of time lying on their backs may develop a bald spot on the back of their head due to friction.
  • Hormones: Hormonal changes can cause hair loss in babies. For example, some babies may experience a temporary loss of hair around 3-6 months of age due to hormonal changes.
  • Cradle cap: This is a common condition that affects many babies. It's characterized by scaly patches on the scalp that can cause hair loss.
  • Nutrition: A lack of essential vitamins and minerals can lead to hair loss in babies.
  • Medical conditions: In rare cases, hair loss in babies can be a sign of an underlying medical condition.

How to stop your baby from losing hair

If you're worried about your baby's hair loss, there are several steps you can take to help prevent it:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Avoid putting your baby's hair in tight braids or ponytails, as this can cause tension and pulling on the hair.
  • Use a soft brush: Use a soft brush to gently comb your baby's hair, and avoid using hair accessories that can pull or tug on the hair.
  • Massage the scalp: Massaging your baby's scalp can help stimulate hair growth and improve circulation.
  • Get enough nutrients: Make sure your baby is getting enough vitamins and minerals through their diet. Breast milk or formula should provide your baby with all the necessary nutrients, but if your baby is on solid foods, make sure they're getting a balanced diet.
  • Don't over-wash: Avoid washing your baby's hair too frequently, as this can strip the scalp of natural oils and lead to dryness and hair loss.
  • Consult a doctor: If you're concerned about your baby's hair loss, consult a doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Step-by-step guide to caring for your baby's hair

Caring for your baby's hair is simple and straightforward. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wet your baby's hair with warm water.
  2. Apply a gentle baby shampoo to your baby's hair.
  3. Gently massage the shampoo into your baby's scalp.
  4. Rinse the shampoo out of your baby's hair with warm water.
  5. Pat your baby's hair dry with a soft towel.
  6. Use a soft brush to gently comb your baby's hair.
  7. Avoid using hair accessories that can pull or tug on the hair.

Tips for preventing hair loss in babies

Here are some additional tips for preventing hair loss in babies:

  • Avoid exposing your baby's head to direct sunlight for extended periods of time.
  • Use a baby hat or cap to protect your baby's head from the sun.
  • Use a humidifier to keep the air moist, as dry air can lead to dry skin and hair.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your baby's hair, such as hair dyes or perms.
  • Make sure your baby is getting enough sleep, as sleep deprivation can lead to hair loss.

FAQs about baby hair loss

Is it normal for babies to lose hair?

Yes, it's normal for babies to lose some hair in their first few months of life. This is because the hormone levels in their bodies are adjusting to life outside the womb.

When should I be concerned about my baby's hair loss?

If your baby is losing an excessive amount of hair or if you notice bald spots on your baby's head, you should consult a doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Will my baby's hair grow back?

Yes, most babies will grow back the hair they lost in their first few months of life. However, it may take several months for the hair to grow back completely.

Hair care products for babies

When it comes to hair care products for babies, it's important to choose gentle, natural products that won't irritate your baby's skin. Here are some of our top picks:

  • California Baby Super Sensitive Shampoo & Bodywash
  • Mustela Foam Shampoo for Newborns
  • Babyganics Baby Shampoo + Body Wash
  • The Honest Company Purely Simple Fragrance-Free Shampoo + Body Wash

Remember, caring for your baby's hair is simple and straightforward. By following these tips and using gentle hair care products, you can help prevent hair loss and keep your baby's hair healthy and shiny.
