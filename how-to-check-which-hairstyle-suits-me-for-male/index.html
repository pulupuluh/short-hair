How To Check Which Hairstyle Suits Me For Male

Written by Eveline Apr 02, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you are looking for a new hairstyle, you might be wondering which one would suit you the best. With so many options available, it can be difficult to make a decision. Fortunately, there are some tips and tricks you can use to determine which hairstyle is right for you. In this article, we will show you how to check which hairstyle suits you best.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Consider Your Face Shape
  • Step 2: Think About Your Hair Type
  • Step 3: Consider Your Lifestyle
  • Step 4: Look for Inspiration
  • Tips for Choosing the Right Hairstyle
  • FAQs

Step 1: Consider Your Face Shape

One of the most important things to consider when choosing a hairstyle is your face shape. Different hairstyles can complement different face shapes, so it's important to choose one that works well with yours. Here are some general guidelines:

  • If you have a round face, try a hairstyle with more volume on top to elongate your face.
  • If you have a square face, try a hairstyle with softer lines to balance out your strong jaw.
  • If you have an oval face, lucky you! You can pull off almost any hairstyle.
  • If you have a heart-shaped face, try a hairstyle that adds volume at the bottom to balance out your wider forehead.

Remember that these are just guidelines, and you should choose a hairstyle that makes you feel confident and comfortable.

Step 2: Think About Your Hair Type

Another important factor to consider is your hair type. Different hairstyles work better with different hair textures and thicknesses. Here are some suggestions:

  • If you have thick hair, try a hairstyle that allows you to control the volume.
  • If you have thin hair, try a hairstyle with layers to add volume.
  • If you have curly hair, try a hairstyle that embraces your curls.
  • If you have straight hair, try a hairstyle that adds texture.

Again, these are just suggestions, and you should choose a hairstyle that works best with your hair type and personal style.

Step 3: Consider Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle can also play a role in choosing the right hairstyle. If you have a busy schedule, you may want a low-maintenance hairstyle that doesn't require a lot of styling. If you work in a professional environment, you may want a more conservative hairstyle. Here are some examples:

  • If you have a busy schedule, try a shorter hairstyle that is easy to style.
  • If you work in a professional environment, try a classic, clean-cut hairstyle.
  • If you are more laid-back, try a longer, more casual hairstyle.

Remember that your hairstyle should fit your lifestyle, not the other way around.

Step 4: Look for Inspiration

Finally, it's always a good idea to look for inspiration when choosing a new hairstyle. You can find inspiration in magazines, online, or by looking at pictures of celebrities with similar face shapes and hair types. Here are some tips:

  • Bring pictures of hairstyles you like to your stylist to help them understand what you are looking for.
  • Consider trying out a virtual hairstyle app to see how different hairstyles would look on you.
  • Ask friends or family for their opinions on different hairstyles.

Remember that ultimately, the decision is yours, and you should choose a hairstyle that makes you feel confident and comfortable.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hairstyle

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Don't be afraid to try something new! A new hairstyle can be a great way to reinvent yourself.
  • Consider the maintenance involved in your new hairstyle before making a decision.
  • Keep in mind that hairstyles can always be changed if you don't like the way they look.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about choosing the right hairstyle:

Q: Can I pull off a certain hairstyle if I don't have the same face shape as the model in the picture?

A: Absolutely! While certain hairstyles may be more flattering on certain face shapes, you can still pull off almost any hairstyle with confidence.

Q: How often should I get a haircut?

A: The frequency of your haircuts will depend on your personal preference and the type of hairstyle you have. Shorter hairstyles usually require more frequent haircuts, while longer hairstyles can go longer between cuts. Talk to your stylist to determine the best schedule for you.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've chosen your new hairstyle, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair.

By following these tips, you can ensure that your new hairstyle looks great and stays healthy. Remember, the most important thing is to choose a hairstyle that makes you feel confident and comfortable.
