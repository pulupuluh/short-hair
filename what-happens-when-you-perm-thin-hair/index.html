What Happens When You Perm Thin Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Jun 19, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your thin hair falling flat? A perm might seem like an easy solution to add volume and texture to your hair. However, before you book your appointment, it's essential to understand what happens when you perm thin hair. In this post, we'll break down the process, the effects, and how to care for permed thin hair.

Table of Contents

  • What is a Perm?
  • Can You Perm Thin Hair?
  • What Happens When You Perm Thin Hair?
  • How to Care for Permed Thin Hair
  • Tips for Perming Thin Hair
  • FAQ

What is a Perm?

A perm is a chemical treatment that alters the structure of your hair. It's a process of breaking down the natural bonds in your hair and reforming them in a new shape using a chemical solution. The solution is applied to your hair, and then it's wrapped around rods to create curls or waves.

Can You Perm Thin Hair?

Yes, you can perm thin hair, but it's essential to understand that the process can be damaging. Perming thin hair can cause breakage, dryness, and frizz. It's crucial to have a consultation with your stylist before perming your hair, especially if you have fine or thin hair.

What Happens When You Perm Thin Hair?

When you perm thin hair, the chemical solution breaks down the natural bonds in your hair. This allows the hair to be reshaped around the rods to create curls or waves. The solution is then rinsed out, and a neutralizer is applied to reform the natural bonds in your hair.

However, perming thin hair can cause damage, breakage, and dryness. The chemicals used in the perm process can be harsh on thin hair, leading to damage and breakage. Permed hair can also become dry and brittle, requiring extra care to keep it healthy.

Effects of Perming Thin Hair

Perming thin hair can have both positive and negative effects. Some of the positive effects include:

  • Adding volume and texture to thin hair
  • Creating curls or waves that last for several months
  • Reducing the need for styling products

However, some of the negative effects of perming thin hair include:

  • Breakage and damage to the hair
  • Dryness and brittleness
  • Frizziness and tangling

How to Care for Permed Thin Hair

Proper care is essential to keep permed thin hair healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips to help you care for your permed hair:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, as they can cause further damage to permed hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep the hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause further breakage.

Tips for Perming Thin Hair

If you're considering perming your thin hair, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose a stylist who has experience perming thin hair.
  • Have a consultation with your stylist before the perm to discuss your hair type and the desired outcome.
  • Avoid washing your hair for at least two days after the perm to allow the curls to set.
  • Avoid using products with alcohol, as they can dry out permed hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.

FAQ

1. Can perming thin hair make it thicker?

No, perming thin hair cannot make it thicker. Perming can add volume and texture to thin hair, but it cannot change the thickness of the hair.

2. How long does a perm last?

A perm can last for several months, depending on the hair type and how well it's cared for. Typically, a perm will last between three and six months.

3. Can you color permed hair?

Yes, you can color permed hair, but it's essential to wait at least two weeks after the perm to avoid damaging the hair.

4. How often can you perm your hair?

You should wait at least three months between perms to avoid damaging the hair. It's also essential to consider the health of your hair before perming again.

In conclusion, perming thin hair can add volume and texture, but it's essential to understand the process and effects before making a decision. Make sure to choose a stylist who has experience perming thin hair and to care for your permed hair properly to keep it healthy and looking great.
