How To Cut Children s Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank May 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon every time your child needs a haircut? Cutting your child's hair with clippers can be a great way to save money and give them a stylish haircut. However, it can be intimidating if you've never done it before. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your child's hair with clippers.

How to Cut Children's Hair with Clippers

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting your child's hair, you'll need to gather a few supplies. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Clipper guards (various sizes)
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Stool or chair for your child to sit on

Once you have all of your supplies ready, it's time to get started.

Step 2: Prep Your Child's Hair

Before you start cutting, you'll want to make sure your child's hair is clean and free of tangles. Wet their hair with the spray bottle and use the comb to get rid of any knots or tangles. Then, put the haircutting cape or towel around their neck to catch any hair that falls.

Step 3: Choose Your Guard Size

Clipper guards come in various sizes, so it's important to choose the right one for the length of hair you want to leave. Start with a larger guard and work your way down if you want a shorter cut.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Turn on your clippers and start cutting your child's hair. Start at the back of their head and work your way up to the crown. Use the clipper guard to create an even cut. Make sure to go slowly and be careful around the ears and neck.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you've cut the hair with the clippers, it's time to blend the hair to create a more natural look. Use the scissors to trim any longer hairs that weren't cut by the clippers. Blend the hair around the ears and neck for a seamless look.

Step 6: Clean Up

Once you're finished cutting your child's hair, it's time to clean up. Use the comb and scissors to get rid of any stray hairs. Then, remove the haircutting cape or towel and shake off any loose hairs.

Tips for Cutting Children's Hair with Clippers

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results when cutting your child's hair with clippers:

  • Start with a larger guard and work your way down for a shorter cut.
  • Go slowly and be careful around the ears and neck.
  • Blend the hair with scissors for a more natural look.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep hair wet and easier to cut.
  • Keep your child distracted with toys or a movie to make the process easier.

FAQs

Can I cut my child's hair with regular scissors?

You can, but clippers make the process much easier and give you a more even cut. If you're using scissors, make sure they are sharp and be careful not to cut too much at once.

How often should I cut my child's hair?

It depends on how fast their hair grows and how short you want to keep it. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is a good timeframe for a trim.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your child's hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Make sure to wash their hair regularly and use a gentle shampoo and conditioner. If your child has a sensitive scalp, use a detangling spray or leave-in conditioner to make brushing easier.

Cutting your child's hair with clippers can be a great way to save money and give them a stylish haircut. By following these steps and tips, you can be confident in giving your child a great haircut at home.
