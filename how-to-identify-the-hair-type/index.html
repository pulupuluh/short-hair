How To Identify Your Hair Type A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter May 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're someone who cares about their hair, you know how important it is to identify your hair type to ensure that you're using the right hair care products. But how exactly do you go about identifying your hair type? In this guide, we'll walk you through the process step by step.

Table of Contents

  • Step 2: Figure Out Your Hair Texture
  • Step 3: Identify Your Hair Porosity
  • Step 4: Determine Your Scalp Type
  • Tips for Identifying Your Hair Type
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care by Hair Type

Step 1: Determine Your Hair Density

The first step in identifying your hair type is determining your hair density. Hair density refers to the number of hair strands on your head. To determine your hair density, take a small section of hair and hold it up to the light. If you can see your scalp easily, you have low hair density. If you can see your scalp, but it's not very visible, you have medium hair density. If you can't see your scalp at all, you have high hair density.

Step 2: Figure Out Your Hair Texture

The next step is to figure out your hair texture. Hair texture refers to how thick or thin each individual hair strand is. There are three main types of hair texture: fine, medium, and coarse. To determine your hair texture, take a single strand of hair and run your fingers over it. If you can barely feel the strand, you have fine hair. If you can feel the strand, you have medium hair. If the strand feels thick and robust, you have coarse hair.

Step 3: Identify Your Hair Porosity

Hair porosity refers to how well your hair absorbs and retains moisture. There are three types of hair porosity: low, medium, and high. To determine your hair porosity, take a strand of hair and place it in a glass of water. If it sinks to the bottom quickly, you have high porosity hair. If it takes a while to sink, you have medium porosity hair. If it floats on top, you have low porosity hair.

Step 4: Determine Your Scalp Type

Your scalp type is just as important as your hair type when it comes to choosing the right hair care products. There are four types of scalp: oily, dry, combination, and normal. To determine your scalp type, look at your scalp and see if it's flaky or greasy. If your scalp is flaky, you have a dry scalp. If it's greasy, you have an oily scalp. If it's both, you have a combination scalp. If it's neither, you have a normal scalp.

Tips for Identifying Your Hair Type

Here are some additional tips to help you identify your hair type:

  • Pay attention to how your hair behaves throughout the day. Does it get oily quickly? Does it get frizzy easily?
  • Take note of how your hair feels when you run your fingers through it. Is it smooth or rough?
  • Consider any chemical treatments you've had in the past. Chemical treatments can change your hair type.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair types:

Q: Can my hair type change over time?

A: Yes, your hair type can change due to hormonal changes, aging, and chemical treatments.

Q: What hair type is the most common?

A: The most common hair type is medium density, medium texture, and medium porosity.

Q: Can I have more than one hair type?

A: Yes, it's possible to have a combination of hair types.

Hair Care by Hair Type

Now that you've identified your hair type, it's time to choose the right hair care products. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Low porosity hair: Use lightweight products that won't weigh down your hair.
  • Medium porosity hair: Use a balance of moisturizing and protein-based products.
  • High porosity hair: Use heavy, moisturizing products to prevent breakage.
  • Fine hair: Use lightweight products to avoid weighing down your hair.
  • Medium hair: Use a balance of moisturizing and protein-based products.
  • Coarse hair: Use heavy, moisturizing products to tame frizz and add shine.
  • Dry scalp: Use gentle, moisturizing products to soothe your scalp.
  • Oily scalp: Use lightweight, clarifying products to remove excess oil.
  • Combination scalp: Use a balance of moisturizing and clarifying products.
  • Normal scalp: Use a gentle, balanced shampoo and conditioner.

By following these tips and guidelines, you can ensure that you're using the right hair care products for your hair type. Happy styling!
