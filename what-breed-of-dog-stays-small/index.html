What Breed Of Dog Stays Small A Complete Guide

Written by Bardi Apr 19, 2023 · 7 min read

If you live in an apartment or have limited space in your home, you might be wondering what breed of dog stays small. Luckily, there are many breeds of dogs that stay small and are perfect for those who want a furry companion without the added responsibility of a large dog. In this guide, we will discuss the different breeds of small dogs, how to take care of them, and answer some frequently asked questions.

What Breeds of Dogs Stay Small?

There are many breeds of dogs that stay small, and choosing the right one for you can be overwhelming. Here are some of the most popular small dog breeds:

  • Chihuahua
  • Pomeranian
  • Yorkshire Terrier
  • Poodle
  • Bichon Frise

These breeds are known for their small size and are perfect for those who want a lapdog or a companion that can fit in small spaces.

How to Take Care of Small Dogs

Small dogs require the same care as larger dogs, but there are some unique considerations to keep in mind. Here are some tips for taking care of small dogs:

Feeding

Small dogs have different nutritional needs than larger dogs, and it's important to choose a high-quality dog food that is appropriate for their size. You should also be mindful of portion control, as small dogs are prone to overeating.

Exercise

Small dogs still need exercise, but their exercise needs are different from larger dogs. Short walks and indoor playtime are perfect for small dogs, and they can also benefit from mental stimulation activities.

Grooming

Small dogs often have long hair that needs to be brushed regularly to prevent mats and tangles. They also need regular nail trims and dental care.

Step-by-Step Guide to Finding the Right Small Dog

Finding the right small dog for you can be overwhelming, but following these steps can make the process easier:

Step 1: Determine Your Lifestyle

Consider your lifestyle and how a dog would fit into it. Do you have small children? Do you have other pets? Do you live in an apartment or a house with a yard? These factors can help you choose the right breed of small dog.

Step 2: Research Breeds

Research different breeds of small dogs and their characteristics. Consider their exercise needs, grooming requirements, and temperament.

Step 3: Meet the Dog

When you have narrowed down your choices, meet the dog in person. Spend time with the dog to see if their personality and energy level align with yours.

Tips for Taking Care of a Small Dog's Hair

Small dogs often have long hair that requires regular grooming. Here are some tips for taking care of a small dog's hair:

Brush Regularly

Brush your dog's hair regularly to prevent mats and tangles. Use a slicker brush or comb to gently remove any knots.

Bathe as Needed

Bathe your dog as needed, but be careful not to overdo it. Too much bathing can strip the coat of natural oils and cause dry skin.

Trim as Needed

Trim your dog's hair as needed, especially around the face, paws, and tail. Use sharp scissors or clippers to avoid injuring your dog.

FAQs

What is the smallest breed of dog?

The Chihuahua is the smallest breed of dog, weighing in at only 2-6 pounds.

Are small dogs good for apartments?

Yes, small dogs are great for apartments as they require less space and exercise than larger dogs.

Do small dogs shed?

Some small dogs do shed, but there are many breeds that are considered hypoallergenic and have minimal shedding.

Choosing the right breed of dog can be a difficult decision, but with the right research and preparation, you can find the perfect small dog for you. Remember to take care of your dog's nutritional, exercise, and grooming needs to ensure a happy and healthy life for your furry friend.
