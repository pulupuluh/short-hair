Best Hot Air Brush For Thick Short Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Jul 04, 2023 · 11 min read

Are you tired of using different tools to style your thick short hair? A hot air brush might be just what you need. It’s a versatile tool that can straighten, curl, and add volume to your hair all at once. But with so many options available, choosing the best hot air brush for your hair can be challenging. In this article, we’ll help you find the perfect hot air brush for your thick short hair.

Description

A hot air brush is a tool that combines a brush and a dryer. It blows hot air through the brush barrel to style your hair. It’s an all-in-one tool that can save you time and effort. It’s also a great tool for those who want to achieve a salon-quality blowout at home. However, not all hot air brushes are created equal. Some are better suited for thick short hair than others.

How to Choose the Best Hot Air Brush for Thick Short Hair

When choosing a hot air brush for your hair, there are a few things to consider:

Barrel Size

The barrel size should be appropriate for your hair length. For short hair, a smaller barrel size is recommended. It can help you achieve tighter curls and more volume. A barrel size of 1 inch or less is ideal for short hair.

Bristle Type

The bristle type can affect how the brush grips your hair. For thick hair, look for a hot air brush with stiff bristles. They can help you detangle your hair and add more volume. For short hair, choose a brush with shorter bristles. They can help you style your hair more precisely.

Heat Settings

Thick hair requires more heat to style. Look for a hot air brush with adjustable heat settings. It can help you customize the heat level to your hair type. For short hair, a lower heat setting may be enough to style your hair.

Top 5 Best Hot Air Brushes for Thick Short Hair

After considering the factors above, we’ve rounded up the top 5 best hot air brushes for thick short hair:

1. Revlon One-Step Hair Dryer & Volumizer

The Revlon One-Step Hair Dryer & Volumizer is a popular choice for thick short hair. It has a 1-inch barrel size and nylon bristles that can detangle your hair while adding volume. It also has two heat settings and a cool shot button to set your style.

2. Conair InfinitiPRO Hot Air Brush

The Conair InfinitiPRO Hot Air Brush has a 1-inch barrel size and titanium ceramic technology that can distribute heat evenly. It also has a removable filter for easy cleaning and three heat settings to customize your styling experience.

3. BaBylissPRO Nano Titanium Rotating Hot Air Brush

The BaBylissPRO Nano Titanium Rotating Hot Air Brush has a 2-inch barrel size and nano titanium technology that can smooth and shine your hair. It also has a rotating barrel that can add volume and curl to your hair. It has three heat settings and a cool shot button to set your style.

4. John Frieda Hot Air Brush

The John Frieda Hot Air Brush has a 1-inch barrel size and titanium ceramic technology that can add shine and reduce frizz. It also has two heat settings and a cool shot button to set your style. It also comes with a swivel cord for easy styling.

5. Remington Pro 2-in-1 Heated Straightening Brush

The Remington Pro 2-in-1 Heated Straightening Brush has a 1-inch barrel size and ceramic technology that can straighten and add volume to your hair. It also has two heat settings and an anti-static coating that can reduce frizz. It also comes with a 4-year warranty.

How to Use a Hot Air Brush on Thick Short Hair

Using a hot air brush on thick short hair can be easy if you follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Wash your hair with a shampoo and conditioner suitable for your hair type. Towel dry your hair until it’s damp.

Step 2: Apply heat protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray or cream to your hair. It can help protect your hair from heat damage.

Step 3: Section your hair

Section your hair into small sections. It can help you style your hair more precisely.

Step 4: Use the hot air brush

Use the hot air brush to style your hair. Start at the roots and work your way to the ends. Use the brush to create curls, waves, or straighten your hair.

Step 5: Set your style

Once you’ve styled your hair, use the cool shot button to set your style. It can also help reduce frizz.

Step 6: Apply finishing products

Apply a finishing spray or serum to your hair. It can help hold your style and add shine.

Tips for Using a Hot Air Brush on Thick Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your hot air brush:

  • Start with a low heat setting and work your way up if necessary.
  • Use the cool shot button to set your style and reduce frizz.
  • Don’t use the hot air brush on wet hair. It can damage your hair.
  • Don’t leave the hot air brush on one section of your hair for too long. It can cause heat damage.
  • Comb your hair before using the hot air brush. It can help you achieve a smoother finish.

FAQs

1. Can I use a hot air brush on short hair?

Yes, a hot air brush can be used on short hair. Look for a brush with a smaller barrel size and shorter bristles.

2. Can a hot air brush damage my hair?

Yes, a hot air brush can damage your hair if used improperly. Always use a heat protectant and don’t use the brush on wet hair.

3. How often should I use a hot air brush on my hair?

You should not use a hot air brush on your hair every day. It’s recommended to use it once or twice a week to avoid heat damage.

Hair Care for Thick Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you take care of your thick short hair:

  • Wash your hair with a shampoo and conditioner suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape.
  • Avoid using hair ties that can cause breakage.

Choosing the best hot air brush for your thick short hair can make a big difference in your styling routine. With our guide, you can find the perfect hot air brush to achieve your desired style. Remember to take care of your hair by using the right products and tools. Happy styling!
