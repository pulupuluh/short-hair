How To Cover Facial Hair With Foundation A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 25, 2023 · 6 min read

Facial hair can be a source of pride for many people, but it can also be a source of frustration when trying to apply makeup. Whether you have a little peach fuzz or a full beard, it can be difficult to cover facial hair with foundation without making it look cakey or unnatural. In this article, we’ll show you how to cover facial hair with foundation using a step-by-step guide, along with some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Prep Your Skin
  • Step 2: Choose the Right Foundation
  • Step 3: Apply the Foundation
  • Step 4: Set Your Makeup
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Facial Hair

Step 1: Prep Your Skin

The first step to covering facial hair with foundation is to prep your skin. This means washing your face with a gentle cleanser and patting it dry with a towel. Next, apply a moisturizer to your face and let it absorb for a few minutes. This will help your foundation go on smoothly and prevent it from settling into fine lines or pores.

Step 2: Choose the Right Foundation

When it comes to covering facial hair with foundation, it’s important to choose the right product. Look for a full-coverage foundation that is long-lasting and oil-free. You may also want to consider using a color-correcting primer to neutralize any redness or discoloration.

Some foundations that work well for covering facial hair include:

  • Estée Lauder Double Wear Foundation
  • MAC Studio Fix Fluid Foundation
  • Maybelline Superstay 24H Foundation

Step 3: Apply the Foundation

Now it’s time to apply the foundation. Start by using a small amount and blending it in with a sponge or brush. Pay special attention to the areas where you have facial hair, making sure to blend thoroughly to avoid any harsh lines or patches.

You may need to apply a second layer of foundation to fully cover your facial hair. If this is the case, wait for the first layer to dry completely before applying the second layer.

Step 4: Set Your Makeup

Once you’ve applied your foundation, it’s important to set it with a powder. This will help to keep your makeup in place and prevent it from rubbing off throughout the day. Use a translucent powder to avoid adding any additional color to your face.

Tips and Tricks

  • Use a color-correcting primer to neutralize any redness or discoloration before applying foundation.
  • Choose a foundation that is long-lasting and oil-free.
  • Blend your foundation thoroughly to avoid any harsh lines or patches.
  • Set your makeup with a translucent powder to keep it in place throughout the day.
  • Consider using a makeup setting spray for additional staying power.

FAQs

Can I use a BB cream instead of foundation?

While BB creams can provide some coverage, they may not be enough to fully cover facial hair. For best results, we recommend using a full-coverage foundation.

Will covering my facial hair with foundation make it look cakey?

If you use the right products and blend your foundation thoroughly, you should be able to cover your facial hair without it looking cakey or unnatural. Be sure to use a small amount of foundation at a time and blend well.

Hair Care for Facial Hair

If you have facial hair, it’s important to take good care of it to keep it looking its best. This includes washing it regularly with a gentle cleanser and conditioning it to keep it soft and manageable. You may also want to consider using a beard oil or balm to keep your facial hair moisturized and healthy.

By following these tips and tricks, you can easily cover facial hair with foundation and achieve a flawless, natural-looking finish.
