The Best Way To Cover Thinning Crown A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Jun 09, 2023 · 9 min read

If you’ve noticed your crown thinning, you’re not alone. Thinning hair, especially on the crown, is a common problem that many people face. It can be caused by various factors, including genetics, age, stress, and hormonal changes. Fortunately, there are several ways to cover thinning crown and make your hair look fuller and thicker. In this article, we’ll explore the best way to cover thinning crown and provide you with some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect look.

Description

Thinning crown is a condition where the hair on the top of your head becomes thinner and less dense. It can be caused by several factors, including genetics, aging, stress, hormonal changes, and medical conditions. Thinning crown can affect both men and women and can lead to self-consciousness and low self-esteem. However, there are several ways to cover thinning crown and make your hair look fuller and thicker.

How to Cover Thinning Crown

There are several ways to cover thinning crown, including:

1. Hairstyles

Choosing the right hairstyle can make a significant difference in the appearance of your thinning crown. Avoid hairstyles that pull your hair back tightly, as they can emphasize the thinning areas. Instead, opt for a layered cut with bangs to add volume to the crown. You can also try a side part, which can help conceal the thinning area.

2. Hair Fibers

Hair fibers are an excellent way to cover thinning crown. They are made of natural keratin and can be sprinkled on the thinning areas to create the illusion of thicker hair. Hair fibers come in different shades to match your hair color and can be easily applied with a special applicator.

3. Hair Extensions

Hair extensions can be a great solution for covering thinning crown. They can add volume and length to your hair, making it look fuller and thicker. However, it’s important to choose the right type of extensions and have them applied by a professional to avoid damaging your hair.

4. Hairpieces

Hairpieces, also known as hair toppers, are another excellent way to cover thinning crown. They are designed to fit over the thinning area and blend seamlessly with your natural hair. Hairpieces come in different sizes and styles to suit your needs and can be attached with clips or adhesive.

Step by Step Guide to Cover Thinning Crown

Here’s a step-by-step guide to cover thinning crown:

Step 1: Choose the Right Hairstyle

Choose a hairstyle that adds volume to your hair and conceals the thinning areas. Avoid hairstyles that pull your hair back tightly or emphasize the thinning spots.

Step 2: Use Hair Fibers

Apply hair fibers to the thinning areas with a special applicator. Choose a shade that matches your hair color and blend well.

Step 3: Try Hair Extensions

If you want to add length and volume to your hair, try hair extensions. Choose the right type of extensions and have them applied by a professional to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 4: Consider Hairpieces

If you have severe thinning crown, consider using a hairpiece. Choose a hairpiece that fits well and blends seamlessly with your natural hair.

Tips for Covering Thinning Crown

Here are some tips for covering thinning crown:

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and make it look thinner.

2. Use Volumizing Products

Use volumizing products, such as mousse and root lifters, to add volume to your hair and make it look fuller.

3. Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

FAQ

Q: Can stress cause thinning crown?

A: Yes, stress can cause thinning crown by disrupting the hair growth cycle and causing hair to fall out prematurely.

Q: Can medication cause thinning crown?

A: Yes, certain medications can cause thinning crown as a side effect. If you’re experiencing thinning crown and are taking medication, talk to your doctor.

Q: Can diet affect thinning crown?

A: Yes, diet can affect thinning crown. A diet lacking in essential nutrients, such as protein and iron, can lead to thinning hair.

Hair Care Tips for Thinning Crown

Here are some hair care tips for thinning crown:

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly to remove dirt and oil buildup, which can clog hair follicles and lead to thinning hair.

2. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that’s free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as brushing can cause breakage and make your hair look thinner.

In conclusion, there are several ways to cover thinning crown and make your hair look fuller and thicker. Choose the right hairstyle, use hair fibers or extensions, consider using a hairpiece, and follow the hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and strong. With a little bit of effort and the right tools, you can achieve the perfect look and boost your confidence.
