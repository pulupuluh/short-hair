How To Cut Curly Hair In Layers Step By Step

Written by Oliver Apr 08, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have curly hair, you may have found that getting a haircut can be a bit tricky. Many hairdressers may not be familiar with curly hair, leading to less-than-ideal results. However, cutting curly hair in layers can help give your locks more shape and movement. In this article, we will go over how to cut curly hair in layers step by step, as well as some tips and hair care advice to keep your curls looking their best.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Gather Your Supplies
  • Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair
  • Step 3: Divide Your Hair Into Sections
  • Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer
  • Step 5: Move on to the Middle Layer
  • Step 6: Finish with the Top Layer
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair in Layers
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting your hair, you will need to gather a few supplies. These include:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb or brush
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can damage your hair and cause split ends.


Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It is best to cut curly hair when it is dry, as wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is. However, you can still wash and condition your hair as usual to prepare it for cutting.

Once you have washed your hair, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze out excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair with a regular towel, as this can cause frizz.


Step 3: Divide Your Hair Into Sections

Divide your hair into three sections: the bottom layer, the middle layer, and the top layer. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

Start with the bottom layer, as this will provide a foundation for the rest of your layers.


Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut the ends with your scissors.

Continue cutting small sections of hair from the bottom layer, making sure to cut in a straight line. Once you have finished the bottom layer, remove the hair clips and shake out your hair to see how it looks.


Step 5: Move on to the Middle Layer

Once you have finished the bottom layer, move on to the middle layer. Take a small section of hair from the middle layer and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut the ends with your scissors.

Continue cutting small sections of hair from the middle layer, making sure to blend the ends with the bottom layer. Once you have finished the middle layer, remove the hair clips and shake out your hair to see how it looks.


Step 6: Finish with the Top Layer

Finally, move on to the top layer. Take a small section of hair from the top layer and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut the ends with your scissors.

Continue cutting small sections of hair from the top layer, making sure to blend the ends with the middle and bottom layers. Once you have finished the top layer, remove the hair clips and shake out your hair to see how it looks.


Tips for Cutting Curly Hair in Layers

  • Start with small sections of hair, as it is easier to control and less likely to result in mistakes.
  • Avoid using thinning shears, as they can cause frizz and damage to curly hair.
  • Use a spray bottle filled with water to dampen your hair as needed, as this can make it easier to work with.
  • Consider getting a professional haircut if you are unsure about cutting your own hair.

FAQs

Can I cut my curly hair while it is wet?

While it is best to cut curly hair when it is dry, you can still cut it while it is wet. Just keep in mind that wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It is recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your curls looking their best and prevent split ends.


Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and prevent frizz. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls when they are wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl defining cream to your hair to help define your curls and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer.

By following these tips and learning how to cut curly hair in layers step by step, you can keep your curls looking their best and achieve a gorgeous, layered haircut.
