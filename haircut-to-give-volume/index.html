Haircut To Give Volume How To Achieve Full And Voluminous Hair

Written by Oliver Jul 10, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have thin or flat hair, you might be searching for ways to give your hair more volume. One of the most effective ways to achieve full and voluminous hair is through a haircut that is specifically designed to add body and texture. In this article, we'll share tips on how to get the perfect haircut to give volume and provide advice on how to care for your hair after the cut.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get a Haircut to Give Volume
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Achieving Full Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Volume After the Haircut
  • FAQs About Getting a Haircut to Give Volume
  • Hair Care Tips for Voluminous Hair

How to Get a Haircut to Give Volume

The first step in getting a haircut to give volume is to find a stylist who is experienced in cutting hair for volume. Look for stylists who have worked with clients with similar hair types and ask to see photos of their work.

When you meet with your stylist, be sure to bring photos of styles that you like and be clear about the amount of volume you want to achieve. Your stylist may recommend layers, texturizing, or other techniques to add body to your hair.

It's also important to consider the length of your hair. Shorter hair can be easier to style for volume, but longer hair can also look full and voluminous with the right cut.

Step-by-Step Guide to Achieving Full Hair

Once you've found the right stylist and discussed your desired look, here are some steps you can take to achieve full and voluminous hair:

1. Wash and condition your hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed to add volume. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are labeled "volumizing" or "thickening."

2. Blow-dry your hair upside down

Blow-dry your hair upside down to add volume at the roots. Use a round brush to lift the hair and create volume as you dry.

3. Use volumizing products

Apply volumizing products, such as mousse or root-lifting spray, to your hair before blow-drying. These products can help to add body and texture to your hair.

4. Style your hair with a curling iron or rollers

To create even more volume, consider using a curling iron or rollers to add waves or curls to your hair. This can help to create the illusion of fuller, thicker hair.

5. Finish with hairspray

Finish your style with a light mist of hairspray to hold the volume in place.

Tips for Maintaining Volume After the Haircut

After you've gotten a haircut to add volume, it's important to maintain your style to keep your hair looking full and voluminous. Here are some tips for maintaining volume:

1. Use the right products

Continue to use volumizing products, such as mousse or root-lifting spray, to add body and texture to your hair.

2. Avoid heavy products

Avoid using heavy products, such as hair oils or serums, which can weigh down your hair and make it look flat. Stick to lighter products that are designed to add volume.

3. Don't wash your hair too often

Washing your hair too often can strip it of natural oils, which can make it look flat and lifeless. Try to wash your hair every other day or less frequently if possible.

4. Consider a touch-up cut

To maintain your style and keep your hair looking full, consider getting a touch-up cut every 6-8 weeks.

FAQs About Getting a Haircut to Give Volume

What is the best haircut for adding volume to thin hair?

The best haircut for adding volume to thin hair is one that incorporates layers and texture. A layered bob, for example, can add volume and movement to thin hair.

Is it possible to add volume to long hair?

Yes, it is possible to add volume to long hair. A stylist can add layers or texture to long hair to create fullness and movement.

Hair Care Tips for Voluminous Hair

Once you've achieved full and voluminous hair, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some hair care tips for voluminous hair:

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner

Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't weigh down your hair or strip it of natural oils.

2. Protect your hair from heat damage

Use heat protectant products when using hot tools, such as curling irons or straighteners, to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Avoid over-styling

Avoid over-styling your hair with hot tools or heavy products, which can damage your hair and make it look flat.

4. Get regular trims

Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your hair look thin and lifeless.

By following these tips and getting the right haircut, you can achieve full and voluminous hair that looks healthy and vibrant. Talk to your stylist about the best haircut for your hair type and start taking care of your hair today!
