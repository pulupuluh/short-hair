Guys Haircuts Fade Sides The Ultimate Guide

Written by Oliver May 12, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you looking for a fresh, new haircut that will make you stand out from the crowd? If so, then you might want to consider getting a fade on the sides of your hair. This style has become increasingly popular among men in recent years, and for good reason – it looks great on just about anyone!

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get a Guys Haircuts Fade Sides
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Fade
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Guys with a Fade

Description

The fade haircut is characterized by a gradual tapering of the hair on the sides and back of the head. This creates a sharp, clean look that is perfect for guys who want a low-maintenance style that still looks stylish and modern.

The fade can be customized to suit your individual preferences. You can choose to have a high fade, where the hair is tapered very short near the top of the head, or a low fade, where the hair is tapered closer to the ears.

The fade can also be combined with other styles, such as a pompadour or a quiff, to create a more unique look that still incorporates the clean, sharp lines of the fade.

How to Get a Guys Haircuts Fade Sides

If you're interested in getting a fade on the sides of your hair, the first step is to find a skilled barber who can help you achieve the look you want. Look for a barber who has experience working with fades and who can show you examples of their work.

When you go in for your appointment, be sure to communicate clearly with your barber about the type of fade you want. Bring in pictures or examples of the style you're interested in, and be open to your barber's suggestions and recommendations.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting a fade on the sides of your hair:

  1. Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it.
  2. Sit in the barber chair and communicate clearly with your barber about the type of fade you want.
  3. Your barber will begin by using clippers to create the initial taper. They may use different clipper guards to achieve the desired length.
  4. Next, your barber will use scissors to blend the hair on the top of your head with the tapered sides.
  5. Finally, your barber will use a straight razor to clean up the edges and create a sharp, defined line.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fade

Once you've got your fade, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking sharp and fresh. Here are some tips to help you maintain your fade:

  • Get regular trims to keep the fade looking clean and well-defined.
  • Use a quality hair product, such as pomade or hair wax, to style your hair and keep it in place.
  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to keep your scalp healthy and your hair looking fresh.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to excessive heat, such as from hair dryers or straightening irons, as this can damage the hair and make the fade less defined.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about guys haircuts fade sides:

Q: How long does a fade last?

A: The length of time a fade lasts will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain the style. You'll likely need to get a trim every few weeks to keep the fade looking sharp.

Q: Can guys with curly or wavy hair get a fade?

A: Yes! Fades can look great on guys with all hair types, including curly and wavy hair. Your barber can adjust the tapering to suit your hair texture and create a look that works for you.

Hair Care Tips for Guys with a Fade

Here are some general hair care tips for guys with a fade:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to keep your scalp healthy and your hair looking fresh.
  • Use a quality hair product to style your hair and keep it in place.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to excessive heat, such as from hair dryers or straightening irons, as this can damage the hair and make the fade less defined.

With these tips and tricks, you can enjoy a stylish, low-maintenance look that will turn heads wherever you go. So why wait? Book an appointment with your barber today and get ready to rock your new fade!
