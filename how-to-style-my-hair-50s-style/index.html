How To Style My Hair 50S Style

Written by Daniel Aug 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you looking to add a touch of vintage glamour to your hair? The 50s style is a classic hairdo that never goes out of fashion. Whether you have long or short hair, you can easily achieve this retro look with a few tips and tricks. In this article, we'll show you how to style your hair 50s style and maintain it for a timeless look.

Description

The 50s hairstyle was characterized by voluminous curls and waves, with a side part and a smooth crown. Women in the 50s used rollers, curling irons, and hairpins to achieve this look. This hairstyle was popularized by Hollywood stars such as Marilyn Monroe, Audrey Hepburn, and Elizabeth Taylor. Today, this vintage hairstyle is still a favorite among women who want to look elegant and sophisticated.

How to

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add texture and volume. Towel dry your hair and apply a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from hot tools.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Apply a styling mousse or gel to your damp hair to give it hold and texture. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to add volume and curl to your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections and curl them using a curling iron or hot rollers. Start from the bottom and work your way up, curling away from your face. Pin each curl with a hairpin and let it cool down.

Step 4: Brush Out Your Curls

Once your hair has cooled down, gently brush out your curls using a paddle brush. This will create soft waves and add volume to your hair.

Step 5: Create a Side Part

Using a comb, create a deep side part on one side of your head. Smooth down the crown of your hair with a brush or comb.

Step 6: Pin Your Hair

Take a section of hair from the front and twist it towards the back of your head. Secure it with a hairpin behind your ear. Repeat on the other side.

Step 7: Finish with Hairspray

Finish off your 50s hairstyle with a generous spritz of hairspray to keep your curls in place.

Tips

- Use hot rollers or a curling iron to create tight curls for a more dramatic look.

- If you have short hair, you can still achieve a 50s hairstyle by using a hairpiece or faux bangs.

- Experiment with different hair accessories such as headbands, scarves, or flower clips to add a personal touch to your hairstyle.

FAQ

Q: Can I achieve a 50s hairstyle with straight hair?

A: Yes, you can achieve a 50s hairstyle with straight hair by using hot rollers or a curling iron to add curls and volume to your hair.

Q: How long does a 50s hairstyle last?

A: A 50s hairstyle can last for a few days if you maintain it properly. Use a silk scarf to protect your hair while you sleep and touch up your curls with a curling iron or hot rollers if needed.

Hair Care

To maintain your 50s hairstyle, use a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil and avoid washing your hair too frequently. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair nourished and healthy. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and always use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.

In conclusion, the 50s hairstyle is a timeless look that never goes out of style. Follow these simple steps to achieve a classic vintage look and make heads turn wherever you go. Don't forget to take care of your hair to maintain its health and beauty.
