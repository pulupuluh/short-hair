What Is The Best Drugstore Mousse For Curly Hair

Written by Frank Jul 09, 2023 Â· 12 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, you have voluminous, bouncy curls that can make heads turn. On the other hand, you have to deal with frizz, tangles, and lack of definition. Thankfully, drugstore mousses can help tame and enhance your curls without breaking the bank. But with so many options out there, how do you know which one is the best for your hair type, texture, and needs? In this article, we'll explore the top drugstore mousses for curly hair and give you tips on how to use them for optimal results.

Description

Mousse is a styling product that comes in a foamy, whipped, or creamy texture. It is designed to add volume, hold, and texture to hair without weighing it down or leaving it stiff. Mousse can be used on wet or dry hair and is compatible with most hair types, including curly hair. However, not all mousses are created equal, and some may contain ingredients that can dry out or damage your curls. That's why it's important to choose a mousse that is formulated for curly hair and contains nourishing ingredients that enhance your natural texture.

How to

Using mousse on curly hair requires a bit of technique and experimentation. Here are some general steps to follow:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair. You can either shampoo and condition your hair or co-wash it with a cleansing conditioner. Make sure to detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  2. Apply a palm-sized amount of mousse to your hair, starting from the roots and working your way to the ends. Use more or less depending on the thickness and length of your hair.
  3. Scrunch your hair to encourage the curls and distribute the mousse evenly. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair without disturbing the curls.
  4. Once your hair is dry, you can fluff your roots and scrunch your curls again to add more volume and definition.

Step by Step

Here's a more detailed guide on how to use mousse on curly hair:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you apply mousse, you should start with clean, damp hair. This means either shampooing and conditioning your hair or co-washing it with a cleansing conditioner. Cleansing conditioners are a great option for curly hair because they gently remove dirt and buildup without stripping the natural oils from your hair. They also condition and detangle your hair at the same time, which saves you time and money.

Once you've cleansed your hair, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls. This will help prevent breakage and ensure that your curls are evenly coated with mousse.

Step 2: Apply Mousse

Take a palm-sized amount of mousse and rub it between your hands to activate the foam. Then, apply the mousse to your hair, starting from the roots and working your way to the ends. Make sure to distribute the mousse evenly and avoid applying too much or too little.

If you have fine or thin hair, you may want to use a mousse that is specifically designed for your hair type. Look for mousses that are lightweight and volumizing, and avoid those that are too heavy or sticky.

If you have thick or coarse hair, you may want to use a mousse that is rich and hydrating. Look for mousses that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, or argan oil, which can nourish and soften your curls.

Step 3: Scrunch Your Hair

Once you've applied the mousse, it's time to scrunch your hair to encourage the curls and distribute the product. To scrunch your hair, cup your curls with your hands and gently squeeze them upwards towards your scalp. This will help create more definition and volume, and prevent your curls from falling flat.

If you want to enhance your curls even more, you can use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer. A diffuser is a tool that disperses the airflow from your dryer and reduces the amount of heat that comes into contact with your hair. This can help dry your hair without disturbing the curls or causing frizz.

Step 4: Finish Your Style

Once your hair is dry, you can fluff your roots and scrunch your curls again to add more volume and definition. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or a hair oil to seal in moisture and add shine to your curls.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when using mousse on curly hair:

  • Choose a mousse that is alcohol-free, sulfate-free, and silicone-free. These ingredients can dry out and damage your curls, especially if you use them frequently.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove buildup from your hair and scalp. This will help your hair absorb the mousse better and prevent product overload.
  • Apply mousse in sections to ensure that all of your curls are coated. You can use clips or hair ties to section off your hair and work on one area at a time.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying. This can create frizz and disturb the curls. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on a low speed and low heat setting.
  • Experiment with different amounts and types of mousse to find the one that works best for your hair. You may need to adjust the amount or frequency of use depending on the weather, humidity, or styling preferences.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about using mousse on curly hair:

1. Is mousse better than gel for curly hair?

It depends on your hair type, texture, and preference. Mousse is generally lighter and less sticky than gel, and can provide more volume and texture. Gel, on the other hand, can provide more hold and definition, but may leave your hair feeling crunchy or weighed down. Some people prefer to use both mousse and gel for optimal results.

2. Can mousse damage curly hair?

Mousse itself is not harmful to curly hair, but some mousses may contain ingredients that can dry out or damage your curls, such as alcohol or sulfates. That's why it's important to choose a mousse that is formulated for curly hair and contains nourishing ingredients that enhance your natural texture.

3. How often should I use mousse on curly hair?

It depends on your hair's needs and how often you wash and style your hair. Some people use mousse every time they wash their hair, while others use it only on special occasions or when they want to enhance their curls. If you use mousse frequently, make sure to clarify your hair once a month to prevent buildup and damage.

Hair Care

Using the best drugstore mousse for curly hair can help you achieve the curls of your dreams without spending a fortune. However, it's important to choose a mousse that is compatible with your hair type and contains nourishing ingredients that enhance your natural texture. By following the tips and techniques outlined in this article, you can create bouncy, defined, and healthy curls that will make you feel confident and beautiful.
