How To Cut Really Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Feb 22, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Cutting really curly hair can be intimidating, but with the right techniques and tools, it can be easy and rewarding. In this article, we'll go through the steps and tips you need to know to cut really curly hair like a pro. Whether you're a professional stylist or just cutting your own hair, this guide will help you achieve the best results.

Table of Contents

  • Description of Really Curly Hair
  • How to Cut Really Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Really Curly Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Really Curly Hair

Description of Really Curly Hair

Really curly hair is characterized by tight, coiled curls that can range from loose to tightly wound. It can be thick or fine and can be prone to frizz and dryness. The key to cutting really curly hair is to take into consideration the curl pattern, density, and texture to achieve the desired result.

How to Cut Really Curly Hair

Cutting really curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Cut Dry

Cutting curly hair when it's wet can result in a shorter length than intended because the curls will shrink when they dry. To avoid this, cut the hair when it's dry.

2. Use the Right Tools

Use sharp scissors or shears to cut curly hair. Blunt scissors can result in split ends and damage the hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair before cutting.

3. Cut in Small Sections

Cutting curly hair in large sections can result in uneven layers. Instead, work in small sections, starting at the back and working your way to the front.

4. Embrace the Curl

Cutting curly hair straight across can result in a triangular shape. Instead, cut the hair in a curved shape to enhance the curl pattern.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics of cutting really curly hair, here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Detangle the Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Section the hair into small sections using hair clips.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Cut the hair in a curved shape, following the natural curl pattern. Cut the hair in small sections, starting at the back and working your way to the front.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

Check for evenness by comparing each section to the previous section you cut. Make any necessary adjustments.

Step 5: Style the Hair

Style the hair as desired, using a diffuser to enhance the curl pattern.

Tips for Cutting Really Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for cutting really curly hair:

  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to cut a little at a time than to cut too much and regret it.
  • Don't pull the hair too tight when cutting. This can result in uneven layers and damage to the hair.
  • Avoid using razors to cut curly hair. Razors can damage the hair and result in split ends.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting really curly hair:

  • How often should I cut my curly hair? It's recommended to cut curly hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.
  • Can I cut my own curly hair? Yes, you can cut your own curly hair, but it's recommended to have a professional do it if you're not experienced.
  • Should I cut my curly hair when it's wet or dry? It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry to avoid shrinkage.

Hair Care for Really Curly Hair

Caring for really curly hair is essential to maintain its health and shape. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid drying out the hair.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to nourish and hydrate the hair.
  • Avoid using heat tools on curly hair as it can damage the hair.
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase to avoid frizziness and breakage.

Now that you know how to cut really curly hair, give it a try and see how easy it can be. With the right techniques and tools, you can achieve the best results for your curly hair.
