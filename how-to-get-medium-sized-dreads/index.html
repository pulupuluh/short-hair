How To Get Medium Sized Dreads A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Feb 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They come in various sizes, from small to medium to large. If you're interested in getting medium-sized dreads, this guide is for you. In this article, we'll go over how to get medium-sized dreads, from prepping your hair to maintenance tips.

Table of Contents

  1. Prepping Your Hair
  2. Sectioning Your Hair
  3. Rolling Your Hair
  4. Interlocking Your Hair
  5. Palm Rolling Your Hair
  6. Maintenance Tips
  7. FAQs
  8. Hair Care

Prepping Your Hair

Before you start the dreadlocking process, you need to prep your hair. First, wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo to remove any buildup. Then, dry your hair completely. Next, part your hair into sections to make the process easier. You can use hair ties or clips to secure each section.

Sectioning Your Hair

The size of your sections will determine the size of your dreads. For medium-sized dreads, aim for sections that are about the width of a pencil. Use a comb to part your hair, and make sure the sections are even. You can also use a rat tail comb to create a zig-zag pattern for a more natural look.

Rolling Your Hair

Rolling your hair is an essential step in the dreadlocking process. It helps to shape your hair into the desired size and shape. To roll your hair, you'll need a dread comb and some dread wax. Start at the roots and comb your hair towards the ends, applying wax as you go. Use your fingers to roll your hair into a cylinder shape. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Interlocking Your Hair

Interlocking is a method used to tighten and maintain dreadlocks. It involves using a tool to pull hair through existing dreadlocks, creating a knot. To interlock your hair, you'll need a small interlocking tool. Starting at the root, insert the tool through the dread and pull it through to the other side. Then, pull the hair through the loop and tighten. Repeat this process for each dread.

Palm Rolling Your Hair

Palm rolling is another method used to tighten and maintain dreadlocks. It involves rolling the dreads between your palms to smooth out any bumps and help them lock together. To palm roll your hair, start at the root and roll the dread between your palms, moving towards the ends. Repeat this process for each dread.

Maintenance Tips

Once you have your medium-sized dreads, it's essential to maintain them to keep them looking great. Here are some maintenance tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with residue-free shampoo
  • Avoid using heavy oils and products that can weigh down your dreads
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage
  • Retwist your dreads every few weeks to maintain their shape

FAQs

How long does it take to get medium-sized dreads?

The length of time it takes to get medium-sized dreads depends on your hair type and how you choose to lock your hair. On average, it can take anywhere from six months to a year to achieve medium-sized dreads.

Can I get medium-sized dreads if my hair is relaxed?

Yes, you can get medium-sized dreads if your hair is relaxed. However, it's essential to make sure your hair is healthy and strong before starting the dreadlocking process.

Do I need to use wax to lock my hair?

No, you don't need to use wax to lock your hair. While wax can help to shape and hold your dreads, it can also lead to buildup and make it harder to maintain your dreads in the long run. You can use other methods, such as interlocking and palm rolling, to maintain your dreads.

Hair Care

Having dreadlocks requires a different hair care routine than other hairstyles. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair regularly
  • Use a residue-free shampoo
  • Condition your hair with a light oil, such as jojoba oil or argan oil
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your dreads
  • Protect your hair at night with a silk or satin pillowcase

By following these tips, you can keep your medium-sized dreads looking great for years to come.

Getting medium-sized dreads can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve the look you want and maintain healthy hair. Follow this guide, and you'll be on your way to having great-looking dreads in no time.
