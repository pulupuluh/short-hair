Do Fringes Suit Small Faces Everything You Need To Know

Written by Daniel Aug 12, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have a small face, you might be wondering if fringes are the right hairstyle for you. The good news is that fringes can look great on small faces, but it's important to choose the right type of fringe that flatters your features. In this article, we'll explore everything you need to know about fringes for small faces, from how to choose the right one to how to care for it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose the Right Fringe
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Rocking a Fringe
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Fringes

How to Choose the Right Fringe

When it comes to fringes for small faces, there are a few key things to keep in mind:

Consider Your Face Shape

The first thing to consider is your face shape. If you have a small face with delicate features, you'll want to avoid heavy or blunt fringes that can overwhelm your face. Instead, opt for a softer, wispy fringe that frames your face without overpowering it.

Think About Your Hair Texture

Your hair texture can also play a role in the type of fringe that will suit you best. If you have fine hair, a blunt fringe can make your hair look thinner, so a softer, layered fringe might be a better option. On the other hand, if you have thick hair, a blunt fringe can help to tame your hair and give it a more polished look.

Consider Your Lifestyle

Finally, think about your lifestyle and how much time you're willing to spend styling your fringe. If you're low-maintenance, a longer fringe that can be swept to the side might be a better option than a shorter, more structured fringe that requires daily maintenance.

Step-by-Step Guide

Once you've chosen the right type of fringe for your face and hair, here's how to style it:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Begin by washing and drying your hair. You'll want to start with a clean, dry base so that your fringe will lay flat and stay in place.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, use a comb to section off the hair that will become your fringe. You can choose to have a center part or a side part, depending on your preference.

Step 3: Cut Your Fringe

If you're cutting your fringe yourself, be sure to start with small snips and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's gone!

Step 4: Style Your Fringe

Once your fringe is cut to the desired length, you can style it using a round brush and a blow dryer. Use the brush to direct the hair in the direction you want it to lay, and use the blow dryer to set the style in place.

Tips for Rocking a Fringe

Here are a few tips for making the most of your fringe:

Experiment with Different Styles

Don't be afraid to try different styles with your fringe, such as sweeping it to the side, parting it in the middle, or wearing it straight across. You might be surprised at the difference a small change can make!

Accessorize Your Fringe

You can also accessorize your fringe with clips, headbands, or even a simple bobby pin. This can help to keep your fringe out of your face and add some extra style to your look.

Be Prepared for Maintenance

Remember that fringes require maintenance, so be prepared for regular trims and touch-ups to keep your fringe looking its best. If you're not comfortable cutting your own fringe, visit a professional stylist for regular maintenance.

FAQ

Can I wear a fringe with glasses?

Yes, you can definitely wear a fringe with glasses! Just be sure to choose a style that doesn't interfere with your glasses, such as a longer, side-swept fringe.

Can I wear a fringe with curly hair?

Absolutely! In fact, a fringe can be a great way to add some structure to curly hair. Just be sure to choose a soft, wispy fringe that complements your curls.

What if I don't like my fringe?

If you're not happy with your fringe, don't panic! You can always grow it out or have it trimmed by a professional stylist. Just be patient and give it some time to grow before you make any drastic changes.

Hair Care Tips for Fringes

Finally, here are some hair care tips to keep your fringe looking healthy and beautiful:

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Be sure to wash your hair regularly to keep your fringe looking clean and fresh. If you have an oily scalp, you may need to wash your hair more frequently than someone with a dry scalp.

Avoid Heat Damage

Heat styling tools can damage your hair, so try to avoid using them as much as possible. If you do use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Condition Your Hair

Conditioner can help to keep your hair and fringe looking soft and healthy. Be sure to use a conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type, and avoid applying it directly to your roots.

In conclusion, fringes can look great on small faces, as long as you choose the right type of fringe and take good care of it. Experiment with different styles and don't be afraid to ask a professional stylist for advice if you're unsure. With a little bit of effort, you can rock a beautiful fringe that flatters your features and adds some extra personality to your look!
