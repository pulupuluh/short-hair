How To Cut Your Own Curly Hair In Layers Step By Step

Written by Luffy Sep 03, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how important it is to keep it healthy and styled. One way to achieve this is by cutting your hair in layers. This technique can add volume and shape to your curls, making them look more defined and bouncy. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting your own curly hair in layers step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Curly Hair in Layers
  • Step by Step Guide

How to Cut Your Own Curly Hair in Layers

Cutting your own curly hair in layers can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done easily. Before you start, make sure you have the following:

  • A pair of hair cutting scissors
  • A fine-toothed comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Once you have these tools, you are ready to start cutting your hair. Follow the step by step guide below.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Detangle Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, wash it with shampoo and conditioner. Once you have washed your hair, use a fine-toothed comb to detangle it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into two parts by parting it down the middle. Then, divide each of these sections into two by parting them from the top of your head to the back of your neck.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start by cutting the bottom layer of your hair. Take one of the sections you created in step 2 and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the ends. Repeat this for the other three sections.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

After you have cut the bottom layer, move on to the middle layer. Take one of the sections and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends at an angle. This will create a layered effect. Repeat this for the other three sections.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, move on to the top layer. Take one of the sections and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends at a steeper angle than the middle layer. This will create a more defined layered effect. Repeat this for the other three sections.

Step 6: Check for Balance

Once you have finished cutting your hair, check for balance. Make sure both sides are even and adjust as needed.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair in Layers

Cutting curly hair in layers can be tricky, but these tips can help make the process easier:

  • Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it is dry. Cutting curly hair when it is wet can result in uneven layers.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create uneven layers.
  • Take your time. Cutting curly hair in layers requires patience and precision.
  • Start with small cuts. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it is cut.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my curly hair in layers without damaging it?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair in layers without damaging it as long as you use sharp hair cutting scissors and follow the right technique.

How do I know if I need layers in my curly hair?

If you have curly hair that is flat at the top and puffy at the bottom, you may benefit from layers. Layers can add volume and shape to your curls, making them look more defined and bouncy.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

After cutting your curly hair in layers, it is important to take care of it to keep it healthy and styled. Here are some hair care tips for curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to detangle it when it is wet.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, like flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.

Now that you know how to cut your own curly hair in layers, you can achieve a stylish and healthy look at home. Follow these steps and tips for best results. Happy cutting!
