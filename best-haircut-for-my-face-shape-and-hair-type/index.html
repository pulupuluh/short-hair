Best Haircut For My Face Shape And Hair Type

Written by Christine May 24, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're looking for the best haircut for your face shape and hair type, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll give you tips on how to find the perfect haircut that will flatter your face shape and enhance your hair type. We'll also provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to achieve the look you want, as well as some hair care tips to keep your locks healthy and shiny.

How to Find Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the best haircut for your face shape is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: round, oval, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, look in the mirror and take note of the following:

  • The length of your face compared to the width
  • The shape of your jawline
  • The shape of your hairline
  • The width of your forehead
  • The length of your chin

Once you've determined your face shape, you can move on to the next step.

Different Hair Types

Before we discuss the best haircut for your face shape, it's important to understand your hair type. There are four basic hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and kinky. Each hair type has its own unique characteristics and requires different care and maintenance.

Straight hair is usually shiny and smooth, but can be prone to oiliness. Wavy hair is usually thicker and has a natural wave pattern. Curly hair can range from loose waves to tight coils and requires more moisture than other hair types. Kinky hair is very tightly coiled and requires a lot of moisture and careful handling.

Best Haircut for Your Face Shape and Hair Type

Now that you know your face shape and hair type, it's time to find the best haircut for you. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Round face shape: go for hairstyles that add height and length, such as a long bob or a pixie cut with volume on top.
  • Oval face shape: you're lucky, as most hairstyles suit this face shape. Experiment with different lengths and styles.
  • Square face shape: soften your angles with a layered haircut or a side-swept fringe.
  • Heart face shape: balance your features with a chin-length bob or a side parting.
  • Diamond face shape: highlight your cheekbones with a textured bob or a shag haircut.
  • Oblong face shape: add width to your face with a chin-length bob or a layered haircut with volume at the sides.

When it comes to hair type, consider the following:

  • Straight hair: go for blunt cuts or long layers to add volume and movement.
  • Wavy hair: embrace your natural texture with a layered haircut or beachy waves.
  • Curly hair: opt for a layered haircut that enhances your curls and reduces bulk.
  • Kinky hair: try a tapered cut or a protective style such as braids or twists.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to achieve the best haircut for your face shape and hair type:

  1. Research hairstyles and haircuts that suit your face shape and hair type.
  2. Show pictures to your hairstylist and discuss your options.
  3. Ask your hairstylist for their professional opinion and advice.
  4. Decide on a length and style that you feel comfortable with.
  5. Make sure your hairstylist understands your hair type and knows how to work with it.
  6. Ask for tips on how to style and maintain your new haircut.
  7. Book regular appointments for trims and touch-ups.
  8. Experiment with different styling products and techniques to find what works best for you.
  9. Take good care of your hair with a healthy diet, regular exercise, and quality hair care products.
  10. Enjoy your new look!

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to keep your locks healthy and shiny:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons and curling irons too often.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Avoid brushing wet hair, as it can cause breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause damage.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth and strength.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about finding the best haircut for your face shape and hair type:

Q: Can I still wear the hairstyle I want even if it doesn't suit my face shape?

A: Yes, of course! These guidelines are just suggestions, and ultimately you should wear the hairstyle that makes you feel confident and happy.

Q: Can I change my hair type?

A: No, your hair type is determined by your genetics. However, you can use styling products and techniques to enhance your natural texture.

Q: How often should I get my haircut?

A: It depends on your hair type and length, but generally you should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Now that you know how to find the best haircut for your face shape and hair type, it's time to book that appointment with your hairstylist. Remember to bring pictures, ask for advice, and have fun experimenting with different styles!
