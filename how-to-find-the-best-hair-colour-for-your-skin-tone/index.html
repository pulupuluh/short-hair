How To Find The Best Hair Colour For Your Skin Tone

Choosing the best hair colour for your skin tone can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. By following a few simple steps, you can find the perfect hair colour that complements your skin tone and enhances your natural beauty. In this article, we'll go over how to find the best hair colour for your skin tone, tips for choosing the right shade, and how to care for your newly coloured hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Find the Best Hair Colour for Your Skin Tone
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Find the Best Hair Colour for Your Skin Tone

The first step in finding the best hair colour for your skin tone is to determine your skin's undertone. Undertones are the colours that are underneath the surface of your skin, and they can be warm, cool, or neutral. To determine your skin's undertone, look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they appear blue or purple, you have cool undertones. If they appear green, you have warm undertones. If you can't tell if they are blue or green, you likely have neutral undertones.

Once you determine your skin's undertone, you can use the following chart to determine which hair colours will complement your skin tone:

As you can see from the chart, if you have cool undertones, you'll want to stick with hair colours that have cool tones, such as ash blonde, platinum, or cool brown. If you have warm undertones, you'll want to stick with hair colours that have warm tones, such as golden blonde, copper, or warm brown. If you have neutral undertones, you can wear both warm and cool hair colours.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to find the best hair colour for your skin tone:

  1. Determine your skin's undertone by looking at the veins on the inside of your wrist.
  2. Use the hair colour chart above to determine which hair colours will complement your skin tone.
  3. Consider your natural hair colour and how much maintenance you're willing to do. If you want a low-maintenance look, choose a hair colour that is close to your natural hair colour.
  4. If you're not sure which hair colour to choose, consult with a hair stylist. They can help you find the perfect hair colour for your skin tone and lifestyle.

Tips for Choosing the Right Shade

Here are some additional tips for choosing the right hair colour:

  • Consider your eye colour. If you have blue or green eyes, cool-toned hair colours will bring out the colour in your eyes. If you have brown eyes, warm-toned hair colours will complement your eyes.
  • Consider your wardrobe. If you wear a lot of cool colours, such as blues and greens, cool-toned hair colours will complement your wardrobe. If you wear a lot of warm colours, such as reds and oranges, warm-toned hair colours will complement your wardrobe.
  • If you're nervous about a drastic change, start with subtle highlights or lowlights to add dimension to your natural hair colour.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about finding the best hair colour for your skin tone:

Can I dye my hair a different colour if I have warm undertones?
Yes, you can dye your hair any colour you like, but some colours may look better than others. If you have warm undertones, stick with warm hair colours to complement your skin tone.
What hair colours should I avoid if I have cool undertones?
Avoid hair colours with warm tones, such as golden blonde or copper. These colours can clash with your cool undertones and make your skin look washed out.
How often should I touch up my hair colour?
It depends on the type of hair colour you choose and how fast your hair grows. Some hair colours require touch-ups every 4-6 weeks, while others can last up to 12 weeks. Consult with your hair stylist to determine the best maintenance plan for your hair.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you've found the perfect hair colour for your skin tone, it's important to take care of your newly coloured hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, too often. Heat can damage coloured hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause your colour to fade faster.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to find the perfect hair colour for your skin tone and keep it looking its best. Remember to consult with a hair stylist if you're unsure which hair colour to choose or how to maintain your newly coloured hair.
