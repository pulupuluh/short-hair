How To Cut Men s Hair With Scissors For Dummies A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Welcome to our beginner's guide on how to cut men's hair with scissors! If you're a newbie to haircuts, don't worry. We've got you covered with our easy-to-follow steps and tips. Whether you're looking to save money or just want to try your hand at a new skill, cutting men's hair with scissors is a great place to start. Let's get started!

Description

Cutting men's hair with scissors is a great way to achieve a neat and tidy look. It's also a more affordable option than going to the barber or hair salon. The process requires only a few tools and some basic techniques. With a little practice, you'll be able to master the art of cutting men's hair with scissors in no time.

How to Cut Men's Hair with Scissors for Dummies

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp to avoid uneven cuts. A comb will help you section the hair and keep it in place. Hair clips will help you keep the sections of hair separated. And a spray bottle filled with water will help you dampen the hair to make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Prep the Hair

Start by washing the hair and drying it thoroughly. Comb the hair to remove any tangles or knots. Then, use the hair clips to separate the hair into sections. Start with the bottom section and work your way up.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it with the scissors. Start at the tip of the hair and work your way up. Make sure to cut a little bit at a time to avoid cutting too much off.

Step 4: Continue Cutting

Work your way around the head, cutting small sections of hair at a time. As you cut, comb the hair to make sure it's even. Use the hair clips to keep the sections of hair separated.

Step 5: Trim the Sides and Back

Once you've finished cutting the top of the hair, it's time to move on to the sides and back. Take a small section of hair from the side or back and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it with the scissors. Use the same technique as before, starting at the tip and working your way up.

Step 6: Blend the Hair

After you've finished cutting the sides and back, it's time to blend the hair. Use the scissors to cut the hair at an angle, creating a smooth transition between the different lengths of hair.

Step 7: Check for Any Unevenness

Once you've finished cutting, check the hair for any unevenness. Use the scissors to trim any areas that need it.

Tips

  • Take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time.
  • Use a comb to keep the hair in place.
  • Keep the scissors sharp to avoid uneven cuts.
  • Don't cut too much off at once.
  • Blend the hair to create a smooth transition between the different lengths.

FAQ

Can I cut men's hair with regular scissors?

No, it's best to use scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Regular scissors may not be sharp enough and can cause uneven cuts.

What's the best way to hold the hair while cutting?

Hold the hair between your fingers, with your fingers pointing towards the floor. This will help you cut the hair evenly.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on the person and the style of hair. Generally, hair should be cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and style.

Hair Care

After you've finished cutting the hair, it's important to take care of it. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type. Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can cause damage. And don't forget to get regular trims to maintain your style.

Cutting men's hair with scissors may seem daunting at first, but with a little practice, you'll be able to master the basics. Follow these steps and tips, and you'll be on your way to creating a neat and tidy look for yourself or someone else. Good luck!
