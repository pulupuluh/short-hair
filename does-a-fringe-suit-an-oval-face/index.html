Does A Fringe Suit An Oval Face

Written by Bardi Mar 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you considering getting a fringe but not quite sure if it's the right decision for your oval-shaped face? Don't worry, you're not alone! The good news is that fringes can look great on oval faces, but it all depends on the type of fringe and how it's styled. In this article, we'll explore the different types of fringes that suit an oval face and provide some tips on how to style them.

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose the Right Fringe for an Oval Face
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling a Fringe for an Oval Face
  • Tips for Maintaining a Fringe on an Oval Face
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for an Oval Face with a Fringe

How to Choose the Right Fringe for an Oval Face

When it comes to choosing a fringe for an oval face, there are a few things to consider. First, you'll want to think about the length of the fringe. Generally, shorter fringes work better for oval faces as they help to balance out the face shape. However, if you have a high forehead, a longer fringe can work well to cover it up.

Another important factor to consider is the type of fringe. There are several different types of fringes, including straight, side-swept, and choppy. Straight fringes work well for oval faces, as do side-swept fringes that are angled to one side. Choppy fringes can also work well, but they're best suited to those with thicker hair.

Lastly, you'll want to consider the thickness of the fringe. If you have thick hair, a thicker fringe can work well to balance out the face shape. However, if you have thinner hair, a thinner fringe may be more suitable.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling a Fringe for an Oval Face

Once you've chosen the right type of fringe for your oval face, it's important to know how to style it. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

  1. Start by blow-drying your hair, making sure to direct the airflow downwards to prevent frizz.
  2. Next, use a round brush to smooth out your fringe, curling it under slightly at the ends.
  3. If you want a straight fringe, use a flat iron to straighten it out.
  4. For a side-swept fringe, use the round brush to sweep the fringe to one side and pin it in place with a bobby pin.
  5. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your fringe in place.

Tips for Maintaining a Fringe on an Oval Face

Now that you've got your fringe looking great, it's important to know how to maintain it. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking neat and tidy.
  • Use dry shampoo to keep your fringe looking fresh between washes.
  • Avoid touching your fringe too much, as this can make it oily.
  • Consider using a silk pillowcase to prevent your fringe from getting tangled while you sleep.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a fringe make an oval face look shorter?

Yes, a shorter fringe can help to balance out the length of an oval face, making it appear shorter.

Can a fringe make an oval face look wider?

No, a fringe shouldn't make an oval face look wider. As long as you choose the right type of fringe for your face shape, it should help to balance out your features.

Can a fringe hide a high forehead?

Yes, a longer fringe can help to cover up a high forehead and balance out an oval face.

Hair Care Tips for an Oval Face with a Fringe

Lastly, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Here are a few hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair from drying out.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it over time.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Brush your hair gently to prevent breakage.

So, does a fringe suit an oval face? The answer is yes, as long as you choose the right type of fringe and style it properly. Whether you opt for a straight, side-swept, or choppy fringe, just remember to keep it trimmed and maintained to keep it looking great. With these tips and tricks, you'll have a fringe that suits your oval face perfectly!
