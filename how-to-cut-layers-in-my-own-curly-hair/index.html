How To Cut Layers In My Own Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 03, 2023 · 9 min read
If you're looking to add some volume and definition to your curly hair, cutting layers is a great option. However, going to a salon can be expensive, and it's not always easy to find a stylist who understands your curls. That's why we've put together this guide on how to cut layers in your own curly hair. With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a beautiful layered look right at home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Layers in My Own Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers in Curly Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Description

Layering curly hair can create more bounce and movement in your locks, which can help to make your curls look more defined. Curly hair tends to have a lot of volume, so cutting layers can help to reduce some of that volume while adding shape and structure to your curls.

Before you start cutting, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a spray bottle filled with water, and a wide-tooth comb. It's also a good idea to have a handheld mirror and a larger mirror so that you can see the back of your head.

How to Cut Layers in My Own Curly Hair

Now that you have everything you need, it's time to get started. Here's how to cut layers in your own curly hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair as you normally would.
  2. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  3. Tilt your head forward and shake your hair out, so that your curls fall forward.
  4. Take a small section of hair at the nape of your neck and comb it straight down.
  5. Hold the section of hair between your index and middle fingers, about an inch or two from the ends.
  6. Use your scissors to cut the ends of the hair at a slight angle, moving your fingers upwards as you cut.
  7. Repeat this process, working your way up through the back of your head and then moving to the sides.
  8. As you cut each section, make sure to keep the sections even in length.
  9. Once you've cut all of the layers, shake out your hair and check for any uneven spots.
  10. If you need to make any adjustments, go back in and trim the ends as needed.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Curly Hair

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you've never done it before. Here are a few tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with small sections of hair, and work your way up to larger sections as you get more comfortable.
  • Cut your hair while it's wet, as this will help you to see the shape and structure of your curls more clearly.
  • Don't cut too much off at once - it's better to start with a small trim and then gradually work your way up to more layers.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head as you go.
  • Remember that curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so don't cut your layers too short.

FAQ

Here are some common questions and answers about cutting layers in curly hair:

Can I cut layers in my curly hair if I have thin hair?

Yes, you can still cut layers in your curly hair if you have thin hair. Just be sure to start with small sections and only cut off a little bit at a time.

Should I cut my curly hair when it's dry or wet?

It's best to cut your curly hair when it's wet, as this will help you to see the shape and structure of your curls more clearly.

Can I cut layers in my own hair if I have short hair?

Yes, you can still cut layers in your own hair if you have short hair. Just be sure to start with small sections and work your way up to larger sections as you get more comfortable.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Cutting your own hair is just one part of a healthy hair care routine. Here are some tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your curls.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and using our step-by-step guide, you can cut layers in your own curly hair and achieve a beautiful, voluminous look. Happy cutting!
