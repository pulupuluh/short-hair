How To Care For Black Hair After Keratin Treatment

Written by William Aug 25, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Getting a keratin treatment for your black hair can be a game changer. It can leave your hair smooth, shiny, and frizz-free. However, it's important to take care of your hair properly after the treatment to ensure that it stays healthy and beautiful. In this article, we will discuss how to care for black hair after keratin treatment.

How to Care for Black Hair After Keratin Treatment

After getting a keratin treatment for your black hair, there are a few things you should keep in mind to maintain the quality and health of your hair. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid washing your hair for at least 48 hours

After getting a keratin treatment, your hair needs time to settle and absorb the treatment. Avoid washing your hair for at least 48 hours after the treatment. This will ensure that the keratin is fully absorbed and will help to extend the life of your treatment.

2. Use sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner

Once you start washing your hair after the 48-hour period, make sure to use sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Sulfates can strip the keratin from your hair, which will reduce the longevity of your treatment. Look for products that are specifically designed for keratin-treated hair.

3. Don't tie your hair up

Avoid tying your hair up for at least 48 hours after the treatment. This will prevent kinks from forming in your hair and will help to maintain the straightness of the keratin treatment. After the 48-hour period, you can tie your hair up, but make sure to use a soft hair tie to prevent any kinks or dents in your hair.

4. Avoid using heat styling tools

Try to avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, for at least a week after the treatment. Heat can damage the keratin in your hair and reduce the longevity of your treatment. If you must use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

5. Use a silk pillowcase

Using a silk pillowcase can help to prevent frizz and breakage in your hair. Silk is gentle on hair and won't cause any friction, which can lead to frizz and breakage. It also helps to maintain the straightness of your keratin treatment.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to care for black hair after keratin treatment:

Step 1: Wait at least 48 hours before washing your hair

Give your hair time to absorb the keratin treatment by avoiding washing your hair for at least 48 hours after the treatment.

Step 2: Use sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner

When you do start washing your hair, make sure to use sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. This will help to maintain the keratin in your hair and extend the life of your treatment.

Step 3: Avoid tying your hair up

Avoid tying your hair up for at least 48 hours after the treatment to prevent any kinks or dents from forming in your hair.

Step 4: Avoid using heat styling tools

Try to avoid using heat styling tools for at least a week after the treatment. If you must use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Step 5: Use a silk pillowcase

Using a silk pillowcase can help to prevent frizz and breakage in your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Your Keratin-Treated Black Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining your keratin-treated black hair:

1. Get regular trims

Getting regular trims will help to prevent split ends and breakage in your hair. This will help to maintain the health of your hair and extend the life of your keratin treatment.

2. Use a leave-in conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner can help to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Look for a leave-in conditioner that is specifically designed for keratin-treated hair.

3. Avoid chlorine and saltwater

Chlorine and saltwater can damage your hair and reduce the longevity of your keratin treatment. Avoid swimming in chlorinated or saltwater pools for at least a week after the treatment.

4. Protect your hair from the sun

The sun can damage your hair and reduce the longevity of your keratin treatment. Use a hat or scarf to protect your hair when you're out in the sun.

FAQs

1. How long does a keratin treatment last?

A keratin treatment can last anywhere from 2-6 months, depending on how well you take care of your hair after the treatment.

2. Can I wash my hair after a keratin treatment?

You should wait at least 48 hours before washing your hair after a keratin treatment.

3. Can I tie my hair up after a keratin treatment?

Avoid tying your hair up for at least 48 hours after the treatment to prevent any kinks or dents from forming in your hair.

Black Hair Care

Taking care of black hair can be a challenge, but it's important to maintain the health of your hair to prevent breakage and damage. Here are some tips for black hair care:

1. Moisturize regularly

Black hair can be prone to dryness, so it's important to moisturize regularly. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

2. Avoid heat styling tools

Heat styling tools can damage black hair and lead to breakage. Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb

Avoid using a brush on black hair, as it can cause breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb instead, and start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to prevent tangles and breakage.

4. Protect your hair at night

Using a silk or satin pillowcase can help to prevent frizz and breakage in black hair. You can also use a satin or silk scarf to protect your hair while you sleep.

By following these tips, you can maintain the health and beauty of your keratin-treated black hair. Remember to be gentle with your hair and avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools. With proper care, your keratin treatment can last for months, leaving you with smooth, shiny, and frizz-free hair.
