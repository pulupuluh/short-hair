How To Know If You Have Thinning Hair

Written by Aprilia Jul 27, 2023 Â· 11 min read

Are you worried that your hair may be thinning? Thinning hair is a common problem that affects both men and women. It can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, stress, and poor nutrition. If you're concerned that your hair may be thinning, here are some signs to look out for:

1. Your Hair Is Falling Out More Than Usual

One of the most obvious signs of thinning hair is increased hair shedding. If you're noticing more hair in your brush or on your pillow than usual, it could be a sign that your hair is thinning. While it's normal to lose some hair every day, if you're shedding more than 100 hairs per day, it's time to take action.

2. Your Scalp Is More Visible

If you're noticing that your scalp is becoming more visible, it could be a sign that your hair is thinning. This is especially true if you're seeing more scalp in areas where your hair used to be thick. Take a closer look in the mirror and see if you can see more of your scalp than you used to.

3. Your Hair Is Getting Thinner

If your hair is starting to look thinner overall, it could be a sign that it's beginning to thin. This can be especially noticeable around the crown of your head or at your hairline. If you're noticing that your ponytail is getting thinner or that you can see more of your scalp when you part your hair, it's time to take action.

4. You're Noticing More Breakage

Thin hair is often more fragile than thick hair, which means it's more prone to breakage. If you're noticing more breakage than usual, it could be a sign that your hair is thinning. This can be especially noticeable when you're brushing or styling your hair.

5. Your Hair Is Taking Longer to Grow

If you're noticing that your hair is taking longer to grow than it used to, it could be a sign that your hair is thinning. This is because thin hair is often weaker and more prone to damage, which can slow down the growth process.

6. You're Seeing More Hair on Your Clothes

If you're noticing more hair on your clothes than usual, it could be a sign that your hair is thinning. This is because thin hair is more likely to fall out throughout the day, which can leave more hair on your clothes.

7. Your Hair Feels Different

If your hair is feeling different than it used to, it could be a sign that it's thinning. This can include a change in texture, such as your hair feeling more dry or brittle than usual. It can also include a change in the way your hair falls or how it looks when you style it.

8. You're Noticing More Scalp Irritation

If you're experiencing more scalp irritation than usual, it could be a sign that your hair is thinning. This is because thin hair is often more sensitive and can be more prone to irritation. If you're experiencing redness, itching, or flaking on your scalp, it's time to take action.

9. Your Hairline Is Receding

If you're noticing that your hairline is receding, it could be a sign that your hair is thinning. This is especially true if you're seeing more of your forehead than you used to. Receding hairlines are more common in men, but they can also affect women.

10. You Have a Family History of Hair Loss

If you have a family history of hair loss, you may be more prone to thinning hair. Genetics can play a big role in hair loss, so if your parents or grandparents experienced hair loss, it's important to be aware of the signs of thinning hair.

How to Address Thinning Hair

If you're noticing any of these signs of thinning hair, it's important to take action. Here are some steps you can take to address thinning hair:

1. Talk to Your Doctor

If you're concerned about thinning hair, the first step is to talk to your doctor. They can help determine the underlying cause of your hair loss and recommend a treatment plan.

2. Use Hair-Thickening Products

There are a variety of hair-thickening products available, such as volumizing shampoos and conditioners, that can help make your hair look thicker and fuller.

3. Try Hair-Growth Treatments

There are also a variety of hair-growth treatments available, such as minoxidil, that can help stimulate hair growth and prevent further hair loss.

4. Consider Hair Transplantation

If your hair loss is more severe, you may want to consider hair transplantation. This involves taking hair from a donor area and transplanting it to the thinning or balding area.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Whether you're experiencing thinning hair or simply want to maintain healthy hair, here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and nutrients can help promote healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting plenty of protein, iron, and B vitamins.

2. Avoid Over-Styling Your Hair

Over-styling your hair can cause damage and breakage, which can contribute to thinning hair. Avoid using harsh chemicals and heat-styling tools too often.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Exposure to the sun can damage your hair and contribute to thinning. Wear a hat or use a UV-protectant spray when you're spending time in the sun.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can stress cause thinning hair?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss or thinning. This is because stress can disrupt the hair growth cycle and cause more hairs to enter the resting phase, which can lead to increased shedding.

Q: Can medications cause thinning hair?

A: Yes, some medications can cause hair loss or thinning as a side effect. Talk to your doctor if you're concerned about your medications affecting your hair.

Q: Can wearing hats cause thinning hair?

A: No, wearing hats doesn't cause thinning hair. However, if you're wearing hats that are too tight or that rub against your scalp, it can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

Q: Can hair extensions cause thinning hair?

A: Yes, hair extensions can cause damage and breakage to your natural hair, which can contribute to thinning over time. Make sure you're using high-quality extensions and not wearing them for too long.

Conclusion

Thinning hair can be a frustrating and stressful experience, but it's important to remember that there are steps you can take to address it. By being aware of the signs of thinning hair and taking action early on, you can help prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.
