How To Tie Up Medium Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of letting your medium length hair hang loose all the time? Do you want to switch up your look with a chic updo? Tying up medium length hair may seem daunting, but it's actually quite simple. In this article, we will guide you through the process of tying up your medium length hair with ease.

How to Tie Up Medium Length Hair

Before we get started, let's gather some supplies. You will need a hair tie, bobby pins, and a comb.

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help the hair tie grip your hair better.

Step 2: Brush Your Hair

Use a comb to brush your hair and remove any tangles.

Step 3: Gather Your Hair

Gather your hair at the back of your head as if you were making a low ponytail.

Step 4: Tie Your Hair

Secure your hair with a hair tie.

Step 5: Twist and Tuck

Twist the ponytail and tuck it into the hair tie.

Step 6: Secure with Bobby Pins

Use bobby pins to secure any loose strands and keep your updo in place.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you tie up your medium length hair with ease:

  • Use a hair tie that matches your hair color for a seamless look.
  • Don't worry if your updo looks messy - it's meant to be relaxed and effortless.
  • Use hairspray to keep your updo in place.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about tying up medium length hair:

Can I tie up my medium length hair if it's layered?

Yes, you can still tie up your medium length hair if it's layered. You may need to use more bobby pins to secure any shorter strands.

Will tying up my hair damage it?

Tying up your hair too tightly or using hair ties with metal can cause damage to your hair. Make sure to use a soft hair tie and avoid pulling your hair too tightly.

Hair Care

To keep your medium length hair healthy and strong, make sure to:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a heat protectant before using any styling tools.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to tie up your medium length hair, you can switch up your look with ease. Whether you're going for a casual or formal look, this updo is perfect for any occasion.
