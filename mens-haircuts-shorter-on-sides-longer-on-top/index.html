How to Get a Mens Haircut Shorter on Sides Longer on Top

The first step to getting a mens haircut shorter on sides longer on top is to find a barber who is experienced in this style. Ask around for recommendations, or do some research online to find a barber in your area who specializes in mens haircuts.

When you arrive at the barbershop, be sure to have a clear idea of what you want. Bring in pictures or examples of the style you are looking for to ensure that your barber understands your vision.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting the Perfect Haircut

Now that you've found a barber and have a clear idea of what you want, it's time to get started on your haircut. Follow these steps to ensure that you get the perfect mens haircut shorter on sides longer on top:

  1. Start by having your barber trim the sides of your hair to the desired length. This can vary depending on your personal preference, but a shorter length is typically best for this style.
  2. Next, have your barber cut the hair on top of your head to the desired length. This length should be longer than the length on the sides, but again, this can vary depending on your personal preference.
  3. Finally, have your barber blend the top and sides of your hair together for a seamless transition between the two lengths.

Now that you have your new haircut, it's time to start styling it. Here are some tips for styling your mens haircut shorter on sides longer on top:

Frequently Asked Questions About Mens Haircuts Shorter on Sides Longer on Top

Q: How often should I get my haircut?

A: It's recommended that you get your haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain your mens haircut shorter on sides longer on top.

Q: What face shape is best suited for this style?

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Mens Haircut Shorter on Sides Longer on Top

Now that you have your new haircut, it's important to take care of it to ensure that it looks great for weeks to come. Here are some hair care tips for maintaining your mens haircut shorter on sides longer on top:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using hot tools like hair dryers and straighteners, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and length of your haircut.
