How To Cut Fade Step By Step A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Mar 10, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you looking to give yourself or someone else a fresh haircut? One popular style is the fade. This style is characterized by a gradual taper from short hair at the top to longer hair at the bottom. Learning how to cut fade step by step may seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools and technique, it can be easily achieved. In this article, we’ll go over how to cut fade step by step, along with some tips and tricks to make the process smoother.

Tools You’ll Need

Before we dive into how to cut fade step by step, let’s go over the tools you’ll need:

  • Clippers with guards
  • Scissors
  • Barber comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Clipper oil

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Hair

Before starting the haircut, make sure the hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can make it difficult to cut accurately.

Step 2: Determine the Length

Using the clippers with the longest guard, cut the hair to the desired length. This will be the starting point for the fade.

Step 3: Create a Guideline

Using the clippers without a guard, create a line around the head where you want the fade to end. This will be your guideline for the rest of the haircut.

Step 4: Start the Fade

Using the clippers with a shorter guard, start to fade the hair from the bottom up. Use a fluid motion and gradually decrease the length of the guard as you move upwards.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you’ve reached the guideline, use scissors or clippers to blend the hair between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair at the bottom.

Step 6: Refine the Fade

Use a comb and scissors to refine the fade and make sure there are no harsh lines or uneven spots.

Step 7: Cut the Top

Using scissors, cut the hair on top to the desired length.

Step 8: Style the Hair

Style the hair as desired using products such as wax, pomade, or gel.

Tips for Cutting Fade

  • Take your time and work slowly to avoid mistakes.
  • Always start with a longer guard and gradually work your way down for a smooth transition.
  • Use a comb to make sure the hair is even and to blend the fade.
  • Remember to oil your clippers to keep them running smoothly.

FAQ

How often should you get a fade?

It depends on the person and their hair growth rate. On average, a fade should be touched up every 2-3 weeks.

Can you do a fade with scissors?

While it is possible to do a fade with scissors, it is much easier and quicker with clippers.

What’s the difference between a fade and a taper?

A fade gradually blends from short to long, while a taper is a more subtle change in length.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition regularly.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling.
  • Trim regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors.
  • Use hair products that are suited for your hair type.

Learning how to cut fade step by step may take some practice, but with the right tools and technique, it can be easily achieved. Remember to take your time and work slowly, and don’t be afraid to ask for help if needed. With a fresh new haircut, you’ll be feeling confident and stylish in no time.
