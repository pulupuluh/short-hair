How To Thin Curly Hair With Thinning Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi May 07, 2023 · 7 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it’s voluminous and full of life, it can also be difficult to manage and style. One common problem that many people with curly hair face is that their hair can become too thick and heavy, making it difficult to style and manage. If you’re dealing with this issue, thinning scissors can be a great tool to help you achieve a more manageable, textured look. In this step-by-step guide, we’ll show you how to thin curly hair with thinning scissors.

How to Use Thinning Scissors

Before we get into the specifics of how to thin curly hair with thinning scissors, it’s important to understand what thinning scissors are and how they work. Thinning scissors are a type of scissors that have teeth on one blade and a straight edge on the other. This allows for a more gradual cut, which can help to thin out hair without creating harsh lines or unevenness.

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before using thinning scissors, it’s important to start with clean, dry hair. This will help you to see the natural texture and shape of your hair, making it easier to determine where to thin and how much to cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, use a comb to section your hair into small, manageable pieces. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you’re not cutting too much hair at once.

Step 3: Hold the Scissors Correctly

When using thinning scissors, it’s important to hold them correctly. Hold the scissors with your thumb in one loop and your middle and ring fingers in the other loop. This will give you more control and precision when cutting.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Starting at the ends of your hair, use the thinning scissors to cut a small amount of hair at a time. This will help to create texture and remove some of the weight from your hair. Be sure to cut at an angle to create a more natural, textured look.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

After you’ve cut the ends of your hair with thinning scissors, use a regular pair of scissors to blend the hair and create a more seamless look. This will help to prevent any harsh lines or unevenness.

Tips for Thinning Curly Hair with Thinning Scissors

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when thinning curly hair with thinning scissors:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use a comb to guide the scissors and ensure that you’re cutting at the right angle.
  • Be careful not to cut too close to the scalp, as this can create unevenness and a choppy look.
  • Take breaks as needed to rest your hand and avoid cramping.

FAQ

Can thinning scissors be used on all hair types?

Thinning scissors can be used on most hair types, but they’re particularly effective on thick, curly hair.

Do thinning scissors damage hair?

When used correctly, thinning scissors should not damage hair. However, it’s important to use them correctly and not cut too much hair at once.

Can thinning scissors replace regular scissors?

No, thinning scissors should be used in conjunction with regular scissors to create a textured, blended look.

Hair Care for Thinning Curly Hair

After thinning your curly hair with thinning scissors, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for taking care of thinning curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling tools whenever possible, or use a heat protectant spray if you must use them.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Thinning curly hair with thinning scissors can be a great way to achieve a more manageable, textured look. By following these step-by-step instructions and tips, you can achieve a beautiful, natural-looking style that will make your curls shine.
