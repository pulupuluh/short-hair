How To Get Hair To Be Less Poofy

Written by Christine May 30, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have curly or frizzy hair, you know how frustrating it can be to deal with poofy hair. No matter how much you try to tame it, it always seems to have a mind of its own. But fear not, there are ways to get hair to be less poofy and achieve a sleeker, smoother look. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks on how to get hair to be less poofy.

Description

Poofy hair can be caused by a variety of factors such as humidity, dryness, and genetics. It can make your hair look frizzy, voluminous, and hard to manage. However, there are ways to tame your poofy hair and achieve a sleeker, more manageable look. By following a few simple steps, you can get your hair to be less poofy and more manageable.

How to

1. Use the right shampoo and conditioner

One of the most important things you can do to get hair to be less poofy is to use the right shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are designed to tame frizz and provide moisture to your hair. Avoid products that contain sulfates, as they can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.

2. Don't over-wash your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to frizz. Try to limit your hair washing to once or twice a week, and use a dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh.

3. Use a deep conditioning treatment

Deep conditioning treatments can help to restore moisture to your hair and tame frizz. Look for products that contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter. Apply the treatment to your hair once a week and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out.

4. Use a heat protectant

If you use heat styling tools like a flat iron or curling wand, make sure to use a heat protectant spray or serum. This will help to protect your hair from damage and prevent it from becoming poofy and frizzy.

5. Don't brush your hair when it's wet

Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so it's important to be gentle when you're brushing it. Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to gently remove any knots or tangles. Avoid using a regular brush, as this can cause your hair to become more poofy and frizzy.

Step by Step

Step 1: Wash your hair with a frizz-taming shampoo and conditioner

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that are designed to tame frizz and provide moisture. Look for products that contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter.

Step 2: Apply a deep conditioning treatment

Once a week, apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair. Leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it out. This will help to restore moisture to your hair and tame frizz.

Step 3: Use a heat protectant

If you use heat styling tools like a flat iron or curling wand, make sure to use a heat protectant spray or serum. This will help to protect your hair from damage and prevent it from becoming poofy and frizzy.

Step 4: Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush

When brushing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to gently remove any knots or tangles. Avoid using a regular brush, as this can cause your hair to become more poofy and frizzy.

Tips

  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as this can cause friction and make your hair more poofy. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze the water out of your hair.

FAQ

Q: Can I use a hair serum to tame my poofy hair?

A: Yes, hair serums can be a great way to tame poofy hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter.

Q: Can I blow dry my hair to make it less poofy?

A: Yes, you can blow dry your hair to make it less poofy. However, make sure to use a heat protectant spray or serum, and avoid using a high heat setting.

Q: Can I use a leave-in conditioner to tame my poofy hair?

A: Yes, leave-in conditioners can be a great way to tame poofy hair. Look for products that are designed to provide moisture and tame frizz.

Hair Care

When it comes to hair care, it's important to use products that are designed for your hair type. If you have poofy hair, look for products that are designed to tame frizz and provide moisture. In addition, make sure to limit your use of heat styling tools, and use a heat protectant spray or serum when you do use them. By following these tips and tricks, you can get your hair to be less poofy and achieve a sleeker, more manageable look.
