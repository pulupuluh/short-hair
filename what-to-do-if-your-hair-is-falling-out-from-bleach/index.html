What To Do If Your Hair Is Falling Out From Bleach A Guide

Written by Robby Aug 25, 2023 · 8 min read

If you've ever bleached your hair, you know how damaging it can be. Unfortunately, sometimes the damage is more than just split ends and breakage – your hair may actually start falling out. This can be a scary and frustrating experience, especially if you love your blonde locks. But don't worry, there are steps you can take to stop the hair loss and help your hair recover.

Description

Bleach is a powerful chemical that can strip your hair of its natural oils and proteins. This can cause your hair to become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. In some cases, the damage can be so severe that your hair starts falling out. This can be a result of over-processing, leaving the bleach on for too long, or using a bleach that is too strong for your hair type.

How to

If you're experiencing hair loss from bleach, there are a few things you can do to help your hair recover:

1. Stop Bleaching Your Hair

The first step is to stop bleaching your hair altogether. Give your hair a break and let it recover. This means avoiding any further chemical treatments, including perms and relaxers.

2. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help to strengthen your hair and prevent further breakage. Look for a treatment that contains keratin, which is a protein that is naturally found in hair. Apply the treatment once a week for best results.

3. Moisturize Your Hair

Dry hair is more prone to breakage, so it's important to keep your hair moisturized. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help replenish your hair's natural oils. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to help keep your hair hydrated throughout the day.

4. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause further breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair. Be careful when styling your hair, and avoid using heat tools as much as possible.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on what to do if your hair is falling out from bleach:

Step 1: Stop Bleaching Your Hair

The first step is to stop bleaching your hair altogether. This means avoiding any further chemical treatments, including perms and relaxers.

Step 2: Use a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair once a week to help strengthen your hair and prevent further breakage. Look for a treatment that contains keratin, which is a protein that is naturally found in hair.

Step 3: Moisturize Your Hair

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help replenish your hair's natural oils. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to help keep your hair hydrated throughout the day.

Step 4: Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause further breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair. Be careful when styling your hair, and avoid using heat tools as much as possible.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you care for your hair:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals that are essential for hair health.

FAQ

Q: Will my hair grow back after it falls out from bleach?

A: Yes, your hair should grow back after it falls out from bleach. However, it may take some time for your hair to fully recover.

Q: Can I bleach my hair again after it falls out?

A: It's not recommended to bleach your hair again after it falls out, as this can cause further damage. Give your hair time to recover before considering any additional chemical treatments.

Q: How long will it take for my hair to recover from bleach?

A: The amount of time it takes for your hair to recover from bleach will depend on the severity of the damage. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months for your hair to fully recover.

Hair Care

Caring for your hair is important, especially if you've experienced damage from bleach. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you can help your hair recover and prevent further damage. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid chemical treatments, and use products that are designed to strengthen and moisturize your hair.
