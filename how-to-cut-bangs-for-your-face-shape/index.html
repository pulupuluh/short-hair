How To Cut Bangs For Your Face Shape A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 10, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you thinking of getting bangs but don't know where to start? Cutting bangs can be a daunting task, especially if you're doing it at home. But with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve salon-worthy bangs that are perfectly suited to your face shape. In this article, we'll show you how to cut bangs for your face shape in a relaxed, easy-to-understand language. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Bangs for Your Face Shape
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Bangs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Your Bangs

Description

Before we dive into the details, let's first understand why it's important to cut bangs that are suited to your face shape. Bangs can help frame your face, accentuate your features, and hide imperfections. But if you don't choose the right style, your bangs can end up making your face look wider, longer, or more angular than it actually is. By choosing the right style for your face shape, you can create a balanced, harmonious look that enhances your natural beauty.

How to Cut Bangs for Your Face Shape

There are several different face shapes, including oval, round, square, heart, and diamond. Each shape has its own unique characteristics, and the right bangs can help balance out those features. Here's a quick guide to the best bang styles for each face shape:

  • Oval face: Side-swept bangs, curtain bangs
  • Round face: Long, layered bangs, side-swept bangs
  • Square face: Soft, wispy bangs, side-swept bangs
  • Heart-shaped face: Side-swept bangs, wispy bangs
  • Diamond face: Long, side-swept bangs, wispy bangs

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know which style of bangs will work best for your face shape, it's time to get cutting! Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting your own bangs:

  1. Wash and dry your hair, making sure it's completely dry before you start cutting.
  2. Section off the front part of your hair that you want to cut into bangs. Use clips to hold the rest of your hair back.
  3. Decide on the length and thickness of your bangs. It's better to start out longer and gradually cut more off, rather than cutting too much at once.
  4. Hold the hair straight out in front of you, and use hair scissors to cut straight across. Make sure to keep the scissors pointed upwards, rather than downwards, to avoid creating a blunt edge.
  5. Check your progress in the mirror, and make any necessary adjustments. Remember, you can always cut more off later!
  6. Once you're happy with the length, use scissors or a razor to add texture and movement to your bangs. This will help them blend in with the rest of your hair.
  7. Style your bangs as desired, using a round brush and a blow dryer to create volume and shape.
  8. Finish with a light hairspray to hold your bangs in place.

Tips for Cutting Bangs

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your own bangs:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair scissors. Kitchen scissors or craft scissors can damage your hair and create uneven edges.
  • Start with dry hair, as wet hair can shrink up and make your bangs shorter than you intended.
  • Take your time, and cut small sections at a time. It's easier to cut more off than to try and fix a mistake.
  • When cutting, keep your head straight and look straight ahead. Don't tilt your head or look down, as this can affect the angle of your bangs.
  • If you're not confident in cutting your own bangs, consider getting them cut by a professional. It's worth the investment to get a perfect, customized cut.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting bangs:

Q: How often should I trim my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to grow out. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

Q: Can I cut bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can! However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so you may want to cut your bangs a little longer than you intend them to be.

Q: What if I mess up my bangs?

Don't panic! If you accidentally cut too much off, try to even out the other side to match. If your bangs are too short, you can pin them back or style them to the side until they grow out.

Hair Care for Your Bangs

Now that you've got your perfect bangs, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your bangs every 2-3 days, using a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Blow dry your bangs using a round brush to create volume and shape.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as these can weigh down your bangs and make them look greasy.
  • Use a dry shampoo in between washes to keep your bangs looking fresh.
  • When sleeping, pin your bangs back or use a headband to keep them out of your face.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut bangs that are perfectly suited to your face shape. Whether you're going for a bold, blunt look or soft, wispy fringe, your bangs will help frame your face and enhance your natural beauty. So go ahead and give it a try - you might just discover your new signature look!
