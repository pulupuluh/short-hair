How To Layer Short Hair Myself Step By Step Guide And Tips

Written by Bardi May 31, 2023 · 7 min read
How to Layer Short Hair Myself: Step-by-Step Guide and Tips | Hair Care 2023

Are you tired of your short hair looking flat and lifeless? Layering your hair can add volume, texture, and movement to your locks. But going to a salon for a haircut can be expensive and time-consuming. Luckily, you can layer short hair yourself with just a few tools and some basic knowledge. In this article, we’ll show you how to layer short hair yourself in a few easy steps.

Table of Contents

  • How to Layer Short Hair Myself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Layering Short Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Layering Short Hair

How to Layer Short Hair Myself

Before starting, gather the right tools: sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. It’s better to work with clean, dry hair, but damp hair can also work. Here’s how to layer short hair yourself:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Section your hair: Divide your hair into three sections – top, middle, and bottom – and clip them separately.

  2. Start with the bottom section: Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight. Hold the ends of the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle. Don’t cut too much at once, as you can always go back and trim more if needed.

  3. Work your way up: Continue cutting small sections of hair at an angle and work your way up to the middle layer. Use the same technique for the middle layer, and then the top layer.

  4. Blend the layers: Once you’ve cut all the layers, remove the clips and let your hair down. Use the scissors to blend the layers together by cutting into the ends at an angle. This will give your hair a natural, layered look.

  5. Check for symmetry: Use a mirror to check the symmetry of your layers. If one side looks longer than the other, trim it to match the other side.

  6. Finish with styling: Style your hair as desired. Layering can add volume and texture, so experiment with different styles to see what works best for you.

  7. Clean up: Clean up any stray hairs and put away your tools.

Tips for Layering Short Hair

Layering short hair can be tricky, but these tips can help:

  • Start with small sections: It’s better to cut a little bit at a time than to cut too much at once.

  • Use sharp scissors: Dull scissors can damage your hair and create uneven layers.

  • Don’t cut straight across: Cutting at an angle creates a softer, more natural look.

  • Check for symmetry: Use a mirror to make sure your layers are even on both sides.

  • Experiment with different styles: Layering can add volume and texture, so try different styles to see what works best for you.

FAQs

Can I layer my hair if it’s already layered?

Yes, you can layer your hair even if it’s already layered. Just make sure to blend the layers together to avoid a choppy, uneven look.

How often should I layer my short hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. Generally, it’s recommended to layer short hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and texture.

Can I layer my hair if it’s curly?

Yes, you can layer curly hair, but it’s best to do it when the hair is dry and styled as you normally wear it. This will help you see how the layers will fall and avoid cutting too much.

Hair Care After Layering Short Hair

After layering your short hair, it’s important to take good care of it to maintain its shape and texture. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

  • Avoid heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and cause split ends.

  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.

  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and texture of your layers.

Now that you know how to layer short hair yourself, you can achieve a fresh, stylish look without breaking the bank. With a little practice and patience, you can master the art of layering and rock your new ‘do with confidence.
