Should I Go Darker Or Lighter Hair Quiz A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Mar 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're considering a hair color change, you might be wondering whether you should go darker or lighter. It's a tough decision, and one that can be influenced by a variety of factors, including your skin tone, natural hair color, and personal style preferences. To help you make the best decision for your hair, we've put together this comprehensive guide, complete with a "should I go darker or lighter hair quiz," tips, and FAQs.

What is the "Should I Go Darker or Lighter Hair Quiz?"

The "Should I Go Darker or Lighter Hair Quiz" is a series of questions designed to help you determine whether a darker or lighter hair color would be best for you. The quiz takes into account factors such as your skin tone, natural hair color, and personal style preferences to provide you with an accurate recommendation.

How to Take the "Should I Go Darker or Lighter Hair Quiz"

Taking the "Should I Go Darker or Lighter Hair Quiz" is easy. Simply answer the questions as honestly as possible. There are no right or wrong answers, so don't worry about being judged on your responses. Once you've completed the quiz, you'll receive a recommendation for whether you should go darker or lighter with your hair color.

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Find a quiet place where you can concentrate.
  2. Take out a piece of paper and a pen to write down your answers.
  3. Answer each question honestly.
  4. When you've answered all the questions, tally up your score.
  5. Check the score key to see whether you should go darker or lighter with your hair color.

Tips for Choosing Your Hair Color

Choosing the right hair color can be a daunting task, but there are some things you can keep in mind to make the process easier. Here are a few tips to help you choose your hair color:

  • Consider your skin tone: If you have a warm skin tone, you might look best with a warm hair color like golden blonde or chestnut brown. If you have a cool skin tone, you might look best with a cool hair color like ash blonde or jet black.
  • Think about your natural hair color: If you're making a drastic change from your natural hair color, be prepared for the upkeep. It might be easier to choose a hair color that's closer to your natural shade.
  • Consider your lifestyle: If you're someone who likes to change their hair color frequently, a lighter shade might be easier to maintain. If you're someone who prefers a low-maintenance hair color, a darker shade might be a better fit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will a darker hair color make me look older?

A: Not necessarily. While some people believe that darker hair colors can make you look more mature, the truth is that it all depends on the shade and how it complements your skin tone.

Q: Can I go from blonde to black hair in one salon visit?

A: It's possible, but it's not recommended. Going from blonde to black in one salon visit can be damaging to your hair, and it's better to take the process slow to maintain the health of your hair.

Q: How do I maintain my new hair color?

A: Maintaining your new hair color depends on the type of color you choose. If you go for a lighter shade, you might need to touch up your roots more frequently. If you go for a darker shade, you might need to use color-safe shampoo to prevent fading. Talk to your stylist about the best way to maintain your new hair color.

Hair Care Tips

Regardless of whether you decide to go darker or lighter with your hair color, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage and color fading.
  • Avoid using heat tools like curling irons and straighteners too often.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

Now that you have all the information you need, take the "Should I Go Darker or Lighter Hair Quiz" and make the best decision for your hair. Remember, at the end of the day, the most important thing is that you feel confident and beautiful in your new hair color.
