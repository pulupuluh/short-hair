How To Enlarge A Vintage Sewing Pattern A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Apr 22, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you love vintage fashion, you know that the right sewing pattern can make all the difference. But what do you do when you find the perfect vintage pattern, only to discover that it's too small for you? Don't worry, because with a little know-how, you can easily enlarge a vintage sewing pattern to fit your body. In this guide, we'll show you how.

Table of Contents

  1. Step 1: Choose the Pattern
  2. Step 2: Take Your Measurements
  3. Step 3: Determine How Much to Enlarge
  4. Step 4: Enlarge the Pattern
  5. Step 5: Adjust the Pattern
  6. Tips for Enlarging a Vintage Sewing Pattern
  7. FAQs
  8. Hair Care While Sewing

Step 1: Choose the Pattern

The first step to enlarging a vintage sewing pattern is to find the right one. Look for patterns that are close to your desired style, and make sure they have enough seam allowance to accommodate any size adjustments you'll be making. You can find vintage sewing patterns online, at vintage clothing stores, or even at thrift shops.


Step 2: Take Your Measurements

Before you start enlarging your pattern, you'll need to take your measurements. Use a tape measure to measure your bust, waist, hips, and any other relevant areas. Write down your measurements and keep them handy for reference.


Step 3: Determine How Much to Enlarge

Now that you have your measurements, you'll need to determine how much to enlarge the pattern. Most vintage patterns will have a size chart that will help you determine how much to add. You can also measure the pattern to see how much you'll need to add to each section.


Step 4: Enlarge the Pattern

Once you know how much to add, you can start enlarging the pattern. You can do this by hand, or you can use a photocopier to enlarge the pattern to the desired size. Make sure to add the same amount to each section of the pattern to maintain the correct proportions.


Step 5: Adjust the Pattern

After you've enlarged the pattern, you may need to make adjustments to ensure a perfect fit. This may include adjusting the waistline, neckline, or hemline. Make sure to try on the pattern and make any necessary adjustments before cutting your fabric.


Tips for Enlarging a Vintage Sewing Pattern

  • Always make a muslin mock-up of your pattern before cutting your fabric to ensure a perfect fit.
  • Use a French curve ruler to ensure smooth, accurate lines when enlarging the pattern.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're unsure about how to enlarge your pattern.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a sewing machine to enlarge my pattern?

A: No, you'll need to enlarge your pattern by hand or using a photocopier.

Q: How much should I add to each section of the pattern?

A: This will depend on your measurements and the size chart for the pattern. Make sure to add the same amount to each section to maintain the correct proportions.

Q: Can I use a modern pattern to enlarge a vintage pattern?

A: No, modern patterns are designed differently than vintage patterns and may not work for enlarging a vintage pattern.


Hair Care While Sewing

While sewing, it's important to take care of your hair. Long hair can get tangled in the machine, so tie it back or wear a hair net. You can also wear a bandana or headscarf to keep hair out of your face while you sew.


Enlarging a vintage sewing pattern may seem daunting at first, but with a little practice, you'll be able to create beautiful, customized vintage clothing that fits you perfectly. Just remember to take your time, measure carefully, and make adjustments as needed.
