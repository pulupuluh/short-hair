Best Haircuts For Oval Face And Thin Hair

Written by Petter Aug 13, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have an oval face and thin hair, chances are you're always on the lookout for the perfect haircut that will flatter your features and add volume to your locks. Look no further! In this article, we've compiled a list of the best haircuts for oval face and thin hair, along with tips on how to care for your hair and make your haircut last longer.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Face Shape
  • Best Haircuts for Oval Face and Thin Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair
  • FAQ

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before we dive into the best haircuts for oval face and thin hair, it's important to determine your face shape. An oval face is longer than it is wide, with slightly wider cheekbones and a gentle narrowing at the forehead and jawline. If you're not sure if you have an oval face, you can use a measuring tape to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. If all three measurements are roughly the same and your face has a gentle curve, you likely have an oval face.

Best Haircuts for Oval Face and Thin Hair

1. Long Layers

Long layers are a great option for oval face and thin hair. They add volume and movement to your locks, while also accentuating your face shape. Ask your stylist to create long, blended layers that start at your chin and fall to the ends of your hair. You can also add some face-framing layers near your cheekbones for added dimension.

2. Bob

A classic bob is another great haircut for oval face and thin hair. A bob that hits at your chin or slightly above will accentuate your jawline and cheekbones, while also adding fullness to your hair. To make the most of this haircut, ask your stylist to add some layers or texture to your ends.

3. Pixie Cut

If you're feeling daring, a pixie cut can be a great option for oval face and thin hair. This short haircut draws attention to your face shape and cheekbones, while also adding volume to your hair. A pixie cut with choppy layers or textured ends can give your hair more body and movement.

4. Blunt Bangs

If you want to change up your look without sacrificing length, blunt bangs can be a great option for oval face and thin hair. Bangs can add volume to your hairline and draw attention to your eyes, while also framing your face shape. Ask your stylist for blunt, thick bangs that fall just above your eyebrows.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

Once you've chosen the perfect haircut for your oval face and thin hair, it's important to take care of your locks to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips:

1. Shampoo Less Often

Thin hair can be more prone to breakage and damage, so try to shampoo less often to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Aim to wash your hair every other day or every few days, and use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.

2. Use a Lightweight Conditioner

Conditioner can help add volume and moisture to your hair, but be sure to use a lightweight formula that won't weigh down your locks. Apply conditioner to the ends of your hair and avoid the roots.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it look flat and lifeless. If you must use heat styling tools, apply a heat protectant spray first and use the lowest heat setting possible.

4. Use Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo can be a lifesaver for thin hair. It can help absorb oil and add volume to your locks between washes. Apply dry shampoo to your roots and massage it in with your fingertips.

FAQ

1. Can I still get a layered haircut if I have thin hair?

Yes! Layers can actually add volume and movement to thin hair. Just be sure to ask your stylist for long, blended layers that won't make your hair look too thin or stringy.

2. Will a bob haircut make my face look rounder?

A bob can actually elongate your face shape and accentuate your cheekbones and jawline. Just be sure to choose a bob that hits at your chin or slightly above, and ask your stylist to add some layers or texture to your ends.

3. How often should I get my hair trimmed?

To keep your hair looking its best, aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks. This will help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and full.

With these tips and haircut ideas, you'll be able to find the perfect style for your oval face and thin hair. Remember to take care of your locks and embrace your natural beauty!
