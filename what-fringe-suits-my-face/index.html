What Fringe Suits My Face

Written by Eveline Jun 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you thinking of getting a fringe but unsure of what style would suit your face shape? Choosing the right fringe can enhance your facial features and make your hairstyle more flattering. In this article, we'll discuss the different types of fringes and which ones work best for each face shape.

Table of Contents

  • Face Shapes
  • Fringe Types
  • How to Choose
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Face Shapes

Before we dive into the different types of fringes, let's first identify the different face shapes. Knowing your face shape is essential in determining which fringe will suit you best.

The most common face shapes are:

  • Round
  • Oval
  • Square
  • Heart
  • Diamond

Each of these face shapes has its unique features and requires a different type of fringe to complement them.

Fringe Types

There are several types of fringes to choose from, and each has its unique style:

  • Straight Fringe
  • Side-Swept Fringe
  • Choppy Fringe
  • Blunt Fringe
  • Curved Fringe

Now, let's take a closer look at each fringe type and which face shape they work best for:

Straight Fringe

The straight fringe is a classic style that falls straight down over your forehead. It works best for oval and heart-shaped faces.

Side-Swept Fringe

The side-swept fringe is a versatile style that can work for any face shape. It's angled to one side and can be worn long or short.

Choppy Fringe

The choppy fringe is edgy and works best for square and diamond-shaped faces. It's textured and cut at different lengths to create a choppy effect.

Blunt Fringe

The blunt fringe is a bold style that works best for oval and heart-shaped faces. It's cut straight across with no angle or texture.

Curved Fringe

The curved fringe is a soft and feminine style that works best for round and heart-shaped faces. It's cut in a curved shape to frame the face.

How to Choose

Now that you know the different types of fringes and which face shape they work best for, it's time to choose the right one for you. Here are some tips:

  • Consider your face shape and which fringes complement it.
  • Think about your hair type and how easy it will be to maintain the fringe.
  • Consult with your hairstylist to determine which style will suit your face shape and hair type best.

Step by Step

If you're ready to get a fringe, here's a step-by-step guide on how to do it:

  1. Choose the type of fringe you want.
  2. Consult with your hairstylist to determine the best length and style for you.
  3. Wash and dry your hair.
  4. Section off the hair you want to cut into a fringe.
  5. Use hairdressing scissors to cut the fringe to the desired length.
  6. Style your fringe by blow-drying it into the desired shape.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting a fringe:

  • Start with a longer fringe and gradually cut it shorter to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Keep your fringe trimmed regularly to maintain its shape.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when blow-drying your fringe to prevent damage.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Can anyone get a fringe?

Yes, anyone can get a fringe, but it's important to choose the right style for your face shape and hair type.

Will a fringe suit me?

Yes, a fringe can suit anyone, but it's important to choose the right style for your face shape and hair type.

Hair Care

Keeping your fringe looking great requires some regular maintenance. Here are some tips for caring for your fringe:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your fringe clean.
  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Blow-dry your fringe into the desired shape.
  • Use a hair oil or serum to keep your fringe looking shiny and healthy.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to choose the perfect fringe to suit your face shape and hair type. Whether you opt for a classic straight fringe or an edgy choppy fringe, a fringe can add a new dimension to your hairstyle and enhance your facial features.
