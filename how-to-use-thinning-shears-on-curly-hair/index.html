How To Use Thinning Shears On Curly Hair

Written by Robby Mar 16, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Thinning shears are a valuable tool for hairdressers and stylists. They can be used to add texture and volume to hair, as well as to remove bulk from thick hair. However, when it comes to curly hair, thinning shears need to be used carefully to avoid creating frizz or uneven ends. In this article, we will explain how to use thinning shears on curly hair.

Description

Curly hair can be difficult to manage, especially if it is thick and voluminous. Thinning shears can be used to reduce the bulk of curly hair without sacrificing its natural texture. By selectively removing some of the hair strands, thinning shears can create a more balanced and lightweight look. However, it is important to use the right technique and tools to avoid damaging the hair or creating an unflattering cut.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Curly Hair

Here are the steps to follow when using thinning shears on curly hair:

Step 1: Choose the right type of thinning shears

Thinning shears come in various shapes and sizes. For curly hair, it is recommended to use a pair of shears with wider gaps between the teeth. This will allow you to remove more hair strands without creating visible lines or gaps. Additionally, it is important to use sharp and clean shears to avoid pulling or damaging the hair.

Step 2: Section the hair

Before using the thinning shears, you need to section the hair properly. Divide the hair into small sections, using clips or hair ties to keep them separate. This will help you to work on one area at a time and to avoid cutting too much hair.

Step 3: Start at the bottom

When using thinning shears on curly hair, it is best to start at the bottom and work your way up. This will prevent you from removing too much hair from the top layers, which can make the hair look flat or frizzy. Begin by cutting the hair at the nape of the neck, using the thinning shears to remove some of the bulk.

Step 4: Cut at an angle

When using thinning shears on curly hair, it is important to cut at an angle rather than straight across. This will help to create a more natural and textured look. Hold the shears at a 45-degree angle, and make small snips along the length of the hair. Make sure to avoid cutting too close to the scalp or the ends of the hair.

Step 5: Blend the layers

After cutting the bottom section, move on to the middle and top layers of the hair. Use the same technique of cutting at an angle, but be sure to blend the layers together to create a seamless look. Use your fingers to lift the hair and to check for any unevenness or gaps.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears on curly hair:

  • Use the thinning shears sparingly, as over-thinning can create frizz or split ends.
  • Work in a well-lit and clean environment, to avoid mistakes or contamination.
  • Follow the natural shape of the hair, rather than forcing it into a different style.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or serum to keep the hair hydrated and protected after using thinning shears.

FAQ

Can thinning shears be used on all types of curly hair?

Thinning shears can be used on most types of curly hair, but they need to be used with care. For very fine or delicate hair, it is best to avoid thinning shears altogether, as they can damage the hair or create an uneven look. For thicker or coarser hair, thinning shears can be a useful tool to create a more manageable and textured look.

Can thinning shears be used on wet or dry hair?

Thinning shears can be used on both wet and dry hair, depending on your preference and the type of hair you are working with. Some stylists prefer to use thinning shears on wet hair, as it allows them to see the natural shape of the hair more clearly. Others prefer to use them on dry hair, as it gives them more control over the final result.

Hair Care after Using Thinning Shears on Curly Hair

After using thinning shears on curly hair, it is important to take good care of the hair to avoid damage or dryness. Here are some tips for hair care after using thinning shears:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair hydrated and soft.
  • Avoid using hot tools or harsh chemicals on the hair, as they can damage the hair and create frizz.
  • Apply a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish the hair and prevent breakage.
  • Trim the ends of the hair regularly to keep them healthy and avoid split ends.

By following these tips and techniques, you can use thinning shears on curly hair to create a more textured and balanced look. Remember to use high-quality tools and to take good care of the hair afterwards to maintain its natural beauty and health.
