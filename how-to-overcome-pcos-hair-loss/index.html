How To Overcome Pcos Hair Loss

Written by Daniel Sep 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you suffer from PCOS, you may have noticed that hair loss is one of the common symptoms. PCOS hair loss is a result of hormonal imbalances that can lead to thinning hair, bald patches, and receding hairlines. But don't worry, there are ways to overcome PCOS hair loss. In this article, we will discuss the causes of PCOS hair loss, how to prevent it, and offer tips on how to care for your hair.

Table of Contents

  1. Causes of PCOS Hair Loss
  2. Preventing PCOS Hair Loss
  3. Tips for Hair Care
  4. FAQs

Causes of PCOS Hair Loss

PCOS hair loss is caused by an imbalance of hormones in the body. Androgen, a male hormone, is produced in higher levels in women with PCOS. This hormone can lead to hair loss by causing the hair follicles to shrink. Additionally, insulin resistance, which is common in women with PCOS, can also contribute to hair loss.

Other factors that can cause PCOS hair loss include stress, nutrient deficiencies, and genetics. Stress can lead to hormonal imbalances, while nutrient deficiencies can cause weak and brittle hair. Genetics can also play a role in hair loss, and women with a family history of hair loss are more likely to experience it.

Preventing PCOS Hair Loss

Preventing PCOS hair loss requires a multifaceted approach. It's important to address the hormonal imbalances in the body, reduce stress, and ensure that your hair is receiving the proper nutrients and care.

Hormonal Imbalances

One way to address hormonal imbalances is through medication. Birth control pills can help regulate hormone levels, while spironolactone can block androgen production. It's important to speak with a healthcare provider to determine the best course of treatment for you.

Reducing Stress

Reducing stress can be done through a variety of methods, including exercise, meditation, and therapy. It's important to find what works best for you and make it a part of your daily routine.

Nutrition

Eating a balanced diet that is high in protein, iron, and vitamins can help ensure that your hair is receiving the proper nutrients. Additionally, taking supplements such as biotin, iron, and vitamin D can help support hair growth.

Tips for Hair Care

In addition to addressing the underlying causes of PCOS hair loss, it's important to care for your hair properly. Here are some tips:

Wash and Condition Regularly

Washing and conditioning your hair regularly can help keep it healthy and prevent breakage. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can dry out hair and cause it to become brittle. If you must use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat on a low setting.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, can pull on the hair and cause breakage. Opt for looser hairstyles that don't put stress on the hair.

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Avoid using a brush on wet hair, as it can cause breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle hair.

FAQs

Q: Will my hair grow back if I treat PCOS?

A: It is possible for hair to grow back once the underlying hormonal imbalances are addressed. However, it may take time and patience.

Q: Can I use hair growth products to treat PCOS hair loss?

A: Hair growth products can be helpful in promoting hair growth, but they should not be used as a sole treatment for PCOS hair loss. It's important to address the underlying hormonal imbalances as well.

Q: Can PCOS hair loss be reversed?

A: While PCOS hair loss can be managed and slowed down, it may not be reversible in all cases. It's important to speak with a healthcare provider to determine the best course of treatment for you.

Q: Can hair extensions or wigs be used to cover up PCOS hair loss?

A: Hair extensions or wigs can be used as a temporary solution to cover up hair loss, but they should not be relied upon as a long-term solution.

PCOS hair loss can be a frustrating symptom, but it's important to remember that there are ways to manage it. By addressing the underlying causes, caring for your hair properly, and being patient, you can take steps towards regaining your confidence and a healthy head of hair.
