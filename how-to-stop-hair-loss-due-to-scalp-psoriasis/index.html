How To Stop Hair Loss Due To Scalp Psoriasis

Written by Aprilia Jul 01, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Scalp psoriasis is a common skin condition that affects many people worldwide. It is characterized by red, scaly patches on the scalp, which can be itchy and painful. Unfortunately, scalp psoriasis can also lead to hair loss. If you are struggling with hair loss due to scalp psoriasis, don't worry, there are ways to stop it. In this article, we will discuss how to stop hair loss due to scalp psoriasis in easy to understand language.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Stop Hair Loss Due to Scalp Psoriasis
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Scalp psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune disease that affects the scalp. It causes skin cells to grow too quickly, resulting in red, scaly patches on the scalp. These patches can be itchy, painful, and can lead to hair loss. Hair loss due to scalp psoriasis can be distressing, but there are ways to manage it.

How to Stop Hair Loss Due to Scalp Psoriasis

If you are experiencing hair loss due to scalp psoriasis, there are several things you can do to stop it. Here are some of the most effective ways:

1. Use medicated shampoos

Medicated shampoos that contain salicylic acid, coal tar, or ketoconazole can help reduce inflammation, itching, and scaling associated with scalp psoriasis. These shampoos can also help prevent hair loss by keeping the scalp clean and healthy. Use these shampoos as directed by your dermatologist.

2. Apply topical treatments

Topical treatments such as corticosteroids, calcipotriene, and tazarotene can help reduce inflammation and scaling on the scalp. These treatments can also help prevent hair loss by keeping the scalp healthy. Apply these treatments as directed by your dermatologist.

3. Take oral medications

If topical treatments and medicated shampoos are not effective, your dermatologist may prescribe oral medications such as methotrexate, cyclosporine, or acitretin. These medications can help reduce inflammation and scaling on the scalp and prevent hair loss.

4. Practice good hair care

Good hair care is essential for preventing hair loss due to scalp psoriasis. Avoid using hot tools, such as hair dryers and straighteners, as they can damage the hair and scalp. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle wet hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on the hair. Be gentle when washing and styling your hair.

Step by Step

Here are the step-by-step instructions to stop hair loss due to scalp psoriasis:

  1. Consult with a dermatologist
  2. Use medicated shampoos as directed
  3. Apply topical treatments as directed
  4. Take oral medications as prescribed
  5. Practice good hair care

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you stop hair loss due to scalp psoriasis:

  • Avoid scratching your scalp
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Protect your scalp from the sun
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Manage stress through meditation or exercise

FAQ

Q: Can scalp psoriasis cause permanent hair loss?

A: In most cases, hair loss due to scalp psoriasis is temporary and the hair will grow back once the condition is under control. However, in some cases, if the hair follicles are damaged, hair loss may be permanent.

Q: Can scalp psoriasis be cured?

A: There is no cure for scalp psoriasis, but it can be managed with medication and lifestyle changes.

Q: Is scalp psoriasis contagious?

A: No, scalp psoriasis is not contagious.

Hair Care

Good hair care is essential for preventing hair loss due to scalp psoriasis. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Avoid using hot tools
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on the hair
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Manage stress through meditation or exercise

In conclusion, hair loss due to scalp psoriasis can be distressing, but there are ways to manage it. Consult with a dermatologist, use medicated shampoos and topical treatments, take oral medications as prescribed, practice good hair care, and follow these tips to stop hair loss due to scalp psoriasis.
