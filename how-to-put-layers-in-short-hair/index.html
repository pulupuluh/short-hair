How To Put Layers In Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jul 19, 2023 Â· 8 min read
If you're looking to add some volume and texture to your short hair, layers are a great way to achieve this. Layers can help give your hair movement and shape, and can make your hair look fuller and thicker. In this post, we'll show you how to put layers in short hair, so you can achieve the look you want.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Put Layers in Short Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Putting Layers in Short Hair
  • FAQ

Description

Adding layers to short hair can help enhance your natural texture and add volume, creating a more dynamic and interesting look. Layers can be added to almost any length of hair, from a pixie cut to a lob, and can be customized to suit your face shape and hair type. Keep in mind that layering short hair requires precision, so it's important to go to a professional stylist if you're not confident in your own hair cutting skills.

How to Put Layers in Short Hair

Putting layers in short hair is a process that involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create movement and volume. Here are the basic steps for putting layers in short hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash and dry your hair, then part it in the middle or on the side.
  2. Section your hair into four parts, using hair clips to keep them separated.
  3. Start at the back of your head, taking a small section of hair and combing it straight down. Hold the section of hair between your fingers at the length you want the shortest layer to be.
  4. Cut the hair at the length you're holding between your fingers, then move on to the next section of hair, working your way around your head.
  5. Once you've cut the shortest layer, move on to the next section of hair, holding it at the length you want the next layer to be. Cut the hair at this length, then move on to the next section of hair.
  6. Continue cutting each section of hair at different lengths until you've achieved the desired amount of layering.
  7. Once you've cut all the layers, use a mirror to check the back of your head to make sure everything is even.
  8. Style your hair as desired, using a curling iron or straightener to enhance the layers.

Tips for Putting Layers in Short Hair

- Always use sharp scissors to cut your hair, as dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends. - Take your time and be patient when cutting your hair, as rushing can lead to mistakes and uneven layers. - Consider your face shape and hair type when deciding on the length and placement of your layers. - If you're not confident in cutting your own hair, go to a professional stylist who can help you achieve the look you want.

FAQ

Can layers be added to any length of short hair?

Yes, layers can be added to any length of short hair, from a pixie cut to a lob. However, the length and placement of the layers will depend on your face shape and hair type.

How often should I get my hair cut if I have layers?

If you have layers in your short hair, it's recommended that you get your hair cut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and length of the layers.

Can I add layers to my own hair?

While it's possible to add layers to your own hair, it's recommended that you go to a professional stylist if you're not confident in your own hair cutting skills. Layering short hair requires precision, and a professional can help you achieve the look you want.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Short Hair

- Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add volume and texture to your hair. - Avoid using heavy styling products, which can weigh down your hair and make it look flat. - Use a heat protectant spray before using a curling iron or straightener to protect your hair from heat damage. - Get regular trims to maintain the shape and length of your layers. - Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. Putting layers in short hair can be a great way to add volume and texture to your hair. Whether you're looking for a subtle change or a dramatic transformation, layers can help you achieve the look you want. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and consider consulting a professional stylist if you're not confident in your own hair cutting skills.
