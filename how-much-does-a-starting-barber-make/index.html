How Much Does A Starting Barber Make

Written by Frank Mar 23, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you considering a career in barbering? One of the important factors to consider is how much money you can make as a starting barber. In this article, we'll discuss the average salary of entry-level barbers and provide some tips on how to increase your earning potential.

Table of Contents

  • Average Salary of a Starting Barber
  • Factors Affecting Salary
  • How to Increase Your Earnings
  • Hair Care Tips for Barbers
  • FAQ

Average Salary of a Starting Barber

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average annual salary of a barber in the United States is $32,890. However, the salary of a starting barber may be lower than this average.

The starting salary of a barber can vary depending on factors such as location, experience, and the type of establishment they work in. On average, a starting barber can expect to earn between $18,000 and $30,000 per year.

Factors Affecting Salary

Location is one of the biggest factors that affects the salary of a barber. Barbers in larger cities tend to earn more than those in rural areas. The type of establishment a barber works in can also affect their salary. For example, barbers working in high-end salons or spas may earn more than those working in budget-friendly chain salons.

Experience is another important factor that affects salary. As a starting barber, you can expect to earn less than someone who has been in the industry for several years. However, as you gain more experience and build up your clientele, your earning potential will increase.

How to Increase Your Earnings

If you're a starting barber looking to increase your earnings, there are several things you can do:

  • Continue your education by attending barbering workshops and classes.
  • Build a loyal clientele by providing excellent service and creating a welcoming atmosphere in your shop.
  • Offer additional services such as beard grooming, hair coloring, or scalp treatments to increase your income.
  • Consider specializing in a particular area such as men's grooming or hair replacement to increase your earning potential.

Hair Care Tips for Barbers

As a barber, it's important to not only know how to cut hair but also how to care for it. Here are some hair care tips for barbers:

  • Use high-quality hair products that are tailored to your client's hair type.
  • Encourage your clients to use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep their hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Provide your clients with tips on how to maintain their hair at home, such as using a wide-tooth comb to detangle wet hair.
  • Be mindful of the tools you use and ensure they are properly sanitized to prevent the spread of bacteria or infections.

FAQ

What is the average hourly wage for a starting barber?

The average hourly wage for a starting barber is $9-$15 per hour.

Can barbers make a good living?

Yes, barbers can make a good living with the right experience, skills, and location. Some barbers even earn six-figure salaries.

Do barbers make more money than hairdressers?

It depends on the location and the type of establishment they work in. In some areas, barbers may earn more than hairdressers, while in others, hairdressers may earn more.

Do barbers get tips?

Yes, it's common for clients to tip their barber. Tipping is typically 15-20% of the total bill.

As a starting barber, your salary may be lower than the average for the industry. However, with hard work, dedication, and a commitment to continual learning and improvement, you can increase your earning potential and build a successful career in barbering.
