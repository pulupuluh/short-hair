Short Hair To Suit Fat Face A Guide For Flattering And Stylish Cuts

Written by Aprilia Aug 28, 2023 Â· 12 min read

Are you tired of struggling to find a haircut that flatters your round or chubby face? Short hair can be an excellent option to slim down your face, draw attention to your best features, and boost your confidence. In this article, we'll share some tips, tricks, and ideas to help you choose the best short hair to suit a fat face. We'll cover everything from the most flattering styles to the best hair care practices to maintain your gorgeous new look. Let's get started!

Description: What is Short Hair to Suit Fat Face?

Short hair to suit a fat face is a hairstyle that flatters and balances out the proportions of a round or chubby face. This type of haircut typically features layers, angles, and volume around the crown and top of the head to elongate the face and create the illusion of a slimmer jawline. Short haircuts can also draw attention to your eyes, cheekbones, and lips, highlighting your best features and enhancing your natural beauty.

How to Choose the Best Short Hair to Suit Fat Face

Choosing the right short hair to suit a fat face can seem daunting, but with a few simple guidelines, you can find the perfect cut for your unique features and style. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Consider Your Face Shape

While round or chubby faces share some common features, such as full cheeks and a soft jawline, there are still subtle differences in shape and structure that can influence your haircut choice. For example, if you have a square jawline, you may want to avoid sharp angles or blunt cuts that can make your face look boxy. Instead, opt for soft layers or side-swept bangs to soften your features.

Think About Your Hair Texture

Short haircuts can work well with a variety of hair textures, from fine and straight to thick and curly. However, certain styles may be more challenging to maintain or style depending on your hair type. For instance, if you have thick, curly hair, you may want to opt for a layered, textured cut that allows your curls to bounce and move freely. If you have fine hair, you may want to choose a cut that adds volume and body, such as a pixie cut with layers or a bob with stacked layers.

Consider Your Lifestyle and Personality

Your haircut should not only flatter your face but also reflect your personality and lifestyle. If you're a busy mom or professional, you may want a low-maintenance cut that requires minimal styling and upkeep. If you're a creative or adventurous person, you may want a bold, edgy cut that showcases your unique style and personality.

Step by Step Guide: How to Get Short Hair to Suit Fat Face

Now that you have an idea of what type of short haircut to choose, here's a step-by-step guide to help you get the look:

Step 1: Find Inspiration

Before you head to the salon, gather some photos or examples of short haircuts that you love and that flatter your face shape and hair type. This will give your stylist a clear idea of what you're looking for and help you avoid any miscommunications or disappointments.

Step 2: Consult with Your Stylist

Once you have a clear idea of what you want, schedule a consultation with your stylist to discuss your options and preferences. Your stylist can also recommend any modifications or adjustments to the cut that will flatter your face shape and hair type.

Step 3: Get the Cut

When you're ready to get the cut, sit back, relax, and let your stylist work their magic. Depending on the cut, they may use scissors, clippers, or a combination of both to create the desired shape and texture.

Step 4: Style and Maintain Your Hair

Once your haircut is complete, your stylist can show you how to style and maintain your new look. Depending on the cut, you may need to use styling products, such as mousse, gel, or hairspray, to add volume, texture, or hold. You may also need to schedule regular trims or touch-ups to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Short Hair

Short hair can be versatile, stylish, and low-maintenance, but it still requires proper care and attention to stay healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips to help you maintain your short hair:

Wash and Condition Regularly

Short hair may not require as much washing as longer hair, but it still needs to be cleaned and conditioned regularly to remove dirt, oil, and product buildup. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for your hair type and texture.

Avoid Heat Damage

Short hair is more susceptible to heat damage from styling tools, such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. To minimize damage, use heat protectant sprays or serums, and avoid using high heat settings or leaving the tools in one spot for too long.

Protect Your Hair from the Elements

Short hair can also be exposed to the sun, wind, and other environmental factors that can damage or dry out your strands. Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the sun, and use a leave-in conditioner or oil to add moisture and shine.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions About Short Hair to Suit Fat Face

Q: Can short hair really slim down a fat face?

A: Yes! Short haircuts can create the illusion of a slimmer face by adding volume and height around the crown and top of the head, elongating the face and drawing attention away from the cheeks and jawline.

Q: What are the best short haircuts for a round face?

A: Some of the best short haircuts for a round face include pixie cuts, layered bobs, angled bobs, and asymmetrical cuts.

Q: How often should I get my short hair trimmed?

A: Depending on the style and your hair growth rate, you may need to get your short hair trimmed every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Hair Care Products for Short Hair to Suit Fat Face

Here are some recommended hair care products for short hair to suit a fat face:

1. Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo can help refresh and revive your hair between washes, absorbing excess oil and adding volume and texture. Try Batiste Dry Shampoo.

2. Texturizing Spray

Texturizing spray can add definition and hold to short hair, creating a tousled or beachy look. Try Bumble and Bumble Surf Spray.

3. Leave-In Conditioner

Leave-in conditioner can add moisture, shine, and manageability to short hair, especially if it's prone to dryness or damage. Try It's a 10 Miracle Leave-In Conditioner.

4. Heat Protectant Spray

Heat protectant spray can shield your hair from the damaging effects of styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons. Try TRESemme Thermal Creations Heat Tamer Spray.

Conclusion

Short hair can be a stylish, flattering, and low-maintenance option for those with round or chubby faces. By following our tips, tricks, and ideas, you can find the best short hair to suit your unique features and style, and maintain healthy, vibrant hair that boosts your confidence and beauty. Remember to consult with your stylist, choose the right products, and have fun experimenting with different cuts and styles. Good luck!
