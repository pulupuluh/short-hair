How To Cut Really Thick Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 31, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have really thick hair, you know how hard it can be to manage. It can be even more challenging to cut it at home. But don’t worry, we’ve got you covered. In this article, we will show you how to cut really thick hair at home. We will guide you through the process step-by-step and provide you with some helpful tips along the way.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Really Thick Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Thick Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care after Cutting

How to Cut Really Thick Hair at Home

Before we start, you will need a few tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties
  • Spray bottle of water
  • Mirror

Now, let’s get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair
Start by washing and drying your hair. Your hair should be completely dry before you start cutting it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair
Section your hair into four parts – two at the top and two at the bottom. Use hair ties to secure each section.

Step 3: Trim the Ends
Start by trimming the ends of your hair. Take one section at a time and trim about an inch off the ends.

Step 4: Cut the Length
Once you have trimmed the ends, it’s time to cut the length. Take one section at a time and cut it to your desired length.

Step 5: Blend the Layers
If you have layers, blend them by taking small sections of hair and trimming them at an angle.

Step 6: Use Clippers (Optional)
If you have clippers, you can use them to trim the sides and back of your hair. Use a number 4 or 5 guard for a shorter cut and a number 6 or 7 guard for a longer cut.

Step 7: Check for Evenness
Check your hair for evenness by running your fingers through it. Trim any uneven sections as needed.

Step 8: Style Your Hair
Once you are happy with your cut, style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Thick Hair

Tip 1: Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.

Tip 2: Start with small cuts and work your way up to your desired length.

Tip 3: Use hair ties to keep your hair in place while you cut it.

Tip 4: Use a spray bottle of water to keep your hair moist while you cut it.

Tip 5: Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet as it will appear longer when dry.

Tip 6: Take breaks if you need to. Cutting thick hair can be tiring.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my own hair if it’s really thick?
A: Yes, you can. However, it’s important to have the right tools and follow the steps carefully.

Q: How often should I cut my thick hair?
A: It depends on how fast your hair grows. On average, every 6-8 weeks is recommended.

Hair Care after Cutting

Tip 1: Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.

Tip 2: Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can damage your hair.

Tip 3: Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Tip 4: Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

Tip 5: Avoid brushing your hair too often as it can cause breakage.

Cutting really thick hair at home can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done. Follow these steps carefully and take your time. If you’re still not confident, consider asking a friend or family member to help you.
