Best Epilator For Thick Coarse Facial Hair Your Ultimate Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 20, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're someone who struggles with thick and coarse facial hair, you know how frustrating it can be to find the right tool to remove it. Shaving only makes it worse, while waxing can be painful and expensive. That's where epilators come in - they can remove hair from the root without breaking it, leaving you with smoother skin for longer. In this guide, we'll help you find the best epilator for thick coarse facial hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Use an Epilator for Facial Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Epilating Facial Hair
  • Tips for Using an Epilator on Thick Coarse Facial Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Thick Coarse Facial Hair

Description

An epilator is a device that uses a series of tweezers to pluck hair from the root. This means that it can remove hair much more effectively than shaving or waxing. While epilators can be used on any part of the body, some models are specifically designed for facial hair. These models are smaller, more precise, and gentler on the skin than regular epilators.

How to Use an Epilator for Facial Hair

Before using an epilator on your face, make sure your skin is clean and dry. It's best to use an epilator in the evening, as your skin may be red and irritated for a few hours after use. Start by choosing a small area to epilate, like your upper lip or chin.

Hold the epilator at a 90-degree angle to your skin and move it in the direction of hair growth. Use short, quick strokes to avoid pulling the skin. You may need to go over the same area a few times to remove all the hair. When you're finished, apply a soothing lotion or aloe vera gel to calm the skin.

Step-by-Step Guide to Epilating Facial Hair

1. Cleanse your skin thoroughly and dry it well.

2. Choose a small area to start with, like your upper lip or chin.

3. Hold the epilator at a 90-degree angle to your skin.

4. Move the epilator in the direction of hair growth, using short, quick strokes.

5. Repeat until all the hair in the area is removed.

6. Apply a soothing lotion or aloe vera gel to calm the skin.

Tips for Using an Epilator on Thick Coarse Facial Hair

1. Exfoliate your skin before using an epilator to remove dead skin cells and help prevent ingrown hairs.

2. Use a numbing cream or ice pack to reduce pain and redness.

3. Choose an epilator with multiple speed settings so you can adjust it to your comfort level.

4. Use the epilator regularly to prevent hair from growing back thicker and coarser.

FAQs

Q: Is epilating facial hair painful?

A: Yes, epilating can be painful, especially on thick and coarse hair. However, the pain should lessen with regular use as your skin gets used to it.

Q: Can I use an epilator on my eyebrows?

A: No, it's not recommended to use an epilator on your eyebrows as it can be difficult to control and may result in uneven or overplucked brows.

Q: How often should I use an epilator on my face?

A: It's best to use an epilator on your face every two to four weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows back.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Coarse Facial Hair

1. Use a gentle cleanser and moisturizer to keep your skin healthy and hydrated.

2. Avoid using harsh chemicals and hot tools on your hair, as this can damage it and make it even coarser.

3. Consider using a hair serum or oil to help soften and tame your hair.

4. If you're struggling with excessive facial hair, talk to your doctor about possible underlying medical conditions or hormonal imbalances.

With these tips and our recommended epilators, you can finally say goodbye to thick and coarse facial hair. Happy epilating!
