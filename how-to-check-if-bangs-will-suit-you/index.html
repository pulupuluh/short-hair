How To Check If Bangs Will Suit You

If you're thinking about getting bangs but you're not sure if they will suit you, you've come to the right place. In this article, we will show you how to check if bangs will suit your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle. We will also give you tips on how to care for your bangs and answer some frequently asked questions.

Check Your Face Shape

The first thing to consider when deciding if bangs will suit you is your face shape. Bangs can work for any face shape, but some styles may be more flattering than others. Here's a quick guide to help you determine your face shape:

Oval: Your face is longer than it is wide, with rounded jaw and forehead.

Round: Your face is as wide as it is long, with a round chin and full cheeks.

Square: Your face is roughly as wide as it is long, with a square jawline and forehead.

Heart: Your face is wider at the forehead and gradually narrows down to a pointy chin.

Diamond: Your face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at the forehead and jawline.

Long: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a prominent forehead, cheekbones, and jawline.

Once you've determined your face shape, you can choose a bangs style that will flatter your features. For example, if you have a round face, you may want to avoid heavy blunt bangs and opt for side-swept or layered bangs instead.

Consider Your Hair Type

Another factor to consider when deciding if bangs will suit you is your hair type. Bangs can work for any hair type, but some styles may require more maintenance than others. Here's a quick guide to help you determine your hair type:

Straight: Your hair is naturally straight and has no curls or waves.

Wavy: Your hair has a natural wave or curl, but is not tightly coiled.

Curly: Your hair is tightly coiled and has a lot of natural volume.

Thick: Your hair is dense and has a lot of volume.

Thin: Your hair is fine and has little volume.

If you have thick, curly hair, you may want to avoid heavy blunt bangs that require a lot of styling. Instead, opt for soft, wispy bangs that blend into your hair. If you have thin, straight hair, you may want to avoid heavy side-swept bangs that can make your hair look even thinner. Instead, opt for layered or textured bangs that add volume and movement to your hair.

Think About Your Lifestyle

The last factor to consider when deciding if bangs will suit you is your lifestyle. Bangs require regular maintenance and styling, so you'll want to make sure you have the time and patience to care for them. Here are some questions to ask yourself:

  • Do you have time to blow-dry and style your bangs every day?
  • Do you exercise regularly and sweat a lot?
  • Do you wear glasses or sunglasses that can interfere with your bangs?
  • Do you live in a humid climate that can make your bangs frizzy?

If you answered yes to any of these questions, you may want to consider a bangs style that requires less maintenance, such as side-swept or wispy bangs. You can also use hair accessories, such as headbands or bobby pins, to keep your bangs off your forehead during workouts or humid days.

Frame Your Face with Bangs

Bangs can be a great way to frame your face and highlight your best features. Here are some bangs styles to consider based on your face shape:

  • Oval: Soft, side-swept bangs that blend into your hair.
  • Round: Layered or textured bangs that add height and length to your face.
  • Square: Soft, wispy bangs that soften your jawline.
  • Heart: Side-swept bangs that draw attention to your eyes and cheekbones.
  • Diamond: Blunt or choppy bangs that add width to your forehead.
  • Long: Thick, blunt bangs that shorten your forehead and elongate your face.

Remember, bangs can be customized to suit your individual features and preferences. You can also consult with your hairstylist to find a bangs style that works for you.

Get a Haircut with Bangs

If you're not ready to commit to bangs just yet, you can try getting a haircut with faux bangs. This involves cutting your hair in a way that creates the illusion of bangs, without actually cutting your hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Part your hair down the middle.
  2. Take a section of hair from the front of your head, near your forehead.
  3. Twist the section of hair and tuck it behind your ear.
  4. Repeat on the other side.
  5. Fluff your hair and adjust the sections to create the illusion of bangs.

This is a great way to test out different bangs styles and see if they suit you before committing to a haircut.

Try On Virtual Bangs

If you're still not sure if bangs will suit you, you can try on virtual bangs using a hairstyle app or website. This allows you to upload a photo of yourself and see how different bangs styles would look on you. Here are some popular apps and websites to try:

  • The Hairstyler
  • Modiface
  • InStyle

This is a fun and easy way to experiment with different bangs styles and find a look that suits you.

Tips for Maintaining Your Bangs

Bangs require regular maintenance to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips to help you care for your bangs:

  • Trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.
  • Blow-dry your bangs with a round brush to create volume and shape.
  • Use a dry shampoo to keep your bangs from getting greasy between washes.
  • Use a lightweight hair oil to keep your bangs smooth and shiny.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can make them greasy and flat.

With the right care and styling, your bangs can look great every day.

FAQs

Will bangs suit my face shape?

Bangs can work for any face shape, but some styles may be more flattering than others. Consider your face shape and consult with your hairstylist to find a bangs style that works for you.

What if I don't like my bangs?

If you're not happy with your bangs, you can always grow them out or try a different style. Be patient and give your hair time to grow. You can also consult with your hairstylist for advice on how to transition
