How To Prepare For Chemo Hair Loss A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Mar 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you or a loved one is about to undergo chemotherapy treatment, you may be wondering how to prepare for hair loss. Chemotherapy can cause hair loss on the scalp, face, and other parts of the body, which can be a difficult side effect to deal with. In this article, we will provide you with tips and advice on how to prepare for chemo hair loss and how to take care of your hair during and after treatment.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prepare for Chemo Hair Loss
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Hair Care During Chemotherapy
  • FAQs

How to Prepare for Chemo Hair Loss

The first step in preparing for chemo hair loss is to talk to your doctor or oncologist. They can give you an idea of when your hair loss will occur and how much hair you can expect to lose. This can help you mentally prepare for the changes that are about to happen.

You may also want to consider cutting your hair short before treatment begins. This can make the hair loss less noticeable and easier to manage. Some people even choose to shave their heads completely, which can be empowering and help them feel more in control of the situation.

Another option is to start wearing scarves, hats, or wigs before your hair starts falling out. This can help you get used to the idea of covering your head and allow you to experiment with different styles and colors.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to prepare for chemo hair loss:

Step 1: Talk to Your Doctor

Speak to your doctor or oncologist about when your hair loss will occur and how much hair you can expect to lose.

Step 2: Consider Cutting Your Hair Short

Cutting your hair short before treatment begins can make the hair loss less noticeable and easier to manage.

Step 3: Start Wearing Scarves, Hats, or Wigs

Start wearing scarves, hats, or wigs before your hair starts falling out to get used to the idea of covering your head and experiment with different styles and colors.

Step 4: Take Care of Your Scalp

Once your hair starts falling out, take care of your scalp by washing it with a gentle shampoo and using a soft brush or comb. Avoid using hot styling tools or chemical treatments on your hair.

Step 5: Protect Your Scalp from the Sun

Protect your scalp from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf when you are outside. Your scalp may be more sensitive to the sun than usual.

Step 6: Experiment with Head Coverings

Experiment with different types of head coverings, such as scarves, hats, and wigs, to find what works best for you.

Step 7: Be Patient

Remember that your hair will grow back after treatment is over. Be patient and kind to yourself during this process.

Tips for Hair Care During Chemotherapy

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair during chemotherapy:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot styling tools or chemical treatments
  • Use a soft brush or comb
  • Protect your scalp from the sun
  • Experiment with different types of head coverings

FAQs

Will all chemotherapy treatments cause hair loss?

No, not all chemotherapy treatments cause hair loss. However, many do, and it is important to speak to your doctor or oncologist about what to expect.

When can I expect my hair to start falling out?

This varies from person to person and depends on the type of chemotherapy you are receiving. Your doctor or oncologist can give you an idea of when to expect hair loss.

Will my hair grow back after treatment?

Yes, your hair will grow back after treatment is over. However, it may take several months for your hair to return to its pre-treatment state.

Preparing for chemo hair loss can be a challenging experience, but there are steps you can take to make it easier. By speaking to your doctor, cutting your hair short, and experimenting with different head coverings, you can prepare yourself for the changes to come. Remember to be patient and kind to yourself during this process, and know that your hair will grow back after treatment is over.
