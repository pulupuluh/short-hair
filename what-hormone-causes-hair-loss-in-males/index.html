What Hormone Causes Hair Loss In Males

Written by Robby Sep 07, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss as a male and wondering what could be causing it? Well, the answer is simple - hormones. Specifically, a hormone called dihydrotestosterone (DHT) is the culprit behind male pattern baldness. In this article, we’ll explore what DHT is, how it causes hair loss, and some tips for managing it.

What is DHT?

DHT is a hormone that’s present in both males and females. It’s derived from testosterone, which is a hormone that’s responsible for male traits such as muscle growth, deep voice, and hair growth. DHT is more potent than testosterone and is responsible for the development of male genitalia during fetal development.

However, as men age, the levels of DHT in their bodies increase. This can cause the hair follicles on the scalp to shrink, leading to hair thinning and eventually hair loss. This is known as male pattern baldness, and it affects up to 70% of men at some point in their lives.

How Does DHT Cause Hair Loss?

DHT binds to the androgen receptors on hair follicles and causes them to shrink. This leads to a shorter lifespan for the hair follicles and eventually, they stop producing hair altogether. This is why hair loss from DHT tends to start at the temples and crown of the head, where the hair follicles are most sensitive to the hormone.

Additionally, DHT can also cause inflammation in the scalp, which can further damage the hair follicles and lead to hair loss.

Managing DHT and Hair Loss

While DHT is a natural hormone and is necessary for male development, managing its effects on hair loss is possible. Here are some tips:

1. Medications

There are medications available that can block the effects of DHT on hair follicles. Finasteride and minoxidil are two FDA-approved medications that have been shown to be effective in managing hair loss caused by DHT. However, these medications can have side effects, so it’s important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any treatment.

2. Diet

Eating a healthy diet that’s rich in vitamins and minerals can help support hair growth and prevent hair loss. Foods that are rich in biotin, iron, and zinc are particularly important for hair health.

3. Hair Care

Using gentle hair care products and avoiding harsh chemicals can help prevent further damage to hair follicles. Additionally, avoiding tight hairstyles that pull on the hair can also help prevent hair loss.

FAQ

What is male pattern baldness?

Male pattern baldness is hair loss that occurs in men due to a combination of genetics and hormones. It typically starts with hair thinning at the temples and crown of the head.

Can DHT be managed?

Yes, there are medications and lifestyle changes that can help manage the effects of DHT on hair loss. However, it’s important to consult with a healthcare provider before starting any treatment.

Is hair loss from DHT reversible?

In some cases, hair loss from DHT can be reversed with the proper treatment. However, the effectiveness of treatment depends on the extent of hair loss and the individual’s response to treatment.

Conclusion

DHT is a hormone that’s responsible for male development, but its effects on hair loss can be managed. Medications, a healthy diet, and gentle hair care can all help prevent further hair loss and promote hair growth. If you’re experiencing hair loss, consult with a healthcare provider to determine the best course of treatment for you.
