How To Cut A Short Fringe Yourself

Written by Aprilia Jun 23, 2023 · 7 min read

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you've never done it before. But with a little patience, practice, and the right tools, you can give yourself a chic, short fringe right at home. In this article, we'll go over how to cut a short fringe yourself with step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut a Short Fringe Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Fringe
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Your Short Fringe

How to Cut a Short Fringe Yourself

Before we jump into the step-by-step guide, let's go over some of the tools you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A fine-toothed comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A handheld mirror or a tri-panel mirror

Now that you have your tools ready, let's get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut a short fringe yourself:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair straight down and part it in the middle.
  2. Find the spot where you want your fringe to start. This could be right above your eyebrows or slightly higher.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the front and comb it straight down. Hold it between your index and middle fingers.
  4. Using your scissors, cut the hair straight across. Be sure to cut less than you think you need, as you can always go back and cut more.
  5. Continue cutting small sections of hair across your forehead, working from the middle outwards. Use your previous cut section as a guide for the next section to ensure it is even.
  6. Once you've cut both sides, take a look in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments. If one side is longer than the other, trim it until they are even.
  7. Use a fine-toothed comb to blend the fringe into the rest of your hair. Comb it down and sideways to create a wispy, natural look.
  8. Use your handheld mirror or tri-panel mirror to check the back of your hair and make sure it is even.

And there you have it – a chic, short fringe cut right at home!

Tips for Cutting Your Own Fringe

Here are some tips to keep in mind while cutting your own fringe:

  • Always cut less than you think you need, as you can always go back and cut more.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors, as dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Keep your hair damp while cutting it, as dry hair can be more difficult to cut and style.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way outwards to ensure an even cut.
  • Practice makes perfect! Don't be discouraged if your first attempt isn't perfect – keep practicing and you'll get the hang of it.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting a short fringe yourself:

Q: How often should I cut my fringe?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you want your fringe to be. If you want to maintain a short fringe, you may need to trim it every 2-4 weeks.

Q: Can I cut my fringe while it's wet?

A: It's best to cut your fringe while it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is. However, you can dampen your hair with a spray bottle if it's too difficult to work with while dry.

Q: Should I cut my fringe myself or go to a salon?

A: It depends on your comfort level and experience with cutting hair. If you're new to cutting hair or are unsure of what you want, it's best to go to a salon. But if you're comfortable with cutting your own hair and know what you want, you can definitely do it yourself!

Hair Care for Your Short Fringe

Now that you've cut your short fringe, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • If you do use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.
  • Trim your fringe regularly to keep it looking neat and tidy.

With these tips and tricks, you can confidently cut a short fringe yourself and keep it looking great. Happy cutting!
