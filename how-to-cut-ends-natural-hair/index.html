How To Cut Ends Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 01, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to give your natural hair a little trim, cutting the ends may be just what you need. Not only can it help get rid of split ends and promote healthy hair growth, but it can also give your hair a more polished and put-together look. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps for cutting natural hair ends, as well as some helpful tips and FAQs to ensure you get the look you want.

  2. Step 2: Divide Your Hair

  4. Step 4: Check Your Work

  7. Hair Care After Cutting Natural Hair Ends

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Sectioning clips (optional)

Make sure your scissors are sharp and specifically designed for cutting hair. Dull scissors can damage your hair and cause split ends.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Using your wide-tooth comb, part your hair down the middle and divide it into two sections. Then, further divide each section into smaller sections using sectioning clips (if you have them) or your fingers.

Make sure each section is even and that you can easily access the ends you want to cut.

Step 3: Trim Your Ends

Starting with one of the smaller sections, take a small amount of hair and twist it tightly. Then, using your sharp scissors, carefully snip the ends of the twist. Repeat this process on each section until you've trimmed all the ends you want to cut.

Remember to only trim a small amount of hair at a time â€“ less than an inch â€“ to avoid cutting off too much and creating uneven layers.

Step 4: Check Your Work

After you've trimmed all the sections, step back and check your work in the mirror. Make sure the length is even and that you haven't missed any sections. If you notice any unevenness, go back and trim those sections until you're happy with the length.

Tips for Cutting Natural Hair Ends

  • Only cut your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can be deceiving, and you may end up cutting off more than you intended.
  • Use sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Trim your hair in a well-lit area so you can see what you're doing.
  • Don't cut too much at once. Trim a little bit at a time until you achieve your desired length.
  • When twisting your hair, make sure it's tight enough to see the ends clearly but not so tight that it causes discomfort.

FAQs About Cutting Natural Hair Ends

How often should I cut my natural hair ends?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows, but most experts recommend trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy ends.

Can cutting my hair make it grow faster?

No, cutting your hair doesn't actually make it grow faster. However, trimming your hair regularly can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can make it easier for your hair to grow longer and healthier over time.

Should I cut my hair myself or go to a professional?

It's up to you, but if you're not confident in your cutting skills or have never cut your hair before, it may be best to go to a professional. They can help ensure you get the look you want and avoid any mistakes that could damage your hair.

Hair Care After Cutting Natural Hair Ends

After trimming your hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain healthy ends. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage and split ends.
  • Protect your hair with a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase at night to prevent breakage.
  • Stay hydrated and eat a healthy diet to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and regularly trimming your natural hair ends, you can help keep your hair looking and feeling its best.
