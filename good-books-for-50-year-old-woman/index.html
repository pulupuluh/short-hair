Good Books For 50 Year Old Woman A Guide To Relaxing Reads

Written by Aprilia Aug 23, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're a 50 year old woman looking for some good books to relax with, you've come to the right place. Reading is a wonderful way to unwind after a long day, or to take some time for yourself on a lazy weekend. In this article, we'll share some of the best books for women in their 50s, covering a range of genres and topics. So grab a cup of tea and settle in for some reading inspiration!

Table of Contents

  • Fiction
  • Memoir
  • Self-Help
  • Travel
  • Romance

Fiction

For a lot of people, fiction is the ultimate escape. Here are some options:

The Nightingale by Kristin Hannah

This historical fiction novel tells the story of two sisters in Nazi-occupied France during World War II. It's a gripping and emotional read that will transport you to another time and place.

The Vanishing Half by Brit Bennett

This novel follows the lives of twin sisters who run away from home as teenagers, and go on to live very different lives. It's a thought-provoking exploration of race, identity, and family.

Memoir

If you're looking for something more personal, memoirs can be a great choice. Here are a couple of recommendations:

Becoming by Michelle Obama

In this memoir, the former First Lady shares her life story, from growing up in Chicago to her time in the White House. It's an inspiring and insightful look at her journey.

The Glass Castle by Jeannette Walls

This memoir tells the story of the author's unconventional and often difficult upbringing. It's a moving and honest portrayal of family and resilience.

Self-Help

If you're looking for some guidance or inspiration, self-help books can be a great choice. Here are a couple of options:

The Power of Now by Eckhart Tolle

In this book, the author shares his philosophy on mindfulness and living in the present moment. It's a transformative read that can help you find more peace and clarity in your life.

The Gifts of Imperfection by Brené Brown

In this book, the author explores the idea of embracing imperfection and living a wholehearted life. It's a powerful and uplifting read that can help you cultivate more self-acceptance and joy.

Travel

If you're dreaming of adventure or just want to escape to another place, travel books can be a great choice. Here's a recommendation:

Wild by Cheryl Strayed

In this memoir, the author shares her experience hiking the Pacific Crest Trail alone, after a difficult period in her life. It's a beautiful and inspiring story of self-discovery and healing.

Romance

Finally, if you're in the mood for a little romance, here are a couple of options:

The Rosie Project by Graeme Simsion

This novel tells the story of a socially awkward genetics professor who embarks on a quest to find the perfect wife, and the woman who challenges his ideas about love and compatibility. It's a charming and humorous read.

The Notebook by Nicholas Sparks

This classic romance novel tells the story of a couple who fall in love in the 1940s, and then reconnect decades later. It's a heartwarming and emotional read.

Tips for Finding Good Books

Of course, these are just a few suggestions. There are countless other great books out there, waiting to be discovered. Here are a few tips for finding your next great read:

  • Ask friends or family members for recommendations
  • Browse bestseller lists or book club picks
  • Check out book reviews or ratings online
  • Visit your local library or bookstore and ask for suggestions

FAQ

What are some good book genres for 50 year old women?

There are many genres that can appeal to women in their 50s, including fiction, memoir, self-help, travel, and romance.

How can I find good books to read?

Some ways to find good books include asking for recommendations, browsing bestseller lists, checking out reviews or ratings online, and visiting your local library or bookstore.

What are some benefits of reading?

Reading can offer many benefits, including reducing stress, improving cognitive function, and increasing empathy and understanding.

Hair Care: Take Care of Your Hair While Reading

While you're enjoying your new book, why not take some time to pamper your hair? Here are a few tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment to nourish and hydrate your hair
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair
  • Try a scalp massage to increase circulation and promote healthy hair growth
  • Consider using a silk pillowcase to prevent hair breakage and tangling while you sleep

By taking care of your hair while you read, you can enjoy some relaxing self-care time and emerge with healthier, more beautiful locks.

Conclusion

Whether you're in the mood for a gripping novel, a personal memoir, or some self-help inspiration, there are plenty of great books out there for 50 year old women. By taking some time to find the right read for you, you can unwind, escape, and expand your horizons in the comfort of your own home.
