How To Cut Hair In Layers Step By Step

Written by Aprilia Jun 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Layered haircuts are a popular choice for many people as they add volume, texture, and movement to the hair. However, getting a layered haircut can be expensive, and not everyone has the time or money to visit a salon regularly. Fortunately, cutting your hair in layers at home is easier than you might think. In this article, we will take you through the step-by-step process of how to cut hair in layers and provide you with tips and tricks to achieve a professional-looking cut.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Hair in Layers
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After the Cut

How to Cut Hair in Layers

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror in front of you, as well as a second mirror to check the back of your head.

When cutting hair in layers, it's essential to work on clean, dry hair. This will help you to see the natural fall of the hair and ensure that you cut each section evenly.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to cut your hair in layers:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into three parts - the top, middle, and bottom. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 2: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of hair. Comb it straight down and cut it straight across. This will be the guide for the rest of the layers.

Step 3: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the middle section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and raise it to the same level as the bottom layer that you cut earlier. Cut the hair straight across, using the bottom layer as your guide.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, and raise it to the same level as the previous layers. Cut the hair straight across, using the bottom and middle layers as your guide.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

To blend the layers together, comb through your hair and check for any uneven sections. If you notice any unevenness, trim them with your scissors until you achieve a smooth, even look.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut a little at a time. You can always cut more, but you can't put the hair back once it's cut.
  • For a softer, more natural look, angle your scissors slightly when cutting the hair.
  • If you have curly hair, cut it when it's dry to see the natural curl pattern and avoid any surprises when it dries.
  • When cutting the top layer, take care not to cut too much hair. It's better to cut less and go back to it than to cut too much and have to start over.

FAQs

How often should I cut my hair in layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the layers lose their shape. As a general rule, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Can I cut my hair in layers if it's very long?

Yes, you can cut long hair in layers. Just make sure to section your hair properly and work in small sections to ensure evenness.

Do I need to use any products after cutting my hair in layers?

It's always a good idea to use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil after cutting your hair to keep it healthy and hydrated. You can also use a texturizing spray or mousse to enhance the layers and add volume to your hair.

Hair Care After the Cut

Once you've cut your hair in layers, it's essential to take good care of it to maintain its shape and health. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to avoid pulling and tugging on your hair, especially when it's wet.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve a professional-looking layered haircut at home. Remember to take your time and work in small sections to ensure evenness. With a little practice, you'll be a pro in no time!
