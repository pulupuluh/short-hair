How To Cut Back Of Boy s Hair With Scissors Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 14, 2023 · 7 min read

Cutting a boy's hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're doing it for the first time. But with a little bit of practice and patience, you can easily master the art of cutting a boy's hair at home. In this article, we'll show you how to cut the back of a boy's hair with scissors.

How to Cut Back of Boy's Hair with Scissors

Cutting the back of a boy's hair can be tricky, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clipper (optional)
  • Hair spray (optional)

Before you start cutting, make sure your child's hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be difficult to cut and can result in uneven haircuts.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut the back of a boy's hair with scissors:

Step 1: Section the Hair

Using a comb, section the hair at the back of the head. Start at the top of the head and comb the hair straight down. Use the comb to divide the hair into two sections – left and right.

Step 2: Cut the Hair

Starting with the left section, hold the hair between your fingers and cut it using the scissors. Cut the hair in a straight line, following the shape of the head. Repeat this process on the right section.

Step 3: Blend the Hair

To blend the hair, use the comb to comb the hair straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut any stray hairs. This will create a seamless blend between the two sections.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

Check the back of the head to make sure the haircut is even. Trim any uneven hairs until the haircut is symmetrical.

Step 5: Clean Up

Use a clipper to clean up the hairline at the back of the neck. Apply hair spray to hold the hair in place.

Tips for Cutting Back of Boy's Hair with Scissors

Here are some tips to help you achieve a great haircut:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid pulling or damaging the hair.
  • Comb the hair straight down to ensure an even cut.
  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time.
  • Blend the hair for a natural-looking haircut.
  • Use a clipper to clean up the hairline for a polished look.

FAQs

Can I cut my child's hair at home?

Yes, you can cut your child's hair at home. With a little bit of practice and patience, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut.

What tools do I need to cut my child's hair?

You will need sharp scissors, a comb, and a clipper (optional). Hair spray can also be used to hold the hair in place.

How often should I cut my child's hair?

It depends on how fast your child's hair grows. On average, children's hair grows about half an inch per month. You should aim to cut your child's hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain a neat and tidy look.

Hair Care

After cutting your child's hair, it's important to take care of their hair to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips:

  • Wash their hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep their hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners on their hair.
  • Trim their hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Cutting the back of a boy's hair with scissors can be easy and fun. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut at home. Just remember to take your time and follow the steps carefully for the best results.
