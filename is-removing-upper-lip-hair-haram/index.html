Is Removing Upper Lip Hair Haram

Written by Luffy Jun 27, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As Muslims, we are always conscious of the actions we take and the consequences they may have on our faith. One common question that many Muslim women ask is whether removing upper lip hair is haram or not. In this article, we will explore this question in detail and provide you with all the information you need to make an informed decision.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How To
  3. Step By Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Removing upper lip hair is a common practice among women. It is done for aesthetic purposes and to improve self-esteem. However, in Islam, any action that alters the natural state of the body is subject to scrutiny. In this case, removing upper lip hair may be considered haram if it involves altering the natural state of the body.

It is important to note that there is no clear consensus on whether removing upper lip hair is haram or not. Some scholars argue that it is permissible as long as it does not cause harm to the body, while others argue that it is not permissible because it alters the natural state of the body.

How To Remove Upper Lip Hair

If you decide to remove your upper lip hair, there are various methods you can use. These include:

1. Waxing

Waxing involves applying hot wax to the upper lip area and then removing it with a strip of cloth. This method is effective, but it can be painful.

2. Threading

Threading involves using a piece of thread to pull out the hair from the root. This method is less painful than waxing, but it can be time-consuming.

3. Tweezing

Tweezing involves using a pair of tweezers to pluck out individual hairs. This method is less painful than waxing, but it can be time-consuming.

Step By Step Guide

Method 1: Waxing

Step 1: Cleanse the upper lip area with a gentle cleanser and dry it thoroughly.
Step 2: Heat the wax according to the instructions on the packet.
Step 3: Apply the wax to the upper lip area using a spatula or applicator.
Step 4: Place a strip of cloth over the wax and press down firmly.
Step 5: Hold the skin taut and quickly pull off the cloth in the opposite direction of hair growth.
Step 6: Repeat steps 3 to 5 until all the hair is removed.
Step 7: Cleanse the area with a gentle cleanser and apply a soothing cream.

Method 2: Threading

Step 1: Cleanse the upper lip area with a gentle cleanser and dry it thoroughly.
Step 2: Hold a piece of thread between your fingers and twist it several times to create a loop.
Step 3: Place the loop over the hair you want to remove and move it back and forth while pulling it in the opposite direction of hair growth.
Step 4: Repeat steps 2 and 3 until all the hair is removed.
Step 5: Cleanse the area with a gentle cleanser and apply a soothing cream.

Method 3: Tweezing

Step 1: Cleanse the upper lip area with a gentle cleanser and dry it thoroughly.
Step 2: Use a pair of tweezers to pluck out individual hairs in the opposite direction of hair growth.
Step 3: Continue tweezing until all the hair is removed.
Step 4: Cleanse the area with a gentle cleanser and apply a soothing cream.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when removing upper lip hair:

  • Always use a gentle cleanser to cleanse the area before and after hair removal.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or products on the area.
  • Apply a soothing cream or aloe vera gel after hair removal to calm the skin.
  • Avoid sun exposure on the area for at least 24 hours after hair removal.

FAQ

1. Is removing upper lip hair haram?

There is no clear consensus on whether removing upper lip hair is haram or not. Some scholars argue that it is permissible as long as it does not cause harm to the body, while others argue that it is not permissible because it alters the natural state of the body.

2. Is it safe to remove upper lip hair?

Yes, it is safe to remove upper lip hair as long as you follow the proper techniques and use safe products.

3. What is the best method for removing upper lip hair?

The best method for removing upper lip hair may vary depending on your personal preferences and skin sensitivity. Some popular methods include waxing, threading, and tweezing.

Hair Care

It is important to take care of your skin and hair after removing upper lip hair. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid touching the area with dirty hands or objects.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or products on the area.
  • Apply a moisturizer to the area regularly to prevent dryness.
  • Use sunscreen on the area to protect it from sun damage.

In conclusion, whether removing upper lip hair is haram or not is a matter of personal interpretation. If you decide to remove your upper lip hair, make sure you follow the proper techniques and take care of your skin and hair afterwards.
