What Does God Say About Hair Dye

Written by Robby Apr 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Have you ever wondered whether dyeing your hair is wrong in the eyes of God? While the Bible doesn't specifically address hair dye, it does offer guidance on how we should approach our appearance. In this blog post, we'll explore what the Bible says about hair dye and offer some tips for making decisions about your hair color.

What Does the Bible Say About Appearance?

The Bible has various references to appearance, but it generally emphasizes the importance of inner beauty over external appearance. For example, in 1 Peter 3:3-4, it says, "Your beauty should not come from outward adornment, such as elaborate hairstyles and the wearing of gold jewelry or fine clothes. Rather, it should be that of your inner self, the unfading beauty of a gentle and quiet spirit, which is of great worth in God's sight."

While this passage doesn't specifically mention hair dye, it suggests that our focus should be on cultivating inner beauty rather than obsessing over our external appearance. However, this doesn't mean that we should neglect our physical appearance altogether.

Is Hair Dyeing a Sin?

There is no explicit biblical prohibition against dyeing hair. However, some Christians may choose not to dye their hair based on personal convictions or cultural norms. In some conservative Christian circles, dyeing hair may be seen as immodest or vain. However, these views are not universal and may vary based on individual interpretation of biblical principles.

How to Make Decisions About Hair Dyeing

If you're uncertain about whether dyeing your hair is appropriate, here are a few tips for making a decision:

1. Consider Your Motivations

Before dyeing your hair, think about why you want to do it. Are you trying to impress others or conform to societal beauty standards? Or are you simply looking for a change and want to express your creativity? If your motivations are purely external or superficial, it may be worth reevaluating your decision.

2. Consider Your Community

If you belong to a religious community that has specific views on hair dyeing, it's important to take those into consideration. While you don't necessarily have to conform to those views, it's important to be respectful of others' beliefs and avoid causing offense.

3. Consider Your Overall Appearance

While hair dyeing may not be explicitly prohibited in the Bible, it's still important to consider how your overall appearance reflects your values as a Christian. For example, if you dye your hair bright purple, it may be perceived as attention-seeking or rebellious, which may not align with biblical principles of modesty and humility.

FAQ About Hair Dyeing

1. Can Christians dye their hair?

Yes, there is no biblical prohibition against hair dyeing. However, Christians may choose not to dye their hair based on personal convictions or cultural norms.

2. Is hair dyeing a sin?

No, there is no explicit biblical prohibition against hair dyeing. However, some Christians may choose not to dye their hair based on personal convictions or cultural norms.

3. What hair colors are appropriate for Christians?

There is no specific hair color that is more appropriate for Christians than others. However, it's important to consider how your hair color reflects your overall appearance and values as a Christian.

How to Care for Dyed Hair

Whether you choose to dye your hair or not, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are a few tips:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can strip the hair of its natural oils and cause color to fade more quickly. Look for a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo that's specifically formulated for color-treated hair.

2. Condition regularly

Conditioner helps to replenish moisture and nutrients to the hair, which can help to prevent breakage and keep your color looking vibrant.

3. Protect from heat damage

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can cause damage to the hair and cause color to fade more quickly. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid excessive heat.

Conclusion

While the Bible doesn't specifically address hair dye, it offers guidance on how we should approach our appearance. As Christians, it's important to prioritize inner beauty over external appearance, but this doesn't mean that we should neglect our physical appearance altogether. Ultimately, the decision to dye your hair is a personal one that should be made based on your own convictions and values.
