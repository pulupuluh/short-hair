How To Wear Long Hair In The Summer Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Jul 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Long hair can be both a blessing and a curse, especially during the summer months. While it can be fun to experiment with different hairstyles, the heat and humidity can make it difficult to keep your locks looking and feeling their best. However, with the right care and styling techniques, you can rock your long hair all summer long. Here are some tips and tricks to help you wear long hair in the summer.

Table of Contents

  1. Hair Care
  2. How to
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ

Hair Care

The first step to wearing long hair in the summer is to make sure it's healthy and well-nourished. This means taking care of your hair with regular washing, conditioning, and deep conditioning treatments. It's also important to protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

You should also avoid using heat tools as much as possible, as they can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. Instead, opt for air-drying or using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to gently dry your hair without causing damage.

How to

When it comes to styling long hair in the summer, there are plenty of options to choose from. Here are a few ideas:

Braids

Braids are a great way to keep your hair off your face and neck while still looking stylish. Try a classic French braid, a fishtail braid, or a crown braid for a boho-chic look.

Ponytails

Ponytails are a classic summer hairstyle that never goes out of style. Try a sleek high ponytail for a sophisticated look, or a messy low ponytail for a more relaxed vibe.

Buns

Buns are another great way to keep your hair off your neck and out of your face. Try a sleek topknot, a messy bun, or a braided bun for a fun and unique look.

Step by Step

If you're not sure how to create these styles, here's a step-by-step guide:

French Braid

  1. Start by brushing your hair and dividing it into three sections at the crown of your head.
  2. Take the right section and cross it over the middle section, then take the left section and cross it over the middle section.
  3. Repeat this process, adding small sections of hair from either side as you go.
  4. Continue braiding until you reach the nape of your neck, then secure the braid with a hair tie.

Messy Bun

  1. Start by pulling your hair into a high ponytail at the crown of your head.
  2. Twist the ponytail around itself to create a bun, then secure it with a hair tie.
  3. Use bobby pins to secure any loose pieces of hair and add texture to the bun.
  4. Finish with hairspray to keep the style in place.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for wearing long hair in the summer:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair after washing.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much to prevent frizz.
  • Apply a hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair to keep them hydrated.
  • Use a dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes.
  • Experiment with different accessories, like headbands or scarves, to add some extra flair to your hairstyles.

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my long hair in the summer?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much you sweat. Generally, you should aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent it from becoming too oily or greasy.

Q: Can I still use heat tools on my long hair in the summer?

A: While it's best to avoid heat tools as much as possible, you can still use them occasionally. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray beforehand to minimize damage.

Q: How can I prevent my hair from getting tangled in the wind?

A: Try tying your hair back in a loose braid or ponytail to keep it from getting tangled in the wind. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or detangler spray to make your hair more manageable.

With these tips and tricks, you can wear your long hair with confidence all summer long. Whether you prefer braids, buns, or ponytails, there's a style that's perfect for you.
