Is Natural Hair Unprofessional Debunking The Myth

Written by William Apr 18, 2023 · 8 min read

In recent years, there has been a lot of debate surrounding the topic of natural hair in the workplace. Some people believe that natural hair is unprofessional, while others argue that it's a form of self-expression and should be celebrated. In this article, we'll take a closer look at this issue and explore whether or not natural hair is truly unprofessional.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Wear Natural Hair Professionally
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Natural Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Natural Hair in the Workplace
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Natural Hair

Description

First, let's define what we mean by "natural hair." Essentially, natural hair refers to hair that has not been chemically processed or altered in any way. This can include afros, braids, twists, and other styles that showcase the natural texture of the hair.

The idea that natural hair is unprofessional stems from a long history of discrimination against Black people and their hair. For many years, Black people were told that their natural hair was "unkept" or "unkempt," and that they needed to straighten it in order to look presentable in the workplace. This belief has persisted for decades, and has resulted in many Black people feeling pressure to conform to Eurocentric beauty standards.

How to Wear Natural Hair Professionally

The truth is, natural hair can be just as professional as any other hairstyle. The key is to find a style that works for you and that you feel confident wearing. Here are a few tips for wearing natural hair in the workplace:

  • Keep it neat and tidy: Just like with any hairstyle, it's important to keep your natural hair neat and tidy. This means regularly washing and conditioning your hair, and keeping it well-moisturized.
  • Choose a style that works for you: There are endless styling options when it comes to natural hair, so choose a style that works for your hair type and your personal style.
  • Accessorize: You can add a professional touch to your natural hair by accessorizing with headbands, clips, or scarves.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Natural Hair

If you're new to natural hair or just looking for some inspiration, here's a step-by-step guide to styling your natural hair:

  1. Start with clean, well-moisturized hair.
  2. Choose a styling product that works for your hair type (e.g. gel, mousse, cream, etc.)
  3. Select a styling method (e.g. twist-out, braid-out, wash-and-go, etc.)
  4. Apply the styling product to your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly.
  5. Follow the steps for your chosen styling method.
  6. Once your hair is dry, fluff it out to add volume and shape.
  7. Accessorize with headbands, clips, or scarves as desired.

Tips for Maintaining Natural Hair in the Workplace

Here are a few tips for maintaining your natural hair in the workplace:

  • Protect your hair at night: Use a satin or silk pillowcase, or wear a satin or silk scarf to bed to prevent breakage and maintain moisture.
  • Keep it moisturized: Natural hair can be prone to dryness, so make sure to keep it well-moisturized by using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.
  • Keep a few emergency styling products at work: You never know when you might need to touch up your hair, so keep a few styling products (e.g. gel, mousse, etc.) at your desk just in case.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it unprofessional to wear natural hair in the workplace?

No, it is not unprofessional to wear natural hair in the workplace. Natural hair can be just as professional as any other hairstyle, as long as it is neat and tidy.

Can I wear braids or twists to work?

Yes, you can wear braids or twists to work. Just make sure they are neat and tidy, and that they do not interfere with your job duties.

Do I have to straighten my hair to look professional?

No, you do not have to straighten your hair to look professional. Natural hair can be just as professional as any other hairstyle, as long as it is neat and tidy.

Hair Care for Natural Hair

Finally, here are a few tips for taking care of your natural hair:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair too frequently.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

As you can see, there is no reason why natural hair should be considered unprofessional. With the right care and styling, natural hair can be just as professional as any other hairstyle. So embrace your natural hair and wear it proudly!
