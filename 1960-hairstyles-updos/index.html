1960 Hairstyles Updos How To Achieve The Classic Look

If you're looking for a classic and elegant hairstyle, then the 1960 hairstyles updos are perfect for you. These updos were popular during the 1960s and continued to be a favorite even today. In this article, we will show you how to achieve the perfect 1960s updo with easy-to-follow steps, tips, and tricks. Let's get started!

How to Achieve the 1960 Hairstyles Updos

The 1960s were all about big hair, beehives, bouffants, and updos. These styles are still popular today because they are timeless and elegant. To achieve the perfect 1960s updo, you need to follow these steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help give your hair the volume it needs for the updo.

Step 2: Blow-dry your hair until it's completely dry. Use a round brush to create volume and lift at the roots.

Step 3: Once your hair is dry, create a center part and section your hair into three parts: the crown, the sides, and the back.

Step 4: Tease the crown section of your hair to create volume. Use a fine-toothed comb to backcomb your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down the length of your hair.

Step 5: Smooth out the teased hair with a soft-bristled brush, and twist it back into a bouffant. Secure it in place with bobby pins.

Step 6: Take the side sections of your hair, and twist them towards the back of your head. Secure them in place with bobby pins, tucking the ends under the bouffant.

Step 7: Finally, take the back section of your hair, and twist it upwards towards the crown. Secure it in place with bobby pins, tucking the ends under the bouffant.

Step 8: Spray your hair with hairspray to keep the updo in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect 1960s updo:

  • Use hot rollers or a curling iron to create large curls before creating the updo.
  • Choose a hairspray that has a strong hold to keep the updo in place all day.
  • Accessorize the updo with a headband, barrette, or flower to add a touch of glamour.
  • Practice the updo a few times before wearing it to an event to perfect your technique.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about 1960s updos:

What hair length is best for a 1960s updo?

Short to medium-length hair is best for a 1960s updo. However, if you have long hair, you can still achieve the updo by creating a faux-bob.

How long does it take to create a 1960s updo?

It can take anywhere from 20 minutes to an hour to create a 1960s updo, depending on your hair type and length.

Can you create a 1960s updo on curly hair?

Yes, you can create a 1960s updo on curly hair. Just make sure to smooth out any frizz and use a strong hold hairspray.

Hair Care for Updos

Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair while wearing updos:

  • Avoid washing your hair every day to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools to prevent damage.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using elastics or hair ties that can cause breakage and opt for soft scrunchies or hair pins.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve the perfect 1960s updo in no time. Whether you're attending a special event or simply want to add a touch of elegance to your everyday look, the 1960s updo is a timeless classic.
