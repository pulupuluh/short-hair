How To Get Nice Curly Hair For Guys A Complete Guide In 2023

Written by Robby Feb 18, 2023 · 12 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing or a curse, depending on how you style and take care of it. If you're a guy with curly hair, you might be wondering how to get nice curly hair that looks healthy, bouncy, and defined. Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll share some tips, tricks, and products that can help you achieve the curly hair of your dreams.

Understand Your Hair Type

The first step to getting nice curly hair is to understand your hair type. Not all curly hair is the same, and different types of curls require different care and styling. Here are some common types of curly hair:

  • Type 2: Wavy hair that forms an "S" shape
  • Type 3: Curly hair that forms a spiral or ringlet shape
  • Type 4: Coily hair that forms a tight, zigzag pattern

Once you know your hair type, you can choose the right products and styling techniques that work best for your curls.

Choose the Right Shampoo

Shampooing your hair is an essential part of any hair care routine, but using the wrong shampoo can dry out your curls and make them frizzy. Look for a shampoo that is designed for curly hair and doesn't contain harsh sulfates or alcohol. Some good options include:

  • Shea Moisture Coconut & Hibiscus Curl & Shine Shampoo
  • DevaCurl No-Poo Original Zero Lather Conditioning Cleanser
  • As I Am Curl Clarity Shampoo

Be sure to massage the shampoo into your scalp gently, and avoid using hot water, which can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Use Conditioner Regularly

Conditioning your hair is just as important as shampooing, if not more. Conditioner helps to hydrate and nourish your curls, making them softer, smoother, and more manageable. Look for a conditioner that is designed for curly hair and contains ingredients like shea butter, jojoba oil, or coconut oil. Some good options include:

  • Cantu Shea Butter for Natural Hair Leave-In Conditioning Repair Cream
  • Ouidad Curl Quencher Moisturizing Conditioner
  • Maui Moisture Curl Quench + Coconut Oil Conditioner

Apply conditioner to your hair after shampooing, and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing thoroughly with cool water.

Style Your Hair with the Right Products

Styling curly hair can be tricky, but using the right products can make all the difference. Here are some products that can help you achieve nice curly hair:

  • Curl cream: This product helps to define and moisturize your curls without weighing them down. Apply it to damp hair, then scrunch and twist your curls to encourage definition. Some good options include Bumble and Bumble Curl Defining Cream, Moroccanoil Curl Defining Cream, and Curls Blueberry Bliss Curl Control Jelly.
  • Gel: Gel can help to hold your curls in place and reduce frizz. Apply it to damp hair, then scrunch your curls and let them air dry or use a diffuser. Some good options include Eco Styler Olive Oil Gel, Giovanni L.A. Natural Styling Gel, and Aunt Jackie's Flaxseed Don't Shrink Curling Gel.
  • Mousse: Mousse can help to add volume and bounce to your curls. Apply it to damp hair, then scrunch your curls and let them air dry or use a diffuser. Some good options include OGX Locking + Coconut Curls Decadent Creamy Mousse, Herbal Essences Totally Twisted Curl Boosting Mousse, and L'Oreal Paris Advanced Hairstyle Curve It Elastic Curl Mousse.

Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair type and styling preferences.

Get a Curly Haircut

A good haircut can make a world of difference when it comes to curly hair. Look for a stylist who specializes in cutting curly hair, and ask for a cut that complements your face shape and curl pattern. Some popular curly haircuts for guys include:

  • The curly undercut: This style features short sides and a longer top, with the curls left to fall naturally.
  • The curly fringe: This style features longer curls on the front of the head, styled into a messy fringe.
  • The curly buzz cut: This style features a short, uniform length all over the head, with the curls left to add texture and volume.

Regular trims can also help to keep your curls looking neat and healthy.

Dry Your Hair Properly

Drying your hair is an important step in your styling routine, but using a towel or blow dryer can cause frizz and damage. Instead, try these drying methods:

  • Air drying: Letting your hair air dry is the gentlest way to dry your curls. After applying styling products, use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low or medium heat setting.
  • Plopping: This method involves using a cotton t-shirt or microfiber towel to wrap your hair and encourage curls to form. After applying styling products, lay the shirt or towel flat, flip your hair onto it, then wrap the ends around your head and secure with a hair tie. Leave it on for 10-20 minutes, then remove and let your hair air dry.

Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel or using hot air, which can cause damage and frizz.

Protect Your Hair from Damage

Curly hair is prone to damage from heat, chemicals, and environmental factors. To protect your curls, try these tips:

  • Use heat protectant: If you use a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron, be sure to use a heat protectant spray or serum to minimize damage.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals: If you color or perm your hair, be sure to use gentle, sulfate-free products and visit a stylist who specializes in curly hair.
  • Wear a hat: If you're going to be outside in the sun or wind, wear a hat or headscarf to protect your curls from damage.

Taking care of your curly hair is a daily commitment, but the results are worth it!

Curly Hair FAQ

Is it possible to make straight hair curly?

While you can't change the natural texture of your hair, you can use styling techniques and products to create the illusion of curls. Try using a curling iron or hot rollers, or experiment with different products like mousse or gel.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and styling routine, but most curly-haired people find that they can go longer between washes than those with straight hair. Aim to wash your hair 1-2 times per week, or as needed based on oiliness and product buildup.

Can I brush my curly hair?

It's generally best to avoid brushing curly hair, as it can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls when they're wet, and avoid touching them too much when they're dry.

Tips for Nice Curly Hair
