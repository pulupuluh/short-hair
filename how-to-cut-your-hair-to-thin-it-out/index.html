How To Cut Your Hair To Thin It Out A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Mar 16, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have thick hair, you know how challenging it can be to style and manage. One solution to this problem is to thin out your hair. Thinning your hair can make it more manageable and give it a lighter, more airy feel. In this article, we will show you how to cut your hair to thin it out. We will cover everything from the tools you need to the step-by-step process, tips, and hair care.

Tools You Will Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you will need a few tools. These include:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Thinning shears
  • Comb
  • Hair clips

Make sure your scissors and thinning shears are sharp. Dull blades can cause split ends and damage your hair.

How to Cut Your Hair to Thin It Out

Thinning out your hair is a process that involves removing some of the bulk from your hair. This can be done in many ways, including using thinning shears or razor cutting. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to cut your hair to thin it out.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash your hair and dry it thoroughly. Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: top, bottom, left, and right. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.
  3. Start with the bottom section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, about 2 inches from the ends.
  4. Using your thinning shears, cut the hair at a 45-degree angle, about halfway between your fingers and the ends of your hair. Do not cut too much hair at once. Repeat this process on all sections of your hair.
  5. Unclip the top section and repeat the same process. Comb a small section of hair straight down, hold it between your fingers, and use the thinning shears to remove some of the bulk from the hair.
  6. Repeat this process on the left and right sections of your hair.
  7. Once you have thinned out all sections of your hair, comb your hair to remove any loose hairs.
  8. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Thinning Your Hair

Thinning out your hair can be a challenging process, especially if you have never done it before. Here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with small sections of hair. It is better to cut too little hair than too much.
  • Do not cut too close to the scalp. This can cause your hair to look thin and uneven.
  • Use thinning shears instead of regular scissors. Thinning shears are designed to remove bulk from your hair without causing damage.
  • Do not thin out your hair too often. Thinning your hair too frequently can cause damage and split ends.

FAQs

Can I thin out my hair at home?

Yes, you can thin out your hair at home. However, if you are not confident in your abilities, it is best to visit a professional stylist.

Will thinning my hair make it look thinner?

No, thinning your hair does not make it look thinner. It only removes some of the bulk from your hair, making it more manageable.

How often should I thin out my hair?

You should only thin out your hair when it is necessary. Thinning your hair too often can cause damage and split ends.

Hair Care After Thinning Your Hair

After thinning out your hair, it is essential to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often. Heat can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach and hair dyes.

Thinning out your hair is an excellent way to make it more manageable and give it a lighter, more airy feel. By following the step-by-step guide and tips in this article, you can achieve great results at home. Remember to take good care of your hair after thinning it out to keep it healthy and beautiful.
