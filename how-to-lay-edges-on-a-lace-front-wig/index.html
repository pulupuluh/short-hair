How To Lay Edges On A Lace Front Wig A Step By Step Guide

How to Lay Edges on a Lace Front Wig: A Step-by-Step Guide | Hair Care Tips

If you're new to wearing wigs or just looking for a way to enhance your look, laying edges on a lace front wig is a great option. This simple technique can help create a sleek, natural-looking hairline that complements your wig perfectly. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps of laying edges on your lace front wig, from prepping your hairline to finishing touches. Read on for our tips and tricks!

Table of Contents:

  • Prepping Your Hairline
  • Applying Edge Control
  • Brushing and Shaping Your Edges
  • Adding Baby Hair
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Laying Edges on a Lace Front Wig
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Your Lace Front Wig

Prepping Your Hairline

Before you can start laying your edges, you need to prepare your hairline. First, make sure your wig is securely in place. Then, use a rat-tail comb to gently pull out any stray hairs or baby hairs from the wig's hairline. Next, use a small brush or toothbrush to lightly brush your hairline in the direction you want your edges to lay.

Some people like to use a little bit of water or oil on their hairline to help with this process. If you use water, make sure to pat your hairline dry before moving on to the next step.

Applying Edge Control

Now it's time to apply your edge control. Choose a product that works well with your hair type and is strong enough to hold your edges in place. Apply a small amount of product to your hairline, starting at the center and working your way outwards. Use your finger or a brush to smooth the product along your hairline, making sure to cover any stray hairs.

Be careful not to apply too much product, as this can cause buildup and weigh down your edges. If you accidentally apply too much, use a clean brush or toothbrush to lightly remove the excess.

Brushing and Shaping Your Edges

Once you've applied your edge control, use a small brush or toothbrush to brush your edges into place. Start at the center and work your way outwards, using small, gentle strokes. Use your fingers to shape your edges as you go, creating the desired look.

Remember to take your time and be gentle. Rushing or using too much force can cause your edges to look unnatural or even break off.

Adding Baby Hair

If you want to create a more natural-looking hairline, you can add baby hair to your lace front wig. To do this, use a small brush or toothbrush to gently pull out some hairs from the wig's hairline. Then, use your edge control to shape the baby hair into small, wispy strands.

Be careful not to add too much baby hair or make it too thick, as this can look unnatural. Start with a small amount and build up as needed.

Finishing Touches

Finally, it's time to add some finishing touches. Use a clean brush or toothbrush to lightly brush over your edges, smoothing out any stray hairs or flyaways. You can also use a little bit of hairspray or edge control to set your edges in place.

Once you're happy with your edges, take a step back and admire your work! Your lace front wig should now have a sleek, natural-looking hairline that complements your face shape and style.

Tips for Laying Edges on a Lace Front Wig

  • Choose an edge control product that works well with your hair type and is strong enough to hold your edges in place.
  • Be gentle when brushing and shaping your edges, and take your time to achieve the desired look.
  • Start with a small amount of baby hair and build up as needed to create a natural-looking hairline.
  • Use a clean brush or toothbrush to remove any excess edge control or product from your hairline.
  • Set your edges in place with a little bit of hairspray or edge control.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I lay edges on a synthetic lace front wig?

Yes, you can lay edges on a synthetic lace front wig. However, be careful not to use too much heat or product, as this can damage the fibers of the wig.

How often should I lay my edges?

This depends on your hair type and the style you're trying to achieve. Some people lay their edges every day, while others only do it for special occasions. Listen to your hair and do what feels best for you.

Can I use regular hair gel instead of edge control?

You can use regular hair gel instead of edge control, but it may not be as effective at holding your edges in place. Make sure to choose a gel that works well with your hair type and is strong enough to provide the desired hold.

Hair Care Tips for Your Lace Front Wig

  • Use a wig cap or wrap your hair before putting on your lace front wig to protect your natural hair.
  • Be gentle when brushing or styling your wig to avoid damaging the fibers.
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to help maintain its shape.
  • Wash your wig regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it looking fresh.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your wig, as this can damage the fibers.

By following these tips and tricks, you can lay edges on your lace front wig with ease and achieve a natural-looking hairline. Remember to be gentle, take your time, and have fun experimenting with different styles!
