How To Make Men s Hair Not Puffy Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Sep 06, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Do you have puffy hair that just won't stay in place? Are you tired of your hair looking frizzy and out of control? Well, you're not alone! Many men struggle with puffy hair, but there are ways to tame it and get the sleek, stylish look you desire. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to make men's hair not puffy, so you can look your best every day.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Make Men's Hair Not Puffy

Before we dive into the specifics, let's talk about what causes puffy hair in the first place. The most common culprits are humidity, dryness, and damage from styling tools or chemicals. To combat puffy hair, you'll need to address these underlying issues and adopt some new hair care habits.

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Dry hair is more likely to become puffy, so it's essential to keep your locks moisturized. Look for a shampoo and conditioner that are designed to hydrate your hair, such as those with argan oil, shea butter, or coconut oil. These ingredients will help to seal in moisture and prevent frizz.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

In addition to using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, you may want to try a leave-in conditioner. This product is designed to provide extra hydration and protection to your hair, and it will help to prevent your locks from becoming puffy. Apply a small amount to your hair, focusing on the ends, and comb through to distribute evenly.

3. Avoid Over-Brushing

Brushing your hair too much can actually make it puffy. When you brush your hair, you're distributing the natural oils from your scalp throughout your locks. This can be a good thing, as it helps to keep your hair healthy and shiny. However, if you brush your hair too much, you'll end up spreading the oils too thin, which can lead to dryness and frizz.

4. Blow-Dry Your Hair on a Low Setting

Blow-drying your hair can cause damage and dryness, which can lead to puffy locks. However, if you need to use a blow dryer, make sure to use it on a low setting. High heat can cause your hair to become brittle and frizzy, so it's best to avoid it. Additionally, make sure to hold the dryer at least six inches away from your hair to prevent damage.

5. Use a Hair Serum or Oil

A hair serum or oil can help to smooth down your hair and prevent it from becoming puffy. Look for a product that contains ingredients like argan oil, jojoba oil, or coconut oil. Apply a small amount to your hair, focusing on the ends, and comb through to distribute evenly.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics, let's walk through a step-by-step guide to making men's hair not puffy:

  1. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  3. Avoid over-brushing your hair.
  4. Blow-dry your hair on a low setting, holding the dryer at least six inches away from your hair.
  5. Apply a hair serum or oil to your hair, focusing on the ends.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to keep in mind when trying to make men's hair not puffy:

  • Avoid using hot styling tools, such as straighteners or curling irons, which can cause damage and dryness.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, which can dry out your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your locks of natural oils.
  • If you have curly hair, consider using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to prevent frizz.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about making men's hair not puffy:

Q: Can I use a hair gel or mousse to prevent puffy hair?

A: Yes, you can use a hair gel or mousse to help keep your hair in place and prevent it from becoming puffy. However, be sure to choose a product that is designed to hydrate your hair, as many gels and mousses can be drying.

Q: What should I do if my hair is already puffy?

A: If your hair is already puffy, you may want to try using a hair straightener to smooth it down. However, be careful not to use the straightener on wet hair, as this can cause damage. Instead, blow-dry your hair first, and then use the straightener on a low setting.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair or work out frequently, you may need to wash your hair every day. However, if you have dry hair or don't sweat much, you may be able to get away with washing your hair every other day or even less frequently.

Hair Care Routine

Finally, here's a sample hair care routine that you can follow to make men's hair not puffy:

  • Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner every other day.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing.
  • Blow-dry your hair on a low setting, holding the dryer at least six inches away from your hair.
  • Apply a hair serum or oil to your hair, focusing on the ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make men's hair not puffy and achieve the sleek, stylish look you desire. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid over-brushing, and use moisturizing products to keep your locks healthy and hydrated. With a little effort and patience, you can have the perfect hair every day!
