How To Style Permed Hair In The First 48 Hours

Written by Oliver Jul 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Perming your hair is a great way to add volume and curl to your locks. However, styling permed hair in the first 48 hours after getting a perm can be challenging. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to style permed hair in the first 48 hours. We will also share some tips and frequently asked questions about permed hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Style Permed Hair in the First 48 Hours
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Styling Permed Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Permed Hair

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step in styling permed hair is to wash your hair. Use a gentle shampoo that is specifically formulated for permed hair. Avoid using hot water, as it can damage your curls. Instead, use lukewarm or cool water to wash your hair.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to your locks. This will help to moisturize and protect your curls. Avoid using a heavy conditioner, as it can weigh down your curls. Instead, opt for a lightweight formula that is designed for permed hair.

Step 3: Towel Dry Your Hair

Gently towel dry your hair to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a soft towel or a t-shirt to blot your hair.

Step 4: Apply a Curl-Enhancing Product

Apply a curl-enhancing product to your hair to enhance your curls. You can use a mousse, gel, or cream depending on your hair type and the look you want to achieve. Apply the product to your hair from the roots to the ends, scrunching your curls as you go.

Step 5: Air Dry Your Hair

Avoid using a blow dryer to dry your hair, as it can cause frizz and damage your curls. Instead, let your hair air dry. If you need to speed up the drying process, use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, you can style it as desired. You can wear your hair down, or you can put it up in a ponytail or bun. Avoid using hair accessories that are too tight, as they can cause your curls to become flat.

Tips for Styling Permed Hair

Here are some tips for styling permed hair in the first 48 hours:

  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your curls
  • Use a silk pillowcase to prevent your curls from getting tangled while you sleep
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as it can cause frizz and disrupt your curls
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and make your curls frizzy
  • Avoid brushing your hair, as it can cause your curls to become flat

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I wash my permed hair in the first 48 hours?

Yes, you can wash your permed hair in the first 48 hours. However, you should use a gentle shampoo and avoid using hot water.

Can I use a blow dryer on my permed hair?

Avoid using a blow dryer on your permed hair, as it can damage your curls. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer.

How often should I wash my permed hair?

You should wash your permed hair every 2-3 days to avoid drying out your curls.

Hair Care for Permed Hair

Here are some tips for caring for permed hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo that is specifically formulated for permed hair
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing it
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, on your permed hair
  • Avoid brushing your hair, as it can cause your curls to become flat
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

Styling permed hair in the first 48 hours can be challenging, but with the right products and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, bouncy curls. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips to style your permed hair in the first 48 hours. With proper care and maintenance, your permed hair can look fabulous for months to come.
