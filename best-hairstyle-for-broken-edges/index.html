Best Hairstyles For Broken Edges A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Jun 15, 2023 · 7 min read

Dealing with broken edges can be a frustrating and challenging experience. Not only do they affect the overall appearance of your hair, but they can also be painful and uncomfortable. Fortunately, there are a variety of hairstyles that can help you cover up your broken edges while also promoting hair growth and preventing further damage. In this guide, we’ll be discussing the best hairstyles for broken edges and providing you with tips on how to care for your hair to prevent future breakage.

Description

Broken edges occur when the hair around your hairline becomes damaged, leading to breakage and thinning. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including tight hairstyles, chemical treatments, heat styling, and harsh hair products. Not only can broken edges be unsightly, but they can also be painful and uncomfortable. The good news is that there are plenty of hairstyles that can help you cover up your broken edges while also promoting hair growth and preventing further damage.

How to

When it comes to choosing the best hairstyle for your broken edges, there are a few things to keep in mind. First and foremost, you want to avoid any styles that put too much tension on your hairline. This includes tight braids, ponytails, and weaves. Instead, opt for looser styles that allow your hair to breathe and grow.

Another important factor to consider is the length of your hair. If your hair is short, you may want to consider a tapered cut or a pixie cut, which can help to minimize the appearance of your broken edges. If you have longer hair, you may want to consider wearing your hair in a protective style, such as twists or braids.

Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions for creating some of the best hairstyles for broken edges:

Tapered Cut

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair.
  2. Blow-dry your hair, using a heat protectant to prevent further damage.
  3. Use clippers to create a tapered cut, with shorter hair around the hairline and longer hair on top.
  4. Style your hair as desired using a hair gel or pomade.

Twists

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair.
  2. Section your hair into small sections.
  3. Apply a styling product to each section, such as a twisting butter or gel.
  4. Twist each section of hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Allow your hair to dry completely before unraveling the twists.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for your hair and preventing further damage to your edges:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioner regularly to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow and hair growth.

FAQ

What causes broken edges?

Broken edges can be caused by a variety of factors, including tight hairstyles, chemical treatments, heat styling, and harsh hair products.

Can broken edges be fixed?

Yes, broken edges can be fixed with proper hair care and the right hairstyles. It’s important to avoid putting too much tension on your hairline and to use gentle hair products that won’t cause further damage.

How can I prevent further damage to my edges?

To prevent further damage to your edges, avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair, use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase, use a deep conditioner regularly, and massage your scalp regularly to promote hair growth.

Hair Care

When it comes to caring for your hair and preventing broken edges, it’s important to use gentle hair products that won’t cause further damage. Look for products that are free of sulfates, parabens, and other harsh chemicals. It’s also important to avoid heat styling tools and to use a deep conditioner regularly to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Finally, be sure to massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow and hair growth.
