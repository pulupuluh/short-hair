When Is The Best Time To Use A 60 Degree Wedge

Written by Oliver Feb 21, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you a golfer who is wondering when the best time is to use a 60 degree wedge? Look no further! In this article, we will discuss the best situations to use this wedge, how to use it, and some tips to improve your game.

Table of Contents:

  • Best Situations to Use a 60 Degree Wedge
  • How to Use a 60 Degree Wedge
  • Tips to Improve Your Game with a 60 Degree Wedge
  • FAQs

Best Situations to Use a 60 Degree Wedge

The 60 degree wedge is a high loft wedge that is great for shots that require a high trajectory and a short distance. Here are some of the best situations to use a 60 degree wedge:

  • Chipping around the green
  • Bunker shots
  • Pitch shots from the rough or fairway
  • Flop shots over obstacles

Using a 60 degree wedge in these situations will help you get the ball high in the air and stop it quickly on the green.

How to Use a 60 Degree Wedge

Using a 60 degree wedge requires some skill and practice, but it can be a valuable tool in your golf game. Here are the steps to using a 60 degree wedge:

  1. Choose the right club: Make sure a 60 degree wedge is the right club for the shot you want to make.
  2. Get in position: Take your stance with your feet shoulder-width apart and the ball in the center of your stance.
  3. Open your stance: Open your left foot slightly if you’re right-handed, or your right foot if you’re left-handed.
  4. Open the clubface: Rotate the clubface open slightly to create loft.
  5. Take your swing: Swing the club back and through with a smooth, even tempo.
  6. Follow through: Continue your swing through the ball and finish with your chest facing the target.

Remember to practice your swing with a 60 degree wedge to get comfortable with the club and improve your accuracy.

Tips to Improve Your Game with a 60 Degree Wedge

Here are some tips to help you improve your game with a 60 degree wedge:

  • Practice your swing regularly to improve your accuracy and consistency.
  • Experiment with different stances and clubface positions to find what works best for you.
  • Use a soft touch when hitting the ball to create spin and control.
  • Focus on hitting the ball first, then the ground.
  • Try to create a descending strike to get the ball high in the air and stop it quickly on the green.

By incorporating these tips into your game, you can improve your accuracy and control with a 60 degree wedge.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a 60 degree wedge for full shots?

A: While a 60 degree wedge is primarily designed for short shots around the green, it can be used for full shots if necessary. However, it requires a lot of skill and practice to hit full shots accurately with a 60 degree wedge.

Q: Can I use a 60 degree wedge out of the rough?

A: Yes, a 60 degree wedge can be used to hit pitch shots from the rough. However, it requires a steep angle of attack to get the ball out of the rough and onto the green.

Q: Should I use a 60 degree wedge if I’m not confident with it?

A: It’s always best to use a club you’re confident with, so if you’re not comfortable with a 60 degree wedge, try using a lower loft wedge instead.

Now that you know when the best time is to use a 60 degree wedge, get out on the course and start practicing! With some skill and practice, the 60 degree wedge can be a valuable tool in your golf game.
