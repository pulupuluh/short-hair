How To Cut Full Bangs At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of constantly going to the salon to get your bangs trimmed? Do you want to save money and learn how to cut full bangs at home? Look no further! In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut full bangs at home.

How to Cut Full Bangs at Home

Cutting your own bangs can be daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a breeze. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • Clips to hold back the rest of your hair

Step 1: Choose the Right Bangs for Your Face Shape

Before you start cutting, it's important to choose the right bangs for your face shape. Here are some guidelines:

  • If you have a round face, go for side-swept bangs that are longer on the sides.
  • If you have a square face, opt for soft, wispy bangs.
  • If you have a heart-shaped face, choose a piecey, textured fringe.
  • If you have an oval face, you can pull off just about any type of bangs.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and blow-drying your hair. Then, use a comb to create a straight part down the middle of your head.

Next, use clips to hold back the rest of your hair, leaving only the section you want to cut bangs from.

Step 3: Measure and Cut

Hold the section of hair straight up in front of your face, then use your comb to comb it straight down. Use your fingers to pinch the hair where you want to cut it, then use your sharp scissors to make the cut.

Remember to cut small sections at a time and to cut vertically, not horizontally. This will create a more natural, textured look.

Step 4: Check Your Work

Once you've cut your bangs, use a mirror to check your work. Make any necessary adjustments by trimming small sections at a time.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

Now that you've cut your bangs, it's time to style them. Use a round brush and a blow dryer to create volume and shape.

If you want a more textured look, use a flat iron to create waves or curls in your bangs.

Tips for Cutting Full Bangs at Home

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect full bangs:

  • Always cut less than you think you need. You can always go back and trim more if necessary.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut your bangs when they're wet. They'll look shorter when they dry.
  • Take breaks and step away from the mirror. This will give you a fresh perspective and help you avoid over-cutting.

FAQs About Cutting Full Bangs at Home

Can I cut full bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut full bangs if you have curly hair. Just make sure to cut them when they're dry and to cut them a little longer than you want them to be, as they'll shrink up when they dry.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic! You can always trim your bangs a little more or style them differently to hide the mistake.

Hair Care for Full Bangs

Once you've cut your full bangs, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but don't over-wash it. This can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause your bangs to look greasy.
  • Use a dry shampoo in between washes to keep your bangs looking fresh.
  • Blow dry your bangs every day to keep them looking smooth and styled.
  • Avoid touching your bangs throughout the day, as this can make them look greasy and oily.

Now that you know how to cut full bangs at home, you'll never have to go to the salon for a trim again! Just remember to take your time, follow the steps, and use the right tools.
