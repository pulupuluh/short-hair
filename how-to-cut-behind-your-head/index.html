How To Cut Behind Your Head A Step By Step Guide

Have you ever tried to cut your own hair and struggled to reach the back of your head? It can be a difficult task, but with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve a great cut all around. In this article, we'll show you how to cut behind your head in relaxed English language, with easy-to-follow steps and helpful tips. Let's get started!

How to Cut Behind Your Head

Cutting the hair behind your head can be tricky, but it's not impossible. Here are a few things you'll need to get started:

  • A mirror
  • A pair of sharp hair-cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Once you have all of these items, you're ready to start cutting!

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to cut behind your head:

  1. Start by wetting your hair with the spray bottle. This will make it easier to comb through and cut.
  2. Use the comb to part your hair down the middle, dividing it into two sections.
  3. Take one section of hair and comb it straight back. Hold it with your non-dominant hand.
  4. Using your dominant hand, hold the scissors and cut the hair straight across. Be careful not to cut too much at once!
  5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 on the other side of your head.
  6. Comb your hair straight back again, this time taking a smaller section of hair.
  7. Cut the hair straight across using the same technique as before. Be sure to cut in small increments until you achieve the desired length.
  8. Repeat steps 6 and 7, taking small sections of hair each time until you've cut all the way around your head.
  9. Check your work in the mirror, making any necessary adjustments until you're satisfied with the length and style.

Tips for a Great Cut

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the best possible cut:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut gradually. It's easier to cut more hair off than to add it back on!
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's dry, as this can lead to uneven cutting and split ends.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting your own hair can be tricky, but with practice, you'll get better!
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, consider visiting a professional stylist for help.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting hair behind your head:

  • Can I cut my hair straight across the back? Yes, you can! This is the most common technique for cutting hair behind your head.
  • What if I make a mistake? Don't worry! If you accidentally cut too much hair, you can always wait for it to grow back or visit a professional stylist to fix the problem.
  • How often should I cut the hair behind my head? This depends on your hair type and personal preference. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

Hair Care After Your Cut

After cutting your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are a few tips to follow:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it's important to take your time and follow the right steps to achieve the best possible results. With the tips and techniques outlined in this article, you'll be able to cut hair behind your head like a pro in no time!
