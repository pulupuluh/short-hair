Buzz Cut Should I Shave My Head A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 07, 2023 · 12 min read

Are you considering a buzz cut or even shaving your head? This decision can be a big one, as it involves a significant change to your appearance. In this article, we’ll explore the pros and cons of a buzz cut and shaving your head, as well as how to do it, tips and tricks, and hair care advice. Let’s dive in!

Table of Contents

  • Pros and Cons of a Buzz Cut or Shaved Head
  • How to Buzz Cut or Shave Your Head
  • Tips and Tricks for a Successful Buzz Cut or Shaved Head
  • FAQ About Buzz Cuts and Shaved Heads
  • Hair Care for Buzz Cuts and Shaved Heads

Pros and Cons of a Buzz Cut or Shaved Head

Before you make the decision to buzz cut or shave your head, it’s important to consider the pros and cons of each option. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

Pros of a Buzz Cut

A buzz cut can be a great option for a number of reasons:

  • Low maintenance: A buzz cut requires very little upkeep, making it a great option for people who don’t want to spend a lot of time styling their hair.
  • Cool and comfortable: Buzz cuts can be a great option for the summer months, as they keep your head cool and comfortable.
  • Can be stylish: A buzz cut can be a stylish option, especially when combined with a well-groomed beard or other facial hair.

Cons of a Buzz Cut

While there are many pros to a buzz cut, there are also a few cons to consider:

  • No hiding bad hair days: With a buzz cut, there’s no hiding a bad hair day. If you wake up with bedhead or messy hair, everyone will be able to see it.
  • Requires frequent maintenance: While a buzz cut is low maintenance overall, it does require frequent trimming to keep it looking neat and tidy.
  • May not be suitable for everyone: Finally, it’s worth noting that a buzz cut may not be suitable for everyone. If you have a particularly round or square face, for example, a buzz cut may not be the most flattering option.

Pros of a Shaved Head

Here are a few reasons why you might consider shaving your head:

  • Freedom from hair: Shaving your head can be liberating, especially if you’ve always struggled with your hair or felt self-conscious about it.
  • Low maintenance: Like a buzz cut, a shaved head requires very little maintenance.
  • Easily customizable: With a shaved head, you can experiment with different designs or patterns, if you’re feeling adventurous.

Cons of a Shaved Head

However, there are also some downsides to consider:

  • Requires frequent shaving: To keep your head smooth and hair-free, you’ll need to shave it regularly.
  • Can be high maintenance: While a shaved head requires less maintenance than some other hairstyles, it can still require more upkeep than a buzz cut.
  • May not be suitable for everyone: Finally, it’s worth noting that a shaved head may not be suitable for everyone. If you have a particularly lumpy or bumpy head shape, for example, shaving your head may not be the most flattering option.

How to Buzz Cut or Shave Your Head

If you’ve decided to go for a buzz cut or shaved head, the next step is to learn how to do it. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

How to Buzz Cut Your Hair

  1. Choose the right clipper guard: Start by choosing the right clipper guard for your desired length. For a true buzz cut, you’ll want to use a guard that’s no longer than a #4.
  2. Start at the sides: Begin by trimming the sides of your head, using the clipper guard to ensure an even length all around.
  3. Move to the top: Once you’ve trimmed the sides, move on to the top of your head. Again, use the clipper guard to ensure an even length.
  4. Clean up the edges: Use a pair of scissors or a razor to clean up the edges around your ears and at the nape of your neck.
  5. Finish with a shower: Finally, take a shower to wash away any loose hairs and to clean your scalp.

How to Shave Your Head

  1. Choose the right razor: Start by choosing the right razor for the job. A safety razor or a straight razor may work best, depending on your preference.
  2. Trim your hair: Before you start shaving, use a pair of clippers or scissors to trim your hair down as short as possible.
  3. Apply shaving cream: Apply a generous amount of shaving cream to your scalp, making sure to cover all the areas you want to shave.
  4. Start shaving: Begin shaving your head, using short, even strokes. Be careful not to apply too much pressure, as this can cause irritation or cuts.
  5. Clean up: Once you’ve finished shaving, rinse your head with warm water and apply a soothing aftershave balm or lotion to help prevent irritation.

Tips and Tricks for a Successful Buzz Cut or Shaved Head

Here are a few tips and tricks to keep in mind as you buzz cut or shave your head:

  • Use a mirror: It can be hard to see the back of your head, so make sure to use a mirror to check your progress as you go.
  • Go slow: Take your time as you buzz cut or shave your head. Rushing can lead to mistakes or unevenness.
  • Start with a longer guard: If you’re unsure about how short you want to go, start with a longer clipper guard or trim your hair down with scissors before shaving your head completely.
  • Consider going to a barber: If you’re nervous about doing it yourself, consider going to a barber or stylist for your first buzz cut or shaved head.

FAQ About Buzz Cuts and Shaved Heads

Is shaving your head bad for your hair?

No, shaving your head is not bad for your hair. In fact, it can actually be good for your hair, as it removes damaged or dead hair and encourages healthy growth.

Will my hair grow back differently if I shave my head?

No, your hair will not grow back differently if you shave your head. However, it may appear thicker or coarser at first, due to the blunt ends of the newly grown hair.

Do I need any special products for a buzz cut or shaved head?

Not necessarily. However, you may want to invest in a good quality razor or clipper, as well as a soothing aftershave balm or lotion to prevent irritation.

Hair Care for Buzz Cuts and Shaved Heads

While a buzz cut or shaved head may be low maintenance, it’s still important to take care of your scalp and hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Moisturize your scalp: Use a moisturizing lotion or oil to keep your scalp from becoming dry or flaky.
  • Protect your scalp from the sun: Wear a hat or use sunscreen to protect your scalp from the sun’s rays.
  • Keep it clean: Wash your scalp regularly to prevent buildup of sweat, oil, and dirt.
  • Massage your scalp: Massaging your scalp can help improve blood flow and promote healthy hair growth.

And there you have it – everything you need to know about buzz cuts and shaved heads. Whether you’re looking for a low-maintenance hairstyle or want to make a bold statement with your look, a buzz cut or shaved head may be the perfect choice for you. Good luck!
