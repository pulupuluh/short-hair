Can Cutting Hair Reduce Hair Fall The Truth Revealed In 2023

Written by Frank Mar 24, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're struggling with hair fall, you've probably tried every product and treatment out there to stop it. But have you ever considered that cutting your hair might be the solution you've been looking for? In this article, we'll explore whether cutting your hair can reduce hair fall, and give you tips on how to do it right.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Hair to Reduce Hair Fall
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Hair
  • Tips for Cutting Your Hair to Reduce Hair Fall
  • FAQs About Cutting Hair and Hair Fall
  • Hair Care Tips to Prevent Hair Fall

How to Cut Your Hair to Reduce Hair Fall

The idea behind cutting your hair to reduce hair fall is simple: by trimming your hair regularly, you remove split ends and prevent breakage, which can lead to hair fall. However, it's important to cut your hair the right way to see the best results.

1. Use the Right Tools

Invest in a good pair of scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. These will give you a clean cut and prevent damage to your hair.

2. Cut Your Hair When It's Dry

Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so it's best to cut your hair when it's dry. This will also give you a more accurate idea of how much you're cutting.

3. Cut Your Hair Regularly

How often you should cut your hair depends on how fast it grows and the length you want to maintain. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Hair

If you're confident in your hair cutting skills, you can do it yourself at home. Here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see the length you want to cut.

2. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

3. Cut Your Hair

Starting with the bottom section, trim a small amount of hair at a time. Use the previous section as a guide for length. Continue until you've cut all sections.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair to Reduce Hair Fall

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair:

  • Don't cut too much at once. Gradual trimming is better for your hair than a drastic chop.
  • Use hair shears rather than regular scissors, which can damage your hair.
  • Consider getting a professional trim if you're not confident in your hair cutting skills.

FAQs About Cutting Hair and Hair Fall

Does cutting your hair make it thicker?

No, cutting your hair doesn't change the thickness of your hair. However, it can make your hair appear thicker by removing split ends and preventing breakage.

Can cutting your hair cause hair fall?

No, cutting your hair doesn't cause hair fall. In fact, regular trimming can prevent hair fall by removing split ends and preventing breakage.

How often should you cut your hair to prevent hair fall?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent hair fall caused by split ends and breakage. However, this may vary depending on your hair type and how fast it grows.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Hair Fall

Cutting your hair is just one way to prevent hair fall. Here are some additional hair care tips:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals and heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals that support hair health.
  • Try using a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish your hair.

By following these tips and regularly cutting your hair, you can reduce hair fall and achieve healthier, stronger hair.
