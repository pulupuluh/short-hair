Low Maintenance Medium Sized Dogs Australia A Guide For Easy Care

Written by Luffy May 06, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you looking for a low maintenance dog that won't require too much time and effort? If you live in Australia and prefer a medium sized breed, then you're in luck. In this article, we'll provide you with a guide on low maintenance medium sized dogs in Australia, including how to choose the right breed, tips on care and grooming, and frequently asked questions.

How to Choose the Right Breed

Before choosing a low maintenance medium sized dog, it's important to consider your lifestyle and preferences. Some breeds may require less grooming but need more exercise or attention, while others may be less active but need more grooming. Here are some factors to consider when choosing a breed:

Activity Level

If you prefer a more laid-back dog, then look for breeds that are known to be less active, such as the Bulldog, Basset Hound, or Greyhound. On the other hand, if you enjoy outdoor activities and want a companion that can keep up, consider breeds like the Australian Cattle Dog or the Border Collie.

Grooming Needs

For easier grooming, choose breeds with shorter coats, such as the Beagle, Boxer, or Whippet. Breeds with longer coats, such as the Afghan Hound or the Standard Poodle, may require more frequent brushing and trimming.

Temperament

Consider a breed's personality and behavior traits. Some breeds may be more independent and require less attention, such as the Basenji or the Shar Pei. Others may be more social and affectionate, like the Golden Retriever or the Labrador Retriever.

Tips on Care and Grooming

Regardless of the breed you choose, here are some general tips on low maintenance care and grooming:

Regular Exercise

Even low maintenance dogs need regular exercise to maintain their health and wellbeing. Aim for at least 30 minutes of daily activity, such as walks, runs, or playtime.

Healthy Diet

Feed your dog a balanced and nutritious diet that meets their specific needs. Consult with your veterinarian for recommendations on the best food choices.

Regular Check-ups

Take your dog to the veterinarian for regular check-ups and preventative care, such as vaccinations and parasite control.

Brushing and Bathing

Brush your dog's coat regularly to prevent matting and shedding. Bathe your dog as needed, but not too frequently as it can strip their skin of natural oils.

Teeth Care

Regularly clean your dog's teeth to prevent dental problems. You can use a toothbrush and toothpaste specifically designed for dogs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about low maintenance medium sized dogs in Australia:

Q: What is the easiest medium sized dog to take care of?

A: The Beagle and the Boxer are two of the easiest medium sized dogs to take care of, as they have short coats and are generally low maintenance.

Q: Do all medium sized dogs need a lot of exercise?

A: No, not all medium sized dogs require a lot of exercise. Some breeds, such as the Bulldog or the Basset Hound, are more laid-back and may prefer less activity.

Q: Can low maintenance dogs still make good companions?

A: Yes, absolutely. Low maintenance dogs can still make great companions and provide love and affection to their owners.

Hair Care

One of the main concerns of many dog owners is shedding. While it's impossible to completely eliminate shedding, there are some tips you can follow to minimize it:

Regular Brushing

Brush your dog's coat at least once a week to remove loose hair and prevent matting. Use a brush that's appropriate for your dog's coat type, such as a slicker brush or a bristle brush.

Bathing

Bathe your dog regularly to keep their coat clean and healthy. Use a shampoo that's specifically designed for dogs and avoid using human shampoo as it can be too harsh for their skin.

Diet

Feed your dog a balanced and nutritious diet that's rich in essential fatty acids. Omega-3 and Omega-6 fatty acids can help improve the health and condition of their coat.

Supplements

If your dog has a particularly dry or dull coat, you may want to consider adding a supplement to their diet. Fish oil or flaxseed oil are good options that can help improve the condition of their skin and coat.

Conclusion

Low maintenance medium sized dogs are a great option for those who want a furry companion without the high maintenance. By choosing the right breed and following some basic care and grooming tips, you can enjoy a happy and healthy relationship with your dog. Remember to consult with your veterinarian for specific recommendations and advice.
