How To Get Dreadlocks With Short Straight Hair

Written by Bardi May 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have short straight hair, you may have thought that getting dreadlocks is not possible. But, with the right technique, you can achieve the hairstyle you desire. In this article, we will guide you on how to get dreadlocks with short straight hair. We will provide you with step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice. Let's get started.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Dreadlocks with Short Straight Hair
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for Getting Dreadlocks with Short Straight Hair

  • Hair Care Tips for Dreadlocks

How to Get Dreadlocks with Short Straight Hair

The first thing you need to do is to grow out your hair to at least 2 inches long. This will make it easier for you to create dreadlocks. Once your hair is long enough, you can start the process of creating your dreadlocks.

Tools You Will Need

  • Dreadlock wax or gel
  • A fine-toothed comb
  • Hair clips
  • Rubber bands

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before you start the process of creating your dreadlocks, make sure to wash your hair thoroughly. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or oil from your hair. This will make it easier for you to work with your hair.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Using a fine-toothed comb, divide your hair into small sections. The size of the sections will depend on how thick or thin you want your dreadlocks to be. Use hair clips to keep the sections separated.

Step 3: Apply Wax or Gel

Apply a small amount of dreadlock wax or gel to each section of your hair. This will help to hold the hair together and create the dreadlocks. Make sure to apply the wax or gel evenly throughout each section.

Step 4: Backcomb Your Hair

Starting at the roots of each section, use a fine-toothed comb to backcomb your hair. This will create knots in your hair, which will eventually turn into dreadlocks. Make sure to backcomb each section of your hair thoroughly.

Step 5: Twist Your Hair

Once you have backcombed each section of your hair, twist the hair in a clockwise direction. This will help to tighten the knots and create the dreadlocks. Secure the ends of each section with a rubber band.

Step 6: Maintain Your Dreadlocks

Once you have created your dreadlocks, you will need to maintain them. This includes washing them regularly and reapplying wax or gel to keep them tight. You may also need to retwist your hair every few weeks to keep your dreadlocks looking neat.

Tips for Getting Dreadlocks with Short Straight Hair

  • Be patient. Creating dreadlocks takes time and effort.
  • Avoid using too much wax or gel, as this can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or oil from your hair before starting the process.
  • Consider using a dreadlock shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get dreadlocks with short straight hair?

Yes, you can get dreadlocks with short straight hair. You will need to grow out your hair to at least 2 inches long and follow the steps outlined in this article.

How long does it take to create dreadlocks?

The time it takes to create dreadlocks will depend on the length and thickness of your hair. It can take anywhere from a few hours to a few days to create dreadlocks.

How do I maintain my dreadlocks?

To maintain your dreadlocks, you will need to wash them regularly and reapply wax or gel to keep them tight. You may also need to retwist your hair every few weeks to keep your dreadlocks looking neat.

Hair Care Tips for Dreadlocks

  • Wash your dreadlocks regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners, as they can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a dreadlock wax or gel to keep your hair tight.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.

With these tips and instructions, you can achieve the dreadlock hairstyle you desire, even with short straight hair. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair to keep your dreadlocks looking great.
