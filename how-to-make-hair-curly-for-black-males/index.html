How To Make Hair Curly For Black Males A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 08, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're a black male with straight hair, you may have wondered how to make your hair curly. The good news is that there are several ways to achieve this look, from using natural methods to chemical treatments. In this article, we'll explore the best ways to make hair curly for black males, including step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • Natural Methods
  • Chemical Treatments
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for Making Hair Curly
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Natural Methods

One of the easiest ways to make hair curly is to use natural methods. Here are some options to try:

Twist-Outs

Twist-outs involve twisting sections of hair and letting them dry before unraveling them for a curly effect. To do a twist-out, follow these steps:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Divide hair into small sections.
  3. Apply a styling product like a curl cream, gel, or mousse.
  4. Twist each section of hair tightly, all the way to the end.
  5. Let hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on a blow dryer.
  6. Once hair is dry, gently unravel the twists and style as desired.

Twist-outs can last for several days with proper care and maintenance.

Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are another natural method for creating curls. Here's how to do them:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Divide hair into small sections.
  3. Apply a styling product like a curl cream, gel, or mousse.
  4. Twist each section of hair tightly, then wrap the twist around itself to form a knot.
  5. Secure the knot with a hair tie or bobby pin.
  6. Repeat until all sections of hair are knotted.
  7. Let hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on a blow dryer.
  8. Once hair is dry, gently unravel the knots and style as desired.

Bantu knots can also last for several days with proper care and maintenance.

Chemical Treatments

If you're looking for a more permanent solution to make hair curly, chemical treatments may be the way to go. Here are some options to consider:

Relaxer

A relaxer is a chemical treatment that permanently straightens hair. However, if used correctly, it can also be used to make hair curly. Here's how to do it:

  1. Choose a relaxer formula that is specifically designed for making hair curly.
  2. Apply the relaxer to your hair, following the instructions on the package.
  3. Leave the relaxer on for the recommended amount of time.
  4. Rinse your hair thoroughly with water.
  5. Apply a neutralizing shampoo to stop the chemical process.
  6. Follow up with a deep conditioner to restore moisture to your hair.

It's important to note that relaxers can be damaging to hair if not used correctly, so it's best to seek the advice of a professional stylist before attempting this treatment at home.

Perm

A perm is another chemical treatment that can be used to make hair curly. Here's how to do it:

  1. Choose a perm formula that is specifically designed for black hair.
  2. Apply the perm solution to your hair, following the instructions on the package.
  3. Leave the perm solution on for the recommended amount of time.
  4. Rinse your hair thoroughly with water.
  5. Apply a neutralizing shampoo to stop the chemical process.
  6. Follow up with a deep conditioner to restore moisture to your hair.

As with relaxers, it's best to seek the advice of a professional stylist before attempting a perm at home.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Whether you choose a natural method or a chemical treatment, these step-by-step instructions will help you achieve a curly look:

  1. Start with clean hair.
  2. Apply a styling product like a curl cream, gel, or mousse.
  3. Divide hair into small sections.
  4. Follow the instructions for your chosen method (twist-outs, Bantu knots, relaxer, or perm).
  5. Let hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on a blow dryer.
  6. Once hair is dry, gently unravel or style as desired.

Tips for Making Hair Curly

Here are some tips to help you make your hair curly:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle hair before styling.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage hair.
  • Protect hair at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or using a satin or silk pillowcase.
  • Maintain your curls by refreshing them with a spray bottle of water and styling product.

FAQs

Can I make my hair curly without using chemicals?

Yes, there are several natural methods for making hair curly, such as twist-outs and Bantu knots.

Will chemical treatments damage my hair?

Chemical treatments like relaxers and perms can be damaging to hair if not used correctly. It's best to seek the advice of a professional stylist before attempting these treatments at home.

How long will my curls last?

The longevity of your curls depends on the method used and how well you maintain them. Natural methods like twist-outs and Bantu knots can last for several days, while chemical treatments can last for several months.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

To keep your curls looking their best, it's important to follow a hair care routine that includes:

  • Using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  • Deep conditioning regularly to restore moisture to hair.
  • Using a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define curls.
  • Avoiding heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons.
  • Protecting hair at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or using a satin or silk pillowcase.

Conclusion

With these tips and techniques, you can make your hair curly and embrace your natural texture. Whether you choose a natural method or a chemical treatment, it's important to follow a hair care routine that keeps your curls healthy and looking their best.
