How To Fade Your Own Hair Black A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 31, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of your current hair color and want to switch things up? Fading your hair black can be a bold and exciting change. However, going to a salon can be expensive, and you may not have the time to spare. Why not try doing it yourself? With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a faded black look at home. Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you get started.

How to

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wash and condition it the day before and let it air dry. Avoid using any styling products or oils that can make your hair harder to work with.

Step 2: Gather Your Supplies

Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Bleach powder
  • Developer (20 or 30 volume)
  • Mixing bowl and brush
  • Gloves
  • Black hair dye
  • Conditioner

Make sure to wear old clothes or a cape to avoid staining your clothes.

Step 3: Bleach Your Hair

Starting at the back of your head, section your hair and apply bleach to your ends. Work your way up to your roots, making sure to apply evenly. Leave it on for the recommended time (usually 30 minutes), then wash it out with shampoo and conditioner.

Step 4: Apply Black Hair Dye

Once your hair is dry, mix your black hair dye according to the instructions on the box. Apply it to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Make sure to saturate your hair evenly, and let it sit for the recommended time (usually 25-30 minutes).

Step 5: Rinse and Condition

After the time is up, rinse your hair with cold water until the water runs clear. Apply conditioner and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

Tips

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as it can strip the color and cause it to fade faster.
  • Wear gloves when handling bleach and dye to avoid staining your hands.
  • It’s better to go slow and do multiple applications than to try to achieve the perfect shade in one go.

FAQ

Can I fade my hair black if it’s already dyed?

Yes, you can. However, you may need to bleach your hair first to remove the previous color.

How long will the color last?

The duration of the color depends on several factors, such as how often you wash your hair and how well you maintain it. On average, it can last between 4-6 weeks.

Can I use a different color instead of black?

Yes, you can. Just make sure to follow the instructions on the box and adjust the timing accordingly.

Hair Care

Fading your hair black can be damaging to your hair, so it’s essential to take care of it properly. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the color and causing further damage. You can also use a hair mask once a week to provide extra nourishment and moisture.

With this step-by-step guide and a little bit of patience and care, you can successfully fade your hair black at home. Enjoy your new look!
