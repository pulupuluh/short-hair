How Do I Get My Natural Wavy Hair Back

Written by Christine Mar 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of constantly straightening your hair and missing your natural wavy texture? You're not alone! Many people with naturally wavy hair struggle to maintain their curls, especially if they've been chemically relaxed or straightened. Don't worry, though, getting your natural waves back is possible. Here's how to do it:

Description

Before we dive into the steps to get your natural wavy hair back, let's talk about what causes wavy hair to lose its texture. One of the most common reasons is chemical straightening or relaxing treatments, which alter the hair's natural texture. Heat styling tools like straighteners and blow dryers can also cause damage to your hair, resulting in a loss of wave pattern. Additionally, using the wrong hair products or not taking care of your hair properly can lead to a loss of wave definition.

How to Get Your Natural Wavy Hair Back

Step 1: Stop Using Chemical Treatments

The first step to getting your natural waves back is to stop using chemical treatments like relaxers or straighteners. These treatments alter the natural texture of your hair and can be damaging to your strands. Instead, embrace your natural texture and work with it.

Step 2: Reduce Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like straighteners and blow dryers can also cause damage to your hair and lead to a loss of wave pattern. Try to reduce your use of these tools and opt for air drying your hair instead. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Step 3: Use the Right Products

Using the right hair products is crucial to getting your natural waves back. Look for products specifically designed for wavy or curly hair. These products will help define your waves and keep them looking their best. Avoid products with harsh chemicals or sulfates, which can be damaging to your hair.

Step 4: Scrunch Your Hair

One of the easiest ways to enhance your natural waves is to scrunch your hair. After washing your hair, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently scrunch your hair. This will help define your waves and encourage them to form.

Step 5: Use a Diffuser

If you must use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment. This will help distribute the heat evenly and prevent your waves from getting frizzy. Start on low heat and low speed, and work your way up as needed.

Tips for Maintaining Your Natural Waves

Once you've got your natural waves back, it's important to maintain them. Here are some tips to help keep your waves looking their best:

  • Don't over-wash your hair. Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to frizz and a loss of wave definition.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry. This can cause frizz and disrupt your wave pattern. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Invest in a silk or satin pillowcase. These materials are gentle on your hair and can help prevent frizz and breakage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I restore my natural waves if my hair has been straightened?

Yes, it's possible to restore your natural waves even if your hair has been straightened. However, it may take some time and patience. Follow the steps outlined above and be gentle with your hair.

How often should I scrunch my hair?

You can scrunch your hair as often as you like, but it's best to avoid over-manipulating your hair. Every few days should be sufficient.

Hair Care for Natural Waves

Caring for your natural waves is essential to maintaining their health and texture. Here are some tips for keeping your waves looking their best:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips, you can get your natural wavy hair back and keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Embrace your natural texture and work with it to create the perfect waves.
