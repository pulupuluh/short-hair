Why Does My Hair Look Shorter When I Straighten It

Written by Robby Mar 20, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’ve ever straightened your hair only to find that it looks significantly shorter than it did before, you’re not alone. This is a common experience for many people, and there are several reasons why it happens. In this article, we’ll explore some of the factors that contribute to this phenomenon and offer tips for preventing it from happening.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

When you straighten your hair, it can appear shorter for a few reasons. One of the main causes is that the hair strands are stretched out when they are straightened, which can cause them to lose some of their length. Additionally, if your hair is naturally curly or wavy, straightening it can cause it to lose volume, which can make it look shorter.

How To Prevent Your Hair From Looking Shorter When Straightening It:

There are several things you can do to prevent your hair from looking shorter when you straighten it:

1. Use A Heat Protectant:

Using a heat protectant spray or serum before straightening your hair can help to minimize damage and prevent breakage. This can help to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent it from looking shorter than it actually is.

2. Don’t Pull Your Hair Too Tightly:

If you pull your hair too tightly when straightening it, you can cause the strands to stretch out and lose some of their length. Be gentle when straightening your hair and avoid pulling it too tightly.

3. Use A Quality Straightener:

A high-quality straightener will be less damaging to your hair and will help to keep it looking healthy and full. Look for a straightener with ceramic plates and adjustable heat settings.

Step By Step Guide To Straightening Your Hair:

Here’s a step-by-step guide to straightening your hair:

Step 1: Wash And Condition Your Hair:

Start by washing and conditioning your hair using a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Rinse thoroughly and towel dry your hair.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant:

Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Section Your Hair:

Section your hair into manageable sections using clips or hair ties. Start at the bottom and work your way up.

Step 4: Straighten Your Hair:

Take a small section of hair and run the straightener over it, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Repeat this process until you have straightened all of your hair.

Step 5: Finish With Serum:

Apply a small amount of serum to your hair to add shine and prevent frizz.

Tips For Straightening Your Hair:

Here are some additional tips for straightening your hair:

1. Don’t Straighten Wet Hair:

Always make sure your hair is completely dry before straightening it. Straightening wet hair can cause damage and breakage.

2. Use A Wide-Tooth Comb:

Before straightening your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle it. This will help to prevent breakage and make the straightening process easier.

3. Don’t Straighten Your Hair Too Often:

Straightening your hair too often can cause damage and breakage. Try to limit the number of times you straighten your hair to once or twice a week.

FAQ:

Q: Will Straightening My Hair Damage It?

A: Straightening your hair can cause damage if you don’t use proper techniques and products. Always use a heat protectant and avoid pulling your hair too tightly.

Q: How Can I Add Volume To Straightened Hair?

A: To add volume to straightened hair, try using a volumizing mousse or spray before blow drying. You can also flip your head upside down and use a round brush to lift the roots.

Hair Care For Straightened Hair:

Here are some tips for caring for your hair after straightening it:

1. Use A Leave-In Conditioner:

Using a leave-in conditioner can help to keep your hair soft and hydrated after straightening it.

2. Avoid Heat Styling:

Try to avoid using heat styling tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.

3. Trim Regularly:

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and full.

In conclusion, your hair may look shorter after straightening it due to the stretching of the hair strands and loss of volume. However, by using proper techniques and products, you can prevent this from happening and keep your hair looking healthy and full.
