How To Know If A Haircut Will Look Good A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Mar 01, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of getting haircuts that don't suit your face shape or hair texture? Do you want to know how to choose a haircut that will flatter your features and make you feel confident? Look no further, because we've got you covered with this comprehensive guide on how to know if a haircut will look good.

Table of Contents:

  • Understanding Your Face Shape
  • Considering Your Hair Texture
  • Consulting with Your Hair Stylist
  • Trying Virtual Hairstyle Apps
  • Taking Inspiration from Celebrities

Understanding Your Face Shape

One of the most important factors to consider when choosing a haircut is your face shape. Your face shape can help determine which haircuts will flatter your features and which ones won't. The four main face shapes are:

  • Round
  • Oval
  • Square
  • Heart

If you're unsure what your face shape is, you can use a measuring tape to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Then, compare the measurements to determine your face shape. Once you know your face shape, you can research haircuts that are ideal for your specific shape.

Considering Your Hair Texture

In addition to your face shape, your hair texture is another important factor to consider when choosing a haircut. If you have fine hair, for example, you may want to avoid heavy layers that can weigh down your hair. On the other hand, if you have thick hair, you may want to consider a layered haircut to help thin out your hair and make it more manageable.

It's also important to consider your styling habits when choosing a haircut. If you prefer low-maintenance hairstyles, you may want to opt for a shorter haircut that requires less styling. However, if you enjoy experimenting with different hairstyles, you may want to choose a longer haircut with more versatility.

Consulting with Your Hair Stylist

Your hair stylist is a valuable resource when it comes to choosing a haircut that will look good on you. They can assess your face shape and hair texture and recommend haircuts that will flatter your features. It's important to communicate your hair goals and any concerns you may have with your stylist so they can provide the best possible advice.

It's also a good idea to bring pictures of hairstyles you like to your appointment. This can help your stylist get a better idea of the look you're going for and make recommendations based on your preferences.

Trying Virtual Hairstyle Apps

If you're hesitant to try a new haircut without seeing how it will look first, virtual hairstyle apps can be a helpful tool. These apps allow you to upload a photo of yourself and try on different hairstyles to see how they would look on you. While the results may not be 100% accurate, they can give you a good idea of what to expect.

Taking Inspiration from Celebrities

Celebrities can be a great source of inspiration when it comes to choosing a new haircut. Look for celebrities with similar face shapes and hair textures as you and take note of the hairstyles they wear. You can also browse magazines and social media for hairstyle inspiration.

Keep in mind that while a certain haircut may look great on a celebrity, it may not necessarily be the best choice for you. It's important to take into account your individual features and preferences when choosing a haircut.

FAQs

Q: How often should I get a haircut?

A: This depends on your hair type and the length of your haircut. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: What should I do if I don't like my new haircut?

A: If you're unhappy with your new haircut, communicate your concerns with your stylist. They may be able to make adjustments to the haircut or offer suggestions for alternative styles.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've chosen a haircut that looks good on you, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for your hair type
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently
  • Protect your hair from UV rays with a hat or hair products containing SPF
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Stay hydrated and eat a balanced diet for healthy hair growth

By following these tips and choosing a haircut that flatters your features, you can achieve a stylish and confidence-boosting look.
