Does Alopecia Always Come Back

Written by Christine Mar 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

In this article, we will explore the question of whether alopecia always comes back. We will discuss the causes of alopecia, the different types of alopecia, and the likelihood of alopecia returning after treatment. We will also provide some tips on how to care for your hair if you have alopecia.

What is Alopecia?

Alopecia is a medical condition that affects the hair follicles. It can cause hair loss on the scalp, face, and other areas of the body. There are several types of alopecia, including:

  • Androgenetic alopecia
  • Alopecia areata
  • Traction alopecia
  • Scarring alopecia

Does Alopecia Always Come Back?

The answer to this question depends on the type of alopecia and the treatment used. In some cases, alopecia can be treated and the hair will grow back. However, in other cases, the hair may not grow back or may grow back and then fall out again.

Androgenetic Alopecia

Androgenetic alopecia, also known as male or female pattern baldness, is a genetic condition that causes hair loss. It is the most common type of alopecia and affects both men and women. This type of alopecia is progressive, which means it gets worse over time. While there are treatments available to slow down or stop the progression of androgenetic alopecia, there is no cure. In some cases, hair transplant surgery may be an option to restore hair growth.

Alopecia Areata

Alopecia areata is an autoimmune condition that causes hair loss in patches on the scalp, face, and other areas of the body. The hair loss can be temporary or permanent, and it can come and go. In some cases, the hair may grow back on its own without treatment. However, in other cases, treatment may be needed to stimulate hair growth. Even with treatment, there is no guarantee that the hair will grow back.

Traction Alopecia

Traction alopecia is a type of hair loss that is caused by pulling on the hair. This can be due to tight hairstyles such as braids, weaves, or ponytails. Traction alopecia can be reversed if the hair is no longer pulled on and the hair follicles are not damaged. However, if the hair follicles are damaged, the hair may not grow back.

Scarring Alopecia

Scarring alopecia is a type of hair loss that is caused by damage to the hair follicles. This can be due to inflammation, infection, or injury. The hair loss is permanent because the hair follicles are destroyed. Treatment for scarring alopecia may involve reducing inflammation or infection, but it cannot restore hair growth.

How to Care for Your Hair if You Have Alopecia

If you have alopecia, it is important to take care of your hair to prevent further damage and to promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair
  • Use gentle hair care products
  • Avoid heat styling tools
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Consider using hair growth products such as minoxidil

FAQ

Can alopecia be cured?

There is no cure for alopecia, but there are treatments available to slow down or stop the progression of hair loss and to stimulate hair growth.

Does hair always grow back after alopecia?

Whether hair grows back after alopecia depends on the type of alopecia and the treatment used. In some cases, hair may grow back on its own without treatment. In other cases, treatment may be needed to stimulate hair growth. However, there is no guarantee that the hair will grow back.

Can stress cause alopecia?

Stress can contribute to alopecia, but it is not the sole cause. Other factors such as genetics, autoimmune disorders, and hormonal imbalances can also play a role in the development of alopecia.

Conclusion

In conclusion, whether alopecia always comes back depends on the type of alopecia and the treatment used. While there is no cure for alopecia, there are treatments available to slow down or stop the progression of hair loss and to stimulate hair growth. It is important to take care of your hair if you have alopecia to prevent further damage and to promote healthy hair growth.
