How To Cut Long Hair Straight Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a way to save money on haircuts, or just want to try something new, cutting your own hair at home is a great option. However, if you have long hair, it can be tricky to achieve a straight cut without any professional help. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut long hair straight yourself, with easy-to-follow steps and tips for a successful DIY haircut.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Long Hair Straight Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Success
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After the Cut

Description

Before you get started, it's important to understand what you're getting into. Cutting your own long hair straight can be a challenging task, especially if you've never done it before. You'll need to have the right tools, a steady hand, and patience. However, with the right guidance and practice, you can achieve a great result and save money on salon visits.

How to Cut Long Hair Straight Yourself

The first thing you'll need to do is gather your tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror, preferably a full-length one, so you can see the back of your head.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting your long hair straight:

  1. Begin by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. This will make it easier to cut and ensure a more accurate result.
  2. Using your comb, section your hair into four equal parts: two at the front and two at the back.
  3. Starting at the back, take one section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers at the length you want to cut it.
  4. Cut the hair straight across using your scissors. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as this can result in an uneven cut.
  5. Move on to the next section of hair, working your way around your head. Make sure to cut each section to the same length.
  6. Once you've cut all the sections, check for any uneven spots or stray hairs. Trim these as needed.
  7. Repeat the process with the front sections of hair, being careful to maintain a straight line across your forehead.
  8. Finally, check your hair in the mirror to ensure you're happy with the overall result.

Tips for Success

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best result:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up, rather than cutting too much at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Make sure your hair is completely dry before cutting, as wet hair can appear longer and result in an uneven cut.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and be patient.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about cutting long hair straight:

  • Can I cut my hair straight with a razor?

    • While some people prefer to use a razor for cutting hair, it can be more difficult to achieve a straight line. We recommend using scissors for a more precise cut.

  • How often should I cut my hair?

    • This can vary depending on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it's recommended to trim long hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

  • What if I make a mistake?

    • If you accidentally cut too much, don't panic. You can always trim a little more to even out the length. If you're really unhappy with the result, you can always seek professional help at a salon.

Hair Care After the Cut

Congratulations, you've successfully cut your long hair straight yourself! Now it's time to take care of your new haircut. Make sure to use a good quality conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. You may also want to use a heat protectant if you plan to use hot styling tools. And of course, don't forget to take some selfies and show off your new look!
