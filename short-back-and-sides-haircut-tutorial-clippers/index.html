Short Back and Sides Haircut Tutorial Clippers - How to Guide

If you're looking for a classic, stylish haircut that's easy to maintain and looks great on almost anyone, then the short back and sides haircut is the way to go. It's a timeless look that's been popular for decades, and it's not going anywhere anytime soon. In this tutorial, we'll show you how to achieve the perfect short back and sides haircut using clippers.

How to Achieve the Perfect Short Back and Sides Haircut with Clippers

Before we get started, you'll need a few things:

  • A set of clippers with adjustable guards
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A towel
  • A cape or old shirt to protect your clothing

Once you have everything you need, you can get started on your haircut:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and will help you achieve a more even cut. Once your hair is dry, comb it out so that it's free of tangles and knots.

Step 2: Choose Your Guard Size

Next, choose the guard size you want to use. For a classic short back and sides haircut, you'll want to use a smaller guard on the sides and back of your head and a larger guard on top. Start with a guard that's longer than what you think you'll need, and work your way down gradually. It's always better to take off less hair than to take off too much.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start at the nape of your neck and work your way up, using the clippers and the chosen guard size. Move the clippers in an upward motion, against the direction of hair growth, to ensure an even cut. Work your way around the sides and back of your head, making sure to keep the clippers level with your head to avoid any uneven patches.

Step 4: Taper the Sides

Once you've finished cutting the back and sides of your head, it's time to taper the sides. Switch to a smaller guard size and work your way up from the bottom of your hairline, blending the shorter hair into the longer hair on top. Use the clippers without a guard to create a clean line around your sideburns and the hairline around your ears.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Finally, it's time to cut the top of your hair. Use a larger guard size and work your way from the front to the back of your head. You can leave the hair slightly longer on top if you prefer a more modern look, or you can keep it short for a more traditional look.

Tips for Achieving the Perfect Short Back and Sides Haircut

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the perfect short back and sides haircut:

  • Take your time and go slowly. It's better to take off less hair than to take off too much.
  • Use the mirror to check the back of your head as you go.
  • Make sure to keep the clippers level with your head to avoid any uneven patches.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.

FAQ

How often should I get a short back and sides haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it starts to look shaggy. Most people get a short back and sides haircut every 4-6 weeks.

Can I do a short back and sides haircut myself?

Yes, you can do a short back and sides haircut yourself, but it can be tricky to get it right. If you're not confident in your skills, it's always better to go to a professional.

Hair Care

To keep your short back and sides haircut looking great, you'll need to take good care of your hair. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat on your hair. You should also get regular trims to keep your haircut looking sharp.

Now that you know how to achieve the perfect short back and sides haircut with clippers, you can rock this classic look with confidence. Remember to take your time, go slowly, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
