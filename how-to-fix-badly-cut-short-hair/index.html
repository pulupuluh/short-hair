How To Fix Badly Cut Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Having a bad haircut can be frustrating, especially when it's short hair that doesn't leave much room for hiding mistakes. But don't worry, you don't have to wear a hat or grow out your hair to fix a bad haircut. In this article, we'll show you how to fix badly cut short hair in easy steps.

Description

A bad haircut can happen for many reasons, from miscommunication with your hairdresser to trying to cut your hair at home. Whatever the reason, a bad haircut can leave you feeling self-conscious and unhappy with your appearance. But don't fret, there are ways to fix it.

How to Fix Badly Cut Short Hair

Here are some steps you can follow to fix a bad haircut:

1. Assess the Damage

Take a good look at your hair and identify what you don't like about the cut. Is it too short? Uneven layers? Crooked bangs? Understanding what needs to be fixed will help you determine the best course of action.

2. Don't Panic

It's important to stay calm and not panic when you have a bad haircut. Panicking can lead to impulsive decisions that may make the situation worse. Take a deep breath and remind yourself that it's just hair, and it will grow back.

3. Visit a Professional

If you're not confident in fixing your hair yourself, it's best to visit a professional hairstylist. They'll be able to assess the damage and offer solutions that work for your hair type and face shape. Be honest with them about what you don't like about the cut and what you want to achieve.

4. Trim the Ends

If you're comfortable trimming your hair yourself, you can start by trimming the ends. This will help remove any uneven or damaged parts of your hair. Use sharp scissors and make small snips, working in sections.

5. Add Layers

Adding layers can help create movement and texture to your hair, making it look more natural and less choppy. Use thinning shears to add layers or ask your hairstylist to do it for you.

6. Style Your Hair

Styling your hair can also help disguise a bad haircut. Experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you. Use a flat iron or curling iron to create waves or curls, or use a texturizing spray to add volume and texture.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you fix a bad haircut:

  • Be patient. It takes time for hair to grow back, so don't expect immediate results.
  • Use hair accessories, such as headbands, clips, or hats, to hide any areas you're not happy with.
  • Keep your hair healthy by using a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Consider getting a wig or hair extensions if you're not happy with your hair while it's growing out.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing a bad haircut:

How long does it take for hair to grow back?

On average, hair grows about half an inch per month. However, this can vary depending on genetics, age, and health. It may take several months to a year for your hair to grow back to its original length.

Can I fix a bad haircut myself?

If you're comfortable with trimming your hair, you can fix some aspects of a bad haircut yourself. However, for more complicated fixes, it's best to visit a professional hairstylist.

What are some quick ways to hide a bad haircut?

Wearing a hat, using hair accessories, or styling your hair differently are all quick ways to hide a bad haircut.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important, especially if you've had a bad haircut. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too often.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat tools.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Fixing a bad haircut can be stressful, but with the right steps and a little patience, you can have hair that makes you feel confident and happy. Remember, it's just hair, and it will grow back.
