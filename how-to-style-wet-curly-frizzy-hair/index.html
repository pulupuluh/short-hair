How To Style Wet Curly Frizzy Hair A Guide For 2023

Written by Aprilia Jul 01, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizzy, unmanageable curls? Do you struggle to style your wet hair without it turning into a mess? If so, you're not alone. Many people with curly hair struggle to find the right products and techniques to keep their hair looking great. In this guide, we'll show you how to style wet curly frizzy hair in a way that works for you. From washing to drying to styling, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  2. How to Dry Your Hair
  3. How to Style Your Hair
  4. Tips for Managing Frizz
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Wash Your Hair

The first step in styling wet curly frizzy hair is to properly wash it. You'll want to use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for curly hair. Look for products that are sulfate-free and contain moisturizing ingredients like coconut oil, shea butter, or argan oil. You should also avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it even more prone to frizz.

When washing your hair, start by wetting it thoroughly. Apply a small amount of shampoo to your scalp and massage it in gently, being careful not to tangle your hair. Rinse thoroughly and apply conditioner from mid-length to the ends of your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair while the conditioner is still in it. Rinse thoroughly with cool water to seal the cuticle and prevent frizz.

How to Dry Your Hair

Once you've washed your hair, it's time to dry it. The key to avoiding frizz is to be gentle with your hair and use the right tools. Start by gently squeezing out excess water with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt. Avoid rubbing your hair with a regular towel, as this can create friction and cause frizz.

If you have time, let your hair air dry for a bit before using any styling tools. If you need to use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment and set it to the lowest heat setting. Hold the diffuser close to your scalp and move it around in a circular motion to dry your roots. Then, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently scrunch your hair and encourage your curls to form.

How to Style Your Hair

Now that your hair is clean and dry, it's time to style it. The key to styling wet curly frizzy hair is to use the right products and techniques. Start by applying a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair, working it through with your fingers or a wide-tooth comb. Then, use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to gently dry your curls, scrunching them as you go.

If you prefer to air dry your hair, you can use a curl defining gel or mousse to help your curls hold their shape. Apply the product to your hair and use your fingers to scrunch your curls as they dry. You can also try using a plopping technique, which involves wrapping your hair in a t-shirt or microfiber towel to encourage your curls to form.

Tips for Managing Frizz

Even with the right products and techniques, curly hair can still be prone to frizz. Here are some tips to help you manage frizz:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage while you sleep.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can create frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, rather than a brush.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

FAQs

Q: Can I wash my curly hair every day?

A: It's best to avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz. Try washing your hair every 2-3 days, or using a dry shampoo in between washes.

Q: How can I prevent my curls from getting tangled?

A: Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up. Avoid brushing your hair, as this can create more tangles and breakage.

Q: What can I do if my hair is still frizzy after styling?

A: Try using a small amount of anti-frizz serum or hair oil to smooth down any flyaways or frizz. You can also try using a hair spray or gel to hold your curls in place.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

In addition to the tips and techniques we've covered, there are a few general hair care practices that can help keep your curly hair looking great:

  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos or styling products that contain sulfates or alcohol.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and techniques, you can learn how to style wet curly frizzy hair in a way that works for you. With the right products and practices, you can embrace your natural curls and keep them looking great year-round.
