How To Stop My Curls From Going Flat Tips And Tricks

Written by Luffy Aug 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you know how frustrating it can be when your curls fall flat. It can happen for a variety of reasons, from humidity to heat damage to using the wrong products. But don't worry - there are plenty of ways to keep your curls looking bouncy and full. Here are some tips and tricks to help you stop your curls from going flat.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop My Curls from Going Flat
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Healthy Curls

How to Stop My Curls from Going Flat

The first step to stopping your curls from going flat is to identify why it's happening. Here are some common reasons:

  • Humidity
  • Heat damage
  • Using the wrong products
  • Over-washing or under-washing
  • Not using enough product

Once you've identified the cause, you can take steps to correct it. Here are some general tips:

1. Use the Right Products

Using the right products for your hair type is essential. Look for products that are designed for curly hair and are free from sulfates, parabens, and silicones. These ingredients can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and frizzy. Instead, opt for products that contain natural ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and aloe vera.

2. Apply Products Correctly

When applying products to your hair, make sure you're doing it correctly. Start with a leave-in conditioner or curl cream, then apply a gel or mousse to hold the curl in place. Be sure to apply the products evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

3. Don't Over-Wash or Under-Wash

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, causing it to become dry and frizzy. On the other hand, not washing it enough can cause buildup and make your curls look limp. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, and use a sulfate-free shampoo.

4. Use Heat Sparingly

Heat can damage your hair and cause your curls to fall flat. Try to avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

5. Sleep with Your Hair Up

Sleeping with your hair down can cause it to become flat and tangled. Instead, try sleeping with your hair in a loose bun or pineapple (a high ponytail on top of your head). This will help preserve your curls overnight.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to keeping your curls looking bouncy and full:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Apply Products

Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to damp hair. Then, apply a gel or mousse to hold the curl in place. Be sure to apply the products evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Dry Your Hair

Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair. Start on low heat and speed, and work your way up. Make sure to scrunch your hair as you dry it to enhance your curls.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, you can style it as desired. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently comb through your curls, or use your fingers to separate them for a more natural look.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for keeping your curls looking their best:

  • Use a microfiber towel or t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel. This will help prevent frizz.
  • Try a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to help prevent tangles and frizz.

FAQs

Q: Can I use regular shampoo on my curly hair?

A: It's best to avoid regular shampoos that contain sulfates, as these can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and frizzy. Look for sulfate-free shampoos that are designed for curly hair.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days. Washing it too often can strip it of its natural oils, while not washing it enough can cause buildup and make your curls look limp.

Hair Care for Healthy Curls

Keeping your curls looking their best requires a good hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Sleep with your hair in a loose bun or pineapple to preserve your curls.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much.

With these tips and tricks, you can keep your curls looking bouncy and full.
