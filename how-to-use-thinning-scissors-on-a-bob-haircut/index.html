How To Use Thinning Scissors On A Bob Haircut

Written by Oliver Feb 23, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of having thick, heavy hair? Do you want to add texture and movement to your bob haircut? Thinning scissors may be the solution you are looking for. In this article, we will guide you on how to use thinning scissors on a bob haircut.

Description

Thinning scissors are a type of scissors that have teeth on one blade and a straight edge on the other. They are designed to remove bulk from the hair without changing the overall length. Thinning scissors work by cutting some strands of hair while leaving others intact, creating a textured and layered look.

How to

Before using thinning scissors, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Comb your hair straight and divide it into sections. Start with the bottom section and work your way up.

Step by Step

1. Hold the thinning scissors at a 45-degree angle and slide them down a section of hair, starting about an inch away from the scalp.

2. Open and close the scissors as you move down the length of the hair. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can create choppy layers.

3. Continue thinning the section until you achieve the desired texture. Repeat the process on the other sections of hair.

Tips

1. Practice on a small section of hair first to get the hang of using thinning scissors.

2. Always use sharp thinning scissors to avoid damaging your hair.

3. Avoid thinning the top layer of your hair, as this can create unevenness and make your hair look thin and wispy.

FAQ

Q: Can I use thinning scissors on curly hair?

A: Yes, but be careful not to thin too much as curly hair tends to bounce up and appear shorter.

Q: How often should I use thinning scissors on my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and desired look. For most people, once every three to four months is sufficient.

Hair Care

After using thinning scissors on your hair, it is important to take good care of it. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and always use a heat protectant. Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and texture.

Thinning scissors can be a great tool for adding texture and movement to your bob haircut. With these simple steps, you can achieve a salon-worthy look at home. Happy styling!
