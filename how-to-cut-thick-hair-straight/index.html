How To Cut Thick Hair Straight A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 23, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Cutting your own hair can be challenging, especially if you have thick hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a straight cut that looks professional. In this article, we'll walk you through how to cut thick hair straight, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning
  • Cutting
  • Blending
  • Tips for Cutting Thick Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Tools You'll Need

Before you begin cutting your hair, you'll need the following tools:

  • Hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • Hair clips
  • A mirror

It's important to use hair cutting scissors rather than regular scissors, as they're designed to make precise cuts and won't damage your hair.

Preparation

Begin by washing and conditioning your hair, and then towel dry it until it's damp. Make sure your hair is completely detangled, as knots can make it difficult to cut your hair straight.

Next, choose the length you want to cut your hair to and make a mental note of it.

Sectioning

To ensure an even cut, you'll need to section your hair into four parts:

  • Front left
  • Front right
  • Back left
  • Back right

Use hair clips to secure each section out of the way, leaving one section free to cut at a time.

Cutting

Begin by taking a small section of hair from the free section and combing it straight down. Use your fingers to hold the hair at the length you want to cut it to, and then cut straight across using your hair cutting scissors.

Repeat this process, working your way from the back of your head to the front, until you've cut all of the hair in the section.

Once you've finished one section, unclip the next section and repeat the process.

Blending

Once you've cut all four sections of your hair, it's time to blend them together. To do this, take small sections of hair from each section and comb them together. Use your hair cutting scissors to trim any uneven ends, and continue until your hair is blended together evenly.

Tips for Cutting Thick Hair

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Cut your hair when it's damp, not wet or dry
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time
  • Don't pull your hair too tight when cutting it
  • Take breaks if your arms get tired

FAQ

Can I cut my hair straight without sectioning it?

While it's possible to cut your hair straight without sectioning it, it's much more difficult to achieve an even cut. Sectioning your hair ensures that you're cutting all of your hair to the same length.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, it's important to use hair cutting scissors, as they're designed to make precise cuts and won't damage your hair like regular scissors can.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it healthy and looking great. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep your ends healthy.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut your thick hair straight at home and achieve a professional-looking result.
