Why Is The Back Of My Curly Hair Frizzy

Written by Petter Aug 20, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Curly hair can be a challenge to manage, and one of the most frustrating issues is dealing with frizz. You may have noticed that the back of your curly hair tends to be particularly prone to frizz, leaving you with an untamed, unkempt look. In this article, we'll explore some of the reasons why the back of your curly hair may be frizzy and offer some tips and solutions to help you tame those unruly locks.

Description

Frizz is a common problem for curly-haired individuals, and it can be caused by a variety of factors. The back of your hair is often more prone to frizz because it is more exposed to the elements, such as wind and humidity. Additionally, the back of your hair is often the most neglected area when it comes to styling and maintenance, which can exacerbate the problem.

How to

If you're dealing with frizzy hair, there are several things you can do to help tame those unruly locks. Here are a few tips:

1. Moisturize

One of the most important things you can do for your hair is to keep it moisturized. Curly hair tends to be drier than other hair types, so it's important to use a moisturizing conditioner after every wash. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help keep your hair hydrated throughout the day.

2. Use the Right Products

When it comes to styling curly hair, using the right products is key. Look for products that are designed for curly hair, such as curl-enhancing creams or gels. Avoid products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and make frizz worse.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can be damaging to your hair, and it can also contribute to frizz. Instead of using a straightener or curling iron, try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser to dry your curls. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to taming frizzy hair:

1. Wash and Condition

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to avoid any product buildup that can contribute to frizz.

2. Apply Products

Apply a curl-enhancing cream or gel to your hair, focusing on the back where frizz is most likely to occur. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

3. Air-Dry or Diffuse

Allow your hair to air-dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls. Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as this can cause friction and make frizz worse.

4. Touch Up

Once your hair is dry, you may need to touch up any areas that are still frizzy. Use a small amount of curl-enhancing cream or gel to smooth out any flyaways or frizz.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for taming frizzy hair:

1. Sleep on Silk

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help reduce frizz by minimizing friction between your hair and the pillowcase. You can also tie your hair up in a silk scarf before bed to help keep your curls smooth and frizz-free.

2. Don't Overwash

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and frizz. Try to wash your hair no more than once or twice a week, and use a dry shampoo in between washes to freshen up your hair.

3. Trim Regularly

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can contribute to frizz. Aim to get a trim every 8-12 weeks to keep your hair looking its best.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about frizzy hair:

Q: Can frizzy hair be fixed?

A: While you can't completely eliminate frizz, there are things you can do to reduce it. Follow the tips in this article to help tame your frizzy hair.

Q: Does humidity cause frizzy hair?

A: Yes, humidity can contribute to frizz by causing your hair to absorb moisture from the air. Using anti-humidity products and avoiding heat styling can help reduce frizz in humid conditions.

Q: Is it bad to brush curly hair?

A: Brushing curly hair can cause it to become frizzy and lose its shape. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls.

Hair Care

Taming frizzy hair can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your curls looking smooth and healthy. Remember to moisturize your hair regularly, use the right products, and avoid heat styling whenever possible. With a little patience and persistence, you can achieve the beautiful, frizz-free curls you've always wanted.
