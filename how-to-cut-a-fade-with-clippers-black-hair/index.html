How To Cut A Fade With Clippers On Black Hair

Written by Aprilia Jul 29, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of paying high prices at the barbershop every time you need a haircut? Do you want to learn how to cut a fade with clippers on black hair? This tutorial will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to achieve a flawless fade at home.

Description

A fade is a popular hairstyle that involves gradually cutting the hair shorter towards the bottom. The hair on the sides and back of the head is typically cut shorter than the hair on top, resulting in a tapered look. This style is perfect for black hair and can be achieved with clippers.

How to Cut a Fade with Clippers on Black Hair

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Scissors
  • Clipper guards (sizes 1-4)

Step-by-Step Process

Follow these steps to cut a fade with clippers on black hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it dry completely. This will make it easier to work with and ensure a more even cut.

Step 2: Determine the Length

Decide how long you want the hair on top to be and use scissors to cut it to that length. Be sure to cut it evenly and in a straight line.

Step 3: Choose a Guard Size

Select a guard size for your clippers. Start with a larger guard (size 4) and work your way down to a smaller guard (size 1) for a more gradual fade.

Step 4: Create a Guideline

Use the clippers without a guard to create a guideline around the sides and back of your head. This will help you determine where to start the fade.

Step 5: Start with the Largest Guard

Attach the largest guard (size 4) to your clippers and use it to cut the hair around the guideline. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using smooth and even strokes.

Step 6: Move to a Smaller Guard

Switch to a smaller guard (size 3) and repeat the process, cutting the hair a little higher up the head. Continue with progressively smaller guards until you reach the desired length.

Step 7: Blend the Top and Sides

Use the clippers without a guard to blend the hair on the sides and top of your head. Be sure to blend the hair evenly to achieve a seamless transition.

Step 8: Clean Up the Edges

Use scissors to trim any stray hairs around the edges of the hairline and ears.

Step 9: Style as Desired

Style your hair as desired using your favorite hair products.

Tips for Cutting a Fade with Clippers on Black Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect fade:

  • Start with a larger guard and work your way down to a smaller guard for a more gradual fade.
  • Use the clippers without a guard to blend the hair on the sides and top of your head.
  • Be patient and take your time to ensure an even cut.
  • Practice makes perfect – don't be afraid to experiment with different guard sizes and techniques.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting a fade with clippers on black hair:

Can I cut a fade with regular hair clippers?

Yes, you can use regular hair clippers to cut a fade. However, it's recommended to use clippers with multiple guard sizes for more precision.

What guard size should I use for a fade?

Start with a larger guard (size 4) and work your way down to a smaller guard (size 1) for a more gradual fade.

Do I need to wet my hair before cutting a fade?

No, you can cut a fade on dry hair. However, it's recommended to wash your hair before cutting to ensure a more even cut.

Hair Care

To maintain your fade, it's important to keep it trimmed every 2-4 weeks. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Cutting a fade with clippers on black hair is a simple process that can save you time and money. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking fade at home. Follow these steps and tips to achieve a flawless fade every time!
