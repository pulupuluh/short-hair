How To Cut Older Men s Hair For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jun 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you a beginner looking to cut an older man's hair? It may seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools and technique, you can give a great haircut. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut an older man's hair step-by-step, including tips and FAQs to help you along the way. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • The Cut
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Cutting Older Men's Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Older Men

Tools You'll Need

Before we get started, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Clippers with guard sizes 1-4
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clipper oil
  • Towel or cape
  • Hair spray (optional)

Preparation

Start by washing the hair and towel-drying it thoroughly. Next, put the towel or cape around the person's neck to protect their clothing. Comb the hair to remove any tangles and make it easier to cut.

Decide on the Length

Before you start cutting, decide on the length you want to achieve. Use the clippers with guard size 4 to cut the hair to the desired length. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up, using a sweeping motion with the clippers. Repeat this process until the hair is the desired length all around.

Blend the Sides and Back

Use the clippers with guard size 3 to blend the sides and back. Start at the bottom and work your way up, moving the clippers in an upward motion. Make sure to blend the hair evenly, using a sweeping motion.

The Cut

Use Scissors to Cut the Top

Use scissors to cut the hair on top. Start at the front and work your way to the back, using a comb to lift the hair and cut it to the desired length. Use the tip of the scissors to create texture and movement in the hair.

Blend the Top and Sides

Use scissors to blend the top and sides. Comb the hair down and use the scissors to cut the hair to the desired length, blending it with the sides. Use the tip of the scissors to create texture and movement in the hair.

Finishing Touches

After you've cut the hair, use the clippers with guard size 1 to trim the hair around the ears and neckline. Use a comb to lift the hair and trim any stray hairs.

Style the Hair

Apply hair spray (optional) and style the hair with a comb or your fingers. Make sure the person is happy with the cut before you finish.

Tips for Cutting Older Men's Hair

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting the hair too short.
  • Use a sweeping motion with the clippers and scissors to create a natural look.
  • Take your time and make small cuts to avoid mistakes.
  • Ask the person if they are happy with the cut before you finish.

FAQ

How often should older men get their hair cut?

It depends on the person's hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, older men should get their hair cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain a neat and tidy appearance.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. Use the scissors to trim the hair to the desired length and blend it with the rest of the hair. If it's a big mistake, consider taking the person to a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care for Older Men

To keep the hair healthy and looking its best, older men should:

  • Wash the hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep the hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners.
  • Eat a healthy diet with plenty of vitamins and minerals.
  • Get regular exercise and plenty of rest.

With these tips and techniques, you can give an older man a great haircut. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and ask for feedback along the way. Good luck!
