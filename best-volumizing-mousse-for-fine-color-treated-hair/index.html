Look no further than the best volumizing mousse for fine color treated hair! In this article, we will discuss the top mousse options, how to use them, and some tips for caring for your hair.

The Best Volumizing Mousses for Fine Color Treated Hair

When it comes to choosing a volumizing mousse for fine color treated hair, there are a few things to keep in mind. You want to choose a mousse that will add volume without weighing hair down or causing damage. Here are some of the best options:

Schwarzkopf OSIS+ Volume Up

This mousse provides weightless volume and long-lasting hold, perfect for fine color treated hair. It also contains a UV filter to protect color from fading.

TIGI Catwalk Root Boost Spray

This spray mousse is perfect for adding volume at the roots of your hair. It also contains conditioning agents to protect against damage.

Redken Full Frame 07 All-Over Volumizing Mousse

This mousse provides all-over volume and body without weighing hair down. It also contains a heat protectant to prevent damage from styling tools.

How to Use Volumizing Mousse on Fine Color Treated Hair

Using volumizing mousse on fine color treated hair is easy, but it's important to use it correctly to avoid damage. Here are some tips:

  • Start with damp hair
  • Shake the can well before use
  • Dispense a golf ball-sized amount of mousse into your palm
  • Distribute the mousse evenly through your hair, focusing on the roots
  • Style your hair as desired

It's important not to use too much mousse, as this can weigh hair down and cause damage. Start with a small amount and add more if necessary.

Step-by-Step Guide for Using Volumizing Mousse

Here is a step-by-step guide for using volumizing mousse on fine color treated hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a color-safe shampoo and conditioner
  2. Towel dry your hair until it is damp
  3. Shake the can of mousse well
  4. Dispense a golf ball-sized amount of mousse into your palm
  5. Flip your head upside down and apply the mousse to the roots of your hair
  6. Massage the mousse into your scalp to distribute it evenly
  7. Flip your head back up and apply mousse to the rest of your hair, focusing on the ends
  8. Blow dry your hair using a round brush, focusing on the roots to add volume
  9. Style your hair as desired
  10. Finish with a light hairspray to hold the style in place

Tips for Caring for Fine Color Treated Hair

Using volumizing mousse is just one way to add volume to fine color treated hair. Here are some other tips for caring for your hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip color and natural oils
  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner
  • Get regular trims to keep split ends at bay
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to keep hair healthy and hydrated

FAQs About Volumizing Mousse for Fine Color Treated Hair

Can volumizing mousse damage fine color treated hair?

If used correctly, volumizing mousse should not damage fine color treated hair. However, using too much or applying it incorrectly can cause damage.

How often should I use volumizing mousse?

You can use volumizing mousse as often as you like, but it's important not to use too much or it can weigh hair down.

Can I use volumizing mousse on wet hair?

Yes, you can use volumizing mousse on wet hair. In fact, it's best to apply it to damp hair for the best results.

Can volumizing mousse help my hair hold a curl?

Yes, volumizing mousse can help your hair hold a curl. Apply it to damp hair before blow drying and styling with a curling iron.

Adding volume to fine color treated hair doesn't have to be difficult. By choosing the right volumizing mousse and using it correctly, you can achieve the look you want without causing damage. Follow these tips and tricks for perfect, voluminous hair every time!
