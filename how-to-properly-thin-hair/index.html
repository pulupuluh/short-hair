How To Properly Thin Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Mar 19, 2023 · 7 min read

Thinning hair is a common problem, and many people want to know how to properly thin hair without damaging it. Thinning hair can be caused by genetics, medications, and even stress. Thinning hair can also be a result of using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on a regular basis. Fortunately, there are several ways to properly thin hair without causing any damage.

Description

Thinning hair is a process of removing hair from the scalp to reduce volume and density. This can be done by a professional stylist or at home with the right tools and techniques. Thinning hair can help manage thick and unruly hair, add texture and volume, and create a more flattering hairstyle.

How to Thin Hair Properly

Step 1: Gather the Right Tools

The first step in properly thinning hair is to gather the right tools. You will need a pair of thinning shears, a comb, and hair clips. Thinning shears are designed to cut hair at different lengths, which helps to create a layered look. It is important to invest in high-quality shears to avoid damaging the hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start thinning your hair, it is important to wash and dry it. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils. Once you have washed your hair, gently dry it with a towel or a hair dryer on low heat.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to thin your hair and ensure that you don't miss any sections. Use the comb to create a part in your hair where you want to start thinning.

Step 4: Use the Thinning Shears

Start by using the thinning shears on the ends of your hair. Hold the shears at an angle and cut the hair in a zigzag pattern. This will create a more natural look and prevent any harsh lines. Continue to thin your hair in small sections until you achieve the desired look.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

After you have thinned your hair, it is important to blend the layers. Use the shears to cut any sections that look uneven or too thick. This will create a more seamless look and ensure that your hair looks natural.

Tips for Thinning Hair

Here are some tips for thinning hair properly:

  • Start with a small section of hair and work your way up to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Use the right tools and invest in high-quality shears.
  • Don't thin your hair too often, as this can cause damage.
  • Take breaks between thinning sessions to avoid over-thinning your hair.
  • Consult with a professional stylist if you are unsure about how to properly thin your hair.

FAQs

Can thinning your hair make it grow back thicker?

No, thinning your hair will not make it grow back thicker. However, it can help manage thick and unruly hair and create a more flattering hairstyle.

How often should I thin my hair?

You should only thin your hair when necessary. Thinning your hair too often can cause damage and make it more difficult to style.

Hair Care for Thinned Hair

After you have thinned your hair, it is important to take care of it properly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils. Avoid using heat styling tools on a regular basis and use a heat protectant when you do. Trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy and avoid split ends.

Thinning your hair can be a great way to manage thick and unruly hair and create a more flattering hairstyle. By following these steps and tips, you can properly thin your hair without causing any damage.
