How To Keep Thick Hair From Getting Frizzy Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Apr 07, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizzy, unmanageable hair? If you have thick hair, you may be more prone to frizz, especially during humid weather or after using heat styling tools. Don't worry, though - with a few simple tips and tricks, you can keep your hair looking smooth and sleek. In this article, we'll go over how to keep thick hair from getting frizzy, step by step, and answer some frequently asked questions about hair care.

Table of Contents

  • Step by Step Process

How to Keep Thick Hair from Getting Frizzy

There are many things you can do to prevent frizz and keep your hair looking smooth and shiny. Here are some tips:

1. Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

Choosing the right shampoo and conditioner is key to keeping your hair healthy and hydrated. Look for products that are specifically designed for thick, frizzy hair, and avoid anything that contains sulfates or alcohol, which can strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can cause damage to your hair and make it more prone to frizz. Try to limit your use of these tools, and always use a heat protectant spray before styling.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

If you have thick, curly hair, a wide-tooth comb is your best friend. Use it to detangle your hair after washing, and avoid using a brush, which can cause breakage and frizz.

4. Apply Hair Oil

Applying a small amount of hair oil to the ends of your hair can help to smooth down frizz and add shine. Just be careful not to use too much, as this can make your hair look greasy.

5. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to prevent frizz and breakage, as it reduces friction and prevents your hair from rubbing against rough cotton fibers.

Step by Step Process

To keep your thick hair from getting frizzy, follow these steps:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Blot your hair dry with a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt.
  3. Apply a small amount of hair oil to the ends of your hair.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  5. Avoid heat styling tools, or use them sparingly and with a heat protectant spray.
  6. Sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips to keep your thick hair looking smooth and frizz-free:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can transfer oils from your scalp to your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to add extra moisture and shine.

FAQ

Q: Why is my thick hair so frizzy?

A: Thick hair is more prone to frizz because it has more layers and is more porous than fine hair. This means that it absorbs moisture from the air more easily, causing it to puff up and become frizzy.

Q: Can I use a hair dryer on my thick hair?

A: Yes, you can use a hair dryer on your thick hair, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray and a diffuser attachment to reduce frizz.

Q: How often should I wash my thick hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it every day. If you have dry hair, you can wash it every few days. Try to avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.

Hair Care Routine

Here is a sample hair care routine for thick, frizzy hair:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner every 2-3 days.
  • Blot your hair dry with a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt.
  • Apply a small amount of hair oil to the ends of your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid heat styling tools, or use them sparingly and with a heat protectant spray.
  • Sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can transfer oils from your scalp to your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to add extra moisture and shine.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your thick hair looking smooth, shiny, and frizz-free. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair type, and don't be afraid to ask your hair stylist for advice. With a little bit of effort, you can achieve the beautiful, healthy hair you've always wanted.
