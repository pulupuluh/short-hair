How To Cut A Fade By Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 13, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to give yourself a fresh haircut, learning how to cut a fade by yourself is a great skill to have. A fade haircut involves gradually tapering the hair from longer on top to shorter on the sides and back. This style can be achieved with clippers and a steady hand, making it a great DIY option for those who don't want to visit a barber. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting a fade by yourself, along with tips and FAQ to make the process easier.

How to Cut a Fade by Yourself

Step 1: Choose the Right Clippers

To cut a fade, you'll need clippers with different guard sizes. You'll want to have at least three guards: one for the top of your head, one for the sides, and one for the back. Some clippers come with interchangeable guards, while others require you to purchase them separately. Make sure to choose clippers that feel comfortable in your hand and have a good battery life.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Before you start cutting, decide on the length you want for each section of your hair. You can use a mirror to see the back of your head and get an idea of how short you want the fade to be. You can also use a comb to lift the hair on top of your head and trim it to your desired length.

Step 3: Start with the Top

Begin by using the longest guard to trim the hair on top of your head. Start at the front and work your way to the back, using a comb to lift the hair and ensure an even cut. If you want a shorter top, switch to a shorter guard and repeat the process.

Step 4: Move to the Sides

Next, switch to a shorter guard and trim the hair on the sides of your head. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the comb to lift the hair and ensure an even cut. Blend the length from the top to the sides by using a guard in between the two lengths.

Step 5: Fade the Hair

To create a fade, switch to an even shorter guard and trim the hair around the edges of the sides and back of your head. Use a sweeping motion to blend the hair from the shorter length to the longer length on top. Repeat this process with progressively shorter guards until you achieve the desired fade.

Step 6: Clean up the Edges

Once you've achieved the desired fade, use a trimmer to clean up the edges around your ears and the back of your neck. You can also use scissors to trim any longer hairs on top that you may have missed.

Tips

  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress and make sure the fade is even.
  • Don't forget to clean your clippers and trimmer after use.

FAQ

How long does a fade haircut last?

A fade haircut typically lasts around two to three weeks before the hair starts to grow back and lose its shape.

Can you cut a fade with scissors?

While it's possible to cut a fade with scissors, it's much easier and more precise to use clippers.

Can you fix a bad fade?

If you've made a mistake while cutting your own fade, it's best to visit a professional barber to fix it. Trying to fix it yourself may result in further mistakes and a worse haircut.

Hair Care

To maintain your fade, it's important to keep your hair clean and hydrated. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the natural oils from your hair. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized. Avoid over-styling your hair with heat tools and chemicals, as this can damage your hair and undo the fade.

Cutting a fade by yourself can be a little intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it's a great way to save money and give yourself a fresh look. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
