How To Cut Short Hair At Home Pixie A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 06, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune to get your hair cut at a salon? Or maybe you just want to try something new and daring for your short hair? Whatever your reason may be, cutting your own hair at home can seem like a daunting task, especially if you have a pixie cut. But fear not, with this step-by-step guide, you'll be able to cut your own hair at home and rock that pixie look in no time!

Table of Content

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

A pixie cut is a short hairstyle that is typically cut close to the scalp on the sides and back and longer on top. It's a bold and daring look that can be both edgy and feminine. However, getting a pixie cut at a salon can be expensive, and maintaining it can be time-consuming. That's why cutting your own hair at home can be a great option for those who want to save money and time.

How To

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have some hair clips to help section off your hair.

Step-by-Step

Follow these steps to cut your own hair at home:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. It's easier to cut clean, dry hair.
  2. Section off your hair. Use hair clips to separate the top section of your hair from the sides and back.
  3. Start with the sides. Use your comb to lift a small section of hair on one side of your head. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to your desired length. Repeat on the other side.
  4. Move to the back. Use your comb to lift a small section of hair in the middle of the back of your head. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to your desired length. Work your way down the back of your head in small sections.
  5. Trim the top. Use your comb to lift a small section of hair at the front of your head. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it to your desired length. Work your way back towards the crown of your head in small sections.
  6. Blend the top and sides. Use your comb to lift a section of hair at the top of your head. Comb it towards the side and blend it with the hair on the side. Repeat on the other side.
  7. Check for symmetry. Use your mirror to check that both sides are even.
  8. Trim any uneven ends. Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven ends.
  9. Style as desired. Use a styling product to add texture or volume to your hair.

Tips

Cutting your own hair can be tricky, but these tips can help:

  • Start with small cuts. It's easier to take more off than to put it back on.
  • Use your mirror to check your progress. It's important to see what you're doing from all angles.
  • Cut in a well-lit area. Good lighting can make a big difference in how your hair turns out.
  • If you're unsure, ask for help. It's always better to get a second opinion, especially if it's your first time cutting your own hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair at home:

Is it hard to cut your own hair?

Cutting your own hair can be challenging, especially if you have a more complex hairstyle. However, with practice and patience, it can be done.

What kind of scissors should I use to cut my hair?

You should use hair cutting scissors, which are sharper and more precise than regular scissors. Do not use kitchen scissors or other household scissors.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, you should trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your style.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are suited for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. Regular trims can also help keep your hair looking its best.

Now that you know how to cut short hair at home pixie, you can save time and money by doing it yourself. With the right tools and a little bit of practice, you can achieve a bold and daring look that's all your own.
