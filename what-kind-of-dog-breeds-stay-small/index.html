What Kind Of Dog Breeds Stay Small A Guide For Pet Lovers

Written by Eveline Jun 02, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you looking for a furry friend that won't take up too much space in your home? Do you want a dog that's easy to handle and won't require too much physical activity? If yes, then you're in the right place! In this article, we'll discuss what kind of dog breeds stay small and are perfect for apartment living or small homes. We'll also provide tips on how to take care of them and answer some frequently asked questions about these breeds.

Table of Contents

  • Why Choose Small Dog Breeds?
  • How to Choose the Right Small Dog Breed?
  • Top 5 Small Dog Breeds That Stay Small
  • Tips for Taking Care of Small Dog Breeds

Why Choose Small Dog Breeds?

Small dog breeds are popular for several reasons. Firstly, they don't take up too much space in your home, making them ideal for apartment living or small homes. Secondly, they're easy to handle and don't require too much physical activity, which is perfect for people who don't have a lot of time to spare for long walks or playtime. Lastly, they're adorable and make great companions!

How to Choose the Right Small Dog Breed?

Choosing the right small dog breed can be a difficult task, but it's important to take your time and do your research. Consider your lifestyle and living arrangements, as well as your family's needs and preferences. Take into account the breed's temperament, energy level, and grooming requirements. Some small dog breeds are more high-maintenance than others, so make sure you choose one that suits your lifestyle and schedule.

Factors to Consider When Choosing a Small Dog Breed:

  • Temperament
  • Energy Level
  • Grooming Requirements
  • Size
  • Trainability
  • Health Concerns

Top 5 Small Dog Breeds That Stay Small

Here are our top picks for small dog breeds that stay small:

1. Chihuahua

The Chihuahua is one of the smallest dog breeds in the world, weighing only 2-6 pounds. They're energetic and affectionate, but can be stubborn at times. They're great for apartment living and don't require too much exercise.

2. Pomeranian

The Pomeranian is a fluffy ball of cuteness that weighs only 3-7 pounds. They're playful and friendly, but can be vocal at times. They're great for families with children and don't require too much exercise.

3. Yorkshire Terrier

The Yorkshire Terrier, or "Yorkie", is a small dog breed that weighs only 4-7 pounds. They're energetic and playful, but can be stubborn at times. They're great for apartment living and are very loyal to their owners.

4. Shih Tzu

The Shih Tzu is a small dog breed that weighs only 9-16 pounds. They're affectionate and friendly, but can be stubborn at times. They're great for families with children and don't require too much exercise.

5. French Bulldog

The French Bulldog is a small dog breed that weighs only 16-28 pounds. They're affectionate and playful, but can be stubborn at times. They're great for apartment living and don't require too much exercise.

Tips for Taking Care of Small Dog Breeds

Small dog breeds require special care and attention to ensure they stay healthy and happy. Here are some tips for taking care of small dog breeds:

  • Provide regular exercise and playtime
  • Feed them a healthy diet
  • Take them for regular check-ups at the vet
  • Brush their teeth and groom them regularly
  • Provide them with a comfortable and safe living environment
  • Train and socialize them from a young age

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Are small dog breeds good for families with children?

A: Yes, small dog breeds can be great companions for families with children. However, it's important to supervise children around dogs and teach them how to interact with them properly.

Q: Do small dog breeds shed a lot?

A: It depends on the breed. Some small dog breeds, such as the Pomeranian, shed a lot, while others, such as the Shih Tzu, don't shed much at all.

Q: Are small dog breeds easy to train?

A: It depends on the breed. Some small dog breeds, such as the Chihuahua, can be stubborn and difficult to train, while others, such as the Yorkshire Terrier, are more eager to please and easier to train.

Hair Care for Small Dog Breeds

Small dog breeds require regular grooming to keep their coats clean and healthy. Here are some tips for hair care for small dog breeds:

  • Brush their coat regularly to prevent matting and tangling
  • Trim their nails regularly to prevent overgrowth and discomfort
  • Clean their ears and eyes regularly to prevent infections
  • Bathe them when necessary, but don't overdo it as it can strip their coat of natural oils
  • Visit a professional groomer regularly for a trim and tidy-up

Small dog breeds are perfect for people who want a furry companion that won't take up too much space in their home. They're easy to handle and make great companions. Just make sure you choose the right breed for your lifestyle and take good care of them to ensure they stay healthy and happy.
