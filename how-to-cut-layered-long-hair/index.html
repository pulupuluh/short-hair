How To Cut Layered Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi May 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have long hair and are looking for a change, adding layers can give your hair more movement and dimension. However, cutting layered long hair can be tricky if you don't know what you're doing. In this article, we'll go over how to cut layered long hair, including tips and hair care advice.

Table of Contents:

  1. How to Cut Layered Long Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Layered Long Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for Layered Long Hair

How to Cut Layered Long Hair

Cutting layered long hair requires some skill and practice. It's best to start with clean, dry hair and use sharp scissors. You can cut your hair yourself or have a friend or professional do it for you. Follow these steps for a beautiful layered haircut:

1. Section Your Hair

Use a comb to create a middle part and divide your hair into two sections. Then, create a horizontal part about an inch above the bottom of your hair and clip the top section away. This will be the first layer you cut.

2. Determine the Length of Your Layers

Decide how long you want your layers to be. For longer layers, cut less hair; for shorter layers, cut more hair. Use the first layer you cut as a guide for the rest of your hair.

3. Cut the First Layer

Hold the first layer of hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. You can use a comb to guide your scissors and ensure a straight cut. Be sure to keep your fingers parallel to the floor to maintain the length you want.

4. Cut the Rest of Your Layers

Move up to the next layer of hair and repeat the process, using the first layer as a guide. Continue cutting layers until you reach the top section of your hair.

5. Blend Your Layers

After you've cut all your layers, use your scissors to blend the ends of your hair. This will create a soft, natural look and prevent any harsh lines.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for cutting layered long hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into two sections with a middle part.
  3. Create a horizontal part about an inch above the bottom of your hair and clip the top section away.
  4. Cut the first layer of hair straight across, using a comb to guide your scissors.
  5. Use the first layer as a guide to cut the rest of your layers.
  6. Blend the ends of your hair with your scissors to create a natural look.

Tips for Cutting Layered Long Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting layered long hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with clean, dry hair to prevent any surprises.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, especially if you're new to cutting hair.
  • Be patient and take your time. It's better to cut a little at a time than to cut too much.
  • Use a comb to guide your scissors and ensure a straight cut.

FAQs

Can I cut my own layered long hair?

Yes, you can cut your own layered long hair. However, it's best to start with small cuts and work your way up to the length you want.

How do I know how much hair to cut for each layer?

Decide on the length you want for your layers and cut a little bit at a time. Use the first layer as a guide for the rest of your hair.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim your hair a little more or get it fixed by a professional.

Hair Care for Layered Long Hair

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some hair care tips for layered long hair:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking fresh.

Cutting layered long hair can be a fun and easy way to switch up your look. Follow these tips and steps for a beautiful, layered haircut.
