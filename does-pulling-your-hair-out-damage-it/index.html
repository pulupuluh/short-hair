Does Pulling Your Hair Out Damage It A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Jul 21, 2023 · 7 min read

If you are someone who suffers from trichotillomania, or the compulsive urge to pull out your hair, you may be wondering whether this habit is causing any damage to your precious locks. The short answer is yes, pulling your hair out can cause damage, but the extent of the damage depends on various factors. In this post, we will explore the impact of hair pulling on your hair health and provide you with some tips on how to prevent and manage the damage.

How Does Pulling Your Hair Out Damage It?

Hair pulling can cause physical damage to your scalp and hair follicles, leading to hair loss, thinning, and breakage. When you pull your hair out, you are putting excessive tension on the hair shaft, which can weaken it over time. If you repeatedly pull out your hair from the same spot, you may also cause scarring on your scalp, which can prevent hair from growing back in that area.

How Can You Prevent Hair Damage from Pulling?

The best way to prevent hair damage from pulling is to stop the habit altogether. However, this can be easier said than done, especially for those with trichotillomania. Here are some tips that can help:

1. Seek Professional Help

If you are struggling with trichotillomania, it is essential to seek professional help from a therapist or counselor. They can help you identify the triggers that lead to hair pulling and provide you with coping mechanisms to manage the urge.

2. Wear Protective Styles

Wearing protective styles such as braids, weaves, or wigs can help reduce the temptation to pull your hair out. These styles can also help to protect your natural hair from further damage.

3. Keep Your Hands Busy

Keeping your hands busy with other activities such as knitting, drawing, or playing an instrument can help distract you from the urge to pull out your hair.

How Can You Manage Hair Damage from Pulling?

If you have already caused damage to your hair from pulling, here are some tips on how to manage it:

1. Trim Your Hair

If your hair is severely damaged, you may need to trim it to remove the split ends and prevent further breakage. A professional hair stylist can help you determine the right length and style for your hair type and face shape.

2. Use Hair Growth Products

Using hair growth products such as biotin supplements, scalp treatments, and hair masks can help stimulate hair growth and improve the overall health of your hair.

3. Practice Gentle Hair Care

When dealing with damaged hair, it is crucial to practice gentle hair care. This includes using a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, avoiding heat styling tools, and using a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.

FAQs

Q. Can hair pulling cause permanent hair loss?

A. Yes, hair pulling can cause permanent hair loss if it leads to scarring on the scalp.

Q. Is trichotillomania curable?

A. While there is no cure for trichotillomania, it can be managed with professional help and self-care practices.

Q. Can stress cause hair pulling?

A. Yes, stress and anxiety can trigger the urge to pull out hair in some people.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips that can help prevent hair damage:

1. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers on high heat as they can cause heat damage to your hair. If you must use these tools, use a heat protectant spray and use them on low heat.

2. Maintain a Healthy Diet

Eating a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals such as biotin, vitamin C, and iron can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair damage.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid wearing tight hairstyles such as high ponytails and braids as they can cause tension on your hairline and lead to hair loss.

Overall, pulling your hair out can cause damage to your hair and scalp, but there are ways to prevent and manage the damage. Seek professional help if you are struggling with trichotillomania, and practice gentle hair care to maintain healthy hair.
