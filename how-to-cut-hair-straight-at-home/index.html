How To Cut Hair Straight At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Apr 16, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve a straight and even cut at home. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting hair straight at home, from preparing your hair to finishing touches. By the end, you'll have the confidence to tackle your own haircuts and save money on salon visits.

  • Tools You'll Need

  • Cutting Your Hair

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to prepare it properly. Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it straight. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting, as wet hair can stretch and lead to uneven cuts.

Next, choose a well-lit area to cut your hair in, preferably near a window or bright light source. You'll also want to wear a shirt that you don't mind getting hair on, and lay down a towel or sheet to catch any stray hairs.

Tools You'll Need

To cut your hair straight at home, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Bobby pins or hair ties
  • Mirror (preferably a large one and a handheld one)
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Sectioning Your Hair

Once you have your tools ready, it's time to section your hair. Start by parting your hair down the middle, all the way from your forehead to the back of your neck. Then, use the comb to separate one side of your hair from the other.

Clip the other side out of the way with bobby pins or a hair tie, and then divide the section you're working on into two parts - top and bottom. Use the comb to create a horizontal parting at the back of your head, separating the top section from the bottom section. Clip the top section out of the way for now.

Cutting Your Hair

With the bottom section of your hair loose, use the comb to straighten out any tangles or knots. Then, take a small section of hair from the back and hold it between your fingers, with the tips of your fingers pointing towards the floor.

Using your scissors, cut the hair straight across, making sure to use the tips of the scissors and not the entire length. This will give you more control and precision as you cut. Cut a little bit at a time, and keep checking your work in the mirror to make sure you're cutting straight.

Once you're happy with the length of the back section, move on to the sides. Take a small section of hair from the side and hold it between your fingers, with the tips of your fingers pointing towards the back of your head. Cut the hair straight across, following the length of the back section as a guide. Repeat on the other side.

Blending Your Hair

Now that you've cut the back and sides of your hair, it's time to blend them together. Unclip the top section of your hair and let it down. Use the comb to create a horizontal parting, separating the top section from the bottom section.

Take a small section of hair from the top section and hold it between your fingers, with the tips of your fingers pointing towards the floor. Use the scissors to cut the hair straight across, blending it with the bottom section. Repeat this process, working your way around your head until all the hair is blended together.

Finishing Touches

Once you've blended your hair together, it's time to do some finishing touches. Check your work in the mirror and trim any stray hairs or uneven sections. You may also want to add layers or texture to your hair, depending on your desired style.

When you're happy with your haircut, give your hair a final comb through and style it as desired. You can use a straightening iron or blow dryer to achieve a sleek, straight look, or let your hair air dry for a more natural style.

Tips for Cutting Hair at Home

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut a little bit at a time, checking your work in the mirror as you go.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair or creating uneven cuts.
  • Keep your hair tension even as you cut, using your fingers to hold the hair taut.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can stretch and lead to uneven cuts.
  • Be patient with yourself - cutting hair takes practice, and it's okay to make mistakes.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my hair straight at home if it's curly?

A: Yes, you can cut curly hair straight at home, but it may require more practice and patience. Make sure to straighten your hair before cutting, and consider using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair without disrupting the curls.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and desired length, but most people should get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain their style and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Consider getting regular trims to maintain your style and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your hair straight at home and achieve a salon-quality look without the cost. Happy cutting!
