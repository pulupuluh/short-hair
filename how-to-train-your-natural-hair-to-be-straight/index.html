How To Train Your Natural Hair To Be Straight

Written by Oliver May 11, 2023 · 9 min read

In this article, we will discuss how to train your natural hair to be straight. This guide is designed to help you achieve the straight hair look you desire without damaging your hair. We will cover everything from how to prepare your hair for straightening, to how to maintain your straightened hair. So, let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  2. Straightening Your Hair
  3. Maintaining Your Straightened Hair
  4. Tips for Straightening Natural Hair
  5. Frequently Asked Questions

Preparing Your Hair for Straightening

Before you start straightening your hair, it's important to prepare it properly. This will help to minimize damage and ensure that your hair remains healthy and strong.

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This will help to remove any product buildup or residue that may be present in your hair. Rinse your hair thoroughly and then apply a deep conditioner to help nourish and moisturize your hair.

Step 2: Dry Your Hair

Next, dry your hair using a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt. Avoid using a regular towel as this can cause frizz and damage to your hair. Once your hair is dry, apply a heat protectant spray or serum to help protect your hair from heat damage.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair

Using a wide-toothed comb, gently detangle your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots. This will help to prevent breakage and damage to your hair.

Straightening Your Hair

Now that you have prepared your hair, it's time to start straightening it. Follow these steps to achieve the straight hair look you desire.

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into small sections. This will help to ensure that you straighten every strand of hair. Clip each section up using hair clips.

Step 2: Use a Flat Iron

Using a high-quality flat iron, start straightening each section of hair. Start at the roots and slowly work your way down to the ends. Be sure to use a comb to help guide the hair through the flat iron. Repeat this process on each section of hair until your entire head is straightened.

Step 3: Finish with Hair Serum

Once you have straightened your hair, apply a light hair serum to help reduce frizz and add shine. Be sure to only use a small amount of serum as too much can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Maintaining Your Straightened Hair

Now that you have achieved the straight hair look you desire, it's important to maintain it properly. Here are some tips to help you maintain your straightened hair.

Tip 1: Avoid Getting Your Hair Wet

Avoid getting your hair wet for at least 48 hours after straightening it. This will help to ensure that your hair remains straight and smooth.

Tip 2: Use a Satin Pillowcase

Using a satin pillowcase can help to reduce frizz and prevent your hair from becoming tangled while you sleep.

Tip 3: Touch Up Your Hair

As your hair grows, you may need to touch up your straightened hair. Be sure to only touch up the roots and avoid straightening the ends of your hair as this can cause damage.

Tips for Straightening Natural Hair

Straightening natural hair can be challenging, but with the right tips, you can achieve the straight hair look you desire without damaging your hair. Here are some tips to help you straighten your natural hair.

Tip 1: Use a Heat Protectant

Using a heat protectant is essential when straightening natural hair. This will help to minimize heat damage and ensure that your hair remains healthy and strong.

Tip 2: Don't Use High Heat

Avoid using high heat when straightening your natural hair. Use a lower heat setting to reduce the risk of heat damage.

Tip 3: Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

Using a wide-toothed comb can help to prevent breakage and damage to your hair when detangling it before straightening.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about straightening natural hair.

Q: Can I straighten my natural hair without using heat?

A: Yes, you can use other methods such as wrapping your hair or using rollers to achieve a straight hair look without using heat.

Q: How often should I straighten my natural hair?

A: It's best to avoid straightening your natural hair too often. Aim to straighten your hair no more than once a month to avoid damage.

Q: How can I prevent heat damage when straightening my natural hair?

A: Use a heat protectant, avoid using high heat, and be sure to only straighten your hair when it's completely dry.

Hair Care for Straightened Hair

Proper hair care is essential to maintain healthy, strong, and straightened hair. Here are some tips to help you care for your straightened hair.

Tip 1: Moisturize Your Hair

Be sure to moisturize your hair regularly to prevent it from becoming dry and brittle. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help nourish and moisturize your hair.

Tip 2: Avoid Using Harsh Products

Avoid using harsh products such as sulfates and alcohol as they can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage.

Tip 3: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can help to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and strong.

By following these tips, you can train your natural hair to be straight without damaging it. Remember to prepare your hair properly, use a high-quality flat iron, and maintain your straightened hair properly to keep it looking healthy and strong.
