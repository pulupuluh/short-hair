Why Does the Back of My Skull Stick Out?

If you've ever looked in the mirror and wondered why the back of your skull seems to stick out, you're not alone. Many people notice this about their head shape, and it can be a source of self-consciousness or worry. In this article, we'll explore some possible reasons why the back of your skull might stick out and what you can do about it.

What Causes the Back of the Skull to Stick Out?

There are a few different factors that can contribute to the appearance of a protruding skull at the back of your head. Here are some of the most common:

Genetics

One of the most significant factors that determine the shape of your skull is your genetics. Some people are simply born with a head shape that includes a more prominent occipital bone, which is the bone at the back of the skull. If this is the case for you, there may not be much you can do to change it.

Posture

Poor posture can also contribute to the appearance of a protruding skull at the back of your head. If you spend a lot of time hunched over a desk or looking down at your phone, you may be putting extra pressure on the back of your skull. Over time, this can cause the bone to shift and become more prominent.

Medical Conditions

In some cases, a protruding skull at the back of your head may be a symptom of an underlying medical condition. For example, some people with scoliosis (a curvature of the spine) may also experience changes to their head shape. If you're concerned that your head shape may be related to a medical issue, it's important to talk to your doctor.

How Can You Address a Protruding Skull?

If you're unhappy with the appearance of a protruding skull at the back of your head, there are a few things you can do to address it:

Improve Your Posture

One of the most straightforward ways to reduce the appearance of a protruding skull at the back of your head is to improve your posture. Be mindful of how you're sitting and standing throughout the day, and try to keep your shoulders back and your head held high. This can help redistribute the pressure on your skull and encourage it to settle into a more natural shape.

Try Hair Styling Tricks

Another way to address a protruding skull is to use hair styling tricks to create the illusion of a more balanced head shape. For example, a shorter haircut or a style with more volume on top can help balance out the appearance of a protruding back of the head.

Consider Cosmetic Procedures

In some cases, people may choose to pursue cosmetic procedures to address a protruding skull. For example, a skilled plastic surgeon may be able to modify the shape of the skull through procedures like bone shaving or augmentation.

FAQ

Is a protruding skull at the back of the head dangerous?

No, a protruding skull at the back of the head is not typically dangerous. However, if you're concerned about changes to your head shape, it's always a good idea to talk to your doctor to rule out any underlying medical issues.

Can improving my posture really help reduce the appearance of a protruding skull?

Yes, improving your posture can make a big difference in the appearance of a protruding skull. By redistributing the pressure on your skull, you can encourage it to settle into a more natural shape over time.

Hair Care Tips for a Protruding Skull

If you're using hair styling tricks to address a protruding skull at the back of your head, it's important to take good care of your hair to ensure it looks its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add volume to your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to create more lift at the roots.
  • Avoid heavy, greasy products that can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.
  • Try using a dry shampoo to add texture and volume to your hair between washes.

By following these tips and tricks, you can help reduce the appearance of a protruding skull and feel more confident about your head shape.
