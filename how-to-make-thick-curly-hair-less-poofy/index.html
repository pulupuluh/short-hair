How To Make Thick Curly Hair Less Poofy Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia May 12, 2023 · 8 min read

Thick curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it's beautiful and voluminous, it can also be prone to frizz and poofiness. If you're tired of your curls taking on a life of their own, don't worry – there are ways to tame the poof and keep your curls looking gorgeous. In this post, we'll cover some tips and tricks for making thick curly hair less poofy.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make Thick Curly Hair Less Poofy
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Managing Poofy Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

How to Make Thick Curly Hair Less Poofy

The key to managing poofy hair is to keep it hydrated and nourished. Curly hair tends to be dryer than straight hair, so it's important to use moisturizing products and avoid heat styling whenever possible. Here are some steps to follow:

1. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner

Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair and contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and glycerin. These ingredients will help hydrate your hair and keep it from becoming too dry and frizzy.

2. Apply a leave-in conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help lock in moisture. This will help prevent your hair from becoming too dry and frizzy.

3. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and cause it to become even more poofy. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

4. Use a diffuser

A diffuser attachment for your hair dryer can help distribute heat more evenly and prevent your curls from becoming too poofy. Use a low heat setting and gently scrunch your hair with the diffuser.

5. Embrace the frizz

Finally, remember that some frizz is inevitable with curly hair. Don't stress too much about it – a little bit of frizz can actually add to the overall look of your curls.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for making your thick curly hair less poofy:

  1. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to damp hair.
  3. Avoid heat styling whenever possible. If you must use heat, use a low temperature and a heat protectant spray.
  4. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair. Use a low heat setting and gently scrunch your hair with the diffuser.
  5. Embrace the frizz – a little bit of frizz can add to the overall look of your curls.

Tips for Managing Poofy Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing poofy hair:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz while you sleep.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry – this can cause it to become even more poofy. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's damp.
  • Consider getting a haircut that's specifically designed for curly hair. A good haircut can help manage poofiness and keep your curls looking healthy and defined.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about managing poofy hair:

What causes poofy hair?

Poofy hair can be caused by a number of factors, including dryness, humidity, and damage from heat styling.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and how oily your scalp is. Some people with curly hair can get away with washing their hair once a week, while others need to wash it more frequently. Experiment to find a washing schedule that works for you.

Can I use oil to manage poofy hair?

Yes, oil can be a great way to hydrate your hair and prevent frizz. Look for lightweight oils like argan oil or jojoba oil, and apply a small amount to your hair after washing it.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for managing curly hair:

  • Avoid using sulfates in your shampoo, as they can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use an old t-shirt or a microfiber towel to gently squeeze excess water out of your hair.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause it to become more poofy.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to help hydrate your hair and keep it healthy.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your thick curly hair less poofy and more manageable. Remember to be patient – it may take some trial and error to find the right products and techniques that work for your hair type. But with a little bit of effort, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls that you'll love.
