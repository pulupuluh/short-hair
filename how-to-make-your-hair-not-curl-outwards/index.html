How To Make Your Hair Not Curl Outwards A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Mar 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you know that it can be a challenge to keep your curls under control. One of the most frustrating things about curly hair is when it curls outwards, giving you a triangular shape that you definitely didn't ask for. In this article, we'll explore how to make your hair not curl outwards, with step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice to help you achieve your desired look.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Make Your Hair Not Curl Outwards
  2. Step-by-Step Instructions
  3. Tips for Managing Outward Curls
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care Advice

How to Make Your Hair Not Curl Outwards

The first step in dealing with outward curls is to understand what causes them. Most often, outward curls are the result of hair that is too dry or damaged. When the hair cuticle is damaged, it can't hold moisture properly, which can cause the hair to curl outward. Here are some steps you can take to prevent this from happening:

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

One of the best things you can do for your hair is to use a deep conditioning treatment on a regular basis. This will help to restore moisture to your hair and prevent it from becoming dry and damaged. Look for a treatment that is specifically designed for curly hair, as these products will be formulated to provide extra moisture and nourishment.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can be very damaging to curly hair, as it can cause the hair to become dry and brittle. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage. Otherwise, try to avoid heat styling altogether and let your hair air dry instead.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When you're combing your hair, be sure to use a wide-tooth comb. This will help to minimize damage and prevent tangles. Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up, gently detangling as you go.

4. Avoid Brushing Your Hair

Brushing curly hair can cause it to frizz and become even more difficult to manage. Instead, use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Rinse thoroughly and gently squeeze out any excess water.

2. Apply a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the treatment in your hair for the recommended amount of time, then rinse thoroughly.

3. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help keep it moisturized throughout the day.

4. Style Your Hair

To prevent outward curls, it's best to let your hair air dry. However, if you need to style your hair, use a diffuser on your blow dryer to minimize damage.

Tips for Managing Outward Curls

Here are some additional tips to help you manage outward curls:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to minimize frizz
  • Avoid using hair ties with metal, as they can cause breakage
  • Try sleeping with your hair in a loose bun or braid to prevent tangles
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz

FAQ

Q: How often should I deep condition my hair?

A: It's best to deep condition your hair once a week, or as needed based on your hair's condition.

Q: Should I avoid using shampoo altogether?

A: No, it's important to use shampoo to cleanse your hair and scalp. Just be sure to use a moisturizing shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

Hair Care Advice

In addition to the tips above, it's important to take good care of your hair on a daily basis. This includes eating a healthy diet, drinking plenty of water, and avoiding harsh chemicals and styling products. Be gentle with your hair, and try not to over-manipulate it. With the right care and attention, you can help prevent outward curls and achieve the beautiful, healthy hair you deserve.
