Top Fade Haircut The Ultimate Guide

Written by Robby May 07, 2023 Â· 8 min read

The top fade haircut has become a popular hairstyle in recent years, and it's not hard to see why. This versatile haircut works well for all hair types and can be styled in a variety of ways. Whether you're looking for a low fade or a high fade, this guide will provide you with all the information you need to achieve the perfect top fade haircut.

What is a Top Fade Haircut?

A top fade haircut is a hairstyle that involves cutting the sides and back of the head very short, while leaving the hair on top longer. The hair on top can be styled in different ways, such as slicked back, messy, or textured. The fade can be low, medium, or high, depending on the preference of the person getting the haircut.

How to Get a Top Fade Haircut

Getting a top fade haircut is easy if you follow these steps:

Step 1: Choose the Type of Fade

The first step in getting a top fade haircut is to decide on the type of fade you want. A low fade starts near the ears and gradually fades into the hairline, while a high fade starts at the top of the head and fades down to the sides and back. A medium fade is somewhere in between.

Step 2: Decide on the Length of the Hair on Top

The next step is to decide on the length of the hair on top. This will depend on your personal preference and the style you want to achieve. If you want a slicked-back look, you'll need to leave more length on top, while a messy or textured style will require less length.

Step 3: Cut the Hair on the Sides and Back

Once you've decided on the type of fade and the length of the hair on top, it's time to start cutting. Use clippers to cut the hair on the sides and back of the head, starting with the longest guard and gradually working your way down to the shortest guard.

Step 4: Blend the Fade

After you've cut the hair on the sides and back, it's time to blend the fade. Use a comb and scissors to blend the hair around the ears and the hairline, making sure there are no harsh lines.

Step 5: Style the Hair on Top

Finally, it's time to style the hair on top. Use a comb, brush, or your fingers to create the desired style, whether it's slicked back, messy, or textured.

Tips for Maintaining a Top Fade Haircut

Here are some tips for maintaining a top fade haircut:

  • Visit a barber or stylist every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape of the fade
  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair healthy
  • Apply a styling product, such as pomade or wax, to keep the hair in place
  • Brush or comb the hair regularly to prevent tangles

FAQ

How long does a top fade haircut last?

A top fade haircut can last anywhere from 2-6 weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain the shape of the fade.

Can anyone get a top fade haircut?

Yes, anyone can get a top fade haircut, regardless of hair type or texture. However, it's important to consult with a barber or stylist to determine the best type of fade for your hair.

Is a top fade haircut easy to maintain?

Yes, a top fade haircut is relatively easy to maintain, as long as you visit a barber or stylist regularly and use the right hair care products.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important, especially if you want to maintain a top fade haircut. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and looking great:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a high-quality shampoo and conditioner
  • Use a styling product, such as pomade or wax, to keep the hair in place
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage the hair
  • Trim the ends of your hair regularly to prevent split ends

With the right care and maintenance, a top fade haircut can be a stylish and versatile hairstyle that works for any occasion. Whether you're going for a classic look or something more modern, the top fade haircut is a great choice.
