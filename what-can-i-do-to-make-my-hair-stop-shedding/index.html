What Can I Do To Make My Hair Stop Shedding

Are you experiencing excessive hair shedding? Don't worry, you're not alone. Hair shedding is a common problem that affects both men and women. In this article, we'll discuss what causes hair shedding and what you can do to stop it.

Table of Contents

  • Causes of Hair Shedding
  • Hair Care Tips to Stop Hair Shedding
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Causes of Hair Shedding

Hair shedding can be caused by various factors such as hormonal changes, genetics, stress, poor nutrition, and certain medical conditions. It's important to identify the cause of your hair shedding so you can take the necessary steps to stop it.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes such as pregnancy, menopause, and thyroid disorders can cause hair shedding. These hormonal changes can disrupt the normal hair growth cycle, resulting in more hair shedding than usual.

Genetics

Genetics also play a role in hair shedding. If your parents or grandparents have a history of hair loss, you're more likely to experience excessive hair shedding as well.

Stress

Stress can cause hair shedding by disrupting the normal hair growth cycle. When you're under stress, your body produces more androgens, which can cause hair follicles to shrink and eventually fall out.

Poor Nutrition

Your hair needs proper nutrition to grow and stay healthy. A diet that's deficient in vitamins and minerals can cause hair shedding. Make sure to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, and protein.

Medical Conditions

Certain medical conditions such as alopecia areata, scalp infections, and autoimmune diseases can cause hair shedding. If you suspect that a medical condition is causing your hair shedding, consult with your doctor for proper diagnosis and treatment.

Hair Care Tips to Stop Hair Shedding

Now that you know what causes hair shedding, let's discuss what you can do to stop it. Here are some hair care tips that can help reduce hair shedding:

Use a Gentle Shampoo

Harsh shampoos can strip your hair of its natural oils, causing it to become dry and brittle. Use a gentle shampoo that's free of sulfates and parabens to keep your hair healthy and strong.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and cause it to break and fall out. If you must use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Don't Brush Your Hair When Wet

Your hair is most vulnerable to damage when it's wet. Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet as this can cause it to break and fall out. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can improve blood flow to your hair follicles, promoting healthy hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp for a few minutes every day.

Take Supplements

Supplements such as biotin, vitamin D, and iron can help promote healthy hair growth. Consult with your doctor before taking any supplements to make sure they're safe for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much hair shedding is normal?

It's normal to shed between 50 to 100 hairs per day. If you're shedding more than that, it may be a sign of excessive hair shedding.

Can stress cause hair shedding?

Yes, stress can cause hair shedding by disrupting the normal hair growth cycle.

Can hair shedding be reversed?

Yes, hair shedding can be reversed by identifying the cause and taking the necessary steps to stop it.

How long does it take to see results?

It may take several weeks or months to see results. Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.


By following these hair care tips and identifying the cause of your hair shedding, you can stop excessive hair shedding and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine. If you continue to experience excessive hair shedding, consult with your doctor for proper diagnosis and treatment.
