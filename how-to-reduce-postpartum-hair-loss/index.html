Reducing Postpartum Hair Loss Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Apr 19, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Postpartum hair loss is a common occurrence in new mothers that can be both frustrating and worrying. It is important to understand that this is a natural process and is caused by hormonal changes in the body. However, there are ways to reduce the severity of hair loss and help your hair recover faster. Here are some tips and tricks to help you reduce postpartum hair loss:

How to Reduce Postpartum Hair Loss

Step 1: Maintain a Healthy Diet

Eating a well-balanced diet is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure to include foods that are rich in vitamins and minerals such as biotin, iron, zinc, and vitamin C. These nutrients help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. Foods such as eggs, spinach, salmon, and nuts are great additions to your diet.

Step 2: Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water is not only good for your overall health but also for hair growth. Staying hydrated helps to nourish hair follicles and promote healthy hair growth. Aim to drink at least 8 glasses of water a day.

Step 3: Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools such as hair straighteners and curling irons can damage your hair and cause breakage. Avoid using these tools as much as possible or use them on a low heat setting. Opt for air-drying your hair and using heatless styling methods such as braids and buns.

Step 4: Use Gentle Hair Products

Using harsh hair products can further damage your hair and exacerbate postpartum hair loss. Choose gentle hair products that are free from sulfates, parabens, and other harsh chemicals. Look for products that are specifically designed for postpartum hair loss.

Tips for Reducing Postpartum Hair Loss

Tip 1: Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help stimulate hair growth and increase blood flow to the hair follicles. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

Tip 2: Take Supplements

Taking supplements such as biotin and iron can help promote healthy hair growth. Consult with your doctor before taking any supplements.

Tip 3: Practice Stress-Relieving Techniques

Stress can exacerbate hair loss. Practice stress-relieving techniques such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises to help reduce stress levels.

FAQs

Q: How long does postpartum hair loss last?

A: Postpartum hair loss typically lasts for 6-12 months.

Q: Will my hair grow back?

A: Yes, your hair will grow back. However, it may take some time for your hair to fully recover.

Q: Can breastfeeding cause postpartum hair loss?

A: Yes, breastfeeding can cause postpartum hair loss. Hormonal changes in the body during breastfeeding can contribute to hair loss.

Hair Care for Postpartum Hair Loss

It is important to take care of your hair during postpartum hair loss. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Avoid pulling your hair back tightly
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal clasps
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is wet
  • Avoid harsh chemical treatments such as coloring or perming

Reducing postpartum hair loss can be a challenge, but with these tips and tricks, you can help your hair recover faster and look healthier. Remember to be patient and take care of yourself during this time.
