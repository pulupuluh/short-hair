How Do I Know My Hair Type As A Black Male

Written by Christine Jul 03, 2023 · 8 min read

As a black male, understanding your hair type is crucial in maintaining healthy hair. Knowing your hair type can help you choose the right products, styles, and techniques to keep your hair healthy and looking its best. In this article, we’ll discuss how to identify your hair type and provide some tips on how to care for it.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Identify Your Hair Type
  2. Step-by-Step Guide to Identifying Your Hair Type
  3. Tips for Caring for Your Hair Type
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Products for Your Hair Type

How to Identify Your Hair Type

There are several different hair typing systems out there, but one of the most commonly used is the Andre Walker Hair Typing System. This system categorizes hair into four types based on texture:

  • Type 1: Straight
  • Type 2: Wavy
  • Type 3: Curly
  • Type 4: Coily/Kinky

Each type is then broken down further into subcategories based on thickness and pattern.

Type 1: Straight Hair

Type 1 hair is straight with no curl or wave. It can be fine or coarse and has a natural shine to it.

Type 2: Wavy Hair

Type 2 hair has a slight wave to it, ranging from loose waves to more defined S-shaped waves. It can be fine or coarse and tends to be prone to frizz.

Type 3: Curly Hair

Type 3 hair has tight curls that can range from loose curls to tight corkscrews. It is typically fine or medium in texture and prone to dryness and frizz.

Type 4: Coily/Kinky Hair

Type 4 hair is the most tightly coiled and kinky of all hair types. It can range from fine to coarse and has a tendency to be very dry and prone to breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide to Identifying Your Hair Type

Now that you understand the different hair types, here’s a step-by-step guide to help you identify your own:

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry without any products.
  2. Look at your hair in natural light and determine if it is straight, wavy, curly, or coily/kinky.
  3. Determine the thickness of each strand of hair. Fine strands are barely visible, medium strands are visible but not thick, and thick strands are easily visible.
  4. Determine the pattern of your curls or waves. Type 2 hair has a loose S-pattern, Type 3 hair has a tighter S-pattern, and Type 4 hair has a Z-pattern.
  5. Take note of any frizz or dryness in your hair.

Once you have determined your hair type, you can start to choose the right products and techniques to care for it.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair Type

Now that you know your hair type, here are some tips to help you care for it:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help reduce frizz.
  • Avoid over-brushing or combing your hair, as this can lead to breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as this can also lead to breakage.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish your hair.

FAQs

What is the best way to care for Type 4 hair?

Type 4 hair is the most tightly coiled and kinky of all hair types and requires a lot of moisture to prevent breakage. Consider using a leave-in conditioner, deep conditioning treatment, and oil-based products to help keep your hair moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools and over-manipulating your hair.

What is the best way to care for Type 2 hair?

Type 2 hair has a slight wave to it and tends to be prone to frizz. Consider using a sulfate-free shampoo, conditioner, and leave-in conditioner to help keep your hair moisturized and reduce frizz. Avoid over-brushing or combing your hair, as this can lead to breakage.
