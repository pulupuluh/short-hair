How To See If A Haircut Suits You

Written by Bardi Jun 10, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of getting haircuts that don't suit you? Do you wish you could find the perfect haircut that flatters your face shape and features? If so, you've come to the right place! In this article, we'll show you how to see if a haircut suits you, step by step. We'll also give you some tips on how to maintain your new hairstyle and answer some frequently asked questions about hair care. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape
  • Step 2: Consider Your Hair Texture and Type
  • Step 3: Look for Inspiration
  • Step 4: Consult with Your Hair Stylist

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding a haircut that suits you is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and long. Each face shape has its own unique features and characteristics, and certain hairstyles will flatter each shape more than others. To determine your face shape, look in the mirror and measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Then, compare these measurements to the following guidelines:

  • Oval: Forehead is slightly wider than the chin, and the length of the face is about one and a half times the width.
  • Round: Prominent cheeks and a rounded chin, with the width and length of the face roughly equal.
  • Square: Prominent jaw and forehead, with the width of the face roughly equal to its length.
  • Heart: Wide forehead and cheekbones, with a narrow chin.
  • Diamond: Narrow forehead and jawline, with wide cheekbones.
  • Long: Long and narrow face, with the forehead, cheekbones, and jawline roughly the same width.

Once you've determined your face shape, you can start looking for hairstyles that will flatter your features.

Step 2: Consider Your Hair Texture and Type

Another important factor in finding a haircut that suits you is your hair texture and type. Do you have straight, wavy, curly, or coily hair? Is your hair thick or thin? Is it fine or coarse? These factors can all affect how a hairstyle will look on you. For example, if you have fine, straight hair, you may want to avoid heavy layers that can make your hair look limp. If you have thick, curly hair, you may want to opt for a layered cut that will help to reduce volume and add definition.

Step 3: Look for Inspiration

Now that you know your face shape and hair type, it's time to start looking for inspiration. Browse through magazines, websites, and social media to find hairstyles that you like and that will flatter your features. Pay attention to the length, shape, and layers of the hairstyles you like, and consider how they would look on you.

Step 4: Consult with Your Hair Stylist

Once you've found some hairstyles that you like, it's time to consult with your hair stylist. Bring in pictures of the hairstyles you like and discuss your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle with your stylist. They will be able to recommend a haircut that will flatter your features and be easy for you to maintain. Be open to their suggestions and trust their expertise!

Tips for Maintaining Your New Hairstyle

Now that you have a new haircut that suits you, it's important to take care of it. Here are some tips for maintaining your new hairstyle:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo and conditioner that are appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use hair products, such as styling creams and hairsprays, to help maintain your style.

Frequently Asked Questions about Hair Care

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it every day. If you have dry hair, you may only need to wash it once or twice a week. If you exercise regularly, you may need to wash your hair more frequently to remove sweat and dirt.

Q: How can I prevent split ends?

A: The best way to prevent split ends is to get regular trims. You should also avoid using too much heat on your hair and use a wide-toothed comb instead of a brush to detangle wet hair.

Q: How can I add volume to my hair?

A: There are several ways to add volume to your hair, such as using volumizing shampoos and conditioners, blow drying your hair upside down, and using a round brush to lift your roots while blow drying.

Q: How can I prevent frizz?

A: To prevent frizz, you should avoid rubbing your hair with a towel after washing it, as this can cause friction and damage your hair cuticles. Instead, gently squeeze out excess water and let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on your blow dryer. You can also use anti-frizz products, such as serums and oils, to help tame frizz.

We hope this article has helped you to see if a haircut suits you and given you some tips on how to maintain your new hairstyle. Remember to consult with your hair stylist and be open to their suggestions. With the right haircut and hair care routine, you can look and feel your best!
