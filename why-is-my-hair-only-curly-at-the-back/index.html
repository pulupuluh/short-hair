Why Is My Hair Only Curly At The Back

Written by Christine Jun 01, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Do you have a head full of hair, but only the back portion is curly? This can be frustrating, especially if you're trying to achieve a uniform hairstyle. In this blog post, we'll explore the reasons behind this phenomenon and what you can do to embrace your unique hair texture.

Description

Curly hair is caused by the shape of the hair follicle. If the follicle is oval-shaped, the hair will grow out as curly, while a round-shaped follicle will produce straight hair. However, it's not just the shape of the follicle that determines curl pattern. Other factors, including genetics, hormones, and environmental influences, can also play a role.

How to

If you're looking to enhance your natural curl pattern, there are a few things you can do. One of the most effective ways to encourage curls is by scrunching your hair while it's wet. This will help to define your curls and prevent frizz.

Another technique is plopping, which involves wrapping your wet hair in a t-shirt or microfiber towel to help enhance your natural curl pattern. You can also try diffusing your hair with a blow dryer on a low heat setting.

Step by Step

Here's how to plop your hair:

  1. Start with freshly washed hair.
  2. Flip your head over and place a t-shirt or microfiber towel on a flat surface.
  3. Lower your head onto the towel, positioning your hair in the center.
  4. Wrap the sides of the towel around your head, tying the sleeves or corners at the back of your neck.
  5. Leave your hair in the towel for 15-20 minutes, allowing it to dry and set.
  6. Remove the towel and gently scrunch your curls to release them.

Tips

When styling curly hair, it's important to use products that are specifically designed for your hair type. Look for products that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and aloe vera to help hydrate and define your curls.

You should also avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and disrupt your natural curl pattern. Instead, embrace your curls and let them shine.

FAQ

Q: Why is my hair only curly at the back?

A: There could be several reasons for this. One possibility is that the hair at the back of your head is more exposed to humidity, which can enhance curl formation. Another factor could be genetics, as your hair may naturally grow out in a different pattern at the back of your head.

Q: Can I train my hair to be more curly?

A: While you can't change the shape of your hair follicles, you can encourage your natural curl pattern by using the right products and styling techniques.

Hair Care

Caring for curly hair can be a challenge, but it's essential to maintaining healthy, defined curls. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls and keep them hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, and let your hair air dry whenever possible.

By following these tips and embracing your unique hair texture, you can rock your curly locks with confidence.
