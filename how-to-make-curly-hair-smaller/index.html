How To Make Curly Hair Smaller Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver May 11, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that it can be difficult to manage. One of the biggest challenges is making your curls smaller. Smaller curls can be easier to style and manage, and can give you a more polished look. In this article, we’ll share some tips and tricks for making curly hair smaller.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make Curly Hair Smaller
  • Step-by-Step: Making Curly Hair Smaller
  • Tips for Making Curly Hair Smaller
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Make Curly Hair Smaller

There are a few different methods you can use to make your curly hair smaller:

  • Blow drying: Using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer can help to stretch your curls and make them smaller.
  • Braiding: Braiding your hair while it’s wet and leaving it in overnight can help to make your curls smaller and more defined.
  • Twisting: Twisting small sections of your hair while it’s wet can also help to make your curls smaller and more defined.

Step-by-Step: Making Curly Hair Smaller

Here’s a step-by-step guide to making your curly hair smaller:

  1. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair to remove excess water.
  3. Apply a styling product, such as a curl cream or gel.
  4. Section your hair into small sections.
  5. Blow dry each section using a diffuser attachment. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends.
  6. Once you’ve blow dried all sections, use a hair pick to separate your curls and give them more volume.
  7. If you want to make your curls even smaller, braid or twist small sections of your hair while it’s wet.
  8. Leave the braids or twists in overnight, or until your hair is completely dry.
  9. Remove the braids or twists and use your fingers to separate your curls.
  10. Finish with a hairspray or other finishing product, if desired.

Tips for Making Curly Hair Smaller

Here are some additional tips for making your curly hair smaller:

  • Use a microfiber towel or T-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel, which can cause frizz.
  • Avoid using a brush on your curly hair, as it can cause breakage and frizz.
  • If you don’t have a diffuser attachment for your blow dryer, you can use a bowl diffuser or a sock diffuser.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find the one that works best for your hair.
  • Don’t be afraid to try different techniques, such as braiding, twisting, or using rollers.

FAQs

Q: Can I make my curly hair smaller without using heat?

A: Yes, you can make your curly hair smaller by braiding or twisting it while it’s wet and leaving it in overnight.

Q: Will making my curly hair smaller damage it?

A: No, making your curly hair smaller using the techniques outlined in this article should not damage your hair as long as you use a heat protectant and don’t apply too much tension to your hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

In addition to using the techniques outlined in this article to make your curly hair smaller, it’s important to take good care of your hair on a daily basis. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your curls moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and frizz.

By following these tips and using the techniques outlined in this article, you can make your curly hair smaller and more manageable. Remember to be patient and experiment with different techniques and products until you find what works best for your hair.
