What To Do With Short Hair While Growing It Out

Written by Daniel Jul 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you in the process of growing out your short hair? It can be a frustrating and awkward phase to go through, but fear not! There are plenty of things you can do to make the transition smoother and more manageable. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks on what to do with short hair while growing it out.

Table of Contents

  • How To
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How To

The key to growing out short hair is patience. Depending on how short your hair was to begin with, it could take anywhere from six months to a year (or longer) to grow it out to your desired length. In the meantime, there are a few things you can do to make the process easier:

1. Experiment with different hairstyles

Just because your hair is short doesn't mean you can't play around with different styles. Talk to your stylist about what options you have based on your current length, and try out a few different looks until you find something you like. This can help make the growing-out process feel less monotonous.

2. Use hair accessories

Hair accessories are a great way to add some flair to your hair while it's growing out. Headbands, barrettes, and hair clips can all be used to keep your hair out of your face and add some interest to your look. Just be careful not to use accessories that are too tight or that could damage your hair.

Step by Step

If you're struggling with how to style your short hair as it grows out, here are some step-by-step instructions for a few different looks:

1. Messy Bun

  1. Brush your hair back and gather it into a ponytail at the crown of your head.
  2. Twist the ponytail around itself to create a bun.
  3. Secure the bun with bobby pins, making sure to tuck any loose ends in.
  4. Pull out a few pieces around your face to create a messy, undone look.

2. Half-Up Top Knot

  1. Start by sectioning off the top half of your hair, from your temples to the crown of your head.
  2. Gather this section into a high ponytail and secure it with an elastic band.
  3. Twist the ponytail around itself to create a bun.
  4. Secure the bun with bobby pins, making sure to tuck any loose ends in.
  5. Let the rest of your hair hang loose.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to make the growing-out process easier:

1. Invest in hair treatments

As your hair grows, it can become dry and damaged. Invest in a good hair treatment, such as a deep conditioning mask, to keep your locks looking healthy and shiny.

2. Get regular trims

While it may seem counterintuitive to cut your hair when you're trying to grow it out, getting regular trims can actually help your hair grow faster and healthier. Aim for a trim every six to eight weeks to keep your ends from becoming too damaged.

FAQ

Q: How long will it take to grow out my short hair?

A: It depends on how short your hair was to begin with and how quickly it grows. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month, so it could take anywhere from six months to a year (or more) to grow out a short haircut.

Q: Can I dye my hair while it's growing out?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair while it's growing out. Just be sure to use a gentle, nourishing dye and avoid over-processing your hair.

Hair Care

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair as it grows out. Here are some tips:

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that are gentle on your hair and scalp, and avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates.

2. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Try to avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, and let your hair air dry whenever possible.

With these tips and tricks, you can make the process of growing out short hair a little bit easier. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair, and before you know it, you'll have the long, luscious locks you've been dreaming of!
