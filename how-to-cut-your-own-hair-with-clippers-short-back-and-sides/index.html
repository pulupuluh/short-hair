How To Cut Your Own Hair With Clippers Short Back And Sides

Written by Frank May 11, 2023 · 9 min read

If you're looking for a quick and easy way to maintain your short back and sides hairstyle, cutting your own hair with clippers is a great option. Not only is it cost-effective, but it's also convenient and can be done from the comfort of your own home. In this blog post, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair with clippers short back and sides, as well as some tips and FAQs to help you get the best results. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair With Clippers Short Back and Sides
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Step-by-Step Guide

Before you begin, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a set of hair clippers with adjustable guards, a comb, a handheld mirror, and a towel or cape to drape over your shoulders. Here are the steps to follow:

  1. Start by washing and towel-drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and will help the clippers glide smoothly through your hair.
  2. Attach the appropriate clipper guard to your clippers. The size will depend on how short you want your hair to be. For a short back and sides, start with a #3 or #4 guard.
  3. Begin by trimming the sides of your head. Use the comb to lift a section of hair and hold it straight out from your head. Run the clippers up the length of the hair, using the comb as a guide to keep the clippers level. Repeat this process, working your way around the sides of your head.
  4. Next, move to the back of your head. Use the handheld mirror to see the back of your head and use the same technique as before, using the comb to lift a section of hair and running the clippers up the length of the hair.
  5. Finally, trim the top of your head. Use the longer clipper guard or switch to scissors if you want to leave some length on top. Use the comb to lift a section of hair and trim evenly across the top of your head.
  6. Once you've finished trimming, use the handheld mirror to check for any uneven spots or missed areas. Make any necessary touch-ups and then clean up any loose hairs with a brush or towel.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results when cutting your own hair with clippers:

  • Start with a longer clipper guard and work your way down if you want your hair shorter. It's easier to take more off than to try to fix a mistake if you cut your hair too short.
  • Take your time and work in small sections. It's better to spend a little more time on each section to ensure an even cut.
  • Use the comb to lift the hair straight out from your head. This will help you see the length you're cutting and ensure an even cut across all sections.
  • If you're using scissors to trim the top, make sure they're sharp and use a comb to hold the hair in place while you cut. Don't try to cut freehand or you'll risk uneven results.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair with clippers:

  • Can I use regular scissors instead of clippers? While it's possible to use scissors to cut your own hair, it's more difficult to get an even cut and you'll need to have more experience with cutting hair. Clippers are the better option for beginners.
  • What if I make a mistake? If you accidentally cut your hair too short or create an uneven cut, it's best to wait a few days for your hair to grow out a bit before attempting to fix it. You can also visit a professional stylist for help.
  • How often should I cut my own hair? This will depend on how fast your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. As a general rule, aim to trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your style.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain your style. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage. If you need to style your hair with heat, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.
  • Consider using a styling product like gel, wax, or pomade to help hold your style in place and add texture.
  • If you have any concerns about your hair health or style, don't hesitate to visit a professional stylist for advice.

Cutting your own hair with clippers can be a great way to save time and money while maintaining your short back and sides hairstyle. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. Follow the steps and tips above, and you'll be on your way to a great haircut in no time!
