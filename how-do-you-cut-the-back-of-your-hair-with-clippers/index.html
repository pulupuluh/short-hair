How to Cut the Back of Your Hair with Clippers

Cutting the back of your hair can be tricky, but with the right technique, you can achieve a polished look. Here are the basic steps:

1. Gather Your Tools

You'll need a few items to get started:

  • Clippers with the appropriate guard (usually a #2 or #3 will work for most hair types)
  • A handheld mirror or a mirror propped up on a counter
  • Scissors or hair cutting shears
  • A spray bottle of water
  • A comb

2. Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see where you need to cut and ensure that the clippers glide smoothly over your hair.

3. Section Your Hair

Using your comb, create a horizontal part about an inch above your ears. Clip the top section of your hair out of the way with a hair clip.

4. Start at the Nape of Your Neck

Begin at the nape of your neck and work your way up. Use the clippers with the guard attached to trim the hair in a straight line, moving from the bottom of your hairline to the top. Take your time and make sure you're cutting evenly.

5. Move to the Sides

Once you've finished the back, move to the sides of your head. Use the clippers to trim the hair in a straight line, moving from the bottom of your hairline to the top.

6. Blend the Sides and Back

Use the scissors to blend the hair on the sides with the hair on the back. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle to create a seamless transition.

7. Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, use the handheld mirror to check the back of your head. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that the hair is even.

If you're still feeling unsure about the process, here's a step-by-step guide to cutting the back of your hair with clippers:

Tips for a Successful Haircut

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you're cutting the back of your hair with clippers:

  • Take your time and go slow. It's better to cut a little bit at a time rather than making a big mistake.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair as you cut. This will help you see where you need to trim and ensure that you're cutting evenly.
  • Keep a handheld mirror nearby so you can check the back of your head as you're cutting.
  • Use the scissors to blend the hair on the sides with the hair on the back for a polished, professional look.

FAQ

Is it easy to cut the back of your hair with clippers?

Cutting the back of your hair can be tricky, but with the right tools and technique, it's definitely doable. Take your time and go slow, and don't be afraid to make adjustments as you go.

What kind of clippers should I use?

Most clippers come with guards that can be attached to achieve different lengths. For cutting the back of your hair, a #2 or #3 guard should work for most hair types.

Can I cut my hair wet?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut evenly.

Hair Care After Your Haircut

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain your new style. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it looking neat and polished.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

Cutting the back of your hair with clippers may seem intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. Take your time, go slow, and don't be afraid to make adjustments as you go. With a little practice, you'll be a pro in no time!
