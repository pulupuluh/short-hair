How To Cut Layers Into The Back Of Your Hair

Written by Aprilia Aug 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to add some texture and volume to your hair, cutting layers into the back is a great way to do it. While it might seem like a daunting task, with some patience and practice, you can easily achieve a professional-looking result.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prepare Your Hair
  • How to Section Your Hair
  • How to Cut the First Layer
  • How to Cut the Remaining Layers
  • Tips for a Perfect Cut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Layers

How to Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting, you want to make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can stretch and look longer, which can result in an uneven cut. If you have curly hair, make sure to straighten it beforehand so you can see the length and shape more clearly.

Next, gather all the tools you'll need, including a pair of sharp hair scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also helpful to have a mirror behind you to check your progress.

How to Section Your Hair

Start by dividing your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Take one of the back sections and gather it all together in your hand. Hold the hair between your index and middle finger and slide your fingers down to the length you want to cut.

Using your other hand, grab the hair just below your fingers and use the comb to create a straight line. Once you have the line, clip the rest of the hair away so it doesn't get in the way.

How to Cut the First Layer

Take down the first section of hair you clipped away and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and slide your fingers down to the length you want to cut. Use your scissors to cut the hair in a straight line.

Once you've cut the first layer, take another section of hair from the back and repeat the process. Make sure to use the first layer as a guide so all the layers are even. Continue until you've cut all the layers in the back section.

How to Cut the Remaining Layers

Once you've finished the back section, move on to the front sections. Take one of the front sections and repeat the same process as the back. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up.

It's important to remember to cut each layer slightly longer than the layer below it to create a natural-looking cascade effect. You can also use the first section as a guide for the remaining sections.

Tips for a Perfect Cut

  • Use sharp hair scissors to avoid split ends or uneven cuts.
  • Take your time and work in small sections to ensure accuracy.
  • Don't cut too much at once. You can always go back and cut more if needed.
  • Check your progress in the mirror frequently to avoid any mistakes.

FAQs

How often should I cut layers into my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, cutting layers every six to eight weeks can help maintain the shape and texture of your hair.

Can I cut layers into my hair myself?

While it's possible to cut your own hair, it's always recommended to go to a professional stylist for the best results. If you do decide to cut your own hair, make sure to follow these steps carefully and use the right tools.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting layers into your hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain the shape and texture. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and cause split ends.

By following these simple steps, you can easily cut layers into the back of your hair and achieve a beautiful, voluminous look. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll be rewarded with a stunning new hairstyle.
