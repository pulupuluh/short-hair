What Makes Dreads Thicker A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Jul 22, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you looking to achieve thicker dreadlocks? If so, you're not alone. Many people with dreadlocks are always searching for ways to make their locks thicker and fuller. In this article, we'll explore what makes dreads thicker and provide you with some tips on how to achieve thicker locks.

Description

Dreadlocks are a hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are created by twisting or braiding hair until it forms a rope-like structure. Over time, the hair will continue to lock and form thicker, more mature dreadlocks.

There are several factors that can contribute to thicker dreadlocks. These factors include genetics, hair type, maintenance, and products. Let's explore each of these factors in more detail.

How to Make Dreads Thicker

If you're looking to make your dreadlocks thicker, there are several things you can do:

1. Genetics

Unfortunately, genetics plays a big role in how thick your dreadlocks will be. Some people are blessed with naturally thick hair, while others have finer hair that may take longer to form thick dreadlocks.

2. Hair Type

The type of hair you have can also affect how thick your dreadlocks will be. Coarse, curly hair tends to form thicker dreadlocks than fine, straight hair. However, with proper maintenance and care, anyone can achieve thicker dreadlocks.

3. Maintenance

Maintenance is key when it comes to achieving thicker dreadlocks. Regularly washing and conditioning your hair will help keep it healthy and strong, which will lead to thicker dreadlocks. You should also avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair, as these can weaken the hair and make it more prone to breakage.

4. Products

Using the right products can also help make your dreadlocks thicker. There are many products on the market that are specifically designed for dreadlocks, such as locking gels and creams. These products can help keep your hair moisturized and promote healthy, strong locks.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're ready to start your journey towards thicker dreadlocks, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. This will help keep your hair healthy and strong, which will lead to thicker dreadlocks.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into small, even sections. This will make it easier to create and maintain your dreadlocks.

Step 3: Twist or Braid Your Hair

Once your hair is sectioned, twist or braid each section until it forms a rope-like structure. Be sure to twist or braid tightly, as this will help your dreadlocks form faster and thicker.

Step 4: Maintain Your Dreadlocks

Regularly washing and conditioning your hair, as well as using locking gels and creams, will help keep your dreadlocks healthy and strong. You should also avoid using heat styling tools or harsh chemicals on your hair, as these can weaken the hair and make it more prone to breakage.

Tips for Thicker Dreadlocks

Here are some additional tips for achieving thicker dreadlocks:

1. Be Patient

Dreadlocks take time to form, so be patient and don't get discouraged if your locks aren't thick right away.

2. Avoid Over-Washing

While it's important to keep your hair clean, over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can lead to dry, brittle locks. Aim to wash your hair once or twice a week.

3. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help prevent breakage and tangling, which can lead to thinner dreadlocks.

FAQs

Q: Can I use regular shampoo on my dreadlocks?

A: No, it's best to use a sulfate-free shampoo that is specifically designed for dreadlocks.

Q: Will coloring my hair affect my dreadlocks?

A: Yes, coloring your hair can weaken it and make it more prone to breakage. If you do choose to color your hair, be sure to use a gentle, ammonia-free formula.

Q: Can I use a hair dryer on my dreadlocks?

A: It's best to avoid using heat styling tools on your dreadlocks, as these can weaken the hair and make it more prone to breakage.

Hair Care for Thicker Dreadlocks

Proper hair care is essential for achieving thicker dreadlocks. Here are some tips to keep your locks healthy and strong:

1. Wash and Condition Your Hair Regularly

Regularly washing and conditioning your hair will help keep it healthy and strong, which will lead to thicker dreadlocks.

2. Use Natural Products

Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals or synthetic fragrances. Instead, opt for natural products that are gentle on your hair.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Exposure to the sun can damage your hair and lead to thinner dreadlocks. Wear a hat or head wrap to protect your hair when you're outside.

In conclusion, achieving thicker dreadlocks requires patience, proper maintenance, and the right products. With these tips and tricks, you'll be well on your way to thicker, fuller locks.
