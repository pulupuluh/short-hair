Best Eyeshadow Palette For Over 60 A Guide To Flattering Eye Makeup

Written by Eveline Jun 20, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As we age, our skin and features change, and our makeup needs to adapt to match those changes. One area that can be particularly challenging is eye makeup, as our eyelids may become more hooded, and fine lines and wrinkles may appear. But don't worry, with the right eyeshadow palette, you can still achieve a beautiful, youthful look. In this article, we'll explore the best eyeshadow palettes for women over 60, and provide tips on how to create a flattering eye makeup look.

Table of Contents

  • Best Eyeshadow Palette for Over 60
  • How to Apply Eyeshadow for Over 60
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Flattering Eye Makeup
  • Tips for Flattering Eye Makeup
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Women Over 60

Best Eyeshadow Palette for Over 60

When choosing an eyeshadow palette, look for shades that complement your skin tone and eye color. Neutral shades such as browns, taupes, and grays work well for most women, as they create a soft, natural look. Avoid bright, bold colors that can be overpowering and draw attention to fine lines and wrinkles.

One of the best eyeshadow palettes for women over 60 is the Urban Decay Naked2 Basics Eyeshadow Palette. This palette features six neutral shades, ranging from cool to warm tones, that can be combined to create a variety of looks. The formula is velvety and blendable, making it easy to apply and build up coverage as needed. Plus, the compact size makes it perfect for travel or on-the-go touch-ups.

Another great option is the Tartelette Amazonian Clay Matte Eyeshadow Palette. This palette features 12 matte shades in warm browns, cool grays, and soft pinks that are perfect for creating a natural, subtle look. The formula is infused with Amazonian clay, which helps to nourish and hydrate the skin, making it a great option for mature skin.

How to Apply Eyeshadow for Over 60

Before applying eyeshadow, it's important to prep your eyelids with a primer to help your eyeshadow stay put and prevent creasing. Next, choose your eyeshadow colors and brushes. For a natural look, you can use a fluffy blending brush to apply a light, neutral shade all over your lid, and then use a smaller brush to apply a darker shade in the crease to create depth. You can also use a shimmer shade on the inner corner of your eye to brighten and open up your eyes.

When applying eyeshadow, be gentle and use a light hand. Avoid pulling or tugging on your eyelids, as this can cause wrinkles and fine lines to appear more pronounced. Blend your eyeshadow well to create a seamless, natural look.

Step-by-Step Guide to Flattering Eye Makeup

Here's a step-by-step guide to creating a flattering eye makeup look:

  1. Apply a primer to your eyelids to help your eyeshadow stay put and prevent creasing.
  2. Choose your eyeshadow colors and brushes.
  3. Apply a light, neutral shade all over your lid using a fluffy blending brush.
  4. Use a smaller brush to apply a darker shade in the crease to create depth.
  5. Apply a shimmer shade on the inner corner of your eye to brighten and open up your eyes.
  6. Use a pencil or liquid eyeliner to define your lash line.
  7. Curl your lashes and apply mascara.
  8. Finish off your look with a swipe of highlighter on your brow bone and the inner corner of your eye.

Tips for Flattering Eye Makeup

Here are some additional tips for creating a flattering eye makeup look:

  • Use a light hand when applying eyeshadow to avoid pulling or tugging on your eyelids.
  • Blend your eyeshadow well to create a seamless, natural look.
  • Choose neutral shades that complement your skin tone and eye color.
  • Avoid bright, bold colors that can be overpowering and draw attention to fine lines and wrinkles.
  • Use a primer to help your eyeshadow stay put and prevent creasing.
  • Apply mascara to your top lashes only to avoid drawing attention to fine lines and wrinkles around your eyes.

FAQs

What eyeshadow colors are best for women over 60?

Neutral shades such as browns, taupes, and grays work well for most women over 60, as they create a soft, natural look. Avoid bright, bold colors that can be overpowering and draw attention to fine lines and wrinkles.

How should I apply eyeshadow to avoid tugging on my eyelids?

Use a light hand when applying eyeshadow, and avoid pulling or tugging on your eyelids. You can also try using your finger to apply eyeshadow, as this can be more gentle than using a brush.

Hair Care Tips for Women Over 60

In addition to choosing the right eyeshadow palette, it's important to take care of your hair as you age. As we get older, our hair may become thinner and more brittle, so it's important to use gentle, nourishing products that won't damage your hair. Here are some tips for caring for your hair over 60:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as this can be gentler than using a brush.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish your hair and keep it healthy.

With the right eyeshadow palette and hair care routine, you can look and feel your best at any age.
