How To Do Big Chop On Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Apr 06, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you tired of dealing with your damaged or chemically treated hair? Do you want to start fresh with a new hairstyle? If so, you might want to consider doing the big chop on your natural hair. This process involves cutting off all your relaxed or damaged hair and starting anew with your natural curls. In this article, we’ll show you how to do a big chop on natural hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Do Big Chop on Natural Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Successful Big Chop
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After the Big Chop

How to Do Big Chop on Natural Hair

The big chop is a process that involves cutting off all your relaxed or chemically treated hair. This process can be scary for some people, but it is the first step towards embracing your natural curls. Here are the steps you should follow:

1. Decide on the Length

Before you start the big chop process, you need to decide on the length of your hair. Some people prefer to cut off all their hair, while others prefer to leave a few inches. The length you choose will depend on your personal preference and the thickness of your hair.

2. Get the Right Tools

You will need the right tools to do a big chop on natural hair. These tools include a pair of sharp scissors and a comb. You should also have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp during the cutting process.

3. Section Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, you should section it into manageable parts. Use a comb to create partings and clip each section away. This will make the cutting process easier and less overwhelming.

4. Cut Your Hair

Once you have sectioned your hair, you can start cutting it. Take one section of hair and use the scissors to cut it as close to the scalp as possible. Continue cutting each section until you have cut off all your relaxed or chemically treated hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to do a big chop on natural hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before you start the big chop process, you should wash your hair with a gentle shampoo. This will help remove any dirt or product buildup in your hair.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will make it easier to section your hair and cut it.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable parts using a comb. Clip each section away to make it easier to cut.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair

Take one section of hair and use the scissors to cut it as close to the scalp as possible. Continue cutting each section until you have cut off all your relaxed or chemically treated hair.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you have cut your hair, you can style it however you like. You can leave it as is or use products to define your curls.

Tips for a Successful Big Chop

Here are some tips to help you have a successful big chop:

1. Be Prepared

Before you start the big chop process, make sure you have all the tools and products you need. This will make the process smoother and less stressful.

2. Have Realistic Expectations

Remember that your hair will look different after the big chop. You may experience shrinkage, and your curls may be tighter than you are used to. Be patient and embrace your new look.

3. Protect Your Hair

After the big chop, your hair will be more fragile than usual. Protect it by using a silk or satin pillowcase, avoiding heat styling, and deep conditioning regularly.

FAQ

Q: Will the big chop damage my hair?

A: No, the big chop will not damage your hair. It is a natural process that involves cutting off your relaxed or chemically treated hair to embrace your natural curls.

Q: How often should I deep condition my hair after the big chop?

A: You should deep condition your hair once a week to help it stay moisturized and healthy.

Hair Care After the Big Chop

After the big chop, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

1. Moisturize Your Hair

Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized. This will help prevent breakage and keep your curls defined.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands. Heat can damage your hair and cause it to break.

3. Protect Your Hair at Night

Use a silk or satin pillowcase to protect your hair while you sleep. This will help prevent breakage and keep your curls defined.

Doing the big chop on natural hair can be a scary process, but it is the first step towards embracing your natural curls. Follow these steps and tips to have a successful big chop and enjoy your new hairstyle.
