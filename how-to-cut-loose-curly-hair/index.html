How To Cut Loose Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 04, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have loose curly hair, you know that finding the right haircut can be a challenge. It's important to find a stylist who understands your hair type and can work with your natural texture. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting loose curly hair, including tips for achieving a great haircut and maintaining healthy, beautiful locks.

Description

Cutting loose curly hair can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. With the right tools, techniques, and a bit of patience, you can achieve a beautiful, healthy cut that complements your natural curls. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about cutting loose curly hair, including tips for finding the right stylist, how to prepare for your haircut, and how to care for your curls after the cut.

How to

Before you book your appointment with a stylist, it's important to do your research. Look for a stylist who specializes in curly hair and has experience working with your hair type. You can also ask for recommendations from friends or family members with similar hair types. Once you've found a stylist, schedule a consultation to discuss your hair goals and determine the best cut for your curls.

When it comes to cutting loose curly hair, it's important to work with your hair's natural texture. Avoid using a razor or thinning shears, which can create frizz and damage your curls. Instead, opt for a blunt cut or layers that enhance your curls' natural shape.

To begin the cut, start with dry, detangled hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to separate your hair into sections, and clip each section to keep it out of the way. Starting at the back of your head, use sharp scissors to cut small sections of hair at a time, working your way around your head. Take care to cut each section evenly, and avoid cutting too much at once.

Once you've finished cutting your hair, you can style your curls as desired. If you're unsure about how to style your new cut, ask your stylist for tips and recommendations.

Step by Step

Step 1: Find a stylist who specializes in curly hair and schedule a consultation.

Step 2: Wash and detangle your hair, and let it air dry or use a diffuser to dry it.

Step 3: Use a wide-tooth comb to separate your hair into sections, and clip each section to keep it out of the way.

Step 4: Starting at the back of your head, use sharp scissors to cut small sections of hair at a time, working your way around your head.

Step 5: Take care to cut each section evenly, and avoid cutting too much at once.

Step 6: Once you've finished cutting your hair, style your curls as desired.

Tips

- Use sharp, high-quality scissors to avoid damaging your curls.

- Avoid using a razor or thinning shears, which can create frizz and damage your curls.

- Cut your hair when it's dry to see how your curls will fall naturally.

- Consider getting layers to enhance your curls' natural shape.

- Talk to your stylist about the best products and styling techniques for your hair type.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my loose curly hair?

A: It's generally recommended to get a trim every 8-12 weeks to maintain healthy hair. However, the frequency of your haircuts may depend on your hair's growth rate and your personal hair goals.

Q: Should I cut my curly hair when it's wet or dry?

A: It's generally recommended to cut curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may not accurately represent your curls' natural shape.

Q: What should I do to care for my curls after the cut?

A: To maintain healthy, beautiful curls after the cut, it's important to use sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners, avoid heat styling tools, and regularly deep condition your hair.

Hair Care

To keep your loose curly hair healthy and beautiful, it's important to follow a regular hair care routine. This may include washing your hair with sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners, using a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls, and regularly deep conditioning your hair to keep it hydrated and nourished.

It's also important to avoid heat styling tools, as they can damage your curls and create frizz. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser to dry it on low heat.

If you're struggling with frizz or dryness, consider using a hair oil or serum to add moisture and shine to your curls. You can also experiment with different styling techniques, such as plopping or diffusing, to find the best method for your hair type.

Remember, cutting loose curly hair is all about working with your hair's natural texture and enhancing its unique beauty. With the right tools, techniques, and hair care routine, you can achieve healthy, beautiful curls that make you feel confident and beautiful.
