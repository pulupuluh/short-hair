How Do I Know What Colour Hair Suits Me

Written by Aprilia Jun 11, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Choosing a hair color can be a daunting task, especially if it's your first time experimenting with different shades. With so many options available, ranging from bold and edgy to subtle and natural, it's easy to get overwhelmed. But don't worry, we're here to help you figure out which hair color suits you best. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about finding the perfect hair color for your skin tone and personality.

Table of Content

  • How To
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Choosing the right hair color is not just about following the latest trends or picking a color that you like. It's about finding a shade that complements your skin tone, eye color, and personality. The right hair color can enhance your natural beauty, make you look younger, and boost your confidence. On the other hand, the wrong hair color can make you look washed out or harsh.

How To

The first step in finding the right hair color is to determine your skin tone. Your skin tone can be warm, cool, or neutral. Warm skin tones have a yellow or golden undertone, while cool skin tones have a pink or blue undertone. Neutral skin tones have a mix of warm and cool undertones.

The easiest way to determine your skin tone is to look at the veins on your wrist. If your veins appear green, you have a warm skin tone. If they appear blue or purple, you have a cool skin tone. If you can't tell if your veins are green or blue, you most likely have a neutral skin tone.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to determine your skin tone:

  1. Look at the veins on your wrist.
  2. If your veins appear green, you have a warm skin tone.
  3. If your veins appear blue or purple, you have a cool skin tone.
  4. If you can't tell if your veins are green or blue, you most likely have a neutral skin tone.

Tips

Once you've determined your skin tone, you can start picking hair colors that will complement it. If you have a warm skin tone, you should look for hair colors that have golden or honey undertones, such as caramel, chestnut, or auburn. If you have a cool skin tone, you should look for hair colors that have ashy or cool undertones, such as platinum blonde, ash brown, or black. If you have a neutral skin tone, you can pull off both warm and cool hair colors.

It's important to consider your eye color and natural hair color when choosing a new hair color. If you have blue or green eyes, you can enhance them with warm hair colors like honey blonde or copper. If you have brown eyes, you can complement them with cool hair colors like ash brown or platinum blonde. If you have red or strawberry blonde hair, you can enhance it with warm hair colors like caramel or copper. If you have dark brown or black hair, you can complement it with cool hair colors like ash brown or jet black.

FAQ

Q: Can I dye my hair a completely different color than my natural hair?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair any color you like. However, if you're going for a drastic change, it's best to consult a professional hairstylist to avoid damaging your hair or ending up with a color that doesn't suit you.

Q: How often should I touch up my hair color?

A: It depends on the type of hair color you choose and how fast your hair grows. Some hair colors, such as demi-permanent or semi-permanent, fade gradually over time and may need touch-ups every 4-6 weeks. Other hair colors, such as permanent, may last longer and only need touch-ups every 8-12 weeks.

Q: How can I maintain my new hair color?

A: To maintain your new hair color, it's important to use color-safe shampoo and conditioner, avoid washing your hair too often, and protect your hair from UV rays and heat styling tools. You can also use hair masks and treatments to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Hair Care

After you've dyed your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips for maintaining your colored hair:

  • Use color-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair color from fading.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause your color to fade faster.
  • Protect your hair from UV rays and heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair and cause your color to fade.
  • Use hair masks and treatments to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Visit a professional hairstylist for regular trims and touch-ups.

Now that you know how to determine your skin tone and choose the right hair color for you, it's time to experiment and have fun! Whether you decide to go for a bold new look or a subtle change, remember to take care of your hair to keep it looking beautiful.
