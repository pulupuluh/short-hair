What To Say After Haircut Arabic A Guide To Communicating With Your Stylist

Written by Daniel Jun 21, 2023 · 8 min read

Getting a haircut can be a nerve-wracking experience, especially if you are not fluent in the language spoken in the salon. If you have recently had a haircut Arabic, you may be wondering what to say to your stylist to ensure that you are happy with the results. In this guide, we will provide you with some tips on how to communicate effectively with your stylist, what to say after haircut Arabic, and how to take care of your new haircut.

Description: Understanding the Language Barrier

Language barriers can be a significant challenge when getting a haircut in a salon where the stylist speaks a different language. You may be worried about not being able to communicate your preferences, resulting in a haircut that you are not happy with. In some cases, the stylist may also be unsure of what you want and may give you a haircut that is not what you had in mind.

How to: Communicating with Your Stylist

The key to ensuring that you get the haircut you want is to communicate effectively with your stylist. Here are some tips on how to do that:

1. Show Pictures

One of the easiest ways to communicate what you want is to show pictures to your stylist. You can bring in pictures of hairstyles that you like and ask the stylist to replicate them. Pictures can be a universal language, and your stylist may be able to understand what you want by looking at them.

2. Use Simple Language

When communicating with your stylist, it is best to use simple language. Avoid using complex words or phrases that your stylist may not understand. Stick to simple phrases such as "shorter," "longer," "layered," or "rounded."

3. Repeat Your Requests

It is essential to repeat your requests to ensure that your stylist understands what you want. If you feel that your stylist did not understand what you said, repeat your request using simpler language.

Step by Step: What to Say After Haircut Arabic

After your haircut Arabic, you may be wondering what to say to your stylist. Here are some phrases that you can use:

1. Shukran (Thank you)

The first thing you should say after your haircut Arabic is "shukran." This means "thank you" in Arabic and is a polite way to show your appreciation for the stylist's work.

2. Ana mabsout (I am happy)

If you are happy with your haircut, you can say "ana mabsout." This means "I am happy" in Arabic and is a great way to let your stylist know that you are satisfied with the results.

3. Ana mitshajilak (I will come back)

If you are planning on returning to the salon, you can say "ana mitshajilak." This means "I will come back" in Arabic and is a way to show your loyalty to the stylist.

Tips: Taking Care of Your Haircut

Once you have had your haircut Arabic, it is essential to take care of it to ensure that it looks great for as long as possible. Here are some tips:

1. Use the Right Products

Using the right products can help keep your haircut looking fresh and stylish. Ask your stylist for recommendations on products that will work best for your hair type.

2. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help maintain the shape of your haircut and prevent split ends. Ask your stylist how often you should come in for a trim.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Using heat-styling tools such as blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons can damage your hair. Use heat protectant products and avoid using these tools too often.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

1. How can I communicate with my stylist if I don't speak Arabic?

You can use pictures to show your stylist what you want, and you can also use simple phrases in English such as "shorter," "longer," "layered," or "rounded."

2. What should I say to my stylist if I am not happy with my haircut?

If you are not happy with your haircut, you can ask your stylist to make changes. Be specific about what you want, and show pictures if necessary.

3. How often should I get a haircut?

The frequency of your haircuts depends on your hair type and the style you have. Ask your stylist for recommendations on how often you should come in for a trim.

Hair Care: Final Thoughts

Getting a haircut Arabic can be a great experience if you know how to communicate effectively with your stylist. By using pictures and simple language, you can ensure that you get the haircut you want. After your haircut, remember to take care of your hair by using the right products, getting regular trims, and protecting it from heat.
