How To Layer Long Hair Yourself With Scissors

Written by Christine May 20, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking for a way to add some volume and dimension to your long hair, layering is a great option. And the best part? You can do it yourself! All you need is a pair of sharp scissors, some clips, and a little bit of patience. In this article, we'll walk you through how to layer long hair yourself with scissors, step by step.

How-To: Layer Long Hair Yourself with Scissors

Layering long hair yourself with scissors might seem daunting, but it's actually a fairly simple process. Here's what you need to do:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start layering your hair, it's important to make sure it's clean and dry. Wash your hair with your favorite shampoo and conditioner, then blow dry it until it's completely dry.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Next, divide your hair into sections. Use clips to secure the top section of your hair out of the way, then clip the bottom section of your hair into two or three sections.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Starting with the bottom section of your hair, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting at the length you want the layer to be. Continue cutting small sections of hair at an angle until you've finished the bottom section.

Step 4: Move on to the Next Section

Once you've finished the bottom section of your hair, unclip the next section and repeat the process. Remember to cut each section at an angle and to cut small sections of hair at a time.

Step 5: Finish with the Top Section

Finally, unclip the top section of your hair and repeat the process. Remember to cut at an angle and to cut small sections of hair at a time.

Tips for Layering Long Hair Yourself with Scissors

Here are some tips to keep in mind as you layer your long hair with scissors:

  • Start with small sections of hair
  • Cut at an angle for a natural look
  • Use sharp scissors to make clean cuts
  • Take breaks if your arms get tired
  • Don't be afraid to go slow and take your time

FAQ: Layering Long Hair Yourself with Scissors

Can I layer my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can layer curly hair. Just make sure you cut each section of hair at an angle to avoid creating too many layers.

How often should I layer my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows, but most people layer their hair every 6-8 weeks.

Can I layer my hair if it's thin?

Yes, layering can actually help add volume to thin hair. Just be careful not to cut too much off, as it can make your hair look even thinner.

Hair Care After Layering

It's important to take care of your hair after layering to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week
  • Avoid using heat tools too often
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the layers
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to avoid tangling
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat tools

Layering long hair yourself with scissors is a great way to add some dimension and volume to your hair. With a little bit of practice and patience, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home.
