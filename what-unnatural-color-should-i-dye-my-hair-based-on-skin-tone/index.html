What Unnatural Color Should I Dye My Hair Based On Skin Tone

Written by Oliver Mar 12, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your natural hair color and looking for a change? Dying your hair an unnatural color can be a fun way to express yourself and spice up your look. However, with so many colors to choose from, it can be overwhelming to decide which one will look best on you. In this article, we will discuss what unnatural color you should dye your hair based on your skin tone.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Choosing the right hair color for your skin tone is crucial to ensure that the color complements your complexion and enhances your features. The right hair color can brighten up your face and make your eyes pop, while the wrong color can make you look washed out and dull.

Unnatural hair colors like blue, pink, and purple are becoming increasingly popular, but not all colors will suit everyone. It's essential to take into consideration your skin tone and undertones when choosing a hair color.

How To

The first step in choosing an unnatural hair color is to determine your skin tone. There are three types of skin tones: warm, cool, and neutral.

If you have a warm skin tone, you have yellow or golden undertones. Your skin may have a peachy or golden hue, and you may tan easily. Warm skin tones look best with warm hair colors like red, orange, and yellow. These colors will complement your skin tone and bring out your features.

If you have a cool skin tone, you have pink or blue undertones. Your skin may have a pink or rosy hue, and you may burn easily in the sun. Cool skin tones look best with cool hair colors like blue, purple, and green. These colors will complement your skin tone and enhance your features.

If you have a neutral skin tone, you have a mix of warm and cool undertones. Your skin may have a beige or olive hue, and you may tan and burn equally. Neutral skin tones can pull off both warm and cool hair colors, but it's best to choose a color that complements your undertones.

Step By Step

To choose the right hair color based on your skin tone, follow these steps:

  1. Determine your skin tone.
  2. Choose a warm or cool hair color based on your skin tone.
  3. Consult with a hairstylist to determine the best shade for you.
  4. Do a strand test before dyeing your entire head to ensure that you like the color.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing an unnatural hair color based on your skin tone:

  • Consider your eye color when choosing a hair color. Certain colors can make your eyes pop and enhance your features.
  • If you're unsure about which color to choose, start with a temporary or semi-permanent dye to see how the color looks on you.
  • Take good care of your hair after dyeing it to maintain the color and prevent damage.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about choosing an unnatural hair color based on skin tone:

Q: Can I dye my hair an unnatural color if I have dark skin?

A: Yes, absolutely! Unnatural hair colors look great on all skin tones, including dark skin. Just make sure to choose a color that complements your skin tone.

Q: Can I dye my hair an unnatural color if I have warm undertones?

A: Yes! Warm undertones look great with warm hair colors like red and orange. Just make sure to choose a shade that complements your skin tone.

Q: Can I dye my hair an unnatural color if I have cool undertones?

A: Yes! Cool undertones look great with cool hair colors like blue and purple. Just make sure to choose a shade that complements your skin tone.

Hair Care

After dyeing your hair an unnatural color, it's essential to take good care of your hair to maintain the color and prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the color.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day to prevent the color from fading.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and cause the color to fade.

Now that you know what unnatural color you should dye your hair based on your skin tone, it's time to book that appointment with your hairstylist and get ready to rock your new look!
