How To Care For Big Chop Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Jun 01, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have recently done the big chop or are planning to do so, congratulations on embracing your natural hair! The big chop is a liberating experience, but it also requires a lot of care and attention to keep your hair healthy and strong. In this article, we will guide you through the steps on how to care for big chop hair, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  2. Step by Step Guide

How to Care for Big Chop Hair

After doing the big chop, your hair may feel different and require different care than before. Here are some steps to follow:

1. Moisturize and Seal

One of the key steps to caring for big chop hair is to keep it moisturized. Use a leave-in conditioner or moisturizer to keep your hair hydrated, and seal in the moisture with an oil or butter. This will help prevent breakage and keep your hair healthy.

2. Protect your Hair at Night

When you sleep, your hair can become tangled and dry out. To prevent this, wrap your hair in a silk or satin scarf or use a satin pillowcase. This will help protect your hair and keep it healthy.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Using heat on your hair can cause damage and breakage. Try to avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. Instead, embrace your natural texture and use protective styles like braids or twists.

4. Trim your Ends Regularly

Trimming your ends regularly can help prevent split ends and breakage. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and strong.

5. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb can help prevent breakage and tangles. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, gently detangling your hair as you go.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to care for big chop hair:

Step 1: Wash your Hair

Use a gentle shampoo to wash your hair, focusing on your scalp. Rinse thoroughly and follow up with a conditioner.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-in Conditioner or Moisturizer

Apply a leave-in conditioner or moisturizer to your hair to keep it hydrated and healthy.

Step 3: Seal in Moisture with an Oil or Butter

Seal in the moisture with an oil or butter of your choice. This will help prevent breakage and keep your hair healthy.

Step 4: Style your Hair

Embrace your natural texture and style your hair in a way that makes you feel confident and beautiful.

Step 5: Protect your Hair at Night

Wrap your hair in a silk or satin scarf or use a satin pillowcase to protect your hair while you sleep.

Tips for Caring for Big Chop Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for big chop hair:

  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated from the inside out.
  • Use a protein treatment once a month to strengthen your hair.
  • Avoid using products with harsh chemicals or sulfates.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, and protein.

FAQs about Big Chop Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about caring for big chop hair:

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have a very active lifestyle or sweat a lot, you may need to wash your hair more often. Otherwise, aim to wash your hair every 7-10 days.

Q: Can I still use heat on my hair?

A: While it is best to avoid heat styling, you can still use heat on your hair occasionally. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting low.

Q: How long will it take for my hair to grow back?

A: Hair growth varies from person to person, but most people will see significant growth within 6-12 months.

Hair Care Advice

Caring for big chop hair requires patience, dedication, and a willingness to experiment with different products and styles. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling, and keep it hydrated and healthy. With time and care, your hair will grow back stronger and more beautiful than ever before.
