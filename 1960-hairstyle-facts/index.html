1960 Hairstyle Facts A Look Back In Time

Written by Oliver May 02, 2023 Â· 6 min read

The 1960s was a decade of change and innovation in fashion, music, and culture. Hairstyles during this time were no exception, with iconic looks that are still popular today. In this article, we'll take a trip down memory lane and explore some interesting facts about 1960 hairstyles.

Table of Contents

  • How to Achieve 1960 Hairstyles
  • Step-by-Step Guide to 1960 Hairstyles
  • Tips for Maintaining 1960 Hairstyles

How to Achieve 1960 Hairstyles

To achieve 1960 hairstyles, you'll need to start with the right cut. Popular looks during this time included the beehive, bouffant, and pixie cut. The beehive was a high, voluminous style that was achieved by teasing the hair and using lots of hairspray. The bouffant was similar, but with less height and more volume at the sides. The pixie cut was short and choppy, with lots of layers.

Once you have the right cut, you can style your hair using hot rollers, curling irons, and backcombing. Hairspray was a must to keep the style in place. Accessories like headbands, scarves, and hair clips were also popular during this time.

Step-by-Step Guide to 1960 Hairstyles

Here's a step-by-step guide to achieving the iconic 1960 beehive hairstyle:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Separate a section of hair at the crown of your head and tease it with a fine-tooth comb.
  3. Smooth the teased section back, securing it with bobby pins.
  4. Use hot rollers to curl the rest of your hair.
  5. Once the rollers are cool, remove them and brush out the curls.
  6. Gently backcomb the ends of your hair to add volume.
  7. Use hairspray to hold the style in place.
  8. Finish off with a headband or hair clip for added flair.

Tips for Maintaining 1960 Hairstyles

To keep your 1960s hairstyle looking its best, follow these tips:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as brushing can ruin the shape of the style.
  • Don't wash your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it harder to style.
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • When backcombing, be gentle to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Use hairspray sparingly to avoid weighing down your hair.

FAQ About 1960 Hairstyles

Q: Who was the most iconic 1960s hairstyle icon?

A: One of the most iconic 1960s hairstyle icons was actress and model Brigitte Bardot, known for her voluminous, tousled hair.

Q: What was the most popular 1960s hairstyle for men?

A: The most popular 1960s hairstyle for men was the "mod" cut, which was short on the sides and longer on top, with a side part.

Q: What was the inspiration behind 1960s hairstyles?

A: 1960s hairstyles were inspired by the changing cultural landscape of the time, including the rise of feminism and the hippie movement.

Hair Care Tips for 1960 Hairstyles

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your style.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much hairspray, as this can dry out your hair.
  • If you color your hair, use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading.

In conclusion, 1960s hairstyles were a reflection of the changing times, with bold and innovative looks that are still popular today. By following these tips and using the right tools, you can achieve your own 1960s-inspired hairstyle and rock it with confidence.
