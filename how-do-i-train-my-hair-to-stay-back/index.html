How Do I Train My Hair To Stay Back A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline May 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're wondering how to train your hair to stay back, you're not alone. Many people struggle with getting their hair to stay in place, especially when they're trying to achieve a certain style. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for training your hair to stay back, so you can look and feel your best.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Train Your Hair to Stay Back
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Description

Training your hair to stay back requires patience, consistency, and the right hair products. Whether you want to achieve a slicked-back look or simply keep your hair out of your face, there are several steps you can take to train your hair to stay put.

How to Train Your Hair to Stay Back

The first step in training your hair to stay back is to start with clean, damp hair. This will help the hair products you use to better adhere to your hair and provide a stronger hold. Once your hair is clean and damp, you can begin styling.

There are several hair products you can use to help train your hair to stay back, including pomades, gels, and waxes. When choosing a product, look for one that provides a strong hold without leaving your hair feeling sticky or greasy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to training your hair to stay back:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a small amount of your chosen hair product to your palms and rub your hands together.
  3. Starting at the crown of your head, work the product through your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly.
  4. Using a comb or brush, style your hair back, making sure to smooth out any bumps or flyaways.
  5. Allow your hair to dry completely.
  6. If necessary, add more product to any areas that need a stronger hold.
  7. Repeat this process daily until your hair begins to naturally stay back on its own.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for training your hair to stay back:

  • Be consistent: The more you style your hair in the same way, the more likely it is to stay that way.
  • Use a high-quality hair product: Cheaper products may not provide the hold you need to keep your hair in place.
  • Avoid touching your hair throughout the day: The oils from your hands can break down the hold of your hair product.
  • Try blow-drying your hair in the direction you want it to stay: This can help set the style in place.
  • Consider getting a shorter haircut: Shorter hair is often easier to style and keep in place.

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to train your hair to stay back?

A: It depends on your hair type and how often you style it. For some people, it may take a few weeks, while for others, it may take several months.

Q: Can I train my hair to stay back without using hair products?

A: It's possible, but it may be more difficult. Using a hair product can provide a stronger hold and make it easier to train your hair to stay back.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, to keep your hair healthy and looking its best, here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and soft.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair looking neat and healthy.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you should be well on your way to training your hair to stay back. Remember to be patient and consistent, and don't be afraid to experiment with different hair products and styling techniques to find what works best for you.
