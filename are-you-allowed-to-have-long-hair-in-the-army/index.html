Are You Allowed To Have Long Hair In The Army

Aspiring soldiers may wonder if they're allowed to have long hair in the Army. The answer is not straightforward, and it depends on various factors. In this blog post, we'll explore the Army's grooming standards, which include hair length requirements, and whether long hair is allowed or not.

Army Grooming Standards

The Army has strict grooming standards that all soldiers must follow. These standards are in place to ensure uniformity, discipline, and professionalism. The Army's grooming standards cover various aspects, such as hair, facial hair, nails, tattoos, and makeup. Failure to comply with these standards can result in disciplinary action, including discharge from the Army.

Hair Length Requirements

The Army's hair length requirements for men and women are different. Male soldiers are required to have their hair cut above the ears and around the neck, with no hair touching the collar. The hair on top of the head must be neatly groomed and not exceed two inches in length. Female soldiers are allowed to have longer hair than male soldiers. However, the hair must be worn in a bun or a single ponytail that does not extend beyond the bottom of the collar.

Long Hair in the Army

The Army does not prohibit soldiers from having long hair. However, the length of the hair must comply with the grooming standards mentioned earlier. Male soldiers cannot have hair touching their collars, and female soldiers must keep their hair in a bun or a single ponytail that does not extend beyond the collar. So, if you want to have long hair in the Army, you must ensure that it complies with the Army's grooming standards.

How to Have Long Hair in the Army

If you want to have long hair in the Army, you must ensure that it complies with the Army's grooming standards. Here are some tips on how to have long hair in the Army:

1. Keep Your Hair Neat and Clean

Long hair requires more care and attention than short hair. You must keep your hair clean, well-groomed, and neatly styled. Use a shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type and comb or brush your hair regularly to prevent tangles and knots.

2. Use Hair Accessories

If you're a female soldier with long hair, you can use hair accessories such as hair ties, hairpins, and hair clips to keep your hair in place. Make sure that the accessories are plain and match your hair color.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Even if you're growing your hair long, you must trim it regularly to keep it in shape and prevent split ends. It's recommended to trim your hair every six to eight weeks.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to have long hair in the Army:

Step 1: Understand the Army's Grooming Standards

Before growing your hair long, make sure that you understand the Army's grooming standards and how they apply to hair length.

Step 2: Choose the Right Hairstyle

Choose a hairstyle that complies with the Army's grooming standards. If you're a male soldier, keep your hair short and well-groomed. If you're a female soldier, wear your hair in a bun or a single ponytail that does not extend beyond the collar.

Step 3: Take Care of Your Hair

Long hair requires more care and attention than short hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type and comb or brush your hair regularly. Trim your hair regularly to keep it in shape and prevent split ends.

Step 4: Use Hair Accessories

If you're a female soldier with long hair, use hair accessories such as hair ties, hairpins, and hair clips to keep your hair in place. Make sure that the accessories are plain and match your hair color.

Tips for Long Hair in the Army

Here are some tips for maintaining long hair in the Army:

1. Keep Your Hair Clean and Neat

Long hair requires more care and attention than short hair. Keep your hair clean, well-groomed, and neatly styled.

2. Use Hair Accessories

If you're a female soldier with long hair, use hair accessories such as hair ties, hairpins, and hair clips to keep your hair in place.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Even if you're growing your hair long, you must trim it regularly to keep it in shape and prevent split ends.

4. Avoid Extreme Hairstyles

Avoid extreme hairstyles that do not comply with the Army's grooming standards. Stick to simple and neat hairstyles that are easy to maintain.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can male soldiers have long hair in the Army?

Male soldiers can have long hair in the Army as long as it complies with the Army's grooming standards. The hair must be cut above the ears and around the neck, with no hair touching the collar, and the hair on top of the head must not exceed two inches in length.

Can female soldiers wear their hair down in the Army?

No, female soldiers cannot wear their hair down in the Army. The hair must be worn in a bun or a single ponytail that does not extend beyond the collar.

Can soldiers dye their hair in the Army?

Soldiers can dye their hair in the Army as long as the color is natural and complements the soldier's complexion. Unnatural hair colors, such as blue or green, are not allowed.

Hair Care in the Army

Proper hair care is essential for soldiers in the Army. Soldiers must keep their hair clean, well-groomed, and neatly styled to comply with the Army's grooming standards. Soldiers must also maintain good hygiene practices and avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as these can dry out the hair and scalp. It's also recommended to trim the hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain a neat appearance.

Now that you know the Army's grooming standards and how they apply to hair length, you can decide whether you want to have long hair in the Army or not. Remember to maintain proper hair care and grooming practices to comply with the Army's standards and maintain a professional appearance.
