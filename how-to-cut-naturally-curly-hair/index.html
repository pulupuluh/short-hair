How To Cut Naturally Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Description

If you have naturally curly hair, you may be hesitant to cut it for fear of ruining your curls. However, with the right technique and approach, cutting curly hair can enhance your natural texture and give your curls new life. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps of how to cut naturally curly hair, from preparing your hair for the cut to finishing touches.

How to Cut Naturally Curly Hair

Before you begin cutting your curly hair, it’s important to have the right tools on hand. You’ll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle with water

Once you have your tools, you’re ready to start cutting your curly hair. Here’s how:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with dry, detangled curls. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step 2: Section your hair. Use your wide-tooth comb to separate your hair into sections, starting with the bottom layer and working your way up. Secure each section with a clip.

Step 3: Start cutting. Begin with the bottom layer of your hair, taking small sections at a time. Use your scissors to cut each section, holding the hair between your fingers and cutting at an angle.

Step 4: Work your way up. Once you’ve finished the bottom layer, release the next section of hair and repeat the process. Continue working your way up until you’ve cut all of your hair.

Step 5: Check for balance. Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, take a step back and check for balance. If you notice any uneven sections, trim them as needed.

Tips for Cutting Naturally Curly Hair

Cutting curly hair can be tricky, but these tips can help you achieve a beautiful, natural-looking cut:

  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and result in uneven cuts.
  • Cut your hair dry. Cutting curly hair while it’s wet can lead to unexpected shrinkage once it dries.
  • Avoid cutting too much. When in doubt, cut less hair than you think you need to. You can always go back and trim more later.
  • Embrace your natural texture. Curly hair is beautiful, so don’t try to straighten it out too much.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about cutting naturally curly hair:

Can I cut my curly hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair yourself if you feel comfortable doing so. However, if you’re unsure, it’s always a good idea to consult with a professional stylist.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It’s recommended to cut curly hair every 8-12 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Should I cut my curly hair when it’s wet or dry?

It’s best to cut curly hair when it’s dry to avoid unexpected shrinkage once it dries.

How to Care for Naturally Curly Hair

After you’ve cut your curly hair, it’s important to take care of it to keep it healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for caring for naturally curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair, as it can break up your curls and cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls moisturized and defined.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, which can damage your hair and cause frizz.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your naturally curly hair and enjoy beautiful, healthy curls. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and embrace your natural texture for the best results.
