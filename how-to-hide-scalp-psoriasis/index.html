How To Hide Scalp Psoriasis Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline Mar 27, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you struggling with scalp psoriasis and looking for ways to hide it? You’re not alone! Psoriasis is a common skin condition that affects many people, causing red, scaly patches on the skin. When it affects the scalp, it can be especially challenging to hide. But don’t worry, in this article, we’ll share some simple tips and tricks to help you cover up your scalp psoriasis and feel more confident.

What is Scalp Psoriasis?

Scalp psoriasis is a skin condition that causes red, scaly patches on the scalp. It can be itchy, painful, and embarrassing, especially if it’s visible. Scalp psoriasis is a chronic condition, which means it can come and go throughout your lifetime. While there is no cure for psoriasis, there are ways to manage the symptoms and reduce flare-ups.

How to Hide Scalp Psoriasis

1. Use a Hat or Headscarf

One of the easiest ways to hide scalp psoriasis is to wear a hat or headscarf. This can help cover up the affected areas and protect your scalp from the sun. Choose a hat or scarf that fits comfortably and doesn’t irritate your scalp. You can also experiment with different styles and colors to find what works best for you.

2. Try a Hairstyle that Covers Your Scalp

If you’re not a fan of hats or scarves, you can try a hairstyle that covers your scalp. For example, you could wear your hair down, opt for a side part, or try a messy bun or low ponytail. These styles can help cover up the affected areas and make you feel more comfortable.

3. Use Hair Accessories

Hair accessories can be your best friend when it comes to hiding scalp psoriasis. Headbands, hair clips, and barrettes can all help cover up the affected areas and add some style to your hair. Just make sure that the accessories don’t irritate your scalp or pull on your hair too much.

4. Use a Scalp Concealer

Scalp concealers are specially designed to cover up psoriasis and other scalp conditions. They come in different forms, such as sprays, powders, and creams, and can be applied directly to the affected areas. Just make sure to choose a product that matches your hair color and won’t irritate your scalp.

Step by Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to hiding scalp psoriasis:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo that won’t irritate your scalp. Use lukewarm water and avoid hot water, as it can make psoriasis worse. Rinse thoroughly and pat your hair dry with a towel.

Step 2: Apply Scalp Concealer

Next, apply a scalp concealer to the affected areas. Use a small amount and apply it evenly, blending it in with your fingers or a brush. Make sure to choose a product that matches your hair color.

Step 3: Style Your Hair

Once the concealer is dry, style your hair as desired. Try a hairstyle that covers your scalp, such as a messy bun or low ponytail, or use hair accessories to add some flair.

Step 4: Protect Your Scalp

Finally, protect your scalp from the sun and other irritants. Wear a hat or scarf when outdoors, and avoid using harsh hair products or styling tools that can make psoriasis worse.

Tips for Managing Scalp Psoriasis

In addition to hiding scalp psoriasis, there are some tips you can follow to manage the condition:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Choose a gentle shampoo that won’t irritate your scalp. Look for products that are free of sulfates, fragrances, and other harsh ingredients.

2. Avoid Hot Water

Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can make psoriasis worse. Stick to lukewarm water instead.

3. Moisturize Your Scalp

Use a moisturizer or oil to keep your scalp hydrated and prevent dryness. Look for products that are specifically designed for psoriasis.

4. Don’t Scratch Your Scalp

As tempting as it may be, avoid scratching your scalp, as it can worsen psoriasis and lead to infection. Instead, try using a cool, wet cloth or a medicated lotion to soothe the itch.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Scalp Psoriasis be Cured?

There is no cure for scalp psoriasis, but there are ways to manage the symptoms and reduce flare-ups. Talk to your doctor about treatment options.

Is Scalp Psoriasis Contagious?

No, scalp psoriasis is not contagious. It is a non-infectious skin condition.

Can Scalp Psoriasis Cause Hair Loss?

Scalp psoriasis can cause temporary hair loss, but it usually grows back once the condition is under control.

Hair Care for Scalp Psoriasis

When you have scalp psoriasis, it’s important to take extra care of your hair. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid Harsh Hair Products

Avoid using harsh hair products that can irritate your scalp, such as hairspray, gel, or mousse. Stick to gentle, fragrance-free products instead.

2. Use a Soft Brush or Comb

Use a soft brush or comb to avoid irritating your scalp. Avoid using a hairbrush with sharp bristles or a fine-tooth comb, as they can scratch your scalp.

3. Be Gentle When Styling Your Hair

Be gentle when styling your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair too much. This can worsen psoriasis and lead to hair loss.

With these tips and tricks, you can easily hide scalp psoriasis and feel more confident in your appearance. Remember to take care of your scalp and hair, and talk to your doctor about treatment options if you’re experiencing severe symptoms.
