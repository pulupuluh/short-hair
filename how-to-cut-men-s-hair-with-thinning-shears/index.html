How To Cut Men s Hair With Thinning Shears

Written by Aprilia Aug 13, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're looking for a way to thin out your hair without losing too much length, then thinning shears might be the answer. Thinning shears are scissors that have teeth or notches on one side, while the other side is a smooth blade. They can be used to remove bulk from hair, create texture, and blend layers. In this article, we'll show you how to cut men's hair with thinning shears, step by step.

How to

Step 1:

Start by washing and drying the hair. It's important to work with clean, dry hair so you can see the natural texture and any areas that need to be thinned out.

Step 2:

Comb the hair straight down from the crown. This will help you see how the hair falls naturally and where it needs to be thinned out.

Step 3:

Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the thinning shears to cut into the hair, starting at the halfway point and working your way down to the ends.

Step 4:

Continue to work in small sections, moving around the head until you've thinned out all the hair that needs it. Don't overdo it – you can always go back and thin out more later if needed.

Step 5:

Once you've finished thinning out the hair, use regular scissors to trim any areas that need it. This will help create a more polished look and remove any stray hairs.

Tips

  • Use a comb to section off the hair before you start cutting. This will help you work in a more organized way and prevent you from accidentally cutting too much hair.
  • Always start with a small section of hair and work your way up. You can always thin out more if needed, but it's harder to add hair back once it's been cut.
  • Hold the thinning shears at an angle when cutting to create a more subtle, natural look.
  • Don't use thinning shears on wet hair – this can cause the hair to become too thin and lose its natural shape.

FAQ

How often should I use thinning shears on my hair?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. If you have thick, fast-growing hair, you may need to use thinning shears every 6-8 weeks. If your hair is thinner or grows more slowly, you may only need to use them every 3-4 months.

Can I use thinning shears on curly hair?

Yes, but be careful not to overdo it. Curly hair tends to be more fragile than straight hair, so use thinning shears sparingly and avoid cutting into the curls themselves.

Can I use thinning shears on my beard?

Yes, but again, be careful not to overdo it. Use the thinning shears to remove bulk from the beard, but avoid cutting into the actual hairs.

Hair Care

After using thinning shears, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair – this can cause damage and breakage. If you need to use heat, use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet to promote hair growth and strength.

With these tips and techniques, you can use thinning shears to create a stylish, modern look that's easy to maintain. Happy cutting!
