Haircut Straight Across Vs Layers Which One Should You Choose

Written by Petter Sep 12, 2023 Â· 6 min read

When it comes to getting a haircut, there are a lot of different options to choose from. Two of the most popular styles are the straight across cut and the layered cut. While both styles can look great, there are some key differences you should consider before making your decision.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

A straight across cut is exactly what it sounds like - the hair is cut straight across at the same length all the way around. This style is great for people who want a simple, low-maintenance look. Layers, on the other hand, involve cutting the hair at different lengths to create depth and movement. This style is great for people who want a more textured, voluminous look.

How to

The best way to decide which style to choose is to consider your hair type and face shape. Straight across cuts work well on people with thin or fine hair, as they can create the illusion of fuller, thicker hair. They also work well on people with round faces, as they can help to elongate the face. Layers, on the other hand, work well on people with thick or curly hair, as they can help to tame frizz and create definition. They also work well on people with long or oval faces, as they can help to frame the face.

Step by step

If you've decided to go for a straight across cut, the process is fairly simple. Here are the steps:

  • Wash your hair and let it dry naturally, or blow dry it straight.
  • Section your hair into four parts, using clips to hold each section in place.
  • Cut each section straight across, making sure to keep the length consistent.
  • Once you've finished cutting, style your hair as desired.

If you've decided to go for layers, the process is a bit more involved. Here are the steps:

  • Wash your hair and let it dry naturally, or blow dry it straight.
  • Section your hair into four parts, using clips to hold each section in place.
  • Starting with the bottom section, cut small sections of hair at an angle to create layers.
  • Continue cutting each section at an angle, working your way up to the top section.
  • Once you've finished cutting, style your hair as desired.

Tips

Whether you choose a straight across cut or layers, there are a few tips you should keep in mind:

  • Make sure to use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once - it's better to start small and work your way up.
  • Consider getting your hair cut by a professional stylist to ensure the best results.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and vibrant.

FAQ

Q: Will a straight across cut make my hair look flat?

A: Not necessarily. If you have thin or fine hair, a straight across cut can actually create the illusion of fuller, thicker hair.

Q: Will layers make my hair look too thin?

A: Not if they're done correctly. Layers can actually help to create the illusion of thicker, more voluminous hair.

Hair Care

No matter which style you choose, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a good quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle wet hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

Ultimately, the decision between a straight across cut and layers comes down to personal preference and individual style. Whichever you choose, make sure you feel confident and comfortable with your new look.
