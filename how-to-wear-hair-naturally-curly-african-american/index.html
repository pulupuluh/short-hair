How To Wear Hair Naturally Curly African American

Written by Bardi Jun 10, 2023 · 6 min read

As an African American with naturally curly hair, it can be challenging to find the right products and styling techniques to make your hair look its best. However, with the right care and attention, you can rock your natural curls and feel confident in your hair every day. In this post, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to wear hair naturally curly African American.

Table of Contents

  • How to Wear Hair Naturally Curly African American
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Styling Naturally Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Naturally Curly African American Hair

How to Wear Hair Naturally Curly African American

The first step to styling your natural curls is to embrace them! One of the biggest mistakes people make with curly hair is trying to straighten it or make it conform to a particular style. Instead, work with your hair's natural texture and find a style that accentuates your curls.

There are many different ways to wear naturally curly African American hair, from loose waves to tight coils. Here are some popular styles:

  • Bantu knots
  • Braids or twists
  • Pineapple updo
  • Wash and go
  • Crown braid

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to achieving a wash and go style:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, which is especially damaging for curly hair.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair while it's still wet. This will help to define your curls and keep them hydrated.
  3. Apply a styling product like a curl cream or gel. Use a generous amount and rake it through your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly.
  4. Scrunch your hair with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to encourage curl formation.
  5. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting.

Tips for Styling Naturally Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when styling your naturally curly African American hair:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet, as this will cause less breakage than using a brush.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Invest in a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz while you sleep.
  • Be gentle with your hair and avoid pulling or tugging on it too much, as this can cause breakage and damage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about styling naturally curly African American hair:

Q: Should I use a diffuser attachment on my blow dryer?

A: Yes, a diffuser attachment can help to dry your hair without causing frizz or disrupting your curls.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how dry or oily your scalp is. Some people with curly hair can go several days between washes, while others may need to wash their hair every other day.

Hair Care for Naturally Curly African American Hair

Caring for your naturally curly African American hair is essential to maintaining healthy, beautiful curls. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or applying a leave-in conditioner with SPF.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and techniques, you can wear your naturally curly African American hair with confidence and style.
