How To Big Chop Your Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 15, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're ready to embrace your natural hair texture and want to start fresh, a big chop might be just what you need. But going to a salon can be expensive, and you might not feel comfortable trusting someone else with such a big change. That's why we've put together this step-by-step guide to help you big chop your own hair at home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Big Chop Your Own Hair
  • Tips for a Successful Big Chop
  • FAQ About Big Chopping
  • Hair Care After the Big Chop

How to Big Chop Your Own Hair

Before you begin, gather your supplies. You'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Mirror
  • Trash bag or newspaper to catch hair

Now, follow these steps:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. If you have any products in your hair, wash them out and let your hair air dry.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair ties or clips to keep each section separate.
  3. Starting with one section, use the scissors to cut off as much hair as you're comfortable with. It's okay to start small and go shorter if you want.
  4. Repeat with the other three sections, cutting each one to a similar length.
  5. If you want an even shorter cut, you can use clippers to buzz the sides or back of your head.
  6. Once you're happy with the length, use the comb to check for any uneven spots and trim as needed.
  7. Step back and admire your new look!

Congratulations, you've just big chopped your own hair! But before you go out and show it off, there are a few tips you should keep in mind.

Tips for a Successful Big Chop

Here are some things to keep in mind when big chopping your hair:

  • Start with clean, dry hair for the most accurate length.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. It can be hard to cut the back of your head on your own.
  • Start small and work your way up to a shorter length if you're nervous.
  • Know that your hair will grow back, so don't stress too much if you're not in love with your new look right away.
  • Enjoy the freedom of having short hair and experiment with new styles!

FAQ About Big Chopping

Here are some frequently asked questions about big chopping:

  • Will my hair grow back the same texture? Your natural hair texture will grow back, but it may take time for it to fully show.
  • Will big chopping damage my hair? No, cutting your hair won't damage it. In fact, it can be a healthy way to start fresh and get rid of any damaged ends.
  • Do I need to cut off all of my hair to big chop? No, you can big chop to any length you're comfortable with. Some people choose to cut off all of their hair, while others prefer a shorter cut that still leaves some length.

Hair Care After the Big Chop

After you've big chopped, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and promote growth. Here are some tips:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair at night by sleeping on a satin pillowcase or wearing a satin bonnet.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of any split ends.
  • Experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for your hair.

Now that you know how to big chop your own hair, you're ready to take the plunge and embrace your natural texture. Remember, it's just hair and it will grow back!
