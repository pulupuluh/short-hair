How To Cut Your Hair By Yourself Brad Mondo Style

Written by William May 14, 2023 · 7 min read

Cutting your own hair might sound daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be an easy and affordable way to keep your locks looking fresh. In this blog post, we’ll show you how to cut your hair by yourself Brad Mondo style, using his expert tips and tricks for a salon-worthy cut at home.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To Cut Your Hair By Yourself Brad Mondo Style
  • Step By Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting

Description

Brad Mondo is a celebrity hairstylist and YouTube personality known for his bold and creative hair transformations. He has over six million subscribers on his channel, where he shares tips and tutorials on all things hair. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut your own hair using Brad Mondo’s techniques, so you can achieve a professional-looking cut at home.

How to Cut Your Hair By Yourself Brad Mondo Style

Before you start cutting your hair, you’ll need to gather the right tools. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors or shears
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror

Once you have your tools, you’ll need to decide on the type of cut you want. You can find inspiration for different hairstyles on Brad Mondo’s YouTube channel or by browsing online. Once you’ve decided on your desired style, it’s time to get started!

Step By Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your hair by yourself Brad Mondo style:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Use a comb to detangle your hair, and section it off into manageable pieces using hair ties or clips.
  2. Decide on the length you want to cut. Brad Mondo recommends starting with a longer length and gradually cutting more if needed.
  3. Use your scissors or shears to cut small sections of hair at a time. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across, making sure to keep the hair taut.
  4. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you’ve reached your desired length.
  5. For layers, take small sections of hair and hold them at a 45-degree angle away from your head. Cut into the hair at an angle to create layers.
  6. If you’re using clippers, start with a longer guard and gradually work your way down to a shorter length. Use the clippers to trim the sides and back of your head.
  7. When you’re finished cutting, shake out your hair and use a mirror to check for any uneven spots. Make any necessary touch-ups.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for cutting your hair by yourself Brad Mondo style:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Invest in high-quality scissors or shears to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Use a comb to make sure your hair is taut before cutting.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as this can cause it to appear shorter than it actually is.
  • If you’re unsure about cutting your own hair, start with small trims and work your way up to more drastic changes.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your hair by yourself Brad Mondo style:

Is it possible to cut my own hair?

Yes, it’s possible to cut your own hair with the right tools and techniques. Brad Mondo’s YouTube channel is a great resource for learning how to cut your hair at home.

What tools do I need to cut my own hair?

You’ll need sharp scissors or shears, clippers (optional), a comb, hair ties or clips, and a mirror.

Can I cut my hair when it’s wet?

No, it’s best to cut your hair when it’s dry to avoid any uneven spots.

Hair Care After Cutting

After you’ve cut your hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain a healthy appearance. Brad Mondo recommends using a high-quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible. You should also schedule regular trims to keep your hair looking its best.

Now that you know how to cut your hair by yourself Brad Mondo style, you can save time and money by skipping the salon and cutting your own hair at home. Just remember to take your time, start with small sections, and follow Brad’s tips and tricks for a professional-looking cut!
