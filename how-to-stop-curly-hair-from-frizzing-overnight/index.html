How To Stop Curly Hair From Frizzing Overnight

Written by William May 15, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that frizz is the ultimate enemy. It can ruin your perfectly styled curls and make you look like you just rolled out of bed. One of the worst things about frizz is that it can happen overnight, leaving you with a head full of unruly hair in the morning. But fear not, there are ways to prevent your curly hair from frizzing while you sleep. Here's how:

Table of Contents

  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

Preventing frizz starts with your nighttime routine. Here are some tips to help you keep your curls under control:

1. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent frizz by reducing friction between your hair and the pillowcase. Silk is also less absorbent than cotton, so it won't soak up all of the moisture from your hair.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Before you go to bed, apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair. This will help keep your curls hydrated and prevent them from drying out and frizzing overnight.

3. Tie Your Hair Up

Try tying your hair up in a loose bun or pineapple on top of your head. This will help prevent your curls from getting flattened while you sleep.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to preventing frizz overnight:

Step 1: Apply Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair before you go to bed. You can use a spray or a cream, depending on your preference.

Step 2: Tie Your Hair Up

Tie your hair up in a loose bun or pineapple on top of your head. Use a scrunchie or a soft hair tie to avoid leaving any creases in your hair.

Step 3: Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleep on a silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent your curls from getting tangled and frizzy.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you prevent frizz:

1. Use a Diffuser

If you blow dry your hair, use a diffuser to help define your curls and prevent frizz.

2. Don't Touch Your Hair

Try not to touch your hair too much throughout the day. The oils from your hands can transfer to your hair and cause it to frizz.

3. Use a Microfiber Towel

When you're drying your hair, use a microfiber towel instead of a regular towel. Microfiber towels are gentler on your hair and won't cause as much friction.

FAQ

Q: Can I use a regular pillowcase?

A: While you can use a regular pillowcase, it's not recommended if you're trying to prevent frizz. Cotton pillowcases can cause friction and absorb moisture from your hair, which can lead to frizz.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with curly hair find that washing their hair once or twice a week is enough, while others prefer to wash their hair more frequently.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for preventing frizz. Here are some tips to help you take care of your curly hair:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for curly hair. Look for products that are moisturizing and contain ingredients like shea butter or coconut oil.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning can help keep your curls hydrated and prevent them from drying out and frizzing. Try to deep condition your hair at least once a week.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat can be damaging to curly hair and cause it to frizz. Try to avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners as much as possible.

By following these tips, you can prevent frizz and wake up with beautiful, defined curls every morning.
