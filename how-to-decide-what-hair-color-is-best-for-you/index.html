How To Decide What Hair Color Is Best For You

Written by Daniel May 30, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're considering changing your hair color, it's important to choose the shade that best complements your skin tone, eye color, and lifestyle. In this article, we'll provide you with tips on how to decide what hair color is best for you, and answer some frequently asked questions about hair coloring. We'll also give you some advice on how to care for your newly colored hair.

Table of Contents

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in deciding what hair color is best for you is to determine your skin tone. Your skin tone can be warm, cool, or neutral. A warm skin tone has a yellow or golden undertone, while a cool skin tone has a pink or blue undertone. Neutral skin tones have a balance of warm and cool undertones.

Once you've determined your skin tone, you can choose a hair color that complements it. If you have a warm skin tone, consider shades of red, gold, or caramel. If you have a cool skin tone, try shades of ash or platinum blonde, or cool brown hues like espresso or black. Neutral skin tones can pull off a wide range of colors.

Step 2: Consider Your Natural Hair Color

Your natural hair color can also play a role in deciding what shade to choose. If you have dark hair, you may want to consider going a few shades lighter to add dimension. If you have light hair, you can experiment with darker shades or highlights.

It's also important to choose a color that will complement your eye color. For example, if you have blue eyes, warm hues like honey or caramel can bring out the blue in your eyes. If you have green eyes, try shades of red or copper. Brown eyes can pull off a wide range of colors, but shades of chocolate or warm chestnut can be particularly flattering.

Step 3: Think About Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle can also influence your hair color choice. If you have a busy schedule or don't want to spend a lot of time and money on salon appointments, consider a low-maintenance color like balayage or ombre. If you're in a more professional setting, you may want to stick with natural-looking shades.

If you're feeling adventurous, you can experiment with bold colors like pastel pink or blue. Keep in mind that these shades may require more maintenance and touch-ups.

Step 4: Consult a Professional

If you're still unsure about what hair color is best for you, consult a professional stylist. They can help you determine your skin tone, natural hair color, and lifestyle, and recommend a shade that will look great on you. They can also give you advice on how to care for your newly colored hair.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Color

  • Consider your skin tone and natural hair color when choosing a shade
  • Think about your lifestyle and how much maintenance you're willing to do
  • Consult a professional if you're unsure
  • Try on wigs or use virtual try-on tools to get a sense of how a shade will look on you
  • Take into account any allergies or sensitivities you may have to hair dye

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions About Hair Coloring

Q: Will coloring my hair damage it?

A: Coloring your hair can cause some damage, but using high-quality products and getting regular trims can help minimize it.

Q: How often will I need to touch up my roots?

A: This depends on the color you choose and how fast your hair grows. Generally, root touch-ups are needed every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Can I dye my hair at home?

A: Yes, but it's important to follow the instructions carefully and use high-quality products. If you're unsure, consult a professional.

Hair Care Tips for Colored Hair

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to preserve color
  • Avoid washing your hair every day to prevent fading
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep hair healthy
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine

By following these tips and steps, you can choose a hair color that will flatter your skin tone, natural hair color, and lifestyle. Whether you opt for a subtle change or a bold new look, make sure to take good care of your newly colored locks to keep them looking their best.
