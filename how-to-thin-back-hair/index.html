How To Thin Back Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning back hair can be a challenging task, especially if you don't know how to do it correctly. However, don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will give you a step-by-step guide on how to thin back hair in the most effective way possible.

How to Thin Back Hair

Before we begin, let's start with the basics. Thinning hair refers to the process of reducing the thickness of your hair, particularly in certain areas. It's a popular technique used by many people with thick hair to make it more manageable and easier to style. Here are some steps you can follow to thin back hair:

Step 1: Get the Right Tools

Before you start thinning your hair, ensure that you have the right tools. You will need a pair of hair thinning scissors and a comb. Hair thinning scissors have a unique design that allows them to cut hair at different lengths. They are perfect for creating layers and reducing hair volume without affecting hair length.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any dirt or product buildup. Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and easy to comb through.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Using a comb, divide your hair into sections. Start by separating the back portion of your hair from the rest of your hair. Secure the remaining hair with a hair tie or clip.

Step 4: Thin Your Hair

Take a small section of your hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the thinning scissors to cut away some of the hair from the mid-lengths to the ends. Work your way through each section of your hair, cutting more or less depending on how much hair you want to remove.

Step 5: Blend Your Hair

Once you have thinned out your hair, use the comb to blend the layers together. Comb your hair from the roots to the ends to ensure that there are no visible lines or unevenness.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Now that you've thinned out your hair, it's time to style it. You can use a hairdryer and a brush to create volume or use a straightener to create a sleek and smooth look.

Tips for Thinning Back Hair

Here are some helpful tips to keep in mind when thinning back hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and gradually work your way through each section.
  • Be careful not to cut too much hair at once. You can always go back and thin it out more if needed.
  • Don't thin your hair too much, or it may become too thin and look unnatural.
  • Use hair-thinning scissors specifically designed for the task to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Consider consulting a professional hairdresser if you're not confident in your abilities.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I thin my hair at home?

Yes, you can thin your hair at home using hair-thinning scissors. However, if you're not confident in your abilities, it's best to consult a professional hairdresser.

How often should I thin my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to thin your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care after Thinning Back Hair

After thinning your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage it further.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair nourished and hydrated.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.

Thinning back hair can be a great way to make your hair more manageable and easier to style. By following these steps, you can thin your hair at home and achieve the perfect look. Remember to take care of your hair after thinning it to keep it healthy and looking great.
