Best Way To Thin Out Thick Curly Hair A Complete Guide

Written by William Jun 14, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thick curly hair can be a blessing, but it can also be a curse. While it's great to have a full head of hair, it can be challenging to manage, especially when you want to create a sleek, polished look. Thinning out your hair can help you achieve this look, but it's essential to do it correctly. In this article, we'll discuss the best way to thin out thick curly hair, including how to do it, step by step, tips, FAQs, and hair care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Thin Out Thick Curly Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Thinning Out Thick Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

How to Thin Out Thick Curly Hair

The best way to thin out thick curly hair is by using thinning shears. Thinning shears are scissors that have one straight blade and one serrated blade. The serrated blade cuts hair at different lengths, which helps to thin it out. You can use thinning shears on damp or dry hair, but it's best to use them on dry hair.

What You'll Need

To thin out your hair, you'll need the following:

  • Thinning shears
  • Comb
  • Clips

Step by Step Guide

Here's how to thin out your hair using thinning shears:

  1. Wash and dry your hair
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles
  3. Section your hair into small sections using clips
  4. Take one section of hair and comb it straight
  5. Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle and cut the hair halfway up the strand
  6. Repeat steps 4-5 on all sections of your hair
  7. Comb your hair to check if you've thinned it out evenly
  8. Style your hair as desired

It's essential to be careful when using thinning shears as they can easily damage your hair. Make sure you don't cut too much hair at once, and always start with a small amount. You can always thin out more hair later if needed.

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up
  • Use sharp thinning shears to avoid damaging your hair
  • Don't thin out your hair too often as it can damage your hair
  • Make sure your hair is dry before using thinning shears
  • Don't use thinning shears on the top layer of your hair as it can create a choppy look

FAQs

Can I thin out my hair without using thinning shears?

Yes, you can use a razor or scissors to thin out your hair, but it's best to use thinning shears as they are specifically designed for this purpose.

How often should I thin out my hair?

You should only thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks to avoid damaging your hair.

Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner?

No, thinning out your hair will not make it look thinner. It will only remove some of the bulk, making it easier to manage and style.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

Thick curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry as it can cause breakage

By following these tips, you can keep your thick curly hair looking its best.

Wrap Up

Thinning out thick curly hair can be a great way to achieve a sleek, polished look. By using thinning shears and following the step-by-step guide, tips, and hair care advice in this article, you can thin out your hair without damaging it. Remember to be careful when using thinning shears and only thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and looking its best.
