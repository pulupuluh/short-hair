What To Do When Your Hair Is Shedding A Lot

Introduction

It is normal to lose some hair every day, but when hair shedding becomes excessive, it can be a cause for concern. Excessive hair shedding can lead to thinning hair and even baldness if not addressed promptly. In this article, we will discuss what causes hair shedding and provide tips on how to deal with it.

Causes of Hair Shedding

There are several reasons why your hair may be shedding excessively. One of the most common causes is stress. When you are stressed, your body produces hormones that can lead to hair loss. Other causes of hair shedding include:

  • Genetics
  • Pregnancy
  • Postpartum
  • Menopause
  • Iron deficiency
  • Thyroid problems
  • Autoimmune diseases
  • Chemotherapy

How to Deal with Hair Shedding

If you are experiencing excessive hair shedding, there are several things you can do to deal with it. Here are some tips:

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Consult a doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions that may be causing your hair shedding.

2. Use a wide-toothed comb when detangling your hair. This will help to prevent breakage and minimize hair shedding.

3. Be gentle when washing your hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

4. Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons. These tools can cause damage to your hair, leading to breakage and hair shedding.

5. Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals. This will help to promote healthy hair growth and minimize hair shedding.

Tips to Prevent Hair Shedding

1. Avoid wearing tight hairstyles such as braids and ponytails. These styles can pull on your hair, leading to breakage and hair shedding.

2. Use a satin or silk pillowcase when sleeping. These fabrics are gentler on your hair and can help to prevent breakage and hair shedding.

3. Massage your scalp regularly. This will help to increase blood flow to your scalp and promote healthy hair growth.

4. Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair such as relaxers and dyes. These chemicals can damage your hair, leading to breakage and hair shedding.

FAQs

Q: How much hair shedding is considered normal?

A: On average, it is normal to lose between 50-100 hairs per day.

Q: Can stress cause hair shedding?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair shedding by disrupting the hair growth cycle.

Q: Can hair shedding be prevented?

A: While some hair shedding is normal, there are steps you can take to minimize it. These include eating a healthy diet, avoiding harsh chemicals, and being gentle when washing and combing your hair.

Hair Care for Healthy Hair

Healthy hair starts with a healthy scalp. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly to remove dirt and oil buildup.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair such as relaxers and dyes.
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to increase blood flow and promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips, you can help to minimize hair shedding and promote healthy hair growth.
