How To Cut Short Layers On Top Of Long Hair

Written by Frank Apr 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have long hair and want to add some movement and dimension to your look, cutting short layers on top of your long hair is a great option. This technique creates volume and texture, making your hair look fuller and more dynamic. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting short layers on top of long hair.

How to Cut Short Layers on Top of Long Hair

To cut short layers on top of long hair, you will need a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Large mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut short layers on top of long hair:

  1. Begin by washing your hair and letting it air dry. This will make it easier to work with and will give you a better idea of how your hair naturally falls.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles, and then part it down the middle.
  3. Take a section of hair from the top of your head and clip the rest of your hair out of the way. This section should be about 1-2 inches wide and run from your hairline to the crown of your head.
  4. Hold the section of hair straight up and use your scissors to cut into the hair, creating the first layer. Make sure to cut at an angle to create a soft, blended effect.
  5. Repeat this process with the remaining sections of hair on the top of your head, working from front to back. Each section should be slightly shorter than the one before it, creating a graduated effect.
  6. Once you have finished cutting the top layers, unclip the rest of your hair and blend the layers into the length of your hair. You can do this by cutting into the ends of your hair, creating a soft, feathered effect.
  7. Check your work in the mirror, making any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Short Layers on Top of Long Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting short layers on top of long hair:

  • Make sure your hair is completely dry before you begin cutting it.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections as you become more comfortable with the process.
  • Use a comb to guide your scissors as you cut, making sure to cut at an angle for a blended effect.
  • Take your time, and don't rush the process. It's better to cut a little at a time than to cut too much and have to start over.
  • If you're not confident in your ability to cut your own hair, consider visiting a professional stylist.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short layers on top of long hair:

Q: Will cutting short layers on top of long hair make my hair look thinner?
A: No, cutting short layers on top of long hair can actually make your hair look fuller and more voluminous. Q: How often should I cut my hair if I have short layers on top of long hair?
A: It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style of your hair. Q: Can I cut short layers on top of curly hair?
A: Yes, you can cut short layers on top of curly hair, but it's important to work with a stylist who is experienced with cutting curly hair to achieve the best results.

Hair Care After Cutting Short Layers on Top of Long Hair

Once you have cut short layers on top of your long hair, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray.

Now that you know how to cut short layers on top of long hair, you can give your hair a fresh, new look. Happy cutting!
