How To Maintain Your Lace Front Wig Hair Care Tips

Written by Luffy Jun 14, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you a fan of lace front wigs? Whether you wear them for fashion or to protect your natural hair, it’s important to know how to maintain them properly. In this article, we’ll share some tips on how to keep your lace front wig in tip-top shape.

Table of Contents

  • How to Maintain Your Lace Front Wig
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Lace Front Wig
  • FAQs about Lace Front Wig Maintenance

How to Maintain Your Lace Front Wig

Maintaining your lace front wig is crucial to ensure it lasts for a long time. Here are some steps you can take to keep it looking fresh and new:

1. Brushing and Detangling

Before and after wearing your lace front wig, use a wide-tooth comb or wig brush to gently detangle it. Always start from the ends and work your way up to avoid damaging the hair fibers.

2. Washing and Conditioning

Wash your lace front wig every two to three weeks with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Rinse the wig thoroughly with lukewarm water and avoid rubbing or wringing it. Pat it dry with a clean towel and let it air dry on a wig stand.

3. Styling

Avoid using excessive heat on your lace front wig, as it can damage the fibers. Instead, use a low-heat setting on your styling tools and always apply a heat protectant spray before styling.

4. Storage

When not in use, store your lace front wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to maintain its shape. Keep it away from direct sunlight and heat sources to prevent damage.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you’re new to wearing lace front wigs, here’s a step-by-step guide to maintaining them:

Step 1: Detangle the Wig

Before washing your lace front wig, detangle it gently with a wide-tooth comb or wig brush.

Step 2: Wash the Wig

Fill a sink or basin with lukewarm water and add a small amount of sulfate-free shampoo. Submerge the wig in the water and swish it around gently. Rinse the wig thoroughly with lukewarm water.

Step 3: Condition the Wig

Apply a small amount of conditioner to the wig, focusing on the ends. Let it sit for a few minutes, then rinse it out with lukewarm water.

Step 4: Dry the Wig

Pat the wig dry with a clean towel and let it air dry on a wig stand. Avoid using a hair dryer or other heat sources to dry the wig.

Tips for Maintaining Your Lace Front Wig

Here are some additional tips to help you maintain your lace front wig:

  • Use a wig cap to protect your natural hair and scalp.
  • Avoid sleeping in your wig, as it can cause tangles and matting.
  • Store your wig in a cool, dry place to prevent mold and mildew.
  • Use a wig spray or serum to add shine and moisture to your wig.
  • Don’t wear your wig in chlorinated or saltwater, as it can damage the fibers.

FAQs about Lace Front Wig Maintenance

Here are some frequently asked questions about maintaining lace front wigs:

How often should I wash my lace front wig?

You should wash your lace front wig every two to three weeks to keep it clean and fresh.

Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my wig?

No, you should use sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for wigs to avoid damaging the fibers.

Can I dye my lace front wig?

Yes, you can dye your lace front wig, but it’s best to have it done professionally to avoid damaging the fibers.

Hair Care for Lace Front Wig Wearers

Wearing a lace front wig can be a great way to protect your natural hair and switch up your look. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair while wearing a wig:

  • Wear a wig cap to protect your natural hair and scalp.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or oil.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause tension on your hairline.
  • Take breaks from wearing your wig to give your natural hair a chance to breathe.
  • Eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips, you can maintain your lace front wig and keep your natural hair healthy and strong. Whether you wear wigs for fashion or protection, taking care of them properly is key to prolonging their lifespan.
