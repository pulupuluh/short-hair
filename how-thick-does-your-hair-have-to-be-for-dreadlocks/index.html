How Thick Does Your Hair Have To Be For Dreadlocks

Written by Robby Jun 21, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. It's a style that is often associated with the Rastafarian culture and is popular among people of all races, genders, and ages. One of the most common questions people ask when considering getting dreadlocks is, "how thick does your hair have to be for dreadlocks?"

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

The thickness of your hair is an important factor when it comes to getting dreadlocks. If your hair is too thin, it may not be able to hold the knots needed to create the dreads. On the other hand, if your hair is too thick, it may take longer to form the dreads and may be more difficult to maintain.

Ultimately, the thickness of your hair will depend on your individual hair type and texture. However, there are some general guidelines that can help you determine whether your hair is thick enough for dreadlocks.

How to

The easiest way to determine whether your hair is thick enough for dreadlocks is to consult with a professional hair stylist. They will be able to examine your hair and give you a better idea of whether your hair is suitable for dreads.

However, if you want to check for yourself, there are a few things you can do:

1. Check your hair thickness

Take a small section of your hair and twist it together. If it holds the twist without unraveling, your hair is likely thick enough for dreadlocks.

2. Check your hair texture

If you have curly or kinky hair, your hair may be suitable for dreadlocks even if it is not very thick. This is because curly and kinky hair tends to hold knots better than straight hair.

Step by step

If you have determined that your hair is thick enough for dreadlocks, you can start the process of creating them. Here are the basic steps:

1. Clean your hair

It's important to start with clean hair, as dirt and oil can make it more difficult to form the dreads. Shampoo and condition your hair thoroughly.

2. Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using a comb. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness and length of your hair.

3. Backcomb the sections

Starting at the roots, use a small-toothed comb to backcomb each section of hair. This will create knots that will eventually form into dreadlocks.

4. Twist and knot the hair

Once you have backcombed each section, twist and knot the hair, working your way down to the ends. Secure the ends with a rubber band or hair tie.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when creating dreadlocks:

  • Be patient - it can take several months for your hair to fully lock into dreadlocks.
  • Avoid using products that contain wax, as this can make it difficult to maintain and wash your dreadlocks.
  • Keep your scalp clean and moisturized to prevent itching and discomfort.
  • Consider using a specialized shampoo and conditioner for dreadlocks.

FAQ

Q. Can I get dreadlocks if my hair is thin?

A. It depends on the texture of your hair. If you have fine, straight hair, it may be more difficult to form dreadlocks. However, if you have curly or kinky hair, your hair may be suitable for dreadlocks even if it is not very thick.

Q. How long does it take for dreadlocks to form?

A. It can take several months for your hair to fully lock into dreadlocks, depending on the thickness and length of your hair.

Q. How do I maintain my dreadlocks?

A. Regularly wash and moisturize your scalp and hair, and avoid using products that contain wax. You may also need to retwist and tighten your dreadlocks periodically.

Hair Care

Once you have created your dreadlocks, it's important to take good care of them to keep them looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your scalp and hair regularly to remove dirt and oil.
  • Use a specialized shampoo and conditioner for dreadlocks.
  • Avoid using products that contain wax, as this can make it difficult to maintain and wash your dreadlocks.
  • Keep your scalp moisturized to prevent itching and discomfort.
  • Retwist and tighten your dreadlocks periodically to keep them looking neat and uniform.

In conclusion, there is no one-size-fits-all answer to the question of how thick your hair has to be for dreadlocks. It all depends on your individual hair type and texture. However, by following the tips and guidelines in this article, you can determine whether your hair is suitable for dreadlocks and take the necessary steps to create and maintain them.
