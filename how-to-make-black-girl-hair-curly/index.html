How To Make Black Girl Hair Curly A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Mar 19, 2023 Â· 10 min read

As a black girl, you might have naturally kinky or straight hair, but you can make it curly with the right techniques and products. Curly hair can add volume, texture, and personality to your look, and there are many ways to achieve it. In this article, we will show you how to make black girl hair curly in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language. You'll learn how to choose the right products, how to style your hair, and how to take care of your curls.

How to Make Black Girl Hair Curly

There are several methods to make black girl hair curly. The most common ones are:

  • Using a curling iron or wand
  • Using rollers or flexi-rods
  • Using a perm or relaxer
  • Using a twist-out or braid-out

Each method has its pros and cons, and the choice depends on your hair type, the desired curl pattern, and your styling skills. In this article, we will focus on the twist-out method, which is a popular and low-damage way to make black girl hair curly.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to make black girl hair curly with a twist-out:

  1. Start with clean and detangled hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or a curl cream to your hair. This will help to define your curls and keep them hydrated. You can also use a styling gel or mousse if you want more hold. Distribute the product evenly throughout your hair, using your fingers or a brush.
  3. Divide your hair into small sections. The size of the sections depends on the thickness and length of your hair. Use hair clips or bands to keep the sections separate.
  4. Take one section and twist it around your finger or a curling rod. Start from the roots and twist all the way to the ends. If you want tighter curls, twist the section more tightly. If you want looser curls, twist the section more loosely.
  5. Repeat step 4 for all the sections. Make sure that each section is twisted in the same direction, either clockwise or counterclockwise.
  6. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer. Avoid touching your hair while it's drying, as this can cause frizz and disrupt the curl pattern.
  7. Once your hair is completely dry, unravel the twists. Use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to separate the curls and create more volume. You can also use a hair pick or a afro comb to lift the roots and create more height.
  8. Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to your curls to add shine and reduce frizz. You can also use a hairspray or a styling cream to set the curls and keep them in place.

Tips for Making Black Girl Hair Curly

Here are some tips to help you make black girl hair curly:

  • Choose the right products for your hair type and texture. Experiment with different brands and formulations to find what works best for you.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and weaken the curls. If you must use a curling iron or wand, use a heat protectant spray and set the temperature to a low or medium setting.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin or silk bonnet or scarf. This will help to retain moisture and prevent frizz and tangles.
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage. This will also make your curls look more defined and healthy.
  • Stay hydrated and eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals that support hair growth and health. Drink plenty of water and eat foods like salmon, avocado, spinach, and eggs.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about making black girl hair curly:

  • Can I make black girl hair curly without using any products?
    It's possible to make black girl hair curly without using any products, but it will be harder to define and hold the curls. Products like leave-in conditioner, curl cream, and gel can help to moisturize your hair and enhance your natural curl pattern.
  • How long does a twist-out last?
    A twist-out can last for a few days to a week, depending on your hair type, the weather, and your styling routine. You can refresh your curls by spritzing them with water or a curl refresher spray and re-twisting them at night.
  • Can I use a perm or relaxer to make black girl hair curly?
    You can use a perm or relaxer to make black girl hair curly, but these methods can be more damaging and irreversible than a twist-out or other natural methods. If you want to use a perm or relaxer, make sure to go to a professional stylist and follow the instructions carefully.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some tips to take care of your curly hair:

  • Wash your hair with lukewarm or cool water, as hot water can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause frizz.
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos that contain sulfates, as they can dry out your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and nourished. You can use a store-bought deep conditioner or make your own with natural ingredients like avocado, honey, and coconut oil.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and disrupt the curl pattern. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet and coated with conditioner or oil.
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and humidity by wearing a hat or scarf. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or a curl cream with UV protection.

With these tips and techniques, you can make black girl hair curly and enjoy your beautiful and unique curls. Experiment with different styles and products, and don't be afraid to rock your natural texture. Curly hair is a symbol of diversity, resilience, and beauty, and you should embrace it with pride and confidence.
