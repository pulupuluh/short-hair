My Hair Fell Out After I Dyed It What Happened And How To Fix It

Written by Oliver Jun 11, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're reading this article, chances are you've recently experienced a hair disaster. You dyed your hair and now it's falling out, leaving you feeling frustrated, embarrassed, and hopeless. But don't worry, you're not alone. Many people have gone through the same thing, and there are ways to fix it. In this blog post, we'll discuss why your hair fell out after you dyed it, how to fix it, and how to prevent it from happening again.

Why Did My Hair Fall Out After I Dyed It?

There are several reasons why your hair may have fallen out after you dyed it:

1. Chemical Damage

One of the most common reasons for hair loss after dyeing is chemical damage. Dyeing your hair can cause the hair shaft to weaken and break, leading to hair loss. This is especially true if you've used a harsh dye or left the dye on for too long.

2. Allergic Reaction

Another reason for hair loss after dyeing is an allergic reaction to the dye. This is more common with permanent dyes, which contain more chemicals than semi-permanent or temporary dyes. If you're allergic to the dye, your scalp may become inflamed and itchy, leading to hair loss.

3. Overprocessing

If you've dyed your hair multiple times or used other chemical treatments, you may have overprocessed your hair. Overprocessing can cause the hair to become weak and brittle, leading to breakage and hair loss.

How to Fix Hair Loss After Dyeing

If your hair has already fallen out after dyeing, there are a few things you can do to help it grow back:

1. Stop Dyeing Your Hair

The first thing you should do is stop dyeing your hair. This will give your hair time to recover and grow back.

2. Use Gentle Hair Products

Use gentle hair products that won't further damage your hair. Look for products that are sulfate-free, paraben-free, and contain natural ingredients.

3. Take Supplements

Taking supplements like biotin, vitamin D, and iron can help promote hair growth and strengthen your hair.

How to Prevent Hair Loss After Dyeing

If you want to dye your hair in the future without experiencing hair loss, there are a few things you can do:

1. Choose a Gentle Dye

Choose a gentle dye that won't damage your hair. Look for dyes that are free of ammonia and peroxide.

2. Don't Overprocess Your Hair

Don't dye your hair too often or use other chemical treatments like perms or relaxers. These can weaken your hair and cause it to fall out.

3. Take Care of Your Hair

Take care of your hair by using gentle hair products, avoiding heat styling, and getting regular trims. This will keep your hair healthy and strong.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your hair healthy:

1. Wash Your Hair Less Often

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and breakage. Try washing your hair every other day or every few days instead.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush. This will prevent breakage and damage.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons. These can damage your hair and cause it to break.

FAQ

Q: Can hair loss after dyeing be permanent?

A: In some cases, hair loss after dyeing can be permanent. This is more likely if you've used a harsh dye or left the dye on for too long.

Q: Will my hair grow back after falling out from dyeing?

A: Yes, your hair should grow back after falling out from dyeing. It may take some time, but with the right care, your hair should recover.

Q: Can I dye my hair again after it falls out from dyeing?

A: It's best to wait until your hair has fully recovered before dyeing it again. This will give your hair time to strengthen and prevent further damage.

Remember, hair loss after dyeing is a common problem that can be fixed. By taking care of your hair and avoiding harsh chemicals, you can keep your hair healthy and strong.
