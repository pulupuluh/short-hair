How To Curl Really Long Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver May 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have long and thick hair, you know how challenging it can be to style it. While straight hair is lovely, there are times when you just want to add some curl and volume to your locks. Curls can give you a playful, romantic, or sophisticated look, depending on how you style them. But how do you curl really long and thick hair?

Table of Contents:

  • How to Curl Really Long Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Curling Long Thick Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Long Thick Hair

How to Curl Really Long Thick Hair

Before we dive into the details, let's talk about the tools you need to curl your hair. You will need a curling iron, heat protectant spray, hair clips, and hairspray. Make sure your curling iron has a barrel size of at least 1 inch or more, depending on the size of the curls you want.

Step 1: Wash and Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair, and then apply a heat protectant spray to your damp hair. This will protect your hair from heat damage and make it easier to style. Blow dry your hair or let it air dry to about 80% before styling.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward. The smaller the sections, the tighter the curls will be. If you want loose waves, make larger sections.

Step 3: Curl Your Hair

Take one section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron barrel. Hold the curling iron vertically and wrap the hair around the barrel away from your face. Hold for 10-15 seconds, and then release the curl. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 4: Set Your Curls

Once you have curled all of your hair, let it cool for a few minutes. Then, gently tousle your hair with your fingers to loosen the curls. Finish with a light mist of hairspray to set your curls in place.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide to help you curl your long thick hair:

  1. Wash and prep your hair with a heat protectant spray.
  2. Divide your hair into sections using hair clips.
  3. Take one section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron barrel, away from your face.
  4. Hold for 10-15 seconds, and then release the curl.
  5. Repeat this process for each section of hair.
  6. Let your curls cool for a few minutes.
  7. Gently tousle your hair with your fingers to loosen the curls.
  8. Finish with a light mist of hairspray to set your curls in place.

Tips for Curling Long Thick Hair

Here are some tips to help you curl your long thick hair:

  • Use a curling iron with a large barrel size to create loose waves.
  • Make smaller sections to create tighter curls.
  • Don't overheat your hair; use a lower heat setting and hold for a shorter amount of time.
  • Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Finish with a light mist of hairspray to set your curls in place.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about curling long thick hair:

Q: How do I prevent my curls from falling flat?

A: Use a hairspray with strong hold to keep your curls in place. You can also curl your hair in smaller sections and use a higher heat setting for longer-lasting curls.

Q: How often should I curl my hair?

A: Curling your hair too often can damage it. Ideally, you should only curl your hair once or twice a week. Use a deep conditioning treatment to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Hair Care for Long Thick Hair

Here are some tips to keep your long thick hair healthy and strong:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Brush your hair gently and avoid pulling or tugging on it.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and always use a heat protectant spray.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to nourish your hair from the inside out.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

Now that you know how to curl really long thick hair, you can experiment with different styles and looks. Remember to take care of your hair, and always use a heat protectant spray when styling with heat.
