How To Get Frizz Out Of Naturally Curly Hair

Written by Bardi Mar 21, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizzy hair every time you style your naturally curly locks? Frizz can be a frustrating issue for many curly-haired individuals, but fortunately, there are ways to combat it. In this article, we will explore how to get frizz out of naturally curly hair and provide some helpful tips and tricks for keeping your curls smooth and defined.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Get Frizz Out of Naturally Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Helpful Tips
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Frizzy Curls

How to Get Frizz Out of Naturally Curly Hair

Before we dive into the tips and tricks for getting rid of frizz, it's important to understand what causes frizz in the first place. Frizz occurs when the cuticle layer of the hair is raised, allowing moisture to enter the hair shaft and cause it to swell. This swelling causes the hair to become frizzy and unmanageable.

The key to getting rid of frizz is to keep the hair cuticle smooth and flat. This can be achieved through proper hair care and styling techniques. Here are some steps you can take to get frizz out of your naturally curly hair:

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Start with clean, moisturized hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  2. Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel to dry it. Instead, gently squeeze out excess water with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your damp hair. This will help to define your curls and keep them hydrated.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair. Be gentle to avoid causing damage or breakage.
  5. Avoid using heat tools on your hair as much as possible. If you must use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment and dry your hair on a low heat setting.
  6. Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day, as this can cause frizz.
  7. If you notice frizz starting to form, use a frizz-fighting serum or oil to smooth it down.
  8. Sleep with your hair in a pineapple or loose bun to preserve your curls overnight.
  9. Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as these can damage the hair cuticle and cause frizz.
  10. Get regular trims to prevent split ends, which can contribute to frizz.

Helpful Tips:

Here are some additional tips for keeping your naturally curly hair frizz-free:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz while you sleep.
  • Avoid using products with alcohol or sulfates, as these can dry out your hair and cause frizz.
  • Try using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause frizz.
  • Consider getting a silk or satin bonnet to wear at night to protect your curls while you sleep.

FAQs:

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting frizz out of naturally curly hair:

Q: Can I use a straightening iron to get rid of frizz?

A: While a straightening iron can temporarily smooth down frizz, it can also damage your hair and cause more frizz in the long run. It's best to avoid using heat tools on your hair as much as possible.

Q: What's the best type of brush to use on curly hair?

A: A wide-tooth comb or a Denman brush are both good options for detangling curly hair without causing damage.

Q: Can I use hairspray to keep my curls in place?

A: While hairspray can be helpful for setting your curls, it can also cause them to become stiff and crunchy. Instead, try using a lightweight curl-defining cream or gel to keep your curls looking defined and bouncy.

Hair Care for Frizzy Curls:

Here are some additional tips for caring for your naturally curly hair:

  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, as this can cause it to become dry and frizzy.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your curls without causing them to become frizzy.
  • Consider getting a hair cut that is specifically designed for curly hair, as this can help to enhance your natural curls and prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using too much product in your hair, as this can weigh it down and cause it to become limp and lifeless.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your naturally curly hair looking smooth, defined, and frizz-free. Remember to be gentle with your hair and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat tools as much as possible. With a little bit of care and attention, you can achieve the beautiful, bouncy curls you've always wanted!
