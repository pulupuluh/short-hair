Description

A hot air brush is a versatile styling tool that combines the functions of a hair dryer and a brush. It is designed to help you achieve a salon-worthy blowout at home in a matter of minutes. If you have short hair, a hot air brush can be a game-changer for you. It can add volume, texture, and shape to your hair without damaging it. However, not all hot air brushes are created equal. Some are better suited for short hair than others.

How to Choose the Best Hot Air Brush for Short Hair UK 2021

Before we dive into the reviews, let's first discuss some factors to consider when choosing a hot air brush for short hair:

Barrel Size

The barrel size of a hot air brush is an important factor to consider, especially if you have short hair. A smaller barrel size is better suited for short hair as it can provide better control and precision.

Bristle Type

The bristle type of a hot air brush can also affect its performance on short hair. Generally, nylon bristles are better suited for short hair as they can detangle and smooth out the hair without snagging or pulling.

Heat Settings

It is important to choose a hot air brush with adjustable heat settings. This will allow you to customize the heat according to your hair type and texture. For short hair, a lower heat setting is recommended to prevent damage and breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide to Using a Hot Air Brush on Short Hair

Now that you have chosen the perfect hot air brush for your short hair, here is a step-by-step guide on how to use it:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Pat your hair dry with a towel and let it air dry until it is about 80% dry.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Spray a heat protectant all over your hair to protect it from heat damage.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using clips. This will make it easier to style your hair and ensure that every strand is evenly heated.

Step 4: Start Styling

Starting from the bottom section, take a small section of hair and wrap it around the barrel of the hot air brush. Use the brush to gently pull the hair downwards while applying heat. Repeat this process until all sections of your hair are styled.

Step 5: Finish with a Cool Shot

Once you have styled your hair, finish with a cool shot to set the style and reduce frizz.

Tips for Using a Hot Air Brush on Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your hot air brush:

  • Start with dry hair that is about 80% dry to avoid excessive heat exposure.
  • Use a heat protectant to prevent heat damage.
  • Section your hair into small sections to ensure even heat distribution.
  • Choose a barrel size that is appropriate for your hair length and texture.
  • Use a lower heat setting for short hair to prevent damage.

FAQs

1. Can a hot air brush damage short hair?

Yes, a hot air brush can damage short hair if used incorrectly. It is important to choose a hot air brush with adjustable heat settings and use a lower heat setting for short hair to prevent damage.

2. Can I use a hot air brush on wet hair?

No, it is not recommended to use a hot air brush on wet hair. Start with dry hair that is about 80% dry to avoid excessive heat exposure.

3. How often should I clean my hot air brush?

You should clean your hot air brush after every use to prevent product buildup and prolong its lifespan. Use a soft cloth or brush to remove any hair or debris from the bristles and wipe the barrel with a damp cloth.
