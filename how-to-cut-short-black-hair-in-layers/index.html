How To Cut Short Black Hair In Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy May 02, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have short black hair and want to add some dimension and texture to your look, cutting your hair in layers is a great option. Layers can add volume, movement, and shape to your hair, making it more dynamic and interesting. In this article, we'll show you how to cut short black hair in layers, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Short Black Hair in Layers
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Short Black Hair in Layers
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Cutting Short Black Hair in Layers

How to Cut Short Black Hair in Layers

Before we get started, it's important to note that cutting hair can be tricky, especially if you're doing it for the first time. If you're not confident in your cutting skills, we recommend going to a professional stylist. However, if you're feeling brave and want to try it out yourself, keep reading.

The first thing you'll need is a good pair of scissors. We recommend using hair cutting scissors, as they are sharper and more precise than regular scissors. You'll also need a comb, hair clips, and a mirror.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural texture and shape of your hair.

Step 2: Divide your hair into sections. Use hair clips to separate your hair into four sections: two on the top of your head and two on the bottom.

Step 3: Take the top section of your hair and comb it straight up. Hold it between your index and middle fingers, and use your other hand to cut the ends off. Start by cutting off a small amount, and then gradually cut more until you reach the desired length.

Step 4: Release the top section of your hair and comb it out. Use your fingers to separate the hair into smaller sections, and then cut each section at an angle. Start by cutting off a small amount, and then gradually cut more until you reach the desired length.

Step 5: Repeat the process for the bottom section of your hair.

Step 6: Use a mirror to check the back of your hair, and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Short Black Hair in Layers

Tip 1: Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut.

Tip 2: Cut slowly and carefully. It's better to cut off a little bit at a time than to cut off too much and regret it later.

Tip 3: Keep your hair wet while cutting. Wet hair is easier to cut and style than dry hair.

Tip 4: Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're not confident in your cutting skills, ask a friend or family member to help you.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my hair in layers?

A: It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, we recommend getting a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and maintain its shape.

Q: Can I cut my hair in layers if I have thin hair?

A: Yes, layers can actually help add volume and texture to thin hair. Just be careful not to cut too much, as thin hair can be more fragile and prone to damage.

Hair Care After Cutting Short Black Hair in Layers

After cutting your hair in layers, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

- Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair soft and hydrated.

- Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. If you do use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray.

- Trim your hair regularly to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

- Experiment with different styling products, like mousse or texturizing spray, to enhance your layers and add volume.

Cutting short black hair in layers can be a fun and easy way to update your look. Just remember to go slow, use sharp scissors, and ask for help if you need it. With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to create a beautiful, layered hairstyle that you'll love.
