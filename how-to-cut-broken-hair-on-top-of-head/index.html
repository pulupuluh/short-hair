How To Cut Broken Hair On Top Of Head A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 14, 2023 · 7 min read

Broken hair on top of the head can be frustrating and difficult to manage. Whether you’ve experienced breakage due to chemical treatments or excessive heat styling, it’s important to know how to properly cut your hair to avoid further damage. In this article, we’ll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut broken hair on top of the head, as well as tips for hair care to prevent further breakage.

Description

Broken hair on top of the head can be caused by a variety of factors, such as chemical treatments, heat styling, and rough handling. It can appear as split ends, frayed strands, or even bald spots. Cutting broken hair can help to remove the damaged ends and create a fresh, healthy look.

How to Cut Broken Hair on Top of Head

Before you start cutting your hair, it’s important to have the right tools on hand. You’ll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and clips to section your hair. Follow these steps to properly cut your broken hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup that can make it difficult to cut your hair. Once your hair is dry, comb it out to remove any tangles.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using clips, section your hair into manageable parts. Start by parting your hair down the middle, then sectioning it into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section so that it stays out of the way while you work on another section.

Step 3: Identify the Broken Strands

Using your comb, gently comb through each section of your hair to identify the strands that are broken or damaged. These strands will appear shorter than the rest of your hair and may be frayed or split at the ends.

Step 4: Cut the Broken Strands

Using your scissors, carefully trim the broken strands. Start by cutting a small amount at a time, checking your progress as you go. It’s better to cut too little than too much, as you can always go back and trim more if needed.

Step 5: Blend the Cut

Once you’ve cut the broken strands, use your scissors to blend the cut into the rest of your hair. This will create a seamless, natural look and prevent any noticeable lines or gaps.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, style it as desired. Use a heat protectant before using any heat styling tools, and avoid using excessive heat or chemicals that can cause further damage and breakage.

Tips for Hair Care

In addition to cutting your hair, there are several steps you can take to prevent further breakage and promote healthy hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is free of harsh chemicals
  • Avoid using excessive heat or chemical treatments
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle your hair
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair when styling or brushing
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth

FAQ

Can I cut my own broken hair?

Yes, you can cut your own broken hair. However, it’s important to have the right tools and follow the proper steps to avoid further damage. If you’re unsure, it’s best to consult a professional stylist.

How often should I cut my hair to prevent breakage?

It’s recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent breakage and promote healthy hair growth.

What should I do if my hair is severely damaged?

If your hair is severely damaged, it’s best to consult a professional stylist. They can provide treatments and advice on how to repair and prevent further damage.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for preventing breakage and promoting healthy hair growth. Use gentle products, avoid excessive heat and chemical treatments, and eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals. With these tips, you can keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
