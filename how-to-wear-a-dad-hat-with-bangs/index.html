How To Wear A Dad Hat With Bangs A Guide For Fashionable Ladies

Written by Bardi Jun 25, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you a fan of dad hats but struggle with how to wear them with your bangs? Fear not, because we've got you covered! In this blog post, we'll show you step-by-step how to rock a dad hat with bangs like a pro. We'll also give you some tips on how to style your hair and take care of your bangs. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Wear a Dad Hat with Bangs
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

How to Wear a Dad Hat with Bangs

First things first, let's talk about what a dad hat is. A dad hat is a soft, unstructured baseball cap that has a slightly curved brim. It's called a "dad hat" because it's the type of hat that dads tend to wear. However, it has become a popular fashion accessory for both men and women.

Now, let's talk about how to wear a dad hat with bangs. The key is to make sure your bangs are styled in a way that complements the hat. Here are some tips:

1. Sweep your bangs to the side

If you have long bangs, try sweeping them to the side before putting on your dad hat. This will create a more effortless, laid-back look that complements the hat. Use a bit of hairspray to keep your bangs in place.

2. Pin your bangs back

If you have shorter bangs or don't want to sweep them to the side, you can also pin them back with bobby pins. Make sure the bobby pins are hidden under the hat so they don't detract from the overall look.

3. Wear your hat further back on your head

Another option is to wear your dad hat further back on your head, so it sits above your bangs. This works particularly well if you have wispy, textured bangs that you want to show off.

4. Embrace the messy look

Finally, don't be afraid to embrace the messy look. Dad hats are all about laid-back, casual style, so don't worry if your bangs aren't perfectly styled. A little bit of messiness can actually add to the overall vibe.

Step-by-Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to wearing a dad hat with bangs:

1. Style your bangs

Before putting on your dad hat, style your bangs in a way that complements the hat. You can sweep them to the side, pin them back, wear your hat further back on your head, or embrace the messy look.

2. Choose your outfit

Dad hats work well with casual, laid-back outfits. Think jeans, t-shirts, and sneakers. Choose an outfit that complements the hat and your personal style.

3. Put on your dad hat

Place the dad hat on your head, making sure it's centered and sits comfortably. Adjust the brim as needed.

4. Check your bangs

Make sure your bangs are styled in a way that complements the hat. Use a mirror to check that they're not sticking out awkwardly.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for wearing a dad hat with bangs:

1. Choose the right hat

Make sure your dad hat fits your head properly and is comfortable to wear. You don't want it to be too tight or too loose.

2. Keep your hair clean

Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and fresh. This will help your bangs stay in place and look their best.

3. Use hairspray

If you have trouble keeping your bangs in place, use a bit of hairspray to hold them down.

FAQ

Q: Can I wear a dad hat with straight bangs?

A: Absolutely! Straight bangs can look great with a dad hat. Just make sure to style them in a way that complements the hat, such as pinning them back.

Q: Can I wear a dad hat with curly hair?

A: Yes! Dad hats work well with all hair types, including curly hair. Just make sure to style your bangs in a way that complements the hat.

Q: Can I wear a dad hat with a ponytail?

A: Definitely! A dad hat can look great with a ponytail. Just make sure the hat sits comfortably on your head and doesn't interfere with your ponytail.

Hair Care

Taking care of your bangs is important for maintaining their shape and style. Here are some tips:

1. Trim your bangs regularly

Visit your hairstylist every few weeks to get your bangs trimmed. This will help them stay in shape and prevent them from getting too long or unruly.

2. Blow-dry your bangs

Blow-drying your bangs can help set them in place and prevent them from sticking out awkwardly. Use a round brush to create a smooth, polished look.

3. Use a hair mask

Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. This will help prevent breakage and split ends, which can detract from your bangs' style.

And there you have it – our guide to wearing a dad hat with bangs! With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock a dad hat like a pro, no matter what your hairstyle is. Have fun experimenting with different styles, and don't be afraid to mix and match to find the perfect look for you.
