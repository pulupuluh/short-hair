How To Fade Your Own Hair Step By Step A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hair game by giving yourself a fresh fade, you've come to the right place. Fading your own hair can be a bit daunting, especially if you're new to the process. But with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking fade right at home. In this blog post, we'll take you through the step-by-step process of fading your own hair, as well as share some tips and tricks to make the process as smooth as possible.

How to Fade Your Own Hair: Step by Step

Before we dive into the actual process of fading your hair, let's go over the tools you'll need. You'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Guard attachments
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Spray bottle with water

Step 1: Decide on the Length

The first step in fading your own hair is deciding on the length you want. The length you choose will determine the guard attachment size you'll need to use. If you're not sure what length you want, start with a larger guard attachment and work your way down until you find the right length.

Step 2: Start with the Highest Guard Attachment

Once you've decided on the length, start with the highest guard attachment size and work your way down. This will ensure that you don't accidentally cut your hair too short.

Step 3: Cut the Top

Start by cutting the top of your hair using scissors. Use the comb to hold the hair in place and cut off any excess length.

Step 4: Fade the Sides

Next, use the clippers and a lower guard attachment to fade the sides of your hair. Use the attachment to create a line around your head, then gradually fade the hair as you move up.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you've faded the sides, use a higher guard attachment to blend the hair between the top and sides. Use a flicking motion to create a smooth transition.

Step 6: Clean up the Edges

Use the clippers and a small guard attachment to clean up the edges around your hairline and ears.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Use a mirror to check your work and make sure everything looks even.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with the length and fade, style your hair as desired.

Tips for Fading Your Own Hair

Fading your own hair can be a bit tricky, but with these tips, you'll be able to achieve a professional-looking fade right at home.

  • Start with a larger guard attachment and work your way down until you find the right length.
  • Use a comb to hold the hair in place while cutting.
  • Use a flicking motion to create a smooth transition between the top and sides.
  • Use a mirror to check your work and make sure everything looks even.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process.

FAQ

Can I fade my own hair if I'm a beginner?

Yes, you can! Fading your own hair can be a bit daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, anyone can do it.

What's the best way to blend the hair?

Use a flicking motion to create a smooth transition between the top and sides.

How often should I fade my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to change up your look. Generally, fading your hair every 2-3 weeks is a good rule of thumb.

Hair Care

Fading your own hair can be a bit harsh on your hair, so it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a hair mask once a week
  • Avoid over-washing your hair
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy

And that's it! With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to fade your own hair like a pro. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll end up with a great-looking fade in no time.
