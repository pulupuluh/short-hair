How To Style Permed Hair At Home A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Jul 25, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Permed hair is a great way to add volume and texture to your hair, but styling it can be a challenge. Whether you have loose waves or tight curls, there are a variety of ways to style permed hair at home. In this guide, we will take you through step-by-step instructions on how to style permed hair, as well as tips and tricks for maintaining your permed hair.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Style Permed Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips and Tricks
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Tips for Permed Hair

Description

Permed hair is hair that has been chemically treated to create permanent curls or waves. Perms can be done on any length of hair and can create a variety of curl patterns, from loose waves to tight coils. Styling permed hair can be tricky, but there are a variety of ways to wear your permed hair that will make it look beautiful and natural.

How to Style Permed Hair

Styling permed hair can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, you can create a variety of styles that will look beautiful and natural. Here are some tips for styling permed hair:

1. Use the Right Products

When styling permed hair, it's important to use products that are designed for curly or permed hair. Look for products that are moisturizing and will help define your curls. Some good products to use include curl enhancing creams, leave-in conditioners, and hair oils.

2. Don't Overdo It

When styling permed hair, it's important not to overdo it. Permed hair is already prone to damage and breakage, so it's important to be gentle when styling it. Avoid using too much heat or pulling your hair too tight when styling.

3. Experiment with Different Styles

There are a variety of ways to style permed hair, so don't be afraid to experiment with different styles. Try wearing your hair down, in a ponytail, or in a braid. You can also experiment with different hair accessories, such as headbands or hair clips.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to style permed hair:

1. Start with Clean Hair

Before styling your permed hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls soft and defined.

2. Apply a Curl Enhancing Cream

Apply a curl enhancing cream to your hair to help define your curls. Use a small amount and distribute it evenly throughout your hair.

3. Use a Diffuser

Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair. This will help prevent frizz and keep your curls defined.

4. Experiment with Different Styles

Experiment with different styles, such as wearing your hair down, in a ponytail, or in a braid. You can also try using different hair accessories, such as headbands or hair clips.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks for styling permed hair:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When combing your permed hair, use a wide-tooth comb to prevent breakage and damage.

2. Avoid Heat

Permed hair is already prone to damage, so it's important to avoid using too much heat when styling it. Try using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer instead of a regular nozzle.

3. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Using a silk pillowcase can help prevent damage and breakage to your permed hair while you sleep.

FAQs

1. How often should I wash my permed hair?

You should wash your permed hair every 2-3 days to prevent your curls from becoming dry and frizzy.

2. Can I straighten my permed hair?

Straightening permed hair can be damaging, so it's not recommended. If you want to wear your hair straight, consider using a wig or hair extensions.

3. How long does a perm last?

A perm can last anywhere from 3-6 months, depending on how fast your hair grows.

Hair Care Tips for Permed Hair

Here are some hair care tips for permed hair:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls soft and defined.

2. Avoid Heat

Avoid using too much heat when styling your permed hair. Try using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer instead of a regular nozzle.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your permed hair regularly can help prevent split ends and breakage.

Styling permed hair can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, you can create a variety of styles that will look beautiful and natural. Experiment with different styles and hair products to find what works best for your hair type. Remember to be gentle when styling your permed hair to prevent damage and breakage.
