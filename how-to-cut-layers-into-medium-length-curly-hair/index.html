How To Cut Layers Into Medium Length Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your medium length curly hair looking flat and lifeless? Adding layers can give your curls more definition and movement. But, cutting curly hair can be tricky. Don't worry, we've got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to cut layers into medium length curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Layers into Medium Length Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Description

Medium length curly hair is a versatile and popular hairstyle, but it can be difficult to maintain. Adding layers can help give your curls more shape and volume. However, cutting layers into curly hair can be tricky, as curly hair has a tendency to spring up and shrink when cut. It's important to approach cutting curly hair with care and patience.

How to Cut Layers into Medium Length Curly Hair

To cut layers into medium length curly hair, you'll need a few basic tools: sharp hair cutting scissors, a fine-toothed comb, hair clips, and a spray bottle filled with water. Here are the steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with dry, detangled hair. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Use hair clips to section off your hair into manageable sections. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up.
  3. Mist the hair with water. This will help to activate the curls and make them easier to cut.
  4. Take a small section of hair and twist it. This will help to define the curl and make it easier to see where to cut.
  5. Hold the twisted section of hair between your fingers and cut into it with scissors. Start with small snips and work your way up, cutting at an angle to create layers.
  6. Release the twisted section of hair and let it fall naturally. Check to see if the layers are even and adjust as needed.
  7. Repeat steps 4-6 on the remaining sections of hair.
  8. Once all sections are cut, shake out your curls and style as desired.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

  • Always cut curly hair when it's dry. Wet hair looks longer and can cause you to cut more than intended.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors, as dull scissors can cause split ends and damage the hair.
  • Start with small snips and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Take breaks and step back to look at your work from different angles. This will help you see any uneven areas and make adjustments as needed.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my own curly hair?

A: Yes, but it's important to approach cutting curly hair with care and patience. It's always best to seek the help of a professional stylist if you're unsure.

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how much damage you have. As a general rule, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Now that you've cut layers into your medium length curly hair, it's important to take care of your curls to keep them looking their best. Here are some hair care tips for curly hair:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools, as these can damage your curls.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help keep your curls moisturized.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl-enhancing product to damp hair to help define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to gently detangle your curls when they're wet.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to help reduce frizz and keep your curls looking their best.

By following these tips and techniques, you can create beautiful, defined layers in your medium length curly hair. Remember to approach cutting curly hair with care and patience, and don't be afraid to seek the help of a professional stylist if you're unsure.
