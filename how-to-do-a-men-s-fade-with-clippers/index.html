How To Do A Men s Fade With Clippers Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and trendy haircut, then a men's fade with clippers is an excellent choice. A fade is a sharp, clean cut that gradually tapers from short to long, creating a smooth and seamless transition. It's a versatile hairstyle that can be worn in different ways, from a classic side part to a textured quiff.

Description

A fade haircut is a stylish and modern hairstyle that involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back, gradually tapering it up to a longer length on the top. The result is a smooth and seamless transition that creates a clean and sharp look. The fade can be customized to suit your preferences, whether you want a high, mid, or low fade.

How to Do a Men's Fade with Clippers

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to do a men's fade with clippers:

Step 1: Prepare the Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure the hair is clean, dry, and tangle-free. Use a comb to part the hair where you want the fade to start, usually at the temple or above the ear. Clip the top hair out of the way, leaving the sides and back exposed.

Step 2: Choose the Clipper Guard

Choose the clipper guard size based on the desired length of the fade. Start with a longer guard size, such as #3 or #4, and work your way down gradually to shorter sizes. This will create a smooth and even transition.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start cutting the hair at the bottom of the fade, using the clipper with the chosen guard size. Use short and smooth strokes, moving the clipper upward and outward towards the hairline. Follow the natural curve of the head and avoid creating a harsh line.

Step 4: Blend the Fade

Once you've finished cutting with the first guard size, switch to a shorter guard size and blend the fade by overlapping the previous section. Use a comb to lift the hair and create a seamless transition between the different lengths.

Step 5: Define the Hairline

Use the clipper without a guard to define the hairline around the ears and neck. Use short and gentle strokes, following the natural hairline. You can also use a trimmer to create a clean and sharp outline.

Step 6: Finish the Top Hair

Unclip the top hair and cut it to the desired length using scissors or clippers with a guard. Blend the top hair with the fade by using a scissor-over-comb technique or by using the clipper without a guard at the point where the fade meets the top hair.

Step 7: Style the Hair

Once you've finished cutting, style the hair using a styling product that suits your hair type and desired look. You can create a classic side part, a textured quiff, or a messy fringe, depending on your preferences.

Tips for a Perfect Fade

  • Use a mirror to check the symmetry and balance of the fade from different angles.
  • Take your time and don't rush the cutting process to avoid mistakes.
  • Use a spray bottle to dampen the hair and make it easier to cut.
  • Use different clipper sizes and blending techniques to create a natural-looking fade.
  • Practice on a mannequin or a willing friend before cutting your own hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I do a fade with long hair?

Yes, you can still achieve a fade with longer hair by starting the fade higher up on the head and using longer clipper guards. You can also use scissors to blend the fade and the top hair seamlessly.

2. How often should I get a fade?

It depends on your hair growth and desired look. A fade usually lasts for 2-4 weeks before it grows out and loses its sharpness. You can maintain the fade by getting regular trims or doing it yourself at home.

3. Should I use a clipper or a trimmer for a fade?

A clipper is ideal for cutting the hair short and creating a fade, while a trimmer is best for detailing and defining the hairline. However, some clippers come with detachable blades that can be used as trimmers.

Hair Care Tips for a Fade

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as hairdryers and straighteners too often, as they can damage the hair and scalp.
  • Use a hair oil or serum to moisturize and nourish the hair, especially the ends.
  • Trim the hair regularly to maintain the shape and length of the fade.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and pollutants by wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF.

Now that you know how to do a men's fade with clippers, you can try it out at home and rock a stylish and trendy haircut that suits your personality and lifestyle. Remember to take your time, use the right tools and techniques, and take good care of your hair to maintain a sharp and clean look.
