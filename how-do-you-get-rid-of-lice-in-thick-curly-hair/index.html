How To Get Rid Of Lice In Thick Curly Hair

Written by Bardi Apr 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have thick curly hair and have been struggling to get rid of lice, you are not alone. Lice are a common problem that can affect anyone, regardless of hair type or texture. However, getting rid of lice in thick curly hair can be a bit more challenging than in straight hair. In this article, we will discuss how to get rid of lice in thick curly hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips for Getting Rid of Lice in Thick Curly Hair
  4. FAQs

Step by Step Guide

Here are the steps to follow to get rid of lice in thick curly hair:

Step 1: Preparation

Before you start the lice treatment, you need to prepare your hair. First, make sure you have all the necessary tools and products, such as a lice comb, lice shampoo, and conditioner. Next, detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb. Then, divide your hair into sections using hair clips.

Step 2: Apply Lice Shampoo

Apply lice shampoo to your hair, making sure to cover all areas, including the scalp, hairline, and behind the ears. Massage the shampoo into your hair and leave it on for the recommended time, usually 10-15 minutes. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 3: Comb Out Lice and Nits

While your hair is still wet, use a lice comb to comb out the lice and nits. Start at the roots and comb through to the ends, making sure to cover all areas. Wipe the comb on a tissue after each pass. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 4: Rinse and Condition

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water to remove any remaining lice or nits. Apply conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the conditioner on for a few minutes, then rinse with cool water. This will help to detangle your hair and make it easier to comb.

Step 5: Repeat Treatment

Repeat the lice treatment process every three to four days for at least two weeks to ensure that all lice and nits are eliminated. Be sure to wash all bedding, clothing, and brushes in hot water to prevent reinfestation.

Tips for Getting Rid of Lice in Thick Curly Hair

Here are some tips to make the lice treatment process easier:

  • Use a lice comb with long teeth to help detangle your hair and remove lice and nits.
  • Apply lice shampoo to dry hair for better penetration and coverage.
  • Use a hair dryer to dry your hair after washing to kill any remaining lice.
  • Apply coconut oil or tea tree oil to your hair and scalp to help suffocate and kill lice.
  • Use a vacuum cleaner to clean your home and car to remove any lice or nits.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting rid of lice in thick curly hair:

Q: Can lice survive in thick curly hair?

A: Yes, lice can survive in any hair type or texture, including thick curly hair.

Q: How do I know if I have lice?

A: Look for signs of itching, redness, and small white or brown eggs (nits) attached to the hair shafts.

Q: Can I get rid of lice without using chemicals?

A: Yes, you can use natural remedies such as coconut oil, tea tree oil, or vinegar to help kill lice.

Hair Care to Prevent Lice Infestation

Here are some tips to prevent lice infestation:

  • Avoid sharing combs, brushes, hats, and other hair accessories.
  • Wash your hair regularly with lice prevention shampoo.
  • Keep your hair tied up in a bun or ponytail to minimize contact with other people's hair.
  • Inspect your hair regularly for signs of lice or nits.
  • Use a lice repellent spray on your hair and clothes.

By following these steps and tips, you can effectively get rid of lice in thick curly hair and prevent future infestations. Remember, lice are a common problem that can happen to anyone, but with the right treatment and prevention measures, you can keep your hair healthy and lice-free.
