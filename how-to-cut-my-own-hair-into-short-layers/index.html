How To Cut My Own Hair Into Short Layers

Written by Luffy Mar 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of going to the salon and spending a fortune on haircuts? Do you want to save money and learn how to cut your own hair into short layers? You've come to the right place! In this article, we will guide you through the step-by-step process of cutting your hair into short layers.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut My Own Hair into Short Layers
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Short Layers
  • FAQs

Description

Short layers are a popular hairstyle that can add volume and texture to your hair. They are also easy to maintain and style. However, going to the salon can be expensive, and sometimes, you might not be satisfied with the result. Cutting your own hair into short layers can be a cost-effective and satisfying experience. With the right tools, techniques, and patience, you can achieve a salon-worthy look.

How to Cut My Own Hair into Short Layers

Before you start cutting your hair, there are a few things you need to prepare:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Once you have everything you need, you can start cutting your hair into short layers. Here's how:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with dry, clean hair. It's easier to see the layers when your hair is dry.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Use the comb to part your hair horizontally, starting from the bottom of your head. Pin up the top sections of your hair to keep them out of the way.
  3. Start cutting the bottom section. Hold a small section of hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. This will be the guide for the rest of the layers.
  4. Take the next section of hair, and comb it straight down. Hold it between your fingers, using the previous section as a guide, and cut it to the same length.
  5. Continue cutting the rest of the sections, working your way up to the top of your head.
  6. Once you have cut all the sections, remove the pins from the top sections of your hair.
  7. Comb your hair straight down, and cut the top sections to the same length as the bottom sections.
  8. Blend the layers. Use your scissors to blend the layers together by cutting into the ends of your hair at an angle.
  9. Check your hair in the mirror. Make sure the layers are even and balanced on both sides.
  10. Style your new short layers. You can blow-dry, curl, or straighten your hair to create different looks.

Tips for Cutting Short Layers

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's easier to cut your hair in small sections than trying to cut large sections at once.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Be patient. Cutting your own hair takes time and practice. Don't rush it.
  • Don't cut too much at once. You can always go back and cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair into short layers if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your hair into short layers if you have curly hair. However, it's important to cut your hair when it's dry, and not wet, to avoid shrinkage. You can also consider using a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to enhance your natural curls.

How often should I cut my hair into short layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your layers to be. On average, you can cut your hair into short layers every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting Short Layers

After cutting your hair into short layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to cut your own hair into short layers, you can save money and achieve a salon-worthy look at home. Remember to be patient, use the right tools, and take care of your hair after cutting it.
