How To Give A Guy A Fade Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to give a guy a fade haircut, you've come to the right place. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a perfect fade, whether you're a professional barber or just starting out.

Description

A fade haircut is a popular hairstyle that involves gradually tapering the hair from short to long. It's a versatile look that can be customized to suit any face shape and hair type. Fades can be high, mid, or low, and can be combined with other cuts, such as an undercut or a buzz cut.

How to

Step 1: Preparation

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of clippers, a comb, and a pair of scissors. Choose a clipper guard size based on the length you want for the fade. It's also a good idea to have a cape or towel to drape over the client to catch the hair.

Step 2: Start with the Sides

Begin by trimming the hair on the sides and back of the head. Use the clipper guard size you've chosen to create an even length. Work from the bottom up, using the clipper over comb technique to blend the hair into the longer hair on the top of the head.

Step 3: Create a Guide

Next, use the scissors to create a guide for the fade. Choose a point on the head where you want the fade to start, such as the temple or the occipital bone. Cut the hair to the desired length at this point, using the fingers to hold the hair at a 90-degree angle from the head. This will create a guide for the rest of the haircut.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Now it's time to blend the hair from short to long. Use a clipper without a guard to create a bald fade or use a shorter guard to create a shorter fade. Work in sections, starting at the bottom and working your way up, using the clipper over comb technique to blend the hair into the longer hair on top.

Step 5: Refine the Fade

Once you've blended the hair, it's time to refine the fade. Use the scissors to carefully trim any longer hairs that stick out. Check the fade from all angles to ensure it looks even and natural. You can also use the clipper without a guard to create a hard line or a razor to create a skin fade.

Step 6: Clean Up

Once you've finished the fade, use the clippers to clean up the hairline around the ears and neck. Use a comb to brush away any loose hairs and a towel to wipe the client's neck and face. Remove the cape or towel and let the client check out their new haircut in the mirror.

Tips

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to achieve the desired length.
  • Use a mirror to check the fade from all angles as you work.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time to ensure a clean and even fade.
  • Practice makes perfect. The more you cut fades, the easier it will become.

FAQ

Can I give myself a fade haircut?

It's possible to give yourself a fade haircut, but it can be challenging to achieve an even and natural-looking fade. It's best to have a friend or a professional barber help you if you're new to cutting hair.

How often should I get a fade haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade grows out. Most people get a fade haircut every two to four weeks to maintain the look.

Hair Care

To maintain your fade haircut, you'll need to keep it clean and well-groomed. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Style your hair with a pomade or wax to keep it in place and prevent frizz. Visit your barber regularly for touch-ups and trims.
