Should You Get Layers In Your Hair

Written by Petter May 01, 2023 · 8 min read

Welcome to our blog post about whether or not you should get layers in your hair! Layers can add depth and dimension to your hairstyle, but they're not for everyone. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of getting layers and provide tips on how to care for your layered hair.

Table of Contents

  • Why Get Layers?
  • Types of Layers
  • Consider Your Face Shape
  • Consider Your Hair Type
  • Consider Your Hair Length
  • How to Get Layers
  • Layered Hair Care
  • FAQ

Why Get Layers?

Layers can add volume, movement, and texture to your hair. They can also help to frame your face and highlight your best features. If you have thick hair, layers can help to reduce bulk and make your hair more manageable. If you have thin hair, layers can make your hair appear fuller and thicker.

Types of Layers

There are many types of layers, including:

  • Long layers
  • Short layers
  • Choppy layers
  • Textured layers
  • Face-framing layers

The type of layers you choose will depend on your hair type and desired style.

Consider Your Face Shape

When deciding whether or not to get layers, it's important to consider your face shape. Layers can accentuate or soften certain features, depending on the length and placement of the layers. For example:

  • If you have a round face, long layers can help to elongate your face.
  • If you have a square face, soft, wispy layers can help to soften your jawline.
  • If you have a heart-shaped face, chin-length layers can help to balance your features.

Consider Your Hair Type

Layers can work on any hair type, but some types may require more maintenance than others. For example:

  • If you have curly hair, layers can help to enhance your curls and add volume. However, you'll need to use products specifically designed for curly hair to keep your curls defined and frizz-free.
  • If you have straight hair, layers can help to add movement and texture. However, you may need to use a curling iron or styling products to add volume and body to your hair.
  • If you have fine hair, layers can help to make your hair appear fuller and thicker. However, you'll need to be careful not to over-layer your hair, as this can make it look thin and wispy.

Consider Your Hair Length

Layers can be added to any hair length, from short pixie cuts to long, flowing locks. However, the length and placement of the layers will depend on your hair length and desired style. For example:

  • If you have short hair, choppy layers can help to add texture and volume.
  • If you have medium-length hair, long layers can help to add movement and dimension.
  • If you have long hair, face-framing layers can help to accentuate your features.

How to Get Layers

If you've decided that layers are right for you, the next step is to find a stylist who specializes in layered haircuts. When you meet with your stylist, be sure to bring a picture of the style you want, and be prepared to discuss your hair type, face shape, and lifestyle. Your stylist will be able to recommend the best type of layers for your hair and create a customized haircut that flatters your features.

Layered Hair Care

Caring for layered hair requires a bit more maintenance than caring for a one-length haircut. Here are some tips to keep your layered hair looking healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as these can weigh down your hair and make your layers look flat.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your layers and prevent split ends.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot styling tools, such as curling irons and flat irons.

FAQ

Q: Will layers make my hair look thinner?

A: If you have thin hair, layers can actually make your hair appear fuller and thicker. However, it's important not to over-layer your hair, as this can make it look wispy and thin.

Q: Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail with layers?

A: Yes, you can still wear your hair in a ponytail with layers. However, you may need to use bobby pins or hair clips to secure any shorter layers that may fall out of the ponytail.

Q: How often should I get my layered hair trimmed?

A: It's recommended to get your hair trimmed every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your layers and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I add layers to my own hair at home?

A: We do not recommend adding layers to your own hair at home, as this can result in a choppy and uneven haircut. It's best to leave layered haircuts to the professionals.

We hope this article has helped you decide whether or not to get layers in your hair. Remember to consider your face shape, hair type, and desired style when making your decision, and always consult with a professional stylist for the best results.
