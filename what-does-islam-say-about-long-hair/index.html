What Does Islam Say About Long Hair

Written by Petter Mar 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Long hair has been a symbol of beauty and femininity for centuries. Many women strive to grow long, luscious locks, but what does Islam say about long hair? In this article, we will explore the Islamic perspective on long hair and provide tips on how to care for your hair in a way that is in line with Islamic teachings.

Table of Contents

  1. Definition of Long Hair in Islam
  2. Quranic Verses About Hair
  3. Hadith About Hair
  4. Modesty and Hair in Islam
  5. Tips for Caring for Long Hair in Islam
  6. FAQ About Long Hair in Islam

Definition of Long Hair in Islam

There is no specific definition of long hair in Islam. However, it is generally accepted that hair that falls below the shoulders is considered long.

Quranic Verses About Hair

The Quran does not specifically mention long hair, but there are verses that refer to hair in general. One such verse is:

"And say to the believing women that they should lower their gaze and guard their modesty; that they should not display their beauty and ornaments except what (must ordinarily) appear thereof; that they should draw their veils over their bosoms and not display their beauty except to their husbands, their fathers, their husband's fathers, their sons, their husbands' sons, their brothers or their brothers' sons, or their sisters' sons, or their women, or the slaves whom their right hands possess, or male servants free of physical needs, or small children who have no sense of the shame of sex; and that they should not strike their feet in order to draw attention to their hidden ornaments." (Quran 24:31)

This verse emphasizes the importance of modesty and covering one's beauty, which includes hair.

Hadith About Hair

There are several hadiths that mention hair, including:

"Whoever has hair should honor it." (Sunan Ibn Majah)

This hadith emphasizes the importance of taking care of one's hair and not neglecting it.

Modesty and Hair in Islam

In Islam, modesty is highly valued. Modesty includes covering one's hair, especially for women. The hijab is a head covering worn by Muslim women to cover their hair and neck. The hijab is a symbol of modesty and is worn as a sign of submission to Allah.

Long hair can be viewed as immodest if it is worn in a way that draws attention to oneself. It is important to wear hair in a way that is modest and does not attract unwanted attention.

Tips for Caring for Long Hair in Islam

If you have long hair and want to care for it in a way that is in line with Islamic teachings, here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as alcohol is considered haram in Islam.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to excessive heat or sunlight, as this can damage the hair.
  • Style your hair in a way that is modest and does not draw attention to yourself.

FAQ About Long Hair in Islam

Can Muslim women have long hair?

Yes, Muslim women can have long hair as long as they keep it covered and wear it in a way that is modest.

Can Muslim men have long hair?

Yes, Muslim men can have long hair as long as they keep it clean and well-groomed.

Is it haram to cut hair?

No, it is not haram to cut hair. In fact, it is recommended to trim hair regularly to keep it healthy.

Is it haram to dye hair?

No, it is not haram to dye hair as long as the dye does not contain haram ingredients.

Can Muslim women wear their hair down?

No, Muslim women should not wear their hair down in public. Hair should be covered with a hijab or other head covering.

Can Muslim men have beards?

Yes, Muslim men are encouraged to grow beards as long as they keep them clean and well-groomed.

In conclusion, long hair is not forbidden in Islam as long as it is kept covered and worn in a way that is modest. It is important to take care of your hair and keep it clean and healthy. By following these guidelines, you can have long, beautiful hair while still adhering to Islamic teachings.
