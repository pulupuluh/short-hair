How To Style Thin Long Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Mar 05, 2023 · 10 min read

Thin long hair can be difficult to style, as it tends to lack volume and body. However, with a few tips and tricks, you can achieve a variety of beautiful and versatile hairstyles. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know about styling thin long hair, including how to add volume, create texture, and maintain healthy hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Add Volume to Thin Long Hair
  • Creating Texture for Thin Long Hair
  • Styling Thin Long Hair: Step by Step
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Thin Long Hair
  • FAQs About Styling Thin Long Hair

How to Add Volume to Thin Long Hair

If you have thin long hair, you may feel like it lacks volume and body. Fortunately, there are several ways to add volume to your hair without using harsh chemicals or damaging heat styling tools. Here are a few tips:

1. Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Start by using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for thin hair. Look for products that contain ingredients such as biotin, keratin, and collagen, which can help strengthen and add volume to your hair.

2. Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow drying your hair upside down can help add volume and body to your roots. Start by flipping your head upside down and blow drying your hair in that position, using your fingers to lift and separate your hair as you dry it.

3. Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair can also add volume and body to your hair, but be careful not to overdo it. To tease your hair, backcomb small sections of hair at the roots, starting from the crown of your head and working your way down.

Creating Texture for Thin Long Hair

Creating texture is another great way to add interest and dimension to thin long hair. Here are a few techniques you can try:

1. Use a Texturizing Spray

A texturizing spray can help add texture and volume to your hair, without weighing it down. Spray the product onto your hair, then use your fingers to scrunch and tousle your hair for a messy, beachy look.

2. Try a Braided Hairstyle

Braided hairstyles can help add texture and dimension to your hair, while also keeping it out of your face. Try a simple braid, a fishtail braid, or a braided updo for a chic and stylish look.

3. Use a Curling Wand

A curling wand can help create loose, beachy waves that add texture and volume to your hair. Start by wrapping a small section of hair around the wand, then hold it for a few seconds before releasing. Repeat this process all over your head, then use your fingers to tousle and separate the curls.

Styling Thin Long Hair: Step by Step

Now that you know how to add volume and texture to your hair, let’s go over some step-by-step instructions for styling your thin long hair:

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Begin by washing and drying your hair, using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner if possible. Blow dry your hair upside down to add volume to your roots.

2. Create Texture

Add texture to your hair using a texturizing spray, braids, or a curling wand, depending on the look you’re going for.

3. Choose Your Style

Decide on a hairstyle that works for your hair type and face shape. You can try a simple ponytail, a messy bun, or a half-up half-down style.

4. Add Accessories

Accessorize your hair with clips, headbands, or scrunchies, if desired. These can add interest and style to your hair, while also keeping it out of your face.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Thin Long Hair

Thin long hair can be prone to damage and breakage, so it’s important to take good care of it. Here are a few tips for maintaining healthy hair:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up. This can help prevent breakage and damage to your hair.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling wands too often, as these can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage.

3. Get Regular Trims

Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and strong.

FAQs About Styling Thin Long Hair

1. Can I dye my thin long hair?

Yes, you can dye your thin long hair, but be careful not to overdo it. Frequent coloring can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage.

2. How often should I wash my thin long hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle, but most people can get away with washing their hair every 2-3 days. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to damage.

3. How can I prevent my thin long hair from getting tangled?

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid brushing it when it’s wet. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or detangling spray to make it easier to comb through.

4. Can I wear my thin long hair in a high ponytail?

Yes, you can wear your thin long hair in a high ponytail, but be careful not to pull it too tight. Tight hairstyles can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

5. How can I add volume to my thin long hair?

Try blow drying your hair upside down, teasing it at the roots, or using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. You can also try a texturizing spray, braids, or a curling wand to add volume and body to your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve a variety of beautiful and versatile hairstyles for your thin long hair. Remember to take good care of your hair, and avoid using harsh chemicals or damaging heat styling tools too often. With a little patience and practice, you can rock any hairstyle you want!
