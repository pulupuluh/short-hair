How To Cover Front Bald Head Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by Bardi Aug 08, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're experiencing hair loss, you may be wondering how to cover a bald spot on the front of your head. Whether you're looking for a temporary solution or a more permanent one, there are a variety of ways to conceal your bald spot and feel confident in your appearance. In this article, we'll cover a range of tips and tricks for covering a front bald head, as well as hair care advice to keep your remaining hair healthy and strong.

How to Cover Front Bald Head: The Basics

1. Hairstyles

One of the easiest ways to cover a bald spot on your head is to try different hairstyles that provide natural coverage. This could include combing your hair forward or to the side, or trying out a fringe or bangs. If you have longer hair, you could also try a half-up, half-down style, or a messy bun or ponytail that covers the front of your head. Experiment with different styles and find what works best for you.

2. Hair Fibers

Hair fibers are a popular product for covering bald spots, as they provide instant coverage and are easy to use. These products are made from tiny fibers that cling to your existing hair, creating the appearance of thicker, fuller hair. To use hair fibers, simply shake the product onto your hair and then style as usual. Be sure to choose a color that matches your hair for a natural look.

3. Hats and Head Coverings

Another option for covering a bald spot is to wear a hat or head covering. This could include a beanie or cap, a headscarf, or a turban. Not only do these items provide coverage, but they can also add a stylish touch to your outfit.

Step-by-Step: How to Use Hair Fibers

If you decide to use hair fibers to cover your bald spot, here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

Step 1: Choose the Right Product

There are a variety of hair fiber products on the market, so it's important to choose one that works for you. Look for a product that matches your hair color and offers good coverage.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Before applying the fibers, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Style your hair as usual, but avoid using any products that could interfere with the fibers, such as hairspray or gel.

Step 3: Apply the Fibers

Shake the fibers onto your hair, focusing on the areas where you need coverage. Use your fingers to gently pat the fibers into place, and then style your hair as desired.

Step 4: Seal the Fibers

To help the fibers stay in place, you can use a hair fiber sealer. This product is applied after the fibers and helps to lock them in place.

Tips for Hair Care

If you're experiencing hair loss, it's important to take care of your remaining hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are a few tips to help you maintain your hair:

1. Use Gentle Products

Avoid using harsh shampoos and styling products that can damage your hair. Look for products that are formulated for your hair type and are free from sulfates and other harmful ingredients.

2. Limit Heat Styling

Heat can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Try to limit your use of hot tools like curling irons and straighteners, and use a heat protectant when you do style your hair.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a diet rich in vitamins and nutrients can help keep your hair healthy and strong. Be sure to include plenty of protein, fruits and vegetables, and healthy fats in your diet.

FAQ

Q: Can I use hair fibers on wet hair?

A: No, it's important to apply hair fibers to dry hair to ensure proper coverage.

Q: Can hair fibers be removed easily?

A: Yes, hair fibers can be removed with shampoo and water.

Q: How long will hair fibers last?

A: Hair fibers can last up to 24 hours, but may need to be reapplied throughout the day depending on your activity level.

In Conclusion

If you're struggling with a bald spot on the front of your head, there are a variety of ways to conceal it and feel confident in your appearance. Whether you opt for a new hairstyle, hair fibers, or a head covering, there are plenty of options to choose from. And don't forget to take care of your remaining hair with gentle products, limited heat styling, and a healthy diet.
