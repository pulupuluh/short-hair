How To Control Hair Fall In Pcos

Written by Luffy May 24, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you are one of the many women who suffer from PCOS or Polycystic Ovary Syndrome, you know firsthand how frustrating and overwhelming it can be to manage its symptoms. One of the most common symptoms of PCOS is hair fall or hair loss. The good news is that there are steps you can take to control hair fall in PCOS.

How to Control Hair Fall in PCOS

Step 1: Consult with a Doctor

The first step in controlling hair fall in PCOS is to consult with a doctor. A doctor can help you identify the underlying cause of your hair fall and recommend the best course of treatment. They can also help you manage your PCOS symptoms through medication and lifestyle changes.

Step 2: Maintain a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy, balanced diet is crucial for managing PCOS symptoms, including hair fall. Make sure to include plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein in your diet. Avoid processed foods, sugary drinks, and high-fat foods as much as possible.

Step 3: Exercise Regularly

Regular exercise can help regulate your hormones and manage PCOS symptoms, including hair fall. Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate exercise most days of the week, such as brisk walking, jogging, or cycling.

Step 4: Manage Stress

Stress can worsen PCOS symptoms, including hair fall. Try to manage your stress through techniques such as meditation, deep breathing, or yoga. You may also benefit from therapy or counseling.

Step 5: Use Hair Care Products Designed for PCOS

Using hair care products specifically designed for PCOS can help control hair fall. Look for products that contain ingredients such as biotin, niacin, and zinc, which can help strengthen hair and promote healthy growth. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates, which can further damage hair.

Tips for Controlling Hair Fall in PCOS

Here are some additional tips for controlling hair fall in PCOS:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb instead of a brush to avoid damaging hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on hair, such as braids or ponytails
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as much as possible
  • Avoid over-washing hair, which can strip it of its natural oils
  • Consider taking supplements such as biotin, vitamin D, and iron, which can promote healthy hair growth

FAQs About Controlling Hair Fall in PCOS

Q: Can PCOS cause permanent hair loss?

A: In some cases, PCOS can cause permanent hair loss. However, with proper treatment, you can often stop or slow down hair loss and even promote new hair growth.

Q: Will hair fall stop once PCOS is treated?

A: Hair fall may improve once PCOS is treated, but it may not stop completely. It is important to continue managing PCOS symptoms through lifestyle changes and medication to prevent further hair loss.

Q: Can stress cause hair fall in PCOS?

A: Yes, stress can worsen PCOS symptoms, including hair fall. Managing stress through techniques such as meditation, deep breathing, or therapy can help control hair fall.

Conclusion

Controlling hair fall in PCOS may take some time and effort, but with the right approach, it is possible. By consulting with a doctor, eating a healthy diet, exercising regularly, managing stress, using hair care products designed for PCOS, and following the tips above, you can manage hair fall and promote healthy hair growth.
