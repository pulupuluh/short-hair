How To Thin Out Coarse Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Apr 24, 2023 · 7 min read

Coarse hair can be a challenge to manage, and for many people, it's also too thick. If you're looking for ways to thin out your coarse hair, then you're in the right place. This article will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to thin out coarse hair effectively. Here's what you need to know:

Description

Coarse hair is thicker and denser than other hair types, making it difficult to style and manage. People with coarse hair often struggle with frizz, tangles, and dryness. Thinning out your hair can help with these issues by reducing the bulk and weight of your hair. Thinning out your hair can also create a more manageable and polished look.

How to Thin Out Coarse Hair

Here's how to thin out your coarse hair in a few simple steps:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step to thinning out your hair is to wash it. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type. This will help to remove any product buildup and prepare your hair for the thinning process.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

After washing your hair, gently towel dry it. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with a towel, as this can cause damage and breakage. Instead, use a soft towel or an old t-shirt to pat your hair dry.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to thin out your hair evenly.

Step 4: Thin Out Your Hair with Scissors

Using hair thinning scissors, start at the bottom of each section and work your way up. Only thin out the ends of your hair, leaving the top layers untouched. This will create a natural and gradual thinning effect.

Step 5: Comb Your Hair

After thinning out your hair, comb it to remove any loose hairs. This will also help to distribute the thinning effect evenly.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired. You should notice that your hair is now more manageable and easy to style.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your coarse hair:

  • Always use hair thinning scissors, as regular scissors can cause damage and split ends.
  • Work slowly and carefully, only thinning out a small amount of hair at a time.
  • Avoid thinning out your hair too much, as this can lead to a choppy and uneven look.
  • Don't thin out the top layers of your hair, as this can create a flat and lifeless look.
  • Consider getting your hair thinned out by a professional stylist if you're unsure how to do it yourself.

FAQ

1. Can you thin out coarse hair with regular scissors?

No, you should always use hair thinning scissors to thin out your hair. Regular scissors can cause damage and split ends.

2. How often should you thin out your hair?

You should only thin out your hair when necessary, as over-thinning can cause damage and breakage. It's best to consult with a professional stylist to determine how often you should thin out your hair.

3. Will thinning out my hair make it grow back thicker?

No, thinning out your hair will not make it grow back thicker. However, it can make it appear thicker if done correctly.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and strong, it's important to follow a proper hair care routine. This includes washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using heat protectant products when styling, and avoiding harsh chemicals and treatments.

By following these tips and steps, you can successfully thin out your coarse hair and achieve a more manageable and polished look. Remember to always be gentle with your hair and consult with a professional stylist if you're unsure about any steps in the thinning process.
