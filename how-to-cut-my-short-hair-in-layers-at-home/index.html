How To Cut My Short Hair In Layers At Home

Written by Daniel Aug 27, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're looking to add some layers to your short hair. But with the right tools and techniques, you can easily achieve a layered look without having to step foot in a salon. In this article, we'll walk you through how to cut your short hair in layers at home, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Blending Your Layers
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to gather a few tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. Once it's dry, comb through it to remove any tangles. Then, spritz your hair with the water bottle to dampen it slightly. This will make it easier to cut.

Sectioning Your Hair

Next, divide your hair into sections. Use your comb to create a center part, then divide your hair into two equal sections on each side of your head. Clip each section out of the way, leaving just one section of hair to work with.

Cutting Your Hair

Starting with the section of hair at the crown of your head, hold the hair straight up with your fingers and cut the ends using the scissors. Work your way down to the bottom of the section, cutting the hair at an angle to create layers. Repeat this process with each section of hair, working from the back to the front.

Blending Your Layers

Once you've finished cutting all of your hair into layers, it's time to blend them. Take small sections of hair between your fingers and snip away any blunt lines or uneven pieces. This will create a more natural-looking layered effect.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. It's always better to take off less than you think you need, as you can always go back and cut more later.
  • Be patient. Cutting your own hair can take longer than you think, especially if you're not used to it.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut.
  • Use a mirror to check your work from all angles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my hair in layers if it's curly?

Yes, you can still cut your curly hair in layers. Just be sure to cut it when it's dry so you can see the natural curl pattern and avoid cutting too much off.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's generally recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

What if I mess up?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic! You can always go to a salon and have it fixed. Alternatively, you can also try styling your hair in a way that disguises the mistake until it grows out.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

After cutting your hair, be sure to take good care of it. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep it healthy and moisturized, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Trim any split ends as needed to maintain your layered look.

Now that you know how to cut your short hair in layers at home, you can save time and money by doing it yourself. With a little practice, you'll be able to achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home.
