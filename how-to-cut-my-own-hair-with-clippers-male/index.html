How To Cut Your Own Hair With Clippers A Comprehensive Guide For Men

Written by Robby Aug 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're in need of a haircut but don't want to go to a salon or barber, cutting your own hair with clippers can be a great solution. Not only does it save you money and time, but it also gives you the freedom to experiment with different styles and lengths. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to cut your own hair with clippers as a male, along with tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair with Clippers
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Perfect Haircut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

How to Cut Your Own Hair with Clippers

The first step in cutting your own hair with clippers is to choose the right type of clippers. For home use, it's recommended to use a set of professional-grade clippers that come with different attachments and guards for different lengths and styles. You can purchase these clippers at your local beauty or grooming store, or online.

Before getting started, make sure your hair is clean and dry. You'll also want to have a mirror handy so you can see the back of your head. It's also a good idea to have a towel or cape to keep hair clippings off your clothes and skin.

Step-by-Step Guide:

1. Choose the right clipper guard: Depending on the length of hair you want, choose the appropriate clipper guard attachment. The numbers on the guards correspond to the length of hair they will leave behind. For example, #1 will leave 1/8 inch of hair, while #8 will leave 1 inch of hair.

2. Start at the sides: Begin cutting the sides of your head, moving the clippers in an upward motion against the direction of hair growth. Use the longest guard attachment you have to start, and then work your way down in length if you want a shorter cut.

3. Move to the back: Move to the back of your head, using the same upward motion to cut the hair. Use the mirror to check your progress and make sure the cut is even.

4. Cut the top: Depending on the style you want, you may want to leave the top longer than the sides and back. Use a comb to lift the hair on top of your head, and cut it with the appropriate guard attachment. You can also use scissors to blend the top with the sides and back.

5. Blend the edges: Use a lower guard attachment to blend the edges of the hair on the sides and back with the hair on top. This will create a seamless transition between the different lengths.

Tips for a Perfect Haircut:

- Start with a longer guard attachment and work your way down gradually. It's easier to cut more hair than it is to add it back.

- Use a spray bottle to wet the hair if it's easier to work with damp hair.

- Take your time and don't rush the process. It's better to spend a little extra time on each section to ensure an even cut.

FAQs:

Q: Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

A: While it's possible to cut your hair with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Clippers are designed specifically for cutting hair and will give you a more even cut.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and style, but most men should aim to cut their hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain a clean and polished look.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair:

After cutting your own hair with clippers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized. You can also use styling products like gel or pomade to create your desired style. Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers or straighteners too frequently, as this can damage your hair.

By following these tips and steps, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. Cutting your own hair with clippers can be a fun and creative way to experiment with different styles and lengths, while also saving you time and money.
