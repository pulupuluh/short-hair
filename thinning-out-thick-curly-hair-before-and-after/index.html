Thinning Out Thick Curly Hair Before And After A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Jun 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Thick curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it looks voluminous and bouncy, it can also be difficult to manage and style. If you have thick curly hair, you may find that it takes forever to dry, is prone to frizz, and is hard to detangle. One solution to these problems is to thin out your hair. In this article, we'll show you how to thin out thick curly hair before and after, so you can achieve the perfect balance of volume and manageability.

Description

Thinning out your hair is a process that involves removing some of the bulk from your hair strands. This can be done in a variety of ways, including using thinning shears, razor cutting, or point cutting. The goal of thinning out your hair is to create more space between your strands, which will make your hair lighter and less dense. Thinning out your hair can also help to reduce frizz and make your hair easier to style.

How To Thin Out Thick Curly Hair Before and After

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before you start thinning out your hair, it's important to start with clean, dry hair. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would, and then allow it to air dry or blow dry it with a diffuser. Once your hair is dry, you're ready to start thinning it out.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections, starting at the back and working your way forward. Use hair clips to secure each section of hair, so you can focus on one section at a time.

Step 3: Choose Your Thinning Technique

There are several ways to thin out your hair, including using thinning shears, razor cutting, or point cutting. Choose the technique that you feel most comfortable with, and that will give you the results you want. If you're not sure which technique to use, consult with a professional stylist.

Step 4: Thin Out Your Hair Strand by Strand

Start at the bottom of each section of hair and work your way up, thinning out each strand of hair as you go. Use your chosen technique to remove some of the bulk from each strand, being careful not to remove too much hair. You may need to go over each strand several times to achieve the desired level of thinness.

Step 5: Blend Your Layers

Once you've thinned out each strand of hair, it's important to blend your layers together. Use a comb or your fingers to gently blend the layers together, so that your hair looks natural and not choppy.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Now that you've thinned out your hair, it's time to style it. Use your favorite styling products and tools to create the look you want. You may find that your hair is easier to style now that it's lighter and less dense.

Tips

  • Be careful not to remove too much hair, as this can create an uneven or choppy look.
  • Consult with a professional stylist if you're not sure which technique to use.
  • Use a diffuser when blow drying your hair to reduce frizz.
  • Invest in high-quality styling products that are designed for curly hair.

FAQ

1. Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner?

No, thinning out your hair will not make it look thinner overall. It will simply make each strand of hair lighter and less dense, which can make your hair easier to manage and style.

2. Is it safe to thin out my own hair?

Thinning out your own hair can be risky, especially if you're not experienced with the technique you're using. It's best to consult with a professional stylist, who can help you achieve the look you want without damaging your hair.

3. How often should I thin out my hair?

How often you should thin out your hair depends on your hair type, texture, and length. It's generally recommended to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks, but this may vary depending on your individual needs.

Hair Care

After thinning out your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair, and avoid using hot tools on a regular basis. Opt for air drying or using a diffuser whenever possible, and avoid brushing your hair when it's wet. With the right care and maintenance, your newly thinned out hair will look and feel amazing.
