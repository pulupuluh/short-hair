What To Tell Your Barber If You Want Waves A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 24, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your straight hair and looking to get some waves? You've come to the right place. In this post, we'll cover everything you need to know about what to tell your barber if you want waves. From the basics to the tips, we've got you covered. So, let's dive in!

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get Waves
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Waves
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Products for Waves

Description

Before we get into the details of what to tell your barber, let's talk about what waves are. Waves are a hairstyle that features a pattern of curls or ridges in the hair. The hair is cut short on the sides and back and left longer on the top, creating a contrast that emphasizes the waves. This hairstyle is popular among men of all ages and hair types, but it's especially popular among African American men.

How to Get Waves

If you're looking to get waves, the first step is to find a barber who knows how to cut hair for waves. Not all barbers are experienced with this style, so do your research before booking an appointment. Once you've found a barber, it's important to communicate clearly what you want. This is where knowing what to tell your barber comes in.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on what to tell your barber if you want waves:

1. Ask for a Low or Medium Fade

The first step is to ask your barber for a low or medium fade. This means that the hair will be cut shorter on the sides and back and gradually get longer towards the top of the head. A low fade starts at the hairline and gradually fades into the hair, while a medium fade starts a bit higher up.

2. Ask for a Temple Fade

If you want a more defined look, you can ask for a temple fade. This is a type of fade that starts at the temple and gradually fades into the hair. It creates a sharp, clean look that emphasizes the waves.

3. Ask for a Ceaser Cut or Buzz Cut

If you want a shorter, more low-maintenance style, you can ask for a Ceaser cut or buzz cut. These styles involve cutting the hair down to a uniform length all over the head. They're great for those who don't want to spend a lot of time styling their hair.

4. Ask for a Point Cut

A point cut is a technique used by barbers to create texture in the hair. It involves cutting the hair at an angle, which creates a jagged edge. This technique is often used to create waves in the hair.

5. Ask for a Texturizer or Wave Brush

If you have straight hair or your waves aren't as defined as you'd like, you can ask your barber to use a texturizer or wave brush. A texturizer is a chemical treatment that loosens the curl pattern in the hair, making it easier to form waves. A wave brush is a special brush that's used to train the hair to form waves.

Tips for Maintaining Waves

Once you've got your waves, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for maintaining waves:

  • Brush your hair regularly with a wave brush to train the hair to form waves.
  • Wear a durag or wave cap to keep the hair in place while you sleep.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair, as they can damage the waves.
  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting waves:

How long does it take to get waves?

It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months to get waves, depending on your hair type and how often you brush your hair.

Can you get waves with long hair?

While waves are typically associated with short hair, it is possible to get waves with longer hair. However, it may take longer to form the waves and they may not be as defined.

What products should I use to maintain my waves?

There are a variety of products available that are designed to maintain waves, such as wave pomade, wave cream, and wave gel. It's important to choose a product that works well for your hair type and doesn't weigh down your hair.

Hair Care Products for Waves

Here are some hair care products that are great for maintaining waves:

  • Wave Pomade
  • Wave Cream
  • Wave Gel
  • Wave Brush
  • Durag or Wave Cap
  • Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

Now that you know what to tell your barber if you want waves, it's time to book that appointment and get the hairstyle you've always wanted. With the right cut and proper maintenance, you can have waves that turn heads wherever you go.
