How To Get Thicker Hair At 50 Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Apr 18, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Getting older can be tough on your hair. As we age, our hair naturally thins and loses volume. If you're in your 50s and noticing your hair isn't as thick as it used to be, don't worry! There are things you can do to help promote thicker, fuller hair. In this post, we'll share some tips and tricks for how to get thicker hair at 50.

How to Get Thicker Hair at 50: The Basics

Before we dive into specific tips, let's cover some basics. First, it's important to understand that hair thickness is largely determined by genetics. That means if your parents or grandparents had thin hair, you may be predisposed to it as well. However, that doesn't mean you can't do anything to improve your hair's thickness. Second, it's important to take good care of your hair. This means using gentle products, avoiding heat styling as much as possible, and eating a healthy diet.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Get Thicker Hair at 50

Here are some steps you can take to promote thicker hair:

  1. Eat a Healthy Diet: Your hair is made up of proteins, so it's important to eat plenty of them. Foods like eggs, lean meats, and legumes are great sources of protein. You should also eat plenty of fruits and vegetables, which contain vitamins and minerals that are essential for healthy hair.
  2. Use Gentle Products: Avoid shampoos and conditioners that contain harsh chemicals, as these can strip your hair of natural oils and cause damage. Look for products that are designed for your hair type and are free of sulfates and parabens.
  3. Avoid Heat Styling: Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can cause damage to your hair, leading to breakage and thinning. Try to air-dry your hair as much as possible, and use heat styling tools sparingly.
  4. Get Regular Trims: Regular trims can help keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your hair appear thinner.
  5. Consider Supplements: There are supplements available that claim to promote hair growth and thickness. Look for supplements that contain biotin, which is a B vitamin that's essential for healthy hair.

Tips for Thickening Your Hair

Here are some additional tips for thickening your hair:

  • Try a Volumizing Shampoo: Volumizing shampoos can help add volume to your hair, making it appear thicker.
  • Use a Thickening Spray: Thickening sprays can help add volume to your hair as well. Apply the spray to your roots and blow dry your hair upside down to add volume.
  • Consider Hair Extensions: If you're looking for a more drastic solution, hair extensions can add instant volume and thickness to your hair.
  • Avoid Tight Hairstyles: Tight hairstyles like ponytails and braids can cause damage to your hair, leading to breakage and thinning. Try to wear your hair down as much as possible.
  • Massage Your Scalp: Massaging your scalp can help stimulate hair growth by increasing blood flow to the hair follicles.

FAQs: How to Get Thicker Hair at 50

Here are some common questions about how to get thicker hair at 50:

  • Can stress cause hair thinning? Yes, stress can cause hair thinning. If you're experiencing stress, try to find ways to manage it, such as through exercise or meditation.
  • What vitamins are good for hair growth? Vitamins that are good for hair growth include biotin, vitamin E, and vitamin D.
  • What is the best haircut for thinning hair? A layered haircut can help add volume to thinning hair.
  • Is it normal for hair to thin as you age? Yes, it's normal for hair to thin as you age. However, there are things you can do to help promote thicker hair.

Hair Care for Thicker Hair

Once you've achieved thicker hair, it's important to take care of it. Here are some tips for maintaining thick, healthy hair:

  • Wash Your Hair Regularly: Washing your hair regularly can help remove dirt and oil that can weigh it down.
  • Condition Your Hair: Conditioning your hair can help keep it soft, shiny, and healthy.
  • Avoid Over-Brushing: Brushing your hair too much can cause breakage and thinning. Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush.
  • Protect Your Hair from the Sun: UV rays can damage your hair, causing it to become dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF to protect your hair from the sun.
  • Get Regular Trims: Regular trims can help keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can help promote thicker, fuller hair at 50. Remember to be patient, as it may take some time to see results. With a little effort and care, you can achieve the thick, healthy hair you've always wanted!
