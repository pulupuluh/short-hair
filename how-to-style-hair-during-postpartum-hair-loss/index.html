How To Style Hair During Postpartum Hair Loss

Written by Aprilia Mar 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're experiencing hair loss after having a baby, you're not alone. Postpartum hair loss is a common issue that affects many women. While it can be frustrating and discouraging, there are ways to style your hair to make it look fuller and more voluminous. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for styling your hair during postpartum hair loss.

Description

Postpartum hair loss is a temporary condition that occurs after giving birth. During pregnancy, hormones cause hair to remain in the growth phase, resulting in thicker, fuller hair. However, after giving birth, hormone levels drop, causing hair to enter the shedding phase. This shedding can last for several months and result in noticeable hair loss.

How to Style Hair During Postpartum Hair Loss

Step 1: Choose the Right Haircut

Choosing the right haircut can make a big difference in how your hair looks during postpartum hair loss. A shorter cut can create the illusion of thicker hair, while long hair can highlight areas where hair is thinning. Consider a shoulder-length bob or a pixie cut to give your hair more body and volume.

Step 2: Use the Right Hair Products

Using the right hair products can also help to make your hair look fuller and more voluminous. Look for volumizing shampoos and conditioners that are specifically designed for thinning hair. These products can help to add body and texture to your hair, making it easier to style.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow drying your hair upside down can help to add volume and body to your hair. Start by flipping your head upside down and blow drying your roots with a round brush. Once your roots are dry, flip your head back up and continue blow drying the rest of your hair. This technique can help to create the illusion of fuller, thicker hair.

Step 4: Use Hair Extensions

If you're looking for a quick and easy way to add volume to your hair, consider using hair extensions. Clip-in extensions can be easily applied at home and can help to add length and fullness to your hair. Look for extensions that match your hair color and texture for a natural look.

Tips

  • Don't use too many hair products, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and exacerbate hair loss.
  • Consider taking a prenatal vitamin or a hair supplement to help promote hair growth.
  • Wear your hair down or in loose styles to avoid pulling on your hair and causing further damage.

FAQ

Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

Unfortunately, postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented. However, taking care of your hair and scalp can help to minimize the effects of hair loss.

How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss can last for several months, but it typically resolves on its own within a year.

Will my hair grow back after postpartum hair loss?

Yes, your hair will eventually grow back after postpartum hair loss. However, it may take several months for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy thickness.

Hair Care

In addition to styling your hair, it's important to take care of your hair and scalp during postpartum hair loss. Be gentle when brushing and styling your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, and avoid using tight hair ties or elastic bands. You may also want to consider using a scalp treatment or hair mask to nourish and strengthen your hair.

Remember, postpartum hair loss is a temporary condition that will eventually resolve on its own. In the meantime, use these tips and tricks to style your hair and make it look fuller and more voluminous.
