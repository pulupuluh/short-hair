Do Huskies Lose Hair Understanding The Shedding Process

Written by Bardi May 19, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you considering getting a Siberian Husky as your furry companion? Or perhaps you already own one and are experiencing a hairy situation? Either way, you might be wondering, do huskies lose hair? The answer is yes, and in this article, we’ll discuss everything you need to know about the shedding process of this beautiful breed.

Table of Contents

  • Understanding The Shedding Process
  • How To Handle Hair Loss
  • Step-by-Step Guide For Hair Care
  • Tips To Control Hair Fall
  • FAQ About Husky Hair Loss

Understanding The Shedding Process

Huskies have a thick double coat consisting of an undercoat and a topcoat. The undercoat serves as insulation while the topcoat is waterproof. This double coat keeps them warm in winter and cool in summer, but it also means that they shed heavily twice a year.

The shedding process is natural and essential for huskies to maintain a healthy coat. During the shedding season, which usually occurs in spring and fall, the undercoat sheds off to make way for a new coat. This process can last for several weeks, and during this time, you might find hair all over your house.

Why Do Huskies Shed?

Huskies shed to regulate their body temperature. The undercoat keeps them warm in winter, but it can be too hot in summer. Shedding the undercoat helps them stay cool in warm weather. Additionally, shedding helps remove dead hair and skin, which promotes healthy hair growth.

How Much Do Huskies Shed?

Huskies shed heavily twice a year, but they also shed moderately throughout the year. The amount of shedding depends on various factors, such as genetics, health, diet, and environment. Some huskies shed more than others, but in general, you can expect to find hair all over your house if you own a husky.

How To Handle Hair Loss

Dealing with hair loss can be challenging, but there are ways to manage it. Here are some tips:

Brush Your Husky Regularly

Brushing your husky regularly can help reduce shedding by removing loose hair and preventing matting. Use a slicker brush or a shedding tool to brush the undercoat and a bristle brush to brush the topcoat.

Bathe Your Husky Occasionally

Bathing your husky occasionally can also help reduce shedding. Use a dog shampoo and conditioner and rinse thoroughly to remove all soap residue. Be careful not to over-bathe your husky, as it can strip the natural oils from their coat and cause dryness and irritation.

Vacuum and Clean Your House Frequently

To keep your house clean, vacuum and clean frequently. Use a vacuum cleaner with a HEPA filter to trap pet hair and dander. You can also use lint rollers, pet hair removers, and microfiber cloths to clean surfaces.

Step-by-Step Guide For Hair Care

Here’s a step-by-step guide for caring for your husky’s hair:

Step 1: Brush Your Husky’s Coat

Use a slicker brush or a shedding tool to brush the undercoat and a bristle brush to brush the topcoat. Brush against the direction of hair growth to remove loose hair and prevent matting.

Step 2: Bathe Your Husky

Bathe your husky occasionally using a dog shampoo and conditioner. Rinse thoroughly to remove all soap residue.

Step 3: Dry Your Husky’s Coat

Use a towel or a blow dryer on a low heat setting to dry your husky’s coat. Make sure to dry their skin thoroughly to prevent moisture buildup.

Step 4: Trim Your Husky’s Nails

Trim your husky’s nails regularly to prevent them from scratching their coat and causing damage.

Step 5: Clean Your Husky’s Ears

Clean your husky’s ears regularly using a dog ear cleaner and cotton balls. Be gentle and avoid inserting anything into their ear canal.

Tips To Control Hair Fall

Here are some tips to help control hair fall:

Feed Your Husky A Balanced Diet

Feed your husky a balanced diet consisting of high-quality protein, healthy fats, and essential nutrients. A healthy diet can promote healthy hair growth and reduce shedding.

Keep Your Husky Hydrated

Make sure your husky has access to clean water at all times. Dehydration can cause dry skin and coat, which can lead to excessive shedding.

Avoid Stress

Avoid stressful situations as much as possible, as stress can cause hormonal imbalances that can lead to excessive shedding.

FAQ About Husky Hair Loss

Q: How often should I brush my husky?

A: You should brush your husky at least once a week, but during shedding season, you might need to brush them daily.

Q: Can I shave my husky to reduce shedding?

A: No, you should not shave your husky. Their double coat serves as insulation and protects them from the sun, heat, and cold. Shaving can damage their coat and cause skin irritation.

Q: Can I use human shampoo on my husky?

A: No, you should not use human shampoo on your husky. Human shampoos are formulated for human skin, which has a different pH level than dog skin. Using human shampoo can cause skin irritation and dryness.

In conclusion, huskies do lose hair, but it’s a natural and essential process for maintaining a healthy coat. By understanding the shedding process and following the tips and steps outlined in this article, you can manage hair loss and keep your husky’s coat healthy and beautiful.
