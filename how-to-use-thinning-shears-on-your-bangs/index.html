How To Use Thinning Shears On Your Bangs

Description

If you have thick hair, you know that bangs can be difficult to manage. They can easily become too heavy, causing them to fall into your eyes or stick out awkwardly. One solution to this problem is to use thinning shears on your bangs. Thinning shears are scissors that have teeth on one or both blades, allowing them to remove some of the hair while leaving the length intact. In this blog post, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to use thinning shears on your bangs, as well as some tips and hair care advice.


How to Use Thinning Shears on Your Bangs

Before you begin, make sure that your hair is clean and dry. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Section Your Bangs

Use a comb to separate your bangs from the rest of your hair. Clip the rest of your hair back to keep it out of the way.

Step 2: Hold the Thinning Shears

Hold the thinning shears in your dominant hand, with the teeth facing up. Use your other hand to hold the hair you want to thin.

Step 3: Start Thinning Your Bangs

Starting at the roots, use the thinning shears to cut into your bangs. Take small sections at a time, and only cut about halfway into each section. Do not cut all the way through, or you will create noticeable gaps in your bangs.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

Release the hair and check your progress. If you need to thin your bangs more, repeat the process on another section. Be careful not to over-thin your bangs, or they may become too wispy.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you have thinned your bangs to your desired amount, use regular scissors to blend the layers. This will create a more natural look and prevent your bangs from looking choppy.


Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Your Bangs

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears on your bangs:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Hold the shears at a slight angle to create a more natural look.
  • Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.
  • Thinning shears are best used on hair that is at least shoulder-length.
  • Practice on a small section of hair before moving on to your entire bangs.

FAQs

Can I Use Thinning Shears on Thin Hair?

Thinning shears are not recommended for use on thin hair, as they can create noticeable gaps and make the hair look even thinner.

Do Thinning Shears Damage Hair?

When used properly, thinning shears should not cause any damage to your hair. However, if you overuse them or use them incorrectly, you may create split ends or other damage.

How Often Should I Thin My Bangs?

How often you thin your bangs depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. As a general rule, you should thin your bangs every 4-6 weeks.


Hair Care After Using Thinning Shears on Your Bangs

After using thinning shears on your bangs, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and maintain the style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a nourishing hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to maintain the style.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.

Using thinning shears on your bangs can be an effective way to manage thick hair and create a more natural-looking style. Just be sure to follow the steps carefully and take care of your hair afterwards to maintain the look.
