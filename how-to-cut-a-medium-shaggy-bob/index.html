How To Cut A Medium Shaggy Bob A Step By Step Guide

If you're in the mood for a fresh new haircut, a medium shaggy bob might be just what you need. This versatile style looks great on almost any face shape and hair texture. Plus, it's low-maintenance and easy to style at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut a medium shaggy bob step by step. Let's get started!

How to Cut a Medium Shaggy Bob

A medium shaggy bob is a layered haircut that falls between the chin and shoulders. It's characterized by choppy, textured layers that give the hair movement and volume. Here are the basic steps for cutting a shaggy bob:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Before you begin cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help you see the natural texture and movement of your hair, which is important for creating a shaggy bob. You can shampoo and blow-dry your hair, or simply air-dry it.

2. Section your hair

Use a comb to create a center part down the middle of your head. Then, divide your hair into four equal sections: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section out of the way with hair clips.

3. Cut the back sections

Starting with the back sections, take one section at a time and comb it straight down. Hold your scissors vertically and make small, diagonal cuts into the hair. Don't cut straight across â this will create a blunt line. Instead, aim for a choppy, textured look. Repeat this process for each back section.

4. Cut the front sections

Next, move on to the front sections. Take one section at a time and pull it forward. Use your fingers to guide the hair as you make small, diagonal cuts with your scissors. This will help create face-framing layers. Repeat this process for each front section.

5. Check for balance

Once you've cut all four sections, check for balance. Use a mirror to view your hair from all angles. Make any necessary adjustments to the length or texture.

6. Style your hair

Now that you've cut your shaggy bob, it's time to style it. You can air-dry your hair for a natural, tousled look, or use a curling iron or flat iron to create waves or straighten your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide for cutting a medium shaggy bob:

1. Gather your tools

You'll need a few tools to cut your shaggy bob, including:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror

2. Wash and dry your hair

Shampoo and condition your hair as you normally would. Towel-dry your hair and let it air-dry or blow-dry it with a hair dryer.

3. Create a center part

Use a comb to create a center part down the middle of your head. Divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section out of the way with hair clips.

4. Cut the back sections

Start with the back sections. Take one section at a time and comb it straight down. Hold your scissors vertically and make small, diagonal cuts into the hair. Aim for a choppy, textured look. Repeat this process for each back section.

5. Cut the front sections

6. Check for balance

Check for balance by using a mirror to view your hair from all angles. Make any necessary adjustments to the length or texture.

7. Style your hair

Style your hair using a curling iron, flat iron, or other styling tools. You can also air-dry your hair for a natural, tousled look.

Tips for Cutting a Shaggy Bob

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a shaggy bob:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and frayed hair.
  • Don't cut too much off at once â you can always go back and cut more.
  • Work slowly and carefully to create a textured, choppy look.
  • Use a mirror to check for balance and make any necessary adjustments.
  • Consider getting a friend to help you with the back sections.

FAQs

What face shape is best for a shaggy bob?

A shaggy bob looks great on almost any face shape, but it's especially flattering on oval or heart-shaped faces.

What hair texture is best for a shaggy bob?

A shaggy bob works well on most hair textures, but it's especially suited for medium to thick hair. If you have fine hair, consider using volumizing products to add body and texture.

Can I cut a shaggy bob at home?

Yes, you can cut a shaggy bob at home, but it's important to follow the steps carefully and work slowly. Consider getting a friend to help you with the back sections.

Hair Care Tips for a Shaggy Bob

Here are some tips for keeping your shaggy bob looking great:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suited for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair â this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your shaggy bob.
  • Consider using a hair oil or serum to add shine and control frizz.

We hope this guide has been helpful in showing you how to cut a medium shaggy bob. With a little patience and practice, you can achieve a stylish, low-maintenance haircut that looks great on almost any occasion.
