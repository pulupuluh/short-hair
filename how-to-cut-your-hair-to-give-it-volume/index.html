How To Cut Your Hair To Give It Volume A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to add some volume to your hair, a new haircut might be just what you need. Cutting your hair in a certain way can create the illusion of fuller, thicker locks. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your hair to give it volume, including tips on styling and hair care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Hair to Give it Volume
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Styling Your Hair
  • FAQ

How to Cut Your Hair to Give it Volume

The key to creating volume in your hair is to add layers. Layers can help to create movement and texture, making your hair appear fuller and thicker. Here are some tips on how to cut your hair to give it volume:

1. Start with a layered haircut

If you're starting from scratch, ask your stylist for a layered haircut. This will create the foundation for your voluminous look.

2. Add face-framing layers

Ask your stylist to add layers that frame your face. These layers will help to create a flattering shape and add volume around your face.

3. Cut shorter layers on top

To create even more volume, cut shorter layers on top of your head. This will create lift and movement at the crown of your head.

4. Avoid heavy, blunt cuts

Heavy, blunt cuts can make your hair appear flat and lifeless. Instead, opt for layered cuts that create movement and texture.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to cut your hair to give it volume:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Wash and blow-dry your hair before cutting. This will give you a better idea of how your hair looks and moves, allowing you to create the perfect cut.

2. Section your hair

Section your hair into four parts: the top, left side, right side, and back. Use hair clips or hair ties to keep each section separate.

3. Cut the back section

Start with the back section of your hair. Use your fingers to comb through your hair and create a guide for your cut. Cut the hair in a straight line, starting from the back and moving forward.

4. Cut the side sections

Move on to the side sections of your hair. Use the same technique as before, cutting in a straight line from the back to the front.

5. Cut the top section

Finally, move on to the top section of your hair. Use your fingers to create a guide for your cut, cutting the hair in a straight line from the back to the front.

Tips for Styling Your Hair

Now that you've cut your hair to give it volume, it's time to style it. Here are some tips:

1. Use volumizing products

Use volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add volume to your hair. You can also use volumizing mousse or spray to create lift and texture.

2. Blow-dry your hair upside down

Blow-drying your hair upside down can help to create lift and volume at the roots. Use a round brush to add even more volume.

3. Use a curling iron

Curling your hair can create volume and texture. Use a curling iron to create loose waves or tight curls.

FAQ

1. Can I add volume to my hair without cutting it?

Yes! You can add volume to your hair by using volumizing products, blow-drying your hair upside down, and using a curling iron.

2. How often should I cut my hair to maintain volume?

You should aim to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your voluminous look.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair looking and feeling healthy, follow these hair care tips:

1. Use a heat protectant

When using hot tools, always use a heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

2. Avoid over-washing your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it appear flat and lifeless. Aim to wash your hair every other day.

3. Use a deep conditioning treatment

Once a week, use a deep conditioning treatment to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

By following these tips, you can create voluminous, healthy-looking hair. Whether you opt for a layered haircut or use styling products to add volume, there are plenty of ways to achieve the look you want.
