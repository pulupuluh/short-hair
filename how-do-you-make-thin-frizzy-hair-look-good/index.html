How To Make Thin Frizzy Hair Look Good Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline Jul 20, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have thin frizzy hair, you know how challenging it can be to style it. It can be difficult to make it look healthy and voluminous, and it can also be frustrating to deal with frizz. However, there are ways to make your hair look good with a few simple tips and tricks. In this blog post, we will share some of the best ways to style and care for thin frizzy hair.

How to:

1. Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

One of the most important things you can do to make your hair look good is to use the right shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are designed for thin hair and contain ingredients like biotin and keratin, which can help strengthen and volumize your hair. Avoid using products that contain sulfates, as these can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it even more frizzy.

2. Apply a Hair Mask Once a Week

Another great way to care for your thin frizzy hair is to apply a hair mask once a week. Hair masks can help nourish and hydrate your hair, making it look healthier and more manageable. Look for a mask that contains ingredients like argan oil or coconut oil, which can help smooth and tame frizz.

3. Use a Heat Protectant Before Styling

When you style your hair with heat tools like a flat iron or curling wand, it's important to use a heat protectant. This will help prevent damage to your hair and make it look smoother and shinier. Look for a heat protectant spray or serum that contains ingredients like jojoba oil or shea butter.

4. Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

If you want to add volume to your hair, try blow drying it upside down. This will help lift the roots and create more volume at the crown. Use a round brush to smooth out your hair as you blow dry it, and finish with a blast of cool air to set the style.

Step by Step:

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you begin styling your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for thin hair, and towel dry your hair gently to avoid causing frizz.

2. Apply a Heat Protectant

Next, apply a heat protectant to your hair to prevent damage from heat styling tools. Spray or apply the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

3. Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Flip your head upside down and use a round brush to blow dry your hair, focusing on the roots to create volume. Use the brush to smooth out your hair as you blow dry it, and finish with a blast of cool air to set the style.

4. Use a Flat Iron or Curling Wand

Once your hair is dry, use a flat iron or curling wand to style it. Choose the tool that best suits the look you're going for, and work in small sections to ensure that your hair is evenly styled.

5. Finish with a Hairspray or Serum

Finally, finish your style with a light hairspray or serum to add shine and hold. Look for products that are designed for thin hair, and avoid using too much product, as this can weigh your hair down and make it look flat.

Tips:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage
  • Avoid using hot water when you wash your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh your hair down and make it look flat
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

FAQ:

Q: Can I still color my hair if it's thin and frizzy?

A: Yes, you can still color your hair if it's thin and frizzy, but it's important to use a gentle, ammonia-free dye and to avoid over-processing your hair.

Q: Can I use a straightening or smoothing treatment on my thin frizzy hair?

A: Yes, you can use a straightening or smoothing treatment on your hair, but it's important to choose a product that's designed for thin hair and to follow the instructions carefully. These treatments can help tame frizz and make your hair look smoother and shinier.

Hair Care:

Remember, taking care of your hair is key to making it look good. Use gentle products that are designed for thin hair, and avoid using too much heat or over-styling your hair. Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy, and apply hair masks and treatments to nourish and hydrate your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you can make your thin frizzy hair look good and feel great!
