Does Severe Anemia Cause Hair Loss

Written by Eveline May 08, 2023 · 7 min read

If you are experiencing hair loss and also have severe anemia, you may be wondering whether the two are related. The answer is yes – severe anemia can cause hair loss. In this blog post, we will explore the relationship between severe anemia and hair loss and provide tips on how to manage your hair loss if you have anemia.

Table of Contents

  • What is Severe Anemia?
  • How Does Severe Anemia Cause Hair Loss?
  • How to Manage Hair Loss Due to Severe Anemia
  • Tips for Managing Hair Loss
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for People with Severe Anemia

What is Severe Anemia?

Anemia is a condition that occurs when your body does not have enough red blood cells to carry oxygen to your tissues. Severe anemia is when your red blood cell count is very low, and your body is not getting enough oxygen. Some of the symptoms of severe anemia include fatigue, weakness, shortness of breath, and dizziness.

How Does Severe Anemia Cause Hair Loss?

Hair loss is a common symptom of severe anemia. When your body is not getting enough oxygen, your hair follicles may not receive the nutrients they need to grow and stay healthy. As a result, your hair may become thin, brittle, and fall out more easily.

In addition to oxygen deprivation, anemia can also cause hormonal imbalances that affect hair growth. For example, anemia can lead to lower levels of thyroid hormone, which can cause hair loss.

How to Manage Hair Loss Due to Severe Anemia

If you are experiencing hair loss due to severe anemia, the first step is to manage your anemia. This may involve taking iron supplements, changing your diet to include more iron-rich foods, or undergoing blood transfusions.

In addition to treating your anemia, there are also several things you can do to manage your hair loss. For example, you can use hair-thickening shampoos and conditioners, avoid using heat styling tools, and try hair regrowth treatments such as minoxidil.

Tips for Managing Hair Loss

Here are a few tips to help you manage your hair loss:

  • Use gentle hair care products that are free of harsh chemicals.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of iron-rich foods.
  • Take a multivitamin that contains biotin, which can help strengthen your hair.
  • Consider wearing a wig or hairpiece if your hair loss is causing you emotional distress.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair loss and severe anemia:

Can anemia cause permanent hair loss?

If your anemia is properly managed, your hair loss should not be permanent. However, if your anemia goes untreated for a long time, it can cause permanent damage to your hair follicles, which may result in permanent hair loss.

Can taking iron supplements help with hair loss?

Yes, taking iron supplements can help with hair loss caused by anemia. Iron is an essential nutrient for healthy hair growth, and supplementing with iron can help improve the health of your hair.

How long does it take for hair to grow back after anemia?

It can take several months for your hair to grow back after anemia. However, if your anemia is properly managed, you should start to see some improvement in your hair growth within a few weeks.

Hair Care Tips for People with Severe Anemia

If you have severe anemia, it is important to take good care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can further damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as this is less likely to cause damage than a brush.
  • Avoid harsh chemical treatments such as perms and relaxers, as these can weaken your hair.
  • Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the sun and other environmental damage.
  • Be gentle when washing and drying your hair, as rough handling can cause further damage.

In conclusion, severe anemia can cause hair loss, but with proper management and care, you can prevent further damage and promote healthy hair growth. If you are experiencing hair loss and also have anemia, be sure to talk to your doctor to determine the best course of treatment.
