How To Cut Short African American Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Sep 13, 2023 · 6 min read

Cutting short African American hair can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done with ease. In this guide, we’ll take you through the steps to give you the confidence to cut your hair at home. Whether you’re looking to trim your ends or go for a complete chop, we’ve got you covered.

Description

Short haircuts are popular among African American women, and it’s easy to see why. They’re low maintenance, versatile, and can be styled in a variety of ways. However, getting a haircut at the salon can be expensive, which is why many women opt to cut their hair at home. While it can be daunting, cutting your own hair can save you money and give you the freedom to experiment with different styles.

How to Cut Short African American Hair

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle
  • Mirror

Once you have these tools, it’s time to get started.

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with shampoo and conditioner. This will help remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup and make it easier to cut. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel dry.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair into four parts: front, back, and two sides. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Trim Your Ends

Start with the back section and use scissors to trim your ends. Make small cuts, and remember to cut straight across. Repeat for each section.

Step 4: Use Clippers

Using clippers, start at the nape of your neck and work your way up. Use a #2 guard for a shorter cut, or a #4 guard for a longer cut. Be sure to go over each section several times to ensure an even cut.

Step 5: Blend Your Hair

Use scissors to blend your hair. Take small sections and cut at an angle, moving from the back to the front. This will help create a natural-looking shape.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you’ve finished cutting, style your hair as desired. Use a hairdryer and a round brush to add volume and texture, or apply a styling product for a sleeker look.

Tips

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Use sharp scissors and clippers
  • Take your time and make small cuts
  • Consult with a professional if you’re unsure

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair?

Yes, you can. However, it’s important to be cautious and take your time.

What if I make a mistake?

If you’re not happy with the result, don’t panic. Hair grows back, and you can always go to a professional for help.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and length. Generally, short haircuts need to be trimmed every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut without breaking the bank. Remember to take your time, be cautious, and have fun!
