How To Cut Your Own Hair Short Undercut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 06, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're looking to try out a short undercut hairstyle, but don't want to spend a lot of money at a salon, cutting your own hair can be a great option. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and trendy look from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut your own hair short undercut, and provide tips and advice to help you achieve the perfect look.

Table of Contents

  • Preparation
  • Sectioning the Hair
  • Cutting the Hair
  • Trimming and Blending
  • Cleaning Up

Tools Needed

Before you begin cutting your hair, you will need to gather some tools. Here are the essential items you'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Clipper or trimmer
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle

Make sure your scissors and clippers are sharp and clean. Dull blades can make your haircut uneven and difficult to manage. You may also want to invest in a hair cutting cape or towel to keep your clothes clean during the process.

Preparation

Before you begin cutting your hair, wash and dry it thoroughly. Wet hair is more difficult to cut and can result in uneven sections. Once your hair is dry, use a comb to detangle any knots or tangles. You may also want to consider trimming any split ends before you begin the actual cut.

Next, decide on the length of your undercut. Short undercuts typically have a length of 1-2 inches or less. Use your clippers or trimmer to adjust the length of your hair. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one until you achieve your desired length.

Sectioning the Hair

After you've decided on the length, it's time to section your hair. Use a comb to create a part down the middle of your head, from front to back. Then, use hair ties or clips to secure the top section of your hair out of the way. This will allow you to focus on cutting the undercut without getting in the way of the longer hair on top.

Cutting the Hair

Now that your hair is sectioned, it's time to start cutting. Use your clippers or trimmer to shave the hair on the sides and back of your head, leaving the hair on top untouched. Work slowly and carefully, making sure to keep the blade flat against your scalp for an even cut.

If you prefer a more gradual fade, you can use different guards to create a tapered look. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one as you move towards the bottom of your hairline.

Trimming and Blending

Once you've finished cutting the sides and back, it's time to blend the hair on top with the undercut. Use your scissors to carefully trim any stray hairs or uneven sections, and blend the longer hair on top with the shorter hair on the sides and back. Be sure to work slowly and carefully, checking your progress in the mirror frequently.

Cleaning Up

After you've finished cutting and blending your hair, it's time to clean up any loose hairs. Use a clean towel or brush to remove any hair from your neck, shoulders, and face. You may also want to rinse your hair with water to remove any loose hairs or debris.

Styling Your Undercut

Now that your undercut is complete, it's time to style your hair. Apply a small amount of hair product, such as wax or pomade, to your hair and style it as desired. You can slick it back, comb it over, or create a messy, textured look.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with a longer length and work your way down to a shorter one to avoid cutting too much off at once.
  • Use caution when cutting around the ears and neckline, as these areas are sensitive and can be easily nicked.
  • Check your progress in the mirror frequently to ensure an even cut.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, consider practicing on a friend or family member first.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still cut your own hair if you have curly hair. However, it may be more difficult to achieve an even cut, so be sure to work slowly and carefully.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. Depending on the severity of the mistake, you may be able to blend it in with the rest of your hair or trim it shorter to even it out. If you're unsure how to fix the mistake, consider seeking the help of a professional.

Hair Care After Your Cut

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy. You may also want to consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair soft and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and make it harder to manage.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, and with these tips and techniques, you can achieve a stylish short undercut from the comfort of your own home.
