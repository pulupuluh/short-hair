How Do I Choose A Hairstyle A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy May 17, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Choosing a hairstyle can be a daunting task, especially if you are not familiar with the latest trends and styles. With so many options available, it can be overwhelming to decide on a hairstyle that suits your face shape, hair type, and personal style. In this guide, we will provide you with a step-by-step process to help you choose the perfect hairstyle.

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in choosing the right hairstyle is to determine your face shape. Knowing your face shape can help you choose a hairstyle that will complement your features and make you look your best. There are six basic face shapes: round, square, oval, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, pull your hair back from your face and look in the mirror.

Round face: A round face has full cheeks and a rounded chin. Choose hairstyles that add height to the crown and length to the face, like a long bob or a layered cut.

Square face: A square face has a strong jawline and a square forehead. Choose hairstyles that soften the angles of the face, like a long layered cut or a side-swept bang.

Oval face: An oval face has balanced proportions and a slightly rounded hairline. Lucky you, almost any hairstyle will work with your face shape!

Heart face: A heart-shaped face has a wide forehead and a pointed chin. Choose hairstyles that add width to the forehead and chin, like a side-swept bang or a chin-length bob.

Diamond face: A diamond face has a narrow forehead and chin, with wide cheekbones. Choose hairstyles that add fullness to the forehead and chin, like a side-swept bang or a layered cut.

Oblong face: An oblong face is longer than it is wide, with a long straight hairline. Choose hairstyles that add width to the sides of the face, like a curly bob or a layered cut with volume at the sides.

Step 2: Consider Your Hair Type

Now that you know your face shape, the next step is to consider your hair type. Your hair type can impact the style and maintenance of your chosen hairstyle. There are four basic hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily.

Straight hair: Straight hair is smooth and sleek. Choose hairstyles that add texture and volume, like a layered cut or a blunt bob.

Wavy hair: Wavy hair has a natural wave pattern. Choose hairstyles that enhance the waves, like a long layered cut or a beachy wave style.

Curly hair: Curly hair has a defined curl pattern. Choose hairstyles that work with the curls, like a layered cut or a curly bob.

Coily hair: Coily hair has a tight curl pattern. Choose hairstyles that keep the curls defined and moisturized, like a twist-out or a wash-and-go style.

Step 3: Determine Your Personal Style

Your personal style is an important factor in choosing the right hairstyle. Your hairstyle should reflect your personality and make you feel confident and comfortable. Think about your personal style and the image you want to project. Do you prefer a classic, elegant look or a trendy, edgy style? Consider your lifestyle as well. If you are busy and on-the-go, choose a low-maintenance style that can be easily styled.

Step 4: Look for Inspiration

Once you have determined your face shape, hair type, and personal style, the next step is to look for inspiration. Browse through magazines, social media, and celebrity hairstyles to get ideas for your new look. Save pictures of hairstyles that you like and bring them to your hairstylist for reference.

Step 5: Consult with Your Hairstylist

The final step in choosing the right hairstyle is to consult with your hairstylist. Your hairstylist can provide valuable advice on what hairstyle will suit you best. They can also help you choose a hairstyle that works with your hair type and face shape. Be honest with your hairstylist about your hair goals and any concerns you may have.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hairstyle

Here are some additional tips to help you choose the perfect hairstyle:

  • Consider your hair texture and thickness.
  • Choose a hairstyle that flatters your best features.
  • Don't be afraid to try something new.
  • Choose a hairstyle that complements your wardrobe.
  • Keep in mind the maintenance required for your chosen hairstyle.

FAQs

Q: How often should I get a haircut?

A: It depends on your hair type and the style you choose. For shorter styles, you may need to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks. For longer styles, you may be able to go 8-12 weeks between haircuts.

Q: What if I don't like my new hairstyle?

A: Don't panic! It can take some time to get used to a new hairstyle. Talk to your hairstylist about any concerns you may have and they can suggest ways to adjust the style.

Hair Care Tips

Once you have chosen your new hairstyle, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

Choosing the right hairstyle can be a fun and exciting experience. By following these steps and tips, you can find a hairstyle that suits your face shape, hair type, and personal style. Remember to consult with your hairstylist and take care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
