How To Train Your Hair To Flow Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a classic and timeless hairstyle, then a sleek back hairstyle might be just what you need. This hairstyle has been popular for decades and is still in style today. However, not everyone has hair that naturally flows back, and training your hair to do so can take some time and effort. In this article, we'll show you how to train your hair to flow back in a relaxed and easy-to-understand way.

Table of Contents

  • How to Train Your Hair to Flow Back
  • Tips for Training Your Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Train Your Hair to Flow Back

Training your hair to flow back requires patience and consistency. Here are the steps you should take:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

It's important to start with clean hair to get the best results. Wash your hair with a good quality shampoo and conditioner to make sure it's clean and manageable.

Step 2: Apply Some Product

Apply a small amount of gel or pomade to your hair while it's still damp. This will help your hair stay in place and make it easier to style.

Step 3: Use a Comb to Style Your Hair

Use a fine-toothed comb to style your hair back. Start from the front and comb your hair back in small sections. Make sure your hair is going in the same direction, and use the comb to smooth out any bumps or knots.

Step 4: Repeat Daily

To train your hair to flow back, you'll need to repeat this process every day. Over time, your hair will start to naturally fall back and require less styling.

Tips for Training Your Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you train your hair to flow back:

Tip 1: Use a Hat

If you're having a bad hair day, you can wear a hat to cover it up. This will also help train your hair to stay in place.

Tip 2: Use a Blow Dryer

If you're in a hurry, you can use a blow dryer to dry your hair and style it back. Use a low heat setting and a comb to style your hair in the desired direction.

Tip 3: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular haircuts will help keep your hair healthy and make it easier to style. Make sure to trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your desired length.

FAQs

Q: How long will it take to train my hair to flow back?

A: It depends on your hair type and how often you style it. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months to train your hair to flow back.

Q: What products should I use to style my hair?

A: You can use a variety of products, including gel, pomade, or wax. Choose a product that works well with your hair type and gives you the desired hold and shine.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help you maintain healthy hair:

Tip 1: Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair every 2-3 days to keep it clean and healthy. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner that works well with your hair type.

Tip 2: Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and make it harder to style. Try to avoid using them as much as possible or use a heat protectant spray before styling.

Tip 3: Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet with plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals can help keep your hair healthy and strong.

Training your hair to flow back takes time and effort, but with these tips and steps, you can achieve the classic and timeless look you desire. Remember to take care of your hair and maintain a healthy hair care routine to keep your hair looking its best.
