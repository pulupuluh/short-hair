Do You Lose Your Pubic Hair During Chemo

Written by Aprilia Apr 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you are going through chemotherapy, you may be wondering if you will lose your pubic hair. Chemo is an intense treatment that affects the entire body, so it's natural to have questions about what to expect. In this article, we will explore whether or not chemo causes pubic hair loss and provide tips for managing any changes to your hair.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Chemotherapy is a treatment that uses drugs to kill cancer cells. These drugs can affect the entire body, including the hair follicles. Many people experience hair loss during chemo, including hair loss on the head, eyebrows, and eyelashes. However, not everyone loses their pubic hair.

The amount of hair loss you experience during chemo depends on the type and dose of drugs you are receiving. Some drugs cause more hair loss than others, and some people are more sensitive to the effects of chemotherapy. If you do lose your pubic hair, it will typically fall out within a few weeks of starting treatment.

How to

If you are concerned about losing your pubic hair during chemo, there are a few things you can do to prepare:

  • Talk to your doctor about what to expect. They can give you a better idea of whether or not you are likely to lose your pubic hair.
  • Consider purchasing a wig or other head covering if you lose hair on your head. This can help you feel more comfortable and confident during treatment.
  • Invest in comfortable underwear. If you do lose your pubic hair, your skin may be more sensitive than usual. Soft, breathable fabric can help prevent irritation.

Step by Step

Here are the steps you can take to manage pubic hair loss during chemo:

  1. Be patient. Hair loss is a common side effect of chemo, and it will likely grow back after treatment ends.
  2. Avoid shaving or waxing the area. This can irritate the skin and increase the risk of infection.
  3. Keep the area clean and dry. Use gentle soap and warm water to wash the area, and pat dry with a soft towel.
  4. Avoid tight clothing or underwear. This can rub against the skin and cause irritation.
  5. Consider using a moisturizer or barrier cream. This can help soothe the skin and prevent irritation.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips for managing pubic hair loss during chemo:

  • Consider talking to a therapist or counselor. Hair loss can be emotionally difficult, and it can be helpful to talk to someone about your feelings.
  • Experiment with different clothing and underwear styles. You may find that some fabrics are more comfortable than others.
  • Avoid using products that contain fragrances or harsh chemicals. These can irritate the skin.

FAQ

Does everyone lose their pubic hair during chemo?

No, not everyone loses their pubic hair during chemo. The amount of hair loss you experience depends on the drugs you are receiving and your individual sensitivity to chemotherapy.

Will my pubic hair grow back after chemo?

Yes, pubic hair typically grows back after chemo. However, it may be thinner or coarser than it was before treatment.

Can I shave or wax my pubic hair during chemo?

No, it is not recommended to shave or wax the pubic area during chemo. This can irritate the skin and increase the risk of infection.

Hair Care

If you do experience pubic hair loss during chemo, it's important to take care of your skin to prevent irritation and infection. Here are a few tips:

  • Keep the area clean and dry.
  • Avoid tight clothing or underwear.
  • Use a gentle soap and warm water to wash the area.
  • Consider using a moisturizer or barrier cream to soothe the skin.

Remember, hair loss is a common side effect of chemo, and it will likely grow back after treatment ends. In the meantime, take care of yourself and your skin, and don't be afraid to reach out to your doctor or a therapist if you need support.
