How To Get Rid Of Frizzy Wet Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Apr 12, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Frizzy wet hair can be a frustrating problem for anyone, especially if you have naturally curly or wavy hair. It can be difficult to tame and style, and can make you look unkempt and unprofessional. Fortunately, there are several ways to get rid of frizzy wet hair and achieve a smooth, sleek look. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for getting rid of frizzy wet hair, including how to prevent it from happening in the first place.

How to Get Rid of Frizzy Wet Hair

If you're struggling with frizzy wet hair, there are several things you can do to tame it and achieve a smooth, sleek look. Here are some tips:

1. Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

The first step to getting rid of frizzy wet hair is to use the right shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly or wavy hair, as these will help to hydrate and define your curls. Avoid shampoos that contain sulfates, as these can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help hydrate and smooth your hair. This will help to prevent frizz and make it easier to style. Look for a lightweight leave-in conditioner that won't weigh down your hair.

3. Use a Microfiber Towel or T-Shirt to Dry Your Hair

When drying your hair, avoid using a regular towel, as this can cause friction and create more frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently blot your hair. This will help to absorb excess water without causing frizz.

4. Use a Diffuser Attachment on Your Hair Dryer

If you're using a hair dryer, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute heat evenly and prevent frizz. This will help to enhance your natural curls and create a smoother, more defined look.

5. Apply a Hair Oil or Serum

After styling your hair, apply a hair oil or serum to help smooth and tame any remaining frizz. Look for products that contain argan oil or coconut oil, as these are great for hydrating and nourishing your hair.

FAQ

Q: Why is my hair so frizzy when it's wet?

A: Frizzy hair is often caused by a lack of moisture. When your hair is wet, it's more vulnerable to damage and breakage, which can lead to frizz. Using the right products and techniques can help to hydrate and protect your hair, preventing frizz from occurring.

Q: Can I use a regular towel to dry my hair?

A: While it's okay to use a regular towel to dry your hair, it's not the best option if you're trying to prevent frizz. Regular towels can cause friction and create more frizz, whereas a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt will help to absorb excess water without causing frizz.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Frizzy Wet Hair

Preventing frizzy wet hair starts with good hair care habits. Here are some tips to help you keep your hair healthy and frizz-free:

1. Hydrate Your Hair

Drink plenty of water and use hydrating hair products to keep your hair moisturized and healthy. This will help to prevent frizz and breakage.

2. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Use heat protectant products and avoid using hot tools too often. Excessive heat can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends, which can lead to frizz and breakage. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and strong.

4. Avoid Overwashing Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it more prone to frizz. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or use dry shampoo in between washes to refresh your hair.

5. Sleep on a Satin Pillowcase

Satin pillowcases are gentler on your hair than cotton, which can cause friction and create frizz. Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help to prevent frizz and keep your hair smooth and healthy.

By following these tips and tricks, you can get rid of frizzy wet hair and achieve a smooth, sleek look. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and don't be afraid to experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you.
