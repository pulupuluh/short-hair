How To Stop Your Hair From Frizzing After Straightening

Written by Robby Jun 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Frizzy hair can be frustrating, especially after spending time straightening it. However, there are several steps you can take to prevent your hair from frizzing after straightening. In this article, we will provide you with some useful tips to help you achieve a smooth and sleek hairstyle.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Stop Your Hair From Frizzing After Straightening
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

Description

Frizz occurs when hair is dry and lacks moisture, causing the cuticle to lift and allowing humidity to enter. When this happens, the hair shaft swells, and the result is frizz. Straightening your hair can cause even more damage, leading to frizz and flyaways. Luckily, there are several ways to stop your hair from frizzing after straightening.

How to Stop Your Hair From Frizzing After Straightening

Follow these steps to keep your hair smooth and frizz-free after straightening:

1. Use a Heat Protectant

Before straightening your hair, it's important to apply a heat protectant to prevent damage. Look for a product that contains ingredients like argan oil, keratin, or silk protein, which can provide your hair with the necessary moisture to prevent frizz.

2. Don't Overheat Your Hair

Using too much heat can damage your hair and cause frizz. Make sure you're using the correct temperature for your hair type and avoid going over the same section of hair multiple times.

3. Use a Quality Straightener

Invest in a good quality straightener that has ceramic plates. Ceramic plates distribute heat evenly, reducing the risk of hot spots that can damage hair and cause frizz.

4. Use a Lightweight Hair Oil

After straightening your hair, use a lightweight hair oil to add shine and prevent frizz. Look for a product that contains natural oils like argan, coconut, or jojoba, which can help to smooth hair and reduce frizz.

5. Avoid Touching Your Hair

Touching your hair too much can cause frizz. Try to avoid running your fingers through your hair or playing with it throughout the day.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you prevent frizz after straightening your hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to hydrate your hair and prevent frizz.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant

Before straightening your hair, apply a heat protectant to prevent damage.

Step 3: Straighten Your Hair

Using a good quality straightener, straighten your hair in sections. Make sure you're using the correct temperature for your hair type and avoid going over the same section of hair multiple times.

Step 4: Apply a Lightweight Hair Oil

After straightening your hair, apply a lightweight hair oil to add shine and prevent frizz.

Step 5: Avoid Touching Your Hair

Try to avoid touching your hair throughout the day to prevent frizz.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips to help you prevent frizz:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, which can dry out your hair.
  • Use a microfiber towel to dry your hair, as regular towels can cause friction and lead to frizz.
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal clasps, as they can cause breakage and frizz.

FAQs

Why does hair frizz after straightening?

Hair frizzes after straightening because it lacks moisture. Straightening your hair can cause even more damage, leading to frizz and flyaways.

How can I prevent frizz after straightening my hair?

Prevent frizz after straightening your hair by using a heat protectant, avoiding overheating your hair, using a good quality straightener, applying a lightweight hair oil, and avoiding touching your hair too much.

Hair Care

Keeping your hair healthy and moisturized is the key to preventing frizz. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, deep condition once a week, and avoid using products that contain alcohol.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair smooth and frizz-free after straightening. Remember to use a heat protectant, avoid overheating your hair, and use a good quality straightener to prevent damage.
