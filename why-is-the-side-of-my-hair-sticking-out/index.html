Why Is The Side Of My Hair Sticking Out

Written by Christine Apr 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you experiencing the frustration of having one side of your hair sticking out while the other side lays flat? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with this issue, and it can be especially frustrating when you're trying to achieve a polished, put-together look. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why the side of your hair may be sticking out, and provide tips and tricks for taming those unruly strands.

Description

The side of your hair may be sticking out for a variety of reasons, including:

  • Frizz
  • Dryness
  • Damage
  • Unbalanced hair cut
  • Poor hair care routine
  • Humidity
  • Natural hair texture

If you're unsure of the cause, it's a good idea to consult with a hair stylist who can help you identify the issue and provide personalized recommendations.

How to

If you're experiencing the frustration of having one side of your hair sticking out, there are several steps you can take to tame those unruly strands. Here's what to do:

Step 1: Start with a Good Haircut

If your hair is sticking out on one side, it may be because your haircut is unbalanced. A good stylist can help you achieve a balanced cut that will help your hair lay flat and smooth. Make sure to communicate your concerns with your stylist so they can provide you with the best possible cut.

Step 2: Moisturize Your Hair

Dry hair is more prone to frizz and flyaways, so it's important to keep your hair moisturized. Use a high-quality conditioner and consider incorporating a weekly hair mask into your routine to keep your locks hydrated and healthy.

Step 3: Use a Heat Protectant

If you use heat styling tools like a flat iron or curling iron, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage and frizz. Apply the spray to your hair before using any heat tools.

Step 4: Avoid Over-Brushing Your Hair

Brushing your hair too much can cause damage and breakage, which can lead to flyaways and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair, and avoid brushing it when it's wet.

Step 5: Use a Smoothing Product

There are many hair products on the market designed to help tame frizz and flyaways. Look for a smoothing serum or cream that can be applied to the hair to help keep it smooth and sleek.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for taming those unruly strands:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz
  • Avoid cotton pillowcases, as they can cause friction and lead to frizz
  • Consider using a silk or satin pillowcase instead
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils
  • Consider incorporating a hair oil into your routine to help keep your hair hydrated

FAQ

Q: Can humidity cause my hair to stick out?

A: Yes, humidity can cause hair to become frizzy and stick out. Consider using a smoothing product or wearing your hair in a protective style on humid days.

Q: Is it okay to blow-dry my hair?

A: Yes, it's okay to blow-dry your hair, but make sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using too much heat. Use a low to medium heat setting and hold the dryer at least 6 inches away from your hair.

Q: Can I use a straightening iron to tame my flyaways?

A: Yes, a straightening iron can be used to tame flyaways, but make sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using too much heat. Only use the iron on small sections of hair at a time.

Hair Care

Taming unruly hair can be frustrating, but with the right care and styling techniques, you can achieve a smooth, polished look. Remember to keep your hair moisturized, use a heat protectant, and avoid over-brushing and over-washing. With a little patience and care, you can say goodbye to those pesky flyaways and enjoy a sleek, stylish look.
