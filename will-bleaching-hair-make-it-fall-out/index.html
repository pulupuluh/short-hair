Will Bleaching Hair Make It Fall Out

Written by Aprilia Aug 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you considering bleaching your hair but worried that it might cause your hair to fall out? This is a common concern among many people, especially those with chemically relaxed hair. In this article, we will discuss whether bleaching hair can make it fall out and provide tips on how to prevent hair damage during the bleaching process.

Table of Contents

  • How Does Bleaching Hair Work?
  • Will Bleaching Hair Make it Fall Out?
  • How to Bleach Hair Safely
  • Tips for Preventing Hair Damage During Bleaching
  • FAQs

How Does Bleaching Hair Work?

Bleaching is a chemical process that removes the pigment from your hair. The bleach breaks down the melanin in your hair, which is what gives it its color. This process can be damaging to your hair, especially if it is done improperly.

Will Bleaching Hair Make it Fall Out?

While bleaching can cause hair damage, it does not necessarily mean that your hair will fall out. However, if the bleaching process is done improperly, it can cause breakage and damage to your hair, which can lead to hair loss.

The extent of the damage will depend on a variety of factors such as the strength of the bleach, the duration of the process, and the condition of your hair before the bleaching. If your hair is already damaged or weak, the bleaching process can make it more prone to breakage and hair loss.

How to Bleach Hair Safely

If you want to bleach your hair, it is important to do so safely to minimize the risk of hair damage and hair loss. Here are some tips:

  • Consult a professional hair stylist who has experience in bleaching hair. They can assess the condition of your hair and recommend the best course of action.
  • Do a strand test before bleaching your entire head of hair to see how your hair reacts to the bleach.
  • Use a high-quality bleach product that is formulated for your hair type.
  • Follow the instructions on the bleach product carefully and do not leave the bleach on your hair for longer than recommended.
  • Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water after the bleaching process to remove any residue and minimize damage.

Tips for Preventing Hair Damage During Bleaching

Here are some additional tips for preventing hair damage during the bleaching process:

  • Avoid bleaching your hair if it is already damaged or weak.
  • Deep condition your hair before and after the bleaching process to help minimize damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair after bleaching to avoid further damage.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently after bleaching to avoid stripping away natural oils and causing further damage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about bleaching hair:

How long does it take for hair to fall out after bleaching?

There is no set time frame for hair loss after bleaching. It can happen immediately after the process or it can take several weeks or months for the damage to become noticeable.

Can bleached hair be repaired?

Yes, bleached hair can be repaired with proper care and treatment. Deep conditioning treatments, protein treatments, and regular trims can help to minimize damage and restore the health of your hair.

Hair Care After Bleaching

After bleaching your hair, it is important to take extra care to keep it healthy and prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

  • Deep condition your hair regularly to help restore moisture and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair as much as possible to minimize further damage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Get regular trims to remove any damaged or split ends.

Overall, bleaching hair can cause damage and breakage, but it does not necessarily mean that your hair will fall out. By following the tips in this article, you can minimize the risk of hair damage and keep your hair healthy and strong.
