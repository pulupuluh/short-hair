How Long Does It Take For Bald Edges To Grow Back

Written by Aprilia Mar 23, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you struggling with bald edges? It can be frustrating to deal with hair loss, especially when it’s in a noticeable area like the hairline. But the good news is that with proper care and patience, bald edges can grow back over time. In this article, we’ll answer the question that’s on everyone’s mind: how long does it take for bald edges to grow back?

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Grow Back Bald Edges
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Growing Back Bald Edges
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Bald Edges

Description

Bald edges, also known as a receding hairline, can happen for a number of reasons, including genetics, tight hairstyles, and chemical damage from relaxers. When hair falls out at the hairline, it can be difficult to grow back because the hair follicles in that area may be damaged. Additionally, it can take longer for hair to grow back at the hairline compared to other areas of the scalp because of the limited blood flow and slower hair growth cycle.

How to Grow Back Bald Edges

Growing back bald edges requires patience, consistency, and a healthy hair care routine. Here are some tips for growing back your hairline:

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids, weaves, and ponytails can cause tension on the hairline, leading to hair loss. If you’re trying to grow back your hairline, opt for looser styles and avoid pulling your hair too tightly.

2. Use Gentle Hair Products

Chemical relaxers and harsh hair products can damage the hairline and slow down hair growth. Look for gentle hair products that are free of sulfates, parabens, and other harsh chemicals that can irritate the scalp and damage the hair.

3. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp in circular motions for a few minutes each day.

4. Take Hair Growth Supplements

There are a variety of hair growth supplements on the market that can help promote healthy hair growth. Look for supplements that contain biotin, vitamin D, and other essential vitamins and minerals for hair health.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide for growing back your bald edges:

Step 1: Assess the Damage

Before you start trying to grow back your hairline, it’s important to assess the damage. Take a close look at your hairline and determine how much hair loss you’re dealing with. This will help you create a realistic plan for growing back your hairline.

Step 2: Be Patient

It’s important to be patient when growing back your hairline. Hair growth takes time, and it may take several months or even a year to see significant results.

Step 3: Follow a Healthy Hair Care Routine

A healthy hair care routine is essential for growing back your hairline. Use gentle hair products, massage your scalp, and take hair growth supplements to promote healthy hair growth.

Step 4: Protect Your Hairline

Protecting your hairline from further damage is key to growing back your hairline. Avoid tight hairstyles, wear a satin bonnet or scarf at night, and be gentle when brushing or styling your hair.

Tips for Growing Back Bald Edges

Here are some additional tips for growing back your hairline:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet is essential for healthy hair growth. Eat plenty of fruits and vegetables, lean protein, and healthy fats to promote hair health.

2. Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can help keep your hair and scalp hydrated, which is essential for healthy hair growth.

3. Consider Hair Growth Treatments

There are a variety of hair growth treatments available, including laser therapy, scalp micropigmentation, and hair transplants. Talk to a dermatologist or hair loss specialist to determine which treatment may be right for you.

FAQs

How long does it take for bald edges to grow back?

It can take several months to a year for bald edges to grow back, depending on the severity of the hair loss and the individual’s hair growth rate.

Can bald edges be permanent?

In some cases, bald edges can be permanent if the hair follicles have been completely damaged or destroyed. However, in most cases, hair growth can be restored with proper care and treatment.

Hair Care for Bald Edges

In addition to the tips mentioned above, here are some additional hair care tips for bald edges:

1. Be Gentle

Be gentle when brushing or styling your hair, especially around the hairline. Use a wide-tooth comb or a soft-bristled brush to avoid pulling or tugging on the hair.

2. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage the hair and slow down hair growth. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting low.

3. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can lead to hair loss.

In conclusion, growing back bald edges takes time, patience, and a healthy hair care routine. Be gentle with your hair, avoid tight hairstyles, and protect your hairline from further damage. With proper care and treatment, you can restore your hairline and enjoy healthy, beautiful hair.
