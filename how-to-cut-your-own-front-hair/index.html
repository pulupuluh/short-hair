How To Cut Your Own Front Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 28, 2023 · 7 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to your front hair. With the right tools and techniques, however, it’s possible to achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. In this step-by-step guide, we’ll walk you through how to cut your own front hair, including tips, tricks, and frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Your Own Front Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Success
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After Cutting

How to Cut Your Own Front Hair

Before we get started, it’s important to gather the necessary tools. You’ll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a mirror. Once you have your tools, you’re ready to begin.

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

It’s best to cut your hair when it’s clean and dry, as wet hair can be harder to cut accurately. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to cleanse and condition your hair, then dry it completely with a blow dryer or air dry it.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using your comb, create a part in the middle of your hair from your forehead to the back of your head. Then, using the comb, create two sections of hair, one on either side of your head. Clip the hair on each side out of the way.

Step 3: Cut your hair

Starting with one section of hair, comb it straight down over your forehead. Using your scissors, make small snips in the hair, working your way from the center of your forehead outward. Be sure to keep your hand steady and your cuts even. Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Check your work

Once you’ve finished cutting, take a step back and look at your hair in the mirror. Check for any uneven spots or areas that need to be trimmed further. Make any necessary adjustments, then style your hair as desired.

Step-by-Step Guide

To recap, here is a step-by-step guide for cutting your own front hair:

  1. Gather your tools: hair cutting scissors, a comb, and a mirror
  2. Clean and dry your hair
  3. Create a middle part and two sections of hair on either side
  4. Comb one section straight down over your forehead
  5. Cut small snips in the hair, working from the center of your forehead outward
  6. Repeat on the other side
  7. Check your work in the mirror
  8. Make any necessary adjustments
  9. Style your hair as desired

Tips for Success

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your own front hair:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up to larger cuts
  • Use a comb to keep your hair straight and even
  • Check your work frequently to ensure evenness
  • Be patient and take your time
  • If in doubt, seek the advice of a professional stylist

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own front hair:

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, it’s important to use hair cutting scissors, which are sharper and designed specifically for cutting hair.

How often should I cut my front hair?

This will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how you like to wear it. As a general rule, it’s recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don’t panic. Depending on the severity of the mistake, you may be able to make adjustments or wait for the hair to grow out. If in doubt, seek the advice of a professional stylist.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your front hair, it’s important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and appearance. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to cleanse and condition your hair, and avoid using too much heat when styling. If you notice any split ends or damage, consider getting a trim or treatment to keep your hair looking its best.

Cutting your own front hair can be a great way to save time and money, while also giving you the freedom to experiment with different styles. By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home.
