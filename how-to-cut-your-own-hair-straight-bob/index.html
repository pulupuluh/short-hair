How To Cut Your Own Hair Straight Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 11, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're looking for a low-maintenance haircut that still looks polished and put together, the straight bob is a great option. And while going to a salon to get it done can be pricey, you can definitely cut your own hair straight bob at home with the right tools and technique. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps to get a flawless straight bob cut at home.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Straight Bob

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. It's also a good idea to have a handheld mirror or a mirror that you can place on a flat surface to see the back of your head.

When it comes to choosing the length of your straight bob, we recommend starting with a longer length and gradually cutting it shorter. It's always easier to cut more hair off than to try to fix a cut that's too short.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. You want your hair to be clean and free of any styling products so that it's easier to work with. Once your hair is dry, comb it straight and make sure that it's tangle-free.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of the back section and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and use your scissors to cut it straight across. Repeat this process with small sections of hair, working your way up to the top of the back section. Make sure that you're cutting the hair straight across and not at an angle.

Step 4: Cut the Front

Next, move on to the front sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the front and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut it straight across. Repeat this process with small sections of hair, working your way from one side of your head to the other.

Step 5: Check for Evenness

Once you've cut all the sections of your hair, use your comb to check for evenness. Comb your hair straight down and see if the length is the same all the way around. If you notice any areas that are longer than others, go back and trim them until the length is even.

Step 6: Refine the Cut

If you want to add some texture or layers to your straight bob, you can do so by taking small sections of hair and cutting into them at an angle. This will create a choppy, textured look. Just be sure to cut small sections at a time so that you don't accidentally cut off too much hair.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut it shorter to avoid cutting off too much hair.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time.
  • Check for evenness as you go to avoid any mistakes.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're unsure about anything.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair straight bob with curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair straight bob with curly hair. Just be sure to straighten your hair before cutting it so that you can see the length and ensure that it's even.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. It's always better to cut less hair than to cut off too much. You can always go back and trim any areas that need it.

How often should I cut my hair?

How often you should cut your hair depends on your hair type and the length of your bob. Generally, it's recommended that you get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After you've cut your own hair straight bob, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it looking great. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's designed for your hair type and avoid using too much heat styling. You can also add some texture or volume to your hair by using a texturizing spray or volumizing mousse.

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it's definitely doable. Follow these steps and tips to get a flawless straight bob cut at home.
