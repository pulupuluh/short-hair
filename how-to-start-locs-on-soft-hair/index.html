How To Start Locs On Soft Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 13, 2023 · 9 min read

Starting locs on soft hair can be a challenging task, especially if you’re new to the process. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, healthy locs that are easy to maintain. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to start locs on soft hair, as well as provide tips and advice for keeping your locs healthy and strong.

Table of Contents

  • How to Start Locs on Soft Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Starting Locs on Soft Hair
  • FAQs About Starting Locs on Soft Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Healthy Locs

How to Start Locs on Soft Hair

Before you start the process of starting locs on soft hair, it’s important to understand what the process entails. Locs are formed by allowing the hair to naturally mat and tangle together over time, resulting in tightly coiled strands that resemble dreadlocks. To start the process, you’ll need to section your hair and begin to twist or braid each section until the hair begins to lock together.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to starting locs on soft hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or residue. This will help to ensure that your hair is clean and free from any barriers that could prevent it from locking together.
  2. Section your hair into small, even sections. You can use a comb or your fingers to section your hair, depending on your preference.
  3. Apply a locking gel or cream to each section of hair. This will help to hold the hair in place and encourage it to lock together.
  4. Twist or braid each section of hair. Start at the root of the hair and work your way down to the ends, using a comb or your fingers to help guide the hair into place. You can use a small rubber band or hair clip to secure the ends of each section.
  5. Repeat the process for each section of hair, making sure that each section is evenly twisted or braided.
  6. Allow the hair to dry completely. This may take several hours, so be patient and avoid manipulating the hair until it is completely dry.
  7. Once the hair is dry, gently unravel each section and separate the newly formed locs with your fingers. This will help to prevent the hair from matting together too tightly and becoming difficult to manage.
  8. Repeat the process of washing, twisting, and separating your hair every 2-4 weeks until your locs are fully formed.

Tips for Starting Locs on Soft Hair

Here are some tips to help you start locs on soft hair:

  • Use a locking gel or cream to help hold the hair in place and encourage it to lock together.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down the hair and prevent it from locking together.
  • Be patient and avoid manipulating the hair too much during the locking process. This can cause the hair to break or become damaged.
  • Consider using a satin or silk scarf to protect your hair while you sleep, as this can help to prevent the hair from becoming tangled or matted together.

FAQs About Starting Locs on Soft Hair

Here are some common questions and answers about starting locs on soft hair:

Q: How long does it take to start locs on soft hair?
A: The process of starting locs on soft hair can take anywhere from several months to a year or more, depending on your hair type and how quickly your hair locks together. Q: How often should I wash my hair while starting locs?
A: You should aim to wash your hair every 2-4 weeks while starting locs. This will help to keep your hair clean and free from any barriers that could prevent it from locking together. Q: Can I start locs on my own or should I go to a professional?
A: While it is possible to start locs on your own, it is recommended that you seek the help of a professional stylist who has experience with starting locs. They can provide guidance and advice to help ensure that your locs are healthy and strong.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Healthy Locs

Once your locs are fully formed, it’s important to take care of them to help ensure that they remain healthy and strong. Here are some hair care tips for maintaining healthy locs:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to keep it clean and free from any buildup or residue.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down the hair and prevent it from locking together.
  • Be gentle when styling your hair and avoid using heat on your locs, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or oil to help moisturize your locs and prevent them from becoming dry or brittle.
  • Visit a professional stylist regularly to have your locs maintained and retwisted as needed.

Starting locs on soft hair may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, healthy locs that are easy to maintain. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair, and your locs will flourish over time.
