How To Cut Underarm Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 18, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're looking to get rid of underarm hair but don't want to go to a salon or use harsh chemicals, cutting your underarm hair is a great option. This guide will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to cut underarm hair safely and effectively.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Underarm Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Underarm Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Underarm Hair

How to Cut Underarm Hair

Cutting underarm hair is an easy and safe way to get rid of unwanted hair. It's a great option for those who are looking for a more natural and non-invasive method of hair removal. Here's what you'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Shaving cream or soap
  • Warm water

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing your underarms with warm water and soap or shaving cream. This will help soften the hair and make it easier to cut.
  2. Use a comb to comb through your underarm hair. This will help separate the hairs and make it easier to cut them evenly.
  3. Take your scissors and carefully trim the hair down to the desired length. Be sure to use small, precise cuts to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  4. Continue trimming until you have removed all of the unwanted hair.
  5. Rinse your underarms with warm water to remove any loose hair.

Tips for Cutting Underarm Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your underarm hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid pulling or tugging on the hair.
  • Take your time and make small, precise cuts to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Be sure to comb through your hair before cutting it to ensure an even cut.
  • Trim your hair in a well-lit area to avoid missing any spots.
  • Consider using an electric trimmer for a quicker and easier hair removal process.

FAQs

Is cutting underarm hair safe?

Yes, cutting underarm hair is a safe and non-invasive method of hair removal.

How often should I cut my underarm hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows. Most people find that trimming their underarm hair every 2-4 weeks is sufficient.

Will cutting underarm hair make it grow back thicker?

No, cutting underarm hair will not make it grow back thicker. This is a common myth.

Hair Care After Cutting Underarm Hair

After cutting your underarm hair, it's important to take good care of your skin to prevent irritation and ingrown hairs. Here are a few tips:

  • Moisturize your underarms regularly to keep the skin soft and supple.
  • Avoid wearing tight clothing that may rub against your underarms.
  • Exfoliate your skin regularly to prevent ingrown hairs.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or fragrances on your underarms.

Cutting underarm hair is a great option for those who are looking for a more natural and non-invasive method of hair removal. With these simple steps and tips, you can safely and effectively trim your underarm hair at home.
