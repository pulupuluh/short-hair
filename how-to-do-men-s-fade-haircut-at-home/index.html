How To Do Men s Fade Haircut At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Apr 17, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of paying for expensive haircuts every few weeks? Why not try doing a fade haircut at home? With a little practice, you can achieve professional-looking results without breaking the bank. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know to get started, including tools, technique, and hair care tips.

Table of Contents

Tools You’ll Need

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Clippers with adjustable guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Barber cape or towel
  • Haircutting spray bottle
  • Clipper oil
  • Clean towel or cloth

Having the right tools is essential to achieving a successful fade haircut at home. You can find all of these items at your local beauty supply store or online.

Technique

The key to a good fade haircut is blending the lengths seamlessly. This requires using different clipper guard sizes and a steady hand. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to shorter ones to create the fade effect.

Step 1: Decide on Your Fade Type

There are three main types of fades: low fade, mid fade, and high fade. The difference lies in how high up the head the fade starts. Decide which type you want to achieve before you begin.

Step 2: Start with the Right Length

Begin by cutting the sides and back to the desired length using the longest clipper guard. Make sure the hair is dry and use the comb to lift sections of hair as you cut. Work your way around the head, cutting all sides and back evenly.

Step 3: Create the Fade

Next, switch to a shorter clipper guard and start fading the hair. Use the longer guard to create a guideline around the head and then blend the shorter hair into it. Work your way around the entire head, gradually switching to shorter guard sizes until you achieve the desired fade effect.

Step 4: Blend the Sides and Back

Once you have created the fade, it’s time to blend the sides and back into the top. Switch to scissors and use them to cut the top of the hair to the desired length. Then, use the comb to blend the top into the sides and back. Make sure to cut small sections at a time and keep the comb at an angle for a natural-looking blend.

Step 5: Define the Top

Finally, use the scissors to define the top of the hair. Cut it to the desired length and style as desired. You can use hair gel or pomade to create a clean, polished look.

Tips for Success

  • Take your time and work in small sections
  • Use a steady hand and maintain a consistent pressure with the clippers
  • Blend the hair in different directions to avoid harsh lines
  • Use the comb to lift hair as you cut to ensure even length
  • Clean your clippers after each use and oil the blades to keep them sharp

FAQs

Can I achieve a fade haircut at home if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can. Just make sure to cut your hair when it is dry and use a comb to lift the curls as you cut. Keep in mind that curly hair may require additional blending to achieve a seamless fade.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?

No, it’s best to use hair-cutting scissors, as they are sharpened specifically for that purpose. Regular scissors may cause hair to split or fray, resulting in a less clean-cut look.

Hair Care After the Cut

After you’ve finished cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Shampoo and condition regularly to keep it clean and healthy. You can also use hair oil or serum to add shine and moisture. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking fade haircut at home. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and practice regularly. Happy cutting!
