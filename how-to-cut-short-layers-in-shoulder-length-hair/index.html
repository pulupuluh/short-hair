How To Cut Short Layers In Shoulder Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 13, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of your plain, shoulder-length hair and want to spice things up with some short layers? Cutting layers into your hair can add texture, volume, and movement, making it the perfect solution for those looking to switch up their hairstyle. But how do you cut short layers in shoulder length hair? In this article, we’ll guide you through the process, step-by-step.

Description: What are Short Layers in Shoulder Length Hair?

Short layers are hair sections that are cut shorter than the rest of the hair, creating a layered effect. When cut into shoulder-length hair, short layers can add dimension and shape to the hairstyle. They are versatile and can be styled in many ways, from simple waves to more complex updos.

How to Cut Short Layers in Shoulder Length Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You will need sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It’s also a good idea to have a reference photo of the hairstyle you want to achieve.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to see the natural texture and shape of your hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into two sections, one at the front and one at the back. Clip the back section out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Starting at the front, take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle, creating a shorter layer. The angle should be about 45 degrees, but you can adjust it to suit your preference. Repeat this process with the rest of the front section.

Step 4: Cut the Second Layer

Release the back section of your hair and repeat the same process, cutting the hair at an angle to create a second layer. This layer should be shorter than the first layer. If you want more layers, repeat this process until you achieve your desired look.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

After you have cut all the layers, go back and blend them together. Use your scissors to cut into the ends of the hair, creating a softer, more natural look. Be careful not to cut too much, or you may end up with uneven layers.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Now that you have cut your short layers, it’s time to style your hair. You can create loose waves, straighten it, or add some product for extra texture. Experiment with different styles to find the one that suits you best.

Tips for Cutting Short Layers in Shoulder Length Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting short layers:

  • Start with a small section of hair and cut a little at a time. You can always go back and cut more if needed.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Always cut at an angle to create a layered effect.
  • Blend the layers together for a more natural look.
  • Don’t be afraid to seek professional help if you’re unsure about cutting your hair.

FAQs

Can I cut short layers in curly hair?

Yes, you can cut short layers in curly hair. However, it’s important to be careful not to cut too much, as curly hair tends to shrink when it’s cut. It’s also a good idea to seek professional help if you’re unsure about cutting curly hair.

How often should I cut my short layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. As a general rule, you should cut your short layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain their shape and texture.

Hair Care for Short Layers in Shoulder Length Hair

Short layers require regular maintenance to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat when styling.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape of your short layers.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Now that you know how to cut short layers in shoulder length hair, it’s time to try it out for yourself. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don’t be afraid to seek professional help if needed. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, layered hairstyle that suits your personality and style.
