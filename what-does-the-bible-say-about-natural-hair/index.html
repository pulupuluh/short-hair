What Does The Bible Say About Natural Hair

Written by Eveline Apr 22, 2023 · 8 min read

As more and more people are embracing their natural hair, some may wonder what the Bible has to say about it. In this article, we'll explore what the Bible says about natural hair and provide tips for taking care of your natural hair.

Table of Contents:

  • What Does the Bible Say About Hair?
  • What Does the Bible Say About Natural Hair?
  • Tips for Taking Care of Your Natural Hair
  • FAQ About Natural Hair and the Bible

What Does the Bible Say About Hair?

The Bible mentions hair numerous times throughout its books. In the Old Testament, hair was often seen as a symbol of strength and beauty. Samson's long hair was a mark of his covenant with God, while the Song of Solomon describes hair as "flowing" and "black as a raven."

The New Testament also mentions hair, with 1 Corinthians 11:15 stating that "if a woman has long hair, it is her glory." However, it's important to note that the Bible does not mandate any specific hairstyle or length of hair.

What Does the Bible Say About Natural Hair?

While the Bible does not specifically mention natural hair, it does emphasize the importance of embracing one's natural self. Psalm 139:14 states, "I praise you because I am fearfully and wonderfully made." This verse reminds us that we are all unique and special in God's eyes.

Additionally, the Bible encourages us to not conform to societal norms and expectations. Romans 12:2 says, "Do not conform to the pattern of this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind. Then you will be able to test and approve what God's will is—his good, pleasing and perfect will."

Therefore, it's important to embrace our natural hair and not feel pressured to conform to societal beauty standards.

Tips for Taking Care of Your Natural Hair

If you're new to natural hair or just looking for some tips, here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Moisturize regularly to prevent dryness and breakage
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools
  • Protect your hair at night with a satin or silk bonnet or scarf
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends

FAQ About Natural Hair and the Bible

Does the Bible forbid relaxing or straightening hair?

No, the Bible does not forbid relaxing or straightening hair. However, it's important to note that using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools can damage hair and lead to breakage.

Is it a sin to wear weaves or extensions?

No, it is not a sin to wear weaves or extensions. However, it's important to make sure that your hair is still being properly cared for underneath the weave or extension.

Can men have natural hair?

Yes, men can certainly have natural hair. In fact, many men are embracing their natural hair and growing out their curls and coils.

What if my job requires me to have a certain hairstyle?

If your job requires you to have a certain hairstyle, it's important to talk to your employer about any concerns you may have. However, ultimately it's up to you to decide what you feel most comfortable with and what is best for your hair.

What if my family or friends don't approve of my natural hair?

It's important to remember that your hair is a part of who you are and that you should never feel pressured to change it to please others. If your family or friends don't approve of your natural hair, try having an open and honest conversation with them about why it's important to you.

Is it okay to dye natural hair?

Yes, it is okay to dye natural hair. However, it's important to use hair dyes that are gentle and won't damage your hair. Additionally, make sure to properly care for your hair after dyeing to prevent dryness and breakage.

Can I still straighten my natural hair?

Yes, you can still straighten your natural hair. However, it's important to use heat protectant products and not straighten your hair too often to prevent damage.

What if I don't like my natural hair?

It's completely normal to have insecurities about your hair, but it's important to remember that your hair is a part of who you are and that you are fearfully and wonderfully made. If you're struggling with self-acceptance, consider talking to a trusted friend or a mental health professional.

Conclusion

The Bible emphasizes the importance of embracing our natural selves and not conforming to societal beauty standards. Whether you choose to wear your hair natural or relaxed, it's important to properly care for your hair and do what makes you feel most comfortable and confident.
