Description

Starting locs with long hair is possible, but it requires patience and dedication. Locs are a natural hairstyle that involves allowing hair to mat and form into ropes or cords. Starting locs with long hair can take longer than starting with shorter hair, but the end result is worth it. In this article, we will discuss how to start locs with long hair, step by step guide, tips for starting locs, FAQs, and hair care for locs.

How to Start Locs with Long Hair

Starting locs with long hair requires a few steps to ensure the process goes smoothly.

Step 1: Decide on the Method

There are several methods for starting locs, including freeform, twist and rip, and backcombing. Research and decide which method is best for your hair type and lifestyle.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Wash and condition your hair thoroughly to remove any buildup or oils. Dry your hair completely.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into even parts to ensure that each loc is uniform in size. Use rubber bands or hair clips to secure each section.

Step 4: Begin the Method

Start the method you have chosen, following the instructions carefully. Be gentle with your hair to avoid breakage or damage.

Step 5: Maintain Your Locs

After your locs have formed, it is important to maintain them by keeping them clean, moisturized, and free of buildup or lint. This will help them to mature properly and prevent breakage.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or oils from your hair. Follow with a moisturizing conditioner to soften and detangle your hair.

Step 2: Dry Your Hair

Use a microfiber towel or t-shirt to gently dry your hair. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously, as this can cause breakage.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into even parts using rubber bands or hair clips. Make sure each section is the same size.

Step 4: Begin the Method

Start the method you have chosen, following the instructions carefully. Be gentle with your hair to avoid breakage or damage.

Step 5: Maintain Your Locs

After your locs have formed, it is important to maintain them by keeping them clean, moisturized, and free of buildup or lint. This will help them to mature properly and prevent breakage.

Tips for Starting Locs with Long Hair

Starting locs with long hair requires patience and dedication. Here are some tips to help you along the way:

  • Be gentle with your hair to avoid breakage or damage.
  • Use a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid using heavy products on your hair, as this can cause buildup and prevent your locs from forming properly.
  • Be patient - the process may take longer with long hair, but the end result is worth it.

FAQs

Can you start locs with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can start locs with relaxed hair. However, it is important to note that the relaxed hair will eventually break off as the locs mature.

How long does it take to start locs with long hair?

The time it takes to start locs with long hair varies depending on the method used and your hair type. It can take anywhere from 6 months to a year or longer for your locs to fully form.

Can you wash your hair while starting locs?

Yes, you can wash your hair while starting locs. It is important to keep your hair and scalp clean to prevent buildup and promote healthy hair growth.

Hair Care for Locs

Once your locs have formed, it is important to take good care of them to keep them healthy and strong.

  • Wash your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your locs moisturized.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils on your locs, as this can cause buildup and prevent proper matting.
  • Wrap your hair at night with a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
