How To Make Your Hair Not Frizzy After Straightening

Written by Daniel Mar 31, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Frizz is a common problem that many people face after straightening their hair. It can be frustrating to spend time straightening your hair, only to have it become frizzy and unmanageable shortly after. Fortunately, there are some tips and tricks you can use to keep your hair looking smooth and sleek after straightening. Follow these steps to achieve frizz-free locks.

Description

Frizz is caused by dryness and humidity in the air. When you straighten your hair, you are removing moisture from your strands, which can make them more prone to frizz. Additionally, if you live in a humid climate, the moisture in the air can cause your hair to become frizzy. Fortunately, there are some things you can do to combat frizz and keep your hair looking sleek and shiny.

How to

Step 1: Start with clean hair

The first step to preventing frizz after straightening is to start with clean hair. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to help keep your strands hydrated. Avoid using products that contain sulfates, as they can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.


Step 2: Apply a heat protectant

Before you begin straightening your hair, be sure to apply a heat protectant. This will help to protect your strands from heat damage and prevent them from becoming dry and brittle. Apply the heat protectant evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.


Step 3: Use a high-quality straightener

The type of straightener you use can also make a big difference in the outcome of your hair. Invest in a high-quality straightener that has adjustable heat settings. This will allow you to control the heat and prevent your hair from becoming too hot, which can lead to frizz.


Step 4: Straighten in small sections

When straightening your hair, it's important to work in small sections. This will allow you to get close to the roots and ensure that each strand is straightened evenly. Use a comb to help guide the straightener through your hair and prevent any tangles or snags.


Step 5: Finish with a serum

After you've finished straightening your hair, apply a serum to help keep your strands smooth and shiny. Look for a serum that is lightweight and non-greasy. Apply a small amount to the ends of your hair and work your way up, avoiding the roots.

Tips

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before straightening.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much after straightening, as this can cause frizz.
  • Wear a silk scarf or sleep on a silk pillowcase to help prevent frizz while you sleep.

FAQ

Q: Can I use hairspray to prevent frizz?

A: While hairspray can help to hold your style in place, it can also make your hair feel stiff and crunchy. Instead, opt for a lightweight serum or oil to help keep your hair looking smooth and shiny.


Q: How often should I straighten my hair?

A: It's best to limit the amount of heat you apply to your hair. Try to straighten your hair no more than once a week to prevent damage and breakage.

Hair Care

In addition to following these tips for preventing frizz after straightening, it's important to take good care of your hair on a daily basis. This means using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, avoiding harsh chemicals, and protecting your hair from heat damage. With the right care and styling techniques, you can keep your hair looking sleek, shiny, and frizz-free.
