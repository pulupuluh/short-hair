How Long Does Hair Fall Out After Having A Baby

Written by Christine May 13, 2023 · 6 min read

After giving birth, many new mothers experience hair loss, which can be a shocking and upsetting experience. This condition is known as postpartum hair loss, and it is a common occurrence that affects up to 90% of women. If you are experiencing postpartum hair loss, you may be wondering how long it will last and what you can do to manage it. In this article, we will explore the causes of postpartum hair loss, how long it typically lasts, and some tips for managing this condition.

What Causes Postpartum Hair Loss?

During pregnancy, your body produces higher levels of estrogen, which can cause your hair to grow thicker and fuller. However, after giving birth, your estrogen levels drop rapidly, which can cause your hair to enter a resting phase. This phase is known as telogen, and it typically lasts for about 3 months. During this time, you may notice an increase in hair shedding, which can be alarming.

How Long Does Postpartum Hair Loss Last?

The good news is that postpartum hair loss is usually temporary and should resolve within a few months. Most women will begin to notice new hair growth within 6-12 months after giving birth. However, the exact timeline can vary from person to person, and some women may experience longer-lasting hair loss. If you are concerned about your hair loss, it is always a good idea to speak with your healthcare provider.

Tips for Managing Postpartum Hair Loss

While postpartum hair loss is usually temporary, it can still be a difficult experience to go through. Here are some tips for managing this condition:

1. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Try to avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause further damage and breakage. Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to gently detangle your hair, and avoid using heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners.

2. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a well-balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help support healthy hair growth. Focus on consuming foods that are high in protein, iron, and vitamin C.

3. Consider a Haircut

If you are experiencing significant hair loss, you may want to consider a shorter haircut. This can help make your hair appear fuller and can also make it easier to manage.

4. Use Hair Care Products Designed for Hair Loss

There are many hair care products on the market that are specifically designed to help manage hair loss. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, and niacin, which are known to promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Q: Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

A: No, postpartum hair loss is usually temporary and should resolve within a few months.

Q: Can breastfeeding cause postpartum hair loss?

A: No, breastfeeding does not cause postpartum hair loss. The hormonal changes that occur after giving birth are the primary cause of this condition.

Conclusion

Postpartum hair loss can be a difficult condition to deal with, but it is usually temporary and should resolve within a few months. By being gentle with your hair, eating a healthy diet, considering a haircut, and using hair care products designed for hair loss, you can help manage this condition and promote healthy hair growth. If you are concerned about your hair loss, be sure to speak with your healthcare provider.
