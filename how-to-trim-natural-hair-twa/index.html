How To Trim Natural Hair Twa Step By Step Guide And Tips

Written by Aprilia Apr 26, 2023 · 7 min read

Welcome to our ultimate guide on how to trim natural hair TWA! If you have recently transitioned to natural hair, you may have heard the term TWA or Teeny Weeny Afro. This is a popular hairstyle for those who have recently big chopped their relaxed or permed hair. While the TWA may look low-maintenance, it still requires proper maintenance to keep it healthy and manageable. One of the most important steps in maintaining a healthy TWA is regular trimming. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to trim natural hair TWA, along with tips and FAQs.

Why Trim Natural Hair TWA?

Trimming your natural hair TWA is essential to promote hair growth and prevent split ends. Split ends can travel up the hair shaft, causing breakage and damage. Trimming your hair regularly will help to remove the damaged ends and promote healthy hair growth.

How to Trim Natural Hair TWA: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prepare Your Tools

Before you start trimming your natural hair TWA, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It’s important to use sharp scissors to avoid damaging the hair.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Using your comb, gently detangle your hair to remove any knots or tangles. This will make it easier to trim your hair and ensure that you are cutting evenly.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your hair clips, section off your hair into small sections. This will make it easier to trim your hair evenly and ensure that you don’t miss any sections.

Step 4: Start Trimming

Starting with one section of your hair, use your scissors to trim off the ends. It’s important to only trim a small amount at a time, as you can always go back and trim more if needed.

Step 5: Repeat

Repeat the trimming process on each section of your hair, making sure to trim evenly and only cutting a small amount at a time.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Once you have finished trimming your hair, check for evenness by running your fingers through your hair. If you notice any uneven sections, go back and trim them.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

After trimming your hair, style it as desired. You can use a leave-in conditioner or styling product to help add moisture and definition to your hair.

Tips for Trimming Natural Hair TWA

Here are some tips to keep in mind when trimming your natural hair TWA:

  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Only trim a small amount at a time to avoid cutting off too much hair.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Detangle your hair before trimming to ensure even cutting.
  • Section your hair to make it easier to trim evenly.

FAQs

How often should I trim my natural hair TWA?

You should trim your natural hair TWA every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

What type of scissors should I use to trim my hair?

It’s important to use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair.

Can I trim my hair myself or should I go to a salon?

You can trim your hair yourself as long as you follow the proper steps and use the right tools. However, if you are unsure or want a professional opinion, you can go to a salon to have your hair trimmed.

Hair Care for Natural Hair TWA

Trimming your natural hair TWA is just one step in maintaining healthy hair. Here are some additional tips for caring for your natural hair TWA:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Protect your hair at night by using a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools and harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or scarf.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you can maintain a healthy and stylish natural hair TWA. Remember to trim your hair regularly to promote healthy hair growth and prevent split ends.
