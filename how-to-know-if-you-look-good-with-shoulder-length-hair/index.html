How To Know If You Look Good With Shoulder Length Hair

Written by Robby Apr 15, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Are you considering chopping off your hair to shoulder length? It can be nerve-wracking to make such a big change, especially when you're not sure if it will look good on you. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll give you tips on how to know if you look good with shoulder length hair, so you can confidently rock a new look.

Table of Contents

  • Determine Your Face Shape
  • Consider Your Hair Texture
  • Take Your Body Type into Account
  • Age Matters
  • Think About Your Lifestyle
  • Haircare Tips for Shoulder Length Hair
  • FAQs

Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in figuring out if you'll look good with shoulder length hair is to determine your face shape. A shoulder-length cut can flatter most face shapes, but there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • If you have a round face, avoid blunt cuts that end at your chin. Instead, go for layers that hit at your collarbone or longer.
  • If you have a square face, opt for a cut that softens your jawline, like a long bob with layers.
  • If you have a heart-shaped face, go for a cut that's chin-length or longer, with layers that start at your cheekbones.
  • If you have an oval face, lucky you! You can pretty much pull off any length and style.

Keep in mind that these are just general guidelines. If you have a specific feature you want to highlight or downplay, talk to your stylist about the best cut for you.

Consider Your Hair Texture

Your hair texture can also play a role in whether or not shoulder length hair will look good on you. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • If you have fine hair, a shoulder-length cut with layers can add volume and movement.
  • If you have thick hair, a blunt cut that hits at your collarbone can help thin it out.
  • If you have curly hair, a shoulder-length cut with layers can help define your curls and prevent them from looking weighed down.
  • If you have straight hair, a blunt cut can give you a sleek, polished look.

Again, these are just general guidelines. Talk to your stylist about what will work best for your specific hair type and texture.

Take Your Body Type into Account

Believe it or not, your body type can also play a role in whether or not shoulder length hair will look good on you. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • If you have a petite frame, a shoulder-length cut can help elongate your neck and make you appear taller.
  • If you have a larger frame, a shoulder-length cut with layers can balance out your proportions.
  • If you have broad shoulders, avoid a cut that ends at your collarbone. Instead, go for a longer cut that hits at your chest or lower.

Age Matters

Your age can also play a role in whether or not shoulder length hair will look good on you. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • If you're in your 20s or 30s, you can pretty much pull off any length and style.
  • If you're in your 40s or 50s, a shoulder-length cut with layers can be a flattering, youthful look.
  • If you're over 60, a shorter cut may be more flattering. However, if you're in good shape and have a youthful spirit, you can still rock a shoulder-length cut!

Think About Your Lifestyle

Finally, think about your lifestyle when deciding if shoulder length hair will work for you. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • If you're low-maintenance, a shoulder-length cut can be a great option. It's long enough to put up in a ponytail, but short enough to air-dry and go.
  • If you're high-maintenance, keep in mind that shoulder-length hair may require more styling than you're used to.
  • If you're active, a shoulder-length cut can be a great option. It won't get in your way during workouts or outdoor activities.
  • If you have a formal job, a shoulder-length cut can be a professional, polished look.

Haircare Tips for Shoulder Length Hair

Once you've decided to go for a shoulder-length cut, here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and looking great:

  • Invest in a good shampoo and conditioner that's right for your hair type.
  • Get regular trims to keep your ends healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before styling with hot tools.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find what works best for your hair.
  • Consider getting regular deep conditioning treatments to keep your hair shiny and healthy.

FAQs

Q: Will shoulder-length hair look good on me if I have a double chin?

A: A shoulder-length cut can actually be a great option if you have a double chin. It can help elongate your neck and draw attention away from your chin. However, avoid blunt cuts that end at your chin, as they can accentuate it.

Q: Will shoulder-length hair make my face look wider?

A: It depends on your face shape and the cut you choose. If you have a round face, avoid blunt cuts that end at your chin. Instead, go for layers that hit at your collarbone or longer. If you have a square face, opt for a cut that softens your jawline, like a long bob with layers.

Q: Can I still put my shoulder-length hair up in a ponytail?

A: Yes! Shoulder-length hair is long enough to put up in a ponytail, but short enough to air-dry and go. It's a great option for low-maintenance hair.

Now that you know how to determine if shoulder length hair will look good on you, it's time to make the chop! Remember, the most important thing is to choose a cut that makes you feel confident and beautiful.
