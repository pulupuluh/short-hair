Can Thick Hair Be Layered A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thick hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it can look voluminous and luxurious, it can also be difficult to manage and style. One solution to make thick hair more manageable is to get layers. But can thick hair be layered? The answer is yes, and in this guide, we will explore how to layer thick hair, step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care.

Description

Thick hair is characterized by having a high volume of hair strands. This hair type can be difficult to style and manage, and it may require more time and effort to maintain. Layers can help to reduce the weight of thick hair, add movement, and create a more flattering shape. However, it's important to consider the texture, density, and length of your hair before getting layers.

How To Layer Thick Hair

The process of layering thick hair involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create a tapered effect. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to layer thick hair:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before getting it layered. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for thick hair to make it more manageable.

Step 2: Choose The Right Length

The length of the layers will depend on the texture, density, and length of your hair. If you have curly hair, shorter layers can help to define curls. If you have straight hair, longer layers can create movement.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 4: Cut The Layers

Using sharp scissors, cut the hair at an angle to create layers. Start from the bottom section and work your way up. Take a small section of hair and twist it before cutting to create a textured effect.

Step 5: Blend The Layers

After cutting the layers, blend them by using thinning shears or a razor. This will create a seamless transition between the layers and give a more natural look.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when layering thick hair:

  • Consult with a hairstylist who has experience in cutting layers for thick hair.
  • Avoid getting too many layers, as it can make the hair look thin and stringy.
  • Consider getting long layers if you want to keep the length of your hair.
  • Use hair products that are designed for thick hair to maintain its volume and shape.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as it can damage the hair and make it more difficult to style.

FAQ

1. Can I layer my hair if it's already thin?

Yes, layering can add volume and thickness to thin hair. However, it's important to consult with a hairstylist to determine the right length and type of layers for your hair.

2. Can I layer my hair if it's curly?

Yes, layering can help to define curls and create a more flattering shape for curly hair.

3. How often should I get my hair layered?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the layers lose their shape. On average, it's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Hair Care

After getting your hair layered, it's important to maintain its health and shape. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a conditioner that's designed for thick hair to keep it moisturized and hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair as it can strip away natural oils and make it more difficult to style.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid pulling or tugging.
  • Avoid using hair ties with metal clasps as they can damage the hair and cause breakage.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.

Overall, layering thick hair is possible and can help to create a more manageable and flattering hairstyle. By following the steps and tips in this guide, you can achieve a layered look that suits your hair type and face shape.
