Is Point Cutting Good For Curly Hair

Written by Luffy Jul 19, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Curly hair is unique and requires a special approach when it comes to haircuts. One of the most popular techniques used by hairstylists is point cutting. In this article, we will explore whether point cutting is good for curly hair and give you tips on how to maintain your curls after the cut.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Point cutting is a haircutting technique that involves cutting the hair at an angle, creating a textured, layered look. It is a popular technique for adding volume and movement to hair. When it comes to curly hair, point cutting can be a good option for those who want to add definition to their curls and reduce bulk.

How to

If you are considering point cutting your curly hair, it is important to find a stylist who is experienced in working with curly hair. They will be able to assess your hair type and texture and determine if point cutting is right for you.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide to point cutting curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair.
  2. Towel dry your hair and detangle using a wide-tooth comb.
  3. Section your hair into small sections.
  4. Hold the scissors at a 45-degree angle and cut into the ends of your hair.
  5. Repeat on each section until you have achieved your desired look.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when point cutting your curly hair:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Be careful not to cut too much at once, as this can lead to uneven layers.
  • If you have very tight curls, consider leaving the ends a bit longer to avoid creating frizz.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about point cutting curly hair:

1. Will point cutting make my curls less defined?

No, point cutting should not make your curls less defined. In fact, it can enhance your curls by removing bulk and creating texture.

2. Is point cutting suitable for all types of curly hair?

Point cutting can work for most types of curly hair, but it is important to consult with a stylist to determine if it is right for you.

3. How often should I get a point cut?

It depends on the length and texture of your hair, but generally, every six to eight weeks is a good rule of thumb.

Hair Care

To maintain your curly hair after a point cut, it is important to take good care of your hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls moisturized. Avoid using heat tools and harsh chemicals, as these can damage your hair and lead to frizz.

In conclusion, point cutting can be a good option for those with curly hair who want to add definition and reduce bulk. It is important to find a stylist who is experienced in working with curly hair and to take good care of your hair after the cut. By following these tips, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls that turn heads wherever you go.
