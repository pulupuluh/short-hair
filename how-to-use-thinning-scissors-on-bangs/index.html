How To Use Thinning Scissors On Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Apr 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning scissors are a valuable tool for hairstylists and DIY hair enthusiasts alike. If you're looking to add texture and dimension to your bangs, thinning scissors can be a great way to achieve a natural, effortless look. In this article, we'll take you through the process of using thinning scissors on your bangs, step by step. We'll also share some tips and tricks to help you get the best results possible.

How to Use Thinning Scissors on Bangs

Before we get started, make sure you have a good pair of thinning scissors. You'll want to choose a pair with sharp, high-quality blades that are designed for thinning hair. Once you have your scissors, follow these steps:

Step 1: Section Your Bangs

Begin by separating your bangs from the rest of your hair. Use a comb to create a straight line across your forehead, and clip the rest of your hair back out of the way.

Step 2: Prep Your Bangs

Next, comb your bangs straight down and make sure they're completely dry. If your hair is wet or damp, it can be more difficult to achieve an even cut. You may also want to use some styling products to help your hair hold its shape.

Step 3: Hold Your Scissors Correctly

Hold your thinning scissors with your thumb and ring finger, and rest your middle finger on the opposite blade. This will give you the most control and precision while you're cutting.

Step 4: Make Small Cuts

Starting at the ends of your bangs, use the thinning scissors to make small, diagonal cuts. Work your way up towards the roots, taking care not to cut too much hair at once. The key is to create subtle layers and texture, rather than a drastic change in length.

Step 5: Blend as You Go

As you work, use your comb to blend the cut pieces of hair with the uncut pieces. This will help you achieve a more natural, seamless look.

Step 6: Double-Check Your Work

Once you've thinned out your bangs to your desired level, double-check your work in a mirror. Make any necessary adjustments, and don't be afraid to go back and make additional cuts if needed.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors on Bangs

Here are some additional tips to help you get the best results when using thinning scissors on your bangs:

  • Start with a little hair, and work your way up to more. It's much easier to take more hair off than to add it back on.
  • Use your non-dominant hand to hold your bangs taut while you cut. This will help you achieve a more even cut.
  • Take regular breaks to step back and assess your progress. It's easy to get carried away and cut too much, so give yourself time to evaluate your work as you go.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about using thinning scissors on bangs:

Can I use regular scissors instead of thinning scissors?

You can, but thinning scissors are specifically designed for thinning out hair without creating blunt lines. Using regular scissors can result in a choppy, uneven cut.

How often should I use thinning scissors on my bangs?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Some people may only need to use thinning scissors once every few months, while others may need to use them every few weeks.

Can I thin out my bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, but keep in mind that curly hair may appear shorter after it's been thinned out. You may want to leave your bangs a little longer than you would if you had straight hair.

Hair Care After Using Thinning Scissors on Bangs

After using thinning scissors on your bangs, it's important to take good care of your hair. Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. You may also want to consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and in good condition.

Using thinning scissors on your bangs can be a great way to add texture and dimension to your hair. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a natural, effortless look that complements your overall style. Give it a try, and see what a difference it can make!
