How To Cover Bald Spots With Braids A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia May 11, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're dealing with hair loss or thinning, you may be searching for ways to cover up bald spots. Braids are a great solution! In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cover bald spots with braids, step by step. We'll also share some tips and hair care advice to help you maintain your new look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cover Bald Spots with Braids
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for Braiding Hair with Bald Spots
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Braids

How to Cover Bald Spots with Braids

Braids can help cover bald spots by creating a fuller, more voluminous look. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and conditioning it thoroughly. Dry your hair using a towel or a hairdryer on a low setting. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to protect your hair from damage.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, section your hair into small, even parts. Be sure to focus on the areas with bald spots or thinning hair.

Step 3: Start Braiding

Take one section of hair and divide it into three equal parts. Begin braiding the hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Repeat this process for each section of hair, making sure to braid tightly and neatly.

Step 4: Secure the Braids

Once you've finished braiding your hair, use small hair elastics or bobby pins to secure the braids in place. Be sure to hide the elastics or pins underneath the braids for a seamless look.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here's a step-by-step breakdown of how to cover bald spots with braids:

  1. Prepare your hair by washing and conditioning it.
  2. Section your hair into small, even parts.
  3. Braid your hair tightly and neatly, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  4. Secure the braids with small elastics or bobby pins.

Tips for Braiding Hair with Bald Spots

Here are some tips to keep in mind when braiding hair with bald spots:

  • Choose a braiding style that suits your hair type and face shape.
  • Avoid braiding your hair too tightly, as this can cause further hair loss or breakage.
  • Be gentle when braiding the areas with bald spots or thinning hair.
  • Use a hair oil or leave-in conditioner to protect your hair from damage.
  • Consider using hair growth products to help promote hair growth in the bald spots.

FAQ

Can braids cause hair loss?

If done too tightly or for extended periods of time, braids can cause hair loss or breakage. It's important to be gentle with your hair and avoid pulling too tightly when braiding.

How long can I keep my braids in?

You can keep your braids in for up to 8 weeks, but it's important to take breaks in between to give your hair a rest.

How do I care for my braids?

To care for your braids, avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair. Instead, use a lightweight leave-in conditioner or hair oil. Be sure to wash your scalp regularly to prevent buildup and keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care Tips for Braids

Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind when wearing braids:

  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Wash your scalp regularly to prevent buildup and keep your hair healthy.
  • Be gentle when taking out your braids to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Consider using a hair growth product to help promote hair growth in the bald spots.

By following these steps and tips, you can cover bald spots with braids for a fuller, more voluminous look. Remember to be gentle with your hair and take breaks in between braiding to keep it healthy and strong.
