How To Do Low Fade Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Apr 18, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking for a fresh, modern haircut, a low fade might be just what you need. A low fade is a popular hairstyle that gradually tapers from the sides to the back of the head, creating a clean and sleek look. In this article, we'll walk you through how to do a low fade haircut step-by-step, as well as offer some tips and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Do Low Fade Haircut
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Great Low Fade
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Do Low Fade Haircut

Before we get started, it's important to note that doing a low fade haircut requires some skill and practice. If you're not confident in your abilities, it's always a good idea to go to a professional barber or stylist. That being said, if you're ready to give it a try, here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers with adjustable guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircut cape or towel
  • Hair spray or gel

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to achieve a low fade haircut:

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before starting the haircut. This will make it easier to work with and ensure a clean cut.

2. Use clippers to create the initial fade

Start by using clippers with a long guard (around 4 or 5) to create the initial fade. Use the clipper to cut around the sides and back of the head, leaving the hair longer on top. Work your way up the head, gradually switching to shorter guards (3, 2, and 1).

3. Blend the fade with scissors

Once you've created the initial fade with clippers, use scissors to blend the hair on top with the shorter hair on the sides and back. Start by cutting the hair on top to the desired length, then use scissors to blend the hair in the middle of the fade.

4. Create a defined line between the fade and the longer hair on top

Use clippers or scissors to create a defined line between the shorter hair on the sides and back and the longer hair on top. This line should gradually taper from the sides to the back of the head.

5. Clean up the edges

Use clippers or scissors to clean up the edges around the ears and neckline. Make sure to trim any stray hairs.

6. Style the hair on top

Use hair spray or gel to style the hair on top. You can choose to leave it messy or slick it back for a more polished look.

Tips for a Great Low Fade

  • Practice makes perfect! If you're new to cutting hair, start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one.
  • Take your time and be patient. A great haircut takes time and attention to detail.
  • Invest in high-quality clippers and scissors. This will make the process easier and ensure a clean cut.
  • Use a comb to help guide your cuts and ensure an even fade.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help or advice from a professional barber or stylist.

FAQs

What is a low fade?

A low fade is a popular hairstyle that gradually tapers from the sides to the back of the head, creating a clean and sleek look.

Can I do a low fade haircut at home?

Yes, you can do a low fade haircut at home, but it does require some practice and skill. If you're not confident in your abilities, it's always a good idea to go to a professional barber or stylist.

How often should I get a low fade haircut?

This depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want to keep the style. Generally, a low fade should be maintained every 2-3 weeks.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've achieved your desired low fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the look. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it and make it harder to style.
  • Use hair products sparingly and avoid products that contain alcohol, which can dry out your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your low fade.

Congratulations, you've now learned how to do a low fade haircut! With some practice and patience, you'll be able to achieve a clean and stylish look that will turn heads.
