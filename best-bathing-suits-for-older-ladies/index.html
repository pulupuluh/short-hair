How to Choose the Right Bathing Suit

Choosing the right bathing suit can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. Here are some tips to help you choose the right one:

Consider your body type

One of the most important things to consider when choosing a bathing suit is your body type. If you have a pear-shaped body, for example, a one-piece bathing suit with a high-cut leg can help elongate your legs and balance out your hips. If you have an hourglass figure, a tankini or a one-piece with ruching can accentuate your curves.

Opt for support

As we age, our bodies change, and we may need more support in certain areas. Look for bathing suits with built-in bras, underwire, or adjustable straps to provide the support you need.

Choose the right fabric

The right fabric can make all the difference in how your bathing suit looks and feels. Look for bathing suits made from stretchy, supportive materials like spandex or Lycra. Avoid thin or flimsy fabrics that can show lumps and bumps.

Go for comfort

Comfort is key when it comes to swimwear. Look for bathing suits with soft, comfortable materials and a good fit. You don't want to be tugging at your bathing suit all day!

Hair Care Tips for the Beach or Pool

Swimming can be tough on your hair, but there are steps you can take to keep your hair healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

Wet your hair before swimming

Wetting your hair before swimming can help prevent it from absorbing too much chlorine or saltwater.

Wear a swim cap

If you're serious about protecting your hair, consider wearing a swim cap. It can help keep your hair dry and protected from chlorine and saltwater.

Rinse your hair after swimming

Rinsing your hair after swimming can help remove any chlorine or saltwater that may have accumulated. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
