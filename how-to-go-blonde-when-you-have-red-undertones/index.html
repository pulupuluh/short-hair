How To Go Blonde When You Have Red Undertones A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Apr 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have red undertones in your hair, going blonde might seem like a daunting task. However, with the right techniques and products, you can achieve the blonde look you've been dreaming of. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of going blonde when you have red undertones. Read on to learn more.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Go Blonde When You Have Red Undertones
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Going Blonde with Red Undertones
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Going Blonde

Description

Having red undertones in your hair can make going blonde a bit more complicated. Red undertones can cause your hair to turn brassy or orange when lightened, which is not the desired result. However, with some careful planning and the right products, you can achieve a beautiful blonde shade that complements your skin tone and looks natural.

How to Go Blonde When You Have Red Undertones

The key to going blonde when you have red undertones is to neutralize the red tones in your hair before lightening it. This can be done by using a toner or a color corrector. It's important to choose the right product for your hair type and color, so we recommend consulting with a professional stylist before attempting to go blonde on your own.

Choose the Right Shade of Blonde

When choosing a shade of blonde, it's important to take your skin tone into consideration. If you have warm undertones in your skin, you'll want to choose a warm blonde shade such as honey or golden blonde. If you have cool undertones, you'll want to choose a cool blonde shade such as ash blonde or platinum.

Use a Color Corrector

A color corrector can help neutralize the red tones in your hair before lightening it. Look for a color corrector that's specifically designed to neutralize red tones. Apply the color corrector according to the instructions on the packaging, and leave it on for the recommended amount of time.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to go blonde when you have red undertones:

  1. Consult with a professional stylist to determine the best course of action for your hair.
  2. Choose a shade of blonde that complements your skin tone.
  3. Use a color corrector to neutralize the red tones in your hair.
  4. Lighten your hair using a professional-grade lightening product.
  5. Tone your hair to achieve your desired shade of blonde.
  6. Deep condition your hair to restore moisture and prevent damage.
  7. Maintain your blonde hair with regular toning and conditioning treatments.

Tips for Going Blonde with Red Undertones

Here are some tips to keep in mind when going blonde with red undertones:

  • Consult with a professional stylist before attempting to go blonde on your own.
  • Choose a shade of blonde that complements your skin tone.
  • Use a color corrector to neutralize the red tones in your hair before lightening it.
  • Use a professional-grade lightening product to minimize damage to your hair.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to restore moisture and prevent damage.

FAQ

Can I go blonde if I have red undertones in my hair?

Yes, you can go blonde if you have red undertones in your hair. However, it's important to neutralize the red tones before lightening your hair to avoid a brassy or orange result.

What shade of blonde should I choose?

The shade of blonde you choose should complement your skin tone. If you have warm undertones, choose a warm blonde shade such as honey or golden blonde. If you have cool undertones, choose a cool blonde shade such as ash blonde or platinum.

How can I prevent damage to my hair when going blonde?

To prevent damage to your hair when going blonde, use a professional-grade lightening product and deep condition your hair regularly. It's also important to consult with a professional stylist before attempting to go blonde on your own.

Hair Care After Going Blonde

After going blonde, it's important to take good care of your hair to maintain its health and prevent damage. Here are some tips for hair care after going blonde:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading and maintain your blonde shade.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to restore moisture and prevent damage.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, and use a heat protectant when styling your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the health of your hair.

Going blonde when you have red undertones may seem like a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, it's definitely achievable. Whether you choose a warm or cool blonde shade, make sure to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
