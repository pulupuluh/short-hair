How To Style Hair In Summer Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Jul 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Summer is a time of fun, sun, and relaxation. However, it can also be a challenging time for maintaining a great hairstyle. High humidity, sweat, and the constant need for air conditioning can all wreak havoc on your locks. But don't worry, we've got you covered with these tips and tricks on how to style hair in summer.

How to

1. Keep it Simple

In summer, less is more. The more complicated your hairstyle, the more likely it is to be affected by humidity, sweat, and other environmental factors. So, try to keep it simple with a classic ponytail, braid, or bun. These styles are not only easy to do but also protect your hair from the heat and sun.

2. Use Protective Products

Protective products like leave-in conditioners, hair oils, and heat protectants can help keep hair healthy and shiny in summer. They provide a barrier against the sun, salt, and chlorine that can damage hair. Apply these products before styling to prevent frizz and breakage.

3. Embrace the Beachy Waves

Beachy waves are perfect for summer. They are effortless, chic, and low-maintenance. To achieve this look, apply a texturizing spray to damp hair, scrunch it up, and let it air dry. You can also use a curling iron to create loose waves.

4. Up Your Braiding Game

Braids are a great way to keep hair off your face and neck in summer. There are so many different styles to choose from, including French braids, Dutch braids, fishtail braids, and more. Experiment with different braids to find the one that suits your hair type and face shape.

Step by Step

1. Classic Ponytail

Step 1: Brush your hair and gather it into a high ponytail.

Step 2: Secure the ponytail with an elastic band.

Step 3: Wrap a small section of hair around the base of the ponytail to hide the elastic band.

Step 4: Secure the wrapped section of hair with a bobby pin.

2. Beachy Waves

Step 1: Apply a texturizing spray to damp hair.

Step 2: Scrunch your hair up using your fingers to create waves.

Step 3: Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to speed up the process.

3. Dutch Braid

Step 1: Divide your hair into three sections at the top of your head.

Step 2: Cross the right section under the middle section.

Step 3: Cross the left section under the middle section.

Step 4: Add a small section of hair to the right section, then cross it under the middle section.

Step 5: Add a small section of hair to the left section, then cross it under the middle section.

Step 6: Repeat steps 4 and 5 until you reach the nape of your neck.

Step 7: Finish the braid with a regular three-strand braid and secure with an elastic band.

Tips

- Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible in summer.

- Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the sun.

- Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.

- If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray first.

- Don't forget to hydrate your hair by drinking plenty of water and using a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

FAQ

Q: What's the best way to prevent frizz in summer?

A: The best way to prevent frizz in summer is to keep your hair hydrated and use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil. You can also use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction while you sleep.

Q: Can I still color my hair in summer?

A: Yes, you can still color your hair in summer. However, you should avoid excessive sun exposure and use a color-protecting shampoo and conditioner.

Hair Care

Summer can be tough on your hair, but with the right care and styling techniques, you can keep your locks looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to keep it simple, use protective products, and experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for you. Stay hydrated and keep your hair hydrated too, and you'll be rocking the perfect summer hairstyle in no time.
