How To Find Out What Type Of Black Hair You Have

Written by Eveline Aug 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Knowing your hair type is essential to properly care for your tresses. While black hair comes in various textures, it can be challenging to determine what type of hair you have. This article will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to identify your hair type and tips on how to care for your hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Hair Type
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Caring for Your Hair Type
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Routine by Hair Type

How to Determine Your Hair Type

Before we dive into the specifics, it's worth noting that black hair types are generally classified into four categories:

  • Type 1: Straight Hair
  • Type 2: Wavy Hair
  • Type 3: Curly Hair
  • Type 4: Coily Hair

Keep in mind that while these categories are helpful, your hair may not fit neatly into one of them. Here are a few ways to determine your hair type:

Visual Inspection

Take a good look at your hair in its natural state. Is it straight, wavy, curly or coily? Do you have a combination of textures? If you're not sure, you can also examine a single strand of hair to see its natural shape.

Shrinkage Test

Wet your hair and let it air dry. Measure your hair while wet and compare it to the length when it's dry. If it's significantly shorter when dry, you likely have coily hair.

Pull Test

Take a strand of hair and gently pull it between your fingers. If it stretches easily and returns to its original shape, you likely have wavy hair. If it stretches but doesn't bounce back, you probably have curly or coily hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you've determined your hair type, here's a step-by-step guide to help you care for your tresses:

Step 1: Shampoo

Choose a shampoo that's formulated for your hair type. If you have coily hair, look for a moisturizing shampoo to prevent breakage. If you have wavy hair, a lightweight shampoo will suffice.

Step 2: Condition

Conditioner is essential to keeping your hair healthy and hydrated. Opt for a conditioner that's nourishing and moisturizing, especially if you have coily hair.

Step 3: Detangle

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to prevent breakage.

Step 4: Style

Choose a styling product that's appropriate for your hair type. If you have coily hair, a butter or oil-based product will provide the moisture your hair needs. If you have wavy hair, a mousse or gel will help define your waves.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair Type

Here are a few additional tips that will help you care for your hair:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent breakage and minimize frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools frequently, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Moisturize your hair frequently to prevent dryness.

FAQs

What if my hair doesn't fit neatly into one category?

It's common for black hair to have a combination of textures. If this is the case for you, focus on caring for the areas that require the most attention.

How often should I wash my hair?

This varies depending on your hair type and lifestyle. Some people with coily hair may only need to wash their hair once a week, while others with straight hair may need to wash it every other day.

Hair Care Routine by Hair Type

Here's a breakdown of how to care for each hair type:

Type 1: Straight Hair

  • Shampoo every other day
  • Use a lightweight conditioner
  • Use a heat protectant when using heat styling tools

Type 2: Wavy Hair

  • Shampoo every 2-3 days
  • Use a moisturizing conditioner
  • Use a mousse or gel to define your waves

Type 3: Curly Hair

  • Shampoo once a week
  • Use a deep conditioner once a month
  • Use a curl defining cream to enhance your curls

Type 4: Coily Hair

  • Shampoo once a week
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Use a butter or oil-based product to seal in moisture

By following these tips and guidelines, you'll be able to care for your hair properly and achieve healthy, beautiful tresses.
