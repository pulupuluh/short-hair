Why Is My Hair Falling Out At The Crown

Written by Petter Sep 08, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you've noticed that your hair is thinning or falling out at the crown of your head, you may be wondering what's causing it. Hair loss can be distressing and can affect your self-esteem, but understanding the reasons behind it can help you take steps to address the problem. In this article, we'll explore some of the common causes of hair loss at the crown and what you can do about it.

Description

Thinning or balding at the crown of the head is a common type of hair loss in both men and women. This area of the scalp is particularly vulnerable to hair loss because it is where the hair is most sensitive to hormonal changes and genetics. Hair loss at the crown can be gradual or sudden, and it may be accompanied by other symptoms such as itching, flaking, or redness.

How to Determine the Cause of Your Hair Loss

If you're experiencing hair loss at the crown, the first step is to determine the underlying cause. This can be done by consulting with a dermatologist or trichologist, who can examine your scalp and hair and recommend appropriate treatment. Some of the common causes of hair loss at the crown include:

1. Androgenetic Alopecia

Androgenetic alopecia, also known as male or female pattern baldness, is the most common cause of hair loss at the crown. This condition is caused by a genetic predisposition to sensitivity to androgens (male hormones), which can cause miniaturization of the hair follicles and eventually lead to hair loss. Androgenetic alopecia is more common in men, but it can also affect women.

2. Telogen Effluvium

Telogen effluvium is a type of temporary hair loss that occurs when a large number of hair follicles enter the resting (telogen) phase of the hair growth cycle at the same time. This can be triggered by a variety of factors, such as stress, illness, hormonal changes, or medications. Telogen effluvium can cause diffuse hair loss all over the scalp, but it may be more noticeable at the crown.

3. Alopecia Areata

Alopecia areata is an autoimmune condition that causes patchy hair loss on the scalp and other areas of the body. It occurs when the immune system attacks the hair follicles, causing them to become inflamed and damaged. Alopecia areata can affect people of all ages and genders, and it may cause hair loss at the crown.

Step by Step Guide to Prevent Hair Loss at the Crown

If you're concerned about hair loss at the crown, there are steps you can take to prevent further damage and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips:

1. Eat a healthy diet

Eating a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins, minerals, and proteins can help promote healthy hair growth. Focus on foods that are high in biotin, vitamin D, iron, and zinc, which are essential for hair health.

2. Reduce stress

Stress can trigger hair loss by disrupting the normal hair growth cycle. Try to reduce stress by practicing relaxation techniques such as meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises.

3. Avoid tight hairstyles

Tight hairstyles such as braids, weaves, or ponytails can pull on the hair and cause damage to the follicles. Opt for looser hairstyles that don't put excessive tension on the scalp.

4. Use gentle hair care products

Harsh chemicals and styling products can damage the hair and contribute to hair loss. Use gentle, natural products that are free from sulfates, parabens, and other harmful ingredients.

5. Seek professional help

If you're experiencing hair loss at the crown, seek professional help from a dermatologist or trichologist. They can recommend appropriate treatment options such as topical medications, hair transplants, or laser therapy.

Hair Care Tips for Hair Loss at the Crown

If you're experiencing hair loss at the crown, it's important to take good care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons can damage the hair and contribute to hair loss. Avoid using these tools or use them sparingly.

2. Don't over-wash your hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils and cause damage to the follicles. Aim to wash your hair every other day or every few days.

3. Massage your scalp

Massaging your scalp can improve blood flow to the hair follicles and promote healthy hair growth. Use gentle circular motions with your fingertips to stimulate the scalp.

FAQs

Q: Can hair loss at the crown be reversed?

A: The ability to reverse hair loss at the crown depends on the underlying cause. In some cases, hair loss may be reversible with appropriate treatment. However, in other cases, such as androgenetic alopecia, hair loss may be permanent.

Q: Are there any natural remedies for hair loss at the crown?

A: There are some natural remedies that may help promote healthy hair growth, such as using essential oils, taking supplements, or using herbal remedies. However, it's important to consult with a professional before trying any natural remedies, as they may not be appropriate for everyone.

Conclusion

Hair loss at the crown can be distressing, but there are steps you can take to prevent further damage and promote healthy hair growth. By understanding the underlying causes of hair loss and taking good care of your hair, you can help maintain a healthy, full head of hair.
