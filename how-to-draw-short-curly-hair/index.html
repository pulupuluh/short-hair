How To Draw Short Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Mar 06, 2023 · 8 min read

Short curly hair can be tricky to draw, but with a few tips and tricks, you can create a beautiful and realistic drawing. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of drawing short curly hair, from sketching to shading. Whether you are a beginner or an experienced artist, this guide will help you master the art of drawing short curly hair.

How to Draw Short Curly Hair

Drawing short curly hair can seem daunting, but with practice, it can become second nature. The key is to understand the basic shape and texture of curly hair and to use shading techniques to give it depth and dimension. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Sketch the Basic Shape

Start by sketching the basic shape of the hair. For short curly hair, this may mean drawing a rounded shape that follows the contour of the head. Use light, loose strokes to create the initial sketch.

Step 2: Add the Curls

Next, add the curls to the hair. Curly hair typically has tight, coiled curls that vary in size and shape. Use short, curved strokes to create the curls, and vary the length and direction of the strokes to create a natural-looking texture.

Step 3: Define the Hairline

Once you have sketched the basic shape and curls of the hair, define the hairline. For short curly hair, the hairline may be uneven and have wispy strands of hair that frame the face. Use short, wispy strokes to create the hairline, and vary the length and direction of the strokes to create a natural-looking effect.

Step 4: Add Shading and Highlights

Finally, add shading and highlights to the hair to give it depth and dimension. Use darker shades of pencil to create shadows in the areas where the hair is thickest, and lighter shades to create highlights in the areas where the hair is thinner or catches the light. Blend the shades together using a blending tool or your finger to create a smooth, natural-looking effect.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some hair care tips for curly hair:

  • Use sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of natural oils.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle wet hair.
  • Avoid brushing curly hair, as it can cause frizz and damage.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry curly hair without disrupting the curl pattern.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define and moisturize the curls.

By following these tips and practicing your drawing skills, you can create beautiful and realistic drawings of short curly hair. With time and patience, you can master the art of drawing curly hair and create stunning works of art.
