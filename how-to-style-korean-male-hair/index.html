How To Style Korean Male Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Apr 13, 2023 · 12 min read

Korean male hair is known for its versatility and unique style. From short, textured cuts to long, flowing locks, Korean men are always experimenting with new and innovative hair trends. If you're looking to style your hair like a Korean man, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to style Korean male hair. We'll cover the basics, including hair types, face shapes, and styling tools. We'll also provide step-by-step instructions on how to achieve some of the most popular Korean male hairstyles. So, let's get started!

Hair Types

Korean men have a wide range of hair types, from straight and smooth to curly and wavy. It's important to know your hair type so that you can choose a hairstyle that works best for you. Here are some of the most common hair types among Korean men:

  • Straight and smooth
  • Wavy
  • Curly
  • Thick
  • Thin

Each hair type requires a different approach when it comes to styling. For example, if you have thick hair, you may need to use a stronger hold styling product to keep your hair in place. On the other hand, if you have thin hair, you'll want to avoid heavy products that can weigh your hair down.

Face Shapes

Just like hair types, face shapes also play a role in determining the best hairstyle for you. Here are some of the most common face shapes among Korean men:

  • Oval
  • Square
  • Round
  • Heart
  • Diamond

Each face shape has its own unique characteristics, and certain hairstyles can accentuate or downplay those features. For example, if you have a round face, you may want to avoid hairstyles that add volume to the sides of your head, as this can make your face appear even rounder.

Styling Tools

Before we dive into specific hairstyles, let's talk about the tools you'll need to achieve them. Here are some of the most common styling tools used by Korean men:

  • Hair dryer
  • Flat iron
  • Curling iron
  • Round brush
  • Styling cream
  • Pomade
  • Wax
  • Hairspray

Depending on your hair type and the hairstyle you're going for, you may not need all of these tools. However, having a few key styling products on hand can make a big difference in achieving your desired look.

Short Hairstyles

Short hairstyles are a popular choice among Korean men. They're easy to maintain and can be styled in a variety of ways. Here are some of the most popular short hairstyles for Korean men:

Crew Cut

The crew cut is a classic short hairstyle that never goes out of style. It's a simple and clean look that works well for most face shapes and hair types. To achieve a crew cut, simply ask your hairstylist to cut your hair short on the sides and back, leaving about an inch of length on top. You can then style the top of your hair in a variety of ways, from slicking it back to spiking it up.

Undercut

The undercut is a more edgy and stylish take on the crew cut. It involves shaving the sides and back of your head, leaving the top long and textured. This look works well for those with thick hair, as it allows you to show off your natural volume and texture. To style an undercut, simply apply a small amount of styling cream or pomade to your hair and mess it up with your fingers.

Textured Crop

The textured crop is a modern and trendy hairstyle that's perfect for those with wavy or curly hair. It involves cutting your hair short on the sides and back, leaving the top longer and textured. To achieve this look, ask your hairstylist to cut your hair in layers, with the top layers being slightly longer than the bottom layers. You can then style your hair by using a curling iron or flat iron to create loose, beachy waves.

Medium Hairstyles

Medium hairstyles are a great choice for those who want a little more length and versatility in their hair. Here are some of the most popular medium hairstyles for Korean men:

Side Part

The side part is a classic and timeless hairstyle that works well for most face shapes and hair types. It involves parting your hair to one side and combing it over, creating a sleek and polished look. To achieve a side part, simply apply some styling cream or pomade to your hair and comb it over to one side.

Messy Quiff

The messy quiff is a more relaxed and casual take on the classic quiff hairstyle. It involves keeping the top of your hair longer and textured, while keeping the sides and back short. To style a messy quiff, simply apply some wax or pomade to your hair and mess it up with your fingers. You can then use a blow dryer to add volume and texture to your hair.

Layered Cut

The layered cut is a great choice for those with thick hair, as it helps to remove some of the weight and bulk from your hair. It involves cutting your hair in layers, with the top layers being slightly longer than the bottom layers. This creates a natural and textured look that's easy to style. To achieve a layered cut, simply ask your hairstylist to cut your hair in layers, and use a small amount of styling cream or wax to keep your hair in place.

Long Hairstyles

Long hairstyles are a bold and daring choice for Korean men. They require a bit more maintenance and care, but the results can be stunning. Here are some of the most popular long hairstyles for Korean men:

Man Bun

The man bun is a trendy and stylish hairstyle that's perfect for those with long hair. It involves pulling your hair back into a bun at the crown of your head, leaving some strands loose to frame your face. To achieve a man bun, simply pull your hair back into a ponytail and twist it into a bun. You can then use bobby pins or a hair tie to keep it in place.

Long and Straight

For those with naturally straight hair, keeping your hair long and flowing can create a sleek and sophisticated look. To achieve this style, simply let your hair grow out and use a flat iron to keep it straight and smooth. You can also add some texture by using a small amount of wax or pomade to create loose waves.

Wavy and Layered

If you have wavy or curly hair, keeping your hair long and layered can create a natural and textured look. To achieve this style, ask your hairstylist to cut your hair in layers, with the top layers being slightly longer than the bottom layers. You can then use a curling iron or flat iron to create loose waves, and use a small amount of styling cream or wax to keep your hair in place.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when styling your Korean male hair:

  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find one that works best for you.
  • Use styling products that are appropriate for your hair type.
  • Don't be afraid to ask your hairstylist for advice on which hairstyle would work best for you.
  • Take good care of your hair by using a quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible.
