How To Cut A Layered Bob With Bangs Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 18, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're looking for a fresh new hairstyle, a layered bob with bangs can be a great option. Not only is it versatile and easy to style, but it's also a low-maintenance cut that works well for many face shapes. And the best part? You can cut it yourself at home! In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting a layered bob with bangs, along with some tips and hair care advice.

How to Cut a Layered Bob with Bangs Yourself

Cutting a layered bob with bangs may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut at home. Here are the basic steps:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need:

  • Scissors (ideally, hair cutting scissors)
  • A comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A mirror

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by parting your hair in the middle and dividing it into two sections. Secure the top section with a clip or hair tie.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the ends between your fingers and cut them straight across. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way around the back of your head.

Step 4: Add Layers

To add layers, take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight up. Hold the ends between your fingers and cut them at an angle, moving up towards the top layer of hair. Continue adding layers around the back of your head, making sure to blend them seamlessly with the bottom layer.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top section of your hair and comb it straight down. Trim the ends straight across, making sure to match the length of the bottom layer. You can also add some layers to the top section if you want a more textured look.

Step 6: Cut Your Bangs

To cut your bangs, comb them straight down and hold them with your fingers. Cut them straight across, starting from the middle and working your way out towards the sides. If you want side-swept bangs, cut them at an angle.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, use a mirror to check the back and sides of your hair. Make any necessary adjustments, but be careful not to overdo it.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're more of a visual learner, here's a step-by-step guide to cutting a layered bob with bangs:

  1. Part your hair in the middle and divide it into two sections. Secure the top section with a clip or hair tie.
  2. Starting at the back of your head, cut the bottom layer of hair straight across.
  3. Add layers by cutting small sections of hair at an angle, moving up towards the top layer.
  4. Release the top section of your hair and trim the ends straight across, making sure to match the length of the bottom layer.
  5. Cut your bangs straight across or at an angle, depending on your preference.
  6. Check your work using a mirror and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting a Layered Bob with Bangs

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect layered bob with bangs:

  • Start with dry, clean hair to make it easier to see what you're cutting.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as it can be difficult to fix mistakes.
  • Use a mirror to check the back and sides of your hair as you go.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own bangs, consider practicing on a wig or consulting a professional stylist.

FAQs about Cutting a Layered Bob with Bangs

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting a layered bob with bangs:

Can I cut a layered bob with bangs on curly hair?

Yes, you can cut a layered bob with bangs on curly hair, but it's important to keep in mind that the curls will add extra volume and bounce to the cut. Consider cutting your hair when it's dry and in its natural state, so you can see exactly how it will look.

How often should I trim my layered bob with bangs?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

If you make a mistake while cutting, don't panic! Depending on the severity of the mistake, you may be able to fix it by trimming a little more hair or blending it with the surrounding layers. If all else fails, you can always visit a professional stylist to fix the problem.

Hair Care for a Layered Bob with Bangs

Once you've cut your layered bob with bangs, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and stylish. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to the ends of your hair to prevent split ends.
  • Visit a professional stylist for regular trims to maintain the shape of your cut.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut a layered bob with bangs yourself and enjoy a stylish new look!
