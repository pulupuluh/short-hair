Description

Big chopping is a term used in the natural hair community to describe the act of cutting off all of your relaxed or chemically treated hair to start fresh with your natural hair texture. This can be a big decision for many black women who have grown accustomed to having straight hair, but it can also be a liberating and empowering experience.

How to Big Chop Black Hair

The first step in big chopping black hair is to decide when you are ready to make the change. This is a personal decision and can be influenced by a variety of factors, including the desire for healthier hair, the desire for a new look, or the desire to embrace your natural hair texture. Once you have made the decision to big chop, you will need to gather the necessary tools and products.

Tools and Products Needed:

  • Sharp scissors or clippers
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Conditioner
  • Shampoo
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Styling products (optional)

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to big chop black hair:

Step 1: Prepare your hair

Wash and condition your hair as usual. This will help to make the hair easier to cut and also help to prevent breakage.

Step 2: Section your hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to section your hair into four or more sections. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly and also help to prevent tangles.

Step 3: Cut your hair

Starting with one section of your hair, use sharp scissors or clippers to cut off all of the relaxed or chemically treated hair. Cut as close to the roots as possible to ensure that you are removing all of the treated hair. Repeat this process on each section of your hair until all of the treated hair has been removed.

Step 4: Wash and condition your hair

Wash and condition your hair again to remove any loose hair and to prepare your hair for styling. Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair and to provide moisture.

Step 5: Style your hair

You can choose to style your hair in a variety of ways, including a wash-and-go, twist-out, or braid-out. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for your hair texture.

Tips for Big Chopping Black Hair

  • Make sure that you are ready to big chop before you start. It can be a drastic change, so take your time to think it through.
  • Find a stylist who is experienced with cutting natural hair if you are not comfortable doing it yourself.
  • Invest in high-quality hair care products to help nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Be patient with your hair as it grows out. It can take time to adjust to your natural hair texture.

FAQs about Big Chopping Black Hair

Q: Will my hair grow back after I big chop?

A: Yes, your hair will grow back after you big chop. In fact, many women find that their hair grows faster and healthier after they start taking better care of it.

Q: Will my hair be damaged after I big chop?

A: No, your hair will not be damaged after you big chop. The hair that you cut off is already dead, so removing it will not cause any additional damage to your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my hair after big chopping?

A: You should aim to wash your hair once a week or every other week, depending on your hair type and texture. This will help to keep your hair clean and moisturized.

Hair Care after Big Chopping Black Hair

After big chopping, it is important to take care of your natural hair to ensure that it stays healthy and strong. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or oil.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair at night by sleeping with a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Big chopping black hair can be a scary but rewarding experience. By following these tips and taking care of your natural hair, you can have healthy, beautiful hair that you are proud of.
