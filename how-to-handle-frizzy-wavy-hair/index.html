How To Handle Frizzy Wavy Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Aug 20, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have frizzy wavy hair, you know the struggle of trying to tame your locks. No matter what you do, your hair seems to have a mind of its own. But don't worry, there are ways to manage frizz and embrace your natural waves. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for handling frizzy wavy hair.

How to

First and foremost, it's important to understand what causes frizz in wavy hair. Frizz occurs when the cuticle layer of your hair is raised, allowing moisture to enter and causing the strands to swell. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including humidity, heat styling, and using harsh hair products.

To combat frizz, start with a good shampoo and conditioner that is designed for wavy hair. Look for products that are sulfate-free and contain moisturizing ingredients like coconut oil or shea butter.

Once you've washed and conditioned your hair, gently towel dry it to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with a towel, as this can cause more frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently blot your hair.

Next, apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your damp hair. This will help to lock in moisture and prevent frizz. Be sure to apply the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends where dryness and frizz tend to be most prominent.

If you're planning on heat styling your hair, be sure to use a heat protectant spray or serum before using your flat iron or curling wand. This will help to prevent damage and minimize frizz.

When it comes to styling your hair, avoid brushing or combing it when it's dry. This can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet, and then let it air dry or diffuse it with a hair dryer on a low heat setting.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to handling frizzy wavy hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for wavy hair.

Step 2: Gently towel dry your hair to remove excess water.

Step 3: Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your damp hair.

Step 4: If you're planning on heat styling your hair, use a heat protectant spray or serum before using your flat iron or curling wand.

Step 5: Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.

Step 6: Let your hair air dry or diffuse it with a hair dryer on a low heat setting.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for managing frizzy wavy hair:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz while you sleep.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause frizz.
  • Try using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles, such as braids or updos, to help control frizz.
  • Consider getting a haircut that is designed for wavy hair, such as long layers or a textured bob.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about handling frizzy wavy hair:

How often should I wash my hair if I have frizzy wavy hair?

It depends on your hair type and how oily or dry your scalp is. Some people with wavy hair can go several days between washes, while others may need to wash their hair more frequently. Try to find a washing schedule that works for you and your hair.

Can I still straighten my hair if I have frizzy wavy hair?

Yes, you can still straighten your hair if you have frizzy wavy hair. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray or serum before using your flat iron, and try to limit heat styling to once or twice a week to prevent damage.

Will cutting my hair help to reduce frizz?

It can. Getting a haircut that is designed for wavy hair, such as long layers or a textured bob, can help to reduce frizz and make your hair more manageable.

Hair Care

Taking care of frizzy wavy hair requires a little extra effort, but it's worth it to have healthy, beautiful locks. Be sure to use products that are designed for wavy hair, avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling, and experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for you. With these tips and tricks, you can manage frizz and embrace your natural waves.
