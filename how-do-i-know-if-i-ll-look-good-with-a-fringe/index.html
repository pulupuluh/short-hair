How Do I Know If I ll Look Good With A Fringe

Written by Robby May 27, 2023 Â· 10 min read

If you're considering getting a fringe, you might be wondering if it's the right choice for you. Fringes can be a great way to update your look, but they aren't for everyone. In this guide, we'll go over how to know if you'll look good with a fringe, as well as some tips for taking care of your new hairstyle.

Table of Contents

  • How To Know If You'll Look Good with a Fringe
  • Step by Step: Getting a Fringe
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe
  • FAQs About Fringes
  • Hair Care Tips for Your Fringe

How To Know If You'll Look Good with a Fringe

There are a few things to consider when deciding if a fringe will suit you:

1. Face Shape

The shape of your face is a key factor in determining whether a fringe will look good on you. If you have a round face, a fringe can help to create a more angular look. On the other hand, if you have a square face, a fringe can soften your features. If you're not sure what your face shape is, try measuring the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline with a tape measure. Then, compare the measurements to see which shape best describes your face.

2. Hair Type

Your hair type is another important factor to consider. If you have fine hair, a thick fringe can look overwhelming. On the other hand, if you have thick hair, a wispy fringe might not be visible enough. If you have curly hair, keep in mind that a fringe may require more styling than you're used to.

3. Personal Style

Finally, think about your personal style. If you're someone who likes to experiment with new looks, a fringe can be a fun way to switch things up. On the other hand, if you prefer a more classic look, a fringe might not be the best choice for you.

Ultimately, the decision to get a fringe is a personal one. If you're not sure whether it's the right choice for you, try using a virtual makeover tool to see what you would look like with a fringe. You can also ask your hairdresser for their opinion.

Step by Step: Getting a Fringe

Once you've decided to get a fringe, here's what you need to do:

1. Choose Your Fringe Style

There are many different types of fringes to choose from, including blunt, wispy, side-swept, and micro. Look for inspiration online or in magazines to find a style that you like.

2. Book an Appointment

Unless you're confident in your hairdressing skills, it's best to leave fringe cutting to the professionals. Book an appointment with your hairdresser and let them know that you're interested in a fringe.

3. Communicate Your Preferences

When you get to the salon, make sure to communicate your preferences clearly. Let your hairdresser know what type of fringe you're interested in, as well as any concerns you might have (such as wanting a fringe that's easy to style).

4. Sit Back and Relax

Once you're in the chair, sit back and relax. Your hairdresser will take care of everything. They may ask you to tilt your head forward or backward, but other than that, you don't need to do anything.

5. Style Your Fringe

Once your fringe is cut, your hairdresser will likely style it for you. Pay attention to how they style it so that you can recreate the look at home.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe

Here are some tips for keeping your fringe looking great:

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

To keep your fringe looking fresh, make sure to wash your hair regularly. If you have an oily scalp, you may need to wash your hair every day.

2. Blow Dry Your Fringe

Blow-drying your fringe can help to keep it looking smooth and polished. Use a round brush to blow-dry your fringe in the direction that you want it to fall.

3. Use Dry Shampoo

If you don't have time to wash your hair, dry shampoo can be a lifesaver. It can help to absorb excess oil and give your fringe a bit of volume.

FAQs About Fringes

1. Will a fringe suit me if I have curly hair?

Yes, a fringe can look great on curly hair. Just be prepared to spend a bit more time styling it than you're used to.

2. Can I cut my own fringe?

We don't recommend cutting your own fringe unless you're confident in your hairdressing skills. It's easy to make mistakes that can be difficult to fix.

3. How often do I need to get my fringe trimmed?

How often you need to get your fringe trimmed will depend on how fast your hair grows. As a general rule, plan to get your fringe trimmed every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Your Fringe

Finally, here are some hair care tips to keep your fringe looking healthy and beautiful:

1. Use a High-Quality Shampoo and Conditioner

To keep your hair and fringe looking their best, use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are formulated for your hair type.

2. Protect Your Hair from Heat

If you use heat styling tools (like a straightener or curling iron), make sure to use a heat protectant spray first. This can help to prevent damage and breakage.

3. Avoid Overstyling

While it can be tempting to experiment with different hairstyles, avoid overstyling your hair. Too much heat and product can cause damage and breakage.

Now that you know how to know if you'll look good with a fringe, you can make an informed decision about whether it's the right choice for you. Remember, getting a fringe can be a fun way to switch up your look, but it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
