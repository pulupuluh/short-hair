Does Obesity Rate Rise With Age

Written by Luffy Sep 12, 2023 · 5 min read

As we age, our bodies undergo various changes, and one of the most significant changes is the increase in body weight. Obesity is a growing concern worldwide, and it is important to understand whether the obesity rate rises with age or not. In this article, we will explore the relationship between age and obesity, and provide tips on how to maintain a healthy weight as we age.

Table of Content

  • What is Obesity?
  • Does Obesity Rate Rise with Age?
  • Why Does Obesity Rate Rise with Age?
  • Health Risks Associated with Obesity

  • Conclusion

What is Obesity?

Obesity is a medical condition characterized by excessive accumulation of fat in the body, which can lead to various health problems, including heart disease, diabetes, and stroke. Obesity is determined by body mass index (BMI), which is calculated by dividing a person's weight in kilograms by their height in meters squared.

Does Obesity Rate Rise with Age?

Yes, obesity rate does rise with age. According to a study conducted by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) in 2017-2018, the obesity rate among adults aged 20-39 was 28.0%, while the obesity rate among adults aged 40-59 was 40.2%. The obesity rate among adults aged 60 and over was 37.0%. This shows that as we age, the likelihood of becoming obese increases.

Why Does Obesity Rate Rise with Age?

There are several reasons why the obesity rate rises with age. One of the main reasons is a decrease in metabolic rate, which is the rate at which our bodies burn calories. As we age, our metabolic rate decreases, and we require fewer calories to maintain our weight. This means that if we continue to consume the same amount of calories as we did when we were younger, we are more likely to gain weight.

Another reason why the obesity rate rises with age is a decrease in physical activity. As we age, we tend to become less active, which means that we burn fewer calories. This, combined with a decrease in metabolic rate, can lead to weight gain and obesity.

Health Risks Associated with Obesity

Obesity is associated with various health risks, including:

  • Heart disease
  • High blood pressure
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Stroke
  • Certain types of cancer
  • Sleep apnea
  • Joint problems

How to Maintain a Healthy Weight as You Age

Here are some tips on how to maintain a healthy weight as you age:

  • Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, lean protein, and whole grains
  • Avoid processed foods and sugary drinks
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water
  • Engage in regular physical activity, such as walking, swimming, or cycling
  • Strength train to build muscle mass and increase metabolic rate
  • Get enough sleep to allow your body to recover and repair
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques, such as meditation or yoga

Conclusion

Obesity rate does rise with age, and it is important to take steps to maintain a healthy weight as we age. By eating a balanced diet, engaging in regular physical activity, and managing stress and sleep, we can reduce our risk of obesity and the associated health risks.
