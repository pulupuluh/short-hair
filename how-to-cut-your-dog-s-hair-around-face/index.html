How To Cut Your Dog s Hair Around The Face

Written by Petter Feb 17, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Cutting your dog's hair around the face can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done easily and safely. In this article, we will give you a step-by-step guide on how to cut your dog's hair around the face, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

How to Cut Your Dog's Hair Around the Face

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of scissors, clippers, and a comb. It's also a good idea to have some treats on hand to keep your dog calm and happy during the process.

Start by brushing your dog's hair to remove any tangles and knots. Use the comb to separate the hair around the face from the rest of the head. You can also use a clip to hold the hair up and out of the way.

Next, use the clippers to trim the hair on the sides of the face. Start at the top of the head and work your way down to the jawline. Use short, gentle strokes and be careful not to cut too close to the skin. You can also use the scissors to trim any longer hairs that the clippers missed.

Once you have trimmed the sides of the face, move on to the top of the head. Use the scissors to trim the hair on the top of the head, being careful not to cut too much or too close to the skin. You can also use the comb to lift the hair up and trim it to the desired length.

Finally, trim the hair around the eyes and nose. Use the scissors to carefully trim any long hairs that are obstructing your dog's vision or causing them to sneeze. Be sure to trim only a little bit at a time and to keep your dog's eyes and nose safe.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Brush your dog's hair to remove any tangles and knots.

2. Use a comb to separate the hair around the face from the rest of the head.

3. Use clippers to trim the hair on the sides of the face.

4. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that the clippers missed.

5. Use scissors to trim the hair on the top of the head.

6. Use the comb to lift the hair up and trim it to the desired length.

7. Trim the hair around the eyes and nose using the scissors.

Tips for Cutting Your Dog's Hair Around the Face

1. Use sharp scissors and clippers to avoid pulling and tugging on your dog's hair.

2. Don't rush the process. Take your time and be patient with your dog.

3. Use treats to keep your dog calm and happy during the process.

4. Avoid cutting too close to the skin, as this can cause irritation and discomfort for your dog.

5. If you're unsure about how to cut your dog's hair, consider taking them to a professional groomer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I cut my dog's hair around the face?

A: It depends on your dog's breed and hair type. Some dogs may need their hair trimmed every few weeks, while others may only need it once a month.

Q: Can I use human hair clippers to cut my dog's hair?

A: No, it's not recommended. Human hair clippers are not designed for pet hair and can cause discomfort and injury to your dog.

Q: What if my dog is afraid of getting their hair cut?

A: Take it slow and use treats to help your dog feel calm and relaxed. You can also consider using a muzzle to prevent biting or chewing during the process.

Hair Care Tips for Your Dog

1. Brush your dog's hair regularly to prevent tangles and mats.

2. Bathe your dog as needed, but be careful not to over-bathe them, as this can dry out their skin.

3. Use a conditioner to keep your dog's hair soft and shiny.

4. Trim your dog's hair regularly to prevent it from getting too long and causing discomfort.

5. Visit a professional groomer for more advanced grooming needs, such as nail trimming, ear cleaning, and anal gland expression.

Remember, cutting your dog's hair around the face can be a bit tricky, but with the right tools and technique, you can do it safely and easily. Just take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it!
