How To Cure Damaged Hair From Bleaching A Complete Guide

Written by William Apr 26, 2023 · 8 min read

Bleaching your hair can give you a bold new look, but it can also cause serious damage to your hair. If you’re struggling with dry, brittle, or damaged hair, don’t worry – there are steps you can take to get your hair back to good health. In this guide, we’ll explore how to cure damaged hair from bleaching, including tips, tricks, and expert advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cure Damaged Hair from Bleaching
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Keeping Your Hair Healthy
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Products for Damaged Hair

How to Cure Damaged Hair from Bleaching

If you’ve bleached your hair and are dealing with damage, don’t panic – there are steps you can take to cure your hair. The key is to nourish your hair from the inside out, providing it with the nutrients and moisture it needs to repair itself.

Here are some steps you can take to cure damaged hair from bleaching:

1. Trim Your Hair

If your hair is severely damaged, the best thing you can do is to trim it. This will remove the damaged ends and help your hair grow back healthier and stronger.

2. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein is essential for healthy hair, and if your hair is damaged from bleaching, it’s likely that it’s lacking in protein. Using a protein treatment can help to strengthen your hair and prevent further damage.

3. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning is a must for damaged hair. It helps to moisturize and nourish your hair, leaving it soft and supple. Use a deep conditioner weekly and leave it on for at least 20 minutes for best results.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can further damage your hair, so it’s best to avoid it as much as possible. If you need to use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting as low as possible.

5. Be Gentle When Brushing

When your hair is damaged, it’s more prone to breakage. To prevent further damage, be gentle when brushing your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb and start from the ends, working your way up to the roots.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to curing damaged hair from bleaching:

Step 1: Trim your hair

Start by trimming your hair to remove the damaged ends. If you’re not sure how much to trim, consult with a professional stylist.

Step 2: Use a protein treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair to help strengthen it. Follow the instructions on the product for best results.

Step 3: Deep condition

Deep condition your hair at least once a week. Apply the conditioner to your hair and leave it on for at least 20 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 4: Avoid heat styling

Avoid heat styling your hair as much as possible. If you need to use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting as low as possible.

Step 5: Be gentle when brushing

When brushing your hair, be gentle to prevent further damage. Use a wide-tooth comb and start from the ends, working your way up to the roots.

Tips for Keeping Your Hair Healthy

Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help to keep your hair healthy and strong.

2. Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can help to keep your hair hydrated and prevent it from becoming dry and brittle.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Exposure to the sun can damage your hair, so it’s important to protect it with a hat or scarf.

4. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to prevent your hair from becoming tangled and damaged while you sleep.

FAQs

How long does it take to cure damaged hair from bleaching?

The length of time it takes to cure damaged hair from bleaching depends on the severity of the damage. With proper care, it can take several months to a year for your hair to fully recover.

Can damaged hair from bleaching be permanently cured?

While damaged hair from bleaching can be cured, it’s important to remember that your hair will always be more prone to damage after it has been bleached.
