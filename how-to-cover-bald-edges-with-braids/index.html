How To Cover Bald Edges With Braids

Written by Christine Mar 24, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Are you struggling with hair loss or thinning edges? Do you find it difficult to style your hair without revealing your bald spots? Braids are a great solution for covering bald edges while still maintaining a stylish look. In this article, we will give you step-by-step instructions on how to cover bald edges with braids and provide some helpful tips and FAQs.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cover Bald Edges with Braids
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Helpful Tips

How to Cover Bald Edges with Braids

The key to covering bald edges with braids is to create a protective style that does not put too much strain on your hairline. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start braiding, make sure your hair is clean and well-conditioned. This will help prevent breakage and make the braiding process easier. If your hair is dry or brittle, consider using a deep conditioning treatment before braiding.

Step 2: Choose Your Braiding Pattern

There are several braiding patterns to choose from, but the most common ones for covering bald edges are the cornrow and box braid patterns. These patterns allow you to braid your hair close to your scalp, which helps cover any bald spots.

Step 3: Start Braiding

Begin braiding your hair from the nape of your neck and work your way up to your hairline. Make sure to braid your hair tightly but not too tightly that it causes pain or damage to your hairline. Use a hair tie to secure the end of each braid.

Step 4: Cover Your Edges

Once you have braided your hair, you can use a crochet hook or latch hook to add hair extensions to your braids. This will help cover your bald spots and give your hair more volume and length.

Step 5: Finish with a Protective Style

To protect your braids and edges, you can finish the style by wrapping your hair in a silk or satin scarf at night. You can also use a styling gel or edge control product to smooth down any flyaways and keep your edges looking neat and tidy.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here is a more detailed breakdown of the steps to follow when covering bald edges with braids:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioning it with a moisturizing conditioner. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent breakage during the braiding process.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to gently remove any tangles or knots from your hair. This will make it easier to braid your hair and prevent breakage.

Step 3: Choose Your Braiding Pattern

Decide on the braiding pattern you want to use. Cornrows and box braids are popular choices for covering bald edges. Make sure the pattern you choose is not too tight or puts too much strain on your hairline.

Step 4: Start Braiding

Begin braiding your hair from the nape of your neck and work your way up to your hairline. Use a small amount of styling gel or edge control product to help keep your edges smooth and neat.

Step 5: Add Hair Extensions

If you want to add length or volume to your braids, you can use hair extensions. Use a crochet hook or latch hook to loop the extensions into your braids.

Step 6: Finish with a Protective Style

Once your braids are complete, you can finish the style by wrapping your hair in a silk or satin scarf at night. This will help protect your braids and prevent breakage. You can also use a styling gel or edge control product to keep your edges looking neat and tidy.

Helpful Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you cover bald edges with braids:

  • Be gentle when braiding your hair and avoid pulling too tightly on your hairline.
  • Use a moisturizing oil or leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid wearing your braids for too long as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • If you feel any pain or discomfort during the braiding process, take a break and loosen your braids.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about covering bald edges with braids:

Can braids cause hair loss?

If done too tightly or left in for too long, braids can cause hair loss or damage to your hairline. Make sure to choose a braiding pattern that is not too tight and avoid leaving your braids in for more than 6-8 weeks.

What type of hair extensions should I use?

You can use any type of hair extensions that you like, as long as they are not too heavy or bulky. Synthetic hair extensions are a popular choice as they are affordable and come in a wide range of colors and textures.

How often should I wash my braids?

You should wash your braids every 1-2 weeks to keep your scalp clean and prevent buildup. Use a gentle shampoo and be careful not to tug on your braids too much when washing.

Hair Care for Bald Edges

Even with braids, it is important to take care of your hair and scalp to prevent further hair loss or damage. Here are some tips for caring for your hair while wearing braids:

  • Massage your scalp daily to promote blood flow and stimulate hair growth.
  • Use a scalp treatment or oil to nourish your hair and promote healthy growth.
  • Avoid wearing tight hats or headbands that can put pressure on your hairline.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that are essential for healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and using braids to cover your bald edges, you can achieve a stylish and protective hairstyle that promotes healthy hair growth.
