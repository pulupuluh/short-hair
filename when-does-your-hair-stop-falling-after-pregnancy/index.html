When Does Your Hair Stop Falling After Pregnancy

Written by William Aug 31, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you a new mom who is experiencing hair loss after pregnancy? You are not alone. Many new mothers experience hair loss after giving birth, and it can be a frustrating and stressful experience. In this blog post, we will discuss when your hair will stop falling out after pregnancy and provide tips on how to care for your hair during this time.

Table of Contents:

  • What Causes Hair Loss After Pregnancy?
  • When Does Hair Stop Falling After Pregnancy?
  • Tips for Hair Care After Pregnancy
  • FAQs

What Causes Hair Loss After Pregnancy?

Hair loss after pregnancy is a common condition known as postpartum hair loss. It occurs due to hormonal changes in the body during pregnancy. During pregnancy, the body produces higher levels of estrogen, which prolongs the growth phase of the hair cycle. This results in thicker, fuller hair during pregnancy. However, after giving birth, estrogen levels drop, and the hair cycle returns to normal. The hair that was supposed to fall out during pregnancy starts to shed, resulting in hair loss.

When Does Hair Stop Falling After Pregnancy?

The good news is that postpartum hair loss is temporary, and your hair will eventually stop falling out. Hair loss typically peaks around three to four months after giving birth and can continue for up to a year. However, most women notice that their hair starts to grow back around six months after giving birth.

If you are still experiencing hair loss after a year, it is recommended that you consult a dermatologist or a hair specialist to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Tips for Hair Care After Pregnancy

Here are some tips for caring for your hair during postpartum hair loss:

1. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid pulling, tugging, or brushing your hair roughly. Use a wide-toothed comb or a soft-bristled brush to detangle your hair.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as hair dryers, flat irons, and curling irons. Heat can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage.

3. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth. Foods such as salmon, eggs, spinach, and sweet potatoes are good sources of nutrients that promote hair health.

4. Use Hair Care Products Wisely

Use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils. Avoid using hair care products that contain harsh chemicals such as sulfates and parabens.

FAQs

1. Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

No, postpartum hair loss is temporary, and your hair will eventually stop falling out. Most women notice that their hair starts to grow back around six months after giving birth.

2. How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss typically peaks around three to four months after giving birth and can continue for up to a year.

3. Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

Postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented, but you can take steps to care for your hair during this time. Be gentle with your hair, avoid heat styling, eat a balanced diet, and use hair care products wisely.


In conclusion, postpartum hair loss can be a frustrating experience, but it is temporary. Your hair will eventually stop falling out, and new hair will grow back. By following the tips provided in this blog post, you can care for your hair during this time and promote healthy hair growth.
