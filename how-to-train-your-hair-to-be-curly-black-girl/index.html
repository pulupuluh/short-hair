How To Train Your Hair To Be Curly Black Girl A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Apr 03, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you a black girl with straight hair who wants to switch things up and rock some beautiful curls? It's time to train your hair to be curly! This process can be daunting, but with the right tips and tricks, you can achieve gorgeous curly locks. In this guide, we will cover everything you need to know about how to train your hair to be curly black girl, including how to switch up your hair care routine and the best styling techniques to use.

Description

Training your hair to be curly is a process that involves using specific techniques and products to encourage your hair to curl up naturally. This process is especially important for black girls with relaxed hair, as relaxing treatments can often leave hair straight and limp. By training your hair to be curly, you can achieve a beautiful, voluminous look that is sure to turn heads.

How to Train Your Hair to be Curly Black Girl: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Deep Condition Regularly

One of the most important things you can do to train your hair to be curly is to deep condition regularly. This helps to nourish and moisturize your hair, making it more receptive to curling techniques. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Step 2: Use Curl-Enhancing Products

To encourage your hair to curl, it's important to use curl-enhancing products. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl-defining creams and gels. Apply these products to your hair when it is damp, scrunching your hair gently to encourage curls.

Step 3: Try the Plopping Method

The plopping method is a popular technique for encouraging curls in black hair. After applying your curl-enhancing products, use a t-shirt or microfiber towel to plop your hair onto your head, creating a "plop" shape. Leave your hair in this position for several hours, or overnight if possible, to encourage curls.

Step 4: Use a Diffuser

A diffuser is a hair styling tool that helps to dry your hair while keeping your curls intact. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair after applying your curl-enhancing products. This will help to lock in your curls and create a beautiful, voluminous look.

Step 5: Experiment with Different Styles

Once you've trained your hair to be curly, it's time to experiment with different styles! Try out different curl patterns, including tight curls and loose waves. You can also experiment with different hair accessories, such as headbands and hair clips, to create unique and stylish looks.

Tips for Training Your Hair to be Curly Black Girl

Here are some additional tips to help you train your hair to be curly:

  • Be patient - it can take several weeks or even months to see results
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair and prevent it from curling
  • Invest in a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent breakage and help maintain your curls
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it is dry, as this can cause frizz

FAQs

Can I train my hair to be curly if it is already relaxed?

Yes! While it may take a bit more effort, you can still train your hair to be curly if it has been relaxed. Just be sure to deep condition regularly and use curl-enhancing products to encourage your hair to curl naturally.

How often should I deep condition my hair?

You should deep condition your hair once a week to keep it healthy and hydrated. If your hair is particularly dry or damaged, you may want to deep condition more often.

What is the best way to maintain curly hair?

To maintain curly hair, it's important to avoid heat styling tools and use products that are specifically designed for curly hair. You should also avoid brushing or combing your hair when it is dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

When you have curly hair, it's important to take good care of your locks to keep them healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for hair care for curly hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo that is sulfate-free
  • Deep condition regularly to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it is wet
  • Invest in a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent breakage

By following these tips and using the right techniques, you can train your hair to be curly black girl and achieve a beautiful, voluminous look that is sure to turn heads.
