Is Blow Drying Your Hair Better Than Straightening It

Written by Daniel Apr 11, 2023 · 9 min read

When it comes to styling our hair, we often find ourselves torn between using a blow dryer or a straightener. Both tools have their pros and cons, but is one better than the other? In this blog post, we will explore whether blow drying your hair is better than straightening it, and provide tips on how to care for your hair while using these tools.

Table of Contents

  • How To Blow Dry Your Hair
  • Step-By-Step Guide To Straightening Your Hair
  • Tips For Blow Drying And Straightening Your Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Description

Blow drying your hair involves using a hair dryer to dry and style your hair. On the other hand, straightening your hair involves using a flat iron to straighten your hair strands. Both tools use heat to style your hair, but the degree of heat and the process used are different.

How To Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair can be a quick and easy way to style your hair, especially if you have naturally wavy or curly hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Wash your hair and towel dry it to remove excess water.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the heat.
  3. Divide your hair into sections using clips.
  4. Start by blow-drying the roots of your hair first, using a round brush to lift the hair and add volume.
  5. Continue blow-drying the rest of your hair, using the round brush to smooth out any tangles or knots.
  6. Finish off with a blast of cool air to set the style.

Blow drying your hair can be less damaging than straightening it, as the heat is not as intense and the process is gentler on your hair. However, it's important to use a heat protectant spray and to avoid holding the dryer too close to your hair to prevent damage.

Step-By-Step Guide To Straightening Your Hair

Straightening your hair can give you a sleek and polished look, but it can also be damaging if not done correctly. Here's a step-by-step guide to straightening your hair:

  1. Wash your hair and towel dry it to remove excess water.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the heat.
  3. Divide your hair into sections using clips.
  4. Start with the bottom section of your hair and use a comb to detangle it.
  5. Using a flat iron, clamp down on a small section of hair and glide it down the length of the hair.
  6. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working from the bottom up.
  7. Finish off with a serum or spray to add shine and smoothness to your hair.

Straightening your hair can be more damaging than blow drying it, as the heat is more intense and can strip your hair of its natural oils. It's important to use a heat protectant spray and to avoid using the flat iron on wet hair to prevent damage.

Tips For Blow Drying And Straightening Your Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using a blow dryer or flat iron:

  • Always use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the heat.
  • Avoid holding the dryer or flat iron too close to your hair to prevent burning.
  • Use a lower heat setting if possible to prevent damage.
  • Don't use the flat iron on wet hair to prevent damage.
  • Use a serum or spray to add shine and smoothness to your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is blow drying your hair better than straightening it?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Blow drying your hair can be less damaging than straightening it, as the heat is not as intense and the process is gentler on your hair. However, straightening your hair can give you a sleek and polished look.

Q: Can you blow dry and straighten your hair on the same day?

A: Yes, you can blow dry and straighten your hair on the same day. However, it's important to use a heat protectant spray and to avoid using the flat iron on wet hair to prevent damage.

Q: How often should you blow dry or straighten your hair?

A: It's best to limit the use of heat styling tools to once or twice a week to prevent damage to your hair.

Hair Care Tips

Using a blow dryer or straightener can be damaging to your hair if not done correctly. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and shiny:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as it can cause breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

In conclusion, whether blow drying your hair is better than straightening it depends on your hair type and personal preference. Both tools can be damaging if not used correctly, so it's important to use a heat protectant spray and to avoid holding the tools too close to your hair. Remember to also take care of your hair by using deep conditioning treatments and avoiding hot water when washing your hair.
