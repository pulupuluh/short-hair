How To Stop Short Hair Sticking Up Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Aug 20, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have short hair, you might be familiar with the struggle of dealing with hair that sticks up. Whether it's due to genetics, styling, or just the way your hair naturally grows, it can be frustrating to try to tame your locks. Fortunately, there are several tips and tricks that you can use to help prevent your short hair from sticking up. In this article, we'll cover some of the best ways to keep your hair sleek and smooth.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Stop Short Hair Sticking Up
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Short Hair

How to Stop Short Hair Sticking Up

There are several ways to prevent short hair from sticking up. Some methods may work better for certain hair types or textures, so it may take some trial and error to find the right solution for you. Here are some of the most effective ways to keep your hair smooth and sleek:

1. Use a Hair Straightener

One of the easiest ways to keep your short hair from sticking up is to use a hair straightener. This tool can help smooth out any bumps or frizz, and give your hair a sleek, polished look. Make sure to use a heat protectant spray before using the straightener to prevent damage to your hair.

2. Apply a Styling Cream or Gel

Using a styling cream or gel can help keep your hair in place and prevent it from sticking up. Apply a small amount to your fingertips and run it through your hair, focusing on the areas that tend to stick up the most. Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

3. Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair can help smooth out any bumps or frizz and give your hair a sleek look. Use a round brush to help straighten your hair as you blow dry, and make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage. Blow dry your hair in the direction that you want it to lay, and avoid using too much heat, as this can damage your hair.

4. Use Bobby Pins or Hair Clips

If you have a few stubborn strands that just won't stay down, try using bobby pins or hair clips to keep them in place. Use a small amount of hairspray or styling cream on the pin or clip before placing it in your hair to help keep it in place.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you stop short hair from sticking up:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair.
  3. Use a hair straightener to smooth out any bumps or frizz.
  4. Apply a small amount of styling cream or gel to your fingertips and run it through your hair.
  5. Blow dry your hair in the direction that you want it to lay, using a round brush to help straighten your hair.
  6. If you have any stubborn strands, use bobby pins or hair clips to keep them in place.
  7. Finish with a light mist of hairspray to help keep your hair in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you keep your short hair from sticking up:

  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, as this can help prevent breakage and damage.
  • Avoid using too much heat, as this can damage your hair and make it more prone to sticking up.
  • Try using a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

FAQs

Q: Why does my short hair stick up?

A: Short hair can stick up for several reasons, including genetics, styling, and the way your hair naturally grows. It's important to find the root cause of your hair sticking up in order to find the best solution for you.

Q: Can I use hairspray to keep my short hair from sticking up?

A: Yes, hairspray can be an effective way to keep your short hair in place. Just make sure to use a light mist and avoid using too much, as this can make your hair look stiff and unnatural.

Q: What type of hair straightener should I use?

A: It's important to use a high-quality hair straightener that is designed for your hair type. Look for a straightener with ceramic plates, as these are less damaging to your hair than metal plates.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Keeping your short hair healthy and strong is key to preventing it from sticking up. Here are some tips for taking care of your short hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet to promote strong, healthy hair.
  • Get regular haircuts to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can help prevent your short hair from sticking up and achieve a sleek, polished look. Remember to be patient and experiment with different methods to find what works best for you.
