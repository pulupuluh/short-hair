How To Cut Thinning Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 02, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you have thinning hair, it can be frustrating trying to style it or find a haircut that works for you. However, cutting your hair properly can actually make a big difference in how it looks and how much volume it has. In this guide, we will go over how to cut thinning hair, step by step.

Description

Thinning hair is a common problem for both men and women, and it can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, age, and stress. When hair starts to thin, it can become more difficult to style and can look limp and lifeless. However, by cutting your hair in a specific way, you can add volume and make it look fuller.

How to Cut Thinning Hair

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to wash and condition it. This will help remove any buildup or dirt that may be weighing your hair down. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add extra body and volume to your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into four parts: the top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting the sides and back of your hair. Use thinning shears to remove any excess weight and add texture. This will help your hair look fuller and more voluminous. Work your way up to the top of your head, using the same technique.

Step 4: Trim the Top

Once you have finished cutting the sides and back, it's time to trim the top. Use scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Be sure to cut in small sections to avoid taking off too much hair at once.

Step 5: Blend Everything Together

After you have finished cutting your hair, it's important to blend everything together. Use your fingers or a comb to blend the different sections of your hair together. This will help create a seamless look and make your hair appear fuller.

Tips for Cutting Thinning Hair

  • Use thinning shears to remove excess weight and add texture
  • Cut in small sections to avoid taking off too much hair at once
  • Blend everything together to create a seamless look
  • Consider adding layers to your hair to add volume

FAQs

Is it better to cut thinning hair short or long?

It's generally better to cut thinning hair on the shorter side, as longer hair can weigh your hair down and make it look thinner. However, a shorter haircut may not be the best option for everyone, so it's important to consider your face shape and personal style when choosing a haircut.

Can I cut my own thinning hair?

Yes, you can cut your own thinning hair. However, it's important to use the right tools and techniques to avoid making your hair look worse. If you're not confident in your ability to cut your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist.

Hair Care for Thinning Hair

In addition to cutting your hair properly, there are other things you can do to care for thinning hair. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot styling tools that can damage your hair. Consider using a hair thickening spray or mousse to add extra volume, and avoid pulling your hair back tightly, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Conclusion

By following these steps and tips, you can cut thinning hair in a way that adds volume and makes it look fuller. Remember to take your time and use the right tools and techniques to achieve the best results.
