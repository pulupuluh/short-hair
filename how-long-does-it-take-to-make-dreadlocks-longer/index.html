How Long Does It Take To Make Dreadlocks Longer

Written by Oliver Sep 03, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Dreadlocks are a timeless hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are unique, versatile, and can be styled in many different ways. However, one of the most common questions people ask about dreadlocks is how long does it take to make them longer?

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Make Dreadlocks Longer
  • Step by Step Process
  • Tips for Making Dreadlocks Longer
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Description

Dreadlocks can be made longer with time and patience. The length of time it takes to make dreadlocks longer depends on various factors such as the length of your hair, texture, and thickness. The longer your hair, the more time it will take to make your dreadlocks longer. Similarly, if you have thick hair, it will take longer to make them longer than if you have thin hair.

Another factor that determines how long it takes to make dreadlocks longer is the method used to create them. Different methods of creating dreadlocks have different timelines, and some methods are faster than others. For example, crochet hook method is faster than the backcombing method.

How to Make Dreadlocks Longer

To make dreadlocks longer, there are various methods you can use. However, the most common method is to wait for them to grow naturally. This process can take anywhere from several months to several years, depending on the length of your hair and how long you want your dreadlocks to be.

If you don't want to wait that long, you can use extensions to make your dreadlocks longer. Dreadlock extensions are made from synthetic or natural hair and can be added to your existing dreadlocks to make them longer. This method is faster than waiting for your hair to grow naturally, but it does require maintenance to keep the extensions looking good.

Step by Step Process

If you want to make your dreadlocks longer using extensions, here is a step-by-step process:

  1. Decide on the length you want your dreadlocks to be.
  2. Purchase dreadlock extensions that match the color and texture of your hair.
  3. Section your hair and attach the extensions to each section using a crochet hook.
  4. After attaching the extensions, backcomb them to blend them with your existing dreadlocks.
  5. Use a rubber band to secure the extension to your natural dreadlock.
  6. Repeat the process until all the sections of your hair have extensions attached to them.

Tips for Making Dreadlocks Longer

Here are a few tips to help you make your dreadlocks longer:

  • Be patient. Making dreadlocks longer takes time, and it's important to be patient throughout the process.
  • Use natural hair care products to keep your dreadlocks healthy and strong.
  • Regularly wash your dreadlocks to prevent buildup and maintain their health.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your dreadlocks as they can cause damage and breakage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about making dreadlocks longer:

Q. Can I make my dreadlocks longer without using extensions?

A. Yes, you can make your dreadlocks longer without using extensions by waiting for your hair to grow naturally. However, this process can take a long time.

Q. How often should I wash my dreadlocks?

A. You should wash your dreadlocks every 7-10 days to prevent buildup and maintain their health.

Q. Can I use hair dye on my dreadlocks?

A. Yes, you can use hair dye on your dreadlocks. However, it's important to use a dye that is safe for dreadlocks and to follow the instructions carefully.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Dreadlocks require special care to keep them healthy and strong. Here are a few tips for caring for your dreadlocks:

  • Use natural hair care products that are gentle and free of harsh chemicals.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your dreadlocks as they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Regularly wash your dreadlocks to prevent buildup and maintain their health.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.
  • Avoid using rubber bands or tight hair ties on your dreadlocks as they can cause damage and breakage.

Making dreadlocks longer takes time and patience, but it's definitely worth the wait. Whether you decide to wait for your hair to grow naturally or use extensions, the process can be enjoyable with the right care and maintenance.
