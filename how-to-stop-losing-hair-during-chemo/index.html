How To Stop Losing Hair During Chemo

Written by Aprilia Jun 23, 2023 · 7 min read

Chemo can be a lifesaving treatment for cancer patients, but it often comes with a side effect that can be difficult to deal with: hair loss. Losing hair during chemo can be emotionally devastating, but there are steps you can take to minimize hair loss and preserve your self-esteem. In this article, we’ll discuss how to stop losing hair during chemo and provide tips for caring for your hair during and after treatment.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Losing Hair During Chemo
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Caring for Your Hair During and After Treatment
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Products and Services

How to Stop Losing Hair During Chemo

The first step in stopping hair loss during chemo is to speak with your oncologist. They can help you understand the type of chemotherapy you will be receiving and what side effects to expect. Some chemotherapy drugs are more likely to cause hair loss than others, and your doctor can help you anticipate what to expect.

Once you have a better understanding of your treatment, you can take steps to minimize hair loss. One of the most effective ways to do this is to use a cold cap during chemotherapy. A cold cap is a special device that is worn on the head during treatment. The cap is cooled to very low temperatures, which slows down the blood flow to the scalp. This reduces the amount of chemotherapy drugs that reach the hair follicles, which can prevent or minimize hair loss.

In addition to using a cold cap, you can also try taking supplements that promote hair growth. Biotin, for example, is a B vitamin that is known to support healthy hair, skin, and nails. It can be taken as a supplement or found in foods like eggs, almonds, and sweet potatoes.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for how to stop losing hair during chemo:

  1. Talk to your oncologist about your treatment and what side effects to expect.
  2. Consider using a cold cap during chemotherapy to minimize hair loss.
  3. Take supplements that promote hair growth, like biotin.
  4. Eat a healthy diet that supports hair growth.
  5. Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools that can damage hair.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair During and After Treatment

Even if you take steps to prevent hair loss during chemo, you may still experience some thinning or shedding. Here are some tips for caring for your hair during and after treatment:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals.
  • Avoid heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, which can damage hair.
  • Avoid coloring or perming your hair, which can weaken it.
  • Wear a scarf or hat to protect your scalp from the sun and wind.
  • Consider getting a wig or hairpiece if you feel self-conscious about your hair loss.

FAQ

Does everyone lose hair during chemo?

No, not everyone loses hair during chemo. The likelihood of hair loss depends on the type of chemotherapy drugs used and the dose.

Will my hair grow back after chemo?

Yes, most people’s hair will grow back after chemo. However, it may be a different texture or color than before treatment.

How long does it take for hair to grow back after chemo?

It can take several months for hair to grow back after chemo. The exact timeline depends on the individual and the type of treatment they received.

Hair Care Products and Services

If you are experiencing hair loss during chemo, there are a variety of hair care products and services that can help. Some options include:

  • Wigs and hairpieces
  • Hair growth supplements
  • Gentle shampoos and conditioners
  • Scalp cooling systems

Talk to your oncologist or a hair care professional to learn more about your options and find the best solution for you.
