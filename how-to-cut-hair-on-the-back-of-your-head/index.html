How To Cut Hair On The Back Of Your Head A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 07, 2023 · 7 min read

Welcome to our comprehensive guide on how to cut hair on the back of your head! Whether you’re a professional stylist or just someone looking to save a few bucks on haircuts, this guide will provide you with all the tips, tricks, and techniques you need to get the job done right. Below, we’ve outlined a step-by-step process for cutting your hair at the back, as well as some frequently asked questions and hair care tips to keep your locks looking and feeling healthy. Let’s get started!

How to Cut Hair on the Back of Your Head: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you get started, you’ll need to gather a few tools. You’ll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to hold your hair in place. You may also want to consider investing in a handheld mirror so you can see the back of your head more easily.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Next, you’ll want to prep your hair. Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly, then comb it out to remove any tangles or knots. Once your hair is smooth and tangle-free, use your clips to hold the top section of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Start at the Bottom

When cutting your hair at the back, it’s best to start at the bottom and work your way up. This will help you avoid any mistakes or uneven cuts. Use your comb to create a straight line across the bottom of your hair, then trim any split ends or damaged sections.

Step 4: Move Up the Back

Once you’ve finished the bottom section, release the next section of hair and move up the back of your head. Again, use your comb to create a straight line and trim any split ends or damaged sections. Repeat this process until you reach the top section of your hair.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair at the back, use your handheld mirror to check your work. Make sure the length is even and that there are no visible mistakes or uneven sections. If you notice any issues, go back and trim them until everything looks even and polished.

Tips for Cutting Hair on the Back of Your Head

Here are some additional tips to help you get the best results when cutting your hair at the back:

  • Always use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Take your time and don’t rush the process.
  • When in doubt, cut less rather than more. You can always go back and trim more later.
  • If you’re not confident in your cutting skills, consider practicing on a mannequin head or a willing friend before attempting to cut your own hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own hair at the back?

Yes, you can cut your own hair at the back. However, it can be a bit tricky to get the angle and length right, so it’s important to take your time and proceed with caution.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type, length, and personal preferences. Generally, it’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends from forming.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair at the back, don’t panic! You can always go back and trim the section again until it looks even and polished. If you’re really struggling, consider seeking out the help of a professional stylist.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair looking and feeling healthy after cutting it at the back, be sure to follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a UV protection spray.

Cutting your hair at the back can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it’s definitely doable. By following the steps outlined in this guide and taking your time, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. Happy cutting!
