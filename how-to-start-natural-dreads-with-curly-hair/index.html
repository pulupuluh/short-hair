How To Start Natural Dreads With Curly Hair

Written by Aprilia Aug 29, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of the daily grind of maintaining your curly hair? Do you want to try something new and daring? Perhaps it’s time to start natural dreads. Dreads are a unique and stylish way to embrace your natural hair texture. In this article, we’ll show you how to start natural dreads with curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Start Natural Dreads with Curly Hair
  • Step by Step Instructions
  • Tips for Starting Natural Dreads with Curly Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Natural Dreads

Description

Natural dreads, also known as dreadlocks, are formed by allowing the hair to lock and tangle together. There are several ways to start natural dreads, but the most common method is the "neglect" method. This method involves letting your hair do its own thing, without any manipulation or maintenance.

Curly hair is a great texture for starting natural dreads. The curls will naturally lock and tangle together, creating a unique and textured look. However, starting dreads with curly hair can be a bit trickier than starting them with straight hair.

How to Start Natural Dreads with Curly Hair

The first step in starting natural dreads with curly hair is to stop combing or brushing your hair. This will allow your hair to start locking and tangling together.

Next, you will need to separate your hair into sections. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreads you want to create.

Once you have your sections, you can start to twist and knot the hair together. You can use your fingers or a crochet hook to help the hair lock together.

It’s important to note that the process of starting natural dreads can take several months to a year. It’s a slow and gradual process, so be patient and enjoy the journey.

Step by Step Instructions

Here are some step by step instructions for starting natural dreads with curly hair:

  1. Stop combing or brushing your hair.
  2. Separate your hair into sections.
  3. Twist and knot the hair together using your fingers or a crochet hook.
  4. Repeat the twisting and knotting process regularly, about once a week.
  5. Be patient and let your hair do its own thing.

Tips for Starting Natural Dreads with Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you start natural dreads with curly hair:

  • Start with clean hair. Dirty hair can be harder to work with and can lead to an itchy scalp.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup in your hair.
  • Be gentle with your hair. Rough handling can lead to breakage and damage.
  • Use a satin pillowcase or bonnet to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.
  • Don’t use any hair products, including oils and gels. These can prevent your hair from locking together.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting natural dreads with curly hair:

  • Q: Will my curly hair lock together naturally?
  • A: Yes, curly hair is great for starting natural dreads. The curls will naturally lock and tangle together.
  • Q: Can I wash my hair while starting natural dreads?
  • A: Yes, you can wash your hair, but be gentle and avoid using any products.
  • Q: Can I start natural dreads with relaxed hair?
  • A: Yes, you can start natural dreads with relaxed hair, but it may take longer to lock together.

Hair Care for Natural Dreads

Once you’ve started natural dreads with curly hair, it’s important to take care of them. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but be gentle and avoid using any products.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair, including blow dryers and flat irons.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Starting natural dreads with curly hair is a unique and exciting journey. With patience and proper care, you can create a stylish and textured look that embraces your natural hair texture.
