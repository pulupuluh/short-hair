How To Cut Layers In Long Thick Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 26, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have long, thick hair, you may be looking for a way to add some dimension and movement to your locks. One way to achieve this is by cutting layers into your hair. But, going to a salon to get a professional haircut can be expensive, and you may not always have the time to make an appointment. In this blog post, we will show you how to cut layers in long thick hair yourself, step-by-step.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

  • FAQs

Description:

Layered haircuts are a popular way to add volume, texture, and movement to long, thick hair. Layers can be cut in many different styles, from subtle layers that add a slight bit of texture to bold, choppy layers that create a dramatic look. Whatever style you choose, cutting layers in long thick hair yourself can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done.

How to Cut Layers in Long Thick Hair Yourself:

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's go over some of the tools you will need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips to section off hair
  • Spray bottle with water

Step-by-Step Guide:

Follow these steps to cut layers in long thick hair yourself:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you begin cutting.
  2. Section off your hair. Use clips to section off your hair into manageable sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then divide it into two sections, one on each side of your head.
  3. Decide on the length of your layers. Determine how long you want your layers to be. For subtle layers, choose a length that is only slightly shorter than your overall hair length. For more dramatic layers, choose a length that is several inches shorter than your overall hair length.
  4. Start cutting. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of the first section and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle. Cut the hair at the length you decided on in step 3. Continue cutting small sections of hair at an angle until you have cut all the hair in that section. Repeat on the other side.
  5. Move up to the next section. Unclip the next section of hair and repeat the same process. Make sure the layers are even on both sides of your head.
  6. Continue cutting the rest of your hair. Work your way up your head, cutting each section of hair at the length you decided on.
  7. Blend the layers. Once you have cut all the sections of hair, blend the layers together by taking small sections of hair and cutting them at a slight angle. This will create a softer, more natural look.
  8. Check for any uneven spots. Take a look at your hair in the mirror and make sure the layers are even on both sides of your head. If you notice any uneven spots, make small adjustments with your scissors.
  9. Style your hair. Once you are happy with the length and shape of your layers, style your hair as desired.
  10. Clean up. Clean up any loose hair on the floor or counter and sanitize your scissors before putting them away.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Long Thick Hair Yourself:

  • Start with small sections of hair. It's easier to add more layers than to fix a mistake.
  • Don't cut too much off at once. You can always go back and cut more if needed.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Take breaks if needed. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so take a break if your arms get tired.

FAQs:

Q: How often should I cut my hair if I have layers?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the layers grow out. As a general rule, it's a good idea to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your layers looking fresh.

Q: Can I cut layers in curly hair?

A: Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair, but it's important to cut them carefully to avoid creating a frizzy look. It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry, so you can see the natural shape of your curls.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers in Long Thick Hair Yourself:

After cutting layers in your long thick hair, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to give your hair a deep conditioning treatment.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut layers in long thick hair yourself and achieve a beautiful, layered look. Happy cutting!
