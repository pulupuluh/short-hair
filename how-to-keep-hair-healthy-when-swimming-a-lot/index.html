How To Keep Hair Healthy When Swimming A Lot

Swimming is a great exercise that helps to build endurance, strength, and flexibility. However, frequent swimming can cause damage to your hair, especially if you have chemically treated hair. In this article, we will provide you with tips on how to keep hair healthy when swimming a lot.

How to Keep Hair Healthy When Swimming a Lot

Swimming can cause damage to your hair due to the chemicals in the pool, exposure to the sun, and chlorine. Here are some tips on how to keep hair healthy when swimming a lot.

1. Wet Your Hair Before Swimming

Wetting your hair before entering the pool helps to reduce the amount of chlorine and chemicals that your hair absorbs. This is because your hair is like a sponge, and when it is wet, it cannot absorb as much water as when it is dry. You can also apply a leave-in conditioner before entering the pool to create a barrier between your hair and the chlorinated water.

2. Wear a Swimming Cap

A swimming cap is an excellent way to protect your hair from the damaging effects of chlorine and other chemicals in the pool. It also helps to keep your hair out of your face, which can be a distraction when swimming.

3. Use a Clarifying Shampoo

After swimming, use a clarifying shampoo to remove any chlorine and chemicals that may have accumulated in your hair. Clarifying shampoos are specially formulated to remove buildup and leave your hair feeling clean and refreshed.

4. Condition Your Hair

After shampooing, use a deep conditioning treatment to help repair any damage that may have been caused by the chlorine and other chemicals in the pool. This will also help to keep your hair soft, smooth, and shiny.

5. Limit the Use of Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling wands, can cause further damage to your hair, especially if it has been exposed to chlorine and other chemicals in the pool. Try to limit the use of these tools and opt for air-drying your hair instead.

6. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun's UV rays can also cause damage to your hair, especially if it has been exposed to chlorine and other chemicals in the pool. Wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with UV protection to help protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to keep hair healthy when swimming a lot:

  1. Wet your hair before entering the pool.
  2. Wear a swimming cap.
  3. Shampoo your hair with a clarifying shampoo after swimming.
  4. Condition your hair with a deep conditioning treatment.
  5. Avoid using heat styling tools.
  6. Protect your hair from the sun.

Tips to Keep Hair Healthy When Swimming a Lot

Here are some additional tips to keep hair healthy when swimming a lot:

  • Use a leave-in conditioner before entering the pool.
  • Rinse your hair with fresh water after swimming.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using hair elastics that can cause breakage.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my hair after swimming?

A: You should wash your hair after every swimming session to remove any chlorine and other chemicals that may have accumulated in your hair.

Q: Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner after swimming?

A: It is recommended to use a clarifying shampoo and deep conditioning treatment after swimming to remove any buildup and repair any damage that may have been caused by the chlorine and other chemicals in the pool.

Q: Can I swim with chemically treated hair?

A: Yes, you can swim with chemically treated hair, but you should take extra precautions to protect your hair from the damaging effects of chlorine and other chemicals in the pool.

Hair Care Products to Use When Swimming a Lot

Here are some hair care products to use when swimming a lot:

  • Leave-in conditioner with UV protection
  • Clarifying shampoo
  • Deep conditioning treatment
  • Swimming cap

By following these tips and using the right hair care products, you can keep your hair healthy when swimming a lot.
