How To Thin Out Bangs With Thinning Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Apr 03, 2023 · 7 min read

Are your bangs feeling too thick and heavy? Do you want a softer, more natural look? Thinning out your bangs with thinning scissors can give you the perfect style you desire. In this article, we’ll show you how to thin out bangs with thinning scissors, step by step.

How to Thin Out Bangs with Thinning Scissors

Thinning out bangs with thinning scissors is a simple and easy process. Here’s how to do it:

Step 1: Start with Dry Hair

Before you start, make sure your hair is dry. Wet hair can make it difficult to see how much hair you’re actually thinning, so it’s best to work with dry hair.

Step 2: Comb Your Bangs

Using a fine-tooth comb, comb your bangs straight down. This will help you see which areas need thinning out.

Step 3: Hold Your Bangs

Using your fingers, hold your bangs at the point where you want to start thinning.

Step 4: Cut with Thinning Scissors

Using your thinning scissors, start cutting in a zigzag pattern. This will create texture and remove some of the bulk. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as you can always go back and thin out more if needed.

Step 5: Continue Cutting

Continue cutting in a zigzag pattern until you’ve thinned out the desired amount of hair.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

After you’ve finished thinning out your bangs, take a step back and check your progress. If you need to thin out more hair, repeat steps 3-5 until you’re happy with the result.

Tips for Thinning Out Bangs with Thinning Scissors

Thinning out bangs with thinning scissors can be a bit intimidating, especially if you’ve never done it before. Here are some tips to help you get the perfect look:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up.
  • Use a pair of high-quality thinning scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • Don’t cut too much at once. It’s better to thin out a little bit at a time.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can cause split ends.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some answers to common questions about thinning out bangs with thinning scissors:

Can you thin out bangs with regular scissors?

You can thin out bangs with regular scissors, but it’s not recommended. Regular scissors can create uneven cuts and cause split ends. Thinning scissors are designed specifically for this purpose and will give you a cleaner cut.

What kind of thinning scissors should I use?

You should use a pair of high-quality thinning scissors that are designed for hair cutting. Look for scissors with sharp blades and a comfortable grip.

How often should I thin out my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to feel thick and heavy. As a general rule, you should try to thin out your bangs every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care Tips

Thinning out your bangs can be a great way to change up your look, but it’s important to take care of your hair afterwards. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, too often.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

Now that you know how to thin out bangs with thinning scissors, you can give yourself a fresh new look. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you’ll have beautiful, natural-looking bangs in no time.
