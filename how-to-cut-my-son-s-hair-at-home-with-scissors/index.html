How To Cut My Son s Hair At Home With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Mar 24, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts for your son? Or maybe you just want to try your hand at cutting hair? Whatever the reason, cutting your son's hair at home with scissors is a great way to save money and bond with your child. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your son's hair at home with scissors, step-by-step. Let's get started!

How to Cut My Son's Hair at Home with Scissors

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting your son's hair, you'll need to gather a few supplies. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clippers (optional). You may also want to have a spray bottle filled with water to dampen the hair as you cut it.

Step 2: Decide on the Haircut

Before you start cutting, you'll need to decide on the haircut you want to give your son. Are you going for a buzz cut or something more complicated? Look up some pictures online to get an idea of what you want to do.

Step 3: Wash and Dry the Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to wash your son's hair and dry it thoroughly. This will make it easier to cut and will ensure that the hair is clean.

Step 4: Section the Hair

Using the comb, section your son's hair into manageable sections. Start with the sides and work your way to the top of the head. Pin each section up with a hair clip to keep it out of the way.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Starting with the sides, use the scissors to cut the hair. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut in a straight line. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as you can always go back and cut more later.

Step 6: Blend the Hair

Once you've cut the sides, it's time to blend the hair. Use the scissors to cut the hair on top of the head, blending it into the sides. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it at an angle, blending it into the rest of the hair.

Step 7: Trim the Bangs

If your son has bangs, use the scissors to trim them. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it in a straight line. Be careful not to cut too much, as you can always go back and cut more later.

Step 8: Use Clippers (Optional)

If you want to give your son a buzz cut, you can use hair clippers to do so. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one, until you achieve the desired length.

Step 9: Clean Up

Once you're done cutting, use the comb and scissors to clean up any stray hairs. Use a towel to wipe away any hair clippings.

Step 10: Style the Hair

Finally, style your son's hair the way he likes it. Use a little bit of gel or hairspray to keep it in place.

Tips for Cutting Your Son's Hair at Home with Scissors

  • Start with small snips and work your way up. You can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's cut.
  • Keep the scissors pointed down when cutting. This will help you avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Work in a well-lit area so you can see what you're doing.
  • Have a backup plan in case the haircut doesn't turn out the way you want it to. You can always take your son to a professional to fix it.

FAQs About Cutting Your Son's Hair at Home with Scissors

Can I cut my son's hair with regular scissors?

Yes, you can cut your son's hair with regular scissors. However, it's best to use scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair, as they are sharper and will give you better results.

How often should I cut my son's hair?

It depends on how fast his hair grows and how quickly it becomes unruly. As a general rule, you should cut your son's hair every 4-6 weeks.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go back and fix it. If you're not confident in your abilities, it's best to take your son to a professional to fix the mistake.

Hair Care Tips for Boys

Now that you know how to cut your son's hair at home with scissors, it's important to take care of his hair. Here are some hair care tips for boys:

  • Wash his hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools, like blow dryers and flat irons, as they can damage the hair.
  • Encourage your son to wear a hat or use sunscreen when he's outside to protect his scalp from the sun.
  • Trim his hair regularly to keep it looking neat and healthy.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to cut your son's hair at home with scissors like a pro. Have fun and enjoy the bonding experience!
