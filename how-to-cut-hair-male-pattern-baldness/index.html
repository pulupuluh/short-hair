How To Cut Hair Male Pattern Baldness

Written by Aprilia Aug 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Male pattern baldness is a common condition among men, affecting over 50% of the male population. While it can be discouraging to have hair loss, it doesn't mean that you can't still look your best. One of the ways to do so is by cutting your hair in a way that complements your baldness. Here's how to cut hair male pattern baldness:

Description

In this article, we will discuss how to cut hair male pattern baldness. We will go over the steps, tips, and FAQs to ensure that you can achieve the best haircut possible. We will also discuss how to care for your hair after the cut.

How to Cut Hair Male Pattern Baldness

Step 1: Assess Your Hair Loss

The first step in cutting your hair is to assess your hair loss. Determine the areas where you have thinning or bald spots. This will help you decide how to cut your hair and what style will look best on you.

Step 2: Choose a Haircut Style

Once you have assessed your hair loss, the next step is to choose a haircut style that complements your baldness. Some of the most popular styles for men with baldness include buzz cuts, crew cuts, and shaved heads. These styles are low maintenance and require little styling.

Step 3: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of clippers, a comb, and a mirror. If you have long hair, you may also need a pair of scissors.

Step 4: Wash Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to cut and will ensure that you get an even cut.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Start by using the clippers to trim the sides and back of your head. Use a comb to guide the clippers and make sure you are cutting evenly. If you have longer hair on top, use the scissors to trim it to the desired length.

Step 6: Finish Up

Once you have finished cutting, use the mirror to check for any uneven spots. If you notice any, make sure to trim them until you have an even cut.

Tips

- Start with a longer guard on the clippers and work your way down to a shorter guard until you find the right length for your hair.

- If you're unsure about cutting your hair yourself, consider going to a professional barber.

- If you have long hair, consider donating it to a charity that makes wigs for cancer patients.

FAQ

Can I still have a stylish haircut with male pattern baldness?

Absolutely! There are many stylish haircuts that work well with male pattern baldness, including buzz cuts, crew cuts, and shaved heads.

Do I need to use hair products after cutting my hair?

It's up to you. If you want to style your hair, you can use products like gel, pomade, or hairspray. However, these products are optional and not necessary for a good haircut.

How often should I cut my hair?

This depends on the style you choose and how fast your hair grows. Generally, most men with baldness choose to cut their hair every 2-4 weeks to maintain their style.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to ensure that it stays healthy. This includes washing your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo, conditioning it, and using a moisturizing oil or serum to keep it soft and shiny.

Cutting your hair with male pattern baldness doesn't have to be difficult. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can achieve a stylish and low maintenance haircut that complements your baldness. Remember to take care of your hair after the cut to keep it looking healthy and shiny.
