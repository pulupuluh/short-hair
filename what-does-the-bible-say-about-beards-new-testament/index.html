What Does The Bible Say About Beards New Testament

Written by Robby May 07, 2023 · 7 min read

Welcome to our blog post about what the Bible says about beards in the New Testament. In this article, we will be discussing the different interpretations of beards in the New Testament and what they signify. We will also be sharing some tips on how to care for your beard according to biblical principles. Let’s dive in!

Table of Contents

  • Beards in the Bible
  • Beards in the New Testament
  • Interpretations of Beards
  • Tips for Caring for Your Beard

Beards in the Bible

The Bible mentions beards in both the Old and New Testaments. In the Old Testament, beards were a symbol of masculinity and dignity. Men were expected to grow and maintain their beards as a sign of their status as leaders and protectors of their families.

In the New Testament, the significance of beards is less clear. There are only a few mentions of beards, and they do not seem to hold the same cultural importance as they did in the Old Testament.

Beards in the New Testament

The New Testament mentions beards in several places. For example, in 1 Corinthians 11:14, it says, “Does not the very nature of things teach you that if a man has long hair, it is a disgrace to him, but that if a woman has long hair, it is her glory? For long hair is given to her as a covering. If anyone wants to be contentious about this, we have no other practice—nor do the churches of God.”

Some people interpret this passage as a condemnation of long beards on men. However, it is important to note that the passage specifically mentions hair, not beards. Additionally, the context of the passage is about gender roles in worship, not about the length of a man’s beard.

Interpretations of Beards

Interpretations of the significance of beards in the New Testament vary. Some people believe that beards are a sign of masculinity and should be grown and maintained as a way of honoring God. Others believe that beards are irrelevant to spiritual matters and that it is up to each person to decide whether or not to grow one.

Ultimately, the decision to grow a beard or not is a personal one. While the Bible may provide some guidance on the matter, it is up to each individual to determine what is right for them.

Tips for Caring for Your Beard

If you do choose to grow a beard, there are some biblical principles that can guide you in caring for it. Here are a few tips:

Keep it clean

Just as you would wash your hair, it’s important to keep your beard clean. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to keep it soft and manageable.

Trim it regularly

Regularly trimming your beard will keep it looking neat and give it a more polished appearance.

Use beard oil

Beard oil can help moisturize your beard and prevent it from becoming dry and brittle. Look for a natural, unscented oil to avoid any potential allergic reactions.

FAQ

Q: Is it a sin to shave my beard?

A: No, it is not a sin to shave your beard. The Bible provides guidance on the matter, but ultimately the decision to grow or shave a beard is a personal one.

Q: What does the Bible say about mustaches?

A: The Bible does not specifically mention mustaches. However, the principles of cleanliness and modesty can be applied to mustaches as well as beards.

Q: Can women grow beards?

A: Women can grow facial hair, including beards. However, cultural and societal expectations often dictate that women remove facial hair.

We hope this article has provided some useful information about what the Bible says about beards in the New Testament. Remember, the decision to grow or shave a beard is a personal one, and it’s up to each individual to determine what is right for them.
