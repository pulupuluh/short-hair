Can A Perm Make Your Hair Look Thicker

Written by Robby Aug 16, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of having thin, lifeless hair? Do you want to add volume and thickness to your locks? If so, you may be wondering if a perm can help. In this blog post, we’ll explore whether or not a perm can make your hair look thicker, as well as provide tips on how to care for permed hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get a Perm
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Perming Your Hair
  • Tips for Caring for Permed Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Permed Hair

Description

A perm involves using chemicals to change the structure of your hair. The process involves applying a solution to your hair, wrapping it around rods, and then applying a neutralizer to set the curls. Perming your hair can make it look thicker because the curls add volume and texture to your hair, making it appear fuller.

However, it's important to note that perming your hair can also cause damage. The chemicals used in the process can be harsh on your hair, and over-processing can lead to breakage and thinning. It's important to take proper care of permed hair to minimize damage and maintain healthy, voluminous locks.

How to Get a Perm

If you're interested in getting a perm to add thickness and volume to your hair, the first step is to find a reputable stylist who is experienced in perming hair. You'll want to schedule a consultation with your stylist to discuss the process, the chemicals used, and any potential risks or side effects.

Before getting a perm, it's important to make sure your hair is in good condition. If your hair is damaged or weak, it may not be able to withstand the chemicals used in the process. Your stylist may recommend deep conditioning treatments or other hair care routines to prepare your hair for perming.

Step-by-Step Guide to Perming Your Hair

If you decide to move forward with a perm, here's a step-by-step guide to the process:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue.
  2. Section your hair into small sections and wrap each section around a perm rod. The size of the rod will determine the size of the curls.
  3. Apply the perm solution to your hair, making sure to saturate each section thoroughly.
  4. Cover your hair with a plastic cap and let the solution sit for the recommended amount of time.
  5. Rinse your hair with warm water to remove the solution.
  6. Apply the neutralizer to your hair to set the curls.
  7. Rinse your hair again with warm water and remove the perm rods.
  8. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and let it sit for several minutes.
  9. Rinse your hair with cool water to seal the cuticle and reduce frizz.

Tips for Caring for Permed Hair

Perming your hair can be a great way to add volume and thickness, but it's important to take proper care of your hair to minimize damage and maintain healthy, beautiful locks. Here are some tips for caring for permed hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair for the first 48 hours after getting a perm to allow the curls to set.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent further damage to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage permed hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushes can break the curls and cause frizz.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or hair dyes on permed hair, as they can cause further damage and breakage.

FAQs

Q: Can a perm make your hair look thicker?

A: Yes, perming your hair can add volume and texture to your locks, making it appear thicker and fuller.

Q: How often should you get a perm?

A: It's recommended to wait at least 3 months in between perms to prevent damage and breakage.

Q: Can perming your hair cause damage?

A: Yes, perming your hair involves using chemicals that can be harsh on your locks. Over-processing can cause damage and breakage, so it's important to take proper care of permed hair.

Hair Care for Permed Hair

Perming your hair can be a great way to add volume and thickness to your locks, but it's important to take proper care of your hair to maintain healthy, beautiful curls. Here are some tips for caring for permed hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent further damage to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushes can break the curls and cause frizz.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or hair dyes on permed hair, as they can cause further damage and breakage.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it hydrated and healthy.

By following these tips, you can keep your permed hair looking voluminous and healthy for months to come.
