How To Cut Your Own Hair Skin Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 02, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to a skin fade. However, with the right tools and techniques, it is possible to achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own hair skin fade, including tips and FAQs to ensure you get the best results.

Tools You Will Need

Before you begin, it is important to gather all the necessary tools. Here are the items you will need:

  • Clippers with adjustable guards
  • A comb
  • Scissors
  • A handheld mirror
  • Clipper oil

How to Cut Your Own Hair Skin Fade

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

The first step is to prepare your hair. Start by washing and towel-drying your hair. This will remove any dirt or product buildup that may interfere with the cutting process. Next, comb your hair in the direction that it naturally falls. This will help you to see the natural lines of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Start with the Clippers

Begin the cut with clippers and a number 2 guard. Start at the bottom of your head and work your way up, going against the grain. Be sure to use a comb to lift and hold the hair, so that the clippers can easily cut through it. Continue this process until you have trimmed the hair to a uniform length.

Step 3: Fade the Sides

Next, switch to a number 1 guard and begin to fade the sides. Start at the bottom of your head and work your way up, creating a gradient effect. Be sure to blend the hair evenly, using the clipper over comb technique. This will ensure a seamless transition from short to long hair.

Step 4: Blend the Top

Once you have faded the sides, it's time to blend the top. Use scissors to cut the hair on top to the desired length. Be sure to use a comb to lift and hold the hair as you cut. Next, use the clipper over comb technique to blend the top with the sides.

Step 5: Refine the Look

After you have cut the hair to the desired length, it's time to refine the look. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs and to shape the hairline around your ears and neck. Be sure to use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair Skin Fade

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to ensure you don't cut too much off at once.
  • Use a comb to lift and hold the hair as you cut, to ensure a uniform length.
  • Take breaks and step back to assess your progress, to avoid overcutting.
  • Don't be too hard on yourself â it takes practice to perfect the art of cutting your own hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I use any type of clippers?

A: It is recommended to use clippers with adjustable guards, as this will give you more control over the length of the hair.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: This can vary depending on your hair type and how quickly it grows. However, it is generally recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: Don't panic! Hair grows back, and mistakes can be corrected. Take a break, step back, and assess the situation. If necessary, visit a professional stylist to fix any mistakes.

Hair Care After Your Skin Fade

After you have cut your own hair skin fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to remove any buildup and keep it healthy.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair soft and hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Visit a professional stylist every few months to maintain the shape and style of your hair.

Cutting your own hair skin fade may seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it is possible to achieve a professional-looking cut. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be too hard on yourself. With practice, you'll be able to master the art of cutting your own hair.
