What s The Difference Between A Low Fade And A Taper

Written by Eveline Jul 22, 2023 Â· 7 min read

When it comes to men's haircuts, there are a lot of different styles to choose from. Two popular choices are the low fade and the taper. While both styles involve cutting the hair shorter towards the bottom, there are some key differences between the two. In this article, we'll break down the differences between a low fade and a taper.

What is a Low Fade?

A low fade is a haircut that involves cutting the hair shorter towards the bottom of the head. The fade starts at the bottom of the hairline and gradually fades into longer hair towards the top of the head. This style is often worn with longer hair on top that can be styled in a variety of ways.

How to Get a Low Fade

To get a low fade, you'll need to visit a barber or hairstylist who is skilled in this style. They will use clippers to gradually cut the hair shorter towards the bottom of the head. The fade can be blended into the longer hair on top using scissors or clippers.

Tips for Maintaining a Low Fade

To maintain a low fade, you'll need to visit the barber or hairstylist every 4-6 weeks for a trim. You can also use a styling product to keep the longer hair on top in place.

FAQ about Low Fade

Q: Can I get a low fade with curly hair?
A: Yes, a low fade can work well with curly hair. Just make sure to find a hairstylist who is experienced with cutting curly hair.

Q: Will a low fade look good on me?
A: That depends on your face shape and hair texture. It's best to consult with a hairstylist to determine if a low fade is the right choice for you.

How to Care for a Low Fade

To care for a low fade, you'll need to wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. You can also use a styling product to keep the longer hair on top in place.

What is a Taper?

A taper is a haircut that involves cutting the hair shorter towards the bottom of the head. Unlike a fade, which gradually fades into longer hair, a taper is a more gradual transition. The hair is cut shorter towards the bottom of the head, but there is no distinct line between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair on the sides and back.

How to Get a Taper

To get a taper, you'll need to visit a barber or hairstylist who is skilled in this style. They will use clippers and/or scissors to gradually cut the hair shorter towards the bottom of the head. The hair is blended to create a more natural look.

Tips for Maintaining a Taper

To maintain a taper, you'll need to visit the barber or hairstylist every 4-6 weeks for a trim. You can also use a styling product to keep the longer hair on top in place.

FAQ about Taper

Q: Can I get a taper with long hair?
A: Yes, a taper can work well with longer hair. Just make sure to discuss your desired length with your hairstylist.

Q: Will a taper look good on me?
A: That depends on your face shape and hair texture. It's best to consult with a hairstylist to determine if a taper is the right choice for you.

How to Care for a Taper

To care for a taper, you'll need to wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. You can also use a styling product to keep the longer hair on top in place.

Conclusion

While a low fade and a taper are similar in that they involve cutting the hair shorter towards the bottom, there are some key differences between the two. A low fade is a more dramatic transition, while a taper is a more gradual one. Both styles can work well with a variety of face shapes and hair textures, so it's important to consult with a hairstylist to determine which style is right for you.
