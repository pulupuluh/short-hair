The Best Hairstyle For Petite Round Face A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Mar 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Petite round faces are characterized by a shorter, wider face shape with round cheeks and a small chin. This face shape is adorable and youthful, but it can be challenging to find a hairstyle that complements it perfectly. In this article, we will explore the best hairstyles for petite round faces, along with tips, tricks, and hair care advice to help you achieve the look you desire.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Face Shape
  • Best Hairstyles for Petite Round Faces
  • Tips and Tricks for Styling Your Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Petite Round Faces

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before we dive into the best hairstyles for petite round faces, it's crucial to know how to determine your face shape. Here's how:

  1. Stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back.
  2. Use a piece of chalk or lipstick to outline your face on the mirror.
  3. Step back and look at the shape you drew. Is your face wider at the forehead and temples, narrowing at the jawline? If so, you likely have a round face shape.

Best Hairstyles for Petite Round Faces

Now that you've determined your face shape let's explore the best hairstyles for petite round faces.

1. Long Layers

Long layers add dimension and volume to your hair, elongating your face shape. Opt for layers that start at your collarbone or below, with subtle face-framing layers.

2. Bob

A classic bob is an excellent choice for petite round faces. Keep the length at or above your chin to create the illusion of a longer face shape.

3. Pixie Cut

If you're feeling daring, a pixie cut can be incredibly flattering for petite round faces. Keep the top voluminous and the sides short to accentuate your cheekbones.

4. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs add a touch of sophistication and create angles to your face, making it appear slimmer.

5. Beach Waves

Beach waves are universally flattering, and they're perfect for petite round faces. This hairstyle adds texture and volume to your hair, elongating your face shape.

Tips and Tricks for Styling Your Hair

Now that you have some hairstyle inspiration let's dive into some tips and tricks to help you style your hair to perfection.

1. Blow-dry your hair upside down

Blow-drying your hair upside down creates volume and lift at the roots, elongating your face shape.

2. Use a round brush

A round brush is perfect for creating volume and shape. Use it to curl the ends of your hair inward, creating the illusion of a narrower face shape.

3. Avoid heavy bangs

Heavy bangs can shorten your face shape, making it appear even rounder. Opt for side-swept bangs or no bangs at all.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions regarding hairstyles for petite round faces:

1. Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail?

Absolutely! A high ponytail or topknot can elongate your face shape.

2. Should I avoid short hairstyles?

Not necessarily. Short hairstyles like a pixie cut can be incredibly flattering, as long as they're styled correctly.

3. What hair color is best for petite round faces?

It's less about the hair color and more about the hairstyle. However, adding highlights around your face can create the illusion of a more extended face shape.

Hair Care for Petite Round Faces

It's essential to take good care of your hair to ensure it looks its best. Here are some tips for hair care for petite round faces:

1. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner

Volumizing products can add lift and volume to your hair, elongating your face shape.

2. Avoid heavy styling products

Heavy styling products can weigh down your hair, making it appear flatter and wider. Opt for lightweight products instead.

3. Get regular trims

Regular trims keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, allowing your hair to look its best.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to find the perfect hairstyle for your petite round face shape. Remember, it's all about creating angles and elongating your face shape to achieve the most flattering look possible.
