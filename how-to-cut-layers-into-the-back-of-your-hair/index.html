How To Cut Layers Into The Back Of Your Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Feb 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Layers are a great way to add texture and movement to any hairstyle, and cutting layers into the back of your hair can help create a more voluminous and layered look. If you want to try this at home, read on for a step-by-step guide on how to cut layers into the back of your hair.

How to Cut Layers into the Back of Your Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a sharp pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It is best to cut your hair when it is clean and dry. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair and make it easier to cut the layers evenly.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to divide your hair into three sections: the top, middle, and bottom. Clip the top and middle sections out of the way, leaving only the bottom section loose.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting at the back of your head, use your scissors to cut the bottom layer of your hair. Cut straight across, making sure to keep the hair taut and even.

Step 5: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the middle section of your hair and comb it straight down. Use your bottom layer as a guide and cut the middle layer in the same way.

Step 6: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top section of your hair and comb it straight down. Use the middle layer as a guide and cut the top layer in the same way.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

Once you have cut all three layers, use your scissors to blend the layers together. Cut into the hair at a slight angle, creating a soft and natural look.

Step 8: Check Your Work

When you have finished cutting, shake out your hair and run your fingers through it. Check for any uneven spots or areas that need more blending.

Step 9: Style Your Hair

Once you are happy with your new layers, style your hair as desired. You can use a curling iron or flat iron to create more texture and volume.

Tips for Cutting Layers into the Back of Your Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect layered look:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back on.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Cut your hair in a well-lit area so you can see what you're doing.
  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to take your time and cut gradually.
  • When blending the layers, make sure to cut into the hair at a slight angle for a natural look.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about cutting layers into the back of their hair:

Can I cut layers into my hair if it's already layered?

Yes, you can. Just make sure to use your existing layers as a guide and cut the new layers slightly shorter.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain your style. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

What if I make a mistake?

If you accidentally cut too much off or create an uneven look, don't panic. You can always go to a professional stylist to fix it or try to even it out yourself by cutting small sections at a time.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Here are some tips to keep your layered hair looking healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage the ends.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain your layers.
  • Try using a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and keep it looking shiny.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you can cut layers into the back of your hair like a pro. Remember to take your time, use sharp scissors, and have fun experimenting with your new look!
