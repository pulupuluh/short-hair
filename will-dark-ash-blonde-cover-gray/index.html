Will Dark Ash Blonde Cover Gray A Complete Guide

Written by Christine May 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're someone who's been dealing with gray hair for a while now, you're probably wondering if there's a way to cover it up without having to go to a salon every few weeks. One option that many people consider is dyeing their hair at home, and specifically using a dark ash blonde color. But will dark ash blonde cover gray hair? In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about this process.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Dye Your Hair Dark Ash Blonde
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Achieving the Best Results
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care After Dyeing

Description

Dark ash blonde is a color that's in the medium to light brown range, with cool, ashy undertones. It's a popular shade for those who want a natural-looking hair color that's not too light or too dark. When it comes to covering gray hair, dark ash blonde can be a good option for those with light to medium gray hair. It won't work as well on hair that's heavily gray or white, as those colors are much harder to cover up.

How to Dye Your Hair Dark Ash Blonde

Dyeing your hair at home can be a bit intimidating, especially if you've never done it before. But with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. Here's what you'll need:

  • A box of dark ash blonde hair dye
  • Gloves
  • An old shirt or towel to protect your clothing
  • A wide-tooth comb
  • A timer

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to dye your hair dark ash blonde:

  1. Start by doing a patch test 24 hours before you plan to dye your hair, to make sure you're not allergic to the hair dye.
  2. Put on the gloves and mix the hair dye according to the instructions on the box.
  3. Apply the dye to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  4. Use the wide-tooth comb to distribute the dye evenly through your hair.
  5. Set a timer for the amount of time specified on the box (usually around 25-30 minutes).
  6. When the timer goes off, rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water until the water runs clear.
  7. Apply the conditioner that comes with the hair dye, and leave it on for the amount of time specified on the box.
  8. Rinse your hair again, and style as usual.

Tips for Achieving the Best Results

If you want to get the best results from dyeing your hair dark ash blonde, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose a shade that's close to your natural hair color, or slightly darker.
  • If you have a lot of gray hair, consider using a pre-treatment that helps the dye adhere better to your hair.
  • Don't wash your hair for at least 24 hours before dyeing it, to allow your scalp's natural oils to build up and protect your skin.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo a few days before dyeing your hair, to remove any product buildup that could interfere with the dye.

FAQs

1. Will dark ash blonde cover gray hair completely?

It depends on how much gray hair you have. If you have light to medium gray hair, dark ash blonde can cover it up pretty well. But if you have a lot of gray hair, especially if it's white or silver, you may need to use a darker shade or go to a salon for professional help.

2. Can I dye my hair dark ash blonde if it's already been colored?

Yes, you can. But keep in mind that if your hair has been previously colored, the dark ash blonde may not show up as well or may turn out a different color than expected. It's always best to do a strand test first to see how the color will look on your hair.

3. How often do I need to touch up my dark ash blonde hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your gray roots start to show. On average, you'll need to touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care After Dyeing

Once you've dyed your hair dark ash blonde, it's important to take good care of it to keep the color looking fresh and vibrant. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that's specifically designed for color-treated hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can cause the color to fade faster.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to direct sunlight or chlorine, as these can also cause the color to fade.
  • Consider using a color-depositing hair mask or treatment once a week to help maintain the color.

Overall, dyeing your hair dark ash blonde can be a great way to cover up gray hair and achieve a natural-looking hair color. By following the tips and techniques outlined in this article, you can get the best possible results.
