How To Cut Your Hair Very Short At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 16, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you've never done it before. However, with a little bit of patience and practice, you can learn how to cut your hair very short at home. In this guide, we'll take you through the process step-by-step, providing tips and advice along the way. So, let's dive in!

How to Cut Your Hair Very Short at Home

Before we get started, it's important to note that cutting your hair very short can be tricky, so take your time and don't rush the process. You'll also need a few tools to get the job done, including:

  • Scissors
  • Clipper
  • Comb
  • Mirror

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. This will ensure that you have a clean and manageable canvas to work with. Once your hair is dry, comb it out and make sure there are no tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, use your comb to section your hair into small sections. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly and ensure that you don't miss any spots. Start by sectioning your hair into two parts: the top and the bottom.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin cutting your hair with the clipper, starting at the nape of your neck and working your way up. Use a higher guard attachment to start, and gradually work your way down to a lower guard if you want a shorter cut. Make sure to take your time and go slowly, checking your progress in the mirror as you go.

Step 4: Cut the Sides and Back

Once you've finished the back, move on to the sides. Use the same technique, starting with a higher guard and working your way down. Cut the sides in sections, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Remember to check your progress in the mirror as you go.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Now it's time to cut the top of your hair. Start by combing it straight up, and then use your scissors to cut across the top. Take small sections at a time, and make sure to cut evenly. If you want a shorter cut, you can use the clipper with a lower guard attachment to take off more length.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Once you've finished cutting your hair, check for evenness. Use the mirror to check the back and sides, and make any necessary adjustments. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and trim them until everything looks even.

Step 7: Clean Up

Once you're happy with your haircut, it's time to clean up. Use your comb and scissors to trim any stray hairs, and make sure to clean up any hair clippings around your neck and shoulders.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair Very Short at Home

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results when cutting your hair very short at home:

  • Start with a higher guard attachment and work your way down gradually
  • Take your time and don't rush the process
  • Check your progress in the mirror as you go
  • Trim small sections at a time, rather than trying to cut everything at once
  • Use a comb to section your hair and make sure everything is even

FAQ: Cutting Your Hair Very Short at Home

Can I cut my hair very short at home?

Yes, you can! However, it's important to take your time and be patient. Cutting your hair very short can be tricky, so don't rush the process.

What tools do I need to cut my hair very short at home?

You'll need a clipper, scissors, comb, and mirror.

Do I need to section my hair when cutting it very short?

Yes, sectioning your hair will make it easier to cut it evenly and ensure that you don't miss any spots.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair Very Short at Home

After cutting your hair very short at home, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. You may also want to use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

Cutting your hair very short at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With a little bit of practice, you can learn how to do it yourself and save money on salon visits. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and follow these tips for the best results.
