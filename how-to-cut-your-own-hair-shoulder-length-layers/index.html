How To Cut Your Own Hair Shoulder Length Layers

Written by Petter May 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on haircuts at the salon? Well, you're in luck because with a little practice and patience, you can easily cut your own hair into flattering shoulder length layers. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process step by step, answer some commonly asked questions, and give you some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect haircut at home.

How To

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, hair ties, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp while you work.

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before you start cutting. This will ensure that your hair is clean and free of any tangles or knots that could make it difficult to cut. Make sure your hair is completely dry as wet hair tends to look longer and could result in uneven layers.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Tie each section with a hair tie to keep it out of the way while you work.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting with the back sections, take a small section of hair and hold it straight up. Use your scissors to cut the hair at the desired length. Repeat this process, working your way through all the sections in the back.

When you're done with the back, move on to the front sections. Take small sections of hair and pull them to the front. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the chin level and working your way down to the desired length.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all your hair to the desired length, it's time to blend the layers. Take small sections of hair and hold them straight up. Use your scissors to cut into the hair at an angle, creating soft layers. This will help to blend the layers together and create a more natural look.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind while cutting your own hair:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't overthink it. Shoulder length layers are forgiving, so if you make a mistake, it's not the end of the world.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so take a break if your arms get tired.

FAQ

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting your own hair:

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if it's curly. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to bounce up when it dries, so cut a little longer than you think you need to.

What if I mess up?

If you mess up, don't panic. You can always go to a salon and have a professional fix it for you. Or, if you're feeling brave, you can try to fix it yourself.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to cut your own hair into flattering shoulder length layers, you can save money and feel confident in your ability to give yourself a great haircut at home. Happy cutting!
