How To Know If A Fringe Is Right For You

Written by Bardi Jul 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to know if a fringe or bangs would suit you. Adding a fringe to your hair can be a great way to change up your look, but it's important to consider whether it will suit your face shape, hair type, and personal style. Read on to learn more!

How to Know if a Fringe Will Suit Your Face Shape

The first thing to consider when deciding whether to get a fringe is your face shape. Different styles of fringe suit different face shapes, so it's important to choose a style that flatters your features.

Round Face

If you have a round face, a side-swept fringe can help to elongate your face and create more definition. Avoid blunt fringes, as these can make your face appear even rounder.

Square Face

A soft, wispy fringe can help to soften the angles of a square face. Avoid heavy, straight-across fringes, as these can make your face appear boxy.

Oval Face

Lucky you! Oval faces can wear pretty much any style of fringe, so experiment with different lengths and textures to find what works best for you.

Heart-Shaped Face

A long, choppy fringe can balance out a heart-shaped face by drawing attention away from a wider forehead. Avoid short, blunt fringes that can make your forehead appear even wider.

How to Know if a Fringe Will Suit Your Hair Type

Another important factor to consider is your hair type. Different styles of fringe work better with different textures and thicknesses of hair.

Thick Hair

If you have thick hair, a blunt, heavy fringe can work well to add structure and definition to your hair. Avoid wispy, choppy fringes that can get lost in your hair.

Thin Hair

If you have thin hair, a wispy, layered fringe can help to add volume and texture to your hair. Avoid heavy, blunt fringes that can make your hair appear even thinner.

Curly Hair

If you have curly hair, a long, side-swept fringe can work well to frame your face and add definition to your curls. Avoid short, blunt fringes that can disrupt your natural curl pattern.

Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing the Right Fringe

Now that you know what to consider when choosing a fringe, here's a step-by-step guide to help you make the right choice:

  1. Determine your face shape.
  2. Consider your hair type and texture.
  3. Look for inspiration photos of fringes that you like.
  4. Consult with your hairstylist to find a style that will suit you.
  5. Try on clip-in fringes to get a feel for what the style will look like on you.
  6. Take the plunge and get your new fringe!

Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe

Once you've got your new fringe, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking fresh.
  • Blow-dry your fringe with a round brush to help it hold its shape.
  • Avoid touching your fringe too much, as this can make it greasy and cause it to lose its shape.
  • Use dry shampoo to keep your fringe looking fresh between washes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if a fringe will suit me?

Consider your face shape, hair type, and personal style when choosing a fringe. Consult with your hairstylist and try on clip-in fringes to get a feel for what the style will look like on you.

How often should I trim my fringe?

Trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking fresh.

What if I don't like my fringe?

If you don't like your fringe, don't panic! It will grow out in a few weeks. In the meantime, try styling it differently or pinning it back.

Hair Care for Fringes

Keep your fringe looking its best by taking care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Condition your hair to keep it soft and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

We hope this guide has helped you to decide whether a fringe is right for you. Remember, the most important thing is to choose a style that makes you feel confident and happy!
