How To Do A Twist Out On Short Thin Natural Hair

Written by Luffy Apr 15, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have short, thin natural hair, you may be wondering if it’s possible to achieve a twist out. The answer is yes! A twist out is a great way to style your natural hair without using any heat, and it’s perfect for those who have shorter or thinner hair. In this article, we’ll go over how to do a twist out on short thin natural hair, step by step.

Table of Content

  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to

The first thing you need to do is to make sure your hair is clean and moisturized. You can’t achieve a good twist out on dry or dirty hair. So, start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and then apply a leave-in conditioner to your damp hair.

Next, you’ll need to section your hair. Depending on the thickness of your hair, you may need to section it into smaller or larger sections. Use clips to hold each section in place while you work on the other sections.

Step by step

Step 1: Part your hair

Start by parting your hair into small sections. Use a rat-tail comb to create a clean part.

Step 2: Apply a twisting product

Next, apply a twisting product to each section of hair. You can use a curling cream, a twisting butter, or a gel. Be sure to apply enough product to coat your hair evenly.

Step 3: Twist your hair

Start twisting your hair by dividing each section into two pieces. Twist the two pieces together tightly, making sure to keep the twist as close to your scalp as possible. Continue twisting until you reach the end of your hair.

Step 4: Repeat on all sections

Repeat steps 1-3 on all sections of your hair. Make sure the twists are all going in the same direction.

Step 5: Let your hair dry

Let your hair dry completely. You can air dry or sit under a hooded dryer.

Step 6: Undo the twists

Once your hair is completely dry, carefully undo the twists. Use your fingers to separate the twists and create volume.

Tips

Tip 1: Use a satin pillowcase or bonnet to protect your hair while you sleep.

Tip 2: Don’t over-manipulate your hair. Too much twisting or separating can cause frizz and breakage.

Tip 3: Use a light oil or serum to add shine to your hair after undoing the twists.

FAQ

Q: Can I do a twist out on wet hair?

A: No, it’s best to do a twist out on damp or dry hair. Wet hair won’t hold the twists well, and it can take a long time to dry.

Q: How often should I do a twist out?

A: You can do a twist out as often as you like, but be sure to give your hair a break in between. Too much twisting can cause damage and breakage.

Hair Care

It’s important to take good care of your natural hair, especially if it’s short and thin. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly, but don’t over-wash or use harsh shampoos.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and breakage.
  • Protect your hair at night by sleeping with a satin pillowcase or bonnet.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and using the steps outlined in this article, you can achieve a perfect twist out on your short thin natural hair. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you. With a little practice, you’ll be able to create a twist out that looks great and lasts for days.
