What Causes Hair To Suddenly Become Frizzy

Written by Daniel May 31, 2023 · 9 min read

Frizzy hair can be a frustrating experience for anyone. One moment, your hair is smooth and shiny, and the next, it’s a wild mess of curls and flyaways. In this article, we’ll explore the various factors that can cause hair to suddenly become frizzy, as well as some tips on how to prevent and manage frizz.

Humidity

Humidity is one of the most common causes of frizzy hair. When the air is humid, moisture in the air can penetrate the hair cuticle and cause it to swell. This can make hair look frizzy and unruly.

To prevent frizz caused by humidity, try using a humidity-blocking hair product, such as a serum or spray. You can also try wearing a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the humidity.

Heat Damage

Heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can cause damage to the hair cuticle, which can lead to frizz. Additionally, using hot tools on wet or damp hair can cause the hair to become more prone to frizz.

To prevent heat damage and frizz, avoid using hot tools on wet or damp hair. Always use a heat protectant spray or serum before using heat styling tools, and try to limit heat styling to once or twice a week.

Chemical Treatments

Chemical treatments, such as coloring, perming, and relaxing, can cause damage to the hair cuticle, which can lead to frizz. Additionally, chemical treatments can make the hair more porous, which can make it more prone to frizz.

To prevent frizz caused by chemical treatments, always have them done by a professional. Be sure to use a deep conditioning treatment regularly to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

Overwashing

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and frizz. Additionally, using hot water and harsh shampoos can also contribute to frizz.

To prevent frizz caused by overwashing, try to wash your hair no more than two or three times a week. Use lukewarm or cool water, and choose a shampoo that is gentle and moisturizing.

Lack of Moisture

When hair is dry and lacking in moisture, it can become frizzy and brittle. This can be caused by a variety of factors, such as genetics, age, and environmental factors.

To prevent frizz caused by lack of moisture, use a deep conditioning treatment once a week. Additionally, try to avoid using harsh hair products that can dry out your hair.

Prevention Tips

Preventing frizzy hair starts with good hair care habits. Here are some tips to help prevent frizz:

  • Use a gentle, moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid overwashing your hair
  • Use lukewarm or cool water when washing your hair
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid using harsh hair products
  • Use a humidity-blocking hair product to prevent frizz in humid weather
  • Avoid using hot styling tools on wet or damp hair
  • Limit heat styling to once or twice a week

How to Manage Frizzy Hair

If you’re already dealing with frizzy hair, don’t worry – there are several ways to manage it:

  • Use a smoothing serum or oil to tame frizz
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry
  • Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush
  • Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day
  • Use a silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz

Step-by-Step Guide to Managing Frizz

Here’s a step-by-step guide to managing frizz:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle, moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  2. Towel-dry your hair gently
  3. Apply a smoothing serum or oil to your hair, focusing on the ends
  4. Comb your hair with a wide-tooth comb
  5. Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day

Additional Frizz-Fighting Tips

Here are some additional tips to help fight frizz:

  • Wrap your hair in a microfiber towel or t-shirt to reduce friction and prevent frizz
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal clasps, which can cause breakage and frizz
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, which can dry out your hair
  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove buildup from hair products

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about frizzy hair:

Q: Is frizz more common in certain hair types?

A: Yes, frizz is more common in curly and wavy hair types, as the hair is naturally more prone to dryness and frizz.

Q: Can diet affect frizz?

A: Yes, a diet that is lacking in essential fatty acids, such as omega-3s, can contribute to dryness and frizz.

Q: Can stress cause frizz?

A: Yes, stress can affect the health of your hair and contribute to dryness and frizz.

Hair Care for Frizzy Hair

If you have frizzy hair, it’s important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and manageable. Here are some tips for hair care for frizzy hair:

  • Use a gentle, moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid overwashing your hair
  • Use lukewarm or cool water when washing your hair
  • Avoid using hot styling tools on wet or damp hair
  • Limit heat styling to once or twice a week
  • Use a humidifier in your home to add moisture to the air
  • Eat a diet rich in essential fatty acids, such as salmon and avocado

By following these tips, you can prevent and manage frizzy hair and keep your locks looking smooth and shiny.
