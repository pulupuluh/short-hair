How To Cut Your Own Hair Straight Across A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jul 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a simple and cost-effective way to cut your own hair, you might be considering cutting it straight across. This technique is ideal for those who want to maintain their hair length and get rid of split ends. However, cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you're doing it for the first time. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your own hair straight across in a relaxed English language, so you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your own home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Straight Across
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

How to Cut Your Own Hair Straight Across

To cut your own hair straight across, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror

It's also important to work in a well-lit room, so you can see what you're doing.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own hair straight across:

  1. Start by washing your hair and letting it dry completely. This will make it easier to see the ends of your hair.
  2. Comb your hair straight down and divide it into two equal sections, one in the front and one in the back.
  3. Take the front section of your hair and comb it straight down in front of your face. Hold it with your non-dominant hand, about an inch away from the ends.
  4. Use your dominant hand to cut the ends of your hair straight across. Start by cutting a small amount of hair, then gradually cut more until you reach the desired length.
  5. Repeat this process on the back section of your hair. Comb it straight down and cut the ends straight across, using your non-dominant hand to hold the hair and your dominant hand to cut.
  6. Check both sections of your hair in the mirror to make sure they are even. If necessary, trim any uneven bits until both sections are the same length.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting your own hair:

  • Start by cutting a small amount of hair, then gradually cut more until you reach the desired length. It's better to cut too little than too much, as you can always go back and trim more if needed.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • If you have curly hair, consider cutting it when it's dry, as it can appear shorter when it's wet.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so don't rush it.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair straight across:

Can I cut my own hair straight across if I have layers?

If you have layers in your hair, it's best to leave the cutting to a professional. Cutting layers requires a different technique and can be difficult to do on your own.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its health and prevent split ends.

What if I make a mistake?

If you accidentally cut too much hair, don't panic. You can always trim it more or visit a hairdresser to fix it.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

After cutting your own hair straight across, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as it's gentler on your hair than a brush.
  • Trim any split ends as soon as you notice them, to prevent them from spreading.

Now that you know how to cut your own hair straight across, you can save time and money by doing it yourself. Remember to take your time and follow the steps carefully, and you'll have a beautiful, even haircut in no time.
