How To Get Lice Out Of Long Thick Hair

Written by Oliver Jul 28, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you struggling with lice infestation in your long and thick hair? It can be a daunting and frustrating task to get rid of lice, especially when you have long and thick hair. But don’t worry, we’ve got you covered. In this article, we’ll share some tips and tricks on how to get lice out of long thick hair.

Description

Lice are tiny insects that feed on human blood and are usually found on the scalp, behind the ears, and on the neck. They can be easily spread through direct head-to-head contact or by sharing personal items such as combs, brushes, hats, and helmets. Lice infestation can cause itching, redness, and irritation on the scalp. If left untreated, it can lead to infection and other health issues.

How to Get Lice Out of Long Thick Hair

Here are some steps to follow to get rid of lice in long and thick hair:

Step 1: Use a Lice Comb

The first step in getting rid of lice is to use a lice comb. A lice comb is a special comb designed to remove lice and nits (lice eggs) from the hair. Start by dividing your hair into sections and combing each section with the lice comb. Be sure to comb from the roots to the ends of your hair. After each stroke, wipe the comb on a white paper towel to check for lice and nits.

Step 2: Apply Lice Treatment

After combing your hair, apply a lice treatment to kill any remaining lice and nits. There are many over-the-counter lice treatments available at drugstores. Follow the instructions on the package carefully. Be sure to cover your entire scalp and hair with the treatment, and leave it on for the recommended time.

Step 3: Wash Your Hair

After the recommended time, wash your hair with a lice shampoo. Be sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to remove all the lice treatment. Use a clean towel to dry your hair.

Step 4: Repeat the Process

Repeat the process of combing, applying lice treatment, and washing your hair after a few days to ensure that all the lice and nits are gone.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you get rid of lice:

  • Wash all the clothes, towels, and bedding that you have used in hot water to kill any lice and nits.
  • Vacuum your home and car to remove any lice and nits that may have fallen off your hair.
  • Avoid sharing personal items such as combs, brushes, hats, and helmets with others.
  • Tie your hair up in a bun or braid to reduce the risk of lice.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about lice:

Can lice jump from one person to another?

No, lice cannot jump or fly. They can only crawl from one person to another through direct head-to-head contact or by sharing personal items.

Can I get lice from my pets?

No, lice are species-specific, which means that human lice cannot survive on pets and pet lice cannot survive on humans.

Can lice infestation cause other health issues?

Lice infestation can cause itching, redness, and irritation on the scalp. If left untreated, it can lead to infection and other health issues.

Hair Care

After getting rid of lice, it’s important to take care of your hair to prevent future infestations. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a lice shampoo.
  • Use a lice comb regularly to check for lice and nits.
  • Avoid sharing personal items such as combs, brushes, hats, and helmets with others.
  • Tie your hair up in a bun or braid to reduce the risk of lice.

Getting rid of lice can be a challenging task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. Follow the steps and tips in this article to get rid of lice in long and thick hair, and take care of your hair to prevent future infestations.
