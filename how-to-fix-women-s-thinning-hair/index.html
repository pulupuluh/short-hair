How To Fix Women s Thinning Hair Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by Eveline Aug 15, 2023 · 6 min read

Thinning hair can be a frustrating and distressing experience for many women. Female pattern hair loss affects up to 50% of women over the age of 50, and can also occur in younger women. The good news is, there are ways to fix thinning hair and promote healthy hair growth. In this article, we’ll share our top tips, tricks and hair care advice for women with thinning hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Fix Women's Thinning Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Thinning Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Thin Hair

How to Fix Women's Thinning Hair

If you’re experiencing hair thinning, the first step is to speak to your doctor or a trichologist to rule out any underlying health issues. Once you’ve received a diagnosis and treatment plan, there are several things you can do to promote healthy hair growth.

1. Use Hair Growth Treatments

There are several hair growth treatments available that can help to promote healthy hair growth. These include:

  • Topical minoxidil (Rogaine) – this is a medication that is applied to the scalp to stimulate hair growth.
  • Low-level laser therapy – this involves using a laser device to stimulate hair growth.
  • Platelet-rich plasma (PRP) – this involves injecting the patient’s own blood into the scalp to stimulate hair growth.

It’s important to note that these treatments may take several months to show results, and not everyone will respond to them.

2. Change Your Hairstyle

If you have thinning hair, certain hairstyles can make the problem worse. Avoid hairstyles that pull on the hair, such as tight braids or ponytails. Instead, opt for looser styles that don’t put as much strain on the hair.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet is important for overall health, including the health of your hair. Make sure you’re getting enough protein, iron, and vitamins A and C. Foods that are good for hair health include:

  • Salmon
  • Eggs
  • Spinach
  • Sweet potatoes
  • Blueberries

4. Consider Hair Extensions

If you’re looking for a quick fix, hair extensions can help to give the appearance of thicker hair. Make sure to choose high-quality extensions that won’t damage your natural hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here are the steps you can take to fix women’s thinning hair:

  1. Speak to your doctor or a trichologist to rule out any underlying health issues.
  2. Use hair growth treatments.
  3. Avoid hairstyles that pull on the hair.
  4. Eat a healthy diet.
  5. Consider hair extensions.

Tips for Thinning Hair

Here are some additional tips for women with thinning hair:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage the hair.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for thinning hair.
  • Don’t brush your hair when it’s wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Avoid using hair accessories that can pull on the hair, such as elastic bands.

FAQs

Q: Can stress cause hair thinning?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair thinning. This is known as telogen effluvium, and it occurs when the hair enters a resting phase prematurely.

Q: Are there any vitamins that can help with hair growth?

A: Yes, vitamins A, C and E, as well as B vitamins and biotin, are important for hair health and growth.

Hair Care for Thin Hair

Here are some additional tips for taking care of thin hair:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a volumizing mousse or spray to give the appearance of thicker hair.
  • Avoid wearing hats or headbands for long periods of time, as they can cause friction and damage the hair.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can help to fix women’s thinning hair and promote healthy hair growth.
