In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to start freeform dreads with towel, along with some tips and FAQs to help you get started. Let's get started!

What are Freeform Dreads?

Freeform dreads are a natural way of creating dreadlocks without the use of any chemicals or tools. Unlike traditional dreadlocks, which are created using backcombing, interlocking, or twisting techniques, freeform dreads are formed by simply letting your hair do its thing. This means that your hair will lock up naturally over time, without any interference from you. Freeform dreads are unique and have a more organic look and feel compared to traditional dreadlocks.

How to Start Freeform Dreads with Towel

Starting freeform dreads with a towel is an easy and natural way to begin your dreadlock journey. The process involves using a towel to dry your hair after washing, which encourages the hair to lock up and form dreadlocks over time. Here's how you can start freeform dreads with towel:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash your hair as you normally would, using a natural shampoo and conditioner.
  2. After washing, gently squeeze out the excess water from your hair using your hands.
  3. Take a clean towel and wrap it around your head, making sure that all of your hair is covered.
  4. Twist the towel around your hair, squeezing it gently to remove any excess water.
  5. Leave the towel on your head for a few hours or overnight.
  6. Remove the towel and let your hair air dry for the rest of the day.
  7. Repeat this process every time you wash your hair, until your hair starts to form dreadlocks.

Starting freeform dreads with towel is a gradual process, and it may take several months or even years for your hair to fully lock up and form dreadlocks. However, with patience and perseverance, you can achieve the natural, organic look of freeform dreads.

Tips for Starting Freeform Dreads with Towel

Here are some tips to help you get started with freeform dreads:

  • Be patient - starting freeform dreads with towel is a gradual process, and it may take several months or years for your hair to fully lock up and form dreadlocks.
  • Avoid using any chemicals or tools on your hair, as this can damage your hair and prevent it from forming dreadlocks naturally.
  • Keep your hair clean and well-maintained, as this will encourage the hair to lock up and form dreadlocks more quickly.
  • Use a natural shampoo and conditioner, as this will help to keep your hair healthy and free from buildup.
  • Avoid using any products in your hair, as this can prevent it from locking up and forming dreadlocks naturally.
FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting freeform dreads with towel:

How long does it take to start freeform dreads with towel?

Starting freeform dreads with towel is a gradual process, and it may take several months or years for your hair to fully lock up and form dreadlocks.

Can I use a hairdryer instead of a towel to start freeform dreads?

No, using a hairdryer can damage your hair and prevent it from forming dreadlocks naturally. It's best to use a towel to encourage your hair to lock up and form dreadlocks over time.

Do I need to twist my hair to start freeform dreads?

No, twisting your hair can damage your hair and prevent it from forming dreadlocks naturally. Freeform dreads are formed by simply letting your hair do its thing.

Hair Care for Freeform Dreads

Once you've started your freeform dread journey, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for hair care for freeform dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a natural shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using any products in your hair, as this can prevent it from locking up and forming dreadlocks naturally.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent your hair from getting tangled while you sleep.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons, as this can damage your hair and prevent it from forming dreadlocks naturally.
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Congratulations, you've now learned how to start freeform dreads with towel! Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair, and you'll soon have beautiful, natural dreadlocks to show off.
