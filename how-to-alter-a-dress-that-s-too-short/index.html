How To Alter A Dress That s Too Short

Description

Have you ever found the perfect dress, but it's just too short? Don't worry, altering a dress that's too short is easier than you might think. You can make it longer by adding some extra fabric or lengthening the hemline. With the right tools and a little bit of patience, you can transform your too-short dress into the perfect length.

How to

Altering a dress that's too short can be done in a few different ways. The easiest method is to add some extra fabric to the bottom of the dress. This can be done by:

Step by Step

Step 1: Choose the right fabric. You want to find a fabric that matches the dress as closely as possible. If the dress is made of a lightweight fabric, choose a similar fabric that will flow nicely with the dress.

Step 2: Measure the length you want to add to the dress. You can do this by putting the dress on and measuring from the hemline to the desired length. Add an extra inch to account for the seam allowance.

Step 3: Cut the fabric to the desired length. Make sure to cut it in a straight line.

Step 4: Pin the fabric to the bottom of the dress, right sides together. Make sure to align the edges of the fabric with the hemline of the dress.

Step 5: Sew the fabric to the dress using a straight stitch. Make sure to backstitch at the beginning and end of the seam.

Step 6: Trim any excess fabric and press the seam allowance towards the dress.

Step 7: Hem the new length by folding the fabric under twice and sewing it in place.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when altering a dress that's too short:

  • Make sure to use a sharp pair of scissors when cutting the fabric.
  • When sewing the fabric to the dress, use a thread that matches the color of the dress.
  • Iron the dress before and after altering it to ensure it looks neat and polished.
  • If you're not confident in your sewing skills, consider taking the dress to a professional tailor.

FAQ

Q: Can I alter a dress that's too short without sewing?

A: While it's possible to use fabric glue or hem tape to alter a dress, sewing is the most secure and long-lasting method.

Q: How much fabric do I need to add to a dress?

A: This will depend on how much length you want to add to the dress. Measure from the hemline to the desired length and add an extra inch for the seam allowance.

Hair Care

Now that you've altered your dress, it's time to think about your hair. A simple updo or sleek ponytail can complement a longer dress nicely. If you're wearing a shorter dress, consider loose waves or a messy bun for a more casual look.

Remember, altering a dress that's too short is a simple process that can be done with a few basic sewing skills. With the right fabric and tools, you can transform your dress into the perfect length. Just follow these step-by-step instructions and you'll have a dress that fits like a glove.
