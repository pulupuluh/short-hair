How To Find The Best Haircut For Yourself A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Aug 09, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of getting the same haircut over and over again? Do you want to try something new but don't know where to start? Finding the best haircut for yourself can be a daunting task, but with the right approach, you can achieve the perfect look. In this article, we will guide you through the process of finding the best haircut for yourself. From understanding your face shape to choosing the right hairstylist, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  • Understanding Your Face Shape
  • Research and Inspiration
  • Choosing the Right Hairstylist
  • Communication is Key
  • Trying it Out
  • Maintenance and Hair Care
  • FAQs

Understanding Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the best haircut for yourself is to understand your face shape. Your face shape plays a crucial role in determining the most flattering haircut for you. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and long. Each face shape has its own unique characteristics and requires a different approach when choosing a haircut.

For example, if you have an oval face, you can pretty much rock any haircut. However, if you have a round face, you want to avoid haircuts that add width to your face. A layered haircut that falls below your chin is a great option for a round face. Similarly, if you have a square face, you want to soften your jawline with a haircut that has soft layers around your face.

Tip:

If you're not sure what your face shape is, take a selfie and draw the outline of your face on the photo. Compare it to the six basic face shapes to determine which one matches your face.

Research and Inspiration

Once you have a good understanding of your face shape, it's time to do some research and find inspiration for your new haircut. Browse through magazines, Pinterest, and Instagram to find hairstyles that catch your eye. Pay attention to the hair texture, length, and color. Create a mood board or a Pinterest board to keep all your ideas in one place.

It's important to remember that not all hairstyles will work for you. Your hair texture, face shape, and lifestyle must be taken into consideration when choosing a haircut. However, finding inspiration can help you communicate your vision to your hairstylist.

Choosing the Right Hairstylist

Choosing the right hairstylist is crucial when it comes to finding the best haircut for yourself. Look for a hairstylist who specializes in your hair type and has experience with your desired haircut. Read reviews and ask for recommendations from friends and family.

When you find a hairstylist you like, schedule a consultation before your haircut appointment. This will give you an opportunity to discuss your vision and ensure that you and your hairstylist are on the same page.

Communication is Key

Communication with your hairstylist is key when it comes to finding the best haircut for yourself. Be honest about your hair history, lifestyle, and daily routine. Let your hairstylist know what you like and don't like about your current hairstyle.

Remember to bring your research and inspiration with you to your appointment. This will help your hairstylist understand your vision and make suggestions based on your face shape and hair type.

Trying it Out

Before committing to a new haircut, it's a good idea to try it out. Ask your hairstylist to show you different styling techniques and products that will help you achieve the look you want. Take a few selfies and ask for feedback from friends and family.

If you're not happy with your new haircut, don't panic. Hair grows back, and your hairstylist can make adjustments to ensure that you're happy with the final result.

Maintenance and Hair Care

Maintaining your new haircut is just as important as finding the right one. Ask your hairstylist for tips on how to maintain your new haircut and what products to use. Invest in quality hair care products that are tailored to your hair type.

Remember to schedule regular haircuts to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Tip:

Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils. Instead, wash your hair every two to three days and use dry shampoo in between washes.

FAQs

Q: How often should I get a haircut?

A: It's recommended to get a haircut every six to eight weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: What should I do if I don't like my new haircut?

A: Don't panic! Hair grows back, and your hairstylist can make adjustments to ensure that you're happy with the final result.

Q: How can I maintain my new haircut?

A: Ask your hairstylist for tips on how to maintain your new haircut and what products to use. Invest in quality hair care products that are tailored to your hair type.

Q: How do I determine my face shape?

A: Take a selfie and draw the outline of your face on the photo. Compare it to the six basic face shapes to determine which one matches your face.

Finding the best haircut for yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience. By understanding your face shape, doing research, and communicating with your hairstylist, you can achieve the perfect look. Remember to maintain your new haircut with quality hair care products and regular haircuts. Happy hairstyling!
