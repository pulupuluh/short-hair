How To Get Rid Of Brassy Tones In Brown Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Mar 26, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Do you have brown hair that has turned brassy? Don't worry, you're not alone. Brassy tones are a common problem for those with brown hair, and they can be caused by a variety of factors such as exposure to the sun, chlorine, or even using the wrong hair products. In this post, we'll share with you some of the best ways to get rid of brassy tones in brown hair so that you can have the beautiful, shiny hair you deserve.

What Causes Brassy Tones in Brown Hair?

Before we get into how to get rid of brassy tones, let's first understand what causes them. Brassy tones are caused by the oxidation of hair color. This can happen when the hair is exposed to elements like sunlight, chlorine, or even hard water. Over time, this oxidation can cause brown hair to turn orange, yellow, or even red.

How to Get Rid of Brassy Tones in Brown Hair

1. Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a must-have for anyone with brassy brown hair. Purple is the opposite of yellow on the color wheel, so using a purple shampoo can help neutralize any yellow or orange tones in your hair. Be sure to leave the shampoo in your hair for at least 5-10 minutes before rinsing it out for best results.

2. Try a Toner

A toner is another great option for getting rid of brassy tones in brown hair. Toners are semi-permanent hair colors that can help neutralize any unwanted tones in your hair. They're easy to use and can be applied at home or by a professional stylist. Just be sure to choose a toner that's specifically designed for brown hair.

3. Use Apple Cider Vinegar

Apple cider vinegar is a natural way to get rid of brassy tones in brown hair. Mix equal parts apple cider vinegar and water and apply it to your hair after shampooing. Leave it in for a few minutes before rinsing it out. Apple cider vinegar can help remove any buildup on your hair and restore its natural shine.

4. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can be a major culprit when it comes to brassy tones in brown hair. To protect your hair from the sun, wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray. This will help prevent your hair from getting damaged and keep its color looking fresh.

5. Avoid Chlorine

Chlorine can also cause brassy tones in brown hair. If you swim in a pool, be sure to wear a swim cap or use a clarifying shampoo to remove any chlorine buildup. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to help protect your hair from chlorine damage.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Color

Now that you know how to get rid of brassy tones in brown hair, it's important to maintain your hair color. Here are some tips to help keep your hair looking its best:

1. Use a Color-Safe Shampoo

Using a color-safe shampoo can help prevent your hair color from fading. Look for shampoos that are formulated specifically for colored hair.

2. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause your hair color to fade faster. Try to wash your hair every other day or use a dry shampoo in between washes.

3. Use a Deep-Conditioning Treatment

A deep-conditioning treatment can help keep your hair looking healthy and shiny. Look for treatments that are specifically designed for colored hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use regular shampoo on colored hair?

While you can use regular shampoo on colored hair, it's best to use a shampoo that's specifically formulated for colored hair. This will help prevent your hair color from fading and keep it looking fresh.

How often should I use a toner?

How often you use a toner depends on how often you wash your hair and how quickly your hair color fades. Generally, you can use a toner every 4-6 weeks to help maintain your hair color.

Final Thoughts

Getting rid of brassy tones in brown hair doesn't have to be difficult. By using the tips and techniques outlined in this post, you can keep your hair looking beautiful and shiny. Remember to use a color-safe shampoo, protect your hair from the sun and chlorine, and use a deep-conditioning treatment to keep your hair looking its best. With a little bit of effort, you can have the gorgeous, healthy hair you deserve.
