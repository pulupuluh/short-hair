Can You Keep A Shih Tzu Hair Short

Written by Aprilia Jul 17, 2023 · 7 min read

Shih Tzus are adorable little dogs with a lot of hair. While their long and flowing locks make them look like a fluffy teddy bear, it can also be quite difficult to manage. Many owners wonder if they can keep their Shih Tzu hair short. The answer is yes, you can! In this article, we will discuss how you can do it and some tips for maintaining a shorter hairdo for your furry friend.

Description

Shih Tzus are a breed of small dogs that originated in China. They are known for their long hair and cute faces. Their hair grows quickly, and it can be quite challenging to manage. Many owners opt to keep their Shih Tzu's hair short to make it easier to maintain. Short hair also helps keep the dogs cool during hot weather.

How to Keep Shih Tzu Hair Short

Keeping your Shih Tzu's hair short is not difficult, but it does require some effort. Here's how you can do it:

Step 1: Get the Right Tools

To keep your Shih Tzu's hair short, you'll need the right tools. You'll need a quality set of clippers, scissors, and a comb. Make sure that the clippers and scissors are sharp, as this will make the process easier and less stressful for your dog.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Before you start cutting your Shih Tzu's hair, you need to decide on the length you want. A good length for a short hairdo is about 1 inch. You can go shorter if you like, but keep in mind that Shih Tzus have sensitive skin, and too short of a cut can lead to skin irritation and sunburn.

Step 3: Bathe Your Shih Tzu

Before you start cutting your Shih Tzu's hair, give them a good bath. This will help remove any tangles or mats in their hair, making it easier to cut. Use a dog shampoo and conditioner that is gentle on their skin.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Once your Shih Tzu is clean and dry, it's time to start cutting. Use the clippers to trim the hair on their body, leaving it about 1 inch long. Use the scissors to trim the hair on their legs, face, and ears. Be careful when cutting around their face and ears, as these areas are sensitive.

Step 5: Finish with a Comb

Once you've finished cutting your Shih Tzu's hair, use a comb to go over their coat and make sure that it's even. This will help you spot any areas that you may have missed.

Tips for Maintaining a Short Hairdo

Now that you've cut your Shih Tzu's hair short, there are a few things you can do to keep it looking great:

  • Brush their hair regularly to prevent tangles and mats.
  • Keep their hair trimmed every 6-8 weeks to maintain the short hairdo.
  • Use a dog sunscreen on their skin to prevent sunburn.
  • Use a dog coat conditioner to keep their hair soft and shiny.

FAQs

How often should I trim my Shih Tzu's hair?

You should trim your Shih Tzu's hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the short hairdo.

Can I use human clippers on my Shih Tzu?

No, you should use clippers specifically designed for dogs. Human clippers are not powerful enough to cut through a dog's thick hair and can cause discomfort to your furry friend.

Will cutting my Shih Tzu's hair short make them look different?

Yes, cutting your Shih Tzu's hair short will change their appearance. However, they will still be just as adorable as ever!

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your Shih Tzu's hair healthy and clean. Here are some tips:

  • Brush their hair regularly to prevent tangles and mats.
  • Bathe them every 4-6 weeks using a dog shampoo and conditioner.
  • Trim their hair regularly to prevent it from becoming too long and unmanageable.
  • Use a dog coat conditioner to keep their hair soft and shiny.

Keeping your Shih Tzu's hair short is a great way to make it easier to manage and keep them cool during hot weather. With the right tools and some patience, you can give your furry friend a short hairdo that will make them look just as adorable as ever.
