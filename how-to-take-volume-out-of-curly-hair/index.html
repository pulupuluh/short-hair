How To Take Volume Out Of Curly Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Frank May 06, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that sometimes it can be a challenge to manage the volume. While some people love the volume and bounce that curly hair provides, others may feel like their hair is out of control. If you're looking to take volume out of your curly hair, this guide is for you!

  • Description
  • How to Take Volume Out of Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Managing Curly Hair
  • FAQs

Description

Curly hair can be beautiful, but it can also be a challenge to manage. One of the biggest issues people with curly hair face is volume. Curly hair tends to be voluminous, which can make it difficult to style and manage. If you're looking to take volume out of your curly hair, there are several methods and techniques you can use to achieve a more manageable look.

How to Take Volume Out of Curly Hair

There are several methods you can use to take volume out of curly hair:

  • Blow-drying with a diffuser
  • Using a flat iron or straightening brush
  • Using a hair product like mousse or gel
  • Cutting or thinning hair

Blow-drying with a diffuser is a popular method for taking volume out of curly hair. A diffuser is an attachment that fits onto the end of a blow dryer and helps to distribute the heat and airflow evenly. To use a diffuser, start by washing and conditioning your hair. Apply a styling product like mousse or gel to your hair, and then attach the diffuser to your blow dryer. Use the diffuser to dry your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. This will help to reduce frizz and create a more defined curl pattern.

Using a flat iron or straightening brush can also help to take volume out of curly hair. Start by washing and conditioning your hair, and then apply a heat protectant spray. Use the flat iron or straightening brush to smooth out your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Be sure to use a low heat setting to prevent damage to your hair.

Using a hair product like mousse or gel can also help to take volume out of curly hair. Apply the product to wet hair and then use a diffuser to dry your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. This will help to create a more defined curl pattern and reduce frizz.

Cutting or thinning your hair can also help to take volume out of curly hair. A trained stylist can help you determine the best haircut for your hair type and face shape. They may recommend layering or thinning your hair to create a more manageable style.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for taking volume out of curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair
  2. Apply a styling product like mousse or gel
  3. Use a diffuser to dry your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends
  4. Blow-dry with a diffuser or use a flat iron to smooth out your hair
  5. Use a low heat setting to prevent damage to your hair
  6. Cut or thin your hair with the help of a trained stylist

Tips for Managing Curly Hair

Here are some tips for managing curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and frizz
  • Avoid brushing curly hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle wet hair
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to wet hair to help define curls
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about taking volume out of curly hair:

How often should I wash my curly hair?

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's best to wash it no more than twice a week to prevent dryness and frizz.

What kind of haircut is best for curly hair?

A layered or thinned-out haircut is best for curly hair, as it can help to reduce volume and create a more defined curl pattern.

Can I use a regular brush on my curly hair?

No, it's best to avoid using a regular brush on curly hair, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle wet hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to wet hair
  • Avoid brushing curly hair when it's dry
  • Cut or thin your hair with the help of a trained stylist

By following these tips and techniques, you can take volume out of your curly hair and achieve a more manageable, defined look. Remember to take care of your hair by using sulfate-free products and avoiding excessive heat styling.
