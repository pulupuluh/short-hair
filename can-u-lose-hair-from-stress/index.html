Can You Lose Hair From Stress

Written by Oliver Jul 30, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Stress is an inevitable part of life, and it can affect us in many ways. One of the most common questions people ask is, "can you lose hair from stress?" The answer is yes, stress can cause hair loss. In this blog post, we will discuss why stress can cause hair loss, how to prevent it, and how to take care of your hair to keep it healthy.

Table of Contents

  • Why Does Stress Cause Hair Loss?
  • Preventing Hair Loss from Stress
  • Taking Care of Your Hair
  • FAQ

Why Does Stress Cause Hair Loss?

When you're stressed, your body releases hormones that can affect your hair growth cycle. These hormones can cause your hair to enter the telogen phase, which is a resting phase. When this happens, your hair may fall out more easily than usual. In addition, stress can also cause inflammation in the scalp, which can damage hair follicles and lead to hair loss.

Stress can also lead to unhealthy habits that can contribute to hair loss. For example, when you're stressed, you may be more likely to pull or twist your hair, which can cause damage and breakage. You may also be more likely to neglect your hair care routine, which can lead to dry, brittle hair that is more prone to breakage.

Preventing Hair Loss from Stress

The best way to prevent hair loss from stress is to manage your stress levels. There are many ways to do this, including:

  • Exercise regularly
  • Meditate or practice yoga
  • Get enough sleep
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet
  • Take breaks throughout the day to relax and recharge

In addition, you can take steps to protect your hair from damage. For example, avoid pulling or twisting your hair, and be gentle when brushing or styling it. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, and avoid using heat styling tools too often.

Taking Care of Your Hair

Even if you're not experiencing hair loss from stress, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often (2-3 times per week is usually enough)
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot water, which can dry out your hair
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, and avoid using a brush on wet hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant spray when you do
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

FAQ

Can hair loss from stress be reversed?

In many cases, hair loss from stress can be reversed. Once you manage your stress levels and take steps to protect your hair, your hair growth cycle should return to normal. However, it may take several months for your hair to grow back fully.

What are some other causes of hair loss?

Other causes of hair loss include genetics, hormonal changes, certain medications, and medical conditions such as alopecia areata.

How can I tell if my hair loss is from stress?

If you're experiencing hair loss, it's best to see a dermatologist or hair specialist. They can help determine the cause of your hair loss and recommend a treatment plan.



In conclusion, stress can cause hair loss, but there are steps you can take to prevent it. By managing your stress levels and taking care of your hair, you can keep your locks healthy and full. Remember to be gentle with your hair, and give it the TLC it deserves!
