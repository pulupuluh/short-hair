How To Thin Out Curly Hair At Home A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia May 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it looks gorgeous, it can also be difficult to manage and style. One common problem faced by curly-haired individuals is the thickness of their hair. Thick hair can be difficult to style and can feel heavy and uncomfortable. However, thinning out curly hair can be tricky and expensive if done in a salon. In this blog post, we will guide you on how to thin out curly hair at home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Thin Out Curly Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Thinning Out Curly Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Thin Curly Hair

How to Thin Out Curly Hair at Home

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, it's essential to understand the various methods of thinning out curly hair. Some of the most popular methods include:

  • Razor cutting
  • Thinning shears
  • Texturizing scissors
  • Clipping
  • Fingers

Each method has its pros and cons; thus, it's essential to understand each method to choose the one that works best for you. In this guide, we will focus on using thinning shears.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the steps to follow when thinning out curly hair at home using thinning shears:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Wash and condition your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using heavy products that will weigh down your hair.

Step 2: Dry Your Hair

Blow-dry your hair using low heat. Ensure that your hair is completely dry before proceeding to the next step.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable parts. Start with the bottom section and work your way up. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 4: Choose Your Thinning Shears

Choose thinning shears that have at least 30% teeth. Ensure that the shears are sharp and in good condition.

Step 5: Thin Out Your Hair

Hold a section of hair between your fingers and slide the thinning shears down the length of your hair. Do this repeatedly on each section until you achieve your desired level of thinness. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once; otherwise, you may end up with uneven hair.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

After thinning out your hair, check for evenness by running your fingers through your hair. Ensure that there are no uneven spots.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired. You should notice an immediate difference in the thickness and weight of your hair.

Tips for Thinning Out Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out curly hair at home:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Ensure that your thinning shears are sharp to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Thinning out your hair too much can cause it to look stringy and damaged.
  • Be patient and take your time when thinning out your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I Thin Out My Curly Hair Without Thinning Shears?

Yes, you can use other methods such as razor cutting or texturizing scissors. However, thinning shears are the most effective and gentle method for thinning out curly hair.

How Often Should I Thin Out My Curly Hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, you can thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Is It Safe to Thin Out My Curly Hair at Home?

Yes, it's safe as long as you follow the proper steps and use the right tools. If you're unsure, it's always best to seek the help of a professional.

Hair Care for Thin Curly Hair

After thinning out your curly hair, it's essential to take care of it to avoid damage and breakage. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using heavy products that will weigh down your hair.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools frequently.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant.

Thinning out curly hair at home can be a bit intimidating. However, with the right tools and techniques, it's a simple process that can help you achieve the perfect curls. Remember to follow the steps carefully, take your time, and be patient. Happy hair thinning!
