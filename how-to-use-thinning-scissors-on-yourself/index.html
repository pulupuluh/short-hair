How To Use Thinning Scissors On Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 12, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Thinning scissors are a great tool to have in your hair care arsenal. They can help to thin out thick hair, add texture to fine hair, and blend layers for a seamless look. However, using thinning scissors on yourself can be intimidating if you don't know what you're doing. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of using thinning scissors on yourself, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Use Thinning Scissors on Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Using Thinning Scissors
  • FAQs

How to Use Thinning Scissors on Yourself

Before we get into the step-by-step guide, let's talk about what thinning scissors are and what they're used for. Thinning scissors, also known as texturizing scissors, have teeth on one or both blades that allow them to remove some hair while leaving the rest intact. This can help to reduce bulk, add texture, and blend layers.

Using thinning scissors on yourself can be a bit tricky, so it's important to have the right tools and technique. Here's what you'll need:

  • Thinning scissors
  • Hair clips
  • Comb
  • Spray bottle
  • Mirror

Now that you have the right tools, let's get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. Thinning scissors work best on dry hair, so make sure your hair is clean and completely dry before starting.

Step 2: Section your hair. Use hair clips to section your hair into manageable sections. Start with the bottom section and work your way up.

Step 3: Comb through the section. Use a comb to comb through the section of hair you're working on. This will help to ensure that the hair is even and tangle-free.

Step 4: Spray the section with water. Use a spray bottle to mist the section of hair with water. This will help to make the hair easier to cut and ensure that the cut is even.

Step 5: Hold the thinning scissors vertically. Hold the thinning scissors vertically, with the teeth facing up. This will allow you to remove hair without creating blunt lines.

Step 6: Make small snips. Use the thinning scissors to make small snips in the section of hair, starting at the ends and working your way up. Be sure to only cut a small amount of hair at a time to avoid taking off too much.

Step 7: Blend the hair. After you've made a few snips, use the comb to blend the hair and see how it looks. If you need to take off more hair, make another small snip and blend again.

Step 8: Repeat on the rest of your hair. Continue working through each section of hair, using the same technique. Be sure to blend the hair after each snip to ensure that the cut is even.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors

1. Use the right scissors. Thinning scissors are designed specifically for this purpose, so be sure to invest in a good pair.

2. Start with small snips. It's better to take off too little hair than too much, so start with small snips and work your way up.

3. Use a comb to blend. After each snip, use a comb to blend the hair and make sure the cut is even.

4. Work through your hair methodically. Start with the bottom section and work your way up, making sure to cut each section evenly.

FAQs

Q: Can I use thinning scissors on wet hair?

A: It's best to use thinning scissors on dry hair, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut evenly.

Q: How often should I use thinning scissors?

A: This depends on your hair type and style. If you have thick hair, you may want to use thinning scissors every few months to reduce bulk. If you have fine hair, you may only need to use thinning scissors once or twice a year to add texture.

Hair Care Tips

Using thinning scissors on yourself can be a great way to keep your hair looking healthy and stylish. However, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are a few tips:

1. Use a heat protectant. If you use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

2. Don't over-wash your hair. Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and breakage.

3. Use a deep conditioning treatment. A deep conditioning treatment can help to nourish and moisturize your hair, keeping it healthy and strong.

4. Get regular trims. Regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage, keeping your hair healthy and looking its best.

Thinning scissors can be a great tool to add to your hair care routine. By following this step-by-step guide and taking care of your hair, you can achieve a stylish and healthy look that you'll love!
