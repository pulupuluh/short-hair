How Do You Thin Out Your Hair With Scissors A Complete Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 07, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Thinning out your hair with scissors is a great way to reduce its volume and create a more manageable hairstyle. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of how to thin out your hair with scissors, step-by-step. We'll also provide you with some tips and tricks to make the process easier and answer some frequently asked questions about hair thinning. So, let's get started!

How to Thin Out Your Hair with Scissors

Before we get into the details, it's important to note that thinning out your hair with scissors is not the same as cutting it. When you thin out your hair, you are removing some of the bulk without changing the overall length of your hair. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it dry completely. This will make it easier to see where the hair is thickest and where you need to thin it out. Avoid using any styling products or oils as they may make your hair slick and difficult to work with.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable parts using hair clips or elastics. This will make it easier to work with small sections at a time and ensure that you don't thin out your hair unevenly.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Using a fine-toothed comb, comb your hair to remove any tangles and ensure that it is smooth and even. This will help you to see where the hair needs to be thinned out.

Step 4: Start Thinning Out Your Hair

Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Using a pair of thinning shears, cut into the hair at a slight angle, removing some of the bulk. Make sure to cut only a small amount of hair at a time, as you can always go back and thin out more if necessary.

Step 5: Work Through Your Hair

Continue working through your hair section by section, thinning out each one until you achieve the desired level of thickness. Remember to take breaks if your hands get tired or if you need to adjust your position to reach certain areas.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you have finished thinning out your hair, comb through it again to check for any uneven spots or areas that may need more thinning. If necessary, go back and make adjustments until you are happy with the result.

Tips for Thinning Out Your Hair with Scissors

Thinning out your hair with scissors can be a bit challenging, especially if you are doing it for the first time. Here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Invest in a good pair of thinning shears. This will make the process much easier and ensure that you get a clean cut.
  • Use a mirror to check your work as you go. This will help you to see any areas that may need more thinning.
  • Take your time and work through your hair slowly. Rushing can lead to uneven results and mistakes.
  • Don't thin out your hair too much, especially if you have thin or fine hair. This can lead to breakage and damage.

FAQs About Thinning Out Your Hair

Is it better to thin out your hair or to get a haircut?

It depends on what you are looking for. Thinning out your hair can help to reduce its volume and make it more manageable, while a haircut can change the overall length and shape of your hair. If you are happy with the length of your hair but want to reduce its thickness, thinning it out with scissors is a great option.

How often should I thin out my hair?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. As a general rule, you should aim to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks. However, if you have very thick hair, you may need to do it more frequently.

Can I thin out my hair at home?

Yes, you can thin out your hair at home with a pair of thinning shears. However, if you are not confident in your abilities, it's best to leave it to a professional stylist.

Hair Care After Thinning Out Your Hair

After thinning out your hair, it's important to take good care of it to prevent breakage and damage. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, as these can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to detangle it.

Thinning out your hair with scissors can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's easy to achieve a great result. Follow the steps in this guide and use our tips and tricks to get the best possible outcome for your hair.
