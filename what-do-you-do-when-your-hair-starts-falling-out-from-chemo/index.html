What To Do When Your Hair Starts Falling Out From Chemo A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank May 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Getting diagnosed with cancer is one of the most challenging experiences anyone can go through, and chemotherapy treatments often come with a host of side effects, one of the most common being hair loss. While hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy, it can still be a challenging experience for anyone going through it. But you don't have to go through it alone. Here's what to do when your hair starts falling out from chemo.

What Causes Hair Loss During Chemotherapy?

Chemotherapy is a powerful treatment that attacks rapidly dividing cancer cells. Unfortunately, it also attacks healthy cells that divide quickly, including hair follicles. This can lead to hair loss all over the body, including on the scalp, eyebrows, and pubic area.

How to Prepare for Hair Loss During Chemotherapy

While hair loss can be upsetting, there are steps you can take to prepare for it. One way is to cut your hair short before starting treatment. This can make the transition to hair loss less jarring. You can also consider investing in a wig, scarf, or hat to wear during treatment. Many cancer centers have resources to help you find a wig that matches your natural hair color and style.

What to Expect During Hair Loss

Hair loss can occur gradually or all at once, and the amount of hair loss can vary from person to person. Some people lose all their hair, while others only experience partial hair loss. Hair loss can also be accompanied by tenderness or itchiness on the scalp.

How to Care for Your Scalp During Hair Loss

It's important to take care of your scalp during hair loss. This means avoiding harsh shampoos and conditioners and using gentle, fragrance-free products instead. You can also consider using a scalp moisturizer to soothe any tenderness or itchiness.

How to Style Your Hair During Hair Loss

While you may not have as much hair as before, you can still experiment with different styles. Scarves and hats can be a great way to add flair to your look, and you can also experiment with different wig styles. Many wigs can be styled with heat tools like curling irons and straighteners, so you can change up your look whenever you want.

How to Take Care of Your Wig

If you decide to wear a wig, it's important to take care of it properly. This means washing it regularly with a mild shampoo and storing it on a wig stand to maintain its shape. You should also avoid exposing your wig to high heat or direct sunlight, which can damage the fibers.

How to Boost Your Confidence During Hair Loss

Losing your hair can be a blow to your self-confidence, but it's important to remember that hair doesn't define who you are. You can boost your confidence during hair loss by focusing on other aspects of your appearance, like your makeup or clothing. You can also seek support from friends, family, or a therapist who can help you process your emotions during this challenging time.

FAQs

Is hair loss from chemotherapy permanent?

Hair loss from chemotherapy is often temporary, and your hair will usually start to grow back a few months after treatment ends. However, it may grow back differently than before, so it's important to be patient and open to trying new styles.

Can I dye my hair during chemotherapy?

It's generally not recommended to dye your hair during chemotherapy, as the chemicals can irritate your scalp and damage your hair. However, you can consider using a temporary hair color or hair chalk to add some color without damaging your hair.

What should I do if my wig starts to feel uncomfortable?

If your wig starts to feel uncomfortable, you can try adjusting the straps or using a wig cap to create a more secure fit. If the discomfort persists, you may need to have your wig resized or try a different style or brand.

Conclusion

While hair loss can be a challenging experience, there are steps you can take to prepare for it and take care of yourself during the process. Whether you decide to wear a wig, experiment with scarves and hats, or embrace your natural look, remember that your hair doesn't define who you are. With the right support and self-care, you can get through this challenging time.
