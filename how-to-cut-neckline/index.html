How To Cut Neckline A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi May 30, 2023 · 6 min read

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you’re attempting to cut the neckline. However, with a few tips and tricks, you can achieve a professional-looking cut without leaving your home. In this article, we’ll be discussing the proper way to cut neckline and how to care for your hair post-cut.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Neckline
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Neckline
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Post-Cut

How to Cut Neckline

The first step in cutting your neckline is to gather the necessary tools. You’ll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It’s important to use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair and creating split ends.

Next, decide on the shape and length you want for your neckline. This can vary depending on your personal preference, hair length, and hair type. It’s important to have a clear idea of what you want before you start cutting.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a simple step-by-step guide to help you cut your neckline:

  1. Start by combing your hair down and checking the length of your neckline. Use the mirror to ensure that it is even on both sides.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the back of your head and comb it downwards.
  3. Using your scissors, cut the hair at an angle towards the center of your neckline. This will create a soft V-shape.
  4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 on the other side of your head, making sure to cut at the same angle.
  5. Check the length of your neckline in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments.
  6. If you want a more curved neckline, use your scissors to gently curve the edges of the V-shape.
  7. Repeat steps 2-6 until you have achieved the desired length and shape for your neckline.

Tips for Cutting Neckline

Here are a few tips to keep in mind while cutting your neckline:

  • Start by cutting a small amount of hair and gradually work your way up. It’s easier to cut more hair than to fix a mistake.
  • Make sure to cut hair at an angle towards the center of your neckline to create a V-shape.
  • Use a comb to ensure that hair is evenly distributed before cutting.
  • If you’re unsure about the shape or length of your neckline, seek the help of a professional stylist.

FAQs

Can I cut my neckline without a mirror?

No, it’s important to use a mirror to ensure that your neckline is even on both sides.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my neckline?

If you make a mistake, stop cutting immediately and seek the help of a professional stylist.

Hair Care Post-Cut

After cutting your neckline, it’s important to take care of your hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools for a few days to allow your hair to recover from the cut.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and prevent split ends.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape of your neckline.

Cutting your own neckline can be challenging, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking cut. Just remember to take your time and follow the steps carefully. Happy cutting!
