The Best Short Haircut For Long Square Face A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Aug 06, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have a long square face, you know how challenging it can be to find the right haircut that complements your features. The good news is that there are several short haircut options that can accentuate your best features while downplaying any imperfections. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best short haircut for long square face, including how to get it, care for it, and style it.

Description

A long square face is characterized by a broad forehead, strong jawline, and a square chin. The length of the face is more than the width, which can make it appear longer than it is. The goal of choosing the best short haircut for long square face is to add width to the face and balance out the features. A short haircut can also help to draw attention to the eyes, cheekbones, and lips.

How to

The first step in getting the best short haircut for long square face is to consult with a professional hairstylist. They can help you determine the right length, style, and cut that works best for your face shape and hair type. Here are some popular short haircut options for long square face:

1. Bob Cut

A bob cut is a classic short haircut that works well for long square face. The length of the hair can be chin-length or slightly longer, with layers around the face to add volume and texture. The bob cut can be styled straight, wavy, or curly, depending on your preference.

2. Pixie Cut

A pixie cut is a short and edgy haircut that can work well for long square face. The hair is cut close to the scalp, with layers around the crown to add height and volume. The pixie cut can be styled with bangs or without, depending on your preference.

3. Short Shag Cut

A short shag cut is a layered haircut that can add texture and movement to your hair. The layers are cut at different lengths, with the shortest layers around the crown and the longest layers at the bottom. The short shag cut can be styled with bangs or without, depending on your preference.

Step by step

Getting the best short haircut for long square face involves several steps:

Step 1: Consult with a professional hairstylist.

A professional hairstylist can help you determine the right length, style, and cut that works best for your face shape and hair type.

Step 2: Choose a short haircut option.

There are several short haircut options that work well for long square face, including the bob cut, pixie cut, and short shag cut.

Step 3: Get the haircut.

Once you’ve chosen a short haircut option, it’s time to get the haircut. Make sure to communicate with your hairstylist about the length, style, and cut you want.

Step 4: Style the haircut.

After you’ve gotten the haircut, it’s important to style it properly. Use a styling product, such as mousse or gel, to add volume and texture to your hair. You can also use a curling or straightening iron to create different looks.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting the best short haircut for long square face:

1. Choose a haircut that balances out your features.

Choose a short haircut option that adds width to your face and balances out your features.

2. Use styling products to add volume and texture.

Use styling products, such as mousse or gel, to add volume and texture to your hair.

3. Experiment with different styles.

Don’t be afraid to experiment with different styles, such as straight, wavy, or curly.

FAQ

What is the best short haircut for long square face?

The best short haircut options for long square face include the bob cut, pixie cut, and short shag cut.

How do I style my short haircut?

You can style your short haircut with a styling product, such as mousse or gel, and a curling or straightening iron.

Hair Care

Maintaining a short haircut requires regular trims to keep the hair looking neat and tidy. It’s also important to use a shampoo and conditioner that’s appropriate for your hair type. If you have dry hair, use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. If you have oily hair, use a clarifying shampoo and conditioner. Finally, use a heat protectant spray before using a curling or straightening iron to prevent damage to your hair.
