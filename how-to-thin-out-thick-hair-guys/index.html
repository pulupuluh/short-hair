How To Thin Out Thick Hair Guys A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter May 17, 2023 · 9 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it looks great, it can be difficult to manage, especially during hot weather. In this article, we will discuss how to thin out thick hair guys, so you can have a more manageable and stylish look.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Thin Out Thick Hair Guys
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care for Thinning Out Thick Hair

How to Thin Out Thick Hair Guys

Thinning out thick hair is a process that involves removing some of the hair's bulk to create a lighter and more manageable style. Here are some methods you can use to thin out your hair:

1. Razor Cutting

Razor cutting is a technique that involves using a razor blade to cut the hair. This technique is ideal for thick hair as it removes bulk without sacrificing length. Razor cutting can be done by a professional stylist or at home with a razor comb.

2. Texturizing Scissors

Texturizing scissors have teeth that are designed to remove bulk from the hair. The scissors are used to cut the hair in a way that creates texture and movement in the hair. This technique is ideal for thick hair as it creates a more natural and effortless look.

3. Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are scissors that have one serrated blade and one straight blade. The serrations on the blade are designed to remove some of the hair's volume without sacrificing length. This technique is ideal for thick hair that needs to be thinned out.

Step-by-Step Guide

Thinning out thick hair can be done at home or by a professional stylist. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thin out thick hair guys:

Step 1: Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type. This will make it easier to cut and thin out your hair.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will make it easier to work with and prevent any tangles from getting in the way of your cutting.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections, so you can work on one area at a time. Use hair clips to hold the sections in place.

Step 4: Choose Your Method

Choose the method you want to use to thin out your hair, whether it's razor cutting, texturizing scissors, or thinning shears.

Step 5: Cut Your Hair

Start cutting your hair, working on one section at a time. Use your chosen method to remove some of the hair's bulk. Be sure to cut evenly, so your hair looks natural and even.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

Check your progress as you go along. If you're not happy with the results, you can always go back and make adjustments.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your hair's thickness, style it as desired. You can use hair products to create texture and movement in your hair.

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair

Thinning out thick hair can be tricky. Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect look:

1. Don't Overdo It

It's important not to overdo it when thinning out your hair. Removing too much hair can create an uneven and choppy look.

2. Use the Right Tools

Make sure you use the right tools when thinning out your hair. Razor combs, texturizing scissors, and thinning shears are all great options.

3. Work in Small Sections

Work in small sections when thinning out your hair. This will make it easier to achieve an even and natural look.

4. Be Patient

Thinning out your hair takes time and patience. Don't rush the process, and take your time to achieve the perfect look.

FAQ

Q: Will thinning out my hair damage it?

A: Thinning out your hair should not damage it if done correctly. However, it's important to use the right tools and techniques to avoid any damage.

Q: Can I thin out my hair at home?

A: Yes, you can thin out your hair at home. However, it's important to use the right tools and techniques to avoid any damage.

Q: How often should I thin out my hair?

A: How often you should thin out your hair depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, it's recommended to thin out your hair every 3-6 months.

Hair Care for Thinning Out Thick Hair

Thinning out your hair can make it more manageable, but it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy. Here are some hair care tips for thinning out thick hair:

1. Use the Right Products

Use hair products that are designed for your hair type. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent any damage.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as blow dryers and flat irons, as they can damage your hair.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product that contains sunscreen.

4. Get Regular Trims

Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Thinning out thick hair can be a great way to achieve a more manageable and stylish look. Whether you choose to do it at home or by a professional stylist, be sure to use the right tools and techniques to avoid any damage. With these tips, you can achieve the perfect look and keep your hair healthy at the same time.
