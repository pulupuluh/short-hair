How To Cut Your Own Thick Hair Shoulder Length

Written by Aprilia Jun 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you have thick hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting your own thick hair into a shoulder-length style that's easy to maintain and looks great.

Description

Shoulder-length hair is a popular choice for many women, as it's versatile and easy to style. It's also a great length if you have thick hair, as it helps to lighten the weight of your hair and make it more manageable. By cutting your own hair, you can save money and time, and also have more control over the final result.

How to Cut Your Own Thick Hair Shoulder Length

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. You may also want to have a spray bottle filled with water, as wet hair is easier to cut and style.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry, so wash your hair and let it air dry or blow dry it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting, as wet hair can shrink up and make your final cut shorter than you intended.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four equal parts: two on top and two on the bottom. Use hair clips to secure each section. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly and ensure that you don't miss any sections.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Comb out any tangles and hold the hair between your fingers. Use your scissors to trim off the ends, making sure to cut straight across. Work your way around your head, cutting all of the bottom layer to the same length.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Once you've finished the bottom layer, release the top layer and comb it out. Hold a section between your fingers and cut it to the same length as the bottom layer. Work your way around your head, cutting all of the top layer to the same length.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

To blend the two layers together, take a small section of hair from both the top and bottom layers and hold them together. Use your scissors to cut diagonally into the hair, blending the two layers together. Work your way around your head, blending all of the sections together.

Step 7: Trim the Ends

Once you've blended the layers together, check the length of your hair and trim any uneven ends. Use your scissors to cut straight across, making sure to cut only a small amount at a time.

Tips

- Start with a longer length and cut more if needed
- Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends
- Cut small sections at a time to avoid cutting too much
- Take breaks if your arms get tired
- Ask a friend or family member for help if needed

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my hair?
A: It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my hair when it's wet?
A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink up and make your final cut shorter than you intended.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat on your hair. You can also use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.

Cutting your own thick hair shoulder length can be a great way to save money and have more control over your style. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut that looks professional and stylish.
