How To Cut Side Swept Bangs By Yourself With Scissors

Written by Oliver Jun 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle and want to try out side swept bangs, but don't want to go to a salon or hairdresser, then cutting them yourself is a great option. Not only is it cost-effective, but it also gives you the freedom to create the look you want. In this article, we'll show you how to cut side swept bangs by yourself with scissors, step-by-step.

Description

Side swept bangs are a popular hairstyle that can complement any face shape. They are versatile and can be customized to suit your style. They are also easy to maintain and can be styled in various ways. If you're looking to add some flair to your hair, side swept bangs are the perfect choice.

How to Cut Side Swept Bangs By Yourself With Scissors

Step 1: Choose the Right Type of Scissors

The first step is to choose the right type of scissors. You'll need a pair of sharp, straight-edged scissors. Avoid using kitchen or paper scissors as they can damage your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. You can use a comb to part your hair down the middle or on the side, depending on where you want your bangs to fall. Then, tie the rest of your hair back using a hair tie or clip.

Step 3: Decide on the Length of Your Bangs

Decide on the length of your bangs. Use your fingers to measure the length from your hairline to where you want your bangs to fall. Remember to leave some extra length as you can always trim later.

Step 4: Cut Your Bangs

Now it's time to cut your bangs. Hold your hair between your fingers to create a triangle shape. Using your scissors, cut the hair in a straight line, starting from the middle and working your way outwards. Don't cut too much hair at once, as it can be difficult to fix.

Step 5: Check the Length

Check the length of your bangs. If they're too long, trim a little bit more. Remember, it's always better to cut less hair than too much.

Step 6: Blend Your Bangs

Blend your bangs by using your scissors to create soft, feathered edges. This will give your bangs a natural look and prevent them from looking too blunt.

Step 7: Style Your Bangs

Style your bangs by using a round brush and a blow dryer. This will give your bangs volume and shape. You can also use a flat iron to straighten them or curl them with a curling iron.

Tips

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair
  • Start with longer bangs and trim gradually
  • Don't cut too much hair at once
  • Blend your bangs for a natural look
  • Style your bangs with a round brush and blow dryer for added volume

FAQ

Can I cut my bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your bangs even if you have curly hair. However, it's important to cut them when your hair is dry, as curly hair can appear shorter when it's wet. You should also avoid cutting your bangs too short, as they will appear even shorter when your hair dries.

Should I cut my bangs when they're wet or dry?

You should always cut your bangs when they're dry, as wet hair can appear longer. Cutting your bangs when they're dry will give you a more accurate length.

How often should I trim my bangs?

You should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their length and shape.

Hair Care

After cutting your bangs, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair from drying out. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. You can also use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.

With these simple steps and tips, you can easily cut side swept bangs by yourself with scissors. Remember to take your time and be patient, as cutting your own hair can be tricky. With practice, you'll be able to create the perfect side swept bangs that suit your style and face shape.
