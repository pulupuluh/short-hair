How To Trim Back Of Neck With Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 18, 2023 · 8 min read

Trimming the back of your neck can be a daunting task, especially if you have long hair. But fear not, with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily and safely. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the step-by-step process of trimming the back of your neck with long hair, and give you some tips to make the process as smooth as possible.

Description

Trimming the back of your neck is an essential part of maintaining your hairstyle. Neglecting the back of your neck can lead to a messy and uneven appearance, which is never a good look. But if you have long hair, trimming the back of your neck can be a bit tricky. You need to be careful not to cut too much hair, or to accidentally cut yourself with the clippers. That’s why we’ve put together this guide to help you get the job done right.

How to Trim Back of Neck with Long Hair: Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start trimming your hair, you’ll need to gather the right tools. You’ll need a pair of hair clippers, a mirror, and a comb. If you have a handheld mirror, that can be helpful as well.

Step 2: Set Up Your Work Area

Choose a well-lit area to work in, with a large mirror where you can see the back of your head. Sit in a chair or stand in front of the mirror, depending on what works best for you.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Using a comb, gently comb your hair down so that it’s sitting flat against the back of your neck. This will make it easier to trim your hair evenly.

Step 4: Start Trimming

Turn on your clippers and begin trimming the hair at the base of your neck. Start with a higher guard, such as a #4 or #5, and work your way down to a #2 or #3. Be sure to use the clippers in a downward motion, following the natural curve of your neck.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you’ve trimmed the hair at the base of your neck, use a handheld mirror to check your progress. Look for any uneven areas or spots that need more attention.

Step 6: Trim the Hair at the Sides

Next, trim the hair at the sides of your neck, using the same technique as before. Use a higher guard at first, then work your way down to a lower guard for a closer cut.

Step 7: Clean Up the Edges

Once you’ve finished trimming the back and sides of your neck, use a small pair of scissors to clean up the edges. Trim any stray hairs or uneven areas, being careful not to cut too much hair.

Step 8: Check Your Work

Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head again, to make sure that everything looks even and neat. If you notice any areas that need more attention, go back and trim them again.

Tips for Trimming the Back of Your Neck with Long Hair

Trimming the back of your neck can be tricky, but these tips can help make the process easier:

  • Use a higher guard at first, then work your way down to a lower guard for a closer cut
  • Start trimming at the base of your neck, then work your way up to the sides
  • Use a handheld mirror to check your progress as you go
  • Be careful not to cut too much hair, or to accidentally cut yourself with the clippers

FAQ

Q: How often should I trim the back of my neck?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows, but generally you should trim the back of your neck every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Can I trim the back of my neck myself?

A: Yes, you can trim the back of your neck yourself, but it can be tricky if you have long hair. You may want to ask a friend or family member for help, or go to a professional stylist.

Hair Care

Trimming the back of your neck is just one part of maintaining healthy and beautiful hair. Here are some tips to help care for your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Eat a healthy diet with plenty of vitamins and nutrients
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind

With these tips and techniques, you’ll be able to trim the back of your neck with long hair like a pro. Happy trimming!
