What Kind Of Beard Would Suit Me

Written by Christine Jul 12, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of being clean-shaven and want to switch things up with some facial hair? Or perhaps you're already rocking a beard but not sure if it's the right style for you? Whatever your situation, figuring out what kind of beard would suit you can be a daunting task. That's why we've created this guide to help you determine the best beard style for your face shape, hair type, and personal style. So, let's get started!

Description

Before we dive into the different beard styles, let's talk about what makes a good beard. A well-groomed beard should complement your face shape, enhance your features, and match your personal style. It should also be properly maintained, so investing in good quality beard products is essential. Now, let's move on to the different types of beards.

How to

When deciding on a beard style, it's important to consider your face shape. Different beard styles suit different face shapes, so it's important to choose a style that complements your features. Here's a breakdown of the best beard styles for each face shape:

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, congratulations! You can pretty much pull off any beard style. However, a short, well-groomed beard or a medium-length beard with a defined neckline will highlight your features and enhance your natural jawline.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, a beard with rounded edges will soften your features and balance out your face shape. A short beard with a defined neckline or a longer beard with a slight curve to the bottom will work well.

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, a beard with sharp angles will help elongate your face and give it a more defined shape. A goatee or a chinstrap beard with a defined neckline will work well.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart face shape, a beard that adds width to your jawline will balance out your features. A full beard or a chinstrap beard with a defined neckline will work well.

Diamond Face Shape

If you have a diamond face shape, a beard that adds width to your chin will balance out your features. A full beard or a chinstrap beard with a defined neckline will work well.

Step by Step

Now that you know which beard styles suit your face shape, it's time to choose a style that matches your personal style and hair type. Here are some steps to follow when growing and maintaining a beard:

  1. Decide on a beard style that suits your face shape and personal style.
  2. Let your beard grow out for a few weeks to see how it fills in.
  3. Invest in good quality beard products, such as beard oil, beard balm, and a beard comb.
  4. Trim your beard regularly to maintain its shape and remove any split ends.
  5. Cleanse your beard regularly with a beard shampoo to remove dirt and oil buildup.
  6. Moisturize your beard with beard oil or balm to keep it soft and hydrated.
  7. Style your beard daily with a beard comb or brush to keep it looking neat and tidy.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when growing and maintaining a beard:

  • Be patient. It can take several weeks for your beard to fill in and take shape.
  • Use a beard trimmer to trim your beard, and start with a longer guard size and work your way down gradually.
  • Don't forget to trim your mustache and neckline to keep your beard looking neat.
  • Beard oil or balm can help prevent itchiness, flakiness, and beard dandruff.
  • Cleanse your beard regularly to prevent acne and ingrown hairs.
  • If in doubt, consult a professional barber for advice on which beard style suits you best.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about growing and maintaining a beard:

How often should I trim my beard?

You should trim your beard every 1-2 weeks, depending on how fast it grows and how quickly it loses its shape. Use a beard trimmer with a guard size that matches the length you want to maintain.

What should I do if my beard is patchy?

If your beard is patchy, don't worry! Give it time to grow out, and try using a beard oil or balm to nourish the hair follicles. You can also try using a beard filler or a semi-permanent beard dye to fill in any gaps.

How do I prevent beard itchiness?

Beard itchiness is a common problem, especially in the early stages of growing a beard. To prevent itchiness, use a beard oil or balm to moisturize the skin and hair, cleanse your beard regularly with a beard shampoo, and avoid scratching or picking at your beard.

Hair Care

When growing and maintaining a beard, it's important to take care of your hair and skin. Here are some tips for keeping your beard looking and feeling its best:

  • Cleanse your beard regularly with a beard shampoo to remove dirt and oil buildup.
  • Moisturize your beard with beard oil or balm to keep it soft and hydrated.
  • Trim your beard regularly to maintain its shape and remove any split ends.
  • Avoid using hot water or harsh chemicals on your beard, as this can dry out the skin and hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water to keep your skin and hair hydrated.

So, there you have it! Now that you know what kind of beard would suit you, it's time to start growing and maintaining your new look. Remember, patience and good grooming habits are key to a healthy, well-groomed beard. Happy bearding!
