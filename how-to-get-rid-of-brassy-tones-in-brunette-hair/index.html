How To Get Rid Of Brassy Tones In Brunette Hair

Written by Frank May 14, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of the orange or red tones in your brunette hair? These brassy tones can be frustrating to deal with, but there are ways to get rid of them. In this article, we'll show you how to get rid of brassy tones in brunette hair using simple and effective techniques.

Description

Brassy tones are a common problem for those with brunette hair. These tones often occur when the hair is exposed to too much sun, chlorine, or even hair products that contain harsh chemicals. Brassy tones can make your hair look dull, dry, and lifeless. If you're looking for ways to get rid of brassy tones in your hair, you've come to the right place.

How to Get Rid of Brassy Tones in Brunette Hair

1. Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a great way to neutralize brassy tones in your hair. It works by depositing small amounts of purple pigment onto your hair, which cancels out the orange and red tones. To use purple shampoo, wet your hair and apply a small amount of the shampoo. Massage it into your hair and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

2. Try a Toner

A toner is another great option for getting rid of brassy tones in your hair. Toners work by adding cool tones to your hair, which neutralizes the warm tones. You can either get a toner at a salon or try an at-home toner. To use an at-home toner, follow the instructions on the packaging carefully.

3. Use Apple Cider Vinegar

Apple cider vinegar is a great natural remedy for getting rid of brassy tones in your hair. It works by removing buildup and restoring the pH balance of your hair. To use apple cider vinegar, mix equal parts of apple cider vinegar and water. Apply the mixture to your hair and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

Step by Step

Step 1: Wet Your Hair

Start by wetting your hair thoroughly. Use warm water to open up the hair cuticles, which will help the products penetrate deeper into your hair.

Step 2: Apply Purple Shampoo

Apply a small amount of purple shampoo to your hair. Massage it into your hair and focus on the areas where the brassy tones are most noticeable. Leave the shampoo on for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Rinse Your Hair

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water. Make sure to get all of the shampoo out of your hair.

Step 4: Apply Toner

If you're using a toner, apply it to your hair according to the instructions on the packaging. Make sure to apply it evenly and focus on the areas where the brassy tones are most noticeable. Leave the toner on for the recommended amount of time before rinsing it out.

Step 5: Rinse Your Hair

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water. Make sure to get all of the toner out of your hair.

Step 6: Apply Apple Cider Vinegar

Mix equal parts of apple cider vinegar and water. Apply the mixture to your hair and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

Tips

- Use a deep conditioning treatment after using purple shampoo or toner to prevent your hair from becoming dry and brittle.

- Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair gently.

- Avoid using hot tools on your hair, as they can cause damage and contribute to brassy tones.

FAQ

Q: Can I use purple shampoo every day?

A: No, you should only use purple shampoo once or twice a week. Overuse can cause your hair to become too ashy or gray.

Q: Can I use toner on color-treated hair?

A: Yes, toner can be used on color-treated hair. Just make sure to wait at least two weeks after coloring your hair before using a toner.

Q: Can I use apple cider vinegar on my scalp?

A: Yes, apple cider vinegar can be used on your scalp. Just make sure to dilute it with water before applying it to your hair.

Hair Care

To prevent brassy tones from returning, it's essential to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

- Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to maintain the health of your hair.

- Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.

- Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

- Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and techniques, you can get rid of brassy tones in your brunette hair and keep it looking healthy and vibrant.
