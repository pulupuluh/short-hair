What Haircut Would Suit A Round Face

Written by Robby Aug 03, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of trying different haircuts that don't seem to flatter your round face? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people struggle to find the perfect haircut that will complement their facial features. In this article, we will provide you with some tips and tricks on what haircut would suit a round face.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

A round face is characterized by soft curves and a circular shape. This face shape typically has a wider forehead, fuller cheeks, and a rounded chin. The key to finding the perfect haircut for a round face is to create some angles and lengthen the face shape. This can be achieved by choosing haircuts that add height and volume to the top of the head, while keeping the sides and back of the hair shorter.

How To

The first step in finding the perfect haircut for a round face is to determine your face shape. You can do this by pulling your hair back and looking in the mirror to see the shape of your face. Once you have determined that you have a round face, you can start to look for hairstyles that will complement your features.

Consider Your Hair Texture

When choosing a haircut for a round face, it's important to consider your hair texture. If you have fine hair, you may want to opt for a shorter haircut that adds volume and texture. If you have thick hair, you may want to consider a longer haircut with layers to add movement and shape.

Choose the Right Length

The length of your haircut can make a big difference in how it flatters your round face shape. Generally, haircuts that fall just below the chin or longer work well for round faces. This length helps to add some length to the face shape and create a more elongated appearance.

Add Layers

Adding layers to your haircut can help to create movement and texture, which can be especially flattering for round faces. Layers can also help to add volume to the top of the head, which can elongate the face shape.

Step by Step

If you're still unsure about what haircut would suit a round face, here are some step-by-step instructions:

  1. Determine your face shape
  2. Consider your hair texture
  3. Choose the right length
  4. Add layers
  5. Consult with a hairstylist for personalized recommendations

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing a haircut for a round face:

  • Avoid hairstyles that add width to the sides of the face, such as blunt bangs or chin-length bobs
  • Consider side-swept bangs, which can help to create angles and lengthen the face shape
  • Avoid hairstyles that are too voluminous on the sides of the head, as this can make the face appear wider
  • Consider hairstyles with a side part, which can help to create angles and add length to the face shape

FAQ

What is the best haircut for a round face?

The best haircut for a round face is one that adds angles and lengthens the face shape. This can be achieved by choosing haircuts that add height and volume to the top of the head, while keeping the sides and back of the hair shorter.

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a round face?

Hairstyles that add width to the sides of the face, such as blunt bangs or chin-length bobs, should be avoided if you have a round face. Additionally, hairstyles that are too voluminous on the sides of the head can make the face appear wider.

Hair Care

Once you've found the perfect haircut for your round face, it's important to maintain it with regular hair care. This includes washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, using a wide-tooth comb to detangle wet hair, and avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible. Additionally, regular trims can help to keep your haircut looking fresh and healthy.

In conclusion, finding the perfect haircut for a round face can be challenging, but with the right tips and tricks, you can find a style that flatters your features and enhances your natural beauty. Remember to consider your hair texture, choose the right length, and add layers to create movement and texture.
