How To Smooth Frizzy Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline Aug 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Do you have frizzy curly hair that you just can't seem to tame? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people struggle with frizz, especially those with curly hair. Fortunately, there are some simple tips and tricks you can use to smooth out your frizzy curls and achieve a sleek, polished look. In this article, we'll go over the best ways to smooth frizzy curly hair and answer some frequently asked questions about hair care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Smooth Frizzy Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • Frequently Asked Questions

How to Smooth Frizzy Curly Hair

The first step in smoothing out frizzy curls is to find the right products for your hair type. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair and contain ingredients like coconut oil, argan oil, and shea butter. These ingredients will help to moisturize and tame your curls, leaving them soft and smooth.

Another important factor in smoothing frizzy curly hair is to avoid heat styling as much as possible. Heat can damage your hair and make it even more prone to frizz. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to gently dry your curls.

Finally, it's important to use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair gently. Avoid using a brush, as this can cause breakage and make your hair even frizzier.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to smoothing out frizzy curly hair:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  2. Squeeze out excess water from your hair using a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair, focusing on the ends and mid-lengths.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair.
  5. If you need to dry your hair, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to gently dry your curls.
  6. Avoid touching your hair too much while it dries, as this can cause frizz.
  7. Once your hair is dry, apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to the ends to add shine and smoothness.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for smoothing frizzy curly hair:

  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your curls and make them look limp.
  • Try using a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz while you sleep.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it even more prone to frizz.
  • Consider getting a professional keratin treatment or Brazilian blowout to smooth out your curls and reduce frizz.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use a brush on my frizzy curly hair?

A: No, it's best to avoid using a brush on your frizzy curly hair. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my frizzy curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much oil your scalp produces. Generally, you should aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Q: What should I do if my frizzy curly hair is still frizzy after using products?

A: You may need to experiment with different products or consult a hairstylist for advice. It's also possible that your hair may be damaged or in need of a trim.

Hair Care for Frizzy Curly Hair

To keep your frizzy curly hair looking smooth and healthy, it's important to follow a good hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals like bleach or relaxers on your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

By following these tips and tricks, you can smooth out your frizzy curly hair and achieve a sleek, polished look. Remember to be patient and experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair type.
