What Does Excessive Hair Shedding Look Like

Written by Bardi Aug 20, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Excessive hair shedding can be a cause of concern for many people. If you notice more hair than usual falling out, it could be a sign of an underlying issue. In this article, we will discuss what excessive hair shedding looks like and what you can do to prevent it.

Description

Excessive hair shedding is when you notice more hair than usual falling out. It can be caused by a variety of factors such as stress, hormonal changes, medications, and genetics. You may notice more hair on your pillow, in your shower drain, or on your brush.

The average person loses between 50 to 100 hairs per day. However, if you notice more than that, it could be a sign of excessive hair shedding. It's important to note that shedding hair is a normal part of the hair growth cycle. Your hair goes through three phases: anagen (growth), catagen (transition), and telogen (resting). During the telogen phase, your hair sheds naturally to make way for new hair growth.

How to Identify Excessive Hair Shedding

Here are some signs that you may be experiencing excessive hair shedding:

  • You notice more hair than usual falling out
  • Your hair feels thinner or looks patchy
  • You see more hair on your pillow, in your shower drain, or on your brush
  • You experience hair breakage or split ends
  • You have a receding hairline or bald patches

Step by Step Guide

If you think you may be experiencing excessive hair shedding, here are some steps you can take:

  1. Visit your doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions
  2. Review your diet and ensure you are getting enough nutrients
  3. Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair
  4. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  5. Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment to nourish your hair
  6. Try to reduce stress levels through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques

Tips for Preventing Excessive Hair Shedding

Here are some tips to help prevent excessive hair shedding:

  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of vitamins and minerals
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush to detangle your hair
  • Avoid over-washing your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

FAQs

What causes excessive hair shedding?

Excessive hair shedding can be caused by a variety of factors such as stress, hormonal changes, medications, and genetics.

How much hair shedding is normal?

The average person loses between 50 to 100 hairs per day. However, shedding more than that could be a sign of excessive hair shedding.

Can excessive hair shedding be prevented?

Yes, you can take steps to prevent excessive hair shedding such as eating a balanced diet, avoiding tight hairstyles, and protecting your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential for preventing excessive hair shedding. Make sure you are using gentle products and avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools. Eating a balanced diet and reducing stress levels can also help keep your hair healthy and strong.

If you are experiencing excessive hair shedding, it's important to visit your doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions. With the right care and attention, you can help prevent excessive hair shedding and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
