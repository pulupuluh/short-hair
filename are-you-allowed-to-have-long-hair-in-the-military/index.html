Are You Allowed To Have Long Hair In The Military

Written by Christine Aug 23, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're considering enlisting in the military, you may be wondering about the grooming standards and regulations for hair length. In this article, we'll answer the question, "Are you allowed to have long hair in the military?" and provide you with helpful tips on hair care while serving.

Description

The military has strict grooming standards to ensure that all service members present a professional and uniform appearance. This includes regulations on hair length, style, and color. While many branches of the military require short haircuts for men and women, there are some exceptions for religious or cultural reasons.

How to

If you wish to have long hair in the military, you must first consult with a recruiter or military hair specialist to determine if it's allowed in your branch and job field. Some branches, such as the Air Force and Navy, may allow longer hair for women as long as it's neatly styled and secured in a bun or ponytail. However, men are typically required to keep their hair short and neat.

Step by Step

1. Research your branch's grooming standards and regulations on hair length.

2. Consult with a recruiter or military hair specialist to determine if you are allowed to have long hair.

3. If allowed, keep your hair clean and neatly styled in a bun or ponytail.

4. If not allowed, follow the grooming standards and regulations for hair length and style.

Tips

Regardless of your hair length, it's important to practice good hair care while serving in the military. This includes shampooing regularly, using conditioner to prevent breakage, and avoiding harsh chemicals and styling tools that can damage your hair.

You may also want to invest in hair accessories such as hair ties, headbands, and bobby pins to keep your hair securely in place during physical activity or high-wind conditions.

FAQ

Q: Can I have long hair in the Army?

A: The Army has strict grooming standards for hair length, and men are typically required to keep their hair short and neatly trimmed. Women may be allowed to have longer hair as long as it's neatly styled and secured in a bun or ponytail.

Q: Are there any exceptions for religious or cultural reasons?

A: Yes, there may be exceptions for religious or cultural reasons. You should consult with a recruiter or military hair specialist to determine if your branch and job field allow for these exceptions.

Q: How often should I shampoo my hair while serving in the military?

A: It's recommended to shampoo your hair at least once a week, or more frequently if you have oily hair or sweat heavily during physical activity. However, you should avoid using harsh shampoos or over-washing, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy and strong hair while serving in the military. Here are some tips:

- Shampoo and condition your hair regularly using gentle products that are free of harsh chemicals and sulfates.

- Avoid using hot tools such as curling irons and straighteners, as these can damage your hair and cause breakage.

- Use heat protectant products if you must use hot tools.

- Keep your hair trimmed regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

- Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

By following these tips and adhering to the grooming standards and regulations for your branch and job field, you can maintain healthy, strong, and stylish hair while serving in the military.
