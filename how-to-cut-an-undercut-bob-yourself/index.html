How To Cut An Undercut Bob Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 16, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're looking for a bold and edgy haircut, then an undercut bob might be just what you need. This trendy hairstyle features a short, buzzed section underneath the longer layers on top. The contrast in length creates a dramatic look that's both stylish and easy to maintain. If you're feeling brave and want to try cutting your own undercut bob at home, we've got you covered. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve this look.

Description of an Undercut Bob

An undercut bob is a hairstyle that features a shorter length on the bottom layer of hair, with longer layers on top. Typically, the shorter layer is buzzed or shaved, while the top layer can be styled in a variety of ways, such as curled or straightened. This style is popular among those who want a bold and edgy look that's easy to maintain.

How to Cut an Undercut Bob Yourself

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary tools for cutting hair. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a set of clippers, a comb, and hair ties or clips to section your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into two parts: the top section and the bottom section. Use hair ties or clips to secure the top section out of the way.

Step 3: Buzz the Undercut

Using your clippers, buzz the hair on the bottom section of your hair. Start at the nape of your neck and work your way up to the crown. Remember to use the guard that corresponds to your desired length.

Step 4: Blend the Undercut

Once you've buzzed the undercut, use your clippers to blend the hair where the short and long layers meet. Use a shorter guard to gradually blend the two sections together.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layers

Now it's time to cut the longer layers on top. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair to your desired length. Repeat this process on all sections of hair until you've cut all the top layers.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the top layers, use your scissors to blend the layers together. Use point cutting or texturizing techniques to soften any harsh lines.

Step 7: Check for Balance

When you're finished cutting, check to make sure your hair is balanced. If one side is longer than the other, make small adjustments until the length is even.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Now that you've cut your hair, it's time to style it. You can straighten it, curl it, or leave it natural. Play around with different styles until you find the one that works best for you.

Tips for Cutting an Undercut Bob Yourself

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting your own undercut bob:

  • Start with a longer guard on your clippers and work your way down to your desired length.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting hair can be tiring and time-consuming.
  • Don't be afraid to make mistakes. Hair grows back, and you can always visit a professional if you need help fixing any mistakes.

FAQ about Undercut Bob Haircut

How long does an undercut bob last?

Depending on how fast your hair grows, an undercut bob can last anywhere from a few weeks to a few months. You'll need to buzz the undercut again when the hair starts to grow out.

What face shape is best suited for an undercut bob?

An undercut bob works well on oval, heart-shaped, and square faces. If you have a round face, you might want to avoid this style, as it can make your face appear wider.

Can I color my hair after cutting an undercut bob?

Yes, you can color your hair after cutting an undercut bob. Just make sure to follow the instructions on the hair color product and use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to maintain the color.

Hair Care for Undercut Bob Haircut

To keep your undercut bob looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy.

Now that you know how to cut an undercut bob yourself, you can try this bold and edgy hairstyle at home. Remember to take your time, follow the steps carefully, and have fun with your new look!
