Best Hairstyle For Rectangular Face Female Flattering Looks For Your Face Shape

Written by Luffy Apr 13, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Are you tired of trying different hairstyles that don't flatter your rectangular face? Don't worry, we've got you covered. We've gathered the best hairstyle options that will enhance your features and complement your face shape. In this blog post, we'll provide you with the top hairstyles for rectangular face females, along with tips and tricks to maintain them.

Description

A rectangular face has a strong jawline, high forehead, and squared-off chin. The goal is to create more width and softness around the cheekbones and forehead areas. The right hairstyle can balance out the sharp features and enhance the beauty of the face shape.

How to Choose the Best Hairstyle for Rectangular Face Female?

When picking out a hairstyle, the key is to choose a look that adds width to the face. Soft and wavy styles will help create curves, while updos and slicked-back hairstyles will accentuate the sharpness of the face shape. Consider the following factors when choosing the best hairstyle for your rectangular face:

Face Shape

Identify your face shape to determine which styles will work best for you. Rectangular faces are longer than they are wide, with a square jaw and forehead. Avoid styles that elongate the face or accentuate the sharp features.

Hair Texture

Your hair texture can affect how certain hairstyles look on you. Fine hair looks best with layered cuts, while thick hair can handle more volume and weight. Consider your hair type when choosing your hairstyle.

Lifestyle

Your lifestyle can also impact the hairstyle you choose. If you're always on-the-go, opt for low-maintenance styles that require minimal styling. If you have more time to spend on your hair, consider intricate updos and braids.

Best Hairstyle Options for Rectangular Face Female

1. Long Layers

Long layers add volume and movement to the hair, creating the illusion of width. This style is perfect for those with long hair and fine to medium hair texture. Avoid blunt cuts or one-length hair, as they can elongate the face.

2. Soft Waves

Soft waves add volume and curves to the hair, making the face appear wider. Use a curling iron or hot rollers to create loose waves that frame the face. Avoid tight curls or ringlets, as they can accentuate the sharp features.

3. Side Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs soften the forehead and create a more rounded shape to the face. They also draw attention to the eyes and cheekbones. Avoid blunt or straight-across bangs, as they can make the face appear longer.

4. Bob Cut

A bob cut is a versatile style that can work for various hair textures and lengths. Choose a chin-length bob with layers to add volume and curves to the hair. Avoid a blunt bob or a style that ends at the jawline, as they can elongate the face.

5. Half Updo

A half updo is a great way to add volume and texture to the hair while keeping it out of your face. Pull the top half of your hair back and secure it with a clip or hair tie. Leave the rest of your hair down in soft waves or curls. Avoid tight and slicked-back styles, as they can accentuate the sharp features.

Tips to Maintain Your Hairstyle

Now that you've chosen the perfect hairstyle, it's essential to maintain it properly. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking its best:

1. Regular Trims

Visit your hairstylist every four to six weeks for regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

2. Use Heat Protectant

Before using any hot tools, apply a heat protectant to your hair to prevent damage and breakage.

3. Use the Right Products

Use products that are suitable for your hair type and texture. Avoid products that weigh down your hair or make it greasy.

4. Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and environmental pollutants by wearing a hat or using a scarf.

FAQs

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a rectangular face?

Avoid hairstyles that elongate the face, such as sleek and straight styles or center parts. Blunt cuts or one-length hair can also accentuate the sharp features of the face shape.

Can I wear my hair up if I have a rectangular face?

Yes, you can wear your hair up if you have a rectangular face. Opt for soft and loose updos or half updos that add volume and curves to the hair.

What hair color looks best on rectangular faces?

Warm hair colors, such as golden blondes, honey browns, and copper reds, complement the features of rectangular faces. Avoid cool tones, such as ash blondes or cool browns, as they can make the face appear sharper.

Hair Care for Rectangular Face Female

Proper hair care is essential to maintain healthy and beautiful hair. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking its best:

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair every two to three days to keep it clean and healthy. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using hot tools every day, as they can damage your hair. Let your hair air dry whenever possible and use a heat protectant when using hot tools.

3. Protect Your Hair from Damage

Protect your hair from damage by wearing a hat or scarf when exposed to the sun or wind. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and hydrated.

4. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can promote healthy hair growth. Eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins to nourish your hair from the inside out.

In conclusion, rectangular faces can be challenging to style, but with the right haircut and styling techniques, you can enhance your features and create a flattering look. Consider the tips and hairstyle options mentioned in this blog post and experiment with different looks to find the perfect style for you.
