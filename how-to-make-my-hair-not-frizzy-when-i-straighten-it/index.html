How To Make Your Hair Not Frizzy When You Straighten It

Written by Oliver Sep 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, straightening it can be a great way to switch up your look. However, the process of straightening can lead to frizz, which can be frustrating. In this blog post, we will share tips and tricks on how to make your hair not frizzy when you straighten it.

Description

Frizz is caused by the hair cuticle being lifted and allowing moisture to enter the hair shaft. When you straighten your hair, the high heat can cause the cuticle to lift, leading to frizz. However, there are steps you can take to prevent frizz and achieve smooth, straight hair.

How to

Here are some tips on how to make your hair not frizzy when you straighten it:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Before straightening your hair, it's essential to start with clean hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help prevent your hair from drying out.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant to your hair to help prevent damage from the high heat of the straightener. Choose a product that will smooth your hair and help seal the cuticle.

Step 3: Dry Your Hair

Blow-dry your hair before straightening it. Use a round brush to help smooth your hair and prevent tangles.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections before straightening it. This will help ensure that every strand of hair is straightened and prevent you from going over the same section of hair multiple times, which can cause damage and frizz.

Step 5: Use the Right Temperature

Use a straightener with adjustable temperature settings. The temperature you use will depend on your hair type and thickness. Fine or damaged hair should use a lower temperature, while thicker hair can handle a higher temperature.

Step 6: Straighten Your Hair

Start straightening your hair at the roots and work your way down to the ends. Use a comb to help guide the straightener through your hair and prevent tangles.

Step 7: Finish with a Serum or Oil

Once you've finished straightening your hair, apply a serum or oil to help smooth your hair and prevent frizz.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent frizz when straightening your hair:

  • Use a ceramic or tourmaline straightener. These materials help distribute heat evenly and prevent hot spots that can cause damage and frizz.
  • Don't straighten wet hair. This can cause damage and lead to frizz.
  • Avoid touching your hair after straightening it. This can cause the cuticle to lift and lead to frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk pillowcase to help prevent frizz and protect your hair while you sleep.

FAQ

Q: Can I straighten my hair every day?

A: It's not recommended to straighten your hair every day, as this can cause damage and lead to frizz. Try to limit straightening to a few times a week and use a heat protectant each time.

Q: How do I fix frizzy hair after straightening it?

A: Apply a serum or oil to your hair to help smooth it. You can also try using a hair mask or deep conditioner to help hydrate your hair and prevent frizz.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential to prevent frizz and damage. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and apply a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week. Avoid using hot tools on wet hair, and use a heat protectant each time you straighten your hair.

In conclusion, following these steps will help you achieve smooth, straight hair without frizz. Remember to take care of your hair to prevent damage and keep it healthy.
