Why Is My Hair Only Curly When It s Short

Written by Luffy May 16, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Curly hair is unique and beautiful, but it can also be frustrating to manage. For some people, their hair is only curly when it's short. This can be confusing and make it difficult to know how to care for your hair. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why your hair might be curly when it's short, and provide you with some tips on how to care for your curls.

Reasons Why Your Hair is Curly When it's Short

There are a few reasons why your hair might be curly when it's short:

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can affect the texture of your hair. During puberty, many people experience a change in the texture of their hair. This is because the body produces more hormones that affect the hair follicles. As a result, your hair might become curlier or straighter.

Genetics

Genetics play a big role in determining the texture of your hair. If curly hair runs in your family, it's likely that your hair will be curly when it's short. However, genetics are not the only factor that determines the texture of your hair.

Cuticle Damage

If your hair is damaged, the cuticles can become rough and lift away from the hair shaft. This can make your hair appear frizzy and curly. If you have curly hair, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage.

How to Care for Short Curly Hair

If your hair is only curly when it's short, it's important to take care of your curls to keep them looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for caring for short curly hair:

Use a Gentle Shampoo

Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so it's important to use a gentle shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for a shampoo that is sulfate-free and contains moisturizing ingredients like coconut oil or shea butter.

Condition Regularly

Conditioning is essential for keeping curly hair hydrated and healthy. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help restore moisture to your curls.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage curly hair and make it appear frizzy. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Avoid Brushing Your Hair When it's Dry

Brushing curly hair when it's dry can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.

Step-by-Step Guide for Managing Short Curly Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide for managing your short curly hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help keep it hydrated and healthy.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots.

Step 4: Apply a Styling Product

Apply a styling product to your hair to help define your curls. Use a curl cream, gel, or mousse.

Step 5: Diffuse Your Hair

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair. This will help reduce frizz and define your curls.

Tips for Maintaining Short Curly Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining your short curly hair:

Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so it's important to avoid over-washing your hair. Wash your hair no more than two or three times a week.

Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help reduce frizz and prevent breakage.

Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help restore moisture to your curls and keep them looking healthy.

FAQs About Short Curly Hair

Q: Can I straighten my short curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your short curly hair, but it's important to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low to prevent damage.

Q: Why does my hair get frizzy?

A: Frizz is caused by the cuticles of your hair becoming rough and lifting away from the hair shaft. This can be caused by damage, humidity, or dryness.

Q: How often should I deep condition my hair?

A: You should deep condition your hair once a week to help restore moisture to your curls.

Overall, if your hair is only curly when it's short, it's important to take care of your curls to keep them looking healthy and beautiful. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, deep condition regularly, and avoid heat styling. With the right care, you can embrace and love your short curly hair.
