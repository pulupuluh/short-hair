Which Hair Color Will Suit My Face?

Are you tired of your current hair color and looking for a change? Choosing the right hair color can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. In this article, we will explore how to determine which hair color will suit your face.

Table of Contents

  1. Determining Your Skin Tone
  2. Considering Your Eye Color
  3. Choosing the Right Shade
  4. Hair Care Tips
  5. FAQs

Determining Your Skin Tone

The first step in choosing the right hair color is to determine your skin tone. There are two main types of skin tone: warm and cool. Warm skin tones usually have yellow, peach, or golden undertones, while cool skin tones have pink or blue undertones.

To determine your skin tone, look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they appear green, you likely have warm undertones. If they appear blue, you likely have cool undertones. Another way to determine your skin tone is to hold up a piece of white paper to your face. If your skin appears yellowish, you likely have warm undertones. If your skin appears pinkish, you likely have cool undertones.

Considering Your Eye Color

Another factor to consider when choosing a hair color is your eye color. Different hair colors can bring out different aspects of your eyes, making them appear brighter and more vibrant.

If you have blue or green eyes, warm hair colors such as golds, coppers, and auburns can help bring out the warmth in your eyes. If you have brown eyes, cool hair colors such as ash browns and cool blondes can help make your eyes appear brighter.

Choosing the Right Shade

Once you have determined your skin tone and eye color, it's time to choose the right hair color. One way to do this is to stick within two shades of your natural hair color. This will help ensure that the hair color looks natural and complements your skin tone.

If you want to go for a more dramatic change, consider going for a hair color that is the opposite of your skin tone. For example, if you have warm undertones, consider cool hair colors such as ash blondes or cool browns. If you have cool undertones, consider warm hair colors such as honey blondes or golden browns.

Hair Care Tips

After you have chosen the right hair color, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure that the color lasts and looks its best. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for color-treated hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip the color.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can also strip the color.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to chlorine and salt water, as these can cause the color to fade.

FAQs

Can I dye my hair a completely different color than my natural hair color?

Yes, you can dye your hair a completely different color than your natural hair color. However, it's important to choose a hair color that complements your skin tone and eye color.

How often should I touch up my hair color?

This depends on the type of hair color you have and how fast your hair grows. Generally, permanent hair color should be touched up every 4-6 weeks, while semi-permanent hair color should be touched up every 2-4 weeks.

Can I dye my hair at home?

Yes, you can dye your hair at home. However, it's important to follow the instructions carefully and choose a hair color that complements your skin tone and eye color.


Choosing the right hair color can be a fun and exciting way to change up your look. By determining your skin tone and eye color, considering different shades, and taking care of your hair, you can find the perfect hair color to suit your face.
