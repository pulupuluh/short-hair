Does The Bible Say Anything About Redheads

Written by William Mar 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a redhead, you may have wondered if the Bible has anything to say about your hair color. After all, the Bible covers a wide range of topics, from morality to history to prophecy, so it's possible that it might have something to say about red hair. In this article, we'll explore whether or not the Bible mentions redheads and what it has to say about them.

Does the Bible Mention Redheads Specifically?

The short answer is yes, the Bible does mention redheads specifically. The Hebrew word used to describe redheads is "adam," which means "ruddy" or "reddish-brown." This word is used to describe Esau, one of the twin sons of Isaac and Rebecca.

According to Genesis 25:25, "The first one came out red, and his whole body was like a hairy garment; so they named him Esau." This verse tells us that Esau was born with red hair and a lot of body hair.

What Does the Bible Say About Redheads?

While the Bible doesn't say a lot specifically about redheads, it does use the word "adam" in a few other contexts. For example, in Song of Solomon 5:10, the bride describes her beloved as "white and ruddy," using the same word to describe his complexion that is used to describe Esau's hair color.

Additionally, in 1 Samuel 16:12, when Samuel is looking for a new king to replace Saul, he is told to anoint one of Jesse's sons. When he sees Jesse's oldest son, Eliab, he thinks that he must be the one because he is tall and handsome. But God tells Samuel, "Do not consider his appearance or his height, for I have rejected him. The Lord does not look at the things people look at. People look at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart." This passage suggests that God is not concerned with physical appearance, including hair color.

Hair Care Tips for Redheads

While the Bible may not have a lot to say specifically about redheads, there are some hair care tips that can help keep your red hair looking healthy and vibrant.

Use Products Specifically for Red Hair

Using products that are specifically formulated for red hair can help prevent fading and keep your color looking bright. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are designed for red hair, as well as styling products that can help enhance your natural color.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Red hair is particularly susceptible to sun damage, so it's important to protect your hair when you're spending time outdoors. Wear a hat or use a scarf to cover your hair, and use a leave-in conditioner with SPF to help protect your strands from UV damage.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can be damaging to any hair type, but they can be especially damaging to red hair. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

FAQ

Is it true that redheads have a higher pain tolerance?

There is some evidence to suggest that redheads may have a higher pain tolerance than people with other hair colors. A study published in the journal Anesthesiology found that redheads require more anesthesia during surgery than people with other hair colors. However, more research is needed to confirm this finding.

Do redheads really have more fun?

Whether or not redheads have more fun is subjective and varies from person to person. However, many redheads embrace their unique hair color and love the attention and compliments they receive because of it.

Can redheads dye their hair?

Yes, redheads can dye their hair just like anyone else. However, it can be difficult to achieve a natural-looking color, so it's important to work with a professional stylist who has experience with red hair.

Conclusion

While the Bible doesn't say a lot specifically about redheads, it does mention the word "adam" to describe Esau's hair color. Additionally, the Bible teaches us that God does not judge people by their outward appearance, including hair color. If you're a redhead, taking care of your hair with products designed specifically for red hair and protecting it from the sun can help keep it looking healthy and vibrant.
