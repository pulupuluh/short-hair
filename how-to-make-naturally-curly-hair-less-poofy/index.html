How To Make Naturally Curly Hair Less Poofy

Written by Oliver May 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you may have experienced the frustration of dealing with poofy hair. While some people embrace their curls and the volume that comes with them, others want to tame the poofiness and create a more polished look. Fortunately, there are many ways to make naturally curly hair less poofy and more manageable. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to achieve this.

Description

Naturally curly hair is prone to becoming poofy due to its texture and tendency to absorb moisture from the air. This can make it challenging to style and maintain. However, with the right techniques and products, you can reduce the poofiness and create a more defined, sleek look.

How to

Step 1: Use the Right Products

One of the most important factors in reducing poofiness is using the right products. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl-defining creams, gels, or mousses. These can help to enhance your curls while reducing frizz and poofiness. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol, as these can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.

Step 2: Apply Products to Wet Hair

To get the most out of your styling products, apply them to wet hair. This will help to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair and prevent it from weighing down your curls. Use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product from roots to ends.

Step 3: Use a Diffuser

A diffuser is a must-have tool if you have naturally curly hair. It attaches to your hair dryer and helps to distribute heat evenly throughout your curls, which can reduce frizz and poofiness. Use it on a low or medium setting and gently scrunch your hair as you dry it.

Step 4: Don't Over Brush

Brushing your curls too much can cause them to become poofy and lose their shape. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet. Once your hair is dry, avoid brushing it as much as possible. If you need to touch up your curls, use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to reshape them.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for making naturally curly hair less poofy:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day, as this can cause frizz and poofiness.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent your hair from becoming tangled.
  • Consider getting a haircut that is specifically designed for curly hair, as this can help to reduce poofiness and enhance your natural curls.

FAQ

Q: Can I use a hair straightener to reduce poofiness in my curly hair?

A: While a hair straightener can be effective in smoothing out your curls, it can also damage your hair and cause it to become more prone to frizz and breakage. Instead, try using a diffuser and curl-enhancing products to reduce poofiness while maintaining your natural curls.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with curly hair only need to wash their hair once a week, while others may need to wash it more frequently. Experiment to find a washing schedule that works best for you.

Hair Care

Taking good care of your curly hair is essential for reducing poofiness and achieving a polished, defined look. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy, beautiful curls:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause it to become more prone to frizz.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental stressors by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

By following these tips and techniques, you can make naturally curly hair less poofy and achieve a more defined, polished look. Experiment with different products and styling techniques to find what works best for your hair type and personal style. With a little patience and practice, you can create beautiful, healthy curls that you'll love.
