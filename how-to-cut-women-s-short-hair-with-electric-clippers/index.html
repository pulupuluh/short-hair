How To Cut Women s Short Hair With Electric Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 08, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you are looking for a low-maintenance haircut that is both stylish and practical, a short haircut might be the perfect choice for you. And if you want to do it yourself, electric clippers can be a great tool to achieve a professional look at home. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting women's short hair with electric clippers, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Women's Short Hair with Electric Clippers
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Women's Short Hair with Electric Clippers

Before you start cutting, you will need a few tools: a pair of electric clippers, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. You might also want to use a cape to protect your clothes from hair clippings. Once you have all of your tools ready, you can start cutting!

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that the clippers glide smoothly through your hair. You can use a towel to dry your hair or a hair dryer if you prefer.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to section your hair into manageable parts. Start by parting your hair in the middle and then sectioning it from ear to ear. Use hair clips to secure the sections of hair that you are not working on.

Step 3: Choose the Right Clipper Guard

Electric clippers come with different guard sizes, which determine the length of the hair that will be cut. Choose the right guard size for the length you want to achieve. You can always start with a longer guard and then switch to a shorter one if you want to go shorter.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start by cutting the hair on the sides and back of your head. Hold the clippers at a 45-degree angle and move them upwards against the direction of hair growth. Use short strokes and go over the same area several times to ensure that all of the hair is cut evenly. Remember to keep the hair clipper guard flat against the head to prevent any uneven cuts.

Step 5: Cut the Top of Your Head

Once you have finished the sides and back, move on to the top of your head. Use the same technique as before, holding the clippers at a 45-degree angle and moving them against the direction of hair growth. Take your time and ensure that the hair is cut evenly.

Step 6: Blend the Hair

Use the clippers without any guard to blend the hair at the back of your head and around your ears. This will create a seamless transition between the shorter and longer parts of your hair.

Step 7: Check the Hair Length

Once you have finished cutting, check the length of your hair to make sure it is even. Use a mirror to check the back of your head as well. If you notice any uneven spots, go over them again with the clippers.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a summary of the steps to cut women's short hair with electric clippers:

  1. Wash and dry your hair
  2. Section your hair
  3. Choose the right clipper guard
  4. Start cutting the sides and back
  5. Cut the top of your head
  6. Blend the hair
  7. Check the hair length

Tips for Cutting Women's Short Hair with Electric Clippers

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve a professional-looking haircut:

  • Start with a longer clipper guard and work your way down to a shorter one if you want to go shorter
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and make sure it is cut evenly
  • Take your time and don't rush the process
  • Use the mirror to check the back of your head as you go along

FAQ

Can I cut my hair with electric clippers if it is curly?

Yes, you can use electric clippers on curly hair. However, you might need to use a longer clipper guard to achieve the same length as someone with straight hair.

Can I use electric clippers to cut my hair if it is very thick?

Yes, electric clippers are a great tool for cutting thick hair. Just make sure to use a longer clipper guard and take your time to ensure that the hair is cut evenly.

Do I need to oil my electric clippers?

Yes, it is important to oil your electric clippers regularly to keep them running smoothly and prevent them from rusting. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for oiling your clippers.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Once you have cut your hair, it is important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and stylish. Here are some hair care tips for short hair:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent it from becoming dry and brittle
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy

By following these tips and our step-by-step guide, you can achieve a stylish and low-maintenance haircut with electric clippers. Happy cutting!
