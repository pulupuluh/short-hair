What Skin Tone Suits Auburn Hair

Written by Daniel Jun 08, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Auburn hair is a beautiful shade that has a mix of red, brown, and sometimes even orange hues. It is a perfect choice for those who want to add warmth to their hair without going full-on red. But, when it comes to picking the right skin tone to complement auburn hair, things can get a bit tricky. In this post, we will discuss what skin tone suits auburn hair and provide you with tips on how to make the most of this stunning hair color.

Table of Content

  • Description
  • How to Determine Your Skin Tone
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Complement Auburn Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Auburn Hair

Description

Auburn hair is a color that is flattering on a variety of skin tones, but not all skin tones are created equal when it comes to this shade. Generally, those with warm-toned skin, such as olive, golden, or peachy undertones, can pull off this color with ease. However, those with cool-toned skin, such as pink or blue undertones, may find that auburn hair can clash with their complexion.

How to Determine Your Skin Tone

If you are unsure of your skin tone, there are a few simple tests you can do to determine it. Firstly, look at the veins on your wrist. If they appear blue, you likely have cool-toned skin. If they appear green, you likely have warm-toned skin. Secondly, hold a piece of white paper next to your face. If your skin appears pink or rosy in contrast, you likely have cool-toned skin. If your skin appears yellow or golden, you likely have warm-toned skin.

Step by Step Guide

If you have warm-toned skin, auburn hair can be a great choice for you. Here is a step by step guide to help you choose the right shade:

  1. Identify your natural hair color: Auburn hair can look different on people depending on the base color of their hair. If you have naturally dark hair, a deep auburn shade can work well. If you have lighter hair, you may want to opt for a lighter auburn shade.
  2. Choose a shade that complements your skin tone: If you have warm-toned skin, choose a shade of auburn that has warm undertones, such as a copper or ginger shade. If you have cool-toned skin, choose a shade that has cool undertones, such as a burgundy or mahogany shade.
  3. Consider your eye color: Your eye color can also play a role in which shade of auburn will suit you best. If you have blue or green eyes, a reddish-brown shade can complement them well. If you have brown eyes, a deep auburn shade can make them pop.

Tips to Complement Auburn Hair

Once you have chosen the right shade of auburn hair, here are some tips to help you make the most of it:

  • Wear warm-toned makeup: If you have warm-toned skin, wear makeup with warm undertones, such as peach or bronze shades. If you have cool-toned skin, wear makeup with cool undertones, such as pink or purple shades.
  • Avoid clashing colors: If you have warm-toned skin, avoid wearing clothing with cool undertones, such as blues or greens. If you have cool-toned skin, avoid wearing clothing with warm undertones, such as oranges or yellows.
  • Keep your hair healthy: Auburn hair can be prone to fading, so use color-safe shampoo and conditioner, and avoid heat styling as much as possible. Regular trims can also help keep your hair looking its best.

FAQs

Can cool-toned skin pull off auburn hair?

Cool-toned skin can still look great with auburn hair, but it is important to choose a shade that has cool undertones, such as burgundy or mahogany.

What makeup should I wear with auburn hair?

If you have warm-toned skin, wear makeup with warm undertones, such as peach or bronze shades. If you have cool-toned skin, wear makeup with cool undertones, such as pink or purple shades.

How do I keep my auburn hair from fading?

Use color-safe shampoo and conditioner, avoid heat styling as much as possible, and get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care Tips for Auburn Hair

Auburn hair can be prone to fading, so it is important to take good care of it. Here are some hair care tips for auburn hair:

  • Use color-safe shampoo and conditioner: Regular shampoo and conditioner can strip the color from your hair, so use products that are specifically designed for color-treated hair.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling can damage your hair and cause the color to fade more quickly. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting low.
  • Get regular trims: Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your hair look dull and lifeless.

In conclusion, auburn hair can look stunning on a variety of skin tones, but it is important to choose the right shade and take good care of your hair to make the most of this beautiful color. Use the tips and advice in this post to help you achieve the perfect look for you.
