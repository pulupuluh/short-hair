How To Fix Side Bangs That Are Too Thick A Comprehensive Guide

If you're struggling with side bangs that are too thick, you're not alone. Thick side bangs can be tricky to manage and style, but with the right techniques, you can achieve the perfect look. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to fix side bangs that are too thick, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

How to Fix Side Bangs that are Too Thick

Fixing thick side bangs may seem daunting, but with the right approach, it can be a breeze. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Determine the Desired Length

The first step is to decide how long you want your side bangs to be. You can use a ruler or measuring tape to ensure that both sides are even.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section off your hair so that you're only working with your side bangs. You can use hair clips or ties to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Use Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are a great tool for thinning out thick side bangs. Use them to cut small sections of your bangs at a time, starting at the ends and working your way up.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

After thinning out your side bangs, check for evenness by comparing both sides. If one side is longer or thicker than the other, make adjustments until they're even.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

Once you've achieved the desired thickness and length, style your side bangs as desired. You can use a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron to create your desired look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide for fixing side bangs that are too thick:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Make sure your side bangs are completely dry before proceeding.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section off your hair so that you're only working with your side bangs. You can use hair clips or ties to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Use Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are a great tool for thinning out thick side bangs. Start at the ends of your bangs and work your way up, cutting small sections at a time. Make sure to keep your shears at an angle to avoid creating blunt lines.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

After thinning out your side bangs, check for evenness by comparing both sides. If one side is longer or thicker than the other, make adjustments until they're even.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

Once you've achieved the desired thickness and length, style your side bangs as desired. You can use a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron to create your desired look.

Tips for Managing Thick Side Bangs

Thick side bangs can be a challenge to manage, but with these tips, you can keep them looking great:

  • Use a round brush when blow drying your side bangs to create volume and shape.
  • Apply a small amount of hair serum or oil to your side bangs to tame frizz and flyaways.
  • Use a flat iron to straighten your side bangs for a sleek look.
  • Experiment with different parting options to find the best look for your face shape.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing side bangs that are too thick:

How do I know if my side bangs are too thick?

If your side bangs are difficult to style or feel heavy, they may be too thick. You can thin them out using thinning shears or have a professional stylist do it for you.

How often should I trim my side bangs?

Your side bangs should be trimmed every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and healthy.

Can I thin out my own side bangs?

While it's possible to thin out your own side bangs, it's best to have a professional stylist do it for you to avoid any mistakes.

Hair Care for Side Bangs

To keep your side bangs looking great, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Blow dry your side bangs using a round brush to create volume and shape.
  • Apply a small amount of hair serum or oil to your side bangs to tame frizz and flyaways.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your side bangs, as this can cause damage.

By following these tips, you can keep your side bangs looking great and easy to manage. With the right approach, fixing thick side bangs can be a breeze.
