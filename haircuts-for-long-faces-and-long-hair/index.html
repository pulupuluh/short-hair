Haircuts For Long Faces And Long Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Jun 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have a long face and long hair, it can be challenging to find a haircut that flatters your features. However, with the right style and cut, you can enhance your natural beauty and create a look that you love. In this complete guide, we'll share how to find the perfect haircut for your long face and long hair, including tips, step-by-step instructions, and more.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to Find the Right Haircut for a Long Face and Long Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Haircut for a Long Face and Long Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Haircut and Style
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Long Hair and Long Faces

Description

First, let's define what we mean by "long face" and "long hair." A long face is typically longer than it is wide, with a high forehead and a narrow chin. Long hair is hair that falls below the shoulders and can be straight, wavy, or curly.

When it comes to haircuts for long faces and long hair, the goal is to create balance and proportion. You want a haircut that elongates your face and complements your hair texture and length.

How to Find the Right Haircut for a Long Face and Long Hair

The first step in finding the perfect haircut for a long face and long hair is to determine your face shape. You can do this by measuring your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline with a tape measure. If your forehead and jawline are longer than your cheekbones, you have a long face.

Once you've determined your face shape, it's essential to consider your hair texture and length. Long hair can be challenging to manage, so you want to choose a style that flatters your features and is easy to maintain.

Some of the best haircuts for long faces and long hair include:

  • Long layers
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Chin-length bobs
  • Shoulder-length cuts with face-framing layers
  • Long, loose waves
  • Straight, sleek styles

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Haircut for a Long Face and Long Hair

Now that you know what haircut to choose, here's a step-by-step guide to getting the perfect style:

  1. Research and choose a reputable hairstylist who has experience working with long hair and long faces.
  2. Bring photos of the haircut you want to your appointment.
  3. Communicate clearly with your stylist about your expectations and what you like and don't like about your current hairstyle.
  4. Trust your stylist's recommendations for the best cut and style for your face shape and hair texture.
  5. Ask for styling tips and product recommendations to help maintain your new haircut and style.

Tips for Maintaining Your Haircut and Style

Once you have your new haircut and style, it's essential to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner that's designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heated styling tools too often and always use a heat protectant spray.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain your style.
  • Experiment with different styling products, such as mousse or texturizing spray, to find what works best for your hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about haircuts for long faces and long hair:

What haircut is best for long faces?

The best haircuts for long faces are those that add width to your face, such as chin-length bobs or shoulder-length cuts with face-framing layers. Side-swept bangs can also help balance your features.

Can I have bangs with a long face?

Yes! Side-swept bangs are a great option for long faces, as they can help break up the length of your face and add dimension.

How often should I get my hair cut?

It's recommended to get your hair cut every 6-8 weeks to maintain your style and keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care for Long Hair and Long Faces

If you have long hair and a long face, it's essential to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back tightly, as this can create tension on your scalp and lead to breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

With these tips and a great haircut, you can enhance your natural beauty and create a look that you love.
