Will Microblading Damage Eyebrows The Truth Revealed

Written by Bardi Mar 22, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're considering microblading, you might be wondering if it will damage your eyebrows. In this article, we'll explore the truth behind this popular cosmetic procedure and answer some of the most common questions about it. From the description of the procedure to hair care tips, we've got you covered. Let's dive in!

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Microblading is a semi-permanent cosmetic procedure that involves using a handheld tool to create tiny cuts in the skin of the eyebrow area. Pigment is then inserted into the cuts, creating the appearance of fuller, more defined eyebrows. The procedure typically takes two hours and can last up to three years.

Microblading has become increasingly popular in recent years, with many people opting for this procedure instead of traditional eyebrow makeup. Unlike makeup, microblading can save you time and effort in your daily routine. You won't have to worry about smudging or reapplying throughout the day.

How to

If you're interested in getting microblading done, the first step is to find a qualified and experienced professional. Look for someone who has a portfolio of before and after photos and positive reviews from previous clients. You can also ask for recommendations from friends or family members.

Once you've found a professional you trust, you'll have a consultation to discuss your desired eyebrow shape and color. The professional will then use a pencil to draw on the shape of your new eyebrows for you to approve before the actual procedure begins.

Step by Step

The microblading procedure involves the following steps:

  1. The professional will clean the eyebrow area and apply a numbing cream to minimize any discomfort.
  2. The professional will use a handheld tool to make tiny cuts in the skin, creating the appearance of hair strokes.
  3. Pigment will be inserted into the cuts.
  4. After the procedure is complete, the professional will apply a healing ointment to the area and give you instructions for aftercare.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind if you're considering microblading:

  • Research your options thoroughly before choosing a professional.
  • Be prepared for a two-hour procedure.
  • Follow your professional's aftercare instructions closely to ensure proper healing.
  • Avoid getting the area wet for a few days after the procedure.
  • Avoid sun exposure and tanning beds for a few weeks after the procedure.

FAQ

How long does microblading last?

Microblading can last up to three years, but touch-ups may be needed every six months to a year to maintain the desired look.

Will microblading damage my eyebrows?

When done correctly by a qualified professional, microblading should not damage your eyebrows. However, as with any cosmetic procedure, there is a small risk of complications. Be sure to choose a professional you trust and follow their aftercare instructions closely.

Is microblading painful?

The procedure can cause some discomfort, but a numbing cream is typically used to minimize any pain.

Hair Care

After getting microblading done, it's important to take care of your eyebrows to ensure the best results. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Avoid using any exfoliating products on the eyebrow area for at least a week after the procedure.
  • Avoid applying any makeup or skincare products to the eyebrow area for at least a week after the procedure.
  • Avoid touching or scratching the eyebrow area.
  • Avoid using any products containing glycolic acid or retinol on the eyebrow area.
  • Avoid getting facials, chemical peels, or other facial treatments for at least a month after the procedure.

Now that you understand more about microblading and how to take care of your eyebrows after the procedure, you can make an informed decision about whether it's right for you. Remember to choose a professional you trust and follow their aftercare instructions closely to ensure the best results.
