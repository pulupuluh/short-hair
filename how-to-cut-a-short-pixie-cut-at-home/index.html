How To Cut A Short Pixie Cut At Home A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle and want to try something bold and daring, a short pixie cut might be just what you need. And the best part? You don't have to go to a salon and spend a ton of money to achieve this look. With the right tools and technique, you can cut a short pixie cut at home. In this guide, we'll show you how.

How to Cut a Short Pixie Cut at Home

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to have the right tools. You'll need:

  • Sharp hairdressing scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers
  • Clip
  • Mirror

Once you have your tools ready, it's time to start cutting.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut a short pixie cut at home:

  1. Wash and dry your hair. You want to start with clean, dry hair so that you can see how your hair falls naturally.
  2. Section your hair. Use a comb to create a section from your forehead to your crown, and clip the rest of your hair out of the way.
  3. Start cutting. Use hairdressing scissors to cut your hair to your desired length. Remember to cut small sections at a time and to cut vertically rather than horizontally.
  4. Blend the back. Take down the rest of your hair and use the clippers to blend the back and sides of your hair. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one until you achieve the desired length.
  5. Texturize. Use the scissors to texturize your hair by cutting into the ends and creating texture and movement.
  6. Trim the sides. Use the clippers to trim the sides of your hair, starting with a longer guard and working your way down to a shorter one.
  7. Check for symmetry. Use a mirror to check that your hair is symmetrical on both sides.
  8. Style your hair. Use your fingers and a small amount of product to style your hair into your desired look.

Tips for Cutting a Short Pixie Cut at Home

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a short pixie cut at home:

  • Start with longer guards and work your way down to shorter ones to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Cut vertically rather than horizontally to create texture and movement.
  • Check for symmetry regularly to ensure your hair is even on both sides.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or family member.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting a short pixie cut at home:

  • Can I cut a short pixie cut at home if I have curly hair?
    Yes, you can. Just be sure to cut your hair when it's dry so that you can see how it falls naturally.
  • What if I make a mistake?
    Don't worry. Hair grows back, and mistakes can always be fixed. You can always go to a salon to have your hair fixed if needed.
  • How often do I need to cut a short pixie cut?
    It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain your style. Most people need to cut their hair every four to six weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting a Short Pixie Cut at Home

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your style.
  • Use a hair serum or oil to keep your hair moisturized and shiny.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut a short pixie cut at home and achieve a bold and daring new look. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and have fun.
