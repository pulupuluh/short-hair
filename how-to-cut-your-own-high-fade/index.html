How To Cut Your Own High Fade Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby May 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending money and time at the barbershop? Do you want to learn how to cut your own high fade? Look no further! We have put together a step-by-step guide to help you achieve the perfect high fade from the comfort of your own home. Let's get started!

How to Cut Your Own High Fade

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need clippers, a comb, a mirror, and a pair of scissors. You can purchase these items at your local beauty supply store or online.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair thoroughly with shampoo and conditioner. Dry your hair with a towel or blow dryer until it is completely dry.

Step 3: Create a Guideline

Use your clippers to create a guideline around the bottom of your hairline. Start at the nape of your neck and work your way up to the crown of your head. Use a #2 guard for this step.

Step 4: Fade the Sides

Attach a #1 guard to your clippers and start fading the sides of your hair. Use an upward motion with the clippers to create a gradient effect. Start at the bottom and work your way up. Use a mirror to check your progress.

Step 5: Blend the Top

Switch to a #3 guard and blend the top of your hair with the sides. Use a comb to lift your hair and create a seamless blend. Make sure to check your progress in the mirror.

Step 6: Define the Top

Use a pair of scissors to trim the top of your hair to your desired length. Start at the front and work your way to the back. Use a comb to lift your hair and make sure everything is even.

Step 7: Clean Up the Neckline

Use your clippers to clean up the neckline. Make sure it is straight and even. You can also use a razor for a closer shave.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Apply your favorite styling product to your hair and style it to your liking. You can use a comb, brush, or your fingers to create your desired look.

Tips for Cutting Your Own High Fade

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Take your time and check your progress frequently in the mirror.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
  • Practice makes perfect, so don't worry if your first attempt isn't perfect.

FAQs About Cutting Your Own High Fade

Can I cut my own high fade?

Yes, with the right tools and some practice, you can cut your own high fade.

What tools do I need to cut my own high fade?

You will need clippers, a comb, a mirror, and a pair of scissors.

How long does it take to cut your own high fade?

It can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour to cut your own high fade, depending on your skill level.

Can I fix a mistake if I cut too much hair?

Yes, you can fix a mistake by using a longer guard or blending the hair with scissors.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your High Fade

  • Wash your hair regularly with shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it.
  • Trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your high fade.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap.

Cutting your own high fade can be a rewarding experience, but it takes practice and patience. Follow these steps, tips, and hair care advice to achieve the perfect high fade. Remember, practice makes perfect!
