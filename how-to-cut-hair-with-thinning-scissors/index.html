How To Cut Hair With Thinning Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter May 04, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Thinning scissors are an essential tool for any hairstylist. They are used to remove bulk and create texture in hair, making it easier to style and manage. However, using thinning scissors can be tricky if you don't know how to use them properly. In this article, we will show you how to cut hair with thinning scissors in a few easy steps.

How to Cut Hair with Thinning Scissors

Cutting hair with thinning scissors requires a few simple steps:

Step 1: Prepare the Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure the hair is clean and dry. Comb the hair to remove any tangles or knots. If the hair is too thick, you can use regular scissors to thin it out before using thinning scissors.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Divide the hair into sections using hair clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to manage and cut the hair.

Step 3: Use the Thinning Scissors

Hold the thinning scissors at a slight angle and cut a small section of hair at a time. Start at the ends of the hair and work your way up towards the roots. Make sure to keep the scissors moving in a fluid motion to create an even texture.

Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can create uneven layers and thin spots.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

After you have cut all the sections of hair, use regular scissors to blend the hair and remove any remaining bulk. This will create a natural and even look.

Step 5: Style the Hair

Once you have finished cutting the hair, style it as desired using styling products or tools.

Tips for Cutting Hair with Thinning Scissors

Here are some tips to help you cut hair with thinning scissors:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging the hair.
  • Practice on a mannequin or a willing friend before cutting someone's hair.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once to avoid creating uneven layers and thin spots.
  • Blend the hair with regular scissors to create a natural and even look.

FAQs

Can thinning scissors damage my hair?

No, thinning scissors are designed to remove bulk and create texture in hair without damaging it. However, using dull or old scissors can damage the hair.

What type of hair is best suited for thinning scissors?

Thinning scissors are best suited for thick and heavy hair. They can also be used on curly or wavy hair to create texture and reduce bulk.

How often should I use thinning scissors on my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. For most people, using thinning scissors every two to three months is enough to maintain the hair's texture and manageability.

Hair Care After Using Thinning Scissors

After using thinning scissors, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to protect the hair from heat and styling damage.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners too often, as this can damage the hair.
  • Trim the hair regularly to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful after using thinning scissors.

Conclusion

Cutting hair with thinning scissors is a great way to create texture and manageability in thick or heavy hair. By following these simple steps and tips, you can achieve a natural and even look that will make your hair easier to style and manage. Just remember to use sharp scissors, practice on a mannequin or willing friend, and take care of your hair after using thinning scissors.
