What Should You Not Do If Your Hair Is Thinning

Written by Bardi Apr 21, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Thinning hair can be a frustrating and challenging experience for both men and women. While it is normal to lose up to 100 hairs per day, an excessive amount of hair loss can indicate a problem. If you are experiencing thinning hair, it is essential to take care of your hair properly to avoid further damage. This article will discuss what you should not do if your hair is thinning.

Not Combing Hair When Wet

Combing your hair when it is wet can cause hair breakage and damage to the hair follicles. It is best to use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to gently comb through your hair when it is damp. Avoid using a hairbrush, as it can cause more damage to your hair.

Not Using Heat Styling Tools

Using heat styling tools on your hair can cause damage to your hair follicles and lead to hair loss. Avoid using flat irons, curling irons, and hot rollers on your hair. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray on your hair before styling.

Not Eating a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet is crucial for healthy hair growth. Eating a balanced diet that includes protein, vitamins, and minerals can help strengthen your hair and promote healthy hair growth. Avoid diets that are high in sugar and processed foods, as they can lead to nutritional deficiencies that can cause hair loss.

Not Avoiding Stress

Stress can cause hair loss, so it is essential to manage your stress levels. Engage in stress-reducing activities such as exercise, meditation, or yoga to help reduce your stress levels.

Not Using the Right Products

Using the wrong hair products can damage your hair and lead to hair loss. Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals such as sulfates and parabens. Look for products that are specifically designed for thinning hair and contain natural ingredients such as biotin, keratin, and vitamins.

Not Getting Enough Sleep

A lack of sleep can lead to hair loss. Getting enough sleep is essential for healthy hair growth. Aim to get seven to eight hours of sleep per night to help promote healthy hair growth.

Not Over-Brushing

Over-brushing your hair can cause hair breakage and damage to the hair follicles. Avoid brushing your hair too much, especially when it is wet. Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to gently comb through your hair.

Not Over-Washing

Washing your hair too often can strip your hair of its natural oils, leading to dry and brittle hair. Avoid washing your hair every day, and instead, wash your hair every other day or every few days. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is specifically designed for thinning hair.

Not Tying Hair Tightly

Tying your hair too tightly can cause hair breakage and damage to the hair follicles. Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids, ponytails, and buns. Instead, opt for loose hairstyles that do not put too much tension on your hair.

Not Ignoring Medical Conditions

If you are experiencing hair loss, it is essential to seek medical attention. Hair loss can be a symptom of an underlying medical condition such as thyroid problems or autoimmune diseases. Ignoring these conditions can lead to further hair loss and damage to your hair follicles.

Tips for Thinning Hair Care

  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce hair breakage and damage to the hair follicles.
  • Avoid using hair accessories that are too tight, as they can cause hair breakage and damage to the hair follicles.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help strengthen your hair and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to help stimulate blood flow to your hair follicles and promote healthy hair growth.

FAQs

What causes hair loss?

Hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, medical conditions, medications, and stress.

Can stress cause hair loss?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss. Stress can disrupt the normal hair growth cycle and lead to hair loss.

Can hair loss be reversed?

While hair loss cannot be completely reversed, there are treatments available that can help slow down hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.

Conclusion

Thinning hair can be a frustrating and challenging experience, but by taking care of your hair properly, you can avoid further damage. Avoiding the things discussed in this article can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent further hair loss. If you are experiencing hair loss, it is essential to seek medical attention to determine the underlying cause and get the proper treatment.
