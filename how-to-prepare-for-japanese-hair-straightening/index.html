How To Prepare For Japanese Hair Straightening A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 04, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Japanese hair straightening is a popular hair treatment that can give you long-lasting, pin-straight locks. However, before you schedule your appointment, there are a few things you should know to prepare your hair for the treatment. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of preparing for Japanese hair straightening, including what to do before, during, and after the treatment.

What is Japanese Hair Straightening?

Japanese hair straightening, also known as thermal reconditioning, is a hair treatment that uses heat and chemicals to permanently straighten curly or wavy hair. The treatment originated in Japan in the 1990s and has since become popular around the world.

During the treatment, a stylist applies a special solution to your hair that breaks down the bonds that give your hair its natural shape. Then, your hair is flat-ironed at a high temperature to create a pin-straight look. The treatment can take several hours, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

How to Prepare for Japanese Hair Straightening: Before the Treatment

Before you schedule your Japanese hair straightening appointment, there are a few things you should do to prepare your hair.

1. Consult with a stylist

Schedule a consultation with a stylist who specializes in Japanese hair straightening. They will be able to assess your hair type and texture to determine if the treatment is right for you. They can also advise you on how to prepare your hair for the treatment.

2. Stop using chemical treatments

Stop using chemical treatments on your hair at least two weeks before your appointment. This includes relaxers, perms, and color treatments. Chemical treatments can weaken your hair and make it more susceptible to damage during the Japanese hair straightening process.

3. Deep condition your hair

Deep condition your hair in the weeks leading up to your appointment. This will help to strengthen your hair and prevent damage during the treatment.

4. Wash your hair

Wash your hair the day before your appointment. Do not use any styling products, such as gels or hairspray, as they can interfere with the treatment.

5. Don't wear tight clothing

Wear loose, comfortable clothing to your appointment. Tight clothing can cause discomfort during the treatment process.

How to Prepare for Japanese Hair Straightening: During the Treatment

During the Japanese hair straightening process, you will need to follow a few guidelines to ensure the best results.

1. Be patient

Japanese hair straightening can take several hours, depending on the length and thickness of your hair. Be patient and bring something to keep you occupied, such as a book or music.

2. Avoid touching your hair

Avoid touching your hair during the treatment process, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

3. Follow your stylist's instructions

Your stylist will give you instructions on how to care for your hair after the treatment. Follow their instructions carefully to ensure the best results.

How to Prepare for Japanese Hair Straightening: After the Treatment

After your Japanese hair straightening treatment, you will need to take care of your hair to maintain the results.

1. Avoid washing your hair

Avoid washing your hair for at least 72 hours after the treatment. This will give your hair time to adjust to its new shape.

2. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Use a sulfate-free shampoo to wash your hair after the 72-hour waiting period. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage.

3. Use a hair mask

Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

Tips for Maintaining Your Japanese Straightened Hair

Here are a few tips to help you maintain your Japanese straightened hair:

1. Avoid heat styling

Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.

2. Trim your hair regularly

Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

3. Protect your hair

Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and other environmental factors that can cause damage. Wear a hat or use a protective hair product.

FAQs About Japanese Hair Straightening

Q: How long does Japanese hair straightening last?

A: Japanese hair straightening can last up to six months, depending on how fast your hair grows.

Q: Can I color my hair after Japanese hair straightening?

A: It is best to wait at least two weeks before coloring your hair after Japanese hair straightening.

Q: Can I swim with Japanese straightened hair?

A: It is best to avoid swimming for at least two weeks after the treatment to allow your hair to adjust to its new shape.

Conclusion

Japanese hair straightening can give you long-lasting, pin-straight locks. However, it is important to prepare your hair for the treatment to ensure the best results. Follow the steps in this guide to prepare for Japanese hair straightening, and use the tips provided to maintain your straightened hair.
