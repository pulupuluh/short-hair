How To Do A Bald Fade At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Mar 13, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking for a fresh new haircut, a bald fade is a sleek and stylish option. But getting a bald fade at a barber shop can be expensive, especially if you need frequent touch-ups. Fortunately, it's possible to do a bald fade at home with the right tools and technique. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a professional-looking bald fade in the comfort of your own home.

Description

A bald fade, also known as a skin fade or zero fade, is a haircut that gradually fades from longer hair on top to a completely shaved or bald section at the bottom. It's a popular style among men and can be customized with different lengths and textures on top.

How to Do a Bald Fade at Home: Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step to doing a bald fade at home is to gather the right tools. You'll need:

  • Clippers with different guard sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clipper oil
  • Balding clippers or a razor
  • Mirror
  • Towel or cape

Make sure your clippers are fully charged and oiled before you begin.

Step 2: Start with a Longer Guard

Start by using a longer guard on your clippers to create a guideline for the fade. This means cutting the hair to a uniform length around the sides and back of your head. Use the comb to lift the hair and the clippers to trim it to the desired length. Repeat this step with the next shortest guard until you reach the desired length for the fade.

Step 3: Create the Fade

Now it's time to create the fade. Switch to a shorter guard on your clippers and start to trim the hair around the sides and back of your head, gradually moving up from the bottom. Use a flicking motion with the clippers to blend the hair and create a seamless transition from longer hair on top to a bald section at the bottom. Keep checking your progress in the mirror and adjust the guard size as needed to achieve the desired fade.

Step 4: Blend the Top

Once you've created the fade, it's time to blend the hair on top. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs and create a clean and even look. You can also use the clippers with a longer guard to trim the top if desired.

Step 5: Shave the Bottom

Finally, it's time to shave the bald section at the bottom. Use balding clippers or a razor to shave the hair completely, making sure to follow the natural curve of your head. Be careful not to cut yourself, and use a light touch to avoid irritation.

Tips for a Great Bald Fade

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down gradually to avoid mistakes
  • Use the mirror to check your progress and make adjustments as needed
  • Take breaks and step back to observe your work from a distance
  • Be patient and take your time to achieve a smooth and even fade
  • Experiment with different guard sizes and techniques to find what works best for you

FAQ

Can I do a bald fade at home if I have long hair?

Yes, you can still do a bald fade at home if you have long hair. Start by trimming the longer hair on top to the desired length, and then follow the steps for creating the fade.

Do I need a special type of clippers for a bald fade?

No, you don't need a special type of clippers for a bald fade. Any clippers with different guard sizes will work, but it's important to make sure they're fully charged and oiled before you begin.

How often do I need to touch-up my bald fade?

How often you need to touch-up your bald fade will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain the style. On average, you may need to touch-up every 2-3 weeks to keep your bald fade looking fresh.

Hair Care Tips for a Bald Fade

  • Moisturize your scalp with a light lotion or oil to prevent dryness and flakiness
  • Protect your scalp from the sun with a hat or sunscreen
  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to keep it clean and healthy
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair and make the fade less visible
  • Visit a professional barber for touch-ups and maintenance as needed

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and professional-looking bald fade at home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and experiment with different techniques to find what works best for you. Happy styling!
