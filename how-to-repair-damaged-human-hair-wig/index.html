How To Repair Damaged Human Hair Wig Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Feb 18, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Wearing a human hair wig is a great way to change up your hairstyle or to cover up hair loss. However, just like natural hair, wigs can also get damaged due to heat styling, chemical treatments, and everyday wear and tear. When your wig is damaged, it can be frustrating to figure out how to fix it. But don't worry, we've got you covered with our guide on how to repair damaged human hair wig!

Table of Contents

  • How to Repair Damaged Human Hair Wig
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Wig
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Your Human Hair Wig

How to Repair Damaged Human Hair Wig

The first step in repairing a damaged human hair wig is to assess the damage. Look for signs of tangling, shedding or matting. If the damage is minimal, you may be able to fix it easily. However, if the damage is severe, you may need to take your wig to a professional for repair.

Some of the most common ways to repair a damaged human hair wig include:

  • Detangling the hair using a wide-tooth comb or brush
  • Moisturizing the hair with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil
  • Trimming split ends
  • Re-styling the hair using heat tools
  • Replacing damaged or missing hair strands

Step by Step Guide

If you want to repair your damaged human hair wig at home, follow these steps:

  1. Detangle the hair using a wide-tooth comb or brush. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots.
  2. Wash the wig using a mild shampoo and lukewarm water. Avoid rubbing or twisting the hair, as this can cause further damage.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to the hair to moisturize and protect it.
  4. Comb the hair using a wide-tooth comb or brush. Be gentle and avoid pulling or tugging on the hair.
  5. Blow-dry the hair using a low heat setting. Do not use high heat, as this can damage the hair.
  6. Trim any split ends using a pair of sharp scissors. Be careful not to cut too much hair.
  7. Re-style the hair using heat tools, if desired. Use a heat protectant spray to protect the hair from damage.
  8. If there are any missing or damaged hair strands, replace them using a wig repair kit or take the wig to a professional for repair.
  9. Store the wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to maintain its shape and prevent tangling.
  10. Repeat these steps as needed to keep your wig looking healthy and beautiful!

Tips for Maintaining Your Wig

Here are some tips to help you maintain your human hair wig and prevent damage:

  • Avoid using heat tools on the highest setting. Use a low or medium heat setting to prevent damage to the hair.
  • Avoid sleeping in your wig. This can cause tangling and matting.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or products on your wig. Stick to gentle, wig-specific products.
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head when not in use to maintain its shape.
  • Brush or comb your wig regularly to prevent tangling and matting.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about repairing damaged human hair wigs:

  1. Can I repair my damaged wig at home?

    2. Yes, you can repair minor damage to your wig at home using the steps outlined above. However, if the damage is severe, it's best to take your wig to a professional for repair.

  2. How often should I wash my wig?

    3. You should wash your wig every 6-8 wears, or as needed depending on how often you wear it and how much product you use. Washing your wig too often can cause damage, so be sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions.

  3. How do I prevent tangling and matting?

    4. To prevent tangling and matting, brush or comb your wig regularly, store it on a wig stand or mannequin head, and avoid sleeping in it.

Hair Care for Your Human Hair Wig

Here are some tips for caring for your human hair wig:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to wash your wig.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle your wig.
  • Avoid using hot water or high heat settings on your wig. Use lukewarm water and a low heat setting instead.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or products on your wig.
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head when not in use.

By following these tips and tricks, you can repair your damaged human hair wig and keep it looking beautiful for years to come!
