What To Do If Bleach Makes Your Hair Fall Out A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you've ever had your hair bleached, you know how damaging it can be to your hair. Bleaching can cause your hair to become dry and brittle, and in some cases, it can even cause your hair to fall out. If this has happened to you, don't panic. There are steps you can take to help your hair recover. In this guide, we'll walk you through what to do if bleach makes your hair fall out.

Description

Hair loss due to bleach is a common problem that many people face. Bleaching your hair can cause damage to the hair follicles, which can result in hair loss. This can be a traumatic experience for anyone, especially if you've spent a lot of time and money on your hair. However, there are ways to help your hair recover and prevent further damage.

How To

If you've noticed that your hair is falling out after bleaching, the first thing you should do is stop bleaching your hair. This will give your hair time to recover from the damage that has already been done. You should also avoid using any other harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes or relaxers.

The next step is to start taking care of your hair. This means using gentle shampoos and conditioners that are designed to help repair damaged hair. You should also use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help restore moisture to your hair.

If you're experiencing hair loss due to bleach, it's important to maintain a healthy diet. Eating a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote hair growth and strengthen your hair. You should also drink plenty of water to help keep your hair hydrated.

Step By Step

Step 1: Stop bleaching your hair.

Step 2: Avoid using any other harsh chemicals on your hair.

Step 3: Use gentle shampoos and conditioners that are designed to help repair damaged hair.

Step 4: Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help restore moisture to your hair.

Step 5: Maintain a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals.

Step 6: Drink plenty of water to help keep your hair hydrated.

Tips

Here are some additional tips that can help you recover from hair loss due to bleach:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as blow dryers and flat irons.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair, such as ponytails and braids.
  • Consider using a hair growth supplement to help promote hair growth.
  • Be patient. It can take time for your hair to recover from damage.

FAQ

Q: Can bleach cause permanent hair loss?

A: In some cases, bleach can cause permanent hair loss. However, this is rare and usually only occurs if the bleach is left on the hair for too long or if it is applied incorrectly.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after bleach?

A: It can take several months for hair to grow back after bleach. However, this will depend on the severity of the damage and your hair's natural growth rate.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential if you want to prevent further damage and promote hair growth. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Use gentle shampoos and conditioners that are designed to help repair damaged hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help restore moisture to your hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Drink plenty of water to help keep your hair hydrated.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can help promote hair growth and prevent further damage. Remember to be patient, as it can take time for your hair to recover from damage.

Dealing with hair loss due to bleach can be a difficult and emotional experience. However, by taking the right steps and caring for your hair, you can help promote hair growth and prevent further damage. If you're still experiencing hair loss after taking these steps, be sure to consult with a hair care professional for additional guidance and support.
