How To Stop Thick Curly Hair From Frizzing

Written by Oliver Apr 16, 2023 · 8 min read

Thick curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can look great when it's tamed, it can easily turn into a frizzy mess if not properly cared for. In this blog post, we'll share some tips on how to stop thick curly hair from frizzing, so you can rock your curls with confidence.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Thick Curly Hair from Frizzing
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Frizz
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

How to Stop Thick Curly Hair from Frizzing

Before we dive into the tips, it's important to understand what causes frizz. Frizz happens when the hair cuticle is raised, allowing moisture to penetrate the hair shaft. This causes the hair to swell, resulting in frizz. Here are some ways to prevent this from happening:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Avoid harsh shampoos that contain sulfates, as they can strip the hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and frizzy. Instead, opt for a gentle shampoo that is free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals.

2. Apply a Deep Conditioner

After shampooing, apply a deep conditioner to your hair. This will help to restore moisture to your curls and prevent frizz. Leave the conditioner on for at least 5-10 minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, causing it to become dry and frizzy. Try to limit washing your hair to 2-3 times a week, and use a dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh.

4. Use a Microfiber Towel

When drying your hair, avoid using a regular bath towel, as it can cause friction and lead to frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel, which is gentler on your hair and will help to absorb excess moisture.

5. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing and conditioning your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to your curls. This will help to lock in moisture and prevent frizz. Be sure to apply the conditioner evenly throughout your hair.

6. Use a Diffuser

When blow-drying your hair, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute heat evenly and prevent frizz. Start by using a low heat setting and hold the diffuser close to your scalp, then work your way down to the ends of your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're not sure where to start, here's a step-by-step guide on how to prevent frizz:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo.
  2. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and leave it on for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Rinse out the conditioner with cool water.
  4. Dry your hair with a microfiber towel.
  5. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your curls.
  6. Use a diffuser attachment when blow-drying your hair.
  7. Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day.
  8. Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction.
  9. Avoid using too much heat on your hair.
  10. Protect your hair from the elements, such as wind and humidity.

Tips to Prevent Frizz

Here are some additional tips to help prevent frizz:

  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, such as flat irons and curling wands.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Apply a hair oil or serum to your curls to help smooth them out.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends, which can lead to frizz.

FAQs

Q: Can I use a regular towel to dry my hair?

A: It's best to avoid using a regular bath towel, as it can cause friction and lead to frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel, which is gentler on your hair and will help to absorb excess moisture.

Q: Can I use a hair straightener to tame my curls?

A: While a hair straightener can temporarily tame your curls, it can also cause damage and lead to frizz. It's best to embrace your natural curls and use products and techniques that enhance them.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It's best to limit washing your hair to 2-3 times a week, as washing it too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and frizzy.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for curly hair:

  • Avoid using hair products that contain sulfates and other harsh chemicals.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, such as flat irons and curling wands.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to help nourish your curls.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends, which can lead to frizz.

With these tips and tricks, you can say goodbye to frizzy curls and hello to beautiful, defined curls that you'll love showing off!
