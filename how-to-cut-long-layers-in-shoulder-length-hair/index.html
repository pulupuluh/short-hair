How To Cut Long Layers In Shoulder Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter May 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to add some volume and texture to your shoulder length hair, long layers are the perfect solution. But if you're not ready to head to the salon, you can cut long layers in your hair at home with a few basic tools and a little patience. In this article, we'll show you how to cut long layers in shoulder length hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand manner.

Description

Long layers are a versatile haircut that can add volume and texture to any hair type. They work particularly well for those with shoulder length hair, as they create movement and body without removing too much length. Cutting long layers at home can save you time and money, and it's a great way to experiment with different styles and lengths.

How to Cut Long Layers in Shoulder Length Hair

To cut long layers in shoulder length hair, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips

Before you begin, make sure your hair is clean and dry. It's also a good idea to have a picture or idea of the style you want to achieve.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut long layers in your shoulder length hair:

  1. Start by parting your hair down the middle and dividing it into two sections.
  2. Clip one section out of the way.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the top layer of the remaining section and comb it straight up. This will be your guide for the length of the layers.
  4. Hold the hair between your fingers, about an inch from the end.
  5. Using your scissors, cut into the hair at a slight angle, following the guide you created in the previous step.
  6. Release the hair and check the length. If it's too long, trim a little more. If it's too short, leave it for now.
  7. Repeat steps 3-6 on the remaining sections of hair, working your way down to the bottom layer.
  8. Once you've finished one side, clip it out of the way and repeat the process on the other side of your head.
  9. When you're finished, check both sides in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments.
  10. Style your hair as desired and enjoy your new long layers!

Tips for Cutting Long Layers in Shoulder Length Hair

Here are a few additional tips to help you achieve the perfect long layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections as you become more comfortable.
  • Use a comb to comb the hair straight up before cutting, as this will help ensure even layers.
  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to take your time and cut a little at a time than to cut too much and have to start over.
  • Remember that the layers will be shorter at the top of your head and gradually longer towards the bottom, so be mindful of this as you cut.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long layers in shoulder length hair:

Can I cut long layers in curly hair?

Yes, you can cut long layers in curly hair. However, it's important to keep in mind that curly hair shrinks when it dries, so you may want to cut the layers a little longer than you think you need to.

How often should I cut my long layers?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your long layers looking fresh and healthy.

Can I cut long layers in thin hair?

Yes, long layers can work well for thin hair as they create the illusion of volume and texture. Just be careful not to remove too much length, as this can make thin hair look even thinner.

Hair Care for Long Layered Hair

Long layered hair requires a little extra care to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair from drying out.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and keep your layers looking fresh.

Now that you know how to cut long layers in shoulder length hair, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and don't be afraid to make small adjustments as you go. With a little practice, you'll have beautiful long layers in no time!
