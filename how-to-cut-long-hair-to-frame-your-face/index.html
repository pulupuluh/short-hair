How To Cut Long Hair To Frame Your Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 04, 2023 · 7 min read
Are you looking for a fresh new haircut that will flatter your face shape? One of the best ways to achieve a flattering look is to cut your long hair to frame your face. This technique can make your features pop and give you a more youthful appearance. But how do you do it? In this article, we’ll give you a step-by-step guide on how to cut long hair to frame your face.

How to Cut Long Hair to Frame Your Face

Cutting long hair can be intimidating, but it doesn’t have to be. Here are the steps to achieve a face-framing cut:

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have everything you need. You’ll need sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair as you normally would. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 3: Part Your Hair

Part your hair down the middle or on the side, depending on your preference. Use a comb to make a straight part.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Clip the top section of your hair out of the way with a hair clip. This will allow you to focus on the bottom section first.

Step 5: Trim the Bottom Section

Take a small section of hair from the bottom and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to trim the ends, cutting at a slight angle towards your face. Repeat this process for the rest of the bottom section.

Step 6: Unclip the Top Section

Release the top section of your hair and clip the bottom section out of the way.

Step 7: Trim the Top Section

Take a small section of hair from the top and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to trim the ends, cutting at a slight angle towards your face. Repeat this process for the rest of the top section.

Step 8: Blend the Layers

Blend the layers by taking small sections of hair from the top and bottom and combing them together. Use your scissors to cut any pieces that are too long or uneven.

Step 9: Check Your Work

Use a mirror to check your work from all angles. Make any final adjustments as needed.

Step 10: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired. You can use a curling iron or flat iron to add waves or straighten your hair.

Tips

- Start with small cuts and work your way up to larger ones. - Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as it will appear longer than it actually is. - Use a mirror to check your work from all angles. - If you’re unsure about cutting your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQ

How often should I cut my hair to maintain the face-framing look?

It’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Can I achieve this look with curly hair?

Yes, you can achieve a face-framing look with curly hair. Make sure to cut your hair when it’s dry and use a diffuser when styling to enhance your natural curls.

Will this technique work for all face shapes?

This technique can work for most face shapes, but it’s best to consult with a professional stylist to determine the best haircut for your face shape.

Hair Care

To maintain healthy hair, it’s important to use a good shampoo and conditioner that’s suited for your hair type. Use a heat protectant when using styling tools and avoid using too much heat on your hair. Don’t forget to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair. Cutting long hair to frame your face can be a fun and easy way to switch up your look. Just remember to take your time and start with small cuts. With these steps and tips, you’ll be able to achieve a flattering face-framing haircut in no time!
