How To Bald Fade Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Mar 12, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of your usual haircut and looking for something edgy? A bald fade haircut might be the perfect option for you. This popular style features shaved sides that gradually fade into longer hair on top. In this guide, we’ll give you the lowdown on how to achieve this look. Follow our step-by-step instructions and tips to get a perfect bald fade haircut.

Description

A bald fade haircut is a popular men’s hairstyle that features short hair on the sides of the head, which gradually fades into longer hair on top. This style is also known as a skin fade because the hair is shaved down to the skin. With a bald fade, the hair is usually cut short near the ears and gradually gets longer near the crown of the head. The hair on the top of the head can be styled in various ways, such as a slick back or a quiff.

How to Bald Fade Haircut

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need:

  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Scissors
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Haircutting spray or water

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and drying it completely. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that the final result looks neat and clean.

Step 3: Determine the Length of the Fade

Decide how short you want the hair on the sides of your head to be. You can choose a high fade, which means the hair is cut very short near the temples, or a low fade, which means the hair is cut short near the ears. The length of the fade will depend on your personal preference and the shape of your head.

Step 4: Start with the Clippers

Using the clippers, start cutting the hair on the sides of your head. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly. Remember to blend the hair as you go, so there are no harsh lines.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you’ve finished cutting the hair with the clippers, use scissors to blend the hair between the top and the sides. This will create a smooth transition and give you a natural-looking fade.

Step 6: Cut the Top

Now it’s time to cut the hair on top of your head. Use scissors to cut the hair to your desired length. You can choose to keep it longer or shorter, depending on your preference.

Step 7: Finish the Look

Once you’ve finished cutting, style your hair as desired. You can use a styling product to create a textured or sleek look.

Tips

  • Take your time and don’t rush. A good bald fade takes time and patience.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one. This will help you achieve a smoother transition.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly.
  • Blend the hair between the top and the sides to create a natural-looking fade.
  • Use a styling product to create a textured or sleek look.

FAQ

How often should I get a bald fade haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want to maintain your hair. Generally, you should get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to keep your hair looking neat and clean.

Can I do a bald fade haircut at home?

Yes, you can do a bald fade haircut at home if you have the right tools and knowledge. Follow our step-by-step guide or watch tutorials online to get a better understanding of the process.

What face shapes work best with a bald fade?

A bald fade can work with most face shapes, but it looks especially good on oval and square faces.

Hair Care

To maintain your bald fade haircut, you should wash your hair regularly with a good quality shampoo and conditioner. Use a styling product to keep your hair in place and protect it from the sun and other environmental factors. You should also visit a professional stylist regularly to get a trim and keep your hair looking neat and clean.
