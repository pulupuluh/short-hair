How To Fix A Shirt That s Too Low Cut A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 01, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of constantly adjusting your shirt throughout the day because it's too low cut? Or maybe you found the perfect shirt, but it's just a little too revealing for your taste? Whatever the reason may be, we've got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to fix a shirt that's too low cut.

How to Fix a Shirt that's Too Low Cut

Before we dive into the steps, let's first identify the problem. A shirt that's too low cut can be uncomfortable and may not be appropriate for certain occasions. Here are some common reasons why a shirt may be too low cut:

  • The neckline is too deep
  • The shirt is too loose
  • The shirt is designed to be low cut

Now that we've identified the problem, let's move on to the solution. Here's how to fix a shirt that's too low cut:

Step 1: Gather Your Materials

For this DIY fix, you'll need the following materials:

  • The shirt that needs fixing
  • Fabric scissors
  • Fabric glue
  • Fabric tape
  • A piece of fabric that matches your shirt

Make sure you have all of these materials before moving on to the next step.

Step 2: Determine the Desired Neckline

Take the shirt and put it on. Stand in front of a mirror and determine the desired neckline. Mark the spot with a pin or a piece of fabric tape.

Step 3: Cut the Shirt

Take off the shirt and use fabric scissors to cut along the marked line. Make sure to cut in a straight line and to not cut too much off. You can always cut more later if needed.

Step 4: Create a Hem

Take the piece of fabric that matches your shirt and cut a strip that's long enough to cover the cut edge of the shirt. Fold the strip in half and iron it to create a crease.

Using fabric glue, attach the strip to the cut edge of the shirt. Make sure the strip is centered and that there's enough fabric to create a hem.

Step 5: Create the Hem

Using fabric glue, fold the strip over the cut edge of the shirt and create a hem. Make sure the hem is even and straight.

Step 6: Let the Glue Dry

Let the fabric glue dry completely before wearing the shirt.

Tips for Fixing a Shirt that's Too Low Cut

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when fixing a shirt that's too low cut:

  • Use a piece of fabric that matches your shirt for a seamless look
  • Don't cut too much off at once
  • Use fabric glue instead of sewing for a quicker fix
  • Test the fabric glue on a small, inconspicuous area of the shirt before using it on the entire hem

FAQ

Q: Can I use a sewing machine instead of fabric glue?

A: Yes, you can use a sewing machine to create the hem instead of fabric glue.

Q: Can I use a different color fabric for the hem?

A: Yes, you can use a different color fabric for the hem if you want to add a pop of color to your shirt.

Hair Care

Now that your shirt is fixed, let's talk about hair care. If you have long hair, try tying it up in a bun or a ponytail to keep it out of the way while you're adjusting your shirt. You can also use a headband or hair clips to keep your hair in place.

Remember, fixing a shirt that's too low cut is a quick and easy DIY that can save you from constantly adjusting your shirt throughout the day. With these simple steps, you'll have a perfectly fitting shirt in no time.
