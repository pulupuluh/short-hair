How To Revive Your Curly Wig A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 11, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you've been wearing a curly wig for a while, you may have noticed that it starts to lose its bounce and definition over time. This can be frustrating, but the good news is that there are ways to revive your wig and bring it back to life. In this blog post, we'll walk you through the steps to revive your curly wig and answer some common questions about wig care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Revive Your Curly Wig
  • Tips for Wig Care
  • FAQ

How to Revive Your Curly Wig

Reviving your curly wig is a process that involves washing, conditioning, and styling. Here's what you'll need:

  • Wig shampoo
  • Wig conditioner
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Styling products
  • Diffuser or hooded dryer

Step 1: Detangle your wig

Start by gently detangling your wig with a wide-tooth comb. Be careful not to tug or pull too hard, as this can damage the hair fibers. Work in small sections, starting at the tips and working your way up to the roots.

Step 2: Wash your wig

Wet your wig with cool water and apply a small amount of wig shampoo. Gently massage the shampoo into the hair, paying extra attention to the roots and any areas that are particularly oily. Rinse the shampoo out thoroughly with cool water.

Step 3: Condition your wig

Apply a generous amount of wig conditioner to the hair, working it in from the tips to the roots. Leave the conditioner in for a few minutes, then rinse it out with cool water. This will help to restore moisture to the hair and make it more manageable.

Step 4: Style your wig

Apply your favorite styling products to the hair, such as mousse, gel, or curl cream. Use a diffuser or hooded dryer to dry your wig on a low heat setting, scrunching the hair gently as you go to encourage the curls to form. Once your wig is dry, use your fingers to fluff and shape the curls into the desired style.

Tips for Wig Care

Here are some additional tips to help you care for your curly wig:

  • Always use products that are specifically designed for wigs, as regular hair care products can damage the fibers.
  • Don't wash your wig too often â once every 2-4 weeks is usually sufficient.
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to help it maintain its shape.
  • Avoid exposing your wig to heat from styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as this can damage the fibers.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase when sleeping in your wig to prevent tangling and frizz.

FAQ

How often should I wash my wig?

It depends on how often you wear your wig and how much product you use on it. Generally, you should aim to wash your wig every 2-4 weeks to keep it clean and fresh.

Can I use regular hair care products on my wig?

No, you should only use products that are specifically designed for wigs. Regular hair care products can contain ingredients that can damage the fibers of your wig.

How do I store my wig?

You should store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to help it maintain its shape. Avoid storing it in direct sunlight or near sources of heat, as this can damage the fibers.

Can I style my wig with heat?

We recommend avoiding heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage the fibers of your wig. If you need to style your wig, try using a low-heat setting on a diffuser or hooded dryer instead.

How do I prevent tangling and frizz?

Use a silk or satin pillowcase when sleeping in your wig, as this can help to prevent tangling and frizz. You can also try using a leave-in conditioner or detangling spray to keep the hair soft and manageable.

Hair Care

Caring for your curly wig is essential to keeping it looking great. Following these steps and tips can help you revive your curly wig and keep it looking its best for longer.
