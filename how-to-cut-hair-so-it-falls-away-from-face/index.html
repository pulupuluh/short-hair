How To Cut Hair So It Falls Away From Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of constantly having to brush hair out of your face? Cutting your hair so that it falls away from your face can help you achieve a more streamlined look. In this article, we'll take you through a step-by-step guide on how to cut hair so it falls away from the face.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After Cutting

How to Cut Hair So It Falls Away from Face

To cut hair so that it falls away from the face, you'll need to follow a few simple steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Hair

It's essential to start with clean hair. Shampoo and condition your hair as usual, then dry it thoroughly. Use a blow dryer to get rid of any moisture.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Divide your hair into three sections: the back and two sides. Use hair clips to keep the sections separate.

Step 3: Cut the Sides

Starting with one side, comb the hair straight down and cut it at an angle, going from the front to the back. This will create a diagonal line that falls away from the face. Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Work on the back section of your hair. Comb the hair straight down and cut it in a straight line. The length of the hair should be the same as the sides.

Step 5: Check for Balance

After cutting all the sections, check for balance. Comb the hair straight down and check if the length is the same on both sides. Make any necessary adjustments.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to help you cut your hair so that it falls away from your face:

Step 1: Gather the Right Tools

Before you start, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need good quality scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a spray bottle with water.

Step 2: Decide on Length

Decide on the length of your hair. You can go for a short or long cut, depending on your preference.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Divide your hair into three sections: the back and two sides. Use hair clips to keep the sections separate.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

Starting with one side, comb the hair straight down and cut it at an angle, going from the front to the back. This will create a diagonal line that falls away from the face. Repeat on the other side.

Step 5: Cut the Back

Work on the back section of your hair. Comb the hair straight down and cut it in a straight line. The length of the hair should be the same as the sides.

Step 6: Check for Balance

After cutting all the sections, check for balance. Comb the hair straight down and check if the length is the same on both sides. Make any necessary adjustments.

Step 7: Finish the Haircut

Once you're happy with the length and shape of your hair, finish the haircut by trimming any stray hairs and adding layers if desired.

Tips for Cutting Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting hair:

  • Always start with dry hair.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Make small cuts to avoid mistakes.
  • Don't cut too much at once.
  • Take breaks to avoid fatigue.

FAQs

How often should you cut your hair?

It's recommended to get a haircut every six to eight weeks to keep your hair healthy and maintain its shape.

Can you cut your hair when it's wet?

It's not recommended to cut your hair when it's wet as it can lead to uneven cutting and hair damage. Always start with dry hair.

Should you cut your hair yourself?

If you're confident in your abilities, you can cut your hair yourself. However, it's always best to leave it to a professional stylist.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's essential to take care of it to maintain its health and shape. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your haircut.

Cutting your hair so that it falls away from your face can give you a sleek and polished look. With these simple steps and tips, you can achieve the perfect haircut at home.
