How To Cut Layers In Medium Length Hair Yourself Ponytail

Written by Frank Apr 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a way to add some volume and texture to your medium length hair, cutting layers is the perfect solution. And the best part? You can do it yourself at home with just a few simple steps! In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting layers in your medium length hair using a ponytail method.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut Layers in Medium Length Hair Yourself Ponytail
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers in Medium Length Hair
  • FAQs about Cutting Layers in Medium Length Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Step by Step Guide:

Before we begin, make sure you have a pair of sharp scissors and a hair tie. Here's how to cut layers in medium length hair yourself using a ponytail method:

  1. Start by pulling your hair back into a ponytail. The height of the ponytail will determine how short the layers will be. For shorter layers, tie the ponytail higher on your head. For longer layers, tie the ponytail lower on your head.
  2. Next, hold the end of the ponytail straight up and use your scissors to make a straight cut across the bottom. This will be the shortest layer.
  3. Untie the ponytail and shake your hair out. You'll notice the shortest layer at the bottom.
  4. Take a small section of hair from the middle of your head and pull it straight up. Use your scissors to cut this section at an angle, starting at the shortest layer and gradually cutting longer as you move towards the ends.
  5. Repeat this process, working your way around your head and taking small sections of hair to cut at an angle. Make sure to keep the sections even in size to create a uniform look.
  6. Once you've cut all the layers, shake your hair out and style as desired. You'll notice the layers add volume and texture to your medium length hair.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Medium Length Hair:

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting layers in your medium length hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and gradually add more as you become more comfortable with the process.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to take small sections and cut gradually to achieve the desired length.
  • Consider cutting your hair when it's dry rather than wet. This will give you a more accurate idea of how the layers will look once your hair is styled.
  • Don't be afraid to make mistakes! Hair grows back and you can always visit a professional stylist to fix any mistakes.

FAQs about Cutting Layers in Medium Length Hair:

1. How do I know how high to tie my ponytail?

The height of your ponytail will determine how short the layers will be. For shorter layers, tie the ponytail higher on your head. For longer layers, tie the ponytail lower on your head.

2. Can I cut layers in curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair will bounce up after it's cut, so it's important to cut the layers longer than you want them to be.

3. Can I use this method to cut bangs?

No, this method is not recommended for cutting bangs. It's best to visit a professional stylist for a precise and even cut.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair:

Now that you've cut layers into your medium length hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain the style and health of your hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and make it appear frizzy.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your layers.
  • Consider using a hair serum or oil to add shine and tame any flyaways.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or using a hair protectant spray.

That's it! With these simple steps, you can cut layers in your medium length hair yourself using a ponytail method. Remember to take your time and start with small sections of hair to achieve the desired length. And don't forget to take care of your hair to maintain the style and health of your tresses.
