How To Frame Long Hair Around Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Long hair can be both a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it's beautiful and versatile, allowing you to experiment with a variety of styles. On the other hand, it can be overwhelming and difficult to manage, especially when it comes to framing it around your face. If you're struggling to find the best way to frame your long hair, don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll share our top tips and step-by-step guide on how to frame long hair around your face.

Description

Framing your hair around your face is an excellent way to accentuate your features and create a flattering look. Whether you have long layers or a blunt cut, framing your face can make all the difference. However, it's not always easy to get it right, particularly if you're new to styling long hair. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to create a beautiful, face-framing look that you'll love.

How to Frame Long Hair Around Face

Follow these steps to learn how to frame long hair around your face:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start, make sure your hair is clean and dry. If you have fine or thin hair, apply a volumizing product to create more body. If you have thick or curly hair, use a smoothing serum to tame any frizz.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into three sections: the crown, the sides, and the back. Use clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Start with the Sides

Take a section of hair from the front of your head and pull it forward. Hold it with one hand and use the other to snip the ends at an angle. Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Move to the Crown

Take a section of hair from the crown of your head and pull it forward. Hold it with one hand and use the other to snip the ends at an angle.

Step 5: Finish with the Back

Take a section of hair from the back of your head and pull it forward. Hold it with one hand and use the other to snip the ends at an angle.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've finished cutting, use a comb to blend the layers together.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired. You can use a curling iron or straightener to create waves or straighten your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length.
  • Use sharp, professional scissors to prevent split ends.
  • If you're not confident cutting your own hair, seek the help of a professional stylist.
  • Experiment with different angles to find the most flattering look for your face shape.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about framing long hair around your face:

Can I frame my hair around my face if I have a round face shape?

Yes, you can. However, it's best to avoid blunt cuts and opt for soft, layered styles instead.

How often should I trim my hair to maintain the face-framing look?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape.

Can I frame my hair if I have a fringe?

Yes, you can. However, it's best to leave the fringe to a professional stylist to avoid any mistakes.

Hair Care

To keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips to help you maintain your long locks:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

With these tips and step-by-step guide, you can frame your long hair around your face like a pro. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and angles to find the most flattering look for you. Happy styling!
