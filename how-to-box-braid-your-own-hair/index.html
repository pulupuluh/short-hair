How To Box Braid Your Own Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Box braids are a popular protective hairstyle that can last up to 8 weeks with proper maintenance. If you're looking to save money and time by doing it yourself, this step-by-step guide will help you achieve the perfect box braids on your own hair.

Description

Box braids are individual braids that are sectioned off into small squares or "boxes." They are created by weaving synthetic or natural hair extensions with your own hair. Box braids are popular among women of all ages and hair types because they offer versatility, low maintenance, and protection for your natural hair.

How To Box Braid Your Own Hair

Before starting, make sure you have all the necessary tools and products:

  • Rat tail comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Hair extensions (optional)
  • Braiding hair (optional)
  • Braiding beads (optional)
  • Edge control gel or cream
  • Hair oil or shea butter

Now, let's get started:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Wash and condition your hair with moisturizing products to prevent breakage and dryness. Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb and divide it into four sections. Use hair clips or ties to keep the sections separated.

Step 2: Create the Base Braids

Take a small section of hair from one of the four sections and start braiding it tightly from the root to the end. Repeat until all of your hair is in small braids. This will serve as the base for your box braids.

Step 3: Add Extensions (Optional)

If you want longer or thicker box braids, now is the time to add extensions. Take a small section of extension hair and attach it to the base braid using the braiding technique of your choice. Repeat until all of your base braids have extensions added.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small or medium-sized squares or "boxes" using a rat tail comb. Clip or tie each section to keep them separated.

Step 5: Start Braiding

Take a small section of hair from one of the boxes and split it into three strands. Begin braiding the hair in a traditional braid, but add in hair from the box as you go. Continue braiding until you reach the end of the hair or desired length.

Step 6: Secure the Ends

Once you have braided the entire section, use a small elastic band or a braiding bead to secure the end of the braid. Repeat until all of the boxes are braided.

Step 7: Style Your Edges

Apply edge control gel or cream to your hairline and use a small brush or comb to smooth down any flyaways. You can also add beads or other hair accessories to your braids for a unique look.

Step 8: Care for Your Braids

Apply hair oil or shea butter to your scalp and braids to keep them moisturized. Cover your hair with a satin bonnet or scarf at night to prevent frizz and breakage. Wash your braids every 2-3 weeks with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

Tips

  • Take breaks if your arms or hands start to feel tired or sore.
  • Avoid braiding too tightly to prevent hair breakage or scalp irritation.
  • Experiment with different braid sizes and lengths to find your preferred style.
  • Use high-quality hair extensions to prevent tangling and shedding.

FAQ

How long does it take to box braid your own hair?

The time it takes to complete box braids depends on the length and thickness of your hair, as well as the size and number of the boxes. It can take anywhere from 4-10 hours to complete box braids on your own hair.

Can I box braid my hair if it's short?

Box braids can be done on hair as short as 2-3 inches, but it may be more difficult to achieve the same length and thickness as longer hair. You can also use extensions to add length and volume to your braids.

How do I prevent my braids from unraveling?

Make sure to secure the ends of your braids with an elastic band or braiding bead. You can also use a small amount of hair gel or cream to seal the ends and prevent unraveling.

Hair Care

Maintaining your box braids is crucial to keeping your hair healthy and your style looking fresh. Here are some tips for caring for your braids:

  • Wash your braids every 2-3 weeks with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Apply hair oil or shea butter to your scalp and braids to keep them moisturized.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products or exposing your braids to excessive heat or water.
  • Cover your hair with a satin bonnet or scarf at night to prevent frizz and breakage.

Box braids are a great way to protect your hair while still looking stylish. With these step-by-step instructions and tips, you can confidently box braid your own hair at home.
