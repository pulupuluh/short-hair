How To Cut The Back Of A Bob Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a fresh new look, a bob haircut is a great option. However, going to the salon can be expensive and time-consuming. Luckily, cutting the back of a bob yourself is easier than you might think. In this article, we'll walk you through the process step by step, from choosing the right tools to finishing touches.

How to Cut the Back of a Bob Yourself

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. Comb it out so it's free of tangles.
  2. Decide how short you want the back of your bob to be. Use the comb to create a horizontal part around the back of your head where you want to make the cut. Use a hair tie or clip to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of the parted section. Use your fingers to hold the hair straight out from your head, and cut the hair to the desired length. Use this first section as a guide for the rest of the hair.
  4. Work your way up the parted section, taking small sections of hair and cutting them to the same length as the first section. Make sure you cut the hair straight across, using the first section as a guide.
  5. Once you've cut all the hair in the parted section, comb it out and check for any uneven spots. Use the scissors to trim any areas that need it.
  6. If you want a more layered look, use the comb to create angled parts instead of horizontal parts. Cut each section at a slightly different length to create layers.
  7. If you want a shorter bob, use clippers to trim the hair at the nape of your neck. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard until you reach the desired length.
  8. Release the rest of your hair and comb it out. Check for any uneven spots and trim as needed.
  9. Use the mirror to check the back of your hair from different angles, making sure it's even and the length you want.
  10. Finish with any styling you prefer, such as blow-drying or using a straightener.

Tips for Cutting the Back of a Bob Yourself

Here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to the desired length. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Keep the hair damp while cutting to make it easier to control.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging the hair.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you check the back of your hair if you're having trouble seeing it in the mirror.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting the back of a bob yourself:

Can I cut the back of a bob with just scissors?

Yes, you can cut the back of a bob with just scissors. However, clippers can help you achieve a more precise cut.

What length should I cut the back of my bob?

The length of the back of your bob depends on your personal preference. Start with a longer length and work your way down until you find a length you like.

How do I know if the back of my bob is even?

Use a mirror to check the back of your hair from different angles. You can also ask a friend or family member to help you check.

Hair Care After Cutting the Back of a Bob Yourself

After cutting the back of a bob yourself, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your bob.

With these tips and steps, you should be able to confidently cut the back of a bob yourself. Give it a try and enjoy your new look!
