Why Is My Curly Hair Frizzy And Dry

Written by Bardi Apr 03, 2023 · 7 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it can be full of volume and bounce, but on the other hand, it can be frizzy and dry. If you're asking yourself, "why is my curly hair frizzy and dry?" you're not alone. Many people with curly hair struggle with these issues. In this article, we'll explore the causes of frizzy and dry curly hair and provide tips on how to care for it.

Description

Frizzy hair occurs when the hair cuticles are raised, allowing moisture to escape and causing the hair to become dry and brittle. Curly hair is naturally drier than straight hair because the natural oils produced by the scalp have a harder time traveling down the hair shaft due to its shape. When curly hair becomes too dry, it can lose its shape and become frizzy.

Many factors can contribute to frizzy and dry curly hair, including:

  • Overwashing
  • Using the wrong hair care products
  • Using hot styling tools
  • Exposure to humidity
  • Environmental pollution
  • Genetics

How to Care for Frizzy and Dry Curly Hair

Step by Step Guide

If you're struggling with frizzy and dry curly hair, don't worry, there are things you can do to help. Here are some steps you can take to care for your curly hair:

Step 1: Use a Gentle Shampoo

Overwashing can strip your hair of its natural oils, so it's important to use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo. Look for shampoos that are specifically formulated for curly hair and avoid those that contain harsh chemicals.

Step 2: Condition, Condition, Condition

Curly hair needs moisture, so use a conditioner every time you wash your hair. Focus on the ends of your hair, where it tends to be the driest. Consider using a deep conditioner or hair masque once a week for added hydration.

Step 3: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Curly hair is prone to tangles, so use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet. Avoid using a brush, as it can cause breakage and frizz.

Step 4: Use a Microfiber Towel

Traditional towels can rough up the hair cuticle, causing frizz. Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently squeeze excess water out of your hair.

Step 5: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Once you've towel-dried your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help lock in moisture and reduce frizz. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair.

Step 6: Air Dry or Diffuse

Avoid using hot styling tools, as they can cause damage and frizz. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair gently.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you care for your frizzy and dry curly hair:

  • Don't touch your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal parts, as they can snag and break your hair.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to help reduce frizz.
  • Consider getting regular trims to prevent split ends, which can make your hair look frizzy.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about frizzy and dry curly hair:

Q: Can I use oils on my curly hair?

A: Yes, oils can be a great way to add moisture to your hair. Look for lightweight oils, such as argan oil or jojoba oil, and apply them sparingly to the ends of your hair.

Q: Should I brush my curly hair?

A: No, brushing curly hair can cause breakage and frizz. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle, but most people with curly hair can get away with washing their hair once or twice a week. Overwashing can strip your hair of its natural oils, causing it to become dry and brittle.

Hair Care

Caring for frizzy and dry curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right hair care routine, you can keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to use gentle, hydrating products and avoid using hot styling tools. With a little patience and care, you can embrace your natural curls and say goodbye to frizz and dryness.
