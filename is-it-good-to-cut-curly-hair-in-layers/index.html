Is It Good To Cut Curly Hair In Layers

Written by Aprilia Aug 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it is unique and beautiful. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. One question that often comes up is whether it is good to cut curly hair in layers. In this article, we will explore the pros and cons of layering curly hair, how to do it, and how to care for your hair afterwards.

Table of Contents

  • Pros and Cons of Layering Curly Hair
  • How to Cut Curly Hair in Layers
  • Tips for Layering Curly Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Layering Curly Hair

Pros and Cons of Layering Curly Hair

There are several reasons why you might want to consider layering your curly hair. Here are some of the pros:

  • Layers can add volume and body to limp or flat curls.
  • Layers can help distribute the weight of your hair, making it easier to manage and style.
  • Layers can create a more dynamic and textured look.

However, there are also some cons to consider:

  • Layers can create more frizz, especially if your hair is prone to it.
  • Layering too much or too aggressively can result in uneven or choppy hair.
  • Layering may not be suitable for all curl patterns or hair types.

How to Cut Curly Hair in Layers

If you decide to layer your curly hair, it is important to do it correctly to avoid any mishaps. Here are some basic steps to follow:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would.
  2. Detangle your hair gently with a wide-toothed comb or your fingers.
  3. Separate your hair into sections, with each section secured with a hair clip.
  4. Starting with the bottom layer, take a small section of hair and comb it downwards. Hold the section between your index and middle fingers, and cut the ends at an angle.
  5. Repeat this process for each layer, cutting at an angle and making sure to blend the layers together.
  6. Once you have finished layering, style your hair as desired.

It is important to note that if you are not confident in cutting your own hair, it is best to seek the help of a professional stylist.

Tips for Layering Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when layering your curly hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair, and cut gradually. You can always cut more later, but you can't undo too much hair.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid creating split ends.
  • Consider getting a dry cut, as this can help the stylist see how the curls will fall naturally.
  • Opt for longer layers if you have tighter curls, as shorter layers can create too much volume and frizz.

FAQ

How often should I layer my curly hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much you want to maintain your layers. Generally, it is recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your layers.

Will layering my curly hair make it look thinner?

No, layering can actually make your hair look fuller and more voluminous. However, it is important to not overdo it and create too many layers, as this can result in thinning hair.

Can I layer my curly hair if it is very short?

It is possible, but it may be more difficult to achieve a layered look with very short hair. It is best to consult with a stylist to see what options are available.

Hair Care After Layering Curly Hair

Once you have layered your curly hair, it is important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to avoid frizz.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to keep your curls moisturized.

Overall, layering curly hair can be a great way to add volume, texture, and manageability to your hair. However, it is important to weigh the pros and cons and take the necessary precautions to ensure a successful outcome.
