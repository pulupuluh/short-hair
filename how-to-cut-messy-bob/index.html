How To Cut Messy Bob A Relaxed Guide

Written by Christine Jul 17, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're looking for a fresh and effortless hairstyle, a messy bob might be the perfect fit for you. This style is versatile, low maintenance, and can suit any face shape. But before you go to the salon, why not try cutting your hair at home? In this article, we'll show you how to cut a messy bob in a relaxed and easy-to-follow manner.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Messy Bob
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Perfect Cut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Cut Messy Bob

A messy bob is a layered haircut that features choppy ends and uneven lengths. It's a perfect style for those who want a casual, effortless look. Here are the steps to follow:

1. Start with Dry Hair

Make sure your hair is clean, dry, and free of tangles. Wet hair tends to stretch, making it difficult to get an accurate cut. Use a comb or brush to detangle your hair before you start cutting.

2. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into three sections: the top, the sides, and the back. Clip each section separately to keep it out of the way while you work on the other parts.

3. Cut the Back

Start with the back section. Use a mirror to see the back of your head clearly. Hold a section of hair between your fingers, and cut it at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Cut small sections at a time, and avoid cutting straight across.

4. Cut the Sides

For the sides, comb your hair forward, and hold the sections between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Make sure the length of the hair on both sides is even.

5. Cut the Top

Finally, cut the top section. Comb your hair straight up, and hold the sections between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the back and working your way towards the front. Cut small sections at a time, and avoid cutting straight across.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're still unsure about cutting your hair, here's a step-by-step guide to help you:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb or brush, hair clips, and a mirror.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start cutting.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into three sections: the top, the sides, and the back. Clip each section separately to keep it out of the way while you work on the other parts.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Start with the back section. Use a mirror to see the back of your head clearly. Hold a section of hair between your fingers, and cut it at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Cut small sections at a time, and avoid cutting straight across.

Step 5: Cut the Sides

For the sides, comb your hair forward, and hold the sections between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Make sure the length of the hair on both sides is even.

Step 6: Cut the Top

Finally, cut the top section. Comb your hair straight up, and hold the sections between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the back and working your way towards the front. Cut small sections at a time, and avoid cutting straight across.

Tips for a Perfect Cut

Here are some tips to help you achieve a perfect messy bob:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Start with small sections of hair and cut slowly to avoid making mistakes.
  • Don't cut straight across. Cut at an angle to create layers and texture.
  • Don't be afraid to take breaks if you get tired or frustrated.
  • Have a backup plan in case you're not happy with the result. You can always go to a professional stylist for a fix.

FAQs

Can I cut a messy bob with curly hair?

Yes, you can. However, cutting curly hair requires a different technique. It's best to consult a professional stylist who has experience with curly hair.

Can I cut a messy bob with short hair?

Yes, you can. A messy bob is a versatile style that can work with short or long hair.

How often should I cut my messy bob?

It depends on your hair growth and how quickly your hair loses its shape. On average, you should cut your hair every six to eight weeks to maintain the style.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've cut your hair, it's essential to take care of it to keep it healthy and beautiful. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, or use a heat protectant spray before styling.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or applying a leave-in conditioner.

Now that you know how to cut a messy bob, it's time to give it a try. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun. Good luck!
