How To Cut Your Own Hair For Round Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Feb 21, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have a round face, you may be wondering how to cut your own hair to flatter your features. While it can be intimidating to take scissors to your own locks, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting your own hair for a round face, including tips for maintaining your new 'do and avoiding common pitfalls.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair for Round Face
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Successful Cut
  • FAQs

How to Cut Your Own Hair for Round Face

The first step in cutting your own hair is to prepare your tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and preferably a mirror to help you see the back of your head. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle on hand to dampen your hair as you work.

When it comes to cutting hair for a round face, the goal is to create angles and length to elongate the face. Layers can be helpful, as they can add volume and movement to the hair. However, be cautious with bangs, as they can make a round face appear shorter.

Another important consideration is the length of your hair. Shorter styles can work well for round faces, as long as they have some length around the face to create angles. Longer styles can also be flattering, as long as they are layered and have some volume at the crown.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics of cutting hair for a round face, let's walk through the process step by step:

  1. Start with dry, detangled hair. Use your comb to create a center or side part, depending on your preference.
  2. Section off the hair at the crown of your head, leaving the hair around your face loose. This will be the top layer of your hair.
  3. Using your fingers or a comb, grab a small section of hair from the front of your face. Hold the hair between your fingers or comb, and use your scissors to trim the ends at an angle. This will create a soft, face-framing layer.
  4. Repeat step 3 on the other side of your face.
  5. Move to the back of your head, and begin trimming the hair in small sections. Start with the bottom layer, and work your way up to the top layer.
  6. When trimming the back, be sure to create layers by cutting the hair at an angle. This will add volume and movement to your hair.
  7. Once you've finished cutting the back, release the top layer of hair and trim as desired. Again, be sure to create layers to add volume and movement.
  8. When you're happy with the length and style of your hair, use your comb to blend any uneven sections.
  9. Give your hair a final spritz of water, and style as desired.
  10. Admire your handiwork and enjoy your new 'do!

Tips for a Successful Cut

Cutting your own hair can be a bit nerve-wracking, but with these tips, you can achieve a beautiful, flattering cut:

  • Start with small sections of hair, and trim gradually. You can always take more off, but you can't put it back on!
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you have a friend or family member who can assist you, it can be helpful to have a second pair of eyes.
  • Take breaks as needed. Cutting hair can be tiring, so don't be afraid to step away and come back to it later.
  • Invest in high-quality scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve a clean cut.
  • When in doubt, go slow. It's better to take your time and get it right than to rush and make a mistake.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about cutting hair for round faces:

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can! However, keep in mind that curly hair can be more difficult to cut evenly. Consider working with a stylist or practicing on a small section of hair before tackling your entire head.

What if I make a mistake?

It happens to the best of us! If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim a bit more to even things out, or use hair accessories to camouflage any uneven sections.

Hair Care for Round Faces

Once you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for maintaining your new 'do:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, rather than a brush, which can cause breakage.
  • Consider using hair oils or serums to add shine and moisture to your locks.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your own hair for a round face. Whether you opt for a short pixie cut or long layers, you can achieve a flattering, stylish look from the comfort of your own home.
