How To Do A Short Layered Haircut Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Apr 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you looking to switch up your look with a short layered haircut? This versatile style is perfect for those with fine or thick hair, as it can add volume and movement to your locks. Whether you're going for a choppy, textured look or a sleek, smooth finish, a short layered haircut is a great option. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve this style at home.

Table of Contents

  • How to Do a Short Layered Haircut
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Achieving the Perfect Short Layered Haircut
  • FAQs

How to Do a Short Layered Haircut

Before you start, gather all the necessary tools and equipment:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb or brush
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Spray bottle of water

Now, you're ready to begin!

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Divide your hair into sections. Use clips or ties to hold the sections in place.

Step 3: Start with the back of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up. Use the scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting at the length you want to cut and working your way down to the ends. Repeat this step for each section of hair.

Step 4: Move on to the sides of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it away from your head. Use the same cutting technique as before, cutting at an angle from the length you want to cut to the ends of your hair. Repeat for each section of hair.

Step 5: Finally, move on to the top of your hair. Take small sections and cut at an angle, starting from the length you want to cut and working your way down to the ends. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

Step 6: Check your haircut in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure your layers are even and balanced.

Tips for Achieving the Perfect Short Layered Haircut

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start by cutting less hair than you think you need. You can always go back and cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • When cutting the sides and back, make sure to angle the scissors downward to create a tapered effect.
  • For a choppy, textured look, use a point-cutting technique. Hold the scissors vertically and cut into the hair at an angle.
  • For a sleek, smooth finish, use a blunt-cutting technique. Hold the scissors horizontally and cut straight across the hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I do a short layered haircut on curly hair?

A: Yes, short layered haircuts can work well on curly hair. Be sure to cut the hair when it's dry and cut the layers in a way that complements your natural curl pattern.

Q: How often should I get a short layered haircut?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much maintenance you're willing to do. Generally, getting a trim every 6-8 weeks will help maintain your layers and keep your haircut looking fresh.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've achieved your short layered haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. When you do use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray.
  • Invest in a good quality hairbrush or comb. Brushing your hair gently and regularly can help distribute natural oils and prevent breakage.
  • Consider using hair masks or treatments to nourish your hair and keep it healthy.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to achieve the perfect short layered haircut at home. Experiment with different cutting techniques and styles to find the look that's right for you!
