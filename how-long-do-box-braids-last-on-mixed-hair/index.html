How Long Do Box Braids Last On Mixed Hair

Written by Christine Jun 11, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Box braids are a popular hairstyle choice for many people, including those with mixed hair. These braids can last for a long time, which is one of the reasons why they are so popular. In this article, we will discuss how long box braids last on mixed hair, along with some tips for maintaining them.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How To
  3. Step By Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Box braids are a type of protective hairstyle that involves braiding the hair into small, square-shaped sections. These braids can be made using synthetic or natural hair, and they can last for several weeks or even months. The length of time that box braids last on mixed hair depends on various factors, such as the size of the braids, the quality of the hair, and how well the braids are maintained.

How To

If you want to get box braids on your mixed hair, you can either do it yourself or go to a professional stylist. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before getting box braids, it is important to wash your hair to remove any dirt or buildup. Use a clarifying shampoo to deep clean your scalp and hair.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair

After washing your hair, apply a deep conditioner to moisturize and nourish your hair. This will make it easier to braid.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small, square-shaped sections using a comb. The size of the sections will depend on the size of the braids you want.

Step 4: Braid Your Hair

Start braiding your hair from the roots, making sure to braid tightly and evenly. You can add extensions to your braids if you want them to be longer or thicker.

Step 5: Seal Your Ends

Use a lighter or hot water to seal the ends of your braids to prevent them from unraveling.

Step By Step

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to get box braids on mixed hair:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or dirt. Then, condition your hair with a deep conditioner to make it easier to braid.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small, square-shaped sections using a comb. The size of the sections will depend on the size of the braids you want.

Step 3: Add Extensions

If you want your braids to be longer or thicker, you can add extensions. Choose extensions that match the texture and color of your hair.

Step 4: Start Braiding

Start braiding your hair from the roots, making sure to braid tightly and evenly. Use a hair tie to secure the end of the braid.

Step 5: Seal Your Ends

Use a lighter or hot water to seal the ends of your braids to prevent them from unraveling.

Tips

Here are some tips for maintaining your box braids:

  • Wear a silk or satin scarf or bonnet to bed to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Wash your scalp regularly with a mild shampoo to prevent buildup and itching.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your braids.
  • When taking down your braids, be gentle to avoid damaging your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about box braids:

Q: How long do box braids last on mixed hair?

A: Box braids can last for several weeks or even months, depending on how well they are maintained.

Q: Can I wash my box braids?

A: Yes, you can wash your scalp with a mild shampoo to prevent buildup and itching. However, be gentle when washing your hair to avoid damaging your braids.

Q: How do I take down my box braids?

A: To take down your box braids, use a pair of scissors to cut the ends of the braids, then unravel the braids gently.

Hair Care

Here are some tips for overall hair care:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or oil.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

Overall, box braids can last for a long time on mixed hair if they are well-maintained. Follow these tips and steps to get the most out of your box braids.
