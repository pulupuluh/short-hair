What Can You Do When Your Hair Starts Falling Out

Written by Christine Apr 10, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you experiencing hair fall? Don't panic! Hair fall is a common problem among men and women of all ages. There could be several reasons why your hair is falling out, such as stress, hormonal changes, poor nutrition, genetics, and medication. But the good news is that there are many ways you can prevent and treat hair fall. In this article, we will discuss what you can do when your hair starts falling out.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prevent Hair Fall
  • Step-by-Step Treatment for Hair Fall
  • Tips for Hair Care
  • Frequently Asked Questions

How to Prevent Hair Fall

Preventing hair fall is all about taking good care of your hair and scalp. Here are some tips that can help:

Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough protein, vitamins, and minerals in your diet. Include foods such as eggs, fish, nuts, leafy greens, and fruits in your meals.

Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Chemicals in hair products can damage your hair and make it weak. Avoid using products that contain sulfates, parabens, and alcohol. Instead, opt for natural and organic hair care products.

Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can improve blood circulation and promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp gently for a few minutes every day.

Step-by-Step Treatment for Hair Fall

If you're already experiencing hair fall, don't worry! Here's a step-by-step treatment plan that can help:

Step 1: Identify the Cause

The first step in treating hair fall is to identify the cause. Visit a dermatologist to determine the cause of your hair fall. Once you know the cause, you can take steps to treat it.

Step 2: Cleanse Your Scalp

Use a gentle shampoo to cleanse your scalp and remove any dirt and buildup. Avoid using hot water and harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Step 3: Nourish Your Hair

Use a hair mask or conditioner to nourish your hair and keep it healthy. Look for products that contain natural ingredients such as coconut oil, argan oil, and shea butter.

Step 4: Take Supplements

Supplements can help promote hair growth and prevent hair fall. Look for supplements that contain biotin, vitamin D, and iron.

Tips for Hair Care

Here are some additional tips for hair care:

Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can prevent split ends and breakage. Aim to trim your hair every six to eight weeks.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles such as braids, ponytails, and buns can pull on your hair and cause breakage. Avoid tight hairstyles and opt for looser styles instead.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools such as curling irons and straighteners can damage your hair and make it weak. Avoid using heat styling tools or use them sparingly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q. Can stress cause hair fall?

A. Yes, stress can cause hair fall. When you're stressed, your body produces cortisol, which can weaken your hair follicles and cause hair fall.

Q. Can hair fall be reversed?

A. Yes, hair fall can be reversed with proper treatment and care. Consult a dermatologist to determine the cause of your hair fall and take steps to treat it.

Q. Can medication cause hair fall?

A. Yes, some medications can cause hair fall as a side effect. Consult your doctor if you're experiencing hair fall as a result of medication.

Remember, hair fall is a common problem that can be treated with proper care and treatment. Take care of your hair and scalp, eat a balanced diet, and seek professional help if needed. With the right approach, you can prevent hair fall and promote healthy hair growth.
