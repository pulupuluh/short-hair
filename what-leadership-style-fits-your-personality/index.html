What Leadership Style Fits Your Personality

Written by Oliver Aug 15, 2023 · 6 min read

As we go through life, we often find ourselves in positions of leadership, whether at work, in our communities, or even in our families. But not everyone is a natural-born leader, and even those who are may struggle to find the right leadership style that fits their personality. In this article, we'll explore the different leadership styles and how to determine which one is right for you.

Table of Contents:

  • What is Leadership?
  • Leadership Styles
  • Tips for Finding Your Leadership Style
  • FAQs

What is Leadership?

Leadership is the ability to guide and influence others towards a common goal. It's about being able to inspire, motivate, and empower those around you to achieve their full potential. A good leader is someone who can adapt to different situations and personalities, and who has a clear vision for the future.

Leadership Styles

There are many different leadership styles, each with its own strengths and weaknesses. Here are some of the most common:

Autocratic

This style of leadership involves a top-down approach, where the leader makes all the decisions and expects their subordinates to follow without question. It can be effective in situations where quick and decisive action is required, but can also lead to resentment and low morale among team members.

Democratic

This style of leadership involves collaboration and teamwork, with the leader involving their subordinates in the decision-making process. It can be effective in situations where creativity and innovation are required, but can also lead to indecisiveness and a lack of direction.

Laissez-faire

This style of leadership involves giving subordinates a high degree of autonomy, with the leader taking a hands-off approach. It can be effective in situations where team members are highly skilled and self-motivated, but can also lead to a lack of accountability and direction.

Servant

This style of leadership involves putting the needs of others first, with the leader acting as a servant to their subordinates. It can be effective in situations where empathy and compassion are required, but can also lead to a lack of assertiveness and direction.

Tips for Finding Your Leadership Style

So, how do you determine which leadership style is right for you? Here are some tips:

Know yourself

Take a personality test or self-assessment to get a better understanding of your strengths, weaknesses, and communication style.

Observe others

Observe other leaders in your field and take note of their strengths and weaknesses. What works for them may not work for you, but it can give you ideas.

Try different styles

Experiment with different leadership styles in different situations to see what works best for you.

Be open to feedback

Solicit feedback from your team members and be open to constructive criticism. Use it as an opportunity to grow and improve.

FAQs

Q: Can I change my leadership style?

A: Yes, your leadership style can evolve over time as you gain more experience and self-awareness.

Q: Is one leadership style better than the others?

A: No, each leadership style has its own strengths and weaknesses, and what works best for one person may not work for another.

Q: Can I use more than one leadership style?

A: Yes, it's possible to use more than one leadership style, depending on the situation and the needs of your team.


In conclusion, finding the right leadership style that fits your personality is key to being an effective leader. By knowing yourself, observing others, trying different styles, and being open to feedback, you can develop your own unique leadership style that inspires and motivates those around you.
