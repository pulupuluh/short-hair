Where Should I Part My Curly Hair

Written by Petter May 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Curly hair can be a beautiful and unique feature, but it can also be challenging to style. One of the most important decisions you'll make when styling your curly hair is where to part it. Choosing the right part can help you achieve a flattering and manageable look for your curls. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for deciding where to part your curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Part Your Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Parting Curly Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Description

The first step in deciding where to part your curly hair is to consider your face shape. The right part can help balance out your features and create a more symmetrical look. You should also consider the texture and thickness of your hair, as this can affect how your curls fall and how your part will look. Finally, think about your personal style and what kind of look you're trying to achieve.

How to Part Your Curly Hair

There are a few different techniques you can use to part your curly hair:

  • Use a comb: This is the most traditional way to part your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently separate your curls and create a part.
  • Use your fingers: If you don't want to use a comb, you can use your fingers to create a part. This can be a more natural and relaxed look.
  • Try a zig-zag part: A zig-zag part can add some visual interest to your hair and help create a more natural look. Use your comb or fingers to create a zig-zag pattern along your scalp.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for parting your curly hair:

  1. Start with clean, detangled hair.
  2. Decide where you want your part to be.
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to create your part.
  4. Gently separate your curls along your part.
  5. If you want to create a more defined part, use a rat-tail comb to make a more precise separation.
  6. If you're using a zig-zag part, create your pattern along your scalp.
  7. Fluff your curls with your fingers to create volume and shape.
  8. Finish with a light hold hairspray or curl cream to keep your curls in place.

Tips for Parting Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect part for your curly hair:

  • Experiment with different parts to find the one that works best for your face shape and hair texture.
  • Try a deep side part for a dramatic look.
  • Use a hair oil or leave-in conditioner to help detangle your curls before parting.
  • Don't be afraid to switch up your part from time to time to keep your look fresh.
  • Remember that your part doesn't have to be perfectly straight - a slightly messy or uneven part can add some character to your curls.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about parting curly hair:

What part is best for curly hair?

The best part for curly hair depends on your face shape and personal style. A deep side part can be flattering for many people, while a center part can work well for oval-shaped faces. Experiment with different parts to find the one that works best for you.

Can you change your part with curly hair?

Yes, you can change your part with curly hair. However, it may take some time for your curls to adjust to the new part. If you're used to parting your hair in the same place all the time, you may need to train your hair to fall in a different direction.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

In addition to choosing the right part, it's important to take good care of your curly hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid drying out your curls.
  • Condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls without causing damage.
  • Invest in a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent frizz.

Now that you know how to part your curly hair, you can create a variety of different looks and styles. Experiment with different parts and techniques to find the one that works best for you.
