Do Long Haired Dogs Shed More Than Short

Written by Robby May 06, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're a dog owner, you're probably familiar with the struggle of dealing with shedding. Dogs of all breeds and sizes shed their fur, but the amount of shedding can vary depending on the length of their hair. In this article, we'll answer the question "do long haired dogs shed more than short?" and provide some tips on how to manage shedding in your furry friend.

Table of Contents:

  1. Description
  2. How to Manage Shedding in Long Haired Dogs
  3. Step by Step Guide to Reducing Shedding
  4. Tips for Managing Shedding
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care for Long Haired Dogs

Description

Long haired dogs, such as Golden Retrievers, Afghan Hounds, and Old English Sheepdogs, are known for their beautiful flowing coats. However, this beauty comes at a price - long haired dogs shed more than short haired dogs. This is because their hair is longer and thicker, which means there is more surface area for hair to fall out of.

Short haired dogs, such as Beagles, Boxers, and Dalmatians, have hair that is shorter and finer. This means that they shed less than long haired dogs, but they still shed. All dogs shed to some extent, even those that are considered "hypoallergenic."

How to Manage Shedding in Long Haired Dogs

Managing shedding in long haired dogs requires a bit of effort on your part. Here are some tips:

Brush Your Dog Regularly

One of the most effective ways to manage shedding in long haired dogs is to brush them regularly. This helps to remove loose hair before it falls out and ends up all over your furniture and clothes. Use a brush that is appropriate for your dog's coat type, and be sure to brush all the way down to the skin.

Bathe Your Dog

Bathing your dog can help to remove loose hair, as well as dirt and debris that can contribute to shedding. Use a dog-specific shampoo and conditioner, and be sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid any residue that could irritate your dog's skin.

Feed Your Dog a High-Quality Diet

A healthy diet can help to reduce shedding in long haired dogs. Look for dog food that is high in protein and contains omega-3 fatty acids, which can help to support healthy skin and coat.

Step by Step Guide to Reducing Shedding:

  1. Brush your dog daily with an appropriate brush for his coat type.
  2. Bathe your dog once a month with a dog-specific shampoo and conditioner.
  3. Feed your dog a high-quality diet that is high in protein and omega-3 fatty acids.
  4. Keep your dog well hydrated by providing fresh water at all times.
  5. Vacuum your home regularly to remove loose hair.
  6. Use a lint roller or tape to remove hair from clothes and furniture.
  7. Wash your dog's bedding regularly to reduce the amount of hair on it.
  8. Consider using a shedding tool, such as a Furminator, to help remove loose hair.
  9. Visit your vet regularly to ensure your dog is healthy and to address any shedding concerns.

Tips for Managing Shedding

Here are some additional tips for managing shedding in long haired dogs:

  • Avoid shaving your dog's coat, as this can actually make shedding worse.
  • Consider using a hair catcher drain plug in your bathtub to prevent hair from clogging your drain.
  • Use a high-quality air filter in your home to reduce the amount of hair and dander in the air.
  • Consider using a dog-specific vacuum cleaner, as these are designed to pick up pet hair more effectively.

FAQs

Q: Are there any breeds of dogs that don't shed?

A: There is no such thing as a completely non-shedding dog. However, there are some breeds that shed very little, such as the Bichon Frise, Poodle, and Chinese Crested.

Q: Is shedding in dogs seasonal?

A: Yes, shedding in dogs is often seasonal. Dogs tend to shed more in the spring and fall as their coats adjust to changing temperatures and daylight hours.

Q: Can excessive shedding be a sign of a health problem?

A: Yes, excessive shedding can be a sign of an underlying health problem, such as allergies, thyroid issues, or skin conditions. If you notice your dog shedding more than usual, it's a good idea to consult with your vet.

Hair Care for Long Haired Dogs

In addition to managing shedding, it's important to take good care of your long haired dog's coat. Here are some tips:

  • Brush your dog's coat regularly to prevent matting and tangles.
  • Trim your dog's hair around the eyes, ears, and paws to prevent irritation and infection.
  • Take your dog to a professional groomer for a trim every 6-8 weeks.
  • Check your dog's coat regularly for fleas and ticks, and use preventative treatments as needed.

Managing shedding in long haired dogs requires some effort, but it's worth it to keep your furry friend healthy and your home clean. By following the tips in this article, you can reduce shedding and keep your long haired dog's coat looking and feeling great.
