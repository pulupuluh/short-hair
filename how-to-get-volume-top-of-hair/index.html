How To Get Volume At The Top Of Your Hair A Complete Guide

Written by William May 05, 2023 Â· 11 min read

Are you tired of flat, lifeless hair that refuses to hold any volume? Do you find yourself constantly trying to tease, backcomb, and spray your locks into submission, only to end up with a limp, lackluster mane? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle to achieve volume at the top of their hair, especially if they have fine, thin, or straight strands. But fear not, because with the right techniques and products, you can transform your hair from drab to fab in no time. In this post, we'll show you how to get volume at the top of your hair, step by step.

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Top Tips for Adding Volume
  • FAQs

How to Get Volume at the Top of Your Hair

Before we dive into the specifics, let's first understand what causes hair to lack volume at the top. There are several factors that can contribute to this problem, including:

  • Genetics
  • Hormonal changes
  • Nutritional deficiencies
  • Over-styling
  • Using the wrong products

If you're dealing with any of these issues, it's important to address them first. However, even if your hair is healthy and well-nourished, you may still struggle to get volume at the top. Here are some tried-and-true methods to help:

1. Tease Your Hair

Teasing, also known as backcombing or ratting, involves using a comb or brush to push hair against the direction of growth, creating a cushion of volume. To tease your hair:

  1. Take a small section of hair at the crown of your head.
  2. Hold it straight up, away from your scalp.
  3. Starting at the roots, gently backcomb the hair with a fine-toothed comb or brush.
  4. Repeat with additional sections until you achieve the desired volume.
  5. Smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section, using a brush or your fingers to create a seamless look.

Remember, teasing can be damaging to your hair if done too aggressively or frequently. Use this technique sparingly, and be sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your strands healthy.

2. Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

If you're not already using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, now is the time to start. These products are specially formulated to add body and lift to your hair, without weighing it down. Look for formulas that contain ingredients like keratin, biotin, and collagen, which can help strengthen and plump up your strands.

3. Blow-Dry Upside Down

This classic trick is a favorite among hairstylists and beauty bloggers alike. By blow-drying your hair upside down, you can create instant volume at the roots, giving your hair a fuller, more voluminous appearance. Here's how:

  1. Flip your head upside down, so that your hair is hanging down toward the floor.
  2. Using a blow dryer on medium heat, aim the nozzle at your roots and start drying your hair, using your fingers or a brush to lift and tousle the strands as you go.
  3. Continue until your hair is about 80-90% dry, then flip your head back up and finish blow-drying as usual.

For even more volume, you can also try using a round brush to lift your hair at the roots as you blow-dry.

4. Try a Root-Lifting Spray

If you're still struggling to get volume at the top of your hair, a root-lifting spray can be a game-changer. These lightweight sprays are designed to be applied directly to the roots, where they can add lift and body without weighing down the rest of your hair. Simply spray the product onto your roots, then blow-dry as usual.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics, here's a step-by-step guide to getting volume at the top of your hair:

  1. Start with clean, towel-dried hair.
  2. Apply a volumizing mousse or root-lifting spray to your roots, focusing on the crown of your head.
  3. Flip your head upside down and blow-dry your hair, using your fingers or a brush to lift and tousle the strands as you go.
  4. Once your hair is about 80-90% dry, flip your head back up and finish blow-drying as usual.
  5. For even more volume, try teasing your hair at the crown with a fine-toothed comb or brush.
  6. Smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section, using a brush or your fingers to create a seamless look.
  7. Finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold the volume in place.

Voila! You now have voluminous, bouncy hair that's sure to turn heads.

Top Tips for Adding Volume

Here are a few additional tips to help you get the most out of your hair:

  • Don't wash your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it harder to style.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, which can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.
  • Invest in a good-quality blow dryer with a diffuser attachment, which can help distribute heat evenly and reduce damage.
  • Consider getting a layered haircut, which can add natural volume and movement to your hair.
  • If you have long hair, try wearing it in a high ponytail or bun to create the illusion of volume at the crown.

FAQs

Q: Can I get volume at the top of my hair if I have curly or wavy hair?

A: Yes! The same techniques we've discussed here can work for curly or wavy hair as well. Just be sure to use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help define your curls without flattening them.

Q: How often should I tease my hair?

A: Teasing can be damaging to your hair if done too frequently, so it's best to use this technique sparingly. Once a week should be plenty.

Q: Will using a lot of hairspray help me get more volume?

A: Not necessarily. While hairspray can help hold your hair in place, using too much can weigh it down and make it look stiff and unnatural. Use a light mist of hairspray to set your style, and focus on creating volume through other techniques.

Hair Care Tips for Volume

Finally, here are some general hair care tips to keep in mind if you want to maintain volume at the top of your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't weigh down your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too much, which can break the strands and make them look flat.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep your ends looking full and healthy.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals, which can help promote hair growth and strength.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, which can damage your hair and make it harder to style.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be well on your way to achieving the voluminous, bouncy hair of your dreams. Happy styling!
