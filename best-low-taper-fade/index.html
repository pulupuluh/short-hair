Best Low Taper Fade The Ultimate Guide

Written by Petter Jun 21, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Are you looking for a stylish and trendy haircut that will keep you looking fresh? Look no further than the low taper fade! This haircut has become increasingly popular in recent years, and for good reason. It's a versatile style that works for all hair types and face shapes, and it can be easily customized to suit your personal style. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about the best low taper fade.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get a Low Taper Fade
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Low Taper Fade
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Your Low Taper Fade

Description

A low taper fade is a type of haircut where the hair gradually fades from longer to shorter, starting near the top of the head and ending near the neckline. The fade can be blended for a softer look or cut sharply for a more defined appearance. This style is versatile and can be customized to suit your preferences, whether you want a clean, professional look or a more edgy, modern vibe.

How to Get a Low Taper Fade

Getting a low taper fade is a fairly simple process, but it's important to find a skilled barber or stylist who can give you the look you want. Here are the basic steps:

Choose Your Fade Length

The first step is to decide how short you want your fade to be. A low taper fade typically starts around the temples and gradually fades down to the neckline, but you can also opt for a higher or lower fade depending on your preference.

Choose Your Haircut Style

The next step is to choose the style of haircut you want. Whether you prefer a classic crew cut, a textured crop, or a longer style like a quiff or pompadour, a low taper fade can work with any haircut.

Find a Skilled Barber or Stylist

Once you've decided on your fade length and haircut style, it's time to find a barber or stylist who can give you the look you want. Look for someone with experience cutting men's hair and who has a good understanding of different hair types and textures.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting a low taper fade:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before your appointment, make sure to wash your hair and remove any styling products. This will make it easier for your barber or stylist to work with your hair.

Step 2: Discuss Your Desired Look

Once you're in the chair, talk to your barber or stylist about the look you want. Show them pictures if you have any, and be clear about the fade length and haircut style you're going for.

Step 3: Cut the Hair on Top

Your barber or stylist will start by cutting the hair on top of your head to your desired length and style. They may use scissors or clippers depending on your hair type and texture.

Step 4: Begin the Fade

Next, your barber or stylist will begin the fade by using clippers to trim the hair around the sides and back of your head. They'll start with a longer guard and gradually work their way down to a shorter guard to create a gradual fade.

Step 5: Blend the Fade

Once the initial fade is complete, your barber or stylist will blend the fade into the hair on top of your head using scissors or clippers. This will create a seamless transition between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair on the sides and back.

Step 6: Finishing Touches

Finally, your barber or stylist will add any finishing touches necessary to create the exact look you want. This may include trimming your sideburns, cleaning up your neckline, or adding texture to your hair with scissors or a razor.

Tips for Maintaining Your Low Taper Fade

Now that you have your new low taper fade, it's important to keep it looking fresh. Here are some tips:

  • Visit your barber or stylist regularly for touch-ups and trims.
  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner designed for your hair type.
  • Style your hair with a product that provides hold and texture, like a pomade or wax.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.

FAQs

What's the difference between a low taper fade and a high taper fade?

A low taper fade starts around the temples and fades down to the neckline, while a high taper fade starts around the temples and fades up towards the crown of the head. Both styles are popular and can be customized to suit your preferences.

What's the best hair type for a low taper fade?

A low taper fade works well for all hair types, but it's particularly flattering for those with thicker, coarser hair. If you have fine or thinning hair, a low taper fade can help create the illusion of fuller, thicker hair.

Can I get a low taper fade if I have curly hair?

Absolutely! In fact, a low taper fade can look particularly stylish on those with curly hair. Just make sure to find a barber or stylist who is experienced working with curly hair and can create a fade that works with your natural texture.

Hair Care for Your Low Taper Fade

To keep your low taper fade looking its best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a high-quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Visit your barber or stylist regularly for touch-ups and trims.

With these tips and the right haircut, you'll be able to rock a stylish and trendy low taper fade with ease. Whether you're going for a classic or modern look, this haircut is sure to turn heads and keep you looking your best.
