How To Look Good In A Pixie Cut Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Jun 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're thinking of getting a pixie cut, you're not alone. The short and chic hairstyle has been a popular choice among women for years. However, pulling off a pixie cut can be a challenge. With the right tips and tricks, though, you can look amazing in your new 'do. Here's how to look good in a pixie cut.

How to

1. Find the Right Pixie Cut for Your Face Shape

Before you get a pixie cut, it's important to find the right one for your face shape. Different styles of pixie cuts suit different face shapes. For example, if you have a round face, a longer pixie cut with side-swept bangs will look best on you. If you have a heart-shaped face, a choppy pixie cut with volume on top will be the most flattering. Consult with your hairdresser to find the perfect pixie cut for you.

2. Experiment with Color

One way to make your pixie cut stand out is by experimenting with color. A bold or subtle hair color can add dimension and depth to your hair. You can go for highlights, lowlights, or a full color change. Just make sure you choose a color that complements your skin tone and your new cut.

3. Accessorize with Earrings and Headbands

A pixie cut can sometimes leave you feeling bare, but you can easily dress it up with accessories. Earrings and headbands can add some sparkle and personality to your look. Play around with different styles until you find the ones that best suit your new cut.

4. Play with Texture

Pixie cuts don't have to be boring. You can add texture to your hair by using products like texturizing spray or pomade. These products can give your hair a messy or tousled look that can be perfect for a night out or a casual day out.

5. Keep Your Pixie Cut Trimmed

To maintain your pixie cut, you'll need to get it trimmed every 4-6 weeks. This will keep your hair looking fresh and avoid any awkward growing-out stages. A well-maintained pixie cut will always look good.

Step by Step

Step 1: Get Inspired

Before you get a pixie cut, look for inspiration. Browse Pinterest, Instagram, or hairstyle magazines to get an idea of what you like. Save the pictures you like and bring them to your hairdresser. This will help them understand what you want and give you the perfect cut.

Step 2: Consult with Your Hairdresser

After you've found inspiration, schedule a consultation with your hairdresser. Discuss your face shape, lifestyle, and hair type to find the perfect pixie cut for you. Be open to your hairdresser's suggestions and trust their expertise.

Step 3: Prepare for the Cut

Before getting your hair cut, it's important to prepare. Wash your hair and avoid using any hair products. Wear comfortable clothing and bring a picture of the hairstyle you want.

Step 4: Style Your Pixie Cut

After your hair cut, style your pixie cut to perfection. Use a texturizing spray or pomade to add texture to your hair. Use a hair dryer and a round brush to add volume to the top of your hair. Accessorize with earrings or headbands for a touch of personality.

Tips

- Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid drying out your scalp.

- Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.

- Use a heat protectant before using any heat styling tools.

- Try different hair accessories until you find the ones that suit your new cut.

- Have patience during the growing-out stage.

FAQ

Q: Can anyone pull off a pixie cut?

A: Yes, anyone can pull off a pixie cut. It's all about finding the right cut for your face shape and personal style.

Q: Will a pixie cut make my face look rounder?

A: No, a pixie cut can actually make your face look slimmer. A shorter cut can draw attention to your facial features and elongate your neck.

Q: How can I make my pixie cut last longer?

A: To make your pixie cut last longer, try using dry shampoo in between washes. This will help absorb any excess oil and keep your hair looking fresh.

Hair Care

After getting a pixie cut, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your scalp. Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized. Use a heat protectant before using any heat styling tools. Trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your cut. With proper care, your pixie cut will look good for weeks to come.
