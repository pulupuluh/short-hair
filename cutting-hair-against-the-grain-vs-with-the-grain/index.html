Cutting Hair Against The Grain Vs With The Grain

Written by Petter Jun 17, 2023 Â· 8 min read

When it comes to cutting hair, there are two different techniques that can be used: cutting against the grain and cutting with the grain. Both techniques have their pros and cons, and it's important to understand the differences between them in order to achieve the desired results. In this article, we will explore the differences between cutting hair against the grain and cutting hair with the grain, as well as provide tips and advice for both techniques.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Cutting hair against the grain means cutting the hair in the opposite direction of its natural growth. This technique is often used to achieve a closer and smoother cut, as it allows the hair to be cut at a shorter length. Cutting with the grain, on the other hand, means cutting the hair in the same direction as its natural growth. This technique is often used to maintain length and shape, as well as to prevent the hair from becoming too short or damaged.

How to

Before deciding whether to cut hair against or with the grain, it's important to understand the texture and growth pattern of the hair. For example, curly or wavy hair may require a different technique than straight hair. Additionally, it's important to consider the desired length and style of the hair, as well as the tools and products that will be used.

Tools and Products

When cutting hair against the grain, it's important to use sharp scissors or clippers, as well as a comb or brush to help guide the hair. Additionally, a styling product such as pomade or gel may be used to help achieve the desired style and hold the hair in place. When cutting with the grain, a comb and scissors or clippers may be used, as well as a styling product to maintain the shape and length of the hair.

Step by Step

Cutting Against the Grain

1. Start by sectioning off the hair into manageable sections.

2. Use a comb to lift the hair and hold it at a 90-degree angle away from the head.

3. Use sharp scissors or clippers to cut the hair in the opposite direction of its natural growth.

4. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working from the back of the head to the front.

5. Use a styling product to achieve the desired style and hold the hair in place.

Cutting With the Grain

1. Start by sectioning off the hair into manageable sections.

2. Use a comb to lift the hair and hold it in the direction of its natural growth.

3. Use sharp scissors or clippers to trim the hair, following the natural shape and length of the hair.

4. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working from the back of the head to the front.

5. Use a styling product to maintain the shape and length of the hair.

Tips

- Always use sharp scissors or clippers to prevent damage to the hair.

- Section the hair off into manageable sections to ensure even and precise cutting.

- Use a comb or brush to help guide the hair and prevent tangles.

- Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven or choppy results.

- When cutting against the grain, be mindful of the desired length and style of the hair.

FAQ

Q: Is cutting hair against the grain bad for the hair?

A: Cutting hair against the grain can be beneficial for achieving a closer and smoother cut, but it should be done carefully and with the right tools to prevent damage to the hair.

Q: Can cutting hair with the grain help prevent split ends?

A: Yes, cutting hair with the grain can help maintain the shape and length of the hair, which in turn can help prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After cutting hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and appearance. This includes washing and conditioning the hair regularly, using a heat protectant when styling with heat tools, and avoiding harsh chemicals or treatments that can damage the hair. Additionally, trimming the hair every 6-8 weeks can help maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Whether cutting hair against the grain or with the grain, it's important to understand the differences between these techniques and choose the one that is best suited for the desired style and length of the hair. With the right tools, technique, and care, anyone can achieve a great haircut that looks and feels amazing.
