How To Thin Short Hair Without Thinning Scissors

Written by Petter Sep 04, 2023 · 8 min read

Do you have short hair that's thick and heavy? Are you looking for ways to thin it out without using thinning scissors? Thinning scissors can be expensive and not everyone has access to them. Luckily, there are other ways to thin out your hair without using scissors. In this article, we will show you how to thin short hair without thinning scissors.

How to Thin Short Hair Without Thinning Scissors

There are several ways to thin out your hair without using thinning scissors. These include:

  • Using a razor
  • Using thinning shears
  • Using a comb and scissors
  • Using a texturizing spray

Using a Razor

One way to thin out your hair is by using a razor. This method can be a bit tricky, so it's important to use a razor that's specifically designed for hair. Here are the steps:

  1. Wash your hair and let it dry naturally.
  2. Section your hair into small sections.
  3. Hold your hair between your fingers and use the razor to cut it at an angle.
  4. Repeat this process for each section of hair until you achieve the desired thinness.

It's important to be careful when using a razor, as it can be easy to cut too much hair off.

Using Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that have teeth on one blade. They're designed to cut some of the hair while leaving some behind, which can help to thin out your hair. Here are the steps:

  1. Wash your hair and let it dry naturally.
  2. Section your hair into small sections.
  3. Hold your hair between your fingers and use the thinning shears to cut it at an angle.
  4. Repeat this process for each section of hair until you achieve the desired thinness.

Thinning shears can be a bit expensive, but they're a great investment if you want to thin out your hair on a regular basis.

Using a Comb and Scissors

If you don't have a razor or thinning shears, you can use a comb and scissors to thin out your hair. Here are the steps:

  1. Wash your hair and let it dry naturally.
  2. Section your hair into small sections.
  3. Hold your hair between your fingers and use the comb to comb through it.
  4. Use the scissors to cut the hair that's sticking out of the comb.
  5. Repeat this process for each section of hair until you achieve the desired thinness.

This method can be a bit time-consuming, but it's a great option if you don't have any other tools available.

Using a Texturizing Spray

Another way to thin out your hair is by using a texturizing spray. These sprays are designed to add texture and volume to your hair, which can help to thin it out. Here are the steps:

  1. Wash your hair and let it dry naturally.
  2. Spray the texturizing spray onto your hair, focusing on the areas where you want to thin it out.
  3. Use your fingers to work the spray into your hair.
  4. Repeat this process until you achieve the desired thinness.

Texturizing sprays can be a bit expensive, but they're a great option if you want to add some texture to your hair as well.

Tips for Thinning Short Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your short hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up to more if needed.
  • Be careful not to cut too much hair off at once.
  • Take breaks if your hands get tired.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head.
  • Be patient – thinning out your hair can take some time.

FAQs

Can I thin out my hair with regular scissors?

You can technically use regular scissors to thin out your hair, but it's not recommended. Regular scissors can create choppy, uneven layers that don't look natural.

Can I thin out my hair with a razor blade?

If you don't have a razor specifically designed for hair, you can use a razor blade to thin out your hair. However, be very careful as it's easy to cut too much hair off.

Will thinning out my hair make it grow back thicker?

No, thinning out your hair will not make it grow back thicker. It may appear thicker at first, but this is because the hair is more voluminous due to the layers.

Hair Care after Thinning Out Your Hair

After thinning out your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too much, as this can cause breakage.

Thinning out your hair can be a great way to reduce its weight and create a more voluminous look. With these tips and tricks, you can achieve a perfectly thinned-out hairstyle without using thinning scissors.
