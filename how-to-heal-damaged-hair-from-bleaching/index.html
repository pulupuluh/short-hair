How to Heal Damaged Hair from Bleaching

If you've bleached your hair, you may have noticed that your hair becomes dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. Bleaching can damage your hair, but you can heal it. In this article, we'll discuss how to heal damaged hair from bleaching.

Table of Contents

  • How to Heal Damaged Hair from Bleaching
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Further Damage
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Damaged Hair

How to Heal Damaged Hair from Bleaching

The first step in healing damaged hair from bleaching is to stop bleaching your hair. If you continue to bleach your hair, you'll only worsen the damage. Once you've stopped bleaching your hair, you can begin the healing process.

The most important thing you can do to heal your hair is to moisturize it. Bleaching removes the natural oils from your hair, so you need to replace them. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to add moisture to your hair.

You should also avoid heat styling tools, such as blow dryers and flat irons. Heat can further damage your hair, so it's best to air dry your hair and avoid styling tools until your hair has healed.

Trimming your hair regularly will also help to remove damaged hair and promote healthy hair growth.

Step by Step Guide

Here are the steps you can take to heal damaged hair from bleaching:

  1. Stop bleaching your hair
  2. Deep condition your hair once a week
  3. Avoid heat styling tools
  4. Trim your hair regularly

Tips to Prevent Further Damage

Here are some tips to prevent further damage:

  • Avoid chlorine and salt water
  • Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray when spending time in the sun
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about healing damaged hair from bleaching:

Q: Can I dye my hair after bleaching it?

A: It's best to wait until your hair has fully healed before dyeing it again. Dyeing your hair can further damage it, so it's important to wait until your hair is healthy.

Q: How long does it take to heal damaged hair?

A: It depends on the extent of the damage. It can take several months to a year for your hair to fully heal.

Hair Care for Damaged Hair

Once your hair has healed, it's important to take good care of it to prevent further damage. Here are some tips for hair care for damaged hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid heat styling tools
  • Deep condition your hair once a week
  • Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair

By following these tips, you can keep your hair healthy and prevent further damage.
