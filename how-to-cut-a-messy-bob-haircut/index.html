How To Cut A Messy Bob Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Description

A messy bob haircut is a popular hairstyle that gives a relaxed and effortless look. This haircut is perfect for those who want a low-maintenance hairstyle that still looks chic and stylish. However, cutting a messy bob can be tricky, especially if you're not a professional hairstylist. In this article, we'll show you how to cut a messy bob haircut step-by-step, along with tips and tricks to achieve the perfect messy bob.

How to Cut a Messy Bob Haircut

The first step to cutting a messy bob haircut is to gather all the necessary tools, including a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also important to have a clear idea of the length and style you want to achieve.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by shampooing and conditioning the hair to ensure it's clean and tangle-free.

Step 2: Part the hair in the middle and clip the hair on both sides to keep them out of the way.

Step 3: Take a small section of hair from the back and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to cut the hair straight across at the desired length.

Step 4: Continue cutting small sections of hair at the back, working your way up to the crown of the head. Keep the hair at the back slightly longer than the hair at the front.

Step 5: Unclip one side of the hair and comb it straight down. Cut the hair at an angle, starting at the back and working your way to the front. Repeat on the other side.

Step 6: Cut the hair at the front to frame the face. Use a point-cutting technique to create a choppy and textured look.

Step 7: Blend the hair at the back with the hair at the sides using a comb and scissors. Use the point-cutting technique to create a textured and messy look.

Step 8: Use your fingers to texturize the hair and create a messy look. You can also use a texturizing spray or pomade to add more texture and hold.

Tips for Cutting a Messy Bob Haircut

Tip 1: Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean and precise cut.

Tip 2: Cut the hair slightly longer than the desired length, as the hair will naturally bounce up after cutting.

Tip 3: Use the point-cutting technique to create texture and movement in the hair.

Tip 4: Cut the hair dry to see the final length and shape of the haircut.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut a messy bob haircut on my own?

A: It's possible to cut a messy bob haircut on your own, but it's recommended to have a friend or family member assist you. It's also important to have the right tools and a clear idea of the length and style you want to achieve.

Q: How often should I get a messy bob haircut?

A: It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style of a messy bob haircut.

Hair Care After Cutting a Messy Bob Haircut

After cutting a messy bob haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and moisturized. You can also use a texturizing spray or pomade to add more texture and hold. Avoid using heat styling tools frequently, as they can damage the hair and cause split ends. Instead, let the hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on a low heat setting.
