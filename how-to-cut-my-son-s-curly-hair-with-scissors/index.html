How To Cut My Son s Curly Hair With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a parent of a curly-haired child, you know that maintaining their hair can be a challenge. One of those challenges is cutting their hair. You want a haircut that looks good, but you also want to avoid a disaster. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your son's curly hair with scissors. So, let's get started!

Description

This article will provide helpful tips and tricks for parents who want to cut their son's curly hair with scissors. We will cover all the essential steps to ensure a successful haircut, from choosing the right tools to finishing touches. Plus, we will provide you with some useful hair care tips to maintain your child's curly hair.

How to

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step is to gather all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. Make sure the scissors are sharp enough to make clean cuts.

Step 2: Wash and Condition the Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your child's hair is clean and conditioned. Wet hair is easier to work with, so it's best to wash your child's hair beforehand. Use a conditioner that's specifically designed for curly hair to keep it moisturized and tangle-free.

Step 3: Part the Hair

Use a comb to part your child's hair in the middle. Clip one side of the hair to keep it out of the way while you work on the other side.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Hold a small section of hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle. Remember to cut less hair than you think you need, as curly hair tends to shrink up after drying. Continue this process, working from the front to the back of the head, and do the same on the other side.

Step 5: Check for Symmetry

After you've finished cutting both sides, check for symmetry. Comb through the hair to see if the length is even on both sides. If not, trim the longer side until they are even.

Step 6: Trim the Ends

To give your child's hair a more polished look, trim the ends. Take a small section of hair and twist it around your fingers, trimming the split ends that stick out. Be careful not to cut too much hair.

Step 7: Finish the Haircut

After you've trimmed the ends, use a comb to style your child's hair. If you want to add some texture, use the scissors to cut into the hair at an angle. This will give your child's hair a more natural look.

Tips

  • Start by cutting a small amount of hair and gradually cut more if needed.
  • Use a spray bottle to dampen the hair if it starts to dry out while you're cutting.
  • Be patient and take breaks if your child becomes restless.
  • Don't cut your child's hair too short, as it may look uneven when it dries.

FAQ

Can I cut my child's hair with regular scissors?

It's best to use scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair. Regular scissors may not be sharp enough to create clean cuts, which can result in uneven hair.

How often should I cut my child's curly hair?

It depends on how fast your child's hair grows. On average, curly hair should be trimmed every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

What's the best way to maintain my child's curly hair?

Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that's specifically designed for curly hair. Avoid brushing curly hair when it's dry, as it can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to detangle it when it's wet. Also, avoid using heat styling tools as they can damage curly hair.

Hair Care

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking good. Here are some tips to maintain your child's curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that's designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing curly hair when it's dry, as it can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle curly hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as they can damage curly hair.
  • Deep condition your child's hair at least once a month.

By following these tips and the steps outlined in this article, you can give your child a great haircut while keeping their curly hair healthy and beautiful.
