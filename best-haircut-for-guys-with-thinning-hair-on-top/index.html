Best Haircut For Guys With Thinning Hair On Top A Complete Guide

Written by Bardi Mar 29, 2023 Â· 11 min read

Are you one of the many guys dealing with thinning hair on top? Don't worry, you're not alone. In fact, according to the American Hair Loss Association, two-thirds of American men will experience hair loss by the age of 35. But the good news is that there are plenty of hairstyles that can help you conceal your thinning hair and still look stylish. In this article, we'll discuss the best haircut for guys with thinning hair on top, as well as some tips and tricks for keeping your hair healthy and strong.

  • Step-by-Step Guide to Getting the Perfect Haircut
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Guys with Thinning Hair

How to Choose the Best Haircut for Guys with Thinning Hair on Top

When it comes to choosing the best haircut for guys with thinning hair on top, there are a few key factors to consider:

1. Hair Texture

The texture of your hair plays a big role in determining which haircut will work best for you. If you have fine, thin hair, you'll want to avoid styles that are too long or heavy, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look even thinner. Instead, opt for shorter, layered styles that add volume and dimension.

2. Face Shape

Your face shape can also influence which haircut will be most flattering. If you have a round face, you may want to avoid styles that are too short or flat on top, as they can make your face appear even rounder. Instead, opt for styles that add height and volume to the crown of your head, such as a pompadour or quiff. If you have a square or angular face, you can pull off a wider range of styles, including buzz cuts and crew cuts.

3. Lifestyle

Your lifestyle can also play a role in determining which haircut will work best for you. If you're a busy professional who doesn't have a lot of time to spend styling your hair, you may want to opt for a low-maintenance style that can be easily tousled with your fingers. If you're more style-conscious and enjoy experimenting with different looks, you may want to go for a more complex style that requires regular maintenance.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting the Perfect Haircut

Once you've decided on the best haircut for your hair type, face shape, and lifestyle, it's time to head to the barber. Here's a step-by-step guide to getting the perfect haircut:

Step 1: Choose a Barber You Trust

Before you get started, it's important to choose a barber you trust. Look for someone who has experience working with thinning hair and can offer you advice on the best style for your hair type and face shape.

Step 2: Communicate Your Needs Clearly

Once you're in the chair, be sure to communicate your needs clearly. Let your barber know that you're dealing with thinning hair on top and that you're looking for a style that will help conceal it. Be specific about the length and texture you're looking for, and don't be afraid to ask questions if you're unsure about anything.

Step 3: Consider Adding Layers

If you have fine, thin hair, your barber may recommend adding layers to your haircut. Layers can help create the illusion of thickness and volume, and can also make your hair easier to style.

Step 4: Style Your Hair at Home

Once your haircut is complete, your barber will likely style your hair for you. Be sure to ask for tips on how to style your hair at home, as this will help you maintain your new look and keep your hair looking healthy and strong.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

In addition to choosing the right haircut, there are a few other things you can do to keep your hair looking healthy and strong:

1. Use the Right Products

Using the right hair products can make a big difference in the health and appearance of your hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for thinning hair, such as volumizing shampoos and conditioners, and avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol.

2. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Heat can damage your hair and make it look even thinner. To protect your hair, avoid using hot styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, or use them sparingly and always use a heat protectant spray.

3. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your hair look even thinner. Aim to get a trim every 4-6 weeks, or as recommended by your barber.

FAQ

Q: Can I still have long hair if I'm dealing with thinning hair on top?

A: While long hair can be a great look for some guys, it's generally not recommended for those dealing with thinning hair on top. Long hair can weigh down your hair and make it look even thinner, so it's best to opt for shorter styles that add volume and dimension.

Q: Will coloring my hair help conceal my thinning hair?

A: Coloring your hair can be a great way to add depth and dimension to thinning hair, but it won't necessarily make it look thicker. If you're considering coloring your hair, be sure to talk to your barber about the best options for your hair type and skin tone.

Hair Care Tips for Guys with Thinning Hair

Finally, here are a few more hair care tips to help you keep your hair looking healthy and strong:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can help promote healthy hair growth. Make sure to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, and buns can put stress on your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Avoid these styles if possible, or wear them in a looser style.

3. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Be gentle with your hair when you're washing and styling it. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on it.

By following these tips and choosing the right haircut, you can conceal your thinning hair and still look stylish and confident. Remember, hair loss is a common issue that affects many men, so don't be afraid to talk to your barber or a hair care professional for advice and support.
