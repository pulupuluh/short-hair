What Should I Do If My Hair Is Falling Out A Lot

Written by Christine Jun 10, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Experiencing hair loss can be a frustrating and worrying experience, especially when it happens in large amounts. If you're struggling with hair loss, you're not alone. In fact, it's estimated that around 80 million Americans experience hair loss at some point in their lives. But what can you do if your hair is falling out a lot? In this article, we'll explore some of the steps you can take to help manage and prevent hair loss.

How to Identify Hair Loss

Before you can address your hair loss, it's important to understand whether or not you're actually experiencing it. Hair loss can be caused by a number of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, stress, and certain medical conditions. Here are some common signs of hair loss:

  • Thinning hair on the crown or temples
  • A receding hairline
  • Bald patches
  • An increase in hair shedding or hair falling out in clumps

If you're experiencing any of these symptoms, it's important to consult with a healthcare professional to determine the underlying cause of your hair loss.

FAQ About Hair Loss

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair loss:

  • Is hair loss normal? It's normal to lose around 50 to 100 hairs a day, but if you're losing more than that, it could be a sign of hair loss.
  • Can stress cause hair loss? Yes, stress can contribute to hair loss.
  • Is hair loss permanent? It depends on the underlying cause of your hair loss. Some types of hair loss are permanent, while others are temporary.
  • What can I do to prevent hair loss? Maintaining a healthy diet and lifestyle, reducing stress, and avoiding harsh chemical treatments can help prevent hair loss.

Steps You Can Take to Manage Hair Loss

If you're experiencing hair loss, there are steps you can take to help manage it:

  1. Consult with a healthcare professional: A healthcare professional can help determine the underlying cause of your hair loss and recommend appropriate treatment options.
  2. Maintain a healthy diet: Eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth.
  3. Reduce stress: Stress can contribute to hair loss, so finding ways to manage stress can help reduce hair loss.
  4. Avoid harsh chemical treatments: Chemical treatments, such as perms and straightening treatments, can damage hair and contribute to hair loss.
  5. Consider hair loss treatments: There are a variety of hair loss treatments available, including medications, hair transplant surgery, and laser therapy.

Tips for Hair Care

Maintaining healthy hair can help prevent hair loss. Here are some tips for healthy hair care:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling and brushing.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather.
  • Get regular haircuts to prevent split ends and breakage.

When to Seek Professional Help

If you're experiencing severe hair loss or if your hair loss is causing you significant distress, it's important to seek professional help. A healthcare professional or hair loss specialist can help determine the underlying cause of your hair loss and recommend appropriate treatment options. Additionally, if you're experiencing sudden or rapid hair loss, it's important to seek medical attention to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Conclusion

If you're experiencing hair loss, it can be a frustrating and worrying experience. However, there are steps you can take to help manage and prevent hair loss. By consulting with a healthcare professional, maintaining a healthy diet and lifestyle, reducing stress, and avoiding harsh chemical treatments, you can promote healthy hair growth and prevent further hair loss. Additionally, by taking good care of your hair, you can help prevent damage and breakage, which can also contribute to hair loss.
