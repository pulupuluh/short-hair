Which Haircut Suits Me Female A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Jul 09, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you tired of your current hairstyle and wondering which haircut suits you best? Choosing the perfect haircut can be a daunting task, especially if you’re not sure which style will flatter your face shape, hair type, and personal style. In this blog post, we’ll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to determine which haircut suits you best as a female, along with some tips on how to care for your new hairstyle and address frequently asked questions.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before you can determine which haircut suits you best, it’s important to identify your face shape. There are six common face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and examine your face in the mirror. Use a tape measure or ruler to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline, and the length of your face from hairline to chin. Once you have these measurements, you can compare them to the following guidelines:

  • Oval: Forehead is slightly wider than the chin, and cheekbones are the widest part of the face.
  • Round: Face is almost as wide as it is long, with full cheeks and a rounded chin.
  • Square: Forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are all about the same width, with an angular jaw.
  • Heart: Forehead is wider than the cheeks and jawline, with a pointed chin.
  • Diamond: Face is widest at the cheekbones, with a narrow forehead and jawline.
  • Oblong: Face is longer than it is wide, with a long, narrow chin and forehead.

Once you’ve identified your face shape, you can start exploring hairstyles that flatter your features and enhance your natural beauty.

Choosing the Right Haircut for Your Face Shape

Now that you’ve identified your face shape, it’s time to choose a haircut that will complement your features and enhance your natural beauty. Here are some general guidelines:

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, you’re in luck! This versatile face shape looks great with almost any haircut, from a pixie cut to long layers. However, you may want to avoid styles that add too much volume on top, as this can make your face appear longer.

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, you’ll want to choose a haircut that adds height and length to your face. A long, layered cut or a side-swept bang can create the illusion of a slimmer face. Avoid blunt cuts and chin-length bobs, which can make your face appear wider.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, you’ll want to soften your angles with a haircut that adds layers and texture. A long, layered cut or a side-swept bang can flatter your features and create a more balanced look. Avoid blunt cuts and short, choppy styles, which can make your face appear boxy.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart face shape, you’ll want to choose a haircut that adds width to your forehead and temples, while softening your chin. A long, layered cut with side-swept bangs can flatter your features and create a more balanced look. Avoid blunt cuts and short, choppy styles, which can make your chin appear pointy.

Diamond Face Shape

If you have a diamond face shape, you’ll want to choose a haircut that adds width to your forehead and jawline, while softening your cheekbones. A long, layered cut or a side-swept bang can flatter your features and create a more balanced look. Avoid blunt cuts and short, choppy styles, which can make your cheekbones appear wider.

Oblong Face Shape

If you have an oblong face shape, you’ll want to choose a haircut that adds width to your face and creates the illusion of a shorter face. A long, layered cut or a side-swept bang can flatter your features and create a more balanced look. Avoid blunt cuts and chin-length bobs, which can make your face appear longer.

Tips for Maintaining Your New Haircut

Once you’ve chosen your new haircut, it’s important to care for it properly to maintain its shape and style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are appropriate for your hair type.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine.
  • Consider using hair masks and treatments to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Frequently Asked Questions

What haircut suits a small face?

If you have a small face, you’ll want to choose a haircut that doesn’t overwhelm your features. A short, layered cut or a long bob can flatter your features and create a more balanced look.

What haircut suits a big forehead?

If you have a big forehead, you’ll want to choose a haircut that adds width to your forehead and temples. A long, layered cut with side-swept bangs can flatter your features and create a more balanced look.

What haircut suits a chubby face?

If you have a chubby face, you’ll want to choose a haircut that adds height and length to your face. A long, layered cut or a side-swept bang can create the illusion of a slimmer face.

Choosing the perfect haircut can be a challenge, but with these guidelines, you’ll be able to find a style that flatters your features and enhances your natural beauty. Remember to care for your hair properly to maintain its shape and style, and enjoy your new look!
