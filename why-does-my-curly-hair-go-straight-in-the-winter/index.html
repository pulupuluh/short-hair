Why Does My Curly Hair Go Straight In The Winter

Written by Petter Mar 09, 2023 · 11 min read

Winter is a beautiful season, but it can be a nightmare for curly hair. The cold, dry air can wreak havoc on your curls, causing them to lose their shape and become straight. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why curly hair goes straight in the winter, and offer some tips on how to keep your curls looking their best.

  • Heat Styling

Dry Air

One of the main reasons why curly hair goes straight in the winter is because of the dry air. During the winter months, the air is colder and drier, which can cause your hair to lose its natural moisture. When your hair is dry, it becomes more prone to breakage and damage, which can lead to straighter hair.

In addition to the dry air, indoor heating can also contribute to the problem. Heating systems can dry out the air in your home, which can make your hair even drier. To combat this, you can invest in a humidifier, which will add moisture back into the air and help keep your curls from going straight.

Heat Styling

Another reason why curly hair goes straight in the winter is because of heat styling. When it's cold outside, it's tempting to use hot tools to style your hair. However, heat styling can be extremely damaging to your hair, especially if you don't use heat protectant products.

The heat from flat irons and curling irons can cause your hair to lose its natural curl pattern, resulting in straighter hair. To protect your curls, try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. If you must use them, be sure to use a heat protectant product and keep the temperature low.

Lack of Moisture

In addition to the dry air, curly hair can also lose moisture during the winter months. This can be due to a lack of hydration, as well as the use of harsh shampoos and styling products that strip your hair of its natural oils.

To combat this, it's important to use moisturizing products that are designed for curly hair. Look for shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that contain natural oils and ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and argan oil. These ingredients will help keep your hair moisturized and prevent it from going straight.

Static Electricity

One of the most frustrating things about winter is static electricity. This can be especially problematic for curly hair, as it can cause your curls to lose their shape and become straight.

To combat static, try using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil. These products will help weigh your hair down and prevent it from becoming too staticky. You can also try using a metal comb or brush, which will help reduce static electricity.

How To Keep Curls In Winter

Now that we've explored some of the reasons why curly hair goes straight in the winter, let's talk about how to keep your curls looking their best. Here are some tips:

1. Moisturize

As we mentioned earlier, it's important to keep your hair moisturized during the winter months. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a hair mask once a week to give your hair an extra boost of hydration.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

If possible, try to avoid heat styling your hair during the winter months. If you must use hot tools, be sure to use a heat protectant product and keep the temperature low.

3. Use A Humidifier

Invest in a humidifier to add moisture back into the air. This will help keep your hair from becoming too dry and losing its natural curl pattern.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to keeping your curls looking their best in the winter:

Step 1: Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.

Step 2: Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help weigh your hair down.

Step 3: Let your hair air dry, or use a diffuser to dry your hair on a low heat setting.

Step 4: If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant product and keep the temperature low.

Tips for Curly Hair Care

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your curly hair:

1. Don't Overwash

Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so it's important not to overwash. Try to wash your hair no more than two or three times a week.

2. Use A Wide-Tooth Comb

When detangling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to prevent breakage and damage to your curls.

3. Sleep On A Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help reduce frizz and prevent breakage.

FAQs

Q: Can I still use gel and mousse on my curly hair in the winter?

A: Yes, you can still use gel and mousse on your curly hair in the winter. Just be sure to use products that are designed for curly hair and contain moisturizing ingredients.

Q: Will wearing a hat make my hair go straight?

A: Wearing a hat can cause your hair to lose its shape and become straight, especially if the hat is tight or made of a rough material. To prevent this, try wearing a satin or silk-lined hat, or wrap your hair in a silk scarf before putting on your hat.

Q: How often should I deep condition my curly hair?

A: It's a good idea to deep condition your curly hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

Q: Can I use a hair straightener on my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can use a hair straightener on your curly hair, but be sure to use a heat protectant product and keep the temperature low. It's also important to remember that using a straightener too often can damage your hair and cause it to lose its natural curl pattern.

Winter can be tough on curly hair, but with the right care and products, you can keep your curls looking their best. Remember to moisturize, avoid heat styling as much as possible, and use a humidifier to add moisture back into the air. By following these tips, you can prevent your curly hair from going straight in the winter.
