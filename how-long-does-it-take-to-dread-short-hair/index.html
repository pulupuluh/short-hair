How Long Does It Take To Dread Short Hair

Written by Eveline Apr 03, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you considering dreadlocks for your short hair? One of the most common questions that people ask is how long it takes to dread short hair. The answer, however, is not as simple as you might think. In this blog post, we will discuss the factors that affect the time it takes to dread short hair, along with some tips on how to achieve and maintain healthy dreadlocks.

Table of Contents

  • Factors that Affect the Time it Takes to Dread Short Hair
  • How to Dread Short Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Dread Short Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Dreadlocks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Factors that Affect the Time it Takes to Dread Short Hair

Several factors affect the time it takes to dread short hair, including hair texture, thickness, and length. Generally, hair that is thicker and coarser will take longer to dread than hair that is thin and straight. Longer hair also takes longer to dread than shorter hair.

The method used to create dreadlocks also affects the time it takes to form them. Some methods, such as the backcombing method, can create dreadlocks more quickly than other methods, but they can also cause more damage to the hair.

Another factor that affects the time it takes to dread short hair is the maintenance routine. Proper maintenance, including regular washing, separating, and palm rolling, can speed up the process of forming dreadlocks.

How to Dread Short Hair

There are several methods for dreading short hair, including backcombing, twisting, and the neglect method. However, it is important to choose a method that is suitable for your hair type and texture.

The backcombing method involves teasing sections of hair to create tangles, which are then palm rolled to form dreadlocks. This method can be done quickly but can also cause damage to the hair.

The twisting method involves dividing hair into small sections and twisting them to create dreadlocks. This method is gentler on the hair but can take longer to form dreadlocks.

The neglect method involves simply letting your hair grow and tangle naturally. This method can take the longest to form dreadlocks but is the least damaging to the hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Dread Short Hair

If you are considering dreading your short hair, here is a step-by-step guide to the backcombing method:

  1. Wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo.
  2. Divide your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties.
  3. Backcomb each section by holding it taut and combing it towards your scalp, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  4. After backcombing, roll each section between your palms to form a dreadlock.
  5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 for each section of hair, making sure that each dreadlock is of similar size and thickness.
  6. Once all sections are backcombed and rolled, use a crochet hook to pull in any loose strands of hair and tighten the dreadlocks.
  7. After tightening, continue to palm roll each dreadlock regularly to maintain their shape.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Dreadlocks

Maintaining healthy dreadlocks is essential to keep them looking good and prevent damage to your hair. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup.
  • Separate your dreadlocks regularly to prevent them from growing together.
  • Palm roll your dreadlocks regularly to maintain their shape.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup in your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and heat to prevent dryness and damage.

FAQs

How long does it take to dread short hair?

The time it takes to dread short hair depends on several factors, including hair texture, thickness, and length, as well as the method used to create the dreadlocks. Generally, it can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months to form fully matured dreadlocks.

Do I need to cut my hair to start dreadlocks?

No, you do not need to cut your hair to start dreadlocks. You can begin the process with any length of hair, although shorter hair may take longer to form mature dreadlocks.

Can I wash my hair with regular shampoo after dreading?

No, it is not recommended to use regular shampoo on dreadlocks, as it can leave residue and cause buildup in your hair. Use a residue-free shampoo specially formulated for dreadlocks instead.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Caring for dreadlocks requires a bit of extra effort, but it is worth it to keep them healthy and looking good. Here are some tips for caring for your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Separate your dreadlocks regularly to prevent them from growing together.
  • Palm roll your dreadlocks regularly to maintain their shape.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup in your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and heat to prevent dryness and damage.

Overall, the time it takes to dread short hair depends on several factors, including hair texture, thickness, and length, as well as the method used to create the dreadlocks. By choosing a suitable method and maintaining healthy hair care habits, you can achieve and maintain beautiful, healthy dreadlocks.
