How To Put Up Shoulder Length Fine Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Feb 25, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you have shoulder-length fine hair, you know how difficult it can be to style. It doesn't hold curls, gets greasy easily, and just doesn't have the same volume as thicker hair. But fear not! With the right techniques and products, you can put up your shoulder-length fine hair in a way that looks chic and effortless. In this article, we'll walk you through how to put up shoulder length fine hair.

Table of Contents

  1. Prepping Your Hair
  2. Trying Out Different Styles
  3. Frequently Asked Questions
  4. Hair Care Tips for Fine Hair

Prepping Your Hair

The first step in putting up shoulder-length fine hair is to prep it properly. This means washing it with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to give it some body. Avoid using heavy products like serums or oils, as they will weigh your hair down. Instead, opt for lightweight mousse or texturizing sprays.

Once your hair is clean and dry, you can start styling. It's best to work with hair that's a day or two old, as it will have more texture and hold better. If your hair gets greasy easily, you can use dry shampoo to refresh it and add some volume.

Trying Out Different Styles

There are many ways to put up shoulder-length fine hair, depending on your personal style and the occasion. Here are a few ideas:

Low Ponytail

A low ponytail is a classic and easy hairstyle that works well for shoulder-length fine hair. To create this style, gather your hair at the nape of your neck and secure it with an elastic band. You can leave a few face-framing pieces out for a more casual look, or slick your hair back for a more polished vibe.

Half-Up Half-Down

If you want to show off your length but still keep your hair out of your face, try a half-up half-down style. Simply grab the top half of your hair and secure it with a clip or elastic band. You can leave it loose or add some texture with a curling iron or texturizing spray.

Braids

Braids are a great way to add some interest and texture to fine hair. You can try a simple French braid, a fishtail braid, or even a braided crown. Just make sure to use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo beforehand to give your hair some grip.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I add volume to my fine hair?

There are a few ways to add volume to fine hair. First, make sure you're using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. You can also try blow-drying your hair upside down to give it some lift. Another trick is to use a volumizing powder or spray at the roots.

What hairstyles should I avoid with fine hair?

It's best to avoid hairstyles that are too heavy or require a lot of teasing, as they can damage your hair and make it look flat. Avoid slicked-back styles as well, as they will emphasize any greasiness in your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Fine Hair

In addition to styling, it's important to take care of your fine hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Don't wash your hair every day, as this can strip it of natural oils and make it look flat.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too much, as this can cause breakage and make it look frizzy.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and using the right products and techniques, you can put up your shoulder-length fine hair in a way that looks chic and effortless.
