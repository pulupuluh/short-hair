How To Fix Lifting Lace Front Wig Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 17, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you struggling with a lace front wig that keeps lifting? It can be frustrating when your wig starts to shift or come off, especially in public. Fortunately, fixing a lifting lace front wig is not impossible. In this blog post, we’ll go over some simple steps to help you fix your lifting lace front wig, as well as some hair care tips to keep your wig in place.

Table of Contents

  • How to Fix Lifting Lace Front Wig
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Lifting
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Your Lace Front Wig

How to Fix Lifting Lace Front Wig

Before we get to the steps, it’s important to know why your wig may be lifting in the first place. There are several reasons why wigs lift, including:

  • Using the wrong adhesive or not applying it correctly
  • Sweating or excessive moisture
  • Not cleaning the scalp or hairline before application
  • Wearing the wig too long without reapplying the adhesive

Now that you know some of the reasons why your wig may be lifting, let’s go over the steps to fix it.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Remove the wig

The first step to fixing a lifting lace front wig is to remove it. Gently lift the edges of the wig and apply a solvent or adhesive remover to dissolve the adhesive. Use a cotton swab or soft cloth to remove any remaining adhesive residue.

Step 2: Clean the scalp and hairline

Clean your scalp and hairline thoroughly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will remove any oils or dirt that may have built up since your last wig application. Make sure your scalp and hairline are completely dry before reapplying the wig.

Step 3: Apply adhesive

Apply a thin layer of adhesive to your hairline using a brush or cotton swab. Make sure you apply the adhesive evenly and avoid getting it on your hair. Allow the adhesive to dry for a few minutes until it becomes tacky.

Step 4: Attach the wig

Carefully place the wig on your head, starting from the front and working your way back. Use a comb or brush to smooth out any bumps or bubbles. Press down firmly on the wig to ensure it’s securely in place.

Step 5: Style your wig

Style your wig as desired using a wig brush or comb. You can also use heat tools, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to the wig.

Tips to Prevent Lifting

Preventing your lace front wig from lifting is much easier than fixing it. Here are some tips to help keep your wig in place:

  • Use a high-quality adhesive that’s designed for lace front wigs
  • Apply the adhesive correctly and evenly
  • Avoid excessive sweating or moisture by using a wig cap or headband
  • Clean your scalp and hairline before each application
  • Reapply the adhesive every 1-2 weeks or as needed

FAQs

Q: Can I use regular glue to attach my lace front wig?

A: No, regular glue is not designed for use on skin and can cause irritation or damage to your scalp and hairline. Always use a high-quality adhesive that’s specifically designed for lace front wigs.

Q: How long can I wear my lace front wig before I need to reapply the adhesive?

A: It depends on several factors, including the type of adhesive you’re using, your level of activity, and your body’s natural oils. Generally, you should reapply the adhesive every 1-2 weeks or as needed.

Hair Care Tips for Your Lace Front Wig

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your lace front wig looking amazing. Here are some tips to help you care for your wig:

  • Wash your wig every 1-2 weeks with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or wig brush to detangle your wig
  • Avoid using heat tools on your wig unless it’s made of heat-resistant fibers
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head when not in use

With these tips and steps, you should be able to fix your lifting lace front wig and keep it in place. Remember to take good care of your wig and scalp to ensure long-lasting wear and a natural-looking appearance.
