How To Stop Curly Hair From Frizzing When Drying

Written by Daniel Jul 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have curly hair, you know the struggle of trying to keep it from frizzing. One of the biggest challenges is trying to dry your hair without causing frizz. In this article, we will discuss how to stop curly hair from frizzing when drying.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Curly hair is prone to frizz due to its structure. The cuticle of curly hair is not as smooth as straight hair, which makes it more susceptible to frizz. When drying curly hair, friction can cause the cuticle to lift and separate, leading to frizz.

There are several factors that can contribute to frizz when drying curly hair. These include using the wrong products, using too much heat, towel drying, and not using the right technique.

How to

The key to drying curly hair without frizz is to use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer. A diffuser distributes the heat evenly and reduces friction, which can help prevent frizz.

Before you start drying your hair, make sure you use a leave-in conditioner or a curl cream to provide moisture and protection. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, making sure to focus on the ends.

Step by Step

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair.
  3. Divide your hair into sections.
  4. Attach the diffuser to your blow dryer.
  5. Starting at the roots, place a section of hair in the diffuser and hold it there for a few seconds.
  6. Move the diffuser in a circular motion to dry the rest of the hair.
  7. Repeat on each section of your hair.
  8. Once your hair is dry, you can use a serum or oil to tame any remaining frizz.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help prevent frizz when drying curly hair:

  • Avoid towel drying your hair, as it can cause friction and lead to frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to squeeze out excess water.
  • Avoid using too much heat, as it can damage your hair and cause frizz. Use the lowest heat setting possible.
  • Don't touch your hair too much while it's drying, as this can cause frizz. Let the diffuser do the work.
  • If you have time, let your hair air dry partially before using the diffuser.

FAQ

Can I use a regular blow dryer instead of a diffuser?

You can, but it's not recommended. A regular blow dryer can cause frizz by blowing your hair around and creating friction. A diffuser is designed to distribute the heat evenly and reduce friction, which can help prevent frizz.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have dry hair, you may only need to wash it once or twice a week. If you have oily hair or work out frequently, you may need to wash it more often. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

What type of products should I use on my curly hair?

You should use products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as leave-in conditioners, curl creams, and serums. Look for products that provide moisture and protection, and avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for preventing frizz and keeping your curly hair healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as hair dyes and relaxers, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protection spray.

By following these tips and using the right technique, you can stop curly hair from frizzing when drying. Remember to be gentle with your hair, and use products that provide moisture and protection.
