How To Style Natural Hair With No Edges Tips Tricks And Techniques

Written by Aprilia Mar 24, 2023 Â· 10 min read

When it comes to styling natural hair, one of the biggest challenges can be dealing with hair loss around the edges. Whether due to genetics, styling practices, or medical conditions, thinning or absent edges can make it difficult to achieve the styles you love. However, with a few simple tips, tricks, and techniques, you can learn how to style natural hair with no edges and feel confident and beautiful every day. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know, from hair care to styling techniques, so let's get started!

How to Care for Natural Hair with No Edges

Before you can begin styling your natural hair, it's important to take care of it properly. This is especially true if you're dealing with hair loss or thinning around the edges. Here are some tips for caring for your natural hair:

1. Keep Your Hair Moisturized

Dry hair is more prone to breakage and damage, so it's important to keep your natural hair moisturized. This can be achieved by using a leave-in conditioner, a moisturizing hair oil, or a deep conditioning treatment. Be sure to focus on the areas around your edges, as this is where hair is most likely to break or fall out.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids, twists, and ponytails, can put stress on your hairline and cause further damage. Instead, opt for looser styles that don't pull at your edges. You can also try using hair accessories, such as headbands or scarves, to add interest to your styles without putting stress on your hairline.

3. Protect Your Hair at Night

When you sleep, your hair can rub against your pillowcase and cause friction, which can lead to breakage and thinning. To protect your hair at night, try sleeping on a satin or silk pillowcase, or wear a satin or silk scarf or bonnet to bed.

4. Be Gentle When Detangling

When detangling your natural hair, be gentle and use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. Avoid using brushes or fine-tooth combs, as these can cause damage and breakage. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, being careful not to pull or tug at your edges.

How to Style Natural Hair with No Edges

Now that you know how to properly care for your natural hair, let's talk about styling techniques. Here are some tips for styling natural hair with no edges:

1. Use Hair Accessories

Hair accessories can be a great way to add interest to your styles and distract from thinning edges. Try using headbands, scarves, or hair clips to create fun and unique looks.

2. Try a Twist-Out or Braid-Out

A twist-out or braid-out can be a great way to add volume and texture to your natural hair. Simply create twists or braids in your hair, leave them in overnight, and unravel them in the morning for beautiful, defined curls.

3. Experiment with Updos

Updos can be a great way to show off your natural hair and minimize the appearance of thinning edges. Try a bun, a braided updo, or a twisted updo for a sophisticated and stylish look.

4. Embrace Your Natural Texture

If you're dealing with hair loss or thinning edges, it can be tempting to try to hide it with elaborate styles or hair extensions. However, embracing your natural texture and wearing your hair in its natural state can be a beautiful and empowering choice. Try a wash-and-go, a frohawk, or a twist-out for a look that celebrates your natural beauty.

Tips for Maintaining Your Natural Hair with No Edges

Finally, here are some tips for maintaining your natural hair with no edges:

1. Be Patient

Dealing with hair loss or thinning edges can be frustrating, but it's important to be patient and gentle with your hair. Avoid harsh chemicals or excessive heat styling, and give your hair time to grow and recover.

2. Consult a Professional

If you're experiencing significant hair loss or thinning, it may be helpful to consult a professional stylist or a dermatologist. They can help you identify the cause of your hair loss and recommend treatments or styling techniques that can help.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun's UV rays can be damaging to your hair, so it's important to protect your hair from sun exposure. Try wearing a hat or scarf when you're outside, or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF protection.

4. Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can help keep your hair and scalp hydrated, which can promote healthy hair growth. Aim for at least eight glasses of water a day, and eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and nutrients that support hair health.

FAQs About Styling Natural Hair with No Edges

Q: Can I still wear protective styles if I have no edges?

A: Yes, you can still wear protective styles like braids or twists, but be sure to avoid styles that pull at your edges. You can also try using hair extensions to add volume and fullness to your styles.

Q: Will my edges grow back?

A: It depends on the cause of your hair loss. If your hair loss is due to genetics or medical conditions, your hair may not grow back. However, if your hair loss is due to styling practices, such as tight hairstyles or heat styling, your hair may grow back with proper care and gentle styling techniques.

Q: Can I use hair products to stimulate hair growth?

A: There are many hair products on the market that claim to stimulate hair growth, but it's important to be cautious when using these products. Be sure to do your research and consult with a professional before using any new products, and always follow the instructions carefully.

The Bottom Line

Styling natural hair with no edges can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and care, you can achieve beautiful, healthy-looking hair. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and try new styles and techniques until you find what works best for you. With a little creativity and confidence, you'll be able to rock any style with grace and beauty!
