How To Cut Your Own Curly Hair Shoulder Length A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jun 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon every time you need a trim? Learning how to cut your own curly hair can save you time and money in the long run. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your own curly hair shoulder length in the comfort of your own home. From tools to tips, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You'll Need
  2. How to Cut Your Own Curly Hair Shoulder Length
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips and Tricks
  5. Frequently Asked Questions
  6. Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before we begin, let's gather the tools you'll need to cut your own curly hair shoulder length:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb or brush
  • Sectioning clips or hair ties
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle filled with water

How to Cut Your Own Curly Hair Shoulder Length

Now that you have your tools, let's get started with the basics of how to cut your own curly hair shoulder length:

  • Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can appear longer, making it difficult to get an accurate cut.
  • Wear a shirt with a tight neckline to prevent hair from getting inside your clothes.
  • Find a well-lit area with plenty of space to move around.
  • Take your time and be patient. It's better to cut a little bit at a time than to take off too much.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps for a successful at-home haircut:

  1. Part your hair down the middle and divide it into two sections.
  2. Clip or tie one section of hair out of the way.
  3. Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down.
  4. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length.
  5. Continue taking small sections of hair and cutting them to the same length.
  6. Repeat on the other side of your head.
  7. Check the length of your hair in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments.
  8. Trim the ends if needed to create a more polished look.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your own curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and damage to your hair.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will appear longer and you may take off too much.
  • Consider cutting your hair in layers to add volume and texture.
  • Start with a small section of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Take breaks if your arms or hands get tired.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about cutting your own curly hair:

Can I cut my own curly hair if it's very long?

It's possible, but it may be more difficult to get an even cut. Consider cutting your hair in smaller sections to make it more manageable.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it's recommended to trim curly hair every 8-12 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

Don't panic! If it's a small mistake, you may be able to fix it by trimming off a little bit more. If it's a larger mistake, you may need to visit a professional hair stylist to correct it.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it healthy and looking its best:

  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and prevent frizz.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or swim cap.

Now that you know how to cut your own curly hair shoulder length, you can save time and money by doing it yourself. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut in the comfort of your own home.
