My Hair Won t Stop Falling Out After Bleaching What Should I Do

Written by William Apr 07, 2023 Â· 10 min read

If you've recently bleached your hair and noticed that it's falling out more than usual, you're not alone. Many people experience hair loss after bleaching, but the good news is that there are steps you can take to prevent further damage and promote healthy hair growth. In this article, we'll discuss how to care for your hair after bleaching and answer some common questions about hair loss.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Care for Your Hair After Bleaching
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Hair Care
  • Tips to Prevent Hair Loss
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Products to Promote Growth

Description

Hair loss after bleaching is a common problem, but it can be alarming if you're not prepared for it. Bleaching strips your hair of its natural oils and can weaken the hair shaft, causing it to break or fall out. This is especially true if you already have fine or fragile hair. However, there are steps you can take to minimize damage and promote healthy hair growth.

How to Care for Your Hair After Bleaching

If you're experiencing hair loss after bleaching, the first step is to stop bleaching your hair. Give your hair a break and focus on repairing the damage that's already been done. Here are some tips to care for your hair after bleaching:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

After bleaching your hair, it's important to use a shampoo and conditioner that will add moisture and nourishment to your hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates, as these can further damage your hair.

2. Don't Overdo It on Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can be damaging to your hair, especially if it's already weakened from bleaching. Try to limit your use of these tools, and when you do use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

3. Deep Condition Your Hair Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help to repair damaged hair and promote healthy growth. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, biotin, or collagen, which can strengthen your hair and prevent breakage. You can also try making your own deep conditioning treatments using natural ingredients like avocado or coconut oil.

Step-by-Step Guide to Hair Care

If you're not sure where to start with caring for your hair after bleaching, here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Wash Your Hair with a Moisturizing Shampoo

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo that's designed to add moisture and nourishment. Massage the shampoo into your hair and scalp, then rinse thoroughly.

2. Apply a Deep Conditioning Treatment

After shampooing, apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair. Work the product through your hair from root to tip, then leave it on for the recommended amount of time (usually 10-15 minutes). Rinse thoroughly.

3. Towel-Dry Your Hair

Gently towel-dry your hair to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with the towel, as this can cause breakage.

4. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to add extra moisture and protection. You can also use a detangling spray if your hair is prone to tangling.

5. Style Your Hair Gently

If you need to style your hair, do so gently. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, and use heat styling tools sparingly.

Tips to Prevent Hair Loss

Here are some additional tips to prevent hair loss after bleaching:

1. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can help to prevent split ends and breakage. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks, or as often as needed to keep your hair healthy.

2. Be Gentle When Brushing Your Hair

When brushing your hair, be gentle and use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals can help to promote healthy hair growth. Focus on eating foods that are high in protein, iron, and vitamin C, which are all important for hair health.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How long does it take for hair to stop falling out after bleaching?

It can take several weeks or even months for your hair to stop falling out after bleaching. The key is to be patient and take good care of your hair in the meantime.

2. Will my hair grow back after falling out from bleaching?

If your hair falls out from bleaching, it may grow back over time. However, it's important to take steps to promote healthy hair growth and prevent further damage.

3. Can I bleach my hair again if it's falling out?

If your hair is falling out after bleaching, it's not recommended to bleach it again until your hair has fully recovered. Give your hair a break and focus on repairing the damage that's already been done.

Hair Care Products to Promote Growth

If you're looking for hair care products to promote growth and repair damage, here are some options to consider:

1. Biotin Supplements

Biotin is a vitamin that's important for healthy hair growth. Taking biotin supplements can help to promote hair growth and prevent breakage.

2. Keratin Treatments

Keratin treatments can help to strengthen your hair and prevent breakage. Look for products that contain keratin, or consider getting a keratin treatment at a salon.

3. Scalp Serums

Scalp serums can help to nourish your hair follicles and promote healthy growth. Look for products that contain ingredients like caffeine, which can stimulate hair growth.

4. Hair Masks

Hair masks can help to repair damaged hair and add moisture and nourishment. Look for masks that contain ingredients like avocado, coconut oil, or shea butter.

In conclusion, experiencing hair loss after bleaching can be alarming, but there are steps you can take to prevent further damage and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient, take good care of your hair, and avoid bleaching it again until it has fully recovered.
