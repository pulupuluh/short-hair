What Colour Hair Suits 50 Year Olds

Written by William Aug 10, 2023 Â· 8 min read

As we age, our hair colour can change, and we may start to feel like our current hair colour doesn't suit us anymore. However, choosing a new hair colour can be a daunting task, especially if you're over 50. In this article, we'll explore what colour hair suits 50 year olds and provide tips on how to choose the right shade for you.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To Choose The Right Hair Colour
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips For Choosing The Right Hair Colour
  • FAQs

Description

When it comes to choosing a hair colour that suits 50 year olds, there are a few things to consider. Firstly, you'll want to think about your skin tone. As we age, our skin can become duller, so you may want to choose a hair colour that brightens up your complexion. Secondly, you'll want to think about the maintenance of your new hair colour. Some shades require more upkeep than others, so it's important to choose a shade that fits in with your lifestyle.

How To Choose The Right Hair Colour

Choosing the right hair colour can be tricky, but there are a few things you can do to make the process easier.

1. Consider Your Skin Tone

As mentioned earlier, your skin tone is an important factor to consider when choosing a hair colour. If you have warm undertones, you may want to choose a warm hair colour such as golden blonde or auburn. If you have cool undertones, you may want to choose a cool hair colour such as ash blonde or cool brown.

2. Think About Your Lifestyle

When choosing a new hair colour, it's important to think about how much time and money you're willing to invest in maintenance. Some shades require more upkeep than others, so if you're not willing to spend a lot of time and money on your hair, you may want to choose a low maintenance colour such as a natural-looking blonde or brunette.

3. Consult With A Professional

If you're unsure about which hair colour to choose, it's always a good idea to consult with a professional hairstylist. They can help you choose a shade that complements your skin tone and fits in with your lifestyle.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to choose the right hair colour:

1. Determine Your Skin Tone

Look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If your veins appear green, you have warm undertones. If your veins appear blue, you have cool undertones.

2. Choose A Shade That Complements Your Skin Tone

If you have warm undertones, you may want to choose a warm hair colour such as golden blonde or auburn. If you have cool undertones, you may want to choose a cool hair colour such as ash blonde or cool brown.

3. Consider Your Lifestyle

Think about how much time and money you're willing to invest in maintenance. Some shades require more upkeep than others, so choose a shade that fits in with your lifestyle.

4. Consult With A Professional

If you're still unsure, consult with a professional hairstylist.

Tips For Choosing The Right Hair Colour

Here are some tips to keep in mind when choosing a new hair colour:

  • Consider your skin tone.
  • Think about your lifestyle.
  • Choose a shade that complements your natural hair colour.
  • Don't be afraid to try something new.
  • Consult with a professional hairstylist.

FAQs

What hair colours suit 50 year olds?

When choosing a hair colour that suits 50 year olds, it's important to consider your skin tone and lifestyle. Warm hair colours such as golden blonde and auburn can complement warm undertones, while cool hair colours such as ash blonde and cool brown can complement cool undertones.

What hair colours should 50 year olds avoid?

50 year olds should avoid hair colours that are too bright or unnatural looking, as these can be aging. It's also a good idea to avoid shades that require a lot of maintenance, especially if you don't have the time or resources to keep up with them.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've chosen a new hair colour, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

Choosing a new hair colour can be a fun and exciting experience. By following these tips and consulting with a professional hairstylist, you can find a shade that suits you and makes you feel confident and beautiful.
