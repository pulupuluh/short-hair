How To Cut Men s Hair Bald On Top A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to cut your own or someone else's hair bald on top, it's important to approach the task with care and precision. It can be a great look for men who are experiencing hair loss or who simply want a low-maintenance hairstyle. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a clean and even bald cut on the top of the head.

Description

When cutting men's hair bald on top, it's important to remember that this style is all about precision. You want to create a smooth, even surface on the top of the head, while also blending the hair seamlessly into the sides and back. This can be achieved with the right tools, techniques, and a bit of patience.

How to Cut Men's Hair Bald on Top: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. You'll need a pair of clippers with various guard sizes, a comb, a pair of scissors, and a handheld mirror.

Step 2: Determine the Length

Decide how short you want the hair on top to be. If you're starting with longer hair, you may want to use scissors to cut off some of the length before using the clippers. Start with a longer guard size and work your way down until you achieve the desired length.

Step 3: Create a Guide Line

Using the clippers, create a guide line around the head where the hair will be cut short. This can be done by running the clippers along the hairline or using a comb to create a part.

Step 4: Begin Cutting

Starting at the front of the head, use the clippers to cut the hair in the direction it naturally grows. Work your way back towards the crown, using a smaller guard size as you go to create a smooth, even surface. Be careful not to cut too much off at once, as it's easier to take more off than to add it back.

Step 5: Blend the Sides and Back

Once the top of the head is cut to the desired length, use the clippers to blend the hair on the sides and back into the bald section. Use a longer guard size to create a gradual fade, working your way down to a shorter guard size as you move towards the bottom of the hairline.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Use a handheld mirror to check the back of the head for any uneven spots or missed areas. Make any necessary adjustments with the clippers or scissors.

Step 7: Clean Up the Edges

Use the clippers or scissors to clean up the hairline around the ears and the back of the neck. You can also use a straight razor for a more precise finish.

Step 8: Wash and Style

After the cut is complete, wash the hair with shampoo and conditioner to remove any loose hairs. Style as desired with gel, pomade, or other hair products.

Tips for Cutting Men's Hair Bald on Top

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down gradually.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check for evenness.
  • Be patient and take your time to ensure a clean cut.
  • Clean up the edges with scissors or a straight razor for a more precise finish.

FAQ about Cutting Men's Hair Bald on Top

Can I cut my own hair bald on top?

Yes, it's possible to cut your own hair bald on top with the right tools and techniques. However, it may be helpful to have someone else assist you, especially when it comes to checking the back of the head for evenness.

What guard size should I use to start?

Start with a longer guard size, such as a #4 or #5, and work your way down gradually until you achieve the desired length. It's always better to take off less hair than to take off too much at once.

Should I use scissors or clippers?

Both scissors and clippers can be used to cut men's hair bald on top, but clippers may be more efficient for creating a smooth, even surface. Scissors can be used to trim any longer hairs or to clean up the edges.

Hair Care for Balding Men

If you're experiencing hair loss, it's important to take care of the hair you do have. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent further damage, and avoid using harsh styling products that can cause breakage. Consider using a scalp massager to stimulate hair growth, and talk to your doctor about any underlying medical conditions that may be contributing to hair loss.
