How To Cut Curly Hair Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that it can be a challenge to style and maintain. One of the most difficult things to do is to cut your own bangs. However, with the right technique and a little bit of practice, you can achieve the perfect curly bangs. In this article, we will walk you through the steps to cut curly hair bangs, provide some tips and tricks, and answer some frequently asked questions about curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Curly Hair Bangs
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Curly Hair Care

How to Cut Curly Hair Bangs

Cutting curly hair bangs can be a bit tricky, but it is not impossible. Here are some things to consider before you start:

  • Make sure your hair is dry before you start cutting.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once.
  • Curly hair tends to bounce up after it is cut, so keep that in mind when cutting.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid fraying the ends.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know what to consider when cutting curly hair bangs, here is a step-by-step guide to follow:

  1. Start by sectioning off the hair you want to cut. Use a comb to separate the hair from the rest of your hair.
  2. Make sure your hair is dry and styled the way you normally wear it.
  3. Hold the hair that you want to cut between your fingers and comb it straight down.
  4. Use sharp scissors to cut the hair at an angle. Start by cutting a small amount of hair and continue until you have reached the desired length.
  5. Make sure both sides are even by comparing the length of the hair on each side. If they are not even, trim as needed.
  6. Once you have achieved the desired length, use your fingers to separate the hair and check for any stray hairs. Trim as needed.
  7. Style your bangs as desired.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect curly hair bangs:

  • Don't cut your hair when it is wet. Curly hair tends to shrink when it is wet, so cutting it when it is dry will give you a better idea of the length.
  • Use a mirror to ensure that both sides are even.
  • When cutting, make sure to cut at an angle instead of straight across. This will help to avoid a blunt cut that can look unnatural.
  • If your hair is extremely curly, consider cutting it when it is in its natural state. This will help to ensure that the length is accurate.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair bangs:

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair bangs?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows. Typically, curly hair grows slower than straight hair, so you may not need to cut your bangs as often.

Q: Can I cut my curly hair bangs myself?

A: Yes, you can cut your own curly hair bangs. However, it may be helpful to have someone else do it for you if you are not comfortable cutting your own hair.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: If you make a mistake, don't panic. Curly hair is forgiving and mistakes can often be hidden by styling the hair a certain way.

Curly Hair Care

In addition to cutting your curly hair bangs, it is important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve the perfect curly hair bangs. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you will have beautiful bangs in no time.
