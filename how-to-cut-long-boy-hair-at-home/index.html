How To Cut Long Boy Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 24, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Cutting your child's hair at home can be a daunting task, especially if you're not a professional hairstylist. However, with a little patience and the right tools, you can easily give your boy a stylish haircut right in the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting long boy hair at home, from start to finish.

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Long Boy Hair at Home
  • FAQs

How to Cut Long Boy Hair at Home

The first thing you'll need to do is gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clippers. It's important to use sharp scissors, as dull scissors can make the hair look uneven and frayed.

Next, choose the right hairstyle for your boy. If he has long hair, you can choose to trim the ends, layer the hair, or give him a blunt cut. Make sure you have a clear idea of the style you want to achieve before you begin cutting.

Once you have your tools and hairstyle in mind, you're ready to start cutting. Follow these steps for a successful haircut:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Start by washing and drying your boy's hair. Wet hair is easier to cut and style, so make sure the hair is damp before you start cutting. Dry the hair thoroughly before you begin cutting, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

Step 2: Comb the Hair

Use a comb to detangle the hair and make sure it's smooth and even. Comb the hair in the direction it naturally falls, so you can see how the hair will look after it's cut.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Use hair clips to section the hair. Start by clipping the hair on top of the head, then clip the sides and back. This will make it easier to cut the hair in sections and ensure an even cut.

Step 4: Trim the Ends

Take one section of hair at a time and hold it between your fingers. Use the scissors to trim the ends of the hair, cutting in a straight line. Be careful not to cut too much, as you can always go back and trim more if necessary.

Step 5: Layer the Hair

If you want to layer the hair, take a small section of hair at a time and cut it at an angle. This will create layers and add texture to the hair. Repeat this process for each section of hair until you achieve the desired layering effect.

Step 6: Give a Blunt Cut

If you want to give your boy a blunt cut, simply cut the hair straight across. This will create a sharp, clean look. Be sure to cut the hair in small sections, so you can ensure an even cut.

Step 7: Use Clippers

If your boy has thick hair, you may need to use hair clippers to thin out the hair. Use the clippers to trim the hair at the back and sides of the head, creating a gradual taper. Be sure to use a guard on the clippers to avoid cutting too much hair at once.

Step 8: Check for Evenness

After you've finished cutting, comb through the hair and check for any uneven spots. If you notice any unevenness, use the scissors to trim the hair until it's even.

Step 9: Style the Hair

Once you've finished cutting, style the hair using a comb or brush. You can use hair gel or mousse to add texture and hold the style in place.

Tips for Cutting Long Boy Hair at Home

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to larger cuts.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of the head as you cut.
  • Take breaks if your child gets antsy or uncomfortable.
  • Be patient and take your time.

FAQs

How often should I cut my boy's hair?

It's recommended to cut your boy's hair every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking neat and healthy.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim the hair a little shorter or even it out with the rest of the hair. Alternatively, you can take your child to a professional hairstylist to fix any mistakes.

Hair Care for Long Boy Hair

To keep your boy's hair healthy and looking its best, it's important to maintain a regular hair care routine. This includes washing the hair with a gentle shampoo, conditioning the hair regularly, and brushing or combing the hair to prevent tangles.

In addition, it's important to protect the hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray when using hair dryers, curling irons, or straighteners. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to keep the hair soft and moisturized.

By following these tips and techniques, you can give your boy a stylish and professional-looking haircut right in the comfort of your own home.
