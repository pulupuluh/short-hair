How To Stop My Curly Hair Going Frizzy A Complete Guide

Written by Robby Jul 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Curly hair is a blessing, but it can also be a curse. One of the most common problems that people with curly hair face is frizz. Frizz can make your hair look dull, unmanageable, and messy. It can be frustrating to deal with, but there are ways to prevent it. In this article, we will discuss how to stop your curly hair from going frizzy.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Stop My Curly Hair Going Frizzy
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Frizz happens when the hair cuticle is lifted, allowing moisture to enter the hair shaft. This causes the hair to swell and become frizzy. Curly hair is more prone to frizz because the cuticle is naturally more lifted than straight hair. The good news is that there are ways to prevent frizz and keep your curly hair looking healthy and shiny.

How to Stop My Curly Hair Going Frizzy

The first step in preventing frizz is to understand your hair type. Curly hair comes in different textures and thicknesses, and each type requires a different approach to prevent frizz. Here are some general tips that work for most curly hair types:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates are harsh detergents that can strip the natural oils from your hair, leaving it dry and more prone to frizz. Use a sulfate-free shampoo that is gentle on your hair and scalp.

2. Condition your hair regularly

Curly hair needs moisture to stay healthy and shiny. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz.

3. Avoid using heat styling tools

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and cause frizz. If you must use a heat styling tool, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

4. Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair

A diffuser attachment on your blow dryer can help to distribute heat evenly and prevent frizz. Use a low heat setting and gently scrunch your hair with the diffuser to enhance your curls.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to prevent frizz in your curly hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo

Use a sulfate-free shampoo that is gentle on your hair and scalp. Massage the shampoo into your scalp, and rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Condition your hair

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave it on for 5-10 minutes, then rinse thoroughly.

Step 3: Dry your hair with a microfiber towel

Wrap your hair in a microfiber towel and gently squeeze out excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel, as this can cause frizz.

Step 4: Apply a leave-in conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. This will help to hydrate your hair and prevent frizz.

Step 5: Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair

Attach a diffuser to your blow dryer and use a low heat setting. Gently scrunch your hair with the diffuser to enhance your curls and prevent frizz.

Tips

  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Avoid using a brush on your curly hair, as this can break up your curls and cause frizz.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and cause frizz.
  • Wear a silk or satin scarf or pillowcase to prevent friction and minimize frizz.

FAQ

What causes frizz in curly hair?

Frizz in curly hair is caused by the hair cuticle being lifted, allowing moisture to enter the hair shaft. This causes the hair to swell and become frizzy.

Can frizz be prevented?

Yes, frizz can be prevented. Use sulfate-free shampoo, condition your hair regularly, avoid using heat styling tools, use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair, and avoid touching your hair too much.

What are some good products for preventing frizz in curly hair?

Some good products for preventing frizz in curly hair include leave-in conditioner, anti-frizz serum, and curl-defining cream.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for preventing frizz in curly hair. Use gentle products that are formulated for your hair type, and avoid using harsh chemicals and heat styling tools. Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy. Protect your hair from friction by wearing a silk or satin scarf or pillowcase. With the right care, you can keep your curly hair looking shiny, healthy, and frizz-free.
