What Causes Excessive Shedding In Short Haired Dogs

Written by Frank May 12, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Short haired dogs are a popular choice among pet owners because they are easy to groom and maintain. However, some short haired dogs are prone to shedding more than others, causing frustration and inconvenience to their owners. In this article, we will explore the reasons behind excessive shedding in short haired dogs and provide tips on how to manage it.

Table of Contents

  • What Causes Excessive Shedding
  • How to Manage Excessive Shedding
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Reducing Shedding
  • Tips for Managing Shedding in Short Haired Dogs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Short Haired Dogs

What Causes Excessive Shedding

There are several reasons why short haired dogs may shed excessively. These include:

Allergies

Short haired dogs can be allergic to a variety of things, including food, pollen, and dust. When a dog is allergic to something, their immune system goes into overdrive, causing inflammation and itching. This can lead to excessive shedding as the dog scratches and bites at their skin.

Poor Nutrition

Dogs that are not getting the proper nutrients in their diet may shed excessively. A lack of essential vitamins and minerals can cause their coat to become dry and brittle, leading to shedding.

Stress

Stress can also cause a dog to shed more than usual. This can be caused by a change in routine, a new pet in the household, or even loud noises.

Medical Conditions

Some medical conditions can cause excessive shedding in short haired dogs. These conditions include thyroid problems, Cushing's disease, and skin infections.

Hormonal Imbalances

Hormonal imbalances can also lead to excessive shedding. This can be caused by pregnancy, lactation, or even spaying and neutering.

How to Manage Excessive Shedding

The first step in managing excessive shedding in short haired dogs is to identify the underlying cause. Once you know why your dog is shedding excessively, you can take steps to manage it. Here are some tips:

Visit the Vet

If you suspect that your dog's shedding is caused by a medical condition or hormonal imbalance, it is important to visit the vet. They can perform tests to determine the underlying cause and provide treatment.

Upgrade Your Dog's Diet

Feeding your dog a healthy and balanced diet can help reduce shedding. Look for dog food that is high in protein and contains essential vitamins and minerals.

Reduce Stress

If your dog is shedding due to stress, try to identify the source of the stress and eliminate it if possible. If the stressor cannot be removed, try to provide a calm and quiet environment for your dog.

Groom Your Dog Regularly

Grooming your dog regularly can help reduce shedding by removing loose fur. Use a brush or comb that is designed for your dog's coat type and brush them at least once a week.

Step-by-Step Guide to Reducing Shedding

Here is a step-by-step guide to reducing shedding in short haired dogs:

  1. Visit the vet to rule out any medical conditions
  2. Upgrade your dog's diet to a high-quality dog food
  3. Reduce stress in your dog's environment
  4. Groom your dog regularly using a brush or comb designed for their coat type
  5. Use a shedding tool to remove loose fur
  6. Bathe your dog regularly with a mild dog shampoo
  7. Use a conditioner to keep your dog's coat healthy and moisturized
  8. Ensure your dog is getting enough exercise to maintain a healthy coat
  9. Add a supplement to your dog's diet, such as omega-3 fatty acids, to promote a healthy coat
  10. Monitor your dog's shedding and adjust your grooming routine as needed

Tips for Managing Shedding in Short Haired Dogs

Here are some additional tips for managing shedding in short haired dogs:

  • Use a vacuum or lint roller to remove loose fur from your home
  • Wash your dog's bedding regularly to remove loose fur
  • Avoid using human shampoo on your dog, as it can dry out their skin and coat
  • Consider using a de-shedding treatment at a professional groomer
  • Provide your dog with plenty of water to keep their skin and coat hydrated

FAQs

Q: Is excessive shedding in short haired dogs normal?

A: No, excessive shedding is not normal and can be a sign of an underlying issue.

Q: Can allergies cause excessive shedding in short haired dogs?

A: Yes, allergies can cause a dog to shed excessively.

Q: How often should I groom my short haired dog?

A: You should groom your short haired dog at least once a week.

Hair Care for Short Haired Dogs

Short haired dogs require regular grooming to keep their coat healthy and shiny. Here are some tips for caring for your short haired dog's coat:

  • Use a brush or comb designed for short haired dogs
  • Bathe your dog regularly with a mild dog shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your dog's coat healthy and moisturized
  • Trim your dog's nails regularly to prevent them from scratching their skin
  • Provide your dog with a healthy diet that is rich in essential vitamins and minerals

By following these tips and identifying the underlying cause of your dog's shedding, you can help keep your short haired dog's coat healthy and reduce excessive shedding.
