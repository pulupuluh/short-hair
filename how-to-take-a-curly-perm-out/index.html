How To Take A Curly Perm Out A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of your curly perm and want to go back to straight hair? If so, you've come to the right place. This article will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to take a curly perm out. We'll also provide you with tips on how to care for your hair during and after the process. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Take a Curly Perm Out
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Success
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Taking Out a Perm

How to Take a Curly Perm Out

Before we begin, it's important to note that taking out a curly perm can be damaging to your hair. The chemicals used in the perm process can weaken your hair, so it's important to take proper precautions to minimize damage. Additionally, the process can take several hours, so be prepared to spend some time on this.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any product buildup and prepare your hair for the perming solution.

  2. Apply a perm solution neutralizer to your hair. This will stop the perming process and begin to break down the curls. Leave it on for the recommended amount of time (usually around 20 minutes).

  3. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.

  4. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and let it sit for at least 30 minutes. This will help to restore moisture to your hair and minimize damage.

  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water.

  6. Blow dry your hair using a cool setting. Avoid using hot tools, as they can further damage your hair.

  7. Use a straightening iron to straighten your hair. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends. Be sure to use a heat protectant to minimize damage.

  8. Repeat steps 4-7 as needed until your hair is straight.

  9. Trim any split ends or damaged hair to promote healthy hair growth.

  10. Continue to care for your hair using a deep conditioner and avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible.

Tips for Success

  • Do a strand test before starting the process to ensure that your hair can handle it.
  • Use a timer to keep track of how long you've had the neutralizer on your hair.
  • Be gentle when combing or brushing your hair to avoid further damage.
  • Consider getting a professional to help you with the process to minimize damage.
  • Avoid using chemical treatments on your hair for at least a month after taking out the perm.

FAQs

Q: Can I take out a perm at home?

A: Yes, you can take out a perm at home, but it's important to take proper precautions to minimize damage.

Q: Will taking out a perm damage my hair?

A: Yes, taking out a perm can be damaging to your hair, but using a deep conditioner and avoiding heat styling tools can help minimize damage.

Hair Care After Taking Out a Perm

After taking out a perm, it's important to continue to care for your hair to promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to restore moisture to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Get regular trims to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid using chemical treatments on your hair for at least a month after taking out the perm.

Now that you know how to take a curly perm out, you can make an informed decision on whether or not it's the right choice for you. Remember to take proper precautions to minimize damage and to continue to care for your hair after the process is complete. Good luck!
