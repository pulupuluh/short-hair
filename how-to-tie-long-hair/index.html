How To Tie Long Hair Tips Tricks And Styles

Written by Luffy Jul 22, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of your long hair getting in your face all the time? Do you want to learn how to tie your hair in a way that's easy, comfortable, and stylish? Look no further! This article will provide you with step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice so you can confidently rock a variety of hairstyles that will keep your long locks secured and out of the way.

How to Tie Long Hair

There are many ways to tie long hair, but some are easier and more practical than others. Here are some popular methods that you can try:

Ponytail

The classic ponytail is a go-to style for many long-haired folks, and for good reason. It's simple, versatile, and can be worn high or low depending on your preference. Here's how to do it:

  1. Brush your hair back with a comb or your fingers to remove any knots or tangles.
  2. Gather your hair at the crown of your head or at the nape of your neck, depending on where you want your ponytail to sit.
  3. Secure your hair with a hair tie or elastic band.
  4. Fluff up your ponytail for added volume or smooth it down for a sleeker look.

Pro tip: Use a scrunchie or a fabric hair tie instead of a rubber band to prevent breakage and hair damage.

Bun

Buns are another classic hairstyle that can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion. They're also a great way to keep your hair out of your face while still looking stylish. Here's how to do it:

  1. Gather your hair into a high or low ponytail, depending on where you want your bun to sit.
  2. Twist your ponytail into a tight coil.
  3. Wrap the coil around itself to create a bun.
  4. Secure the bun with bobby pins or hairpins.
  5. Tuck any loose ends or flyaways into the bun and adjust as needed.

Pro tip: Use a hair donut or sock bun to create a fuller, more voluminous bun.

Braid

Braids are a great way to add texture and interest to your hair while also keeping it out of your face. There are many types of braids to choose from, including French braids, Dutch braids, fishtail braids, and more. Here's how to do a simple three-strand braid:

  1. Brush your hair back and divide it into three equal sections.
  2. Cross the right section over the middle section, then cross the left section over the new middle section.
  3. Continue crossing the right and left sections over the middle section, pulling the braid tight as you go.
  4. Secure the end of the braid with a hair tie or elastic band.

Pro tip: Add volume to your braid by gently pulling on the outer sections after securing the end.

Tips for Tying Long Hair

While tying your hair may seem like a simple task, there are some tips and tricks that can make the process easier and more comfortable. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Use hair ties or elastic bands that are gentle on your hair to prevent breakage and damage.
  • Don't tie your hair too tightly, as this can cause headaches and hair loss over time.
  • Experiment with different styles and accessories to find what works best for you.
  • Don't tie your hair when it's wet or damp, as this can weaken the hair and cause it to break.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and hair damage while you sleep.

FAQs about Tying Long Hair

Can tying my hair too tightly cause damage?

Yes, tying your hair too tightly can cause headaches, hair loss, and damage to the hair follicles over time. Be sure to use gentle hair ties and don't pull your hair too tight when tying it up.

How often should I tie my hair up?

It's best to give your hair a break from being tied up every day, as this can cause damage and breakage. Try to wear your hair down or in a loose style at least a few times a week to give it a rest.

What's the best way to prevent hair breakage and damage?

There are many things you can do to prevent hair breakage and damage, including using gentle hair ties, avoiding tight hairstyles, using a heat protectant when styling with heat tools, and using a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

Hair Care for Long Hair

Tying your hair up is just one part of long hair care. To keep your locks healthy and strong, it's important to follow a regular hair care routine. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair after washing.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote overall hair health.

By following these tips and tricks, you can tie your long hair in a variety of styles that are easy, comfortable, and stylish. Remember to take care of your hair and give it the love and attention it deserves!
