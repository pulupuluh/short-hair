How Do Thinning Shears Work On Hair

Written by Oliver Apr 01, 2023 · 7 min read

Thinning shears are one of the most popular tools used by hairstylists around the world. They are a type of scissor that has teeth on one or both blades, used to remove bulk from the hair without creating a blunt edge. Thinning shears are used to create texture, movement, and volume in the hair. In this article, we will explore how thinning shears work on hair, how to use them, and how to take care of them.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Hair

Before using thinning shears, it is important to understand the type of hair you are working with. Thinning shears are not recommended for use on fine or thin hair, as they can cause damage and breakage. Thinning shears are best used on thick, coarse, or curly hair.

Here are the steps to use thinning shears on hair:

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair into manageable sections. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don’t miss any areas. Use hair clips or ties to secure the sections of hair that you are not working on.

Step 2: Comb the Hair

Once you have sectioned the hair, comb it thoroughly to get rid of any tangles. This will ensure that the thinning shears can glide through the hair easily.

Step 3: Choose the Right Thinning Shears

Choose the right thinning shears for the job. Thinning shears come in different types, with varying numbers of teeth and blade lengths. The type of thinning shears you use will depend on the thickness and texture of the hair you are working with.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle and cut through the section of hair. Work in small sections, starting at the ends of the hair and working your way up. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can cause unevenness and create a blunt edge.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

After you have cut through the section of hair, blend the hair by combing it out. This will help to ensure that the cut looks natural and not choppy.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when using thinning shears on hair:

  • Always use sharp thinning shears.
  • Don’t use thinning shears on wet hair.
  • Use thinning shears to remove bulk from the hair, not to create a new style.
  • Work slowly and carefully to avoid making mistakes.
  • Use thinning shears sparingly to avoid damaging the hair.

FAQ About Thinning Shears

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning shears:

What are thinning shears?

Thinning shears are a type of scissor that has teeth on one or both blades, used to remove bulk from the hair without creating a blunt edge.

How do thinning shears work?

Thinning shears work by cutting through the hair at an angle, removing some of the bulk without creating a blunt edge.

Can thinning shears damage hair?

Thinning shears can damage hair if they are used incorrectly, or if they are used on fine or thin hair. It is important to use them sparingly and carefully.

How to Take Care of Thinning Shears

Here are some tips for taking care of your thinning shears:

  • Keep your thinning shears clean and dry.
  • Oil your thinning shears regularly.
  • Store your thinning shears in a dry, cool place.
  • Don’t drop your thinning shears, as this can damage the blades.
  • Have your thinning shears sharpened regularly.

Conclusion

Thinning shears are a versatile tool that can be used to create texture, movement, and volume in the hair. They are best used on thick, coarse, or curly hair. When using thinning shears, it is important to work slowly and carefully, and to follow the tips and guidelines outlined in this article. By taking good care of your thinning shears, you can ensure that they will last for many years to come.
