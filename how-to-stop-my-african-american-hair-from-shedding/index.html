How To Stop My African American Hair From Shedding

Written by Aprilia Apr 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of seeing your beautiful locks fall out every time you brush or style your hair? Shedding is a common problem for African American hair, but there are ways to reduce it. In this article, we'll discuss how to stop your African American hair from shedding.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Shedding
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Shedding
  • FAQs
  • Proper Hair Care

How to Stop Shedding

Shedding can be caused by several factors such as genetics, stress, hormonal changes, and improper hair care. It is important to identify the cause of your shedding to determine the best solution for your hair. Here are some ways to stop shedding:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Harsh shampoos can strip your hair of its natural oils, causing it to become dry and brittle. Use a sulfate-free shampoo that is gentle on your hair and scalp. Look for shampoos that contain natural ingredients such as aloe vera, jojoba oil, and tea tree oil.

2. Moisturize Your Hair

Dry hair is more prone to shedding, so it's important to keep your hair moisturized. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated. You can also use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to give your hair an extra boost of moisture.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools such as flat irons and blow dryers can damage your hair, causing it to become weak and prone to shedding. Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

4. Protect Your Hair at Night

Sleeping on a cotton pillowcase can cause friction and damage to your hair, leading to shedding. Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and protect your hair. You can also wrap your hair in a silk or satin scarf or bonnet to keep it protected while you sleep.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to stop your African American hair from shedding:

Step 1: Identify the Cause of Your Shedding

Take a look at your hair care routine and lifestyle to determine what might be causing your shedding. Are you using harsh shampoos? Are you experiencing stress or hormonal changes? Identifying the cause can help you find the best solution for your hair.

Step 2: Use a Gentle Shampoo

Switch to a sulfate-free shampoo that is gentle on your hair and scalp. Look for shampoos that contain natural ingredients such as aloe vera, jojoba oil, and tea tree oil.

Step 3: Moisturize Your Hair

Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated. You can also use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to give your hair an extra boost of moisture.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Step 5: Protect Your Hair at Night

Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and protect your hair. You can also wrap your hair in a silk or satin scarf or bonnet to keep it protected while you sleep.

Tips to Prevent Shedding

Here are some additional tips to prevent shedding:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair such as braids and weaves.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.

FAQs

Q: Is shedding normal for African American hair?

A: Yes, shedding is normal for all hair types. However, excessive shedding can be a sign of a problem.

Q: Can stress cause hair shedding?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair shedding. Try to find ways to manage your stress such as exercise or meditation.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. Generally, it's best to wash your hair every 7-10 days to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Proper Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to reduce shedding and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips for proper hair care:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo that is gentle on your hair and scalp.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Protect your hair at night by using a silk or satin pillowcase or scarf.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.

By following these tips, you can reduce shedding and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, as it may take time to see results.
