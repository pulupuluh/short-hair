How To Tie Up Shoulder Length Curly Hair

Written by Oliver Mar 29, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of your curly hair getting in your face and way when you're working or running errands? Do you want to learn how to tie it up in a way that is both stylish and functional? Look no further! In this article, we'll show you how to tie up shoulder length curly hair in a few easy steps.

  2. Step by Step
  3. Tips for Tying Up Curly Hair
  4. FAQ

How to Tie Up Shoulder Length Curly Hair

The first step in tying up curly hair is to gather all of your hair into a high ponytail. You can use a hair tie or scrunchie to secure it in place.

Next, take a small section of hair from the bottom of the ponytail and wrap it around the base of the ponytail to cover the hair tie. Use bobby pins to secure the end of the section of hair to the base of the ponytail.

Now, take the rest of the hair in the ponytail and twist it into a bun shape. Use bobby pins to secure the bun in place.

You can leave some hair out to frame your face, or you can use bobby pins to pin back any hair that is in your way.

Step by Step

1. Gather all of your hair into a high ponytail.

2. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of the ponytail and wrap it around the base of the ponytail to cover the hair tie.

3. Use bobby pins to secure the end of the section of hair to the base of the ponytail.

4. Twist the rest of the hair in the ponytail into a bun shape.

5. Use bobby pins to secure the bun in place.

6. Leave some hair out to frame your face or pin back any hair that is in your way.

Tips for Tying Up Curly Hair

1. Use a hair tie or scrunchie that won't damage your hair.

2. Don't tie your hair too tight, as this can cause breakage and damage.

3. Use bobby pins that match the color of your hair to make them less noticeable.

4. Experiment with different hairstyles to find one that works best for you.

FAQ

Q. How often should I tie up my curly hair?

A. It's best to avoid tying up your hair every day, as this can cause damage and breakage. Try to alternate between wearing your hair down and tying it up.

Q. Can I tie up curly hair when it's wet?

A. It's best to avoid tying up wet hair, as this can cause damage and breakage. Wait until your hair is dry before tying it up.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

2. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet, as this will help prevent breakage.

3. Use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and keep them moisturized.

4. Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can cause damage to your hair.

5. Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

By following these tips and using the techniques outlined in this article, you can tie up your shoulder length curly hair in a way that is both functional and stylish.
