My Hair Fell Out After Dying It A Complete Guide In 2023

Written by Oliver Apr 09, 2023 Â· 10 min read

If you're reading this, chances are you've experienced the nightmare of having your hair fall out after dying it. It's a common problem that can happen to anyone, and it's essential to know how to prevent it from happening again. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about why your hair fell out after dying it and how to care for your hair afterward.

Description

When you dye your hair, the chemicals used in the process can be harsh and damaging to your hair. If your hair is already in poor condition, it can be more susceptible to damage, which can lead to hair falling out. Hair loss after dyeing is more common in people who have previously relaxed their hair, as the chemicals used in relaxing can weaken the hair follicles, making them more prone to breakage.

How to Prevent Hair from Falling Out After Dying It

There are several steps you can take to prevent hair loss after dyeing your hair:

1. Choose a Professional Hair Colorist

Choosing a professional hair colorist who uses high-quality products and has experience with dyeing relaxed hair is essential. They can help you choose a dye that is less damaging to your hair and prevent over-processing.

2. Avoid Overlapping

Overlapping is when hair dye is applied to already-dyed hair. It can cause the hair to become over-processed and lead to hair loss. To prevent overlapping, use a dye that is close to your natural hair color or wait at least six weeks before re-dyeing your hair.

3. Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning your hair weekly can help restore moisture and prevent hair breakage. Use a deep conditioner that is specifically formulated for relaxed hair, and leave it on for at least 20 minutes before rinsing.

4. Limit Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can cause hair breakage, especially if your hair is already damaged. Limit your use of flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers or use them on low heat settings.

Step by Step: How to Care for Your Hair After Dyeing It

After dyeing your hair, it's essential to take extra care of it to prevent hair loss. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to care for your hair after dyeing it:

1. Rinse Your Hair Thoroughly

After dyeing your hair, rinse it thoroughly with warm water to remove any excess dye. Avoid using hot water, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Use a Shampoo and Conditioner for Color-Treated Hair

Using a shampoo and conditioner specifically formulated for color-treated hair can help maintain the color and prevent further damage to your hair.

3. Avoid Brushing Wet Hair

Wet hair is more fragile and prone to breakage, so it's essential to avoid brushing it when it's wet. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.

4. Air Dry Your Hair

Avoid using a blow dryer to dry your hair, as it can cause further damage. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a microfiber towel to gently blot your hair.

5. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing and conditioning your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help restore moisture and prevent breakage. Use a leave-in conditioner that is specifically formulated for relaxed hair.

Tips for Preventing Hair Loss After Dyeing Your Hair

Here are some additional tips to help prevent hair loss after dyeing your hair:

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles can cause hair breakage, especially if your hair is already damaged. Avoid tight ponytails, braids, and other styles that pull on your hair.

2. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can help promote healthy hair growth. Eat foods that are rich in vitamins and minerals, such as leafy greens, nuts, and fish.

3. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help prevent split ends, which can lead to hair breakage. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

FAQ

1. Can hair loss from dyeing be permanent?

In most cases, hair loss from dyeing is not permanent. With proper care and treatment, your hair should grow back.

2. How long does it take for hair to grow back after falling out?

It can take several months for hair to grow back after falling out. The length of time can vary depending on the cause of hair loss and how well you care for your hair.

3. Can I dye my hair again after it fell out?

If your hair fell out after dyeing it, it's best to wait at least six weeks before dyeing it again. This will give your hair time to recover and prevent further damage.

Hair Care After Hair Loss

If your hair has fallen out after dyeing it, it's essential to take extra care of your hair. Here are some tips for caring for your hair after hair loss:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that is free of harsh chemicals and sulfates. Look for a shampoo that is specifically formulated for thinning hair.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as they can cause further damage. If you must use heat styling tools, use them on low heat settings.

3. Use a Hair Growth Treatment

Using a hair growth treatment, such as minoxidil, can help stimulate hair growth. Talk to your doctor or dermatologist about the best treatment options for your hair loss.

4. Be Gentle When Brushing Your Hair

Brushing your hair gently can help prevent further breakage. Use a soft-bristled brush or a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

5. Consider a Protective Style

Consider wearing a protective style, such as braids or a wig, while your hair grows back. This can help protect your hair from further damage and give it time to recover.

In conclusion, hair loss after dyeing your hair is a common problem that can happen to anyone. However, with proper care and treatment, your hair should grow back. Follow the steps and tips outlined in this guide to prevent hair loss and care for your hair after dyeing it.
