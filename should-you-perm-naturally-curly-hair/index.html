Should You Perm Naturally Curly Hair

Description

Perming naturally curly hair is a topic of debate among many women. Some argue that perming can help define curls and make them more manageable, while others believe it can damage the hair and cause breakage. Ultimately, the decision to perm naturally curly hair should be based on personal preference and hair type.

How to Perm Naturally Curly Hair

Perming naturally curly hair requires a chemical solution that breaks down the hair's natural structure and reforms it into a new shape. This solution is applied to the hair and left on for a certain amount of time, depending on the desired level of curl. After the solution is rinsed out, a neutralizer is applied to set the new shape of the hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for perming naturally curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition the hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Section the hair into small, even sections using clips.
  3. Apply the perm solution to each section of hair, starting at the roots and working down to the ends.
  4. Leave the solution on for the recommended amount of time, as specified in the instructions.
  5. Rinse the hair thoroughly with warm water to remove the solution.
  6. Apply the neutralizer to the hair and leave it on for the recommended amount of time.
  7. Rinse the hair again with warm water.
  8. Blow-dry the hair and style as desired.

Tips for Perming Naturally Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind before perming naturally curly hair:

  • Consult a professional stylist to determine if perming is right for your hair type.
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep hair hydrated before and after perming.
  • Avoid perming hair that is already damaged or overly processed.
  • Be prepared for some hair breakage or damage, as perming can be harsh on the hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about perming naturally curly hair:

  • Can perming damage naturally curly hair? Yes, perming can be damaging to hair, especially if it is already damaged or overly processed.
  • How long does a perm last on naturally curly hair? A perm can last anywhere from two to six months, depending on hair type and how well it is cared for.
  • Can perming enhance natural curls? Yes, perming can help define and enhance natural curls.
  • Is it better to get a professional perm or do it at home? It is recommended to get a professional perm, as the chemicals can be damaging if not used properly.

Hair Care after Perming Naturally Curly Hair

After perming naturally curly hair, it is important to take extra care to keep hair healthy and hydrated. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can further damage hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep hair hydrated.
  • Trim hair regularly to prevent split ends.

In conclusion, perming naturally curly hair can be a great way to define and enhance curls, but it is important to consider the potential damage and consult a professional stylist before making a decision. With proper care and maintenance, permed hair can look beautiful and healthy.
