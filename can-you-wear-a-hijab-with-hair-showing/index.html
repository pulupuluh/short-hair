Can You Wear A Hijab With Hair Showing

Written by Bardi Jul 09, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As a Muslim woman, wearing a hijab is a personal choice that is an important aspect of her faith. However, there is often confusion surrounding whether one can wear a hijab with hair showing. In this blog post, we will discuss the answer to this question and provide some tips on how to do so.

Description

A hijab is a headscarf worn by Muslim women to cover their hair and neck. It is a symbol of modesty and religious observance. Muslim women wear hijabs for various reasons, including religious beliefs, cultural traditions, and personal preferences. However, there is no one-size-fits-all answer to whether you can wear a hijab with your hair showing. It depends on your interpretation of Islamic teachings and your personal beliefs.

How to Wear a Hijab with Hair Showing

If you want to wear a hijab with hair showing, there are a few things you can do to make it work:

1. Use a Headband

A headband can be used to hold your hair in place and prevent it from peeking out from under your hijab. This is a great option for those with short hair or layers that tend to escape from traditional hijab styles.

2. Wear a High Bun

If you have long hair, you can wear it in a high bun that sits on top of your head. This will keep your hair from showing under your hijab and give you a stylish look.

3. Use a Cap

You can wear a cap or an under-scarf to cover your hair before putting on your hijab. This will ensure that your hair is fully covered and won't peek out from under your hijab.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to wear a hijab with hair showing:

Step 1: Tie Your Hair

Tie your hair in a high bun or ponytail. If you have layers, use bobby pins to secure them.

Step 2: Use a Headband

Put on a headband to hold your hair in place and prevent it from showing under your hijab.

Step 3: Wear an Under-Scarf

Wear an under-scarf to cover your hair completely. Make sure it fits comfortably and is not too tight.

Step 4: Put on Your Hijab

Put on your hijab, making sure it covers your neck and chest. Adjust it to your liking.

Step 5: Style Your Hijab

You can style your hijab in different ways, such as draping it over one shoulder or creating a turban-style look. Be creative and have fun!

Tips for Wearing a Hijab with Hair Showing

Here are some tips for wearing a hijab with hair showing:

1. Be Confident

Wearing a hijab with hair showing is a personal choice, so be confident in your decision. Don't let anyone tell you what you can or cannot do.

2. Experiment with Styles

There are many ways to wear a hijab, so experiment with different styles to find what works best for you. You can also mix and match different hijab styles to create a unique look.

3. Choose the Right Fabric

Choose a hijab fabric that is comfortable and breathable. This will help prevent your hair from getting too hot and sweaty, which can cause it to stick out.

4. Practice

Practice makes perfect, so take some time to practice wearing a hijab with hair showing. You can do this in front of a mirror or with a friend to get feedback on your style.

FAQ

Q: Is it permissible to wear a hijab with hair showing?

A: There is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. It depends on your interpretation of Islamic teachings and your personal beliefs.

Q: Can I wear a hijab with short hair?

A: Yes, you can wear a hijab with short hair. Use a headband to hold your hair in place and prevent it from showing under your hijab.

Q: How do I keep my hair from showing under my hijab?

A: You can use a headband, wear a cap or an under-scarf to cover your hair completely before putting on your hijab.

Hair Care

Wearing a hijab with hair showing can cause your hair to become dry and damaged. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy:

1. Moisturize

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to nourish your hair.

2. Avoid Heat

Avoid using heating tools such as a blow dryer or flat iron on your hair. This can cause your hair to become dry and brittle.

3. Trim Regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair healthy. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

In conclusion, wearing a hijab with hair showing is a personal choice that depends on your interpretation of Islamic teachings and your personal beliefs. If you choose to do so, use a headband, wear a cap or an under-scarf to cover your hair completely before putting on your hijab. Experiment with different styles and fabrics to find what works best for you. And don't forget to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and beautiful.
