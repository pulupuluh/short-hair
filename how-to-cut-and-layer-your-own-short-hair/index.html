How To Cut And Layer Your Own Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Feb 23, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of spending money and time at the salon for a simple haircut? Do you want to take matters into your own hands and give yourself a fresh, new look? Cutting and layering your own short hair may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut right at home. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps of cutting and layering short hair, as well as provide tips and tricks for maintaining your new style.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Layering Your Hair
  • Styling Your New Look
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting and layering your hair, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Sectioning clips or hair ties
  • A fine-tooth comb
  • A handheld mirror or a tri-fold mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp and designed specifically for cutting hair. Dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts, which can be difficult to fix later on.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to prepare it properly. Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. Comb out any tangles or knots, and make sure your hair is completely detangled before you start cutting.

Next, decide on the length and style you want to achieve. Consider your face shape, hair texture, and personal preferences. If you're not sure what style you want, search for inspiration online or in magazines.

Cutting Your Hair

Once you've prepared your hair and chosen your desired length, it's time to start cutting. Follow these steps:

  1. Divide your hair into sections. Use sectioning clips or hair ties to separate your hair into four sections: two on top and two on the bottom.
  2. Start with one of the bottom sections. Comb through the hair and hold it between your fingers at the desired length. Use your scissors to cut straight across. Repeat on the other bottom section.
  3. Move on to the top sections. Comb through the hair and bring it straight up using your fingers. Hold it between your fingers at the desired length, and use your scissors to cut straight across. Repeat on the other top section.
  4. Check for any uneven spots and trim as needed. Use your handheld mirror or tri-fold mirror to check the back of your head, and trim any uneven spots with your scissors.

Layering Your Hair

Layering your hair can add depth and texture to your new cut. Follow these steps:

  1. Divide your hair into sections. Use sectioning clips or hair ties to separate your hair into four sections: two on top and two on the bottom.
  2. Start with one of the bottom sections. Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up using your fingers. Use your scissors to cut at a slight angle, starting from the mid-length of your hair and working your way down to the end. Repeat on the other bottom section.
  3. Move on to the top sections. Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up using your fingers. Use your scissors to cut at a slight angle, starting from the mid-length of your hair and working your way down to the end. Repeat on the other top section.
  4. Check for any uneven spots and trim as needed. Use your handheld mirror or tri-fold mirror to check the back of your head, and trim any uneven spots with your scissors.

Styling Your New Look

Now that you've cut and layered your hair, it's time to style it. Use a blow dryer and a round brush to add volume and shape to your hair. You can also use a straightener or curling iron to add waves or curls.

Experiment with different styling products, such as hair gel, mousse, or hairspray, to achieve your desired look. Remember to use heat protectant spray before using any hot styling tools, and to avoid using too much product, which can weigh down your hair.

FAQs

Can I cut my own short hair?

Yes, you can cut your own short hair, but it's important to use the right tools and techniques to achieve a professional-looking cut.

How often should I cut my short hair?

Short hair typically needs to be cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always trim off a little more hair to even it out, or use hair clips or headbands to hide any uneven spots. If the mistake is severe, you may need to seek the help of a professional stylist.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you've cut and styled your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and shine. Follow these tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too frequently, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to comb out any knots or tangles gently.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain your style.
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals to nourish your hair from the inside out.

Cutting and layering your own short hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it's important to take your time and follow the steps carefully. With practice and patience, you'll soon be able to achieve the perfect short haircut right at home.
