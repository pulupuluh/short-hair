Starting Locs With Soft Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Jul 03, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have soft hair and are thinking about starting locs, you may be wondering where to begin. Starting locs with soft hair requires a slightly different approach than with other hair types. In this article, we will provide you with all the information you need to start your loc journey with soft hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Start Locs with Soft Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Starting Locs with Soft Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Soft Hair Locs

Description

Soft hair is hair that is naturally fine, smooth, and silky. It can be more challenging to start locs with soft hair because the hair is more prone to unraveling and slipping out of the locs. However, with proper techniques and care, you can successfully start and maintain locs with soft hair.

How to Start Locs with Soft Hair

Before starting your locs, it's essential to understand that the process can take several months to years to complete. It's a long-term commitment that requires patience and dedication. Here are some steps to consider when starting locs with soft hair:

1. Choose a Starting Method

There are different methods for starting locs, such as the two-strand twist method, comb coil method, and interlocking. Research each method and decide which one works best for your hair type, texture, and lifestyle.

2. Prepare Your Hair

Before starting your locs, make sure your hair is clean and free of any product buildup. Deep condition your hair to ensure it's moisturized and healthy, which will make it easier to maintain your locs.

3. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using a rat-tail comb. The size of the sections will determine the size of your locs. Smaller sections will result in smaller locs, while larger sections will result in larger locs.

4. Start Your Locs

Using your chosen method, begin the locing process by twisting or coiling the hair in each section. Make sure to twist tightly to ensure the hair stays in place.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to starting locs with soft hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start with clean, moisturized hair. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup. Deep condition your hair to ensure it's moisturized and healthy.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using a rat-tail comb. The size of the sections will determine the size of your locs. Smaller sections will result in smaller locs, while larger sections will result in larger locs.

Step 3: Start Your Locs

Using your chosen method, begin the locing process by twisting or coiling the hair in each section. Make sure to twist tightly to ensure the hair stays in place.

Step 4: Maintain Your Locs

Maintain your locs by keeping them clean, moisturized, and well-groomed. Avoid using heavy products that can cause buildup and weigh down your hair.

Tips for Starting Locs with Soft Hair

Here are some tips to help you start and maintain your locs with soft hair:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup before starting your locs.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can cause buildup and weigh down your hair.
  • Wear a satin scarf or bonnet to bed to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

FAQs

How long does it take to start locs with soft hair?

The process of starting locs with soft hair can take several months to years to complete, depending on the method used and how fast your hair grows.

Can you start locs with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can start locs with relaxed hair. However, it's essential to know that relaxed hair may be more prone to breakage and damage, so it requires extra care and attention.

Do you have to shave your head to start locs?

No, you don't have to shave your head to start locs. You can start locs with any hair length, but the process may take longer for shorter hair.

Hair Care for Soft Hair Locs

Maintaining soft hair locs requires proper care and attention. Here are some tips to help you care for your locs:

  • Wash your locs regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Keep your scalp clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can cause buildup and weigh down your hair.
  • Protect your locs from harsh weather conditions, such as extreme heat and cold.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

Starting locs with soft hair may require extra care and attention, but with the right techniques and hair care, you can successfully start and maintain your locs. Remember to be patient and enjoy the journey!
