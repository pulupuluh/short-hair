How To Cut Your Own Hair Layers In Front A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby May 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune for haircuts? Do you want to save money and cut your own hair? If you're looking to add some layers to the front of your hair, you're in luck. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own hair layers in front. From how to section your hair to what tools you need, we've got you covered. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. How to Section Your Hair
  2. Tools You Need
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for a Professional-Looking Cut
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care Tips

How to Section Your Hair

The first step in cutting your own hair layers in front is to section your hair. This will help ensure that you cut the layers evenly. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by separating the top section of your hair from the bottom section. Use a comb to create a horizontal parting just above your ears.
  2. Clip the top section of your hair out of the way.
  3. Next, create a vertical parting down the center of your head. This will create two sections: left and right.
  4. Clip the left section out of the way.
  5. Repeat on the right side.

Once you've sectioned your hair, you're ready to start cutting!

Tools You Need

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • A comb
  • Clips to hold your hair in place

Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it difficult to cut.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you've sectioned your hair and gathered your tools, it's time to start cutting. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start with the front section of your hair. This is the section that frames your face.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the front of your head. This should be about 1 inch wide.
  3. Hold the hair between your fingers and lift it up. Use your fingers as a guide to determine how much hair you want to cut.
  4. Use the scissors to cut the hair straight across. Start at the outer edge and work your way in.
  5. Repeat on the other side.
  6. Take another section of hair from the front of your head. This section should be slightly lower than the first section.
  7. Again, use your fingers as a guide to determine how much hair you want to cut.
  8. Cut the hair straight across using the same technique as before.
  9. Continue taking sections of hair and cutting them until you reach the desired length.
  10. Release the top section of your hair and blend the layers together.

Remember to take your time and cut small sections of hair. It's easier to cut more hair off than to try to fix a mistake.

Tips for a Professional-Looking Cut

If you want your hair to look like it was cut by a professional, here are a few tips:

  • Use sharp scissors
  • Cut small sections of hair
  • Don't cut too much off at once
  • Blend the layers together for a seamless look
  • Check your work in a mirror

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair layers in front if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair layers in front if you have curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair can be more difficult to cut than straight hair. Make sure you cut your hair when it's dry and use caution when cutting curly hair.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended that you cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always trim a little more off to fix it. If it's a major mistake, you may need to visit a professional hairstylist to fix it.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you've cut your own hair layers in front, here are a few tips to keep your hair looking healthy:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat tools too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

Congratulations, you've just learned how to cut your own hair layers in front. With a little practice, you'll be able to save money and cut your own hair like a pro!
