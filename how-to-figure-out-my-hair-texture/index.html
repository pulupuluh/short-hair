How To Figure Out My Hair Texture A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Sep 02, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you struggling to figure out your hair texture? Understanding your hair texture is crucial in determining the right hair care routine and products that work best for you. In this step-by-step guide, we'll walk you through the process of figuring out your hair texture and provide tips on how to care for it.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Observe Your Hair
  • Step 2: Determine Your Hair Type
  • Step 3: Assess Your Hair's Porosity
  • Step 4: Check Your Hair's Elasticity
  • Tips for Caring for Your Hair Texture
  • FAQs about Hair Texture
  • How to Care for Your Hair Texture

Step 1: Observe Your Hair

The first step in figuring out your hair texture is to simply observe your hair. Look at your hair in its natural state and ask yourself the following questions:

  • Is my hair straight, wavy, curly, or coily?
  • Does my hair have a lot of volume or is it flat?
  • Does my hair feel fine or coarse?
  • Is my hair prone to frizz?

Take note of your answers to these questions as they will help determine your hair type and texture.

Step 2: Determine Your Hair Type

Once you have observed your hair, the next step is to determine your hair type. There are four main hair types:

  • Type 1: Straight hair
  • Type 2: Wavy hair
  • Type 3: Curly hair
  • Type 4: Coily hair

Your hair type is determined by the shape of your hair follicle. Straight hair has a round follicle, while wavy, curly, and coily hair have oval or elliptical follicles.

Step 3: Assess Your Hair's Porosity

Porosity refers to your hair's ability to absorb and retain moisture. There are three levels of porosity:

  • Low porosity: Hair that is resistant to absorbing moisture
  • Normal porosity: Hair that absorbs and retains moisture easily
  • High porosity: Hair that absorbs moisture quickly but has difficulty retaining it

To determine your hair's porosity, take a strand of hair and place it in a bowl of water. If it sinks to the bottom quickly, you have high porosity hair. If it takes a while to sink, you have low porosity hair. If it sinks slowly, you have normal porosity hair.

Step 4: Check Your Hair's Elasticity

Elasticity refers to your hair's ability to stretch and return to its natural state without breaking. To test your hair's elasticity, take a strand of hair and gently stretch it. If it stretches and returns to its natural state without breaking, you have good elasticity. If it stretches and breaks, you have low elasticity.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair Texture

  • Use products specifically designed for your hair type and texture.
  • Avoid using heat tools on a daily basis.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions.

FAQs about Hair Texture

What is the difference between hair type and hair texture?

Hair type refers to the shape of your hair follicle, while hair texture refers to the thickness and diameter of each individual strand of hair.

Can my hair texture change over time?

Yes, your hair texture can change due to hormonal changes, aging, and certain medical conditions.

How to Care for Your Hair Texture

Knowing your hair texture is the first step in determining the right hair care routine and products for you. Here are some general tips for caring for each hair texture:

  • Straight hair: Avoid heavy products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Wavy hair: Use a lightweight styling product to enhance your natural waves.
  • Curly hair: Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to combat frizz.
  • Coily hair: Use a leave-in conditioner to provide extra moisture.

By following these tips and understanding your hair texture, you can achieve healthy, beautiful hair that looks and feels its best.
