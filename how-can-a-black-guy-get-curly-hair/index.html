How Can A Black Guy Get Curly Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Christine Aug 15, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're a black guy and you want to get curly hair, you're not alone. Many men of African descent have naturally tight, coily hair that can be difficult to style. However, with some effort and the right techniques, it is possible to achieve curly hair that looks great and is easy to maintain. In this article, we'll show you how to get curly hair as a black guy, including step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Get Curly Hair as a Black Guy

The first step to getting curly hair as a black guy is to understand your hair type. Most black men have hair that is tightly coiled and naturally dry, which can make it difficult to achieve the look you want. However, there are several methods you can use to create curls that look great and are easy to maintain.

Methods for Getting Curly Hair

There are several methods you can use to get curly hair as a black guy. Some of the most popular methods include:

  • Twist-outs: This involves twisting small sections of hair and letting them dry, creating curls that are defined and bouncy.
  • Bantu knots: This method involves twisting small sections of hair and wrapping them into tight knots, creating curls that are more tightly coiled.
  • Rod sets: This involves wrapping small sections of hair around perm rods and letting them dry, creating curls that are uniform in size and shape.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for getting curly hair as a black guy:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Shampoo and condition your hair, then towel-dry it gently.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help keep it moisturized. You can also apply a styling cream or gel to help define your curls.
  3. Divide your hair into small sections, using clips to keep them separate.
  4. Take one small section of hair and twist it tightly, starting at the root and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Repeat this process with each section of hair, until your entire head is twisted.
  6. Let your hair air dry or sit under a hooded dryer until it is completely dry.
  7. Once your hair is dry, gently untwist each section to reveal your curls.
  8. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to separate your curls and create the look you want.
  9. Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to add shine and help control frizz.
  10. Repeat this process as needed to maintain your curls.

Tips for Getting and Maintaining Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you get and maintain curly hair as a black guy:

  • Keep your hair moisturized by using a leave-in conditioner and a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair, as this can lead to dryness and breakage.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to help prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or styling products that can weigh your hair down.
  • Consider getting regular trims to help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting curly hair as a black guy:

Can I get curly hair if my hair is naturally straight?

If your hair is naturally straight, it may be difficult to achieve curly hair without using heat or chemical treatments. However, you can try using perm rods or flexi rods to create temporary curls.

How often should I wash my hair?

It's important to keep your hair clean and free of buildup, but washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils. Aim to wash your hair once or twice a week, depending on your hair type and how active you are.

Can I use a hair dryer to speed up the drying process?

While it's possible to use a hair dryer to speed up the drying process, it's best to let your hair air dry if possible. Using heat on your hair can lead to dryness and breakage, so it's important to be gentle with your hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair requires a bit of extra effort, but it's worth it to keep your curls looking their best. Here are some tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos or styling products that can dry out your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to help keep your hair hydrated.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and keep your curls looking healthy.

By following these tips and using the right techniques, you can achieve curly hair as a black guy that looks great and is easy to maintain. Whether you prefer twist-outs, Bantu knots, or rod sets, there is a method that will work for you. So why not give it a try and see how great your curly hair can look?
