Why Pubic Hair Grows Thicker Understanding The Science Behind It

Written by Daniel Jul 20, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Pubic hair is a natural part of human anatomy, but it's also one that many people are self-conscious about. While some people choose to remove their pubic hair, others prefer to let it grow naturally. However, one thing that many people notice is that pubic hair seems to grow thicker over time. In this blog post, we'll explore why pubic hair grows thicker and what you can do about it.

What Causes Pubic Hair to Grow Thicker?

Pubic hair, like all hair on the body, is made up of protein fibers called keratin. These fibers grow out of hair follicles, which are located in the skin. The thickness of pubic hair is determined by a number of factors, including genetics, hormones, and age.

During puberty, hormones like testosterone and estrogen cause the hair follicles in the pubic area to grow thicker and more densely. This is why many people notice that their pubic hair grows thicker during adolescence. However, even after puberty, the hair follicles in the pubic area can continue to grow thicker due to hormonal fluctuations that occur throughout life.

Is Thicker Pubic Hair Normal?

Yes, thicker pubic hair is completely normal. As we mentioned earlier, the thickness of your pubic hair is determined by a variety of factors, including genetics and hormones. Some people naturally have thicker pubic hair than others, and that's perfectly fine. If you're concerned about the thickness of your pubic hair, there are things you can do to manage it (more on that later).

How to Deal with Thicker Pubic Hair

If you're not a fan of the thickness of your pubic hair, there are a few things you can do to manage it:

1. Shave or Wax

One of the most common ways to manage pubic hair is to shave or wax it. Shaving involves using a razor to remove the hair at the surface of the skin, while waxing involves pulling the hair out at the root using hot wax. Both methods can be effective in reducing the thickness of pubic hair, but they can also be irritating to the skin and cause ingrown hairs.

2. Use Hair Removal Creams

Hair removal creams, also known as depilatory creams, are another option for managing pubic hair. These creams dissolve the hair at the surface of the skin, making it easy to wipe away. However, like shaving and waxing, hair removal creams can be irritating to the skin.

3. Trim with Scissors

If you're not interested in removing all of your pubic hair, you can simply trim it with scissors or clippers. This can help reduce the thickness of the hair without completely removing it.

Pubic Hair Care Tips

Whether you choose to remove your pubic hair or simply manage its thickness, it's important to take care of your skin in the pubic area. Here are a few tips:

1. Keep the Area Clean

Wash the pubic area regularly with a gentle cleanser to keep it clean and free of bacteria.

2. Moisturize the Skin

After removing or trimming pubic hair, apply a moisturizer to soothe the skin and prevent irritation.

3. Avoid Tight Clothing

Tight clothing can rub against the skin and cause irritation, so opt for loose-fitting clothing in the pubic area.

FAQs

1. Does shaving make pubic hair grow back thicker?

No, shaving does not make pubic hair grow back thicker. This is a common myth.

2. Can I use regular hair removal cream on my pubic hair?

No, it's not recommended to use regular hair removal cream on your pubic hair. Pubic hair is thicker and coarser than the hair on your legs or arms, so you'll need a cream that's specifically designed for the pubic area.

3. How often should I trim my pubic hair?

This is a personal preference. Some people prefer to trim their pubic hair once a week, while others may only need to trim it every few weeks.

In conclusion, thicker pubic hair is completely normal and is determined by a variety of factors. If you're not a fan of the thickness of your pubic hair, there are a few ways to manage it, including shaving, waxing, and trimming. Whatever method you choose, be sure to take care of your skin in the pubic area by keeping it clean and moisturized.
