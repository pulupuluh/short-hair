How To Cut Long Layers On Your Own Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 07, 2023 · 7 min read

Long layered hair is a timeless classic look that can be styled in a variety of ways. But it can be pricey to visit a salon to get your hair cut, especially if you want to maintain your long layered hair regularly. That's why learning how to cut long layers on your own hair is a valuable skill to have. In this article, we'll guide you through the step-by-step process of cutting long layers on your own hair.

How To Cut Long Layers On Your Own Hair

Cutting your own hair can seem daunting, but with the right tools and technique, it can be a straightforward process. Here are the steps you need to follow to cut long layers on your own hair.

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors and a comb.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair as you would normally. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair Into Sections

Divide your hair into sections. You can do this by parting your hair down the middle and then dividing each side into two sections. Clip each section out of the way with hair clips.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, and use the scissors to cut at an angle. Make sure you cut at an angle, not straight across, to create a layered effect.

Work your way up your hair, taking small sections, and cutting at an angle. Make sure you cut each section to the same length, so your layers are even.

Step 5: Blend The Layers

Once you have cut all the layers, it's time to blend them. Take a comb and comb through your hair, blending the layers so they look natural.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, check your work. Make sure your layers are even and blended. If you see any uneven sections, go back and trim them.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting long layers on your own hair:

  • Make sure you use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to take small sections and cut slowly than to try to cut a lot at once.
  • Take breaks. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so make sure you take breaks to rest your arms and hands.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long layers on your own hair:

Can I cut long layers on curly hair?

Yes! You can cut long layers on curly hair, but keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries. So, if you want long layers, you may need to cut your hair longer than you think.

Can I cut long layers on short hair?

Long layers are designed for long hair, but you can still add layers to short hair. Keep in mind that short hair can be harder to cut, so it's best to start with small sections and work your way up.

Hair Care

Once you've cut your long layers, it's essential to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a good-quality shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are designed for your hair type, whether it's curly, straight, or somewhere in between.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair too often. Heat can damage your hair, so try to limit your use of hair dryers, straighteners, and curling irons.
  • Get regular haircuts. Even if you're cutting your own hair, it's important to get regular haircuts to keep your layers looking fresh.

Cutting long layers on your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-quality look without leaving your home. So, grab your scissors and get cutting!
