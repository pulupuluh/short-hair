How To Use Texturizing Scissors On Long Hair

Written by Petter Apr 22, 2023 · 8 min read

Texturizing scissors are an essential tool for any hairstylist or barber looking to add texture to long hair. These scissors are designed to create different lengths of hair by removing bulk and thinning out the hair. Texturizing scissors can also add movement and bounce to long hair, making it easier to style and manage. In this article, we will show you how to use texturizing scissors on long hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Use Texturizing Scissors on Long Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Using Texturizing Scissors on Long Hair
  • FAQ

Description

Texturizing scissors are also called thinning shears or blending shears. They are scissors with teeth or notches on one blade and a straight edge on the other blade. These teeth or notches can remove some of the hair while cutting, which creates texture and volume. Texturizing scissors are used for various hair types and lengths, but they are especially useful for long hair.

How to Use Texturizing Scissors on Long Hair

Before using texturizing scissors on long hair, make sure the hair is clean and free of tangles. Use a comb to separate the hair into sections, and clip each section to keep it in place. Take one section of hair and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Use the texturizing scissors to cut small sections of hair, starting from the tips and working your way up.

Make sure to use the texturizing scissors sparingly at first, as you can always go back and cut more if needed. Use the texturizing scissors to create layers and movement in the hair, but be careful not to cut too much hair in one section. Move on to the next section of hair until you have texturized all sections.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to using texturizing scissors on long hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair that is free of tangles.
  2. Divide the hair into sections using a comb and clips.
  3. Take one section of hair and hold it between your index and middle fingers.
  4. Use the texturizing scissors to cut small sections of hair, starting from the tips and working your way up.
  5. Cut at an angle to create layers and movement in the hair.
  6. Use the texturizing scissors sparingly at first, and cut more if needed.
  7. Move on to the next section of hair until you have texturized all sections.
  8. Check the hair for any unevenness or bald spots, and make any necessary adjustments.
  9. Style the hair as desired.
  10. Clean and sanitize the texturizing scissors before and after use.

Tips for Using Texturizing Scissors on Long Hair

Here are some tips for using texturizing scissors on long hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut sparingly, as you can always cut more if needed.
  • Use the texturizing scissors to create layers and movement in the hair, but be careful not to cut too much hair in one section.
  • Use the straight edge of the texturizing scissors to blend the cut sections of hair with the rest of the hair.
  • Check the hair for any unevenness or bald spots, and make any necessary adjustments.
  • Clean and sanitize the texturizing scissors before and after use to prevent the spread of bacteria or infections.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about using texturizing scissors on long hair:

  • What is the difference between texturizing scissors and regular scissors? Texturizing scissors have teeth or notches on one blade and a straight edge on the other blade. These teeth or notches can remove some of the hair while cutting, which creates texture and volume.
  • Can I use texturizing scissors on wet hair? It is best to use texturizing scissors on dry hair, as wet hair can stretch and look different when dry.
  • How often should I use texturizing scissors on long hair? It depends on the individual's hair type and length. Generally, texturizing scissors can be used every 6-8 weeks to maintain the hair's texture and volume.

Hair Care Tips after Texturizing Hair

After texturizing hair, it is essential to take care of the hair to maintain its texture and volume. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type and texture.
  • Avoid using too much heat on the hair, as it can damage the hair and remove its texture.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to keep the hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or hair products that can strip the hair of its natural oils.
  • Trim the hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and texture.

Now that you know how to use texturizing scissors on long hair, you can add texture and volume to your hair or your clients' hair. Remember to use the texturizing scissors sparingly and always clean and sanitize them before and after use. Happy texturizing!
