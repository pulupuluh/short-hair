How To Style Your Hair Like The 50S A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 05, 2023 · 8 min read

Do you love the classic glamour of the 1950s? From the iconic fashion to the retro hairstyles, there’s something undeniably alluring about this era. If you’re looking to channel your inner pin-up girl or rockabilly rebel, you’re in luck. In this post, we’ll show you how to style your hair like the 50s in just a few easy steps. Follow along for a vintage-inspired look that’s sure to turn heads.

Description

The 1950s were an era of impeccable style, from the chic clothing to the carefully coiffed hair. Women of this time often wore their hair in glamorous updos, voluminous curls, or sleek and straight styles. Whether you’re dressing up for a retro-themed party or simply love the vintage look, there are plenty of ways to style your hair like the 50s.

How to Style Your Hair Like the 50s: Step by Step

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you begin styling, make sure your hair is clean and completely dry. This will help your hair hold its shape and prevent any unwanted frizz or flyaways. Use a shampoo and conditioner that’s right for your hair type, and be sure to towel dry your hair thoroughly before moving on to the next step.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair with Product

To help your hair hold its shape, it’s important to use the right styling products. For a classic 50s look, we recommend using a volumizing mousse or spray to add body and texture to your hair. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the roots and ends.

Step 3: Choose Your Style

There are countless ways to style your hair like the 50s, from sleek and straight to voluminous curls and elegant updos. Choose a style that best suits your hair type and personal preference.

Step 4: Create Your Look

Once you’ve chosen your style, it’s time to get to work. Follow these steps for some of the most popular 50s hairdos:

For Vintage Waves:

  1. Divide your hair into sections and curl each section using a medium-sized curling iron.
  2. Once your curls have cooled, gently brush them out using a paddle brush or comb.
  3. Use bobby pins to pin your hair in place, creating a wave pattern that frames your face.
  4. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your waves in place.

For a Classic Updo:

  1. Gather your hair into a high ponytail and secure it with an elastic band.
  2. Twist the ponytail into a bun shape, securing it with bobby pins as you go.
  3. Pin any loose strands in place and finish with a light hold hairspray.

Step 5: Add Accessories

No 50s hairdo is complete without some retro accessories. Try adding a flower clip, headband, or scarf to your hair for a pop of color and vintage flair.

Tips for Styling Your Hair Like the 50s

Here are a few more tips to keep in mind as you create your 50s-inspired look:

  • Experiment with different hair products to find the ones that work best for your hair type.
  • If you’re new to styling your hair, start with a simple look and work your way up to more complicated styles.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help. Enlist a friend or professional stylist to help you achieve the perfect look.

FAQ

Q: Can I style my hair like the 50s if I have short hair?

A: Absolutely! While some 50s hairstyles are more suited to longer hair, there are plenty of options for short hair as well. Try a sleek, side-parted bob or a cute pixie cut with a vintage twist.

Q: How can I make my 50s hairstyle last all day?

A: Using the right styling products and techniques can help your hair hold its shape all day long. Try using a light hold hairspray or setting spray to keep your curls or updo in place.

Q: What kind of accessories should I use for a 50s hairstyle?

A: You can’t go wrong with a classic flower clip, headband, or scarf. Look for accessories in bold colors or prints to really make a statement.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy, Beautiful Hair

Whether you’re styling your hair like the 50s or rocking a modern look, it’s important to take good care of your hair. Here are a few tips for healthy, beautiful hair:

  • Stick to a regular hair care routine, including shampooing, conditioning, and occasional deep conditioning treatments.
  • Avoid over-styling your hair with heat tools, which can cause damage and breakage.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water throughout the day.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be well on your way to rocking a vintage-inspired 50s hairstyle. Have fun experimenting with different looks and accessories, and don’t be afraid to make it your own. Whether you’re channeling Marilyn Monroe or James Dean, a little bit of retro flair is always in style.
