What Grocery Stores Were Popular In The 60S

Written by Petter Mar 13, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you curious about what grocery stores were popular in the 60s? The 1960s was a decade of significant social and cultural changes in America. It was also a time of significant changes in the way people shopped for groceries. In this article, we will take a look at the popular grocery stores during this era.

Popular Grocery Stores in the 60s

The 60s were a time when supermarkets began to dominate the grocery industry. The top grocery stores during this era were Safeway, Kroger, A&P, and Piggly Wiggly. These stores were popular among shoppers due to their wide variety of products and low prices.

Safeway

Safeway was one of the most popular grocery stores in the 60s. The store had a reputation for offering a wide variety of products, including fresh produce, meat, and dairy products. Safeway also introduced the concept of the "discount card," which allowed shoppers to save money on their purchases.

Kroger

Kroger was another popular grocery store in the 60s. The store was known for its low prices and high-quality products. Kroger was also one of the first grocery stores to introduce the concept of a "self-service" store, where customers could pick out their own groceries and pay for them at the checkout.

A&P

A&P was a popular grocery store chain in the 60s. The store was known for its low prices and high-quality products. A&P was also one of the first grocery stores to introduce the concept of a "supermarket," which offered a wide variety of products under one roof.

Piggly Wiggly

Piggly Wiggly was a popular grocery store in the 60s. The store was known for its low prices and high-quality products. Piggly Wiggly was also one of the first grocery stores to introduce the concept of a "self-service" store, where customers could pick out their own groceries and pay for them at the checkout.

Conclusion

The 60s were a time of significant changes in the grocery industry. Supermarkets began to dominate the market, and stores like Safeway, Kroger, A&P, and Piggly Wiggly were popular among shoppers. These stores offered a wide variety of products at low prices, making grocery shopping more accessible to the general public.

Tips for Grocery Shopping in the 60s

If you were grocery shopping in the 60s, here are some tips that might have helped:

  • Bring a list of the items you need to avoid overspending
  • Bring your own bags to save money on bags
  • Take advantage of discounts and coupons
  • Shop during off-peak hours to avoid crowds

How to Save Money on Groceries in the 60s

If you were looking to save money on groceries in the 60s, here are some tips:

  • Buy generic or store-brand products
  • Take advantage of discounts and coupons
  • Buy in bulk to save money
  • Avoid buying pre-packaged and processed foods

Hair Care in the 60s

The 60s were a time of significant changes in fashion and beauty. Women's hairstyles became more voluminous and textured, with trends like the beehive, the bouffant, and the flip becoming popular. Hair care products like hairspray, mousse, and gel were also introduced during this era.

If you were looking to achieve the perfect 60s hairstyle, here are some tips:

  • Tease your hair to create volume
  • Use hairspray to hold your style in place
  • Accessorize with headbands, scarves, and barrettes
  • Try a new hairstyle every few weeks to keep things fresh

Overall, the 60s were a time of significant changes in the grocery industry, fashion, and beauty. By understanding what grocery stores were popular during this era, we can gain a better understanding of the cultural and social changes that occurred during this time.
