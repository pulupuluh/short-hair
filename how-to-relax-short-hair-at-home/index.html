How To Relax Short Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

If you have short hair and want to relax it at home, you've come to the right place. Relaxing hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done safely and effectively. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps of how to relax short hair at home, give you some tips and tricks, and answer some frequently asked questions.

How to Relax Short Hair at Home

Relaxing short hair at home requires some preparation and the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • A relaxer kit
  • A tail comb
  • A timer
  • A towel
  • A neutralizing shampoo
  • A deep conditioner

Before you start, make sure to read the instructions on the relaxer kit carefully. Follow the instructions exactly as written to avoid damaging your hair and scalp.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to relax short hair at home:

  1. Divide your hair into four sections using the tail comb.
  2. Apply petroleum jelly around your hairline and ears to protect your skin from the relaxer.
  3. Follow the instructions on the relaxer kit to mix the relaxer.
  4. Starting at the back of your head, apply the relaxer to one section at a time, using the tail comb to distribute it evenly.
  5. Leave the relaxer on for the amount of time specified in the instructions. Use a timer to keep track of the time.
  6. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  7. Apply the neutralizing shampoo and work it into a lather. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.
  8. Apply the deep conditioner and leave it on for the amount of time specified in the instructions. Rinse thoroughly with cool water.
  9. Blot your hair with a towel to remove excess water. Do not rub your hair.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Relaxing Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you relax your short hair safely and effectively:

  • Do a strand test before applying the relaxer to your entire head to make sure you don't have an allergic reaction.
  • Use a timer to keep track of the time the relaxer is on your hair.
  • Don't leave the relaxer on for longer than the instructions specify.
  • Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water after applying the relaxer.
  • Apply the neutralizing shampoo immediately after rinsing the relaxer out of your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioner to help restore moisture to your hair after relaxing it.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools for a few days after relaxing your hair to avoid damaging it further.

FAQs About Relaxing Short Hair

Q: Can I relax my hair if it's already damaged?

A: It's not recommended to relax hair that's already damaged. Wait until your hair is healthy before you try relaxing it.

Q: How often should I relax my hair?

A: It's best to wait at least eight weeks between relaxer treatments to avoid damaging your hair and scalp.

Q: Can I color my hair after relaxing it?

A: It's best to wait at least two weeks after relaxing your hair before coloring it.

Hair Care After Relaxing Short Hair

After relaxing your short hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly.
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos and conditioners.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid pulling your hair too tightly when styling it.

Now that you know how to relax short hair at home, you can achieve the look you want without having to go to a salon. Just remember to follow the instructions carefully, use the right tools, and take good care of your hair after relaxing it. Happy relaxing!
