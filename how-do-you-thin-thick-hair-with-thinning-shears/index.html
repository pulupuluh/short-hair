How Do You Thin Thick Hair With Thinning Shears A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have thick hair, you know how difficult it can be to manage. It can be heavy, unmanageable, and difficult to style. That's where thinning shears come in. Thinning shears are a tool used by hair stylists to remove bulk and weight from thick hair. In this article, we will show you how to thin thick hair with thinning shears in a step-by-step guide.

Description

Thinning shears are scissors with teeth that are used to remove bulk from the hair. They are not like regular scissors that cut hair straight across. Thinning shears are designed to remove hair in a more gradual and subtle way, which results in a more natural look. They are ideal for people with thick hair who want to reduce the weight and volume of their hair without sacrificing length.

How to Thin Thick Hair with Thinning Shears

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you get an even cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips. This will make it easier to work on one section at a time.

Step 3: Start with the Bottom Layer

Start thinning your hair from the bottom layer. This will ensure that you don't remove too much hair from the top layer, which can make your hair look flat.

Step 4: Hold the Thinning Shears Correctly

Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle to your hair. This will ensure that you don't cut too much hair at once.

Step 5: Cut Your Hair

Using the thinning shears, cut your hair in small sections. Start at the bottom of the hair and work your way up.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

As you work, check your progress in the mirror. Make sure that you are thinning your hair evenly and that you are not removing too much hair.

Step 7: Repeat on the Other Sections

Repeat the process on the other sections of your hair. Remember to start with the bottom layer and work your way up.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you have thinned your hair, style it as usual. You will notice that your hair is lighter and more manageable.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Make sure that you are holding the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle to your hair.
  • Check your progress in the mirror to ensure that you are thinning your hair evenly.
  • Don't over-thin your hair, as this can make it look wispy and thin.
  • Always use sharp thinning shears to ensure a clean cut.

FAQ

Can you use thinning shears on any type of hair?

Thinning shears are best used on thick, heavy hair. If you have fine or thin hair, using thinning shears can make your hair look even thinner.

Can you use thinning shears to create layers?

Yes, thinning shears can be used to create layers. However, it's best to leave layering to a professional stylist, as it can be difficult to do on your own.

How often should you thin your hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to thin your hair every six to eight weeks.

Hair Care

Thinning your hair can make it more manageable, but it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy. Use a good shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type, and avoid using heat styling tools too often.

By following these steps, you can thin your thick hair with thinning shears and enjoy a more manageable, lighter hairstyle. Remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
