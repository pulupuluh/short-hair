How To Care For Hair During Chemo

Written by Aprilia Apr 03, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Dealing with cancer is tough, and one of the most difficult parts of treatment can be hair loss. Chemotherapy drugs can cause hair loss all over your body, including your scalp, eyebrows, and eyelashes. But there are things you can do to help care for your hair during chemo and minimize the damage. In this article, we'll cover the basics of hair care during chemotherapy treatment.

Table of Contents

  • How to Care for Hair During Chemo
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Hair Care During Chemo
  • FAQs

How to Care for Hair During Chemo

Chemotherapy drugs attack rapidly dividing cells in the body, which includes hair follicles. As a result, you'll likely experience hair loss within the first few weeks of treatment. Here are some steps you can take to care for your hair during chemo:

1. Use a gentle shampoo

During chemotherapy, your hair and scalp may become more sensitive. Use a gentle, fragrance-free shampoo to minimize irritation. Avoid harsh chemicals or products that contain alcohol.

2. Avoid heat styling tools

Heat styling tools like blow dryers, curling irons, and straighteners can further damage your hair. Avoid using these tools and let your hair air dry instead. If you must use a blow dryer, use the cool setting.

3. Be gentle when brushing or combing

Use a soft-bristled brush or wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause it to break. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

4. Protect your scalp from the sun

If you've lost your hair, your scalp may be more sensitive to the sun. Wear a hat or scarf when you go outside to protect your scalp from UV rays.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for caring for your hair during chemotherapy:

Step 1: Choose a gentle shampoo

Look for a shampoo that's free of harsh chemicals and fragrances. Try to wash your hair no more than once a week to minimize damage.

Step 2: Detangle your hair

Gently detangle your hair using a soft-bristled brush or wide-tooth comb. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots.

Step 3: Avoid heat styling tools

Avoid using blow dryers, curling irons, and straighteners. Let your hair air dry instead.

Step 4: Protect your scalp from the sun

Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun's UV rays.

Tips for Hair Care During Chemo

Here are some additional tips for caring for your hair during chemotherapy:

  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce friction on your hair while you sleep
  • Avoid using hair ties or clips that are too tight, as these can cause breakage
  • Consider cutting your hair short before starting chemotherapy to make the hair loss less noticeable
  • Try wearing a wig or hairpiece if you're uncomfortable with your hair loss

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair care during chemotherapy:

Q: Will my hair grow back after chemotherapy?

A: Yes, your hair will likely grow back after chemotherapy. However, it may look and feel different than it did before treatment.

Q: How long will it take for my hair to grow back?

A: It can take several months for your hair to grow back after chemotherapy. The amount of time it takes will depend on the type of chemotherapy drugs you receive.

Q: Can I dye my hair during chemotherapy?

A: It's best to avoid dyeing your hair during chemotherapy, as the chemicals can further damage your hair and scalp.

Caring for your hair during chemotherapy can be challenging, but with these tips and tricks, you can help minimize the damage and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and don't hesitate to reach out to your healthcare provider for additional support.
