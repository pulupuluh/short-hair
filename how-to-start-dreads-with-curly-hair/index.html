How To Start Dreads With Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jun 20, 2023 · 7 min read

Description:

Starting dreads with curly hair can be a challenging and time-consuming process. However, with the right techniques and products, you can achieve beautiful, healthy dreads that will last for years to come. This article will provide a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads with curly hair, as well as tips and tricks for maintaining them.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Start Dreads with Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Dreads
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Dreads

How to Start Dreads with Curly Hair:

Starting dreads with curly hair requires patience and dedication. Unlike straight hair, curly hair tends to be more prone to tangling and frizz, which can make the dreading process more challenging. However, with the right products and techniques, anyone can start dreads with curly hair.

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before starting the dreading process, it is essential to wash your hair thoroughly. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or oils from your scalp and hair. This will help create a clean slate for the dreading process.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small, manageable sections using a rat-tail comb. Make sure each section is evenly sized and easy to work with. If your hair is very curly, consider using hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Use a Dread Wax or Gel

Apply a dread wax or gel to each section of hair. This will help hold the hair in place and make it easier to twist into dreads. Make sure to use a small amount of product, as too much can cause buildup and make your hair look greasy.

Step 4: Twist Your Hair

Twist each section of hair tightly, starting from the roots and working your way down to the ends. Use your fingers to create a tight, even twist that will hold its shape. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 5: Secure Your Dreads

Once you have twisted each section of hair, use a small rubber band to secure the dread at the base. This will help the dread hold its shape and prevent it from unraveling.

Tips for Maintaining Dreads:

- Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo. - Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup. - Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage. - Consider using a crochet hook to tighten loose hairs and maintain the shape of your dreads. - Be patient – dreads take time to mature and reach their full potential.

FAQ:

Q: Can I start dreads with short curly hair? A: Yes, you can start dreads with short curly hair. However, the dreading process may take longer than with longer hair. Q: Can I start dreads with natural curly hair? A: Yes, you can start dreads with natural curly hair. In fact, natural hair tends to hold dreads better than chemically treated hair. Q: How long does it take to start dreads with curly hair? A: The time it takes to start dreads with curly hair can vary depending on your hair type and texture. However, it can take anywhere from several weeks to several months for dreads to fully form.

Hair Care for Dreads:

Maintaining healthy dreads requires proper hair care. Here are some tips for keeping your dreads looking their best: - Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo. - Use a deep conditioning treatment once a month to keep your hair moisturized. - Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup. - Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage. - Consider using a crochet hook to tighten loose hairs and maintain the shape of your dreads. Starting dreads with curly hair can be a challenging process, but with the right techniques and products, anyone can achieve beautiful, healthy dreads. Follow this step-by-step guide and use these tips for maintaining your dreads to achieve the look you desire.
