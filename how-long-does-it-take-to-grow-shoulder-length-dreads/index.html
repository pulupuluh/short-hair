How Long Does It Take To Grow Shoulder Length Dreads

Are you thinking of growing shoulder length dreads? Then, you might be wondering how long it takes to achieve this length. The answer to this question depends on several factors, such as your hair type, maintenance routine, and the method you use to grow dreads. In this article, we will discuss how long it takes to grow shoulder length dreads and provide you with tips to achieve your desired length.

Hair Type

The time it takes to grow shoulder length dreads depends on your hair type. If you have straight hair, it might take longer to grow shoulder length dreads compared to someone with curly or coily hair. This is because straight hair tends to be more slippery, making it harder for the hair to lock.

On the other hand, curly and coily hair have a natural tendency to lock, making it easier to grow dreads. If you have curly or coily hair, you can expect to grow shoulder length dreads faster than someone with straight hair.

Maintenance Routine

Your maintenance routine also plays a significant role in how long it takes to grow shoulder length dreads. If you neglect your hair or do not follow a proper maintenance routine, it might take longer to achieve your desired length.

On the other hand, if you take good care of your hair and follow a strict maintenance routine, you can expect to grow shoulder length dreads faster. This includes regular washing, conditioning, and moisturizing your hair.

Method

The method you use to grow dreads also affects the time it takes to achieve shoulder length dreads. There are several methods to grow dreads, such as the backcombing method, twist and rip method, and freeform method.

The backcombing and twist and rip methods tend to produce faster results compared to the freeform method. This is because these methods involve more manipulation of the hair, making it easier for the hair to lock.

Other Factors

Aside from hair type, maintenance routine, and method, other factors also affect the time it takes to grow shoulder length dreads. These include:

  • Length of hair
  • Hair density
  • Age of hair
  • Overall health of hair

These factors can vary from person to person, so it is essential to consider them when growing dreads.

How to Grow Shoulder Length Dreads

If you want to grow shoulder length dreads, there are several things you can do to speed up the process. Here are some tips:

Start with clean hair

Before starting the dreadlocking process, make sure your hair is clean and free of any products. This will make it easier for your hair to lock and prevent any buildup.

Avoid using conditioner

Conditioner can make your hair slippery, making it harder for your hair to lock. Avoid using conditioner when starting the dreadlocking process.

Use a residue-free shampoo

Using a residue-free shampoo will prevent any buildup and make it easier for your hair to lock.

Maintain your hair regularly

Regular maintenance is essential to growing healthy and long dreads. This includes washing, conditioning, and moisturizing your hair regularly.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide to growing shoulder length dreads:

  1. Start with clean hair
  2. Section your hair into small sections
  3. Backcomb or twist and rip each section
  4. Roll each section between your palms to create cylindrical shapes
  5. Secure each dread with a rubber band or hair tie
  6. Let your hair dry and remove the rubber bands or hair ties
  7. Continue to maintain your hair regularly

Tips for Growing Shoulder Length Dreads

Here are some additional tips to help you grow shoulder length dreads:

  • Be patient â growing dreads takes time and requires patience
  • Avoid using wax â wax can cause buildup and prevent your hair from locking
  • Protect your hair at night â use a satin bonnet or pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals â chemicals can damage your hair and slow down the locking process

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to grow shoulder length dreads?

The time it takes to grow shoulder length dreads varies from person to person. It can take anywhere from 6 months to 2 years to achieve shoulder length dreads.

Do I need to cut my hair to grow dreads?

No, you do not need to cut your hair to grow dreads. You can start the dreadlocking process with any hair length.

Can I wash my hair with dreads?

Yes, you can wash your hair with dreads. It is essential to maintain clean and healthy hair to grow long and healthy dreads.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Maintaining healthy hair is essential to growing long and healthy dreads. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo
  • Condition your hair with a dreadlock-friendly conditioner
  • Moisturize your hair with a natural oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as bleach or hair dye
  • Protect your hair at night with a satin bonnet or pillowcase

Conclusion

In conclusion, the time it takes to grow shoulder length dreads depends on several factors, such as hair type, maintenance routine, and the method you use to grow dreads. By following a proper maintenance routine and using the right method, you can achieve your desired length in no time. Remember to be patient, take good care of your hair, and enjoy the journey to growing long and healthy dreads.
