How To Give Yourself A Short Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're tired of your long locks and want to change up your look, why not try giving yourself a short haircut? Not only is it a great way to save money on salon visits, but it's also a fun and empowering experience. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your own hair, step by step. Let's get started!

How to Give Yourself a Short Haircut

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary supplies. You'll need a pair of sharp haircutting scissors, a comb, hair clippers (optional), and a mirror. If you're using clippers, make sure they're fully charged and have the appropriate guard attachments.

Step 2: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before you begin. This will make it easier to work with and ensure a clean, even cut. If you have curly hair, make sure you comb it out while it's still wet to avoid any tangles.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into manageable sections. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward, using hair clips to hold each section in place. Make sure each section is even and symmetrical.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with the back section, begin cutting your hair. If you're using clippers, start with the longest guard attachment and work your way down to a shorter length. If you're using scissors, start by cutting the ends of your hair at a slight angle. Work your way up each section, cutting a little bit at a time, until you reach your desired length.

Step 5: Blend the Sides and Top

Once you've cut the back section, move on to the sides and top. Use your comb to lift sections of hair and blend them with the back. Make sure everything looks even and balanced.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you're finished cutting, use your mirror to check your work. Make sure everything looks even and balanced from all angles. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and trim them carefully.

Step 7: Style Your New Haircut

Now that you've given yourself a fresh new haircut, it's time to style it! Use your favorite hair products to create a look that suits you. Whether you prefer a messy, tousled look or a sleek, polished style, there are plenty of ways to make your new haircut your own.

Tips for Giving Yourself a Short Haircut

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you give yourself a short haircut:

  • Start with a longer length and work your way down gradually. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's gone!
  • Take breaks if your arms or hands get tired. Cutting your own hair can be a workout!
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to mistakes and uneven cuts.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. A friend or family member can help you with hard-to-reach spots.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are a few common questions people have about giving themselves a short haircut:

Is it hard to cut your own hair?

Cutting your own hair can be challenging, especially if you've never done it before. However, with the right tools and a little practice, it's definitely doable!

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! If you make a mistake, you can always trim a little more hair off to even things out. Worst case scenario, you can always visit a professional stylist to fix any major mistakes.

How often should I cut my hair?

How often you cut your hair depends on your personal preference and hair type. Short haircuts typically require more frequent maintenance, so you may need to trim your hair every few weeks to keep it looking fresh.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Now that you've given yourself a short haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to keep your hair moisturized and shiny.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Congratulations, you've successfully given yourself a short haircut! With a little practice and patience, you can achieve amazing results right from the comfort of your own home.
