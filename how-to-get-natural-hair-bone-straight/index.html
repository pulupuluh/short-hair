How To Get Natural Hair Bone Straight A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of dealing with curly, frizzy hair? Do you want to achieve a smooth, bone-straight look without resorting to harsh chemicals or heat tools? Look no further! In this article, we'll show you how to get natural hair bone straight using gentle techniques and minimal products.

How to Get Natural Hair Bone Straight

Getting bone-straight hair without chemicals or heat can seem like a daunting task, but it's possible with the right techniques and products. Here are a few ways to achieve the look:

Blow-Drying with a Round Brush

One of the easiest ways to get natural hair bone straight is to blow-dry it with a round brush. Start by washing your hair and applying a heat protectant spray. Then, divide your hair into sections and use a round brush to blow-dry each section while pulling it taut with the brush. This will help straighten the hair and eliminate frizz.

Using a Straightening Brush

A straightening brush is a great tool for achieving bone-straight hair without heat damage. Simply brush your hair with the straightening brush while applying gentle tension to each section. This will help smooth out the hair and create a sleek, straight look.

Roller-Setting

Roller-setting is another gentle way to straighten natural hair. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Then, divide it into sections and roll each section with a large, foam roller. Let the rollers set for several hours or overnight, then remove them and gently comb out your hair. You should be left with smooth, straight hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Ready to get started? Here's a step-by-step guide to getting natural hair bone straight:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will help remove any buildup and prepare your hair for straightening.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant Spray

Before using any heat tools, it's important to protect your hair from damage. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair, focusing on the ends and any areas that are particularly prone to damage.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into manageable sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to straighten each section individually.

Step 4: Use a Round Brush and Blow Dryer

If you're using a round brush and blow dryer, start at the roots and work your way down each section. Use the brush to pull each section taut as you blow-dry it, which will help straighten the hair and eliminate frizz.

Step 5: Use a Straightening Brush

If you're using a straightening brush, simply brush each section while applying gentle tension. The brush will help smooth out the hair and create a sleek, straight look.

Step 6: Roller-Set Your Hair

If you're roller-setting your hair, divide each section into smaller sections and roll them with a large, foam roller. Let the rollers set for several hours or overnight, then remove them and gently comb out your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Bone-Straight Hair

Now that you've achieved bone-straight hair, it's important to maintain it. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid heat styling as much as possible
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and tangling
  • Apply a light oil or serum to the ends of your hair to keep them moisturized
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that could cause breakage

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are a few common questions about getting natural hair bone straight:

Q: Can I get bone-straight hair without using heat?

A: Yes! Roller-setting is a great way to straighten natural hair without heat damage.

Q: How often should I straighten my hair?

A: It's best to avoid straightening your hair too often, as it can cause damage and breakage. Aim for once a week or less.

Q: Will bone-straight hair work for all hair types?

A: While bone-straight hair can work for many hair types, it may be more difficult for those with very curly or kinky hair.

Hair Care for Straightened Hair

Straightened hair requires a bit of extra care to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo
  • Deep condition your hair regularly
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of natural oils
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that could weigh down your hair

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve natural hair bone straight without resorting to harsh chemicals or heat tools. Give it a try and see how sleek and shiny your hair can be!
