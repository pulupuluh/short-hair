How Long Will It Take My Dreads To Grow

Written by Oliver Apr 29, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you thinking about getting dreadlocks or have already started the process? One of the most common questions people have is how long it will take for their dreads to grow. The answer isn't straightforward since it depends on a few factors, such as your hair type, how you care for your dreads, and how long you want them to be.

Factors That Affect Dreadlock Growth

Before we dive into how long it will take for your dreads to grow, let's go over the factors that can affect their growth:

Hair Type

Everyone's hair grows at a different rate, and the texture of your hair can also impact how fast or slow your dreads will grow. People with coarser hair typically see faster growth in their dreads than those with finer hair.

Dreadlock Method

The method you use to create your dreads can also impact their growth. For example, the backcombing method tends to create tighter dreads that grow slower than the twist and rip method.

Hair Care

How you care for your dreads can significantly impact their growth. Regular maintenance and proper cleansing ensure that your scalp and hair are healthy, allowing your dreads to grow faster. Neglecting your dreads can lead to stunted growth or even breakage.

Length

The longer you want your dreads to be, the longer it will take for them to grow. If you're starting with shorter hair, expect to wait longer for your dreads to reach your desired length.

How Long Will It Take for Your Dreads to Grow?

Now that you know the factors that can affect your dread growth, let's talk about timelines. On average, it can take anywhere from six months to two years for your dreads to grow to their desired length. However, this can vary significantly depending on the factors we mentioned above.

If you're starting with shorter hair, it can take up to a year before your dreads are shoulder-length. If you have longer hair and want your dreads to reach your waist, it can take two or more years.

It's also worth noting that dreadlocks grow differently than regular hair. Instead of growing straight down, they can grow in different directions, causing them to appear shorter than they are. This is why it's essential to measure your dreads when they're wet and stretched to get an accurate length.

Tips for Promoting Dreadlock Growth

If you want to speed up the growth of your dreads, here are some tips to keep in mind:

Be Gentle

When maintaining your dreads, be gentle to avoid damaging them. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to separate your dreads, and avoid pulling or tugging on them.

Keep Your Scalp Clean

A clean scalp promotes healthy hair growth, so make sure to cleanse your scalp and dreads regularly. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.

Eat a Healthy Diet

Your diet can impact the health of your hair, so make sure to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of vitamins and minerals. Foods rich in protein, like eggs and fish, can help promote hair growth.

FAQs About Dreadlock Growth

Can I speed up the growth of my dreads?

While you can't make your dreads grow faster, you can promote healthy growth by caring for them properly. Be gentle when maintaining them, keep your scalp clean, and eat a healthy diet.

Why do my dreads appear shorter than they are?

Dreadlocks grow differently than regular hair and can grow in different directions. This can cause them to appear shorter than they are. Make sure to measure your dreads when they're wet and stretched to get an accurate length.

Will my hair type impact how fast my dreads grow?

Yes, hair type can impact how fast or slow your dreads will grow. People with coarser hair typically see faster growth in their dreads than those with finer hair.

Conclusion

So how long will it take for your dreads to grow? The answer depends on a few factors, such as your hair type, the method you use to create your dreads, and how you care for them. On average, it can take anywhere from six months to two years for your dreads to grow to their desired length. However, with proper care and maintenance, you can promote healthy growth and enjoy your beautiful dreads in no time.
