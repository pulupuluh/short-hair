How To Get A Really High Ponytail The Ultimate Guide

Written by William May 16, 2023 Â· 7 min read
If you're looking for a hairstyle that's easy to do, but still looks polished and put-together, you can't go wrong with a high ponytail. It's a classic look that works for any occasion, from the gym to the office to a night out on the town. In this article, we'll show you how to get a really high ponytail step by step, along with some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get a Really High Ponytail
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips and Tricks
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for High Ponytails

How to Get a Really High Ponytail

Getting a high ponytail can be a bit tricky, especially if you have long or thick hair. Here are the basic steps you'll need to follow: 1. Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles or knots. 2. Gather your hair at the crown of your head, using your fingers to create a smooth, even base. 3. Use a hair tie to secure your ponytail. 4. Use a brush to smooth out any bumps or lumps in your ponytail.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you know the basics, let's go through the steps in more detail. Step 1: Brush Your Hair Before you start, you'll want to make sure your hair is completely tangle-free. Use a brush or comb to remove any knots or snarls. Step 2: Gather Your Hair Using your fingers, gather your hair at the crown of your head. Make sure your hair is as smooth and even as possible, with no bumps or lumps. Step 3: Secure with a Hair Tie Once you've gathered your hair, use a hair tie to secure your ponytail. Make sure it's tight enough to hold your hair in place, but not so tight that it's uncomfortable. Step 4: Smooth Out Your Ponytail Use a brush to smooth out any bumps or lumps in your ponytail. You can also use hairspray or gel to help keep your hair in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you get the perfect high ponytail: - Use a hair tie that matches your hair color, or use a clear hair tie if you don't want it to be visible. - If you have thick or heavy hair, try using two hair ties to help keep your ponytail in place. - To create a sleeker look, use a comb to smooth down any flyaways or baby hairs around your hairline. - If you want your ponytail to look even higher, try backcombing the hair at the crown of your head before you gather it into a ponytail.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I still get a high ponytail if I have short hair? A: Yes! Even if you have short hair, you can still create the illusion of a high ponytail by using hair extensions or a clip-in ponytail. Q: Will a high ponytail damage my hair? A: If you're pulling your hair back too tightly or using hair ties that are too small, you could be causing damage to your hair. Make sure your ponytail is secure, but not so tight that it's uncomfortable.

Hair Care for High Ponytails

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, here are some tips for caring for high ponytails: - Don't wear your hair in a ponytail too often, as it can cause breakage and damage. - Use a hair tie that's gentle on your hair, such as a fabric scrunchie or a spiral hair tie. - Avoid using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners on your hair too often, as they can cause damage and breakage. - Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and nourished.

Conclusion

Now that you know how to get a really high ponytail, you can rock this classic hairstyle with confidence. Whether you're going for a casual or more formal look, a high ponytail is always a great choice. Just remember to take care of your hair and use gentle, non-damaging products to keep your locks looking their best.
