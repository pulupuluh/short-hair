How To Cut Your Own Hair Taper Fade A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 15, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the barbershop every month for a simple taper fade? With the right tools and techniques, cutting your own hair taper fade can be a breeze. In this article, we’ll walk you through the steps to achieve a professional-looking taper fade haircut in the comfort of your own home.

Description

A taper fade haircut is a popular men’s haircut where the hair gradually gets shorter from the top of the head down to the neck. It’s a versatile haircut that can be styled in a variety of ways and is suitable for any hair type. Cutting your own hair taper fade can be tricky, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a clean and polished look.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Taper Fade

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting your own hair taper fade is to gather your tools. You will need:

  • Clippers with various guard sizes
  • A comb
  • Scissors
  • A handheld mirror or a mirror that allows you to see the back of your head
  • A towel or cape to catch hair clippings

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it’s important to wash and dry it. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that the clippers glide smoothly through your hair. Use a towel to pat your hair dry, but don’t rub it vigorously as this can damage your hair.

Step 3: Create the Base

Start by using the longest guard size on your clippers to create a base. This will be the longest length of hair on your head. Start at the bottom of your head and work your way up, using an upward motion with the clippers. Make sure to keep the clippers level to create an even base.

Step 4: Use Shorter Guard Sizes

Once you’ve created the base, switch to a shorter guard size and work your way up the sides and back of your head. Use an upward motion with the clippers, but stop a few inches below where you want the fade to end. This will create a gradual transition between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair on the sides and back.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Next, it’s time to blend the hair. Use a comb to pull the hair up and out from your head, then use the clippers to blend the longer hair on top with the shorter hair on the sides and back. Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to a shorter guard size to create a seamless blend.

Step 6: Cut the Top

Once you’ve blended the hair, it’s time to cut the top. Use scissors to trim the hair on top to your desired length. Work in small sections and be careful not to cut too much off at once.

Step 7: Clean Up the Edges

Use the clippers without a guard to clean up the edges around your ears and neck. Make sure to go slow and be careful not to nick your skin.

Step 8: Check Your Work

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, use a handheld mirror or a mirror that allows you to see the back of your head to check your work. Make any necessary adjustments until you’re happy with the result.

Tips

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair off at once.
  • Use a handheld mirror or a mirror that allows you to see the back of your head to check your work.
  • Be patient and take your time.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair taper fade with just clippers?

Yes, you can cut your own hair taper fade with just clippers. However, using scissors to trim the hair on top can help achieve a more polished look.

How often should I cut my hair taper fade?

It depends on how fast your hair grows. On average, most people need to cut their hair taper fade every 2-4 weeks.

Can I use any type of clippers to cut my hair taper fade?

No, it’s important to use clippers that are designed for hair cutting. Using the wrong type of clippers can result in uneven cuts and damaged hair.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair taper fade, it’s important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and use a heat protectant when styling with hot tools. Regular trims can also help keep your hair looking neat and healthy.
