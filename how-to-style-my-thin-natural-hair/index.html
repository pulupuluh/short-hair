How To Style My Thin Natural Hair

Written by Daniel Mar 10, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your thin natural hair looking limp and lifeless? Do you want to learn how to style it in a way that makes it look fuller and healthier? Look no further! In this article, we will discuss tips and tricks for styling thin natural hair.

Table of Contents

  • Hair Care
  • How to Style Thin Natural Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Styling Thin Natural Hair
  • FAQs

Hair Care

The first step to styling thin natural hair is taking care of it. Healthy hair is easier to style and looks better overall. Here are some tips for hair care:

1. Use a gentle shampoo

Harsh shampoos can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Look for a gentle shampoo that is sulfate-free and contains natural ingredients like aloe vera or coconut oil.

2. Don't over-wash your hair

Washing your hair too often can also strip it of its natural oils. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or less if possible.

3. Use a deep conditioner

A deep conditioner can help nourish your hair and prevent breakage. Use it once a week, or more often if your hair is particularly dry or damaged.

4. Protect your hair at night

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase or using a satin bonnet can help prevent breakage and frizz while you sleep.

How to Style Thin Natural Hair

Now that your hair is healthy, it's time to learn how to style it. Here are some ideas:

1. Twist-out

A twist-out is a classic style for natural hair. To achieve this look, divide your hair into small sections and twist each section tightly. Leave the twists in overnight, then unravel them in the morning for defined curls.

2. Braid-out

A braid-out is similar to a twist-out, but instead of twisting your hair, you braid it. The end result is looser, wavy curls.

3. Bantu knots

Bantu knots are a fun and trendy style for natural hair. Divide your hair into small sections and twist each section tightly. Wrap the twisted section around itself to create a knot, then secure it with a bobby pin. Leave the knots in overnight, then unravel them in the morning for a curly, voluminous look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for achieving a twist-out:

1. Wash and condition your hair

Start with clean, moisturized hair. Use a gentle shampoo and a deep conditioner, then rinse thoroughly.

2. Divide your hair into sections

Divide your hair into small, manageable sections using clips or hair ties.

3. Apply product

Apply a styling product like a curl cream or gel to each section of hair. Make sure to distribute the product evenly.

4. Twist each section

Twist each section of hair tightly, starting at the roots and twisting all the way to the ends. Use your fingers to smooth any flyaways or frizz.

5. Let your hair dry

Leave the twists in overnight, or sit under a hooded dryer to speed up the process.

6. Unravel the twists

Unravel the twists in the morning, using your fingers to separate the curls and create volume. Use a pick or wide-tooth comb to fluff your roots.

Tips for Styling Thin Natural Hair

Here are some additional tips for styling thin natural hair:

1. Use lightweight products

Heavy products can weigh down your hair and make it look even thinner. Use lightweight products like mousses, gels, or leave-in conditioners.

2. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it look even thinner. Try to avoid using them as much as possible.

3. Embrace your natural texture

Don't try to force your hair to do something it doesn't want to do. Embrace your natural texture and work with it.

FAQs

Q: Can I still wear protective styles with thin natural hair?

A: Yes! Protective styles like braids, twists, and weaves can help protect your hair from damage and breakage.

Q: Will using a hair growth oil make my hair thicker?

A: Hair growth oils can help promote healthy hair growth, but they won't necessarily make your hair thicker. However, healthier hair is usually fuller and looks thicker overall.

Q: Can I still dye my hair if it's thin?

A: Yes, but be aware that dyeing your hair can cause damage and breakage. Make sure to use a gentle dye and deep condition your hair regularly.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve beautiful, voluminous styles with your thin natural hair. Remember to take care of your hair and embrace your natural texture!
