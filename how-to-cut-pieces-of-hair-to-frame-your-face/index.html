How To Cut Pieces Of Hair To Frame Your Face

Written by Eveline Jun 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a way to update your hairstyle without making any drastic changes, cutting pieces of hair to frame your face is a great option. This technique can enhance your features, add shape to your hair, and give you a fresh new look. In this article, we'll show you how to cut pieces of hair to frame your face, step by step.

Description

Before we dive into the how-to, let's talk about what framing pieces are. These are small sections of hair that are cut around the face, usually starting at the cheekbone level. They can be long or short, depending on your preference, and they can be cut on either side of your face or both. Framing pieces can work for any hair length, from short bobs to long layers.

How to Cut Pieces of Hair to Frame Your Face

While it's always best to leave major haircuts to the professionals, cutting framing pieces is something you can do at home. Here's how:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air-dry or blow-drying it straight. Comb through any tangles or knots, and make sure your hair is smooth and tangle-free.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section off the hair that you want to cut. The framing pieces should be about 1-2 inches wide and start at the cheekbone level. Depending on the length of your hair, you may want to section off more hair.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair

Take your scissors and cut the section of hair that you've sectioned off. Start by cutting a small amount and gradually cut more if you want the pieces to be shorter. Make sure to cut in a straight line and not at an angle.

Step 4: Check Your Work

After you've cut your hair, take a step back and check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments, and remember that it's always better to cut less than more.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting framing pieces:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut a little bit at a time.
  • Consider using hair cutting shears instead of regular scissors for a more precise cut.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it may look shorter when it dries.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, consider asking a friend or a professional stylist for help.

FAQ

Can framing pieces work for any hair type?

Yes, framing pieces can work for any hair type, from fine and straight to thick and curly.

Do I need any special tools to cut framing pieces?

No, you don't need any special tools. Regular scissors will do the job, but hair cutting shears may give you a more precise cut.

How often should I cut my framing pieces?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain the look. Generally, cutting framing pieces every 6-8 weeks is a good idea.

Hair Care

Once you've cut your framing pieces, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often and always use a heat protectant.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid over-brushing your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you should be able to cut framing pieces that flatter your face shape and give you a fresh new look. Remember to take your time, start small, and always be gentle with your hair. Happy cutting!
