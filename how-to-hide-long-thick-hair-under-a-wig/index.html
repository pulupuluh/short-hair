How to Hide Long Thick Hair Under a Wig - Tips and Tricks

  • FAQ

Description

Wearing a wig is a great way to change your hairstyle and experiment with different looks. However, if you have long, thick hair, it can be challenging to hide it under the wig without creating a bulky and unnatural look. In this article, we will provide you with some tips and tricks on how to hide long thick hair under a wig.

How to

The key to hiding long thick hair under a wig is to create a flat base. Here are the steps to follow:

Step by Step

  1. Start by tying your hair into a low ponytail at the nape of your neck.
  2. Twist the ponytail and wrap it around the base to create a flat bun. Secure it with bobby pins.
  3. Put on a wig cap to keep your hair in place and prevent it from sticking out from under the wig.
  4. Put on your wig and adjust it to fit your head properly.
  5. Use bobby pins to secure the wig to your head, making sure to hide the pins under the wig.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you hide long thick hair under a wig:

  • Use a wig cap that matches your skin tone to create a more natural look.
  • Use a wig grip or a silicone headband to keep your wig in place and prevent it from slipping.
  • Choose a wig with a lace front or a monofilament top for a more realistic hairline.
  • Avoid wearing your wig too tight as it can cause discomfort and headaches.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hiding long thick hair under a wig:

  • Can I wear a wig if I have long thick hair?

    • Yes, you can wear a wig even if you have long thick hair. The key is to create a flat base and use a wig cap to keep your hair in place.

  • How do I choose the right wig for my hair type?

    • When choosing a wig, look for one that matches your hair type and texture. You can also consult with a wig specialist for expert advice.

  • How often should I wash my wig?

    • You should wash your wig every 6-8 wears or when it starts to look dull and dirty. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for washing and caring for your wig.

Hair Care

It's essential to take care of your natural hair to prevent damage and maintain its health. Here are some tips for caring for long thick hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and nourished.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and promote healthy growth.
