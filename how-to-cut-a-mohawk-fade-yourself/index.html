How To Cut A Mohawk Fade Yourself Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank May 12, 2023 · 8 min read

If you want to rock a cool and edgy hairstyle, a mohawk fade can be a great choice. This bold hairstyle features shaved sides and a longer strip of hair in the middle, creating a unique and eye-catching look. And the best part is, you don't have to visit a barber to achieve this hairstyle. With the right tools and techniques, you can cut a mohawk fade yourself at home. In this article, we'll guide you through the process step by step.

Description

A mohawk fade is a hairstyle that features a strip of longer hair in the middle of the head, while the sides are shaved or trimmed very short. This style is known for its edgy and bold look and can be customized in many ways. A mohawk fade can be cut with different lengths and textures, allowing you to express your personality and style.

How to Cut a Mohawk Fade Yourself – Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather the Right Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of clippers with different guard sizes, a comb, scissors, and a handheld mirror. Make sure your clippers are sharp and clean to ensure a smooth and even cut.

Step 2: Determine the Length

The first step to cutting a mohawk fade is to determine the length you want for the top of your hair. You can use the handheld mirror to visualize how the final look will appear. Once you've decided on the length, you can start cutting the top of your hair with scissors. Be sure to cut evenly and straight across, from one side to the other.

Step 3: Create a Guide

To ensure a smooth transition from the longer top to the shorter sides, you'll need to create a guide. Use a comb to create a part on each side of your head, separating the longer hair on top from the shorter hair on the sides. Then, use your clippers to trim the hair on the sides, starting with the longest guard size and gradually moving down to a shorter size. Use the comb as a guide to ensure an even cut.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Once you've trimmed the hair on the sides, you'll need to blend it with the longer hair on top. To do this, use a shorter guard size on your clippers and trim the hair around the edges of the longer hair. Be sure to blend the hair evenly and avoid creating sharp lines.

Step 5: Refine the Look

After you've trimmed and blended the hair, it's time to refine the look. Use your scissors to cut any stray hairs or uneven edges. You can also use your clippers to make any final adjustments. Take your time and be patient to ensure a clean and polished look.

Tips

  • Start with a longer guard size and gradually move down to a shorter size to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a handheld mirror to see the back of your head and ensure an even cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to, especially if you're not used to cutting your own hair.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you if you need it.

FAQ

How long does a mohawk fade last?

A mohawk fade can last for several weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows. You may need to touch up the sides every few weeks to maintain the look.

Can I cut a mohawk fade with curly hair?

Yes, you can cut a mohawk fade with curly hair. However, you may need to adjust the length and texture to suit your hair type.

How do I care for a mohawk fade?

To care for a mohawk fade, you'll need to shampoo and condition your hair regularly. You can also use styling products to enhance the look and hold. Be sure to trim the sides and blend the hair regularly to maintain the style.

Hair Care

Cutting a mohawk fade yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it's important to take care of your hair after the cut. To keep your hair healthy and strong, be sure to shampoo and condition regularly, and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and nourished. Remember, taking care of your hair is just as important as cutting it!
