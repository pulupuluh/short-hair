How To Blow Dry Wavy Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Blow drying wavy curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right technique and products, you can achieve a beautiful, frizz-free style. In this article, we'll go over the step-by-step process of how to blow dry wavy curly hair, as well as tips and tricks to make the process easier.

Table of Contents

  • How to Blow Dry Wavy Curly Hair
  • Tips for Blow Drying Wavy Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Wavy Curly Hair

How to Blow Dry Wavy Curly Hair

Before you begin the blow drying process, it's important to have the right tools and products. You'll need a blow dryer with a diffuser attachment, a wide-tooth comb, and heat protectant spray. Here are the steps to blow dry wavy curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products specifically designed for wavy curly hair. This will help keep your curls hydrated and defined. After washing, gently squeeze out excess water with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to protect it from heat damage. Be sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 3: Detangle

Using a wide-tooth comb, gently detangle your hair starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots. This will help prevent breakage and make the blow drying process easier.

Step 4: Attach Diffuser

Attach the diffuser attachment to your blow dryer. This will help distribute air more evenly and minimize frizz.

Step 5: Start Blow Drying

Starting at the roots, use the diffuser to blow dry your hair on a low heat setting. Use your hands to scrunch your hair as you dry, which will help enhance your curls. Continue this process until your hair is about 80% dry.

Step 6: Finish Drying

Once your hair is mostly dry, switch to a cool setting on your blow dryer and continue to dry your hair until it's completely dry. This will help set your curls and minimize frizz.

Tips for Blow Drying Wavy Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help make blow drying wavy curly hair easier:

  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel. Regular towels can be too rough and cause frizz.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream before blow drying to help define your curls.
  • Don't touch your hair too much while blow drying, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a lower heat setting on your blow dryer to prevent heat damage.
  • Experiment with different diffuser attachments to find one that works best for your hair.

FAQs

How often should I blow dry my wavy curly hair?

It's best to limit blow drying to once or twice a week to prevent heat damage. On other days, you can try air drying or using a diffuser without heat to enhance your curls.

Can I blow dry my wavy curly hair without a diffuser?

While you can technically blow dry your hair without a diffuser, using one will help distribute air more evenly and minimize frizz.

Hair Care for Wavy Curly Hair

To keep your wavy curly hair healthy and beautiful, it's important to follow a proper hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Use products specifically designed for wavy curly hair, which will help keep your curls hydrated and defined.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as this can cause heat damage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent hair breakage and frizz.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and using the right products and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, healthy-looking wavy curly hair. Happy blow drying!
