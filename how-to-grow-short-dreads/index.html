How To Grow Short Dreads A Step By Step Guide

Are you thinking of growing short dreads but don't know where to start? Look no further! In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to grow short dreads and some tips on hair care. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Grow Short Dreads

How to Grow Short Dreads

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's talk about what dreads are and how they form. Dreads are rope-like strands of hair that are formed when hair tangles together and becomes matted. This process is natural and can happen to anyone's hair, regardless of texture or race.

Now, let's talk about how to grow short dreads. The first step is to let your hair grow out to at least two inches in length. This will give your hair enough length to tangle and form into dreads. Then, you'll need to section your hair into small, equal-sized parts. This will help your dreads form evenly and prevent any lumps or bumps.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Shampoo Your Hair

The first step in growing short dreads is to shampoo your hair thoroughly. This will help remove any dirt or product buildup and make your hair easier to work with. Make sure to use a clarifying shampoo to get a deep clean.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into small, equal-sized parts. Use hair ties or clips to hold each section in place. It's important to make sure each section is the same size to prevent any lumps or bumps in your dreads.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Now, it's time to backcomb your hair. Use a fine-tooth comb and start from the tip of each section, working your way up towards the roots. This will help your hair tangle and form into dreads. Be gentle and take your time to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 4: Twist and Roll

After backcombing, twist and roll each section of hair between your palms. This will help the hair to tangle together and form into dreads. Repeat this for each section of hair.

Step 5: Maintain Your Dreads

Once your dreads have formed, it's important to maintain them regularly. This includes washing your hair regularly and palm-rolling your dreads to keep them tight and neat. You may also need to use a crochet hook to tighten any loose hairs or to remove any buildup.

Tips for Maintaining Short Dreads

Here are some tips for maintaining short dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent buildup
  • Palm-roll your dreads to keep them tight and neat
  • Use a crochet hook to tighten any loose hairs or remove buildup
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils that can weigh your dreads down
  • Protect your dreads while sleeping by wearing a satin or silk bonnet

FAQs

How long does it take to grow short dreads?

It can take anywhere from a few months to a year or more to grow short dreads, depending on your hair texture and length.

Can I wash my dreads?

Yes, you should wash your dreads regularly to prevent buildup and keep them clean.

Do I need to use a special shampoo for dreads?

It's recommended to use a clarifying shampoo to get a deep clean and remove any buildup. You can also use a shampoo specifically designed for dreads.

Hair Care for Dreads

Here are some tips for hair care for dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly to prevent buildup
  • Use a clarifying shampoo to get a deep clean
  • Palm-roll your dreads regularly to keep them neat and tight
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils that can weigh your dreads down
  • Protect your dreads while sleeping by wearing a satin or silk bonnet

Now that you know how to grow short dreads, it's time to get started! Follow these steps and tips for maintaining your dreads, and you'll have a beautiful head of locks in no time.
