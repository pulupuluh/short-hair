How To Cut The Back Of A Man s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter May 14, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the barbershop every month? With a few basic tools and some practice, you can learn how to cut the back of a man's hair at home. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps to achieve a professional-looking cut without breaking the bank.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping the Hair
  • Sectioning the Hair
  • Cutting the Hair
  • Blending the Hair
  • Cleaning Up
  • Tips for a Great Cut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After the Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clip
  • Cape or towel

Make sure your clippers are sharp and well-oiled for the best results.

Prepping the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. Comb it out and make sure it's tangle-free. Then, decide on the length you want to cut the hair. You can use the same guard size all over or vary the length for a more textured look.

Sectioning the Hair

Section off the hair at the crown of the head with a clip. This will help you focus on the back of the head without getting distracted by the sides or top. Use a comb to create a center part from the crown to the nape of the neck.

Cutting the Hair

Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up. Use the clippers with the guard size you chose and work in an upward motion. Take small sections at a time and overlap each pass slightly to ensure an even cut. Use the comb to lift the hair away from the head and cut any longer pieces with the scissors. Be careful not to cut too much off at once.

Blending the Hair

Once you've finished cutting the back, it's time to blend the sides and top. Remove the clip from the crown and blend the back into the sides using the clippers or scissors. Use a longer guard size for the sides and top and gradually blend the lengths together for a natural look.

Cleaning Up

Use the clippers to clean up the hairline at the nape of the neck. You can also use the scissors to trim any stray hairs around the ears or sideburns. Remove the cape or towel and brush away any loose hairs.

Tips for a Great Cut

  • Practice on a mannequin head or a willing friend before attempting to cut your own hair.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and check your work frequently.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of the head as you go.
  • Invest in quality tools for the best results.
  • Experiment with different guard sizes and techniques to find what works best for you.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my own hair?

A: Yes, with practice and patience. Start with small sections and work slowly.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and desired length. Generally, every 4-6 weeks for a shorter cut and every 8-12 weeks for a longer cut.

Q: How do I clean my clippers?

A: Follow the manufacturer's instructions for cleaning and oiling your clippers. Remove any hair and debris after each use.

Hair Care After the Cut

After your cut, shampoo and condition your hair as usual. Style with your preferred products and enjoy your professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home.

Cutting the back of a man's hair may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and technique, it's a skill you can master. Follow these steps and tips for a great cut every time.
