Does Thinning Curly Hair Make It Curlier

Written by Robby Aug 16, 2023 Â· 9 min read

In the world of curly hair, there are many myths and misconceptions. One of the most common questions is, "does thinning curly hair make it curlier?" The answer is not as straightforward as you might think. In this article, we will explore the science behind curly hair, the effects of thinning, and whether or not it can make your curls curlier.

The Science Behind Curly Hair

Curly hair is unique because of its shape. Each strand of curly hair is oval or elliptical in shape, rather than round like straight hair. This shape causes the hair to grow in a spiral, which is what creates the curl pattern. The more oval the shape of the hair, the tighter the curl. The less oval the shape, the looser the curl.

Curly hair also tends to be drier than straight hair because the natural oils produced by the scalp have a harder time traveling down the spiral shape of the hair strand. This means that curly hair requires more moisture and hydration to stay healthy.

The Effects of Thinning on Curly Hair

Thinning curly hair can have both positive and negative effects on the curl pattern. When hair is thinned, the weight of the hair is reduced, which can lead to more volume and bounce. However, thinning can also cause the curls to appear frizzy or undefined.

Thinning can also make curly hair more manageable, as it reduces the amount of hair that needs to be styled or maintained. This can be especially helpful for those with thick or dense curly hair.

Does Thinning Make Curly Hair Curlier?

The answer to this question is not a simple yes or no. Thinning can make curly hair appear curlier, but it can also make the curls appear looser or less defined. The effect that thinning has on curl pattern depends on the individual's hair type and the method of thinning used.

If a stylist uses a razor or thinning shears to thin curly hair, it can create more texture and volume, which can lead to a curlier appearance. However, if too much hair is thinned, it can cause the curls to lose their shape and become frizzy.

On the other hand, if a stylist uses a technique called "chunking," it can create a more defined curl pattern. Chunking involves cutting the hair into larger sections, which can help to create more uniform curls.

How to Thin Curly Hair

Thinning curly hair should always be done by a professional stylist who has experience working with curly hair. Here are some common methods that stylists use to thin curly hair:

  • Razor cutting: A stylist uses a razor to cut some of the hair strands in order to create more texture and volume.
  • Thinning shears: A stylist uses thinning shears to cut some of the hair strands, which can create a more layered look.
  • Chunking: A stylist cuts the hair into larger sections to create a more defined curl pattern.

Tips for Thinning Curly Hair

Thinning curly hair can be a tricky process, so it's important to take some precautions to ensure that your curls stay healthy and intact. Here are some tips for thinning curly hair:

  • Choose a stylist who has experience working with curly hair.
  • Communicate with your stylist about the amount of hair you want to thin.
  • Avoid thinning the hair too close to the scalp, as this can cause the curls to lose their shape.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly to keep it healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to already thinned hair.

FAQs About Thinning Curly Hair

Q: Will thinning curly hair make it easier to style?

A: Yes, thinning curly hair can make it easier to style and maintain, especially for those with thick or dense hair.

Q: Will thinning curly hair make it look thinner?

A: Thinning curly hair can make it look thinner, but it can also make it look more voluminous and bouncy.

Hair Care for Thinned Curly Hair

After thinning curly hair, it's important to take extra care to ensure that your curls stay healthy and hydrated. Here are some tips for caring for thinned curly hair:

  • Moisturize regularly with a deep conditioning treatment.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using products with alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your hair's shape and health.

In conclusion, thinning curly hair can have both positive and negative effects on the curl pattern. While it can make the curls appear curlier or more voluminous, it can also cause them to lose their shape and become frizzy. It's important to choose a stylist who has experience working with curly hair and to take extra care to maintain the health and hydration of your hair after thinning.
