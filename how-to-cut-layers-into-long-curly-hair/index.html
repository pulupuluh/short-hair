How To Cut Layers Into Long Curly Hair

Written by Aprilia Jul 02, 2023 · 8 min read

Long curly hair is a beautiful and versatile hairstyle that can be worn in many different ways. However, without the right cut, it can be difficult to manage and style. One solution is to add layers to your long curly hair. Layers can add volume, texture, and movement to your hair, making it easier to style and maintain. In this article, we’ll show you how to cut layers into long curly hair.

Description

Adding layers to long curly hair is a great way to enhance its natural beauty and create a more interesting and dynamic hairstyle. Layers can help to break up the length of your hair, add volume and movement, and create a more personalized look that suits your face shape and style.

How to Cut Layers into Long Curly Hair: Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the supplies you need. You’ll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by dividing your hair into sections. Separate the top section of your hair from the bottom section, and clip it up. Then, divide the bottom section in half and clip each half to the side.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle, starting from the ends and working your way up. Repeat this process, taking small sections of hair and cutting them at an angle, until you’ve cut all the hair in the bottom layer.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Now move on to the middle layer of your hair. Release one of the sections you clipped to the side and repeat the same process as before, cutting the hair at an angle in small sections. Then repeat on the other side.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, release the top section of your hair and cut it in the same way as the other layers. Make sure to blend the layers together so that they flow seamlessly into each other.

Tips for Cutting Layers into Long Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting layers into long curly hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Cut at an angle to create a softer, more natural look.
  • Don’t cut too much hair at once – it’s better to cut less and then go back and cut more if needed.
  • Make sure to blend the layers together so that they flow seamlessly into each other.
  • Be patient and take your time – rushing can lead to mistakes.

FAQ

How often should I cut my layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your layers start to lose their shape. As a general rule, you should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain your layers and keep your hair healthy.

Can I cut layers into my own hair?

While it’s possible to cut your own layers, it’s generally best to leave it to a professional stylist. Cutting your own hair can be tricky, and it’s easy to make mistakes that can be difficult to fix.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your layers, don’t panic. You can always go back and fix it later. If it’s a small mistake, you might be able to blend it in with the rest of your layers. If it’s a bigger mistake, you might need to get it fixed by a professional stylist.

Hair Care Tips for Long Curly Hair with Layers

Now that you’ve cut layers into your long curly hair, it’s important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry – use a wide-toothed comb instead.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and healthy.

With these tips, you can enjoy beautiful, healthy, and manageable long curly hair with layers. Happy styling!
