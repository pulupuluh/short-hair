What Is The Reason For Severe Hair Fall In Female

Written by Aprilia Jun 12, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Hair is an integral part of a woman's beauty. Losing hair can be a traumatizing experience for many women. Hair fall is a common problem among both men and women, but when it comes to women, it can be more severe and stressful. In this blog, we will discuss the possible reasons for severe hair fall in females and what you can do to prevent it.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes are one of the primary reasons for hair fall in women. Hormonal imbalances during pregnancy, menopause, or thyroid disorders can cause hair loss. The hormone dihydrotestosterone (DHT) is responsible for hair loss in women with androgenetic alopecia. It is a hereditary condition that causes hair follicles to shrink, leading to hair thinning and baldness.

Nutritional Deficiencies

If you are not getting the required nutrients through your diet, it can lead to hair loss. Deficiencies in iron, vitamin D, vitamin B12, and zinc can cause hair fall. Iron is essential for transporting oxygen to the hair follicles, and a lack of it can lead to hair loss.

Stress

Stress is another significant factor that can lead to hair fall in females. When you are under stress, your body releases cortisol, a hormone that can affect the hair growth cycle. Stress can also cause hair-pulling disorder called trichotillomania, where a person pulls out their hair as a coping mechanism.

Medications

Certain medications can also cause hair loss in women. Chemotherapy drugs, blood thinners, and medications for depression, acne, and high blood pressure can cause hair fall. It is essential to consult your doctor if you notice excessive hair loss after starting a new medication.

Hairstyling Practices

Hairstyling practices like tight braids, weaves, and ponytails can lead to hair loss in women. These hairstyles put a lot of tension on the hair, leading to traction alopecia. Chemical treatments like hair straightening, perming, and coloring can also damage the hair and cause hair fall.

Genetic Factors

If your mother or grandmother has experienced hair loss, you are at a higher risk of experiencing it too. Female pattern hair loss is a hereditary condition that can lead to hair thinning and baldness.

Medical Conditions

Medical conditions like alopecia areata, lupus, and polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) can cause hair fall in females. Alopecia areata is an autoimmune disorder that causes hair loss in patches. Lupus is an autoimmune disease that can lead to hair thinning and baldness. PCOS is a hormonal disorder that can cause thinning hair in females.

Thinning Hair

Thinning hair is a common problem among women. As you age, your hair becomes thinner and weaker. It is essential to take care of your hair and scalp to prevent hair fall. Thinning hair can also be a sign of an underlying medical condition, so it is essential to consult your doctor if you notice excessive hair loss.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to prevent hair fall:

  • Keep your scalp clean and healthy.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that put tension on the hair.
  • Avoid chemical treatments like hair straightening, perming, and coloring.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet rich in iron, vitamin D, vitamin B12, and zinc.
  • Reduce stress through meditation, yoga, or exercise.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to avoid hair breakage.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools like curling irons and straighteners.
  • Consult your doctor if you notice excessive hair loss.

FAQ

Q. Is hair fall normal?

A. Hair fall is normal up to a certain extent. Losing 50-100 hairs a day is normal, but if you notice excessive hair loss, it could be a sign of an underlying medical condition.

Q. How can I prevent hair fall?

A. You can prevent hair fall by keeping your scalp clean and healthy, avoiding tight hairstyles, eating a healthy and balanced diet, reducing stress, and avoiding chemical treatments.

Q. Can hormonal changes cause hair fall?

A. Yes, hormonal changes during pregnancy, menopause, or thyroid disorders can cause hair loss.

Q. Can stress cause hair fall?

A. Yes, stress can affect the hair growth cycle and cause hair fall.

Q. Can medications cause hair fall?

A. Yes, certain medications like chemotherapy drugs, blood thinners, and medications for depression, acne, and high blood pressure can cause hair fall.

By following these hair care tips and identifying the underlying cause of hair fall, you can prevent further hair loss and promote hair growth. Remember, healthy hair starts with a healthy scalp, so take care of your hair to maintain its natural beauty.
