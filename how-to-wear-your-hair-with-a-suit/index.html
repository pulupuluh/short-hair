How To Wear Your Hair With A Suit A Complete Guide

Written by Robby Jul 23, 2023 · 11 min read

When it comes to dressing up for an event, men often focus on their suits and forget about their hair. However, your hairstyle can make or break your look, especially when paired with a suit. In this guide, we will show you how to wear your hair with a suit, step by step. From choosing the right haircut to styling your locks, we’ve got you covered.

Choosing the Right Haircut

The first step to wearing your hair with a suit is choosing the right haircut. There are several factors to consider, such as your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. Here are some popular haircuts that go well with a suit:

  • Classic Side Part: This cut features short sides and a longer top, with a side part that adds a touch of sophistication.
  • Slicked Back: This style involves combing your hair back and using product to hold it in place. It works well for both short and long hair.
  • Short and Textured: If you have naturally thick hair, a short and textured cut can give you a modern, edgy look.
  • Long and Layered: For those with longer locks, a layered cut can add volume and movement to your hair.

Ultimately, the best haircut for you depends on your personal style and the occasion you’re dressing up for. Consult with your hairstylist to find the perfect cut for your face shape and hair type.

Washing and Conditioning Your Hair

Before you style your hair, it’s important to wash and condition it properly. Use a shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type, and follow these steps:

  1. Wet your hair with warm water.
  2. Apply a dollop of shampoo to your scalp and massage it in gently.
  3. Rinse your hair thoroughly.
  4. Apply conditioner to the ends of your hair, avoiding the scalp.
  5. Leave the conditioner in for a few minutes.
  6. Rinse your hair with cool water to seal the cuticles.

Washing your hair regularly can help prevent dandruff, oil buildup, and other hair problems. However, don’t overdo it, as too much washing can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Drying Your Hair

Once you’ve washed your hair, it’s time to dry it. Here’s how:

  1. Pat your hair dry with a towel to remove excess water.
  2. If you have short hair, you can use a blow dryer on low heat to dry it quickly.
  3. If you have longer hair, let it air dry or use a blow dryer on a cool setting to avoid damaging your hair.

Remember to avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with a towel, as this can cause breakage and frizz.

Choosing the Right Styling Products

Styling products can help you achieve the look you want and keep your hair in place throughout the day. Here are some popular options:

  • Pomade: A classic styling product that provides a shiny, slick finish.
  • Wax: Similar to pomade, but with a matte finish that looks more natural.
  • Gel: A strong hold product that can create a wet or slicked back look.
  • Mousse: A lightweight foam that adds volume and texture to your hair.

When choosing a styling product, consider your hair type and the look you want to achieve. Start with a small amount and add more as needed.

Combing and Brushing Your Hair

Before you apply styling product, comb or brush your hair to remove tangles and knots. Here are some tips:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb for wet hair, and a fine-toothed comb for dry hair.
  • If you have curly hair, use a comb or your fingers to style your hair, as brushing can cause frizz.
  • Brush your hair gently to avoid pulling or breaking it.

Slicking Back Your Hair

Slicked back hair is a popular look for men, especially when paired with a suit. Here’s how to achieve the look:

  1. Apply a small amount of pomade or wax to your hair.
  2. Use a comb to slick your hair back, starting from the hairline and working your way back.
  3. Smooth out any bumps or flyaways with your fingers.

You can create a tight or loose slick back, depending on your personal style. Experiment with different products and techniques to find the perfect look for you.

Creating a Side Part

A side part is a classic hairstyle that adds a touch of elegance to your look. Here’s how to create a side part:

  1. Apply a small amount of pomade or wax to your hair.
  2. Use a comb to create a part on one side of your head.
  3. Smooth out any bumps or flyaways with your fingers.

You can create a deep or shallow side part, depending on your preference. This style works well for both short and long hair.

Styling Short Hair

If you have short hair, there are several ways to style it with a suit. Here are some ideas:

  • Slicked back: Use pomade or wax to slick your hair back for a classic look.
  • Messy textured: Use a texturizing product to create a messy, edgy look.
  • Crew cut: Keep your hair short and neat with a crew cut, which works well for both casual and formal events.

Short hair is low maintenance and easy to style, making it a popular choice for men.

Styling Long Hair

Long hair can be a challenge to style, but with the right approach, it can look great with a suit. Here are some ideas:

  • Slicked back: Use a strong hold product to slick your hair back for a sophisticated look.
  • Man bun: Tie your hair back in a bun for a trendy, casual look.
  • Half up, half down: Pull the top half of your hair back and leave the bottom half down for a relaxed, bohemian look.

Long hair requires more maintenance than short hair, but it can be a versatile and stylish option if you’re willing to put in the effort.

FAQs

Q: Can I wear my hair down with a suit?

A: Yes, you can wear your hair down with a suit, especially if you have longer hair. Just make sure it’s neat and well-styled.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. Generally, you should wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Q: Can I use the same styling product for short and long hair?

A: It depends on the product and your hair type. Pomade and wax work well for both short and long hair, while gel and mousse are better suited for short hair.
