Does Short Hair Make Your Head Look Bigger Or Smaller

Written by Petter Jun 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're considering getting a short haircut, you may be wondering if it will make your head look bigger or smaller. It's a common concern, especially for people with round or oval-shaped faces. In this article, we'll explore this question and provide some tips on how to choose the right short haircut for your face shape.

Description

The answer to this question is not straightforward. Whether short hair makes your head look bigger or smaller depends on several factors, such as your face shape, hair texture, and the type of short haircut you choose.

If you have a round or oval-shaped face, you may want to avoid short haircuts that add volume to the top of your head. This can make your head look rounder and larger. Instead, opt for a short haircut that creates more angles and definition in your face.

On the other hand, if you have a long or narrow face, a short haircut that adds volume to the top of your head can balance out your features and make your head look smaller.

How to

To determine whether short hair will make your head look bigger or smaller, you should consider your face shape and hair texture. Here are some tips on how to choose the right short haircut for your face shape:

Round face

If you have a round face, you should avoid short haircuts that add volume to the top of your head, such as a pixie cut or a bob with blunt bangs. Instead, opt for a short haircut that creates angles and definition in your face, such as a layered bob or a side-swept pixie.

Oval face

If you have an oval face, you can experiment with a variety of short haircuts. However, you should avoid short haircuts that are too voluminous or add width to your face. Instead, opt for a short haircut that emphasizes your cheekbones or jawline, such as a layered pixie or a textured bob.

Long face

If you have a long face, you can benefit from a short haircut that adds volume to the top of your head. This can balance out your features and make your head look smaller. Opt for a short haircut with layers or a textured fringe, such as a shaggy pixie or a messy bob.

Step by Step

Here are some steps you can follow to choose the right short haircut for your face shape:

  1. Determine your face shape â You can do this by measuring the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline.
  2. Research short haircuts â Look for short haircuts that flatter your face shape and hair texture.

  4. Experiment with different styles â Don't be afraid to try different short haircuts until you find the one that suits you best.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing a short haircut:

  • Consider your hair texture â Short haircuts may not work for all hair types, such as curly or thick hair.
  • Use styling products â Styling products can help you achieve the desired look and add volume or texture to your hair.
  • Maintain your haircut â Short haircuts require more frequent maintenance, such as trimming every 4-6 weeks.

FAQ

Does short hair make your head look bigger or smaller?

It depends on your face shape, hair texture, and the type of short haircut you choose. If you have a round or oval-shaped face, you may want to avoid short haircuts that add volume to the top of your head. If you have a long or narrow face, a short haircut that adds volume to the top of your head can balance out your features and make your head look smaller.

What are some short haircuts that flatter round faces?

Some short haircuts that flatter round faces include a layered bob, a side-swept pixie, or a textured bob.

Hair Care

Short haircuts require more frequent maintenance and styling than longer hair. Here are some tips on how to care for your short haircut:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and shiny.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  • Style your hair using a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling wand, depending on the desired look.
  • Trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, whether short hair makes your head look bigger or smaller depends on several factors, such as your face shape, hair texture, and the type of short haircut you choose. By following the tips and steps outlined in this article, you can choose the right short haircut for your face shape and maintain healthy, stylish hair.
