How To Wear A Bandana As A Guy A Complete Guide

Written by Petter Jun 29, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Bandanas are a versatile accessory that can add a touch of style to any outfit. Whether you're going for a casual or edgy look, a bandana can do wonders in elevating your appearance. However, many guys are unsure of how to wear a bandana properly. In this guide, we'll show you the different ways to wear a bandana as a guy, along with some tips and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Wear a Bandana
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Wearing a Bandana
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Wear a Bandana

There are several ways to wear a bandana, depending on your style and preference. Here are some of the most popular ways:

1. Headband

The headband style is perfect for keeping your hair out of your face while adding a pop of color or pattern to your outfit. To achieve this look, fold your bandana into a long strip, then wrap it around your head, tying it at the back.

2. Necktie

A bandana can also be worn as a necktie for a more casual look. Fold your bandana into a triangle, then tie it loosely around your neck, leaving the loose ends hanging down. This works well with a denim jacket or a t-shirt.

3. Pocket Square

If you want to add a touch of style to your suit or blazer, a bandana can be used as a pocket square. Fold your bandana into a square, then tuck it into your jacket pocket. This adds a pop of color to your outfit, without being too flashy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the different ways to wear a bandana, let's go through the steps to achieve each look:

Headband

  1. Take your bandana and fold it in half diagonally, creating a triangle shape.
  2. Hold the bandana with the longest edge at the top, then fold the bottom edge up about 2-3 inches.
  3. Starting from the folded edge, begin folding the bandana in towards the center, creating a long strip.
  4. Wrap the bandana around your head, making sure the folded edge is at the front.
  5. Tie the bandana at the back of your head, making sure it's not too tight or too loose.

Necktie

  1. Take your bandana and fold it in half diagonally, creating a triangle shape.
  2. Hold the bandana with the longest edge at the top, then fold the bottom edge up about halfway.
  3. Roll the bandana tightly from the bottom edge to the top.
  4. Wrap the rolled bandana around your neck, making sure the loose ends are even.
  5. Tie the loose ends in a knot, making sure it's not too tight or too loose.

Pocket Square

  1. Take your bandana and fold it in half diagonally, creating a triangle shape.
  2. Hold the bandana with the longest edge at the top, then fold the bottom edge up about 2-3 inches.
  3. Starting from the folded edge, begin folding the bandana in towards the center, creating a square shape.
  4. Tuck the folded bandana into your jacket pocket, making sure it's even.

Tips for Wearing a Bandana

Here are some tips to keep in mind when wearing a bandana:

  • Choose the right size bandana for the look you're trying to achieve. A smaller bandana works well as a pocket square, while a larger one is better for a headband or necktie.
  • Pair your bandana with complementary colors and patterns. For example, a red bandana looks great with denim, while a paisley bandana goes well with a leather jacket.
  • Experiment with different folds and knots to achieve different looks.
  • Make sure your hair is styled or trimmed properly, as a bandana can draw attention to your hairline.

FAQs

Can I wear a bandana with a suit?

Yes, a bandana can be worn as a pocket square to add a touch of style to your suit. Just make sure the colors and patterns complement each other.

What's the best way to tie a bandana as a necktie?

The best way to tie a bandana as a necktie is to fold it into a triangle, then roll it tightly from the bottom to the top. Wrap it around your neck and tie it in a knot, leaving the loose ends hanging down.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips to keep your hair looking its best when wearing a bandana:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and shiny.
  • Style your hair according to the look you're trying to achieve. For example, a messy hairstyle works well with a casual bandana headband, while a slicked-back style complements a bandana necktie with a suit.
  • Use a hair product, such as gel or wax, to keep your hair in place and prevent flyaways.
  • Avoid wearing a bandana too tightly, as this can cause hair breakage or damage.

Now that you know how to wear a bandana as a guy, it's time to experiment with different looks and find the style that suits you best. Remember, a bandana is a versatile accessory that can add a touch of personality to any outfit.
