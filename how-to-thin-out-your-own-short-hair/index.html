How To Thin Out Your Own Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby May 25, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you looking for a way to give your short hair a little more volume and texture? Thinning out your hair can help achieve that look. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to thin out your own short hair. We will also offer some tips and tricks to help you achieve the best results.

Table of Contents

  • How to Thin Out Your Own Short Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Thinning

How to Thin Out Your Own Short Hair

Thinning out your hair involves removing some of the bulkiness and density of your hair. This will make your hair look less heavy and more voluminous. There are several ways to thin out your own short hair:

  • Using thinning shears
  • Using a razor comb
  • Using a hair thinning blade
  • Using a pair of scissors

Each of these methods has its own advantages and disadvantages. The best method for you will depend on your personal preference and the tools you have available.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thin out your own short hair using thinning shears:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it is completely dry before you begin.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Use hair clips to separate the sections.
  3. Take a small section of hair and hold it with your fingers. Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle to your hair and start cutting the hair in a zigzag pattern. This will help to create a more natural look.
  4. Continue to thin out each section of hair until you have achieved the desired look.
  5. Blend the hair by combing it with a regular comb.
  6. Repeat the process on each section of hair until you have thinned out your entire head of hair.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up.
  • Use the right tools for your hair type.
  • Be patient and take your time.
  • Don't overdo it. It's better to take off too little than too much.
  • Don't use thinning shears on wet hair.

FAQs

Can I use regular scissors to thin out my hair?

While you can use regular scissors to thin out your hair, it is not recommended. Regular scissors can create a blunt cut, which can make your hair look choppy and uneven. Thinning shears are designed to create a more natural look.

How often should I thin out my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. As a general rule, you should thin out your hair every 6 to 8 weeks.

Hair Care After Thinning

After thinning out your hair, it is important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Avoid using heavy hair products that can weigh your hair down.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and texture of your hair.

Thinning out your own short hair can be a great way to achieve a more voluminous and textured look. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve salon-quality results at home. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips to get the best results.
