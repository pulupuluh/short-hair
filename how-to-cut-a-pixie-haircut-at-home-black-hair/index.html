How To Cut A Pixie Haircut At Home Black Hair

Written by Petter Jun 24, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of heading to the salon every few weeks for a haircut? Why not try cutting your hair at home? A pixie haircut is a classic, low-maintenance style that looks great on black hair. With the right tools and techniques, you can easily achieve this look in the comfort of your own home. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of cutting a pixie haircut at home on black hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut a Pixie Haircut at Home Black Hair
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Step by Step

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it’s clean and dry. Comb through it to remove any tangles or knots. It’s also a good idea to section your hair using clips or hair ties to make it easier to work with.

Step 2: Cut the Back and Sides

Start by cutting the back and sides of your hair. Use clippers or scissors to trim the hair to your desired length. A good starting point is to cut it to about half an inch. Keep in mind that you can always go shorter, but it’s harder to add length once it’s been cut.

Step 3: Cut the Top

Next, move on to the top of your hair. Use scissors to cut the hair to your desired length. Keep in mind that the top of a pixie cut is usually longer than the sides and back. To create texture, you can also use thinning shears to remove some of the bulk.

Step 4: Blend It Together

Once you’ve cut the back, sides, and top, it’s time to blend it all together. Use scissors or clippers to blend the different lengths of hair together. This will create a seamless look and ensure that your haircut looks professional.

Step 5: Final Touches

Once you’re happy with the length and overall shape of your haircut, it’s time to make any final touches. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs and make sure everything looks even. You can also use thinning shears to create more texture and movement in your hair.

Tips

  • Invest in quality hair cutting tools, such as scissors, clippers, and thinning shears.
  • Start with a longer length and gradually go shorter to avoid cutting off too much hair.
  • Take your time and cut small sections of hair at a time.
  • If you’re not confident in your cutting skills, start with a smaller section of hair and work your way up to a full pixie haircut.

FAQ

Can I cut a pixie haircut on natural black hair?

Absolutely! This tutorial can work on any type of black hair, whether it’s natural or relaxed.

How often should I cut my pixie haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you want to keep your haircut. Generally, you should plan on cutting your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the length and shape of your haircut.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you accidentally cut off too much hair or make a mistake, don’t panic! You can always go to a salon to have it fixed, or you can try to style your hair in a way that hides the mistake until it grows out.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it so that it stays healthy and looks great. Make sure to wash and condition your hair regularly, and use a deep conditioning treatment once a week. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair. Instead, opt for air-drying or using heat protectant products.

Cutting a pixie haircut at home on black hair is easier than you might think. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut that’s both stylish and low-maintenance. Remember to take your time, start with a longer length, and don’t be afraid to make mistakes. Happy cutting!
