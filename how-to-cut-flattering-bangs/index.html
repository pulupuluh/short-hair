How To Cut Flattering Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Sep 11, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of your current hairstyle and looking for a change? Cutting bangs is a great way to switch things up and frame your face in a flattering way. However, it can be intimidating to cut your own bangs, especially if you have never done it before. In this guide, we will go over the steps to cut your own flattering bangs and provide tips for hair care.

Description

Cutting your own bangs can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a flattering look that suits your face shape and hair type. In this guide, we will provide a step-by-step process for cutting your own bangs, as well as tips for maintaining your new hairstyle.

How to Cut Flattering Bangs

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. It's also important to have a mirror in front of you and good lighting so you can see what you're doing.

Step 2: Choose Your Bangs Style

There are several types of bangs to choose from, including blunt, side-swept, and curtain bangs. Consider your face shape and hair texture when selecting a style. Blunt bangs work well for oval or heart-shaped faces, while side-swept bangs are great for round or square faces.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using the comb, section off the hair you want to cut into bangs. Make sure the section is even and straight.

Step 4: Cut Your Bangs

Starting in the middle of the section, hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across. Work your way out to the sides, cutting small amounts at a time. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back!

Step 5: Check Your Work

After you've finished cutting, take a step back and examine your bangs in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments, such as trimming uneven pieces or cutting more if needed.

Tips for Cutting Flattering Bangs

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own bangs:

  • Start with small snips, especially if you're unsure of the length you want.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to take your time and cut gradually.
  • Consider your hair texture. If you have curly or wavy hair, your bangs may appear shorter when dry, so be cautious when cutting.
  • Use hair clips to section off your hair and keep it out of the way while you cut.
  • When in doubt, seek the advice of a professional stylist.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own bangs:

Can I cut my own bangs with regular scissors?

While it's possible to cut your own bangs with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Investing in a pair of sharp hairdressing scissors will make the process much easier and produce better results.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It's best to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and prevent them from becoming too long.

What if I mess up my bangs?

If you accidentally cut your bangs too short or uneven, don't panic. There are several ways to style your hair to conceal the mistake, such as pinning your bangs back or wearing a headband.

Hair Care

Maintaining your new bangs requires proper hair care. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent oil buildup.
  • Blow-dry your bangs using a round brush to create volume.
  • Use a styling product, such as hairspray or mousse, to keep your bangs in place.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can make them oily and lose their shape.

Cutting your own bangs can be a fun and rewarding experience when done correctly. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a flattering and stylish look. Remember to take your time, start with small snips, and seek the advice of a professional if you're unsure. Happy cutting!
