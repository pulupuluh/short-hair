How To Cut Front Hair Shorter A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Mar 08, 2023 · 6 min read

Cutting your front hair shorter can be a great way to change up your hairstyle and add some freshness to your look. Whether you want to go for a dramatic chop or just remove a few inches, this guide will walk you through the process of cutting your front hair shorter.

Description

Before you start cutting your hair, it’s important to have a clear idea of what you want. Do you want a blunt cut or something more layered? How much do you want to take off? Make sure you have a clear vision in mind before you start cutting.

How to Cut Front Hair Shorter

Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that your hair is clean and free of any product buildup.

Step 2: Section your hair

Section your hair so that just your front hair is left. Use hair clips to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Determine the length

Decide how much hair you want to cut off. Remember that you can always go shorter, but you can’t go longer once you’ve made the cut.

Step 4: Cut your hair

Using a sharp pair of scissors, cut your hair straight across. Make sure you cut small sections at a time, and check your progress in the mirror frequently. This will help you avoid any mistakes.

Step 5: Blend your hair

After you’ve cut your hair, use a comb to blend the cut hair with the rest of your hair. This will create a more natural look.

Step 6: Style your hair

Style your hair as desired. Use a hair straightener or curling iron to create waves or curls, or keep it straight for a sleek look.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your front hair shorter:

  • Start by cutting less than you think you need to. You can always go shorter later.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and a jagged cut.
  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time to avoid any mistakes.
  • Check your progress in the mirror frequently.
  • If you’re unsure about cutting your own hair, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQ

Can I cut my front hair shorter if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your front hair shorter if you have curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so make sure you don’t cut off too much.

Should I cut my hair when it’s wet or dry?

It’s best to cut your hair when it’s dry. This will give you a more accurate idea of how much you’re cutting off and how your hair will look once it’s styled.

How often should I cut my hair?

It’s recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care

Cutting your hair is just one part of maintaining healthy, beautiful hair. Here are a few tips to keep your hair looking its best:

  • Wash your hair 2-3 times a week with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to deep condition your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and using the step-by-step guide above, you can cut your front hair shorter and achieve a fresh, new look. Just remember to take your time, cut small sections at a time, and check your progress in the mirror frequently. Happy cutting!
