How To Cut Layers In Hair To Frame Face A Step By Step Guide

Are you looking for a new hairstyle that will flatter your face shape and highlight your best features? Consider cutting layers in your hair to frame your face. This technique can add dimension and movement to your locks, while also accentuating your cheekbones, jawline, and other facial features.

Description

Layered hairstyles are popular for a reason. They can add volume, texture, and movement to hair, making it look more lively and full of character. Cutting layers around the face can also help to highlight your best features, such as your eyes, cheekbones, and lips.

However, cutting layers in hair is not always an easy task. It requires some skill and technique to get the layers right and ensure a flattering, natural-looking result. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting layers in hair to frame your face, step by step.

How to Cut Layers in Hair to Frame Face

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to have an idea of the style you want to achieve. Look for pictures of layered hairstyles that you like and consider how they would suit your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle. You can also consult with a hairstylist or use virtual hairstyling tools to try out different looks.

Once you have a style in mind, gather the necessary tools and materials. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a fine-toothed comb, hair clips, and possibly a water spray bottle or hair mist to dampen the hair. Find a well-lit area with a mirror and make sure you have enough time and patience to complete the task.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut layers in hair to frame your face:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair that is free of tangles and knots. Comb the hair straight and divide it into sections. Use hair clips to secure the top sections away from the lower sections.
  2. Identify the area around your face where you want to add layers. This can be the front sections, the sides, or both. Take a small section of hair from the chosen area and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers at the desired length and use the scissors to cut across the ends in a straight line.
  3. Release another section of hair and repeat the same process, using the first section as a guide. Make sure to cut the hair at an angle, rather than straight across, to create a more natural look.
  4. Continue to release and cut small sections of hair, working your way around the face. Keep the length and angle consistent, and avoid cutting too much at once. You can always go back and trim more if needed.
  5. Once you've finished cutting the front and side sections, release the top sections and blend them with the lower sections. Use the same cutting technique, taking small sections and cutting at an angle to create layers that flow seamlessly into the rest of the hair.
  6. Check the layers from different angles and adjust as needed. If you want more volume, you can add more layers or remove some weight from the hair. If you want a softer, more natural look, you can blend the layers more.
  7. Finish with some styling products, such as a volumizing mousse, a texturizing spray, or a shine serum, to enhance the layers and add shine and hold to the hair.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting layers in hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work gradually, rather than cutting too much at once.
  • Use sharp, high-quality hair cutting scissors to ensure clean, precise cuts.
  • Cut at an angle, rather than straight across, to create a more natural-looking result.
  • Consider your face shape and features when deciding where to add layers.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment and try different techniques and styles.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting layers in hair to frame your face:

  • Is it easy to cut layers in hair? Cutting layers in hair requires some skill and practice, but it can be done at home with the right tools and technique.
  • What is the best way to cut layers in hair? The best way to cut layers in hair is to start with small sections, cut at an angle, and work gradually, blending the layers with the rest of the hair.
  • How do I know where to add layers? You can add layers around the face to highlight your best features, such as your cheekbones, eyes, or lips. You can also add layers throughout the hair for more volume and texture.
  • Can I cut my own hair? Yes, you can cut your own hair, but it's recommended to have some experience or training, or to seek the help of a professional hairstylist.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Once you've cut layers in your hair, it's important to take good care of it to maintain the style and health of your locks. Here are some hair care tips for layered hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type and texture.
  • Avoid using too much heat styling tools, such as hair dryers, straighteners, and curling irons, which can damage the hair and make it dry and brittle.
  • Use a heat protectant spray or serum before using heat styling tools.
  • Get regular trims to keep the layers looking fresh and healthy.
  • Consider using hair masks, oils, or serums to nourish and hydrate the hair, especially if it's dry or damaged.

Now that you know how to cut layers in hair to frame your face, you can experiment with different styles and techniques to find the perfect look for you. With some practice and patience, you can achieve a flattering, natural-looking result that highlights your best features and adds dimension and movement to your locks.
