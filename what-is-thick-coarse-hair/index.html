What Is Thick Coarse Hair

Written by Oliver Jul 15, 2023 · 9 min read

Thick coarse hair is a hair type that is characterized by its thickness and rough texture. It is typically difficult to manage and can be challenging to style. This hair type is common among people of African, Caribbean, and Hispanic descent, but it can also be found in people of other ethnicities.

The Description of Thick Coarse Hair

Thick coarse hair is often referred to as “kinky” or “curly” hair. It is composed of tightly coiled strands that are thick in diameter. The hair strands are often densely packed together, giving the appearance of a full head of hair. The texture of thick coarse hair is rough and often feels dry to the touch.

How to Determine if You Have Thick Coarse Hair

The easiest way to determine if you have thick coarse hair is to examine the texture and thickness of your hair strands. If your hair strands are thick in diameter and have a rough texture, then you likely have thick coarse hair. Another way to determine your hair type is to consult with a hair care professional who can examine your hair and provide guidance on the best hair care practices.

The Challenges of Managing Thick Coarse Hair

One of the main challenges of managing thick coarse hair is that it can be difficult to style. The hair strands are often tightly coiled, making it challenging to straighten or curl the hair. Additionally, because the hair is thick and dense, it can be challenging to apply products evenly throughout the hair.

How to Care for Thick Coarse Hair

The key to caring for thick coarse hair is to keep it moisturized. Because the hair is naturally dry, it is important to use products that will help to hydrate and nourish the hair. This includes using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, as well as applying a leave-in conditioner or hair oil after washing.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Thick Coarse Hair

Styling thick coarse hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques and products, it is possible to create a variety of styles. Here is a step-by-step guide to styling thick coarse hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

The first step to styling thick coarse hair is to wash and condition your hair. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type. Rinse your hair thoroughly to remove all product residue.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner or Hair Oil

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help moisturize and nourish your hair. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, making sure to focus on the ends and any areas that are particularly dry.

Step 3: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots. Be gentle when detangling your hair to avoid causing breakage or damage.

Step 4: Choose Your Styling Method

There are many different ways to style thick coarse hair, including braids, twists, and bantu knots. Choose the method that works best for you and your hair type.

Step 5: Apply Styling Products

Apply styling products to your hair, making sure to distribute them evenly throughout your hair. This will help to define your curls or waves and keep your style in place.

Step 6: Allow Your Hair to Dry

Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and make your hair more difficult to manage.

Tips for Managing Thick Coarse Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing thick coarse hair:

  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair too often.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol, as these can dry out your hair.

FAQ About Thick Coarse Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about thick coarse hair:

Q: Can I straighten my thick coarse hair?

A: Yes, but it can be challenging to straighten thick coarse hair. Use a heat protectant spray and a flat iron with adjustable temperature settings to minimize damage to your hair.

Q: Can I color my thick coarse hair?

A: Yes, but be aware that coloring your hair can cause damage and dryness. Use a deep conditioning treatment before and after coloring your hair to keep it moisturized.

Q: How often should I wash my thick coarse hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with thick coarse hair prefer to wash their hair once a week, while others prefer to wash their hair every few days.

In Conclusion

Thick coarse hair is a hair type that is characterized by its thickness and rough texture. It can be challenging to manage, but with the right techniques and products, it is possible to create a variety of styles. Remember to keep your hair moisturized, avoid using too much heat, and be gentle when detangling your hair to keep it healthy and strong.
