What Does The Bible Say About Bald Eagles

Written by Frank Aug 18, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're curious about what the Bible has to say about bald eagles, you're not alone. Many people are interested in learning more about these majestic birds and what their significance is in the Bible. In this article, we'll explore what the Bible says about bald eagles and how their symbolism is portrayed in the scriptures.

What is a Bald Eagle?

Before we dive into what the Bible says about bald eagles, let's first understand what they are. Bald eagles are large birds of prey that are found primarily in North America. They are known for their distinctive white head and tail feathers, as well as their powerful talons and sharp beaks. These birds are a symbol of strength, freedom, and courage.

Bald Eagles in the Bible

While bald eagles are not specifically mentioned in the Bible, there are several references to eagles that are believed to be referring to this bird. In the Old Testament, eagles are often used as a metaphor for God's power and protection. For example, in Exodus 19:4 it says, "You yourselves have seen what I did to the Egyptians, and how I bore you on eagles' wings and brought you to myself."

Another example is found in Isaiah 40:31, which says, "But they who wait for the Lord shall renew their strength; they shall mount up with wings like eagles; they shall run and not be weary; they shall walk and not faint." This verse is often interpreted as a reminder that God's strength and protection is available to those who trust in Him.

The Symbolism of Bald Eagles

While bald eagles may not be directly mentioned in the Bible, their symbolism is still present in the scriptures. As mentioned earlier, eagles are often used as a metaphor for God's power and protection. They are also associated with freedom and courage, which are values that are highly regarded in the Bible.

Additionally, eagles are known for their keen eyesight and their ability to soar to great heights. This is another symbol of God's omniscience and omnipotence. Just as an eagle can see things from a great distance and soar above the earth, God sees and knows all things.

Conclusion

In conclusion, while bald eagles are not specifically mentioned in the Bible, their symbolism is still present in the scriptures. They are a powerful symbol of God's strength, protection, and omniscience. So the next time you see a bald eagle soaring through the sky, remember the power and protection that God provides for us.
