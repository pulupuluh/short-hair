How Do You Thin Out Thick Hair

Written by Bardi Jul 11, 2023 · 6 min read

Thick hair is a blessing, but it can also be a curse. It can be difficult to manage and style, and can often feel heavy and overwhelming. If you're looking for a way to thin out your thick hair, you're in luck! In this article, we'll show you how to thin out thick hair in a few simple steps.

Description

Thick hair can be overwhelming and difficult to manage. Thinning out your hair can make it easier to style and manage, and can help it look healthier and more vibrant.

How to Thin Out Thick Hair

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

The first step in thinning out your hair is to start with clean hair. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue from your hair, and then condition as normal. This will help to make your hair more pliable and easier to work with.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Next, divide your hair into sections. This will help you to work on one section at a time, making the process easier and more manageable. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Choose Your Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are a special type of scissors that are designed to remove bulk from your hair. Choose a pair of thinning shears that are sharp and easy to handle.

Step 4: Thin Out Your Hair

Starting at the bottom of each section, use your thinning shears to remove small sections of hair. Work your way up to the top of the section, thinning out the hair as you go. Be sure to keep the shears at an angle, and avoid cutting too much hair at once.

Step 5: Repeat on Each Section

Repeat this process on each section of your hair, working from the bottom to the top. Be sure to take your time and work carefully, as you don't want to remove too much hair at once.

Tips

- Use a comb to help guide the thinning shears through your hair
- Work in a well-lit area
- Take breaks as needed to rest your hands and arms
- Don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or stylist

FAQ

Q: Will thinning out my hair damage it?
A: No, if done properly, thinning out your hair should not cause any damage. However, if you're unsure about how to do it, it's best to consult a professional stylist.

Q: How often should I thin out my hair?
A: This will depend on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it's best to thin out your hair every 3-6 months.

Hair Care

Thinning out your hair can make it easier to manage and style, but it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips to help you care for your hair:

- Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
- Avoid using heat styling tools too often
- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
- Protect your hair from the sun with a hat or scarf
- Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and free from split ends

Thinning out your hair can be a great way to give it a fresh new look. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a thinner, more manageable style that looks great and feels great.
