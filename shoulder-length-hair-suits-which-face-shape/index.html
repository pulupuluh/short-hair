Shoulder Length Hair Suits Which Face Shape

Written by Bardi Apr 28, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you thinking of changing your hairstyle and going for a shoulder-length cut? Well, you’re in luck because shoulder length hair is versatile and suits many face shapes. In this article, we’ll explore which face shapes are best suited for shoulder length hair and provide some tips on how to maintain your new look.

Which Face Shape Suits Shoulder Length Hair?

Shoulder length hair is a great option for those with oval, round, square, and heart-shaped faces. It works well with these face shapes because it frames the face and draws attention to the cheekbones, which is a flattering feature for many people.

If you have an oval face shape, you’re in luck because shoulder-length hair suits this face shape the best. The length of the hair accentuates the face’s natural symmetry, and the layers add texture and volume to the hair.

For those with a round face shape, a shoulder-length cut with layers will help to elongate the face and create the illusion of length. It’s best to avoid blunt cuts or heavy bangs, as they can make the face appear rounder.

If you have a square face shape, shoulder-length hair will soften the angles of your face and add some femininity. A side part and face-framing layers will help to balance out the jawline and create a more flattering shape.

Finally, for those with a heart-shaped face, a shoulder-length cut with layers can help to balance out a wider forehead and create more volume around the chin area.

How to Achieve the Perfect Shoulder Length Haircut

Before you head to the salon, it’s important to consider your hair type and texture. If you have thick hair, you may want to opt for longer layers to avoid a triangular shape. On the other hand, if you have fine hair, shorter layers will add more volume and texture.

When discussing your cut with your stylist, be sure to mention which face shape you have and what you hope to achieve with your new haircut. This will help your stylist to create a cut that is tailored to your specific needs.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Shoulder Length Hair

1. Start by washing and drying your hair.

2. Divide your hair into sections, starting at the back of your head.

3. Use a comb to create a horizontal parting just above the nape of your neck.

4. Cut the hair in a straight line, making sure to keep the hair taut.

5. Repeat this process, moving up the head and cutting each section to the desired length.

6. Once you’ve cut all the sections, check for any uneven areas and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Maintaining Shoulder Length Hair

To keep your shoulder length hair looking its best, be sure to schedule regular trims every six to eight weeks. This will help to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your cut.

It’s also important to invest in quality hair care products that are designed for your hair type. This can include a good shampoo and conditioner, as well as any styling products that you may need.

If you have curly hair, consider using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help define your curls and reduce frizz. For those with straight hair, a round brush can help to add volume and create soft, loose waves.

FAQs

Q: Can shoulder length hair be styled in different ways?

A: Yes, shoulder length hair is versatile and can be styled in many different ways. You can wear it straight and sleek, add loose waves for a more relaxed look, or even braid it for a bohemian vibe.

Q: Is shoulder length hair easy to maintain?

A: Yes, shoulder length hair is relatively easy to maintain. Regular trims and quality hair care products can help to keep your hair healthy and looking its best.

Q: Can I still wear my hair up with a shoulder length cut?

A: Yes, you can still wear your hair up with a shoulder length cut. A low ponytail or messy bun can add a chic and effortless vibe to your look.

Hair Care for Shoulder Length Hair

To keep your shoulder length hair looking its best, it’s important to take good care of it. This can include using a gentle shampoo and conditioner, avoiding excessive heat styling, and protecting your hair from the sun and other environmental factors.

You can also consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish your hair and keep it healthy.

With the right care and styling, shoulder length hair can be a flattering and versatile option for many different face shapes. So, whether you have an oval, round, square, or heart-shaped face, consider giving this classic cut a try.
