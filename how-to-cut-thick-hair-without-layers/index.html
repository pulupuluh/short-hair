How To Cut Thick Hair Without Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Apr 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have thick hair, you know that finding the right haircut can be a challenge. Layers are a popular option, but they aren't always the best choice for everyone. Whether you prefer a blunt cut or just want to try something new, learning how to cut thick hair without layers can help you achieve the look you want.

How to Cut Thick Hair Without Layers

Before you get started, there are a few things you should know about cutting thick hair without layers. First, it's important to use the right tools. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to keep your hair in place. It's also a good idea to work with clean, dry hair so you can see exactly how much you're cutting.

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting thick hair without layers:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 2: Cut the Back Sections

Working with one of the back sections, comb your hair straight down and trim the ends. Use your fingers to measure how much you want to cut, and be sure to cut straight across.

Repeat this process with the other back section.

Step 3: Cut the Front Sections

For the front sections, comb your hair straight down and cut the ends at a slight angle. This will help create a soft, face-framing effect.

Step 4: Check for Balance

After you've cut all four sections, check to make sure your hair is balanced. If one side is longer than the other, trim the longer side until it matches the shorter side.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

If you want to add some texture to your hair, you can use a technique called "point cutting." Simply hold your scissors vertically and cut into the ends of your hair at an angle. This will create subtle layers that blend seamlessly with the rest of your hair.

Tips for Cutting Thick Hair Without Layers

Now that you know how to cut thick hair without layers, here are a few tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always take more off, but you can't add it back on!
  • Take breaks as needed. Cutting thick hair can be tiring, so don't be afraid to take a break and stretch your hands and arms.
  • Invest in high-quality scissors. A sharp pair of scissors will make all the difference when cutting thick hair.
  • Ask for help if you need it. If you're not sure about a certain section, ask a friend or family member for their opinion.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about cutting thick hair without layers:

Can I cut my own hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair! Just be sure to use the right tools and take your time.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it becomes damaged. A good rule of thumb is to get your hair cut every six to eight weeks.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you accidentally cut too much off, don't panic! You can always book an appointment with a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Now that you've learned how to cut thick hair without layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and learning how to cut thick hair without layers, you can achieve the perfect haircut for your hair type and style.
