How To Get Choppy Hair A Relaxed Guide

If you're looking to add some edginess to your hairstyle, choppy hair is the way to go. This style is perfect for those who want to add texture and volume to their hair. In this article, we'll guide you on how to get choppy hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Get Choppy Hair
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips for Maintaining Choppy Hair
  4. Frequently Asked Questions
  5. Hair Care Tips for Choppy Hair

How to Get Choppy Hair

Choppy hair is a great way to add texture and volume to your hair. Here's how you can achieve this look:

1. Choose the Right Haircut

The first step to achieving choppy hair is to get the right haircut. You'll want to go for a layered haircut with choppy ends. This will give your hair the texture and volume it needs to achieve the choppy look.

2. Use the Right Products

Using the right products is crucial when it comes to achieving choppy hair. You'll want to use a texturizing spray or a sea salt spray to add volume and texture to your hair. These products will help create the messy, choppy look you're going for.

3. Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair is an important step in achieving choppy hair. You'll want to blow dry your hair upside down to add volume and texture. Use your fingers to tousle your hair as you blow dry it to create the choppy look.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get choppy hair:

Step 1: Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help add volume to your hair and create a good base for the choppy look.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

After washing your hair, towel dry it to remove excess moisture. You want your hair to be damp, not wet.

Step 3: Apply Texturizing Spray

Apply a texturizing spray or sea salt spray to your hair. This will add texture and volume to your hair.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair upside down to add volume and texture. Use your fingers to tousle your hair as you blow dry it to create the choppy look.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, use a styling product to define the choppy layers. You can use a pomade or wax to create a messy, textured look.

Tips for Maintaining Choppy Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining choppy hair:

1. Use the Right Products

Using the right products is crucial when it comes to maintaining choppy hair. Use a texturizing spray or sea salt spray to add volume and texture to your hair.

2. Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, causing it to become dry and brittle. Try to wash your hair every other day to maintain its natural oils.

3. Get Regular Haircuts

Getting regular haircuts is important when it comes to maintaining choppy hair. This will help keep your hair looking fresh and textured.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is choppy hair suitable for all hair types?

Choppy hair is suitable for all hair types, but it works best on medium to long hair. If you have short hair, you can still achieve a choppy look, but it may require more styling.

2. How often should I get a haircut to maintain choppy hair?

You should get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain choppy hair. This will help keep your hair looking fresh and textured.

3. Can I achieve choppy hair without using heat styling tools?

Yes, you can achieve choppy hair without using heat styling tools. Simply apply a texturizing spray or sea salt spray to your hair and let it air dry. Use your fingers to tousle your hair as it dries to create the choppy look.

Hair Care Tips for Choppy Hair

Here are some hair care tips for choppy hair:

1. Use a Hair Mask

Using a hair mask once a week can help keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Look for a hair mask that's designed for your hair type.

2. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Using heat styling tools can damage your hair, causing it to become dry and brittle. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles can pull on your hair, causing it to become damaged and brittle. Avoid tight hairstyles like tight ponytails and braids.

Now that you know how to get choppy hair, it's time to give it a try. Remember to use the right products and techniques to achieve the perfect choppy look. With a little practice, you'll be able to rock this edgy hairstyle with confidence.
