Does Perm Make Your Hair Thicker Truth And Myths

Description

Perming hair is a popular way to achieve curly, wavy, or voluminous hair. It involves using chemicals to change the structure of your hair. But, does perm make your hair thicker? This is a common question asked by those who want to achieve thicker hair. Unfortunately, the answer is no. Perming hair does not make it thicker.

However, perming hair can create the illusion of thicker hair. This is because perms add volume to the hair shaft by making it fuller and bouncier. It gives the appearance of having more hair than before.

How to Perm Your Hair

If you're interested in getting a perm, you can do it at home or at a salon. Here are the steps to perming your hair:

Step 1: Choose the right perm solution

There are different types of perm solutions for different hair types. Choose the one that suits your hair type.

Step 2: Wash your hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue. Do not use conditioner.

Step 3: Section your hair

Section your hair into small, even sections using hair clips.

Step 4: Apply the perm solution

Apply the perm solution to each section of hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to perming your hair:

Step 1: Buy a perm kit

You can buy a perm kit at your local drugstore or beauty supply store. Make sure to choose a kit that's suitable for your hair type.

Step 2: Wash your hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue. Do not use conditioner.

Step 3: Section your hair

Section your hair into small, even sections using hair clips.

Step 4: Apply the perm solution

Apply the perm solution to each section of hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step 5: Leave the solution on

Leave the solution on for the recommended time specified in the kit instructions.

Step 6: Rinse your hair

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water for at least five minutes. Do not shampoo.

Step 7: Apply the neutralizer

Apply the neutralizer to your hair to stop the perming process.

Step 8: Rinse again

Rinse your hair again with warm water for at least five minutes.

Step 9: Style your hair

Style your hair as desired. You can blow-dry or air-dry your hair.

Tips for a Successful Perm

Here are some tips to keep in mind for a successful perm:

  • Do not wash your hair for at least 48 hours after perming.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools for the first few weeks after perming.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Do not brush your hair when it's wet. Use a wide-tooth comb instead.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol as it can dry out your hair.

FAQs About Perming Hair

Does perming hair damage it?

Perming hair involves using chemicals that can damage it if not done correctly. It's important to follow the instructions carefully and not leave the solution on for too long.

How long does a perm last?

A perm can last for up to six months, depending on how fast your hair grows.

Can you perm colored hair?

Yes, you can perm colored hair. However, it's recommended to wait at least two weeks after coloring before perming.

Hair Care After a Perm

After getting a perm, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as it can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.

In conclusion, perming hair does not make it thicker, but it can make it appear fuller and bouncier. If you're interested in getting a perm, make sure to follow the instructions carefully and take care of your hair after the process.
