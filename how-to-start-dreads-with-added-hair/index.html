How To Start Dreads With Added Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 25, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you interested in starting dreadlocks but have fine or thin hair? Or maybe you want to add length and volume to your existing locks? Whatever your reason, starting dreads with added hair is a great way to achieve the look you want. In this article, we’ll go over the steps to take, tips for success, and how to care for your hair once your dreads are established.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Start Dreads with Added Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Success
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care for Dreads with Added Hair

How to Start Dreads with Added Hair

Starting dreads with added hair involves attaching extensions to your existing hair to create the foundation for your locks. You can use synthetic hair, human hair, or a combination of both. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Choose Your Hair

The first step is to choose the type of hair you want to use. Synthetic hair is less expensive and comes in a wide range of colors, but it doesn’t look as natural as human hair. Human hair is more expensive but blends seamlessly with your natural hair. You can also choose a combination of both.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Before attaching your extensions, you need to prepare your natural hair. Wash and condition your hair to remove any product buildup, then blow-dry it straight. This will ensure that the extensions are attached to clean, dry hair.

Step 3: Attach the Extensions

Using a crochet hook or a latch hook, attach the extensions to your natural hair. Start at the roots and work your way down, making sure to secure the extensions tightly. Repeat this process until you have attached extensions to your entire head.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Once your extensions are attached, you need to section your hair into the size of dreads you want. Use a comb to create even sections and secure each section with a rubber band or hair tie.

Step 5: Backcomb Your Hair

Starting at the roots, backcomb each section of hair by holding it taut and combing towards the roots. This will create knots and tangles that will eventually form into dreadlocks. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 6: Palm Roll Your Hair

After backcombing, use your palms to roll each section of hair in a circular motion. This will help to smooth out the knots and tangles and encourage your hair to form into dreadlocks. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide to starting dreads with added hair:

  1. Choose your hair.
  2. Prepare your hair by washing, conditioning, and blow-drying.
  3. Attach the extensions using a crochet hook or latch hook.
  4. Section your hair into the size of dreads you want.
  5. Backcomb each section of hair.
  6. Palm roll each section of hair.
  7. Repeat steps 5 and 6 until all sections of hair are backcombed and palm rolled.
  8. Use a dread wax or gel to help your hair lock together.
  9. Wrap your hair in a silk scarf overnight to help it set.
  10. Repeat the palm rolling process every few days until your dreads are established.

Tips for Success

Starting dreads with added hair can be a time-consuming process, but it’s worth it to achieve the look you want. Here are some tips to help you along the way:

  • Choose high-quality hair to ensure it lasts and looks natural.
  • Be patient – it can take several months for your dreads to fully form.
  • Be gentle with your hair – avoid harsh brushing or pulling.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent your hair from snagging while you sleep.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can cause buildup and prevent your hair from locking.

FAQ

Can I start dreads with added hair if I have very fine hair?

Yes, starting dreads with added hair is a great option for those with fine or thin hair.

How long does it take for dreads with added hair to fully form?

It can take several months for your dreads to fully form, but this can vary depending on your hair type and how well you care for your hair.

Do I need to use a special shampoo for my dreads?

Yes, it’s important to use a shampoo specifically designed for dreadlocks to prevent buildup and keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care for Dreads with Added Hair

Once your dreads are established, it’s important to take good care of them to keep them healthy and looking their best. Here are some tips for caring for your dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a dreadlock shampoo.
  • Avoid using conditioner, as this can loosen your dreads.
  • Use a residue-free dreadlock wax or gel to help your hair lock together.
  • Wrap your hair in a silk scarf at night to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Regularly palm roll your dreads to maintain their shape.

Starting dreads with added hair can be a fun and exciting way to achieve the look you want. By following these steps and taking good care of your hair, you can enjoy your new dreads for years to come.
