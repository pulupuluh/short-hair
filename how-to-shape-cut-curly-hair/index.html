How To Shape Cut Curly Hair Tips Steps And Faqs

Written by Oliver May 16, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Curly hair can be tricky to cut because of its natural volume and texture. Unlike straight hair, curly hair needs to be shaped to achieve the desired style. Shaping curly hair requires a different approach and technique than cutting straight hair. In this blog post, we will guide you on how to shape cut curly hair, step-by-step. We will also provide some tips, FAQs, and hair care recommendations to help you achieve the perfect curly hair cut.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Shape Cut Curly Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Recommendations

How to Shape Cut Curly Hair

Shaping curly hair involves creating layers and removing the bulk without compromising the natural curl pattern. The goal is to achieve a balanced shape that enhances the curls and minimizes frizz. Here are the basic steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Condition the Hair

Start by washing and conditioning the hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner to hydrate and detangle the curls. Rinse thoroughly and gently towel-dry the hair to remove excess water.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Divide the hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start by parting the hair in the middle or to the side, depending on the desired style. Then, create horizontal sections from the nape of the neck to the crown of the head.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of the hair. Take a small section of hair and twist it gently. Cut the ends using sharp scissors, following the natural curl pattern. Repeat this process for all the sections in the bottom layer.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Once you finish the bottom layer, move on to the middle layer. Take a section of hair from the middle of the head and twist it. Cut the ends using the same technique as before, following the natural curl pattern. Repeat this process for all the sections in the middle layer.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, move on to the top layer of the hair. Take a section of hair from the crown of the head and twist it. Cut the ends using the same technique as before, following the natural curl pattern. Repeat this process for all the sections in the top layer.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

After you finish cutting all the layers, blend them together by taking small sections of hair and cutting them at an angle. This will create a seamless transition between the layers and remove any unwanted bulk.

Step 7: Check for Balance

Check the balance of the hair by combing it straight down and checking for any unevenness or gaps. Make any necessary adjustments by cutting small sections of hair as needed.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to shape cut curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition the hair.
  2. Section the hair.
  3. Cut the bottom layer.
  4. Cut the middle layer.
  5. Cut the top layer.
  6. Blend the layers.
  7. Check for balance.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect curly hair cut:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging the hair.
  • Cut the hair when it is dry to see the natural curl pattern.
  • Start with small sections of hair and cut gradually to avoid cutting too much.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle the hair before cutting.
  • Do not use a razor or thinning scissors on curly hair, as they can cause frizz and damage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about shaping curly hair:

Q: Can I shape cut curly hair at home?

A: Yes, you can shape cut curly hair at home, but it requires some practice and technique. We recommend getting professional advice if you are not confident in cutting your hair.

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and length, but we recommend cutting curly hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Q: What products should I use to care for my curly hair?

A: We recommend using sulfate-free shampoo, moisturizing conditioner, and a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to enhance the natural curl pattern and prevent frizz.

Hair Care Recommendations

Here are some hair care recommendations for curly hair:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage the hair and alter the natural curl pattern.
  • Use a microfiber towel or a cotton t-shirt to dry the hair, as regular towels can cause frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage.
  • Protect the hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a protective spray.

With these tips, steps, and FAQs, you can now confidently shape cut your curly hair at home. Remember to take your time, use the right tools and products, and consult a professional if needed. Happy styling!
