How To Cut Your Hair At Home In Layers

Written by Daniel May 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle without leaving your house, cutting your hair at home in layers is a great way to do it. This guide will walk you through the steps you need to take to get a perfect layered cut. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced haircutter, this guide will help you achieve a professional-looking result.

Description

If you're not familiar with the term, layers are different lengths of hair that are cut into the same hairstyle. Layering can add volume, texture, and movement to your hair, making it a popular haircutting technique. By cutting your hair in layers, you can create a flattering and stylish look that suits your face shape and hair type.

How to Cut Your Hair at Home in Layers

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

To cut your hair at home in layers, you'll need the right tools. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. You may also want to have a spray bottle with water handy to keep your hair damp while you cut it.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Before you start cutting, divide your hair into sections. Use a comb to create a middle part and then divide your hair into two sections. Clip the top section of your hair on the crown of your head, leaving the bottom section loose. You can use additional clips to keep the sections separate.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, comb your hair straight down and trim the ends to your desired length. Make sure your scissors are sharp and cut your hair in a straight line. If you want to add more layers, cut your hair at an angle. This will create a softer look.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Once you've cut the bottom layer, release the top layer and comb it straight down. Cut the ends to match the length of the bottom layer. If you want more layers on top, cut your hair at an angle again.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

After cutting both layers, use your comb to blend the layers together. Comb your hair straight down and run the scissors down the length of your hair, cutting at an angle. This will help blend the layers together and create a seamless look.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your hair, check your work in the mirror. Make sure your layers are even and that your hair looks the way you want it to. If you need to make any adjustments, go back and trim your hair as needed.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair at home in layers:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to larger ones.
  • Use a mirror to check your work from different angles.
  • Remember to comb your hair straight down before cutting it.
  • If you're not sure how short to cut your hair, start with a longer length and work your way up.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to mistakes.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair in layers if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut curly hair in layers. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you may want to cut your hair longer than you would if it were straight.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always trim your hair a little shorter to fix the mistake. If you're unsure, consider reaching out to a professional hairdresser for advice.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair at home in layers, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Consider using a heat protectant spray before using heat tools, such as a curling iron or straightener. Avoid over-washing your hair and use a dry shampoo in between washes.

Now that you know how to cut your hair at home in layers, you can experiment with different styles and lengths. With a little practice, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home.
