How To Cut A Mullet For Dummies A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver May 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you looking to get a stylish and trendy haircut that is easy to maintain? Look no further than the classic mullet! This iconic hairstyle has made a comeback in recent years, and it's a great option for those who want to mix things up. In this article, we'll break down the steps to cutting a mullet for dummies in a relaxed, easy-to-understand language. So, let's get started!

How to Cut a Mullet: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start, make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to keep sections of hair in place.

Step 2: Prep the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. Comb it out so that it's free of tangles and easy to work with.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Next, use the comb to section the hair into three parts: the top, the sides, and the back. Use the hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Start with the top section of hair. Using the comb, lift a small section of hair straight up and cut it straight across with the scissors. Repeat this process throughout the top section of hair until you've achieved the desired length.

Step 5: Cut the Sides

For the side sections, use the comb to lift a small section of hair straight out from the head. Cut it at an angle, starting at the top of the section and angling the scissors towards the bottom. Repeat this process throughout the side sections of hair until you've achieved the desired length.

Step 6: Cut the Back

For the back section, use the comb to lift a small section of hair straight out from the head. Cut it straight across with the scissors. Repeat this process throughout the back section of hair until you've achieved the desired length.

Step 7: Blend the Sections

Once you've cut each section of hair, use the scissors to blend the sections together. This will create a seamless transition between the different lengths of hair.

Step 8: Add Some Texture

To add some texture to the mullet, use the scissors to cut into the ends of the hair. This will create some choppy layers and give the hair a more relaxed, tousled look.

Step 9: Style Your Mullet

Now that you've cut your mullet, it's time to style it! You can use a bit of styling wax or pomade to create some definition and hold. Play around with different styles to find what works best for you.

Tips for Cutting a Mullet

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting a mullet:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more off if needed.
  • Take your time and work in small sections.
  • When cutting the sides, make sure to angle the scissors towards the back of the head for a more blended look.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress and make sure both sides are even.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut a mullet on myself?

While it's possible to cut a mullet on yourself, it's recommended to have someone else do it for you. It can be difficult to get an even cut and blend the sections together when cutting your own hair.

How often should I trim my mullet?

It's recommended to trim your mullet every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking fresh and stylish.

Hair Care Tips

Here are a few tips to keep your mullet looking its best:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can damage the hair and lead to split ends.
  • Get regular trims to keep your mullet looking neat and tidy.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and lead to dryness.

And that's it! With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to cut a mullet for dummies and rock this trendy hairstyle with confidence.
