What To Use For Really Frizzy Hair In 2023

Written by Aprilia Jul 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have really frizzy hair, you know how frustrating it can be to manage. Frizz can make your hair look dull, unmanageable, and even damage it over time. Fortunately, there are many hair products that can help tame your frizz and give you the smooth, shiny locks you desire. In this article, we will discuss the best products to use for really frizzy hair.

How to Choose the Right Products for Frizzy Hair

Before we dive into the different products to use for frizzy hair, it's important to understand how to choose the right products. The first thing you should consider is your hair type. If you have curly or wavy hair, you may need different products than someone with straight hair. You should also consider your hair texture, thickness, and any other concerns you may have (such as dryness or damage).

Once you have a good understanding of your hair type and concerns, you can start looking for products that are specifically designed for frizzy hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, argan oil, or coconut oil, which can help smooth and nourish your hair. You should also look for products that are free from sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils and make frizz worse.

Step-by-Step Guide to Taming Frizzy Hair

Now that you know how to choose the right products for frizzy hair, let's look at a step-by-step guide to taming your frizz:

Step 1: Shampoo and Conditioner

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for frizzy hair. Look for products that are sulfate-free and contain ingredients like keratin or argan oil.

Step 2: Towel Dry

After washing your hair, gently towel dry to remove excess moisture. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel, as this can cause frizz.

Step 3: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your damp hair, focusing on the ends. This will help hydrate and smooth your hair.

Step 4: Use a Heat Protectant

If you plan on using heat styling tools, be sure to apply a heat protectant to your hair. This will help prevent damage and frizz.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Use a styling product that is specifically designed for frizzy hair, such as a smoothing serum or cream. Apply the product to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.

Step 6: Finish with a Hair Spray

Finish off your style with a hair spray that is designed to hold your hair in place without causing frizz.

Tips for Managing Frizzy Hair

In addition to using the right products and following the steps above, there are a few tips you can follow to help manage your frizzy hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make frizz worse.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, rather than a brush.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Invest in a good quality hair dryer with a diffuser attachment, which can help reduce frizz.

Frequently Asked Questions

What causes frizzy hair?

Frizzy hair is often caused by a lack of moisture in the hair, which can be due to genetics, hair damage, or environmental factors like humidity.

How often should I wash my hair if I have frizzy hair?

It's generally recommended that you wash your hair no more than 2-3 times per week if you have frizzy hair.

Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on frizzy hair?

While you can use regular shampoo and conditioner on frizzy hair, it's generally better to use products that are specifically designed for frizzy hair.

Hair Care for Frizzy Hair

In addition to using the right products and following the tips above, it's important to take good care of your hair if you have frizzy hair. This includes getting regular trims to prevent split ends, avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools, and protecting your hair from the sun and other environmental factors. With the right care and products, you can tame your frizz and achieve the smooth, shiny locks you desire.
