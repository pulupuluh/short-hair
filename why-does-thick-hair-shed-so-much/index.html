Why Does Thick Hair Shed So Much Explained In 2023

Written by Luffy Jun 29, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have thick hair, you might notice that you shed more hair than others. Shedding is a natural process that occurs when old hair falls out to make room for new hair. However, excessive shedding can be alarming and make you wonder why it is happening. In this blog post, we will explore the reasons why thick hair sheds so much and provide tips on how to manage it.

What Causes Thick Hair to Shed So Much?

There are several reasons why thick hair sheds so much. One of the main reasons is genetics. If your parents or grandparents have thick hair, you are more likely to have it too. Thick hair has a larger number of hair follicles, which means more hair strands are growing at any given time. This also means that more hair is shed when it reaches the end of its growth cycle.

Other factors that contribute to thick hair shedding include hormonal changes, stress, medication, and diet. Hormonal changes, such as those that occur during pregnancy, can cause hair shedding. Stress can also lead to hair loss, and certain medications can affect hair growth. Poor diet or nutritional deficiencies can also contribute to hair shedding.

How to Manage Thick Hair Shedding

If you are experiencing excessive shedding, there are several steps you can take to manage it. Firstly, it is important to identify and address any underlying causes, such as hormonal imbalances or nutritional deficiencies. You can do this by seeing a doctor or nutritionist who can perform tests to identify any issues.

In addition to addressing underlying causes, there are several lifestyle changes you can make to reduce shedding. These include reducing stress, getting regular exercise, and eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals. You can also use hair care products that are designed to strengthen and nourish hair, such as those containing biotin or keratin.

Step-by-Step Guide to Prevent Thick Hair Shedding

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you prevent thick hair shedding:

  1. Identify any underlying causes
  2. Reduce stress through meditation, yoga, or other relaxation techniques
  3. Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  4. Get regular exercise
  5. Use hair care products that are designed to strengthen and nourish hair
  6. Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair
  7. Massage your scalp regularly to improve blood flow to the hair follicles
  8. Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on the hair
  9. Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  10. Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip away natural oils

Tips to Reduce Thick Hair Shedding

Here are some additional tips to help you reduce thick hair shedding:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush to detangle your hair
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as this can dry it out
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair
  • Avoid pulling your hair back tightly into a ponytail or bun
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol or sulfates

FAQs: Why Does Thick Hair Shed So Much?

Q: Is it normal for thick hair to shed more than other hair types?

A: Yes, it is normal for thick hair to shed more than other hair types because it has a larger number of hair follicles.

Q: Can hormonal changes cause thick hair shedding?

A: Yes, hormonal changes can cause thick hair shedding, especially during pregnancy or menopause.

Q: Can stress cause thick hair shedding?

A: Yes, stress can cause thick hair shedding because it can disrupt the hair growth cycle.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

To keep your thick hair healthy and strong, here are some additional hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, such as flat irons or curling irons
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair
  • Avoid using hair accessories that can damage your hair, such as rubber bands or metal clips
  • Use a heat protectant spray if you do use hot tools on your hair
  • Avoid over-brushing your hair, as this can lead to breakage
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can reduce shedding and keep your thick hair healthy and strong.

Remember, shedding is a natural process that occurs in all hair types. However, if you are experiencing excessive shedding, it is important to address any underlying causes and take steps to manage it. By doing so, you can keep your hair healthy and prevent further shedding.
