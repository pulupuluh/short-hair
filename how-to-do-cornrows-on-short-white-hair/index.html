How To Do Cornrows On Short White Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you have short white hair, you may think that cornrows are not an option for you. However, with the right technique and a little bit of practice, anyone can achieve this classic braided look. In this article, we will walk you through the steps of how to do cornrows on short white hair.

How to Do Cornrows on Short White Hair

Cornrows are a popular hairstyle that involves braiding the hair close to the scalp. While cornrows are often associated with African American hair textures, they can be done on any hair type, including short white hair.

To do cornrows on short white hair, you will need:

  • Rat-tail comb
  • Clips
  • Hair gel or pomade
  • Bobby pins

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to do cornrows on short white hair:

  1. Start by parting your hair into sections with a rat-tail comb. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair. Use clips to hold the sections in place.
  2. Apply a small amount of hair gel or pomade to each section. This will help the hair stay in place and make it easier to braid.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the front of the first section and split it into three equal parts.
  4. Begin braiding the hair by crossing the left section over the middle section, then the right section over the middle section. As you braid, add a small section of hair to each section before crossing it over the middle section.
  5. Continue braiding until you reach the end of the section. Secure the braid with a bobby pin.
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 for each section of hair, working your way from front to back.
  7. Once you have braided all the sections, use a small amount of hair gel or pomade to smooth down any flyaways.

Tips for Cornrowing Short White Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect cornrows on short white hair:

  • Start with clean, detangled hair. This will make it easier to part and braid the hair.
  • Use a rat-tail comb to create clean, straight parts. This will help your cornrows look neat and even.
  • Apply a small amount of hair gel or pomade to each section before braiding. This will help the hair stay in place and make it easier to braid.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cornrowing can be time-consuming, but the end result is worth it.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cornrows:

How long do cornrows last?

Cornrows can last from 2-6 weeks, depending on how well they are maintained.

Will cornrows damage my hair?

Cornrows can cause damage to the hair if they are too tight or left in for too long. It is important to have them done by a professional and to take care of your hair while the cornrows are in.

Can I wash my hair with cornrows?

Yes, you can wash your hair with cornrows, but you should be gentle and avoid pulling on the braids.

Hair Care Tips for Cornrows

Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy while wearing cornrows:

  • Moisturize your scalp regularly with a light oil, such as jojoba or coconut oil.
  • Wash your hair at least once a week, using a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Be gentle when removing the cornrows. Use a detangling spray and a wide-tooth comb to gently loosen the braids.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve beautiful cornrows on short white hair. Remember to be patient and take your time, and you will have a stunning braided hairstyle that will turn heads.
