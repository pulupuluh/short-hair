How To Do Slope Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 01, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a chic and trendy haircut, the slope haircut might be the perfect choice for you. This haircut features a gradual decrease in length from the top of the head to the nape of the neck. It's a versatile style that can be customized to suit your face shape and hair type. In this article, we'll show you how to do a slope haircut in easy-to-follow steps.

How to Do a Slope Haircut

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clippers if you want to do a fade. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type. Dry your hair with a towel until it's damp but not dripping wet.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair into four parts: the top, the sides, and the back. Clip each section of hair to keep it in place.

Step 3: Cut the Top Section

Starting with the top section of hair, comb it straight up and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Repeat this process for the entire top section of hair, making sure to cut it evenly.

Step 4: Cut the Side Sections

Using the same technique, cut the side sections of hair to the desired length. Make sure to blend the top and side sections together for a seamless look.

Step 5: Cut the Back Section

For the back section of hair, comb it straight down and cut it to the desired length. Make sure to blend it with the side sections for a smooth transition.

Step 6: Do the Fade (Optional)

If you want to do a fade, use hair clippers to trim the hair at the nape of the neck. Start with a longer setting and gradually decrease the length until you achieve the desired fade.

Step 7: Clean Up the Edges

Use your scissors to clean up any stray hairs around the edges of the haircut. This will give it a polished look.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, style it to your liking. You can use a hair gel or pomade to add texture and hold.

Tips for Doing a Slope Haircut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when doing a slope haircut:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually decrease it to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Use a comb to keep the hair in place while you cut it.
  • Take breaks if you need to rest your hands or eyes.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you if you're not confident in your cutting skills.

FAQs About Slope Haircuts

Here are some frequently asked questions about slope haircuts:

What face shapes are best suited for slope haircuts?

Slope haircuts work well for oval, round, and heart-shaped faces. If you have a square or diamond-shaped face, you might want to consider a different haircut.

How often should I get a slope haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain the look. Most people get a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care for Slope Haircuts

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair after a slope haircut:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as hair dryers or straighteners.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of the haircut.
  • Use a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy.

With these tips and steps, you'll be able to do a slope haircut with ease. Whether you're looking for a fresh new look or want to try something different, the slope haircut is a stylish and versatile option.
