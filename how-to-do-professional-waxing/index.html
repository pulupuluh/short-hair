How To Do Professional Waxing A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 13, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're looking for a way to get smooth, hair-free skin, waxing is a great option. Professional waxing can be expensive, but with a little practice, you can learn to do it yourself at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of doing professional waxing at home, as well as provide tips and advice for hair care.

Table of Contents

  1. How to
  2. Step by step
  3. Tips
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care

How to

The first step to doing professional waxing at home is to gather your supplies. You'll need wax, wax strips, a wax warmer, and a spatula. You can find these supplies at most beauty supply stores or online.

Once you have your supplies, you'll want to prep your skin for waxing. This means cleaning the area you want to wax and making sure it's dry. Avoid using lotions or oils on the area, as this can make it harder for the wax to adhere to the hairs.

Step by Step

Step 1: Heat the Wax

Start by heating up your wax in the wax warmer. Follow the instructions on your wax to determine the appropriate temperature. Be sure to stir the wax occasionally to ensure it's evenly heated.

Step 2: Test the Temperature

Before applying the wax to your skin, test the temperature on the inside of your wrist. The wax should be warm but not too hot. If it's too hot, let it cool down for a few minutes before testing it again.

Step 3: Apply the Wax

Using the spatula, apply the wax to the area you want to wax in the direction of hair growth. Apply a thin layer of wax, making sure to leave a clean edge for easy removal.

Step 4: Apply the Wax Strip

Place a wax strip over the wax and smooth it down in the direction of hair growth. Be sure to leave a clean edge for easy removal.

Step 5: Remove the Wax Strip

Hold your skin taut with one hand and use the other hand to remove the wax strip quickly in the opposite direction of hair growth. Be sure to pull the wax strip parallel to the skin and not up or away from it.

Step 6: Repeat

Repeat these steps until you've removed all the hair you want to remove.

Tips

  • Start with a small area to get the hang of the process before moving on to larger areas.
  • Apply pressure to the area immediately after removing the wax strip to reduce pain and redness.
  • Use a post-waxing lotion or oil to soothe the skin and reduce redness and inflammation.

FAQ

Can I wax if I have sensitive skin?

Yes, but you'll want to be careful and avoid waxing areas with broken skin or rashes. You may also want to use a wax specifically designed for sensitive skin.

How often should I wax?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows back. Most people need to wax every 4-6 weeks.

Is there anything I should avoid before or after waxing?

Avoid tanning or using a tanning bed before or after waxing, as well as using any products that contain alcohol or exfoliators.

Hair Care

After waxing, it's important to take care of your skin and hair. Avoid hot baths or showers for 24 hours, as well as exposing the area to direct sunlight. You may also want to use a gentle exfoliator to prevent ingrown hairs.

Overall, doing professional waxing at home is a great way to save money and get the smooth, hair-free skin you want. Just be sure to follow these steps carefully and take care of your skin and hair afterwards.
