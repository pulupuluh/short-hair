Long Layers Haircut For Straight Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Luffy Apr 14, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have straight hair and you're looking for a haircut that will give you volume and movement, then the long layers haircut is the perfect choice for you. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about getting a long layers haircut for straight hair, including how to prepare for your appointment, what to expect during the haircut, and how to take care of your new style.

Description

A long layers haircut for straight hair is a haircut that involves cutting the hair into different lengths to create layers. The longest layers are usually cut to frame the face, while the shorter layers are cut throughout the rest of the hair to create volume and movement. This haircut is great for people with straight hair because it helps to add texture and dimension to their hair.

How to

Before you get a long layers haircut for straight hair, it's important to prepare your hair. This means washing your hair the day before your appointment and avoiding any styling products that could weigh down your hair. When you arrive at your appointment, your stylist will assess your hair and determine the best length for your layers.

Once your stylist has decided on the length for your layers, they will begin cutting your hair. They will start by cutting the longest layers around your face and then work their way back to the crown of your head. They will then cut shorter layers throughout the rest of your hair to create volume and movement.

After your haircut is complete, your stylist may blow-dry your hair and style it for you. They may also give you tips on how to style your new haircut at home.

Step by Step

Here is a step by step guide to getting a long layers haircut for straight hair:

  1. Wash your hair the day before your appointment.
  2. Avoid any styling products that could weigh down your hair.
  3. Arrive at your appointment and let your stylist assess your hair.
  4. Decide on the length for your layers.
  5. Have your stylist cut the longest layers around your face.
  6. Have your stylist cut shorter layers throughout the rest of your hair.
  7. Blow-dry and style your hair, if desired.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting a long layers haircut for straight hair:

  • Bring pictures of the haircut you want to show your stylist.
  • Communicate with your stylist throughout the haircut to ensure you're getting the look you want.
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to help maintain your new haircut.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that could weigh down your hair.
  • Consider getting regular trims to maintain your layers.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about long layers haircuts for straight hair:

How long does a long layers haircut for straight hair take?

The length of time it takes to get a long layers haircut for straight hair can vary depending on the length and thickness of your hair. It typically takes between 30 minutes to an hour to complete.

What face shapes are best for a long layers haircut for straight hair?

A long layers haircut for straight hair can work for a variety of face shapes, but it's particularly flattering for those with oval or round-shaped faces.

How often should I get a long layers haircut for straight hair?

It's recommended that you get a long layers haircut for straight hair every six to eight weeks to maintain your layers and keep your hair looking healthy.

Hair Care

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair after getting a long layers haircut for straight hair:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to help maintain your layers.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that could weigh down your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot styling tools.
  • Consider getting regular trims to maintain your layers.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you can enjoy your new long layers haircut for straight hair and keep it looking healthy and voluminous for weeks to come.
