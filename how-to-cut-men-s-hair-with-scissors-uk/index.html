Cutting men's hair with scissors is an art that requires patience, skill and practice. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional look without the need for expensive equipment. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut men's hair with scissors in the UK, along with some tips, FAQs and hair care advice.

Tools

Before you start cutting men's hair with scissors, you need to gather the necessary tools. You will need:

  • Scissors â invest in a good quality pair of hairdressing scissors
  • Comb â choose a wide-toothed comb for detangling and a fine-toothed comb for precision cutting
  • Hair clips â to hold sections of hair in place while you work
  • Spray bottle â to dampen the hair and make it easier to cut
  • Mirror â to see the back of the head

How to Cut Men's Hair with Scissors UK

The first step in cutting men's hair with scissors is to decide on the style you want to achieve. This will depend on the length and texture of the hair, as well as the individual's face shape and personal preferences.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut men's hair with scissors:

  1. Start by washing and drying the hair. Comb it through to remove any tangles.
  2. Section the hair by dividing it into four parts â top, sides and back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.
  3. Start with the back section. Comb the hair straight down and cut across the bottom to the desired length. Use the comb as a guide to ensure a straight line.
  4. Move on to the side sections, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Use the comb to lift each section and cut to the desired length.
  5. Repeat the process on the top section, using the comb to lift the hair and cut to the desired length.
  6. Blend the sections together by using the scissors to cut across the hair at an angle, from one section to the next.
  7. Check the length and shape in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments.
  8. Use the scissors to add texture and layers if desired. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut into it at an angle to create a choppy look.
  9. Finish by trimming around the ears and neckline.
  10. Style the hair as desired using products such as gel or wax.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you cut men's hair with scissors:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length.
  • Use the comb as a guide to ensure a straight line.
  • Don't cut too much off at once â you can always go back and trim more if needed.
  • Keep the hair damp as you work to make it easier to cut.
  • Use the mirror to check the back of the head regularly.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting men's hair with scissors:

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on the style and length of your hair. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to cut hair?

A: No, regular scissors are not designed for cutting hair and can cause damage and split ends. Invest in a good quality pair of hairdressing scissors.

Q: How can I avoid cutting too much off?

A: Start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length. You can always go back and trim more if needed.

Hair Care

To keep your hair looking its best after a cut, follow these tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly using products suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Now that you have a complete guide on how to cut men's hair with scissors in the UK, you can achieve a professional look from the comfort of your own home. Remember to be patient, take your time and follow the steps carefully. Happy cutting!
