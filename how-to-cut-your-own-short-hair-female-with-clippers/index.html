How To Cut Your Own Short Hair Female With Clippers

Written by Oliver Apr 14, 2023 · 7 min read

Cutting your own short hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have never done it before. However, with the right tools and a little bit of practice, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut in the comfort of your own home. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting your own short hair with clippers, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • Tools Needed
  • How to Cut Your Own Short Hair Female with Clippers
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Short Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Tools Needed

Before you start cutting your own hair, you will need the following tools:

  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Scissors
  • Clip or hair tie
  • Mirror
  • Clean towel

Make sure that your clippers are fully charged or plugged in before you start. You don't want them to run out of power halfway through the haircut.

How to Cut Your Own Short Hair Female with Clippers

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating at first, but with a little bit of practice, you can become an expert. Here are the basic steps to follow:

Step by Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb it out and make sure there are no tangles or knots.
  2. Decide on the length you want to cut your hair. You can use the different guards that come with your clippers to achieve different lengths.
  3. Attach the guard that corresponds to the length you want to the clippers.
  4. Starting at the nape of your neck, use the clippers to cut your hair in an upward motion. Move the clippers against the direction of hair growth, and go slowly to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  5. Work your way around your head, using the clippers to cut the hair on the sides and back.
  6. Once you have finished using the clippers, use a pair of scissors to trim any longer hairs that the clippers may have missed. Use the comb to hold the hair in place and cut in a straight line.
  7. If you want to add layers, use the scissors to cut the hair at an angle, instead of straight across.
  8. Use the mirror to check the back of your head and make any necessary adjustments.
  9. Once you are happy with the length and shape of your haircut, use the scissors to trim any stray hairs around your ears or neckline.
  10. Finally, use a clean towel to wipe away any loose hairs and style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Short Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve a great haircut:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one. It's easier to take off more hair than to add it back on.
  • If you have a friend or family member who can help you, have them check the back of your head to make sure it looks even.
  • Don't rush. Take your time and go slowly to avoid mistakes.
  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. They will make a big difference in the final result.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own hair with regular scissors?

We don't recommend it. Regular scissors are not designed for cutting hair and can cause split ends and other damage.

How often should I cut my own hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. As a general rule, we recommend cutting your hair every 4-6 weeks.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! Hair grows back. If you make a mistake, try to fix it with the scissors or a longer guard on the clippers. If all else fails, you can always go to a professional stylist to have it fixed.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and vibrant:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as curling irons or straighteners, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and healthy.

Cutting your own short hair with clippers can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools, a little bit of practice, and our step-by-step guide, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut in the comfort of your own home. Happy cutting!
