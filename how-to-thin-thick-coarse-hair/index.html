How To Thin Thick Coarse Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Apr 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have thick, coarse hair, you know that it can be difficult to manage. It can take forever to dry and style, and can often look unruly and frizzy. One solution to this problem is to thin out your hair. Thinning your hair can make it more manageable and easier to style. In this article, we'll go over how to thin thick coarse hair in a step-by-step guide.

How to Thin Thick Coarse Hair

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you start thinning your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. This will make it easier to see where you need to thin and how much you need to take off.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small, manageable sections. Use hair clips to keep each section separate. This will make it easier to thin your hair evenly.

Step 3: Use Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are special scissors that have teeth on one blade. These teeth help to remove some of the bulk from your hair without taking off too much length. Thinning shears are available at most beauty supply stores.

Step 4: Start at the Ends

Begin thinning your hair at the ends. Hold a small section of hair between your fingers and use the thinning shears to make small, quick cuts. Move up the section of hair, thinning as you go.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've thinned the ends of your hair, blend the layers. Take a small section of hair from the top and bottom layers and cut them together. This will help to blend the layers and create a more natural look.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

After you've thinned your hair, check your progress. Make sure your hair is still even and that you've thinned it out enough.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you've thinned your hair, style it as desired. Your hair should be easier to manage and style after thinning.

Tips for Thinning Thick Coarse Hair

Thinning your hair can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's easy to do. Here are a few tips to keep in mind when thinning your hair:

  • Use thinning shears that are sharp and clean.
  • Don't take off too much hair at once.
  • Blend the layers to create a more natural look.
  • Take your time and be patient.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you thin your hair with regular scissors?

While you can technically thin your hair with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Regular scissors are not designed for thinning and can create uneven and choppy results. Thinning shears are specifically designed to thin hair evenly and create a natural look.

How often should you thin your hair?

How often you should thin your hair depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. On average, it's recommended to thin your hair every three to six months.

Hair Care for Thick Coarse Hair

Thinning your hair can help make it more manageable, but it's still important to take care of your hair. Here are a few tips for caring for thick coarse hair:

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to help tame frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Conclusion

Thinning thick coarse hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and easier to style. With the right tools and techniques, it's easy to do at home. Remember to take your time and be patient, and always use sharp and clean thinning shears.
