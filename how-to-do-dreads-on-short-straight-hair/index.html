How To Do Dreads On Short Straight Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 25, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of your short, straight hair? Want to try something new and unique? Dreads might just be the perfect solution for you. Contrary to popular belief, dreads can be achieved on any hair type, including short and straight hair. In this article, we’ll show you how to do dreads on short straight hair in a simple and easy-to-follow guide.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Do Dreads on Short Straight Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Dreads

Description

Dreads are a unique and stylish way to wear your hair. They are created by twisting and matting the hair together to form thick, rope-like strands. Dreads can be created on any hair type, and contrary to popular belief, they can be achieved on short, straight hair.

Creating dreads on short straight hair requires some patience, but with the right technique and maintenance, you can achieve a beautiful and long-lasting style.

How to Do Dreads on Short Straight Hair

Before you begin the process of creating dreads, you’ll need to make sure that your hair is clean and free of any products. This will make it easier to work with and will help the dreads to form and hold better.

There are a few different methods to create dreads, but the most common method for short, straight hair is the backcombing method. This involves teasing the hair and then rolling it into a tight dread. Here’s how to do it:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by sectioning your hair into small, manageable sections. Use small rubber bands or clips to keep each section separate.
  2. Take one section of hair and backcomb it by teasing it from the ends to the roots. This will create a lot of volume and tangles in the hair.
  3. Once the section is fully teased, use a dread wax or locking gel to help hold the tangles in place. Apply the product to the section and use your fingers to roll the hair into a tight, cylindrical shape.
  4. Continue backcombing and rolling each section of hair until your entire head is covered in dreads.
  5. Once all the dreads are created, use a hair dryer to dry them completely. This will help to set the dreads and prevent them from unraveling.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Maintaining your dreads is crucial to keeping them looking great and lasting a long time. Here are a few tips to help you maintain your dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo. Avoid using regular shampoos or conditioners, as they can leave residue in your dreads and cause them to unravel.
  • Avoid using a hairbrush or comb on your dreads. Instead, use your fingers to separate and maintain them.
  • Consider using a locking spray or dread wax to help maintain the shape and hold of your dreads.
  • Be patient. Dreads take time to form and mature. It may take several months for your dreads to fully form and lock.

FAQs

Can dreads be created on short, straight hair?

Yes, dreads can be created on any hair type, including short, straight hair.

How long does it take for dreads to form?

It can take several months for dreads to fully form and lock.

What products should I use to maintain my dreads?

Use a residue-free shampoo and consider using a locking spray or dread wax to help maintain the shape and hold of your dreads.

Hair Care for Dreads

Proper hair care is crucial to maintaining healthy and long-lasting dreads. Here are a few tips to help you care for your dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using regular shampoos or conditioners, as they can leave residue in your dreads and cause them to unravel.
  • Avoid using a hairbrush or comb on your dreads. Instead, use your fingers to separate and maintain them.
  • Consider using a locking spray or dread wax to help maintain the shape and hold of your dreads.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your dreads and cause them to unravel.

Creating dreads on short, straight hair is a unique and stylish way to wear your hair. With the right technique and maintenance, you can achieve a beautiful and long-lasting style. So, go ahead and try it out – you might just love your new look!
