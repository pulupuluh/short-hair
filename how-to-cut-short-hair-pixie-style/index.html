How To Cut Short Hair Pixie Style A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 24, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you ready to embrace a new look? A pixie cut is a great way to change up your style and make a statement. But before you head to the salon, why not learn how to cut short hair pixie style yourself? Not only will you save money, but you'll also have the satisfaction of knowing you did it yourself. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting a pixie cut, from choosing the right style to finishing touches. Let's get started!

Description

A pixie cut is a short hairstyle that typically features short hair on the sides and back and longer hair on top. It's a versatile look that can be customized to suit any face shape and hair texture. Whether you have fine, thick, curly, or straight hair, a pixie cut can be tailored to your unique features.

How To

Step 1: Choose Your Pixie Style

Before you start cutting, it's important to choose the right pixie style for your face shape and hair texture. Do you want a classic, sleek pixie cut or a more textured, messy look? Do you want bangs or a side-swept fringe? Look for inspiration online or in magazines, and consult with a hairstylist if you're unsure.

Step 2: Gather Your Tools

To cut a pixie style, you'll need a few basic tools: scissors, clippers, a comb, and hair clips. You may also want to invest in a mirror that allows you to see the back of your head.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Starting with clean, dry hair, use your comb to section your hair. Clip the top section up and out of the way, leaving the sides and back loose.

Step 4: Cut the Sides and Back

Using your clippers, start at the bottom of your hairline and work your way up, using an upward motion. Be sure to blend the sides and back for a seamless look. If you prefer a more textured look, use your scissors to create choppy layers.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Unclip the top section of your hair and use your scissors to cut it to your desired length. You may want to point cut or use texturizing shears to create a more natural look. Be sure to blend the top with the sides and back for a cohesive style.

Step 6: Create Bangs or Fringe

If you want bangs or a side-swept fringe, use your scissors to cut them to your desired length. Be sure to take your time and cut small sections at a time to avoid mistakes.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Use your mirror to check the back of your head and make any necessary adjustments. Be sure to trim any stray hairs and blend any uneven sections.

Step 8: Finish and Style

Once you're happy with your cut, style your pixie cut using your favorite products. You may want to use a texturizing spray or pomade to create a messy, tousled look or a smoothing serum for a sleek finish.

Tips

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if you want a shorter style.
  • Use thinning scissors to create a more textured look.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're unsure about cutting your own hair.
  • Invest in high-quality tools to ensure a clean, precise cut.

FAQ

Q: Will a pixie cut suit my face shape?

A: Yes! There's a pixie cut for every face shape, whether you have a round, square, or oval face.

Q: Can I cut my own pixie cut?

A: Yes, with the right tools and technique, you can cut your own pixie cut at home.

Q: How often do I need to trim my pixie cut?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short your pixie cut is. Generally, you should trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your style.

Hair Care

To keep your pixie cut looking its best, be sure to wash and condition your hair regularly. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a nourishing conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. You may also want to use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair soft and smooth. And don't forget to protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before styling.

Now that you know how to cut short hair pixie style, why not give it a try? With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a chic, stylish look that's all your own.
