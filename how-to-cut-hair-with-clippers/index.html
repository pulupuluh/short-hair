How To Cut Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Mar 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to save money on haircuts or just want to try your hand at cutting hair, using clippers can be a great option. With a little practice and the right tools, you can achieve professional-looking results from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting hair with clippers, from choosing the right equipment to finishing touches.

What You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the following items:

  • Clippers
  • Clipper guards in various sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair cape or towel
  • Trash can or garbage bag
  • Electric razor (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prepare the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair, then comb it to remove any tangles. Place the hair cape or towel around the person's neck to catch any hair that falls.

Step 2: Choose the Right Clipper Guard

Clippers come with different guard sizes that determine the length of the hair. Choose the guard that will give you the desired length, starting with the longest guard and working your way down until you find the right one.

Step 3: Begin Cutting

Start at the back of the head, using the clipper to cut upward against the hair's natural growth pattern. Work in sections, using the comb to lift the hair and the clipper to trim it to the desired length.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

To blend the hair, switch to a shorter guard size and use the clipper to create a fade effect. Begin at the bottom and work your way up, gradually reducing the length of the hair until it blends smoothly into the longer hair on top.

Step 5: Trim the Sides and Back

Use scissors to trim any longer hairs around the edges of the hairline, creating a more polished look. Use the electric razor to shave any hair on the neck and around the ears.

Step 6: Finish with Detailing

To finish the haircut, use the clipper without a guard to trim around the ears and create a clean neckline. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs and create a more precise shape.

Tips for Cutting Hair with Clippers

  • Always start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use the comb to lift the hair and the clipper to trim it evenly.
  • Blend the hair by gradually reducing the length with shorter guard sizes.
  • Use the electric razor to shave any hair on the neck and around the ears.
  • Finish with detailing to create a polished look.

FAQs

Can I use clippers to cut long hair?

Clippers are best suited for cutting shorter hair lengths. If you have longer hair, it's best to use scissors to avoid creating choppy or uneven layers.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

If you accidentally cut too much hair, don't panic! You can always use a longer guard size to even out the length or wait for the hair to grow back. If you're really unsure or nervous, it's best to start with a shorter guard size and work your way up.

How often should I replace my clipper blades?

Clipper blades should be replaced every 6-12 months, depending on how often you use them. Dull blades can cause pulling, tugging, and uneven cutting, so it's important to keep them sharp and in good condition.

Hair Care After Cutting with Clippers

After cutting hair with clippers, make sure to clean them thoroughly and oil the blades to keep them in good condition. Encourage the person whose hair you cut to wash and condition their hair regularly to maintain its health and shine.

With this step-by-step guide, you can confidently cut hair with clippers and achieve a professional-looking result. Remember to take your time, use the right equipment, and practice regularly to improve your skills. Happy cutting!
