How to Cut Baby Bangs | Step by Step Guide & Tips in 2023

If you're looking to give your little one a fresh new haircut, adding baby bangs can be a cute and trendy option. Cutting baby bangs can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut without leaving your home.

Tools You Will Need

Before you start cutting baby bangs, make sure you have the right tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Spray bottle filled with water

How to Cut Baby Bangs

Cutting baby bangs requires a steady hand and patience. Follow these steps to achieve a perfect baby bangs haircut:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash your baby's hair and comb it straight.
  2. Section the hair that you want to cut into bangs. Use hair clips or ties to keep the rest of the hair away from the face.
  3. Make sure the hair is damp, not wet. You can use a spray bottle filled with water to dampen the hair.
  4. Starting from the middle of the forehead, comb down the hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair straight across using sharp scissors.
  5. Gradually work your way towards the sides of the forehead, cutting the hair in small sections.
  6. Once you've finished cutting the bangs, check if they are even. Make any necessary adjustments.
  7. Style the bangs as desired. You can use a hair dryer and a round brush to create a slight curve or keep them straight.

Tips for Cutting Baby Bangs

To make the process of cutting baby bangs easier, follow these tips:

  • Make sure your baby is comfortable and not moving around too much.
  • Start by cutting a small amount of hair, you can always cut more if needed.
  • Cut the hair straight across, do not angle the scissors.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting baby bangs can be a bit tricky, but with practice, you'll get better.

FAQs

1. At what age can I cut my baby's bangs?

It's safe to cut your baby's bangs at any age, but it's recommended to wait until your baby is at least 6 months old.

2. Can I cut my baby's bangs myself?

Yes, you can cut your baby's bangs yourself, but make sure you have the right tools and technique.

3. How often should I trim my baby's bangs?

It depends on how fast your baby's hair grows. On average, you can trim the bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care after Cutting Baby Bangs

After cutting baby bangs, it's important to take care of the hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for babies.
  • Comb the hair regularly to prevent tangles.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as hair dryers or straighteners on your baby's hair.
  • Apply a small amount of baby oil or leave-in conditioner to keep the hair moisturized.

Cutting baby bangs can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it's important to take your time and follow the right technique. With these tips and a bit of practice, you can give your little one a stylish new haircut that they'll love.
