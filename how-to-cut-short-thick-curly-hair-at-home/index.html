How To Cut Short Thick Curly Hair At Home

Written by Aprilia May 19, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune to get your short thick curly hair cut? With the right tools and techniques, you can easily cut your hair at home. In this article, we will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to cut short thick curly hair at home. From preparing your hair to finishing touches, we've got you covered. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Preparation
  • Step 2: Sectioning Your Hair
  • Step 3: Cutting Your Hair
  • Step 4: Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Cutting Short Thick Curly Hair at Home
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Short Thick Curly Hair

Step 1: Preparation

The first step to cutting your short thick curly hair at home is to prepare your hair. Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using hot water as it can dry out your hair and make it difficult to manage. Once you've washed your hair, dry it with a towel and comb it out to remove any tangles.

Next, gather all the tools you'll need for cutting your hair. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, hair clips, and a comb. Make sure your scissors are sharp as dull scissors can damage your hair.

Step 2: Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is an important step in cutting short thick curly hair at home. Start by dividing your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Starting with the back sections, remove one clip at a time and comb through your hair to remove any tangles. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off any split ends or damaged hair. Be sure to cut small sections at a time to avoid cutting off too much hair at once.

Step 3: Cutting Your Hair

Once you've trimmed the damaged hair, it's time to start cutting your hair into the desired style. Start with the back sections and work your way towards the front. Be sure to cut small sections at a time and use the comb and your fingers to guide the hair as you cut.

If you want to add layers to your hair, use the point cutting technique. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut into the ends of the hair at an angle. This will create a layered effect.

Step 4: Finishing Touches

Once you've cut your hair into the desired style, it's time to add some finishing touches. Use the comb to even out any uneven sections and trim any stray hairs. If you want to add some texture to your hair, use the point cutting technique to create a choppy look.

Once you're happy with your new haircut, style your hair as desired using hair products such as hair gel or mousse.

Tips for Cutting Short Thick Curly Hair at Home

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time to avoid cutting off too much hair at once.
  • Use the point cutting technique to add layers or texture to your hair.
  • Comb your hair before cutting to remove any tangles.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet as it can be difficult to manage and may result in an uneven cut.

FAQ

Can I cut my short thick curly hair at home?

Yes, with the right tools and techniques you can easily cut your short thick curly hair at home.

What tools do I need to cut my short thick curly hair at home?

You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, hair clips, and a comb.

Can I add layers to my short thick curly hair at home?

Yes, you can add layers to your short thick curly hair at home using the point cutting technique.

Hair Care Tips for Short Thick Curly Hair

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair.
  • Use hair products specifically designed for curly hair to enhance your natural curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools such as hair dryers and straighteners as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Cutting your short thick curly hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can easily achieve a salon-quality haircut without breaking the bank. Remember to take your time, cut small sections at a time, and use the point cutting technique to add layers or texture to your hair. Happy cutting!
