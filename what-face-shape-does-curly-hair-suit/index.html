What Face Shape Does Curly Hair Suit

Written by Daniel Apr 02, 2023 Â· 10 min read

If you have curly hair, you may be wondering what face shape suits your hair type best. The good news is that curly hair can complement a range of face shapes, from oval to heart-shaped. In this article, we'll explore the different face shapes that curly hair suits and provide tips on how to style your hair to flatter your features.

Table of Contents

  • Oval Face
  • Round Face
  • Square Face
  • Heart Face
  • Diamond Face
  • Triangular Face
  • How to Style Curly Hair for Your Face Shape

Oval Face

If you have an oval-shaped face, you're in luck! Oval faces are considered to be the most versatile face shape when it comes to hairstyles. With curly hair, you can play up your natural texture by wearing your hair in loose waves or defined curls. You can also experiment with different hair lengths, from short bobs to long layers. The key is to avoid adding too much volume to your hair, as this can make your face look longer.

Round Face

If you have a round face, you'll want to create the illusion of length and definition. To do this, opt for hairstyles that add height at the crown of your head, such as a curly updo or a half-up, half-down style. You can also try side-swept bangs or layers that frame your face. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to the sides of your head, as this can make your face look wider.

Square Face

If you have a square-shaped face, you'll want to soften your features with curly hair. Try wearing your hair in loose waves or a curly bob that falls just below your chin. You can also experiment with side-swept bangs or a middle part, which can help to break up the symmetry of your face. Avoid hairstyles with blunt edges or straight lines, as this can emphasize your square jawline.

Heart Face

If you have a heart-shaped face, you'll want to create balance by adding volume to the lower half of your face. To do this, opt for hairstyles with curls or waves that start at your chin or below. You can also try a side part with face-framing layers or bangs. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to the top of your head, as this can make your forehead look wider.

Diamond Face

If you have a diamond-shaped face, you'll want to create the illusion of width at the forehead and chin. To do this, opt for hairstyles with volume at the crown of your head, such as a curly ponytail or an updo with loose curls. You can also try side-swept bangs or a middle part with face-framing layers. Avoid hairstyles that add volume at the sides of your head, as this can make your face look narrower.

Triangular Face

If you have a triangular-shaped face, you'll want to create balance by adding volume to the upper half of your face. To do this, opt for hairstyles with volume at the crown of your head, such as a curly half-up, half-down style or a curly updo. You can also try side-swept bangs or layers that frame your face. Avoid hairstyles that add volume to the lower half of your face, as this can make your chin look more prominent.

How to Style Curly Hair for Your Face Shape

Now that you know which hairstyles suit your face shape, it's time to learn how to style your curly hair to flatter your features. Here are some tips:

  • Invest in a good curling cream or gel to define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid using a brush on your curls, as this can break up the natural texture of your hair.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls without disrupting their shape.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find the ones that suit your face shape best.

Tips and Tricks for Styling Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for styling curly hair:

  • Don't wash your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as this can damage your curls.
  • Protect your curls from the elements by wearing a hat or scarf, especially in cold or windy weather.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I wear my curly hair in a straight style?

Yes, you can wear your curly hair in a straight style if you prefer. However, keep in mind that straightening your hair can cause damage, so it's important to use heat protectant products and avoid using heat styling tools every day.

Can I wear a curly wig if I have straight hair?

Yes, you can wear a curly wig even if you have straight hair. Wigs are a great way to experiment with different hairstyles without committing to a permanent change.

How do I prevent frizz in my curly hair?

To prevent frizz in your curly hair, use a curling cream or gel to define your curls and reduce flyaways. Avoid using a brush on your curls, as this can break up the natural texture of your hair. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls without disrupting their shape.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.
  • Sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles that pull on your curls, such as tight ponytails or braids.

With these tips and tricks, you can rock your curly hair with confidence no matter what your face shape is. Remember to embrace your natural texture and have fun experimenting with different hairstyles!
