What Does The Quran Say About Long Hair

Written by Frank Jul 12, 2023 Â· 8 min read

In Islam, the Quran is the holy book that serves as a guide for Muslims in all aspects of life. It contains teachings on various topics, including modesty, cleanliness, and personal grooming. Hair is an essential part of our appearance, and many people wonder what does the Quran say about long hair. In this article, we will explore the Quran's teachings about hair and whether it is permissible to keep long hair for both men and women.

What Does the Quran Say About Hair?

The Quran does not provide specific instructions on how to style or cut hair. However, it does emphasize the importance of cleanliness and personal hygiene. Muslims are encouraged to keep themselves clean and presentable at all times, including their hair.

There are several verses in the Quran that mention hair, including:

Surah Al-Baqarah (2:197): "And take provisions, but indeed, the best provision is fear of Allah. So fear Me, O you of understanding."

Surah An-Nisa (4:119): "And whoever obeys Allah and the Messenger - those will be with the ones upon whom Allah has bestowed favor of the prophets, the steadfast affirmers of truth, the martyrs and the righteous. And excellent are those as companions!"

Surah Al-Ahzab (33:59): "O Prophet, tell your wives and your daughters and the women of the believers to bring down over themselves their outer garments. That is more suitable that they will be known and not be abused. And ever is Allah Forgiving and Merciful."

These verses do not directly address the issue of long hair, but they do provide guidance on how Muslims should conduct themselves in various situations.

Is it Permissible to Keep Long Hair in Islam?

There is no clear-cut answer to this question. In general, Islam encourages modesty and simplicity in all aspects of life, including personal grooming. However, there is no specific ruling in the Quran or in the Hadith that prohibits keeping long hair.

Some scholars suggest that keeping long hair is discouraged for men because it is associated with femininity. However, other scholars argue that as long as the hair is clean and well-groomed, it is permissible for men to keep long hair.

For women, there is no issue with keeping long hair as long as it is kept clean and covered appropriately in public. Islam encourages women to dress modestly and cover their hair in public, but there is no specific ruling on the length of hair.

How to Care for Long Hair in Islam?

For those who choose to keep long hair, it is essential to take good care of it. Here are some tips for caring for long hair in Islam:

1. Keep it Clean

Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo to keep it clean and healthy. Use a conditioner to keep it soft and manageable.

2. Cover it Appropriately

For women, it is important to cover their hair appropriately in public. Use a hijab or a headscarf to cover your hair and maintain your modesty.

3. Avoid Using Harsh Chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals or styling products that can damage your hair. Instead, use natural products like coconut oil or olive oil to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

4. Trim it Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage. This will also help your hair grow longer and healthier.

FAQs About Long Hair in Islam

Is it haram to keep long hair in Islam?

No, it is not haram to keep long hair in Islam, as long as it is kept clean and well-groomed.

Is it sunnah to keep short hair in Islam?

There is no specific sunnah that requires Muslims to keep short hair. However, some scholars suggest that keeping short hair is recommended for men because it is more practical and easier to maintain.

Can men have long hair in Islam?

Yes, men can have long hair in Islam as long as it is clean and well-groomed.

Can women have long hair in Islam?

Yes, women can have long hair in Islam as long as it is kept clean and covered appropriately in public.

Conclusion

There is no clear ruling in the Quran or in the Hadith that prohibits keeping long hair in Islam. However, Islam encourages modesty and cleanliness in all aspects of life, including personal grooming. If you choose to keep long hair, it is important to take good care of it and maintain your modesty by covering it appropriately in public. By following these guidelines, you can keep your long hair healthy and beautiful while still adhering to the teachings of Islam.
