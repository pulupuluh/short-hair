How To Trim Short Curly Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Trimming short curly hair at home can be intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it can be a breeze. In this article, we will go over the steps to trimming your short curly hair at home, as well as provide some tips and advice for maintaining your curls.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Tips for Trimming Short Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Short Curly Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before you start trimming your hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Clips or hair ties to section your hair
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Mirror

Preparation

It's important to prepare your hair before you start trimming. Here's what you need to do:

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry until it's about 80% dry.
  2. Comb through your hair with a wide-tooth comb to remove any tangles.
  3. Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Secure each section with clips or hair ties.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair will make it easier to trim and ensure that you cut all of your curls evenly. Here's how to section your hair:

  1. Start with one of the back sections.
  2. Use your fingers to part your hair down the middle, from the crown of your head to the nape of your neck.
  3. Clip one side up and out of the way.
  4. Take a small section of hair from the unclipped side and twist it to create a small rope-like section.
  5. Hold the twisted section between your fingers and trim off the ends. Make sure to cut straight across and avoid cutting into the curls.
  6. Repeat this process with small sections of hair until you have trimmed all of the curls on that side of your head.
  7. Repeat on the other side of your head.
  8. Move on to the front sections of your hair and repeat the same process.

Cutting Your Hair

Now that your hair is sectioned, it's time to start cutting. Here are some tips:

  • Start with small sections of hair.
  • Hold the section of hair between your fingers and use the scissors to trim off the ends.
  • Make sure to cut straight across and avoid cutting into the curls.
  • Continue trimming small sections of hair until you have trimmed all of your curls.

Tips for Trimming Short Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when trimming your short curly hair at home:

  • Don't pull your hair taut when trimming. This can cause the curls to bounce up and make it harder to cut straight across.
  • Trim a little bit at a time. It's better to trim too little than too much.
  • If you have trouble cutting straight across, try using a guide. You can use a ruler or a piece of paper to help you cut in a straight line.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're having trouble trimming the back of your head, ask a friend or family member to help you.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about trimming short curly hair at home:

  • How often should I trim my short curly hair? It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.
  • Can I trim my hair when it's wet? It's best to trim your hair when it's dry so you can see the true length of your curls.
  • Do I need to use special scissors? While it's not necessary, it's recommended to use sharp hair scissors to prevent damage to your hair.

Hair Care for Short Curly Hair

After you have trimmed your short curly hair, it's important to take care of it to keep your curls looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair, as this can cause damage and frizz.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls moisturized.
  • Try not to touch your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you should be able to trim your short curly hair at home with confidence. Remember to take it slow, use the right tools, and always cut straight across to avoid damaging your curls. With a little practice, you'll be a pro in no time!
