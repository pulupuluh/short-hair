Alopecia Bald Spot Getting Bigger Causes Treatment And Prevention

Written by Luffy Jun 14, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have alopecia, you may have experienced a bald spot getting bigger over time. This can be frustrating and disheartening, but there are things you can do to treat and prevent further hair loss. In this article, we will discuss the causes of alopecia and how to deal with a bald spot getting bigger.

What is Alopecia?

Alopecia is a medical condition that causes hair loss on the scalp and other parts of the body. There are different types of alopecia, including:

  • Androgenetic alopecia (male and female pattern baldness)
  • Alopecia areata (patches of baldness that come and go)
  • Traction alopecia (hair loss caused by tight hairstyles)
  • Scarring alopecia (hair loss caused by scarring on the scalp)

Alopecia can affect anyone, regardless of age, gender, or ethnicity. It is estimated that 6.8 million people in the United States have some form of alopecia.

Causes of Alopecia

The exact cause of alopecia is unknown, but it is believed to be an autoimmune disorder. This means that the body's immune system attacks the hair follicles, causing them to shrink and eventually stop producing hair.

Other factors that may contribute to alopecia include genetics, hormonal imbalances, stress, and certain medications or medical treatments.

How to Deal with a Bald Spot Getting Bigger

If you have a bald spot that is getting bigger, there are several things you can do to treat and prevent further hair loss.

Treatment Options

There is no cure for alopecia, but there are treatments that can help slow down or stop hair loss. These include:

  • Topical medications, such as minoxidil, which can help stimulate hair growth
  • Corticosteroid injections, which can reduce inflammation and promote hair growth
  • Oral medications, such as finasteride, which can block the production of the hormone that causes hair loss
  • Hair transplant surgery, which involves taking hair from one part of the scalp and transplanting it to the bald area

Prevention Tips

While there is no guaranteed way to prevent alopecia, there are some things you can do to reduce your risk of hair loss:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair, such as braids, weaves, and ponytails
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals
  • Reduce stress through relaxation techniques, such as yoga or meditation
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption

FAQ About Alopecia

What is the difference between alopecia and male/female pattern baldness?

Alopecia is a medical condition that can cause hair loss on any part of the body, while male and female pattern baldness specifically affects the scalp. Alopecia can also cause patchy hair loss, while pattern baldness usually results in a receding hairline or thinning hair on the crown of the head.

Can alopecia be cured?

There is no cure for alopecia, but there are treatments that can help slow down or stop hair loss. Hair transplant surgery can also be an option for some people.

Is alopecia hereditary?

There is a genetic component to alopecia, which means that it can run in families. However, not everyone with a family history of alopecia will develop the condition.

Hair Care Tips for People with Alopecia

If you have alopecia, it's important to take good care of your hair and scalp. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid harsh chemicals and treatments that can damage the hair and scalp
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for your hair type
  • Massage your scalp regularly to improve blood flow and promote hair growth
  • Avoid wearing hats or other head coverings that are too tight or can cause friction on the scalp

If you are experiencing a bald spot getting bigger, it's important to talk to your doctor or a hair loss specialist. They can help you determine the cause of your hair loss and recommend the best treatment options for you.
