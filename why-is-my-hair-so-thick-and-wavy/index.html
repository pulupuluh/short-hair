Why Is My Hair So Thick And Wavy

Written by Christine Aug 07, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Do you often wonder why your hair is so thick and wavy? You are not alone. Many people have hair that is naturally thick and wavy, but they often struggle to manage it. In this article, we will explore the reasons why your hair is so thick and wavy, and provide tips on how to care for it.

Description

Thick and wavy hair is characterized by strands that are large in diameter and have a natural, undulating pattern. This type of hair can be challenging to manage, especially when it is long. It tends to be prone to frizz and can be difficult to style.

How to Determine Your Hair Type?

It is essential to determine your hair type before you can effectively care for it. The thickness of your hair can be determined by measuring the circumference of a single strand of hair. If the circumference is greater than 56 mm, your hair is considered thick. If it is less than 56 mm, your hair is considered thin. The texture of your hair can be determined by examining the shape of the hair shaft. If it is straight, your hair is considered straight. If it has an S-shaped wave, it is considered wavy. If it has a tight curl or spiral, it is considered curly.

Step by Step Guide to Care for Thick and Wavy Hair

Step 1: Shampoo and Condition

When washing thick and wavy hair, it is best to use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for this hair type. Look for products that contain hydrating ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and avocado oil. These ingredients will help to nourish your hair and keep it moisturized.

Step 2: Detangle

After washing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle it. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots. Be gentle and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair. If your hair is particularly tangled, you can use a detangling spray to make the process easier.

Step 3: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner is an essential product for those with thick and wavy hair. It will help to keep your hair moisturized throughout the day and prevent frizz. Apply a small amount to your hair, focusing on the ends, and work it through with your fingers.

Step 4: Air Dry

Avoid using a blow dryer on your hair, as this can cause frizz and damage. Instead, let your hair air dry naturally. You can scrunch your hair as it dries to encourage your natural waves.

Tips for Managing Thick and Wavy Hair

Tip 1: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When detangling your hair, always use a wide-tooth comb. This will prevent breakage and damage to your hair.

Tip 2: Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Aim to wash your hair no more than three times a week.

Tip 3: Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to prevent frizz and tangles. Silk is gentle on your hair and will not cause breakage or damage.

FAQ

Q: Is it Possible to Straighten Thick and Wavy Hair?

A: Yes, it is possible to straighten thick and wavy hair. However, you should use caution when using heat styling tools, as they can cause damage to your hair. Always use a heat protectant spray before using a flat iron or curling iron.

Q: Can Thick and Wavy Hair Be Thinned Out?

A: Yes, thick and wavy hair can be thinned out by a professional stylist. They can use thinning shears to remove some of the bulk from your hair, making it easier to manage.

Hair Care Products for Thick and Wavy Hair

When caring for thick and wavy hair, it is essential to use products that are specifically formulated for this hair type. Here are some of the best products for thick and wavy hair:

  • Shea Moisture Coconut & Hibiscus Curl & Shine Shampoo
  • OGX Coconut Curls Conditioner
  • Cantu Shea Butter Leave-In Conditioning Repair Cream
  • DevaCurl SuperCream Coconut Curl Styler
  • Olaplex No. 8 Bond Intense Moisture Mask

Thick and wavy hair can be challenging to manage, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to be gentle with your hair and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools.
