How To Fix Severely Chemically Damaged Hair

Written by Frank Aug 18, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're struggling with severely chemically damaged hair, you're not alone. Many people experience damage from excessive heat styling, coloring, or relaxing treatments. However, the good news is that there are ways to repair and restore your hair to its former glory. In this article, we'll cover how to fix severely chemically damaged hair in relaxed English language, including tips, step-by-step instructions, and FAQs.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How To Fix Severely Chemically Damaged Hair
  3. Step-By-Step Instructions
  4. Tips for Repairing Severely Chemically Damaged Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Tips to Prevent Future Damage

Description

Severely chemically damaged hair can be defined as hair that has been subjected to harsh chemicals, such as relaxers or color treatments, and is now brittle, dry, and prone to breakage. This type of damage can be caused by over-processing, using the wrong products, or not taking proper care of your hair.

Fortunately, with the right approach, you can repair severely chemically damaged hair and restore it to a healthier state.

How to Fix Severely Chemically Damaged Hair

The first step in fixing severely chemically damaged hair is to assess the extent of the damage. Depending on the severity of the damage, you may need to take more drastic measures, such as cutting off the damaged hair and starting fresh. However, in most cases, you can repair the damage with a few simple steps:

Step 1: Deep Condition

The first step in repairing chemically damaged hair is to deep condition it. Deep conditioning treatments are designed to penetrate the hair shaft and provide much-needed moisture and nutrients to the hair. Look for a deep conditioning treatment that contains ingredients like protein, keratin, and natural oils, as these ingredients can help repair damaged hair.

Step 2: Trim Your Hair

If your hair is severely damaged, it may be necessary to trim off the damaged ends. This will help prevent further breakage and allow your hair to grow back healthier. Try to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy.

Step 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can further damage already chemically damaged hair. If possible, avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. Instead, let your hair air dry and style it using non-heat methods.

Step 4: Use Gentle Products

When you have severely chemically damaged hair, you need to be extra gentle. Use gentle shampoos and conditioners that are designed for damaged hair. Avoid products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates, as these can further damage your hair.

Tips for Repairing Severely Chemically Damaged Hair

In addition to the steps outlined above, there are a few additional things you can do to repair severely chemically damaged hair:

  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated from the inside out.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that support hair health.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull and damage your hair.

FAQs

Q: How long will it take to repair severely chemically damaged hair?

A: The time it takes to repair severely chemically damaged hair can vary depending on the extent of the damage. However, with consistent care and attention, you should start to see improvement in your hair's health within a few weeks.

Q: Can I color my hair if it's severely chemically damaged?

A: Coloring severely chemically damaged hair can further damage it. It's best to wait until your hair is healthy before coloring it.

Q: Will cutting my hair help repair chemically damaged hair?

A: Yes, cutting off the damaged ends of your hair can help prevent further breakage and allow your hair to grow back healthier.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Future Damage

Now that you've repaired your severely chemically damaged hair, it's important to take steps to prevent future damage. Here are a few hair care tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Avoid over-processing your hair with harsh chemicals.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull and damage your hair.
  • Use gentle shampoos and conditioners that are designed for your hair type.

In conclusion, severely chemically damaged hair can be frustrating, but it's not impossible to repair. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can restore your hair's health and prevent future damage. Remember to be patient and consistent in your hair care routine, and your hair will thank you for it.
