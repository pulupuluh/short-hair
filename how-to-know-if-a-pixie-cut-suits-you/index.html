How To Know If A Pixie Cut Suits You A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Mar 12, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're looking for a dramatic change in your hairstyle, a pixie cut may be just what you need. But how do you know if this short, sleek style will look good on you? In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to determine whether a pixie cut is the right choice for your face shape, hair type, and personal style.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Consider Your Face Shape
  • Step 2: Think About Your Hair Type
  • Step 3: Assess Your Personal Style
  • Tips for Pulling Off a Pixie Cut
  • FAQs About Pixie Cuts
  • Hair Care for Pixie Cuts

Step 1: Consider Your Face Shape

The first thing to think about when deciding whether a pixie cut suits you is your face shape. While a pixie cut can look great on many different face shapes, some styles may be more flattering than others.

If you have a round face, for example, you may want to avoid a very short, spiky pixie cut, as this can emphasize the roundness of your face. Instead, try a longer, layered pixie with side-swept bangs.

If you have a square or angular face, a short, choppy pixie cut can help soften your features. A longer, asymmetrical pixie with a side part can also be flattering.

If you have a heart-shaped face, a pixie cut with volume on top can balance out your features. Avoid styles that are too flat or tapered, as these can make your forehead look wider.

Step 2: Think About Your Hair Type

The second factor to consider is your hair type. While a pixie cut can work with many different textures, some styles may be easier to maintain than others.

If you have thick, coarse hair, a layered pixie cut can help thin out your hair and make it more manageable. A shorter, tapered pixie can also work well to showcase your natural texture.

If you have fine hair, a longer, layered pixie can add volume and texture. Avoid very short, blunt cuts, as these can make your hair look limp and lifeless.

If you have curly hair, a pixie cut can be a great way to showcase your curls. Look for styles that are longer on top and shorter on the sides, as this can help balance out your curls.

Step 3: Assess Your Personal Style

The final thing to think about is your personal style. While a pixie cut can be a bold and daring choice, it's important to make sure it aligns with your overall look and aesthetic.

If you're someone who loves to experiment with edgy, avant-garde styles, a pixie cut can be a great fit. However, if you prefer a more classic, timeless look, a longer hairstyle may be a better choice.

Consider your wardrobe and makeup as well. If you tend to wear bold, statement-making pieces, a short, dramatic pixie cut can complement your overall look. If you prefer more understated outfits and makeup, a simpler, more low-maintenance pixie might be a better fit.

Tips for Pulling Off a Pixie Cut

If you've decided that a pixie cut is right for you, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Find a stylist who has experience cutting pixie styles, as this can be a tricky cut to get right.
  • Consider bringing in pictures of pixie cuts you like to show your stylist.
  • Be prepared for frequent salon visits to maintain your cut.
  • Invest in high-quality styling products, such as pomades and waxes, to help you achieve your desired look.

FAQs About Pixie Cuts

Can anyone pull off a pixie cut?

While a pixie cut can look great on many different people, it's important to consider your face shape, hair type, and personal style before making the leap. Work with your stylist to find a cut that flatters your features and complements your look.

Will a pixie cut make my hair look thinner?

Not necessarily. A layered pixie cut can actually add volume and texture to fine hair, while a shorter, choppy cut can help thin out thick, coarse hair. Talk to your stylist about your hair concerns and they can help you find the right cut for your needs.

How do I style a pixie cut?

The styling process will depend on your hair type and the specific cut you choose. However, some general tips include using a small amount of styling product to add texture and hold, blow-drying your hair with a small round brush to add volume, and using your fingers to create a messy, tousled look.

Hair Care for Pixie Cuts

To keep your pixie cut looking its best, it's important to take good care of your hair. This includes:

  • Washing and conditioning your hair regularly with products that are suited to your hair type.
  • Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and healthy.
  • Avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible, or using a heat protectant spray if you do use them.
  • Trimming your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your cut and prevent split ends.

With these tips in mind, you'll be able to determine whether a pixie cut is right for you and rock this bold, daring style with confidence!
