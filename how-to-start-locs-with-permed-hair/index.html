How To Start Locs With Permed Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine May 31, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Starting locs with permed hair can be a tricky process, but it's definitely possible with the right techniques and knowledge. In this guide, we'll go over the steps you need to take to start your locs with permed hair, as well as some tips and tricks to help you along the way. Whether you're doing it yourself or going to a professional, this guide will help you achieve the locs you've been dreaming of.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Start Locs with Permed Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Starting Locs with Permed Hair
  • FAQ

Description

Permed hair can be a great starting point for locs, as the curls and texture can help the locs form faster and more easily. However, it's important to note that permed hair can also be more fragile and prone to breakage, so it's important to take extra care during the starting process and throughout the life of your locs.

How to Start Locs with Permed Hair

The first step in starting locs with permed hair is to decide on the method you want to use. There are a few different methods to choose from, including:

  • Twist and Rip
  • Backcombing
  • Crochet
  • Freeform

Each method has its own pros and cons, so it's important to do your research and choose the one that's right for you. Once you've decided on a method, you can start preparing your hair for the locs.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the general steps you'll need to follow to start locs with permed hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup and prepare your hair for the process.
  2. Part your hair into sections using a comb or your fingers. The size of the sections will depend on the method you're using and the size of the locs you want.
  3. Start the locs using the method you've chosen. Each method will have its own specific steps, so be sure to follow the instructions carefully.
  4. Once you've started the locs, be sure to keep them clean and well-maintained. This may involve regular washing, moisturizing, and retwisting or palm rolling.
  5. Be patient! It can take several months or even years for your locs to mature and fully form.

Tips for Starting Locs with Permed Hair

Here are a few tips to help you start your locs with permed hair:

  • Be gentle with your hair. Permed hair can be more fragile than natural hair, so it's important to take extra care during the starting process.
  • Moisturize regularly. Permed hair can be more prone to dryness, so it's important to keep your hair moisturized with oils, creams, or sprays.
  • Be patient. Locs take time to form, so don't get discouraged if it takes a while for your locs to start maturing.
  • Consider going to a professional. If you're not comfortable starting your locs on your own, consider going to a professional loctician who can help you achieve the look you want.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about starting locs with permed hair:

Can you start locs with permed hair?

Yes, you can start locs with permed hair. Permed hair can actually be a great starting point for locs, as the curls and texture can help the locs form faster and more easily.

What is the best method for starting locs with permed hair?

There are several methods you can use to start locs with permed hair, including twist and rip, backcombing, crochet, and freeform. Each method has its own pros and cons, so it's important to do your research and choose the one that's right for you.

How long does it take for locs to form with permed hair?

It can take several months or even years for your locs to fully form and mature. The exact timeline will depend on factors like your hair type, the method you're using, and how well you care for your locs.

Hair Care for Locs with Permed Hair

Once you've started your locs with permed hair, it's important to take good care of them to keep them healthy and strong. Here are a few tips for caring for locs with permed hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to keep your scalp and locs clean.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly with oils, creams, or sprays to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Retwist or palm roll your locs regularly to keep them neat and prevent them from unraveling.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils that can weigh down your locs and make them look greasy.
  • Be gentle with your locs when styling or handling them to prevent breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can start your locs with permed hair and enjoy the unique and beautiful look of locs. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair, and you'll soon have the locs you've been dreaming of.
