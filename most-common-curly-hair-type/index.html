Most Common Curly Hair Type Understanding And Caring For Your Locks

Written by Bardi Aug 27, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Curly hair comes in all shapes and sizes, but there is one type that is the most common: type 3 curls. This hair type is characterized by defined curls that range from loose to tight, and it's often the most challenging to manage. In this article, we'll dive deeper into the most common curly hair type, explore tips for caring for your locks, and answer some frequently asked questions.

What is Type 3 Curly Hair?

Type 3 curly hair is characterized by S-shaped curls that range from loose to tight. This hair type is further broken down into three subtypes:

Type 3a:

Type 3a curls are the loosest of the type 3 curls. They are defined and springy, but they have a lot of bounce and can be easily straightened.

Type 3b:

Type 3b curls are tighter than 3a curls and have a more defined S-shape. They often have a lot of volume and can be prone to frizz.

Type 3c:

Type 3c curls are the tightest of the type 3 curls. They have a corkscrew shape and are densely packed. They can be prone to dryness and require a lot of moisture to thrive.

How to Care for Type 3 Curly Hair

Caring for type 3 curly hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques and products, you can keep your locks healthy and defined. Here are some tips:

1. Moisturize, Moisturize, Moisturize

Type 3 curls tend to be dry, so it's essential to keep them moisturized. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to hydrate your strands and prevent breakage.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Type 3 curls are prone to tangling, so it's essential to use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently. Start from the ends and work your way up to prevent breakage.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your curls and lead to breakage. Try to avoid using hot tools and let your hair air-dry to keep your curls healthy.

4. Use a Microfiber Towel

Traditional towels can rough up your curls and cause frizz. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair gently.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Type 3 Curly Hair

Styling type 3 curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques, you can create defined and bouncy curls. Here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Start with Clean Hair

Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls healthy and defined.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your damp hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends.

3. Use a Curl Enhancing Product

Apply a curl-enhancing product, such as a curl cream or gel, to your hair to define your curls and prevent frizz.

4. Diffuse Your Hair

Use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry your hair gently. Cup the ends of your hair in the diffuser and lift it towards your roots to create volume.

Tips for Maintaining Type 3 Curly Hair

Maintaining type 3 curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques, you can keep your locks healthy and defined. Here are some tips:

1. Sleep on a Satin Pillowcase

Satin pillowcases are gentle on your curls and prevent breakage. They also help to prevent frizz and keep your curls defined.

2. Refresh Your Curls

Use a curl refreshing spray to revive your curls between washes. Spray it onto your hair and scrunch it gently to redefine your curls.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can dry out your curls and cause damage. Wear a hat or use a hair protectant spray to shield your locks from the sun's rays.

FAQs About Type 3 Curly Hair

Q: How often should I wash my type 3 curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair's texture and how much product you use. Generally, it's best to wash your hair once or twice a week.

Q: Can I brush my type 3 curly hair?

A: It's best to use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently. Brushing can cause breakage and disrupt your curls' natural pattern.

Q: What should I do if my curls are dry?

A: Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to hydrate your hair. You can also use a hair oil or leave-in conditioner to keep your locks moisturized.

Conclusion

Type 3 curly hair is the most common curly hair type, and it requires a lot of care and attention to keep it healthy and defined. Use the tips and techniques in this article to care for your locks and create bouncy, defined curls.
