How To Wax Coarse Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Jul 02, 2023 · 12 min read

If you have coarse hair, you know how difficult it can be to manage. Shaving can lead to razor burn and ingrown hairs, while depilatory creams can be harsh on the skin. Waxing, on the other hand, can provide long-lasting results without the risk of skin irritation. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about how to wax coarse hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Wax Coarse Hair
  • Step-by-Step Waxing Guide
  • Tips for Waxing Coarse Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Coarse Hair

How to Wax Coarse Hair

Waxing coarse hair requires a bit of extra effort and attention to detail, but with the right technique, it can be done effectively. Here are the steps to follow:

1. Choose the Right Wax

When it comes to waxing coarse hair, you'll want to use a hard wax that's specifically formulated for coarse hair. Hard wax adheres better to coarse hair and is less likely to break off or damage the hair follicle. Look for wax that contains natural ingredients like beeswax or soy wax, which are gentler on the skin.

2. Prep Your Skin

Before you wax, make sure your skin is clean and dry. Avoid using lotion or oil, as this can create a barrier between the wax and your hair. Exfoliating the skin a day or two before waxing can also help to remove dead skin cells and prevent ingrown hairs.

3. Apply the Wax

Using a spatula or wax applicator, apply a thin layer of wax to the area you want to wax. Apply the wax in the direction of hair growth, and make sure the layer is thick enough to fully cover the hair. Avoid applying wax to the same area multiple times, as this can irritate the skin.

4. Remove the Wax

Once the wax has cooled and hardened, use your fingers to pull the edge of the wax strip in the opposite direction of hair growth. Pull the strip quickly and firmly, keeping it as close to the skin as possible. Repeat until all of the hair has been removed.

5. Soothe the Skin

After waxing, your skin may be red and irritated. Apply a soothing cream or lotion to help calm the skin and reduce inflammation. Avoid using products that contain alcohol or fragrances, as these can further irritate the skin.

Step-by-Step Waxing Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to help you wax coarse hair:

1. Gather Your Supplies

Before you start waxing, make sure you have all the supplies you need. This includes wax, wax applicators, wax strips, and a soothing cream or lotion.

2. Prep Your Skin

As mentioned earlier, make sure your skin is clean and dry before waxing. You may also want to exfoliate the skin a day or two before waxing to remove dead skin cells and prevent ingrown hairs.

3. Heat the Wax

Follow the instructions on the wax packaging to heat the wax. Make sure the wax is heated to the correct temperature – if it's too hot, it can burn the skin, and if it's too cold, it won't adhere to the hair properly.

4. Apply the Wax

Using a spatula or wax applicator, apply a thin layer of wax to the area you want to wax. Apply the wax in the direction of hair growth, and make sure the layer is thick enough to fully cover the hair. Avoid applying wax to the same area multiple times, as this can irritate the skin.

5. Apply the Wax Strip

Once the wax is applied, place a wax strip over the area and press down firmly. Smooth the strip in the direction of hair growth to ensure the wax adheres to the hair.

6. Remove the Strip

Using one hand to hold the skin taut, use the other hand to quickly pull the wax strip in the opposite direction of hair growth. Pull the strip quickly and firmly, keeping it as close to the skin as possible. Repeat until all of the hair has been removed.

7. Soothe the Skin

After waxing, your skin may be red and irritated. Apply a soothing cream or lotion to help calm the skin and reduce inflammation. Avoid using products that contain alcohol or fragrances, as these can further irritate the skin.

Tips for Waxing Coarse Hair

Waxing coarse hair can be tricky, but these tips can help make the process go more smoothly:

1. Don't Shave in Between Waxes

If you're waxing regularly, avoid shaving in between waxes. Shaving can cause the hair to grow back thicker and coarser, which can make waxing more painful and less effective.

2. Apply Pressure to the Skin

When removing the wax strip, apply pressure to the skin with your hand immediately after pulling the strip. This can help to reduce pain and discomfort.

3. Work in Small Sections

Instead of trying to wax a large area all at once, work in small sections. This can help you to be more precise and avoid mistakes.

4. Take Pain Relievers Before Waxing

If you're sensitive to pain, consider taking a pain reliever like ibuprofen or acetaminophen before waxing. This can help to reduce pain and discomfort.

FAQs

Q: Is it safe to wax coarse hair?

A: Yes, waxing is safe for most people with coarse hair. However, if you have sensitive skin or a skin condition like eczema or psoriasis, you may want to speak with a dermatologist before waxing.

Q: How often should I wax coarse hair?

A: It's recommended to wax coarse hair every 4-6 weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows.

Q: Can I wax coarse hair at home?

A: Yes, you can wax coarse hair at home. However, it's important to follow the instructions carefully and use the right type of wax for your hair type.

Hair Care Tips for Coarse Hair

In addition to waxing, there are a few things you can do to help manage coarse hair:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Coarse hair tends to be dry, so it's important to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to help keep it hydrated.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage coarse hair, making it even more difficult to manage. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, and use a heat protectant spray if you must use them.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to keep coarse hair soft and manageable. Apply it to damp hair before styling.

4. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage, which can make coarse hair even more difficult to manage.

5. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays from the sun can damage coarse hair, making it even more dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF to protect your hair from the sun.

By following these tips and techniques, you can effectively wax coarse hair and keep it looking and feeling its best. If you have any questions or concerns, don't hesitate to speak with a dermatologist or hair care professional.
