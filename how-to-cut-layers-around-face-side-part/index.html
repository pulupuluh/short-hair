How To Cut Layers Around Face Side Part A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Mar 08, 2023 · 8 min read

If you're looking for a way to add volume and texture to your hair, cutting layers around your face and side part is a great option. This technique can help to frame your face, accentuate your features, and create a flattering shape for your hair. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps of how to cut layers around your face side part, including tips and tricks to get the best results.

Table of Contents

  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Layers Around Face Side Part
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After Cutting Layers Around Face Side Part

How to Cut Layers Around Face Side Part

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror to help you see the back of your head. You may also want to invest in some hair clips to help keep your hair in place while you work.

The first step in cutting layers around your face side part is to determine where you want the layers to start. Many people choose to start the layers around their chin or cheekbones, but you can adjust this based on your face shape and personal preference.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Part Your Hair

Start by parting your hair on the side where you want the layers to be. Use a comb to create a clean, even part from your hairline to the back of your head.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Clip the top section of your hair out of the way and gather a small section of hair from the front. This section should be about the width of your comb and extend from your part to the outer corner of your eye.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Hold the section of hair between your fingers and use your scissors to cut the ends at an angle. Start by cutting the hair at the length you want the shortest layer to be, then use that as a guide to cut the rest of the section. Be sure to cut at an angle to create a soft, blended look.

Step 4: Cut Subsequent Layers

Unclip the next section of hair and repeat the process, using the first layer as a guide to cut the subsequent layers. Continue until you have cut all of the layers you want on this side of your head.

Step 5: Repeat on the Other Side

Repeat the process on the other side of your head, making sure to mirror the length and angle of the layers on both sides. Once you have finished, check your work in the mirror and make any final adjustments as needed.

Tips for Cutting Layers Around Face Side Part

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when cutting layers around your face side part:

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure precision and control
  • Cut at an angle to create a soft, blended look
  • Check your work in the mirror frequently to make sure the layers are even on both sides
  • Don't cut too much hair at once – it's easier to take off more later than to try to fix a mistake

FAQs

How often do I need to cut layers around my face side part?

This will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how much you want to maintain the length of your layers. As a general rule, you may want to trim your layers every 6-8 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

Can I cut layers around my face side part if I have curly hair?

Absolutely! Cutting layers can add definition and shape to curly hair. However, it's important to work with a stylist who is experienced in cutting curly hair to ensure the best results.

Should I cut my own hair or go to a stylist?

If you're comfortable cutting your own hair and have experience with this technique, you can certainly do it yourself. However, if you're new to cutting hair or want to ensure the best results, it's a good idea to go to a professional stylist.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers Around Face Side Part

After cutting layers around your face side part, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as this can damage your hair
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and healthy

By following these tips and techniques, you can create beautiful, voluminous layers around your face side part. Whether you're looking for a new style or just want to freshen up your look, cutting layers is a great way to achieve the results you want.
