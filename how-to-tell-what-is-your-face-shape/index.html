How To Tell What Is Your Face Shape A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Mar 01, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Knowing your face shape is essential when it comes to choosing the right hairstyle, makeup, and accessories. It can help you highlight your best features and balance out your facial proportions. In this article, we'll show you how to tell what is your face shape in relaxed English language, with easy-to-follow steps and useful tips.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Measure Your Forehead, Cheekbones, and Jawline
  • Step 2: Determine the Widest Part of Your Face
  • Step 3: Identify Your Face Shape
  • Tips for Enhancing Your Face Shape
  • FAQs About Face Shapes
  • Hair Care Tips for Different Face Shapes

Step 1: Measure Your Forehead, Cheekbones, and Jawline

The first step in determining your face shape is to measure your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. You can use a flexible measuring tape or a ruler for this. Here's how:

  1. Forehead: Measure the widest part of your forehead, from one temple to the other.
  2. Cheekbones: Measure across your cheekbones, starting from the point where they are the widest.
  3. Jawline: Measure from the tip of your chin to the point below your ear where your jawbone angles upward.

Write down these measurements for reference in the next steps.

Step 2: Determine the Widest Part of Your Face

Next, you need to identify the widest part of your face. This will help you determine your face shape. Here's how:

  1. Oval: The widest part of an oval face is the forehead, and it tapers down to the jawline.
  2. Round: The widest part of a round face is the cheekbones, and it is almost as wide as the face length.
  3. Square: The forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are almost equal in width on a square face.
  4. Heart: The widest part of a heart-shaped face is the forehead, and it narrows down to a pointed chin.
  5. Diamond: The cheekbones are the widest part of a diamond face, and the forehead and jawline are narrower.
  6. Triangle: The jawline is the widest part of a triangular face, and it narrows up to the forehead.
  7. Rectangle: The forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are almost equal in width on a rectangular face, but the face length is longer.

Once you have identified the widest part of your face, you can move on to the final step.

Step 3: Identify Your Face Shape

Based on your measurements and the widest part of your face, you can now determine your face shape. Here's how:

  1. Oval: The forehead is slightly wider than the chin, and the face length is longer than the width.
  2. Round: The face length and width are almost equal, and the jawline is soft and rounded.
  3. Square: The forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are almost equal in width, and the jawline is sharp and angular.
  4. Heart: The forehead is wider than the cheeks and narrows down to a pointed chin.
  5. Diamond: The cheekbones are the widest part of the face, and the forehead and jawline are narrower.
  6. Triangle: The jawline is wider than the forehead, and the face narrows up to the temples.
  7. Rectangle: The forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are almost equal in width, but the face length is longer.

Now that you know your face shape, you can use this information to choose hairstyles, makeup, and accessories that flatter your features.

Tips for Enhancing Your Face Shape

Here are some tips for enhancing your face shape:

  • If you have a round face, try a hairstyle with layers or angles to create the illusion of length.
  • If you have a square face, soften your jawline with a wavy hairstyle or a side-swept bang.
  • If you have a heart-shaped face, draw attention to your eyes with bold eye makeup or statement earrings.
  • If you have a diamond face, balance your features with a chin-length bob or a cat-eye makeup look.
  • If you have a triangular face, add volume to the top of your head with a voluminous hairstyle or a high bun.
  • If you have an oval or rectangular face, experiment with different hairstyles and accessories to highlight your best features.

Remember, these are just guidelines, and you should choose the styles that make you feel confident and comfortable.

FAQs About Face Shapes

Here are some frequently asked questions about face shapes:

What is the most common face shape?

Oval is considered the most common face shape.

Can your face shape change over time?

Your face shape can change slightly over time due to factors such as aging, weight loss or gain, and changes in bone structure.

Can makeup change your face shape?

Makeup can create the illusion of a different face shape, but it cannot change your actual bone structure.

Hair Care Tips for Different Face Shapes

Here are some hair care tips for different face shapes:

  • If you have an oval face, almost any hairstyle will suit you.
  • If you have a round face, avoid hairstyles that add volume to the sides of your head.
  • If you have a square face, soften your jawline with a layered or wavy hairstyle.
  • If you have a heart-shaped face, avoid hairstyles that create too much volume on top of your head.
  • If you have a diamond face, try a chin-length bob or a layered hairstyle that adds volume to the sides of your head.
  • If you have a triangular face, avoid hairstyles that add volume to the lower half of your face.
  • If you have an oval or rectangular face, experiment with different hairstyles and accessories to highlight your best features.

Remember, the most important thing is to choose a hairstyle that makes you feel confident and comfortable. Your face shape is just a guideline!
